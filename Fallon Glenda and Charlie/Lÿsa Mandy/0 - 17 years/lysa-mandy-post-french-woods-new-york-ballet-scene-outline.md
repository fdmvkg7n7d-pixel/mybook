# SCENE OUTLINE: AFTER FRENCH WOODS, FALLON GLENDA AND CHARLIE TAKE LŸSA MANDY TO NEW YORK CITY FOR A BALLET PERFORMANCE

## Placement

**When:** Immediately after the end of Lÿsa Mandy's **second session / second summer** at French Woods.

**Where:** Transition from camp into **New York City**, culminating in a live ballet performance.

**Lÿsa Mandy's age:** Eight.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene works as the **reward and continuation** of Lÿsa Mandy's French Woods arc.

It should establish that:

1. Fallon Glenda and Charlie do not simply pick her up and take her home.
2. They honor what the summer has deepened in her.
3. Ballet is not only a class activity for Lÿsa Mandy; it is something they now take seriously enough to build a family moment around.
4. The end of camp opens into another beautiful experience rather than snapping shut.

---

## Emotional Core

The emotional core is **recognition**.

Fallon and Charlie are not rewarding Lÿsa Mandy for surviving separation. They are recognizing who she is becoming.

After six weeks of ballet, gymnastics, swimming, routine, and camp confidence, taking her to New York City for a ballet performance says:

- we saw what mattered to you
- we believed you when you said ballet mattered
- your inner life changes how we move through the world with you

---

## Tone

Celebratory, elegant, affectionate, and slightly awe-struck through a child's eyes.

The scene should feel like a bridge between childhood enchantment and serious artistic memory.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — Camp Ends, But the Emotional Energy Does Not Drop
When Fallon Glenda and Charlie pick Lÿsa Mandy up after her second session, the emotional tone is not collapse or recovery.

She is happy to be with them again, but she is also full of camp momentum. They can see immediately that this is not a child shrinking back into home after difficulty. This is a child carrying a whole summer out with her.

### Beat 2 — The New Plan Is Revealed
At the right moment — either at pickup, in the car, or after one quiet transitional beat — Fallon and Charlie tell Lÿsa Mandy they are not going straight home.

They are taking her to **New York City** to see a **ballet performance**.

The reveal should matter.

This is not just an outing. It is a recognition of what this summer has meant to her.

### Beat 3 — Lÿsa Mandy Processes the Scale of It
Lÿsa Mandy's reaction should not be written as generic excitement.

She is eight. The meaning may come in waves:

- first surprise
- then questions
- then a more serious dawning realization that this is *real*
- then the particular kind of focus children get when something precious becomes imaginable

### Beat 4 — Fallon as the Parent Who Notices the Exact Fit
Fallon Glenda feels the rightness of the plan in a very specific way.

After a summer in which Lÿsa Mandy has loved **ballet and gymnastics equally**, this trip does not erase gymnastics or flatten her into one thing. It simply honors one of her deepest joys in a grand way.

The scene should let Fallon feel that satisfaction: not performing motherhood, but getting her daughter *right*.

### Beat 5 — Charlie Holds the Shape of the Surprise
Charlie helps make the trip feel steady rather than overwhelming.

That may mean:

- handling the practical transitions
- keeping the tone calm when Lÿsa Mandy starts asking many questions
- making the bigness of New York and the performance feel exciting without letting it become chaotic

He helps turn the surprise into something she can inhabit.

### Beat 6 — Arrival in New York City
The city should feel large, vertical, and slightly dazzling after camp.

Important contrast:

- French Woods = trees, routine, cabins, movement through known spaces
- New York City = lights, buildings, cultural grandeur, heightened scale

That contrast should make the ballet night feel even bigger.

### Beat 7 — The Performance Night
The ballet performance is the emotional centerpiece.

What matters most is not technical review of the production, but Lÿsa Mandy's experience of it:

- getting dressed for it
- entering the theater
- seeing the stage before it begins
- understanding that ballet can exist on this scale
- feeling that something she loves privately and practically also belongs to a larger, luminous world

### Beat 8 — Fallon Watches Lÿsa Mandy Watch the Ballet
This is one of the strongest beats available.

Instead of only watching the stage, Fallon also watches Lÿsa Mandy's face, posture, stillness, or intake of breath.

For Fallon, this may become one of those maternal moments where love and recognition become nearly the same thing.

### Beat 9 — Charlie Sees the Future in a Small, Quiet Way
Charlie does not need to turn the moment into prophecy.

But he can feel that they are witnessing a memory that may stay with Lÿsa Mandy for years: the first time camp, art, family, and celebration all fused into one evening.

### Beat 10 — After the Performance
Afterward, Lÿsa Mandy is likely full of observations.

She may talk about:

- costumes
- a specific movement
- how the dancers looked
- whether it felt different from class
- whether it made her want to dance more

The scene should let her respond like a child, not an adult critic.

### Beat 11 — Emotional Landing
The final emotional truth is that this New York trip completes a three-part summer arc:

- **French Woods** gave her practice, familiarity, and growth.
- **Letters and Parents Day** proved the separation worked.
- **The ballet trip** tells her that what she loves is visible to the people who love her.

## What This Scene Establishes

1. The end of Lÿsa Mandy's second French Woods summer opens into a family celebration.
2. Fallon and Charlie actively shape memory around what matters to her.
3. Ballet becomes part of her larger family canon, not just an activity block at camp.
4. New York City enters Lÿsa Mandy's branch through art, not tourism alone.
5. The trip works as an emotional capstone to the full French Woods arc.
