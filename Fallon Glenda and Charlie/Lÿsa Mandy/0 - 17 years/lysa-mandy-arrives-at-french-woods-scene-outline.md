# SCENE OUTLINE: LŸSA MANDY ARRIVES AT FRENCH WOODS

## Placement

**When:** Beginning of Lÿsa Mandy's second summer at French Woods.

**Where:** French Woods campus at check-in and cabin arrival.

**Lÿsa Mandy's age:** Eight.

**Camp structure:** She is returning for **two sessions totaling six weeks**.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene establishes that Lÿsa Mandy is no longer a first-timer at camp.

It should show:

1. **recognition instead of fear**
2. **earned confidence instead of forced bravery**
3. why Fallon and Charlie can leave her there with real peace
4. that ballet, gymnastics, and swimming are already part of how Lÿsa Mandy imagines the summer ahead

---

## Emotional Core

The emotional core is **return**.

This is not a wrenching drop-off scene. It is a scene about a child re-entering a place that has already become part of her own life.

For Fallon, the scene is about watching her daughter belong somewhere.
For Charlie, it is about recognizing that belonging and helping Fallon trust it.
For Lÿsa Mandy, it is about stepping back into something known and exciting.

---

## Tone

Warm, grounded, lightly emotional, and confidence-building.

The scene should feel active rather than tragic.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — The Drive In
Fallon and Charlie bring Lÿsa Mandy to French Woods.

She is excited in a specific, lived-in way. She points out things she remembers or asks whether the same activity spaces will be open again this year. Her anticipation is not abstract — it is attached to real memories.

### Beat 2 — Arrival Energy
At check-in, the difference between a first summer and a second one is immediately visible.

Lÿsa Mandy is not frozen. She is alert, observant, and already mentally moving toward camp life. She may still feel a little tightened-up around transitions, but she has proof now that she can do this.

### Beat 3 — She Names What She Wants
Very early in the scene, Lÿsa Mandy talks about what she is excited for:

- ballet
- gymnastics
- swimming

Importantly, she does **not** rank ballet above gymnastics or gymnastics above ballet. She loves both equally, and that should come through naturally.

### Beat 4 — A Familiar Face
A counselor, staff member, or returning camper recognizes her.

This beat matters enormously. The recognition confirms that camp remembers her too. She is not just returning to a place; she is being received by it.

### Beat 5 — Fallon Watches Her Settle
Fallon sees the tiny signals that matter most:

- Lÿsa Mandy answering instead of hiding
- Lÿsa Mandy turning her body toward camp instead of staying locked onto her parents
- Lÿsa Mandy carrying her own excitement

For Fallon, this is the moment the separation becomes bearable.

### Beat 6 — Charlie Reads Both of Them Correctly
Charlie notices Fallon noticing.

He does not intrude on the moment. He simply helps hold the emotional tone steady. If Fallon needs reassurance, he gives it quietly and without making the scene heavier than it is.

### Beat 7 — The Goodbye
The goodbye is loving but not melodramatic.

Lÿsa Mandy hugs them, perhaps reminds them she is staying for both sessions, and then turns back toward camp with genuine momentum. The emotional point is that she has somewhere to go that is truly hers.

### Beat 8 — Fallon Says It Out Loud
Once Lÿsa Mandy is gone into camp flow, Fallon can finally say the thing that matters:

that this is different because it is not her first summer.

That sentence is what unlocks the honeymoon emotionally.

## What This Scene Establishes

1. Lÿsa Mandy is at French Woods for her **second summer**.
2. She is there for **two sessions totaling six weeks**.
3. She loves **ballet and gymnastics equally**.
4. Swimming remains part of her regulation and joy.
5. Fallon can emotionally release because she sees that Lÿsa Mandy is genuinely okay.
