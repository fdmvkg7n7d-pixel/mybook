# SCENE OUTLINE: STANFORD UNIVERSITY VISIT — RETURNING TO THE BAY AREA

## Placement
**When:** Shortly after returning to San Francisco from New York (within the first week home), as part of the transition into the new school year.  
**Where:** Stanford University campus, Stanford, California (approximately one hour south of San Francisco).  
**Lÿsa Mandy’s age:** Eight.  
**Context:** Fallon Glenda and Charlie both attended Stanford University. This visit connects the end of the New York vacation to their shared history and the family’s life in the Bay Area.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene establishes:
- A meaningful link between the family’s past (Fallon and Charlie’s university years) and their present life with Lÿsa Mandy.
- A gentle, educational outing that helps Lÿsa Mandy understand where her parents come from.
- A bridge between the excitement of the summer trip and the grounded rhythm of home and school in the Bay Area.

---

## Emotional Core

The emotional core is **legacy and belonging**.

By visiting Stanford, Fallon and Charlie show Lÿsa Mandy a part of their story that existed before she was old enough to remember. It quietly plants the idea that universities, ambition, and chosen paths are part of the family’s fabric — without pressure. For Lÿsa Mandy, it becomes another layer of understanding who her parents are.

---

## Tone

Warm, reflective, slightly nostalgic, and lightly adventurous. The scene should feel like a natural extension of coming home to the Bay Area.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — The Decision to Visit
A few days after returning from New York, Fallon and Charlie suggest a day trip to Stanford. They frame it casually:

> “We thought it might be nice to show you where Mommy and Daddy went to school.”

Lÿsa Mandy is curious but not overly excited yet. She agrees because it sounds like an adventure with her parents.

### Beat 2 — The Drive South
They drive from San Francisco to Stanford on a clear Bay Area morning. Lÿsa Mandy sits in the back, watching the landscape change from city to the more open, tree-lined roads leading to the campus. Fallon and Charlie point out landmarks along the way.

### Beat 3 — Arrival on Campus
They park and walk onto the Stanford campus. The red-tiled roofs, palm trees, and open quads feel grand but welcoming. Lÿsa Mandy’s eyes widen at the scale of the buildings and the number of students biking and walking between classes.

### Beat 4 — Walking the Main Quad
They stroll through the Main Quad. Fallon and Charlie share small, age-appropriate stories:
- Where they first met on campus.
- Their favorite study spots.
- The time they watched a football game together.

Lÿsa Mandy listens seriously, trying to picture her parents as college students.

### Beat 5 — Visiting a Significant Spot
They stop at a meaningful location:
- The library where Fallon spent many late nights.
- The dining hall where Charlie and Fallon ate together.
- Or a quiet bench under the trees where they once sat and talked about their future.

Fallon and Charlie keep the stories light and affectionate rather than heavy.

### Beat 6 — Lÿsa Mandy’s Reactions
Lÿsa Mandy asks practical questions:
- “Did you live here?”
- “Was it hard?”
- “Did you wear uniforms like Sacred Heart?”

She also notices the ballet studio or arts buildings and connects it silently to her own love of dance.

### Beat 7 — A Small Treat on Campus
They stop for ice cream or a treat at a campus café. Sitting outside, they watch students pass by. Lÿsa Mandy swings her legs and says something observant about how big everything feels.

### Beat 8 — The Drive Home
On the way back to San Francisco, the car is quieter. Lÿsa Mandy looks out the window, processing the day. She may ask if she will go to Stanford one day. Fallon and Charlie answer honestly and without pressure:

> “Maybe. Or maybe you’ll choose somewhere else. What matters is that you get to choose.”

### Beat 9 — Emotional Landing Back Home
That evening, Lÿsa Mandy places a small Stanford postcard or brochure they picked up on her dresser next to her New York souvenirs. The visit becomes another quiet thread in her understanding of her family’s story.

---

## Key Dialogue Possibilities

**Lÿsa Mandy**
> “Did you really go here every day?”
> “Was it scary being so far from home?”
> “Do you think I’ll go to college one day?”

**Fallon Glenda**
> “We were very young when we came here. It felt big and exciting, just like New York felt to you.”
> “We met each other here, and then we met you. So this place is part of our story too.”

**Charlie**
> “Stanford gave us a lot. But the best thing it gave us was each other — and then you.”

---

## What This Scene Establishes

1. Stanford is not just a distant fact — it is a living part of the family’s Bay Area identity.
2. Fallon and Charlie intentionally share their history with Lÿsa Mandy in age-appropriate ways.
3. The visit bridges the summer adventure with the longer arc of education and growth.
4. Lÿsa Mandy begins to see her parents as people with lives before her, which deepens their bond.
5. The scene reinforces the theme that the family values both art (ballet) and ambition (university).
