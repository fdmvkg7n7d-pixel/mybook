# SCENE OUTLINE: LŸSA MANDY'S LETTERS HOME FROM FRENCH WOODS

## Placement

**When:** Across Lÿsa Mandy's second summer at French Woods.

**Where:** French Woods, with the letters received by Fallon and Charlie during or around their Amsterdam honeymoon.

**Lÿsa Mandy's age:** Eight.

---

## Scene Purpose

This outline establishes the emotional function of Lÿsa Mandy's letters home.

They should do more than update logistics. They should:

1. reassure Fallon that Lÿsa Mandy is genuinely settled
2. show Lÿsa Mandy's voice on the page
3. reveal what she notices, values, and repeats
4. mark the six-week camp stay as a real chapter of growth rather than dead time offstage

---

## Emotional Core

The letters are about **connection without collapse**.

Lÿsa Mandy misses home in the ordinary, childlike way that does not undo her camp confidence. Fallon misses her in the ordinary, maternal way that does not invalidate the separation.

The letters allow love to travel without turning into distress.

---

## Tone

Tender, concrete, child-voiced, and lightly funny where possible.

The letters should feel specific rather than polished.

---

## Structure Option

This can be written as either:

- a single scene built around Fallon and Charlie reading multiple letters, or
- several short inserts across the honeymoon and the rest of the six-week camp stretch

---

## Outline

### Beat 1 — The First Letter Arrives
The first letter matters most because it confirms that Lÿsa Mandy actually settled.

The emotional job of the first letter is to reassure.

It should include things like:

- who she has seen again
- what activity she already did
- something matter-of-fact about camp food, cabin life, or swim time
- one line that proves she is not merely enduring camp, but living inside it

### Beat 2 — Fallon Reads for Subtext
Fallon does not only read the letter's content. She reads its emotional temperature.

She is looking for:

- whether Lÿsa Mandy sounds steady
- whether she sounds overbright in a compensating way
- whether she is naming real details
- whether she still sounds like herself

The relief comes when the letter feels recognizably Lÿsa Mandy.

### Beat 3 — Charlie Helps Fallon Receive the Good News
Charlie notices the exact moment Fallon softens.

He may tease gently, point out a detail she missed, or simply sit with her while she reads it twice.

The letters become one of the quiet mechanisms by which the honeymoon remains emotionally possible.

### Beat 4 — The Repeating Interests
Across the letters, certain subjects keep returning:

- ballet
- gymnastics
- swimming

Importantly, the letters should confirm that she continues to love ballet and gymnastics equally. She does not write as if one displaced the other.

### Beat 5 — Child Logic and Texture
The letters should contain the kinds of details a child actually prioritizes:

- whose bunk is near hers
- what her counselor said
- whether the pool felt cold
- something funny or oddly important
- whether she got to do both ballet and gymnastics this week

These details make the letters feel real.

### Beat 6 — Later Letters Show Deeper Ease
By the later letters, the emotional texture should shift from reassurance to ownership.

She is no longer proving she is okay. She is simply reporting her summer life:

- what she is practicing
- who she likes
- what she wants to show her parents later
- what she hopes to do before the six weeks end

### Beat 7 — The Parents' Response
Fallon and Charlie may write back, call, or hold onto favorite lines.

Fallon especially may keep one sentence in her chest — a line that tells her Lÿsa Mandy is not stranded without her, only growing in a place that is hers too.

## Possible Letter Details

Lÿsa Mandy's letters might mention:

- doing **ballet and gymnastics both**
- being excited for **swim time**
- seeing a returning friend
- liking a counselor from last year
- staying for **both sessions**
- a favorite snack, bunk ritual, or routine
- wanting to show Fallon and Charlie something when she gets home

---

## Sample Child-Voice Possibilities

> "I did ballet and gymnastics both so don't think I picked because I didn't."

> "The pool was cold first but then not cold."

> "I saw my friend again and I knew where to go."

> "I am staying all the weeks so you can still go do your trip."

These should feel childlike, direct, and unembarrassed.

---

## What This Establishes

1. Lÿsa Mandy remains emotionally connected to Fallon and Charlie while still thriving apart from them.
2. Her camp life has its own texture and legitimacy.
3. Fallon can receive updates without collapsing back into anxiety.
4. Charlie helps translate reassurance into calm.
5. The six-week second summer becomes part of family canon.
