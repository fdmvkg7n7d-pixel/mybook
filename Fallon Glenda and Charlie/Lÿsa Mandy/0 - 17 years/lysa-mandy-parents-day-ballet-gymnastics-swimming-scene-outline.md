# SCENE OUTLINE: PARENTS DAY — LŸSA MANDY SHOWS FALLON AND CHARLIE BALLET, GYMNASTICS, AND SWIMMING

## Placement

**When:** Parents Day during Lÿsa Mandy's second summer / second season at French Woods.

**Where:** French Woods campus across the activity spaces most important to Lÿsa Mandy.

**Lÿsa Mandy's age:** Eight.

**Continuity:** She is in the middle of a **two-session / six-week** stay, and Fallon Glenda and Charlie are visiting her during that larger summer arc.

---

## Scene Purpose

This is the more specific Parents Day version.

Its job is to show not just that Lÿsa Mandy is doing well at camp, but that she has a real, lived-in relationship to the three activities that define this summer for her:

- **ballet**
- **gymnastics**
- **swimming**

The scene should prove to Fallon and Charlie that Lÿsa Mandy's camp life has texture, competence, and joy.

---

## Emotional Core

The emotional core is **pride through demonstration**.

Lÿsa Mandy is not simply telling her parents that camp matters to her. She is **showing** them.

That matters especially for Fallon, who trusts visible reality more deeply than vague reassurance.

---

## Tone

Bright, proud, affectionate, and specific.

This scene should feel like motion and showing-off in the healthiest sense: a child eager to take her parents through the world she has built.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — Lÿsa Mandy Greets Them with a Plan
When Fallon and Charlie arrive on Parents Day, Lÿsa Mandy is excited to see them — but she also already knows what she wants to show them.

That is important.

She is not only receiving them emotionally. She is guiding them.

### Beat 2 — First Stop: Ballet
Lÿsa Mandy brings them to the ballet space first — or insists it should be first before immediately reconsidering and trying to include everything at once.

What matters here:

- she knows where to go
- she has opinions
- she wants them to see her in relation to the space
- ballet is part of her camp identity, not just one activity on a list

This beat should highlight grace, form, and the ritual side of her summer.

### Beat 3 — Fallon Notices the Specific Confidence
At ballet, Fallon notices not only whether Lÿsa Mandy looks happy, but whether she looks *settled*.

Possible signals:

- she stands in the room like she belongs there
- she knows the vocabulary of what she has been doing
- she is eager to explain, not just perform

For Fallon, these tiny specifics matter more than broad statements.

### Beat 4 — Second Stop: Gymnastics
Lÿsa Mandy is just as invested in showing them gymnastics.

This beat matters because it confirms canonically that she does **not** love ballet more than gymnastics or gymnastics more than ballet.

Gymnastics should bring out:

- strength
- precision
- excitement
- bodily confidence

It gives Charlie a strong visual of how capable she has become inside camp.

### Beat 5 — Charlie Sees the Physical Confidence
Charlie notices something slightly different from Fallon.

Where Fallon is reading emotional safety, Charlie may be struck by:

- how physically sure Lÿsa Mandy looks
- how naturally she moves through the space
- how unembarrassed she is to claim what she likes

This is one of the places where his pride can surface quietly.

### Beat 6 — Lÿsa Mandy Refuses the False Choice
At some point, one parent or another — or perhaps a counselor — lightly asks whether she likes ballet or gymnastics better.

Lÿsa Mandy answers clearly that she likes **both**.

That answer should land as a character truth, not a cute indecision.

### Beat 7 — Third Stop: Swimming
Swimming completes the emotional triangle.

If ballet is grace and gymnastics is strength, swimming is regulation, familiarity, and bodily ease.

This beat should feel different from the first two:

- looser
- calmer
- more instinctive
- less about performance, more about where her body feels at home

### Beat 8 — Parent Recognition Through Contrast
By the time they have seen all three spaces, Fallon and Charlie understand something larger:

French Woods does not only contain Lÿsa Mandy.
It fits her.

That is the real Parents Day revelation.

### Beat 9 — A Counselor Confirms It
A counselor or instructor can lightly confirm what Fallon and Charlie are already seeing.

Possible confirmations:

- that Lÿsa Mandy really has committed to both ballet and gymnastics
- that she looks stronger or more confident in the pool this year
- that second-summer return changed everything for her

The best version is small and specific, not gushy.

### Beat 10 — Emotional Landing
After seeing ballet, gymnastics, and swimming all in one visit, Fallon can finally put language to it:

that Lÿsa Mandy is not splitting herself between different loves.
She is fully herself in all three.

Charlie can answer from the same place, naming that what they are seeing is not just activity participation, but belonging.

## Key Dialogue Possibilities

### Lÿsa Mandy
> "No, you have to see ballet first. Wait. Gymnastics too. No — both."

> "I like them the same."

> "Swimming is different. Swimming just feels good."

### Fallon Glenda
> "She isn't choosing between parts of herself."

> "Everywhere she takes us, she looks like she belongs there."

### Charlie
> "I think she's showing us the map of her whole summer."

> "This place fits her in more than one direction."

---

## What This Scene Establishes

1. Parents Day includes specific, visible proof of Lÿsa Mandy's three-part camp identity.
2. Ballet and gymnastics remain equal loves.
3. Swimming functions as ease, regulation, and physical joy.
4. Fallon and Charlie each see different aspects of the same success.
5. French Woods is now canonically a place where Lÿsa Mandy is legible to herself.
