# SCENE OUTLINE: FALLON GLENDA AND CHARLIE VISIT LŸSA MANDY ON PARENTS DAY AT FRENCH WOODS

## Placement

**When:** Later in Lÿsa Mandy's second summer at French Woods, during her **two-session / six-week** stay.

**Where:** French Woods on **Parents Day**.

**Lÿsa Mandy's age:** Eight.

**Continuity note:** The Amsterdam honeymoon can still happen cleanly within the larger six-week camp structure. This Parents Day visit belongs to the same summer, but not necessarily to the same exact week as the Amsterdam material.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene establishes the emotional payoff of the camp arc.

It should show:

1. that Lÿsa Mandy has genuinely made a temporary life at camp
2. that Fallon and Charlie are now visiting a child who belongs there, not rescuing a child who failed to settle
3. that the separation has strengthened trust rather than strained it
4. that French Woods has become part of family canon rather than a one-off solution

---

## Emotional Core

The emotional core is **reunion without rupture**.

Parents Day should not feel like a recovery mission.
It should feel like the meeting point between two successful realities:

- Lÿsa Mandy's camp life
- Fallon and Charlie's adult life and marriage

They are glad to see one another, but the scene works best if Lÿsa Mandy is not undone by the sight of them. She should be excited, proud, and eager to show them her world.

---

## Tone

Warm, proud, gently emotional, and observational.

The scene should feel fuller and brighter than a goodbye scene.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — Arrival on Parents Day
Fallon and Charlie arrive at French Woods as visitors rather than drop-off parents.

That difference matters. They are no longer managing uncertainty. They are entering a world Lÿsa Mandy has already been inhabiting on her own.

### Beat 2 — Lÿsa Mandy Spots Them
Lÿsa Mandy sees them.

Her reaction should be happy and immediate, but not destabilized. She runs to them or hurries over, thrilled to see them, but with the settled confidence of a child who has already been doing well.

### Beat 3 — Fallon Takes Inventory Instantly
Fallon notices everything at once:

- how Lÿsa Mandy is carrying herself
- whether her body looks loose or tense
- whether she sounds like herself
- whether she is eager to show them things

The relief here is different from the first letter. This time the evidence is physical and undeniable.

### Beat 4 — Lÿsa Mandy Shows Them Her Camp Life
Instead of collapsing back into home-mode immediately, Lÿsa Mandy starts showing them her world.

Possible things she wants to show them:

- where ballet happens
- where gymnastics happens
- the swimming area
- her cabin or bunk area
- the friend or counselor she has been writing about

This beat is crucial. It proves that camp has become legible and meaningful to her.

### Beat 5 — The Friend / Counselor Confirmation
A friend or counselor greets Fallon and Charlie and says something small but anchoring about Lÿsa Mandy.

For example:

- that she has settled beautifully
- that she loves both ballet and gymnastics
- that she is strong in the pool
- that she seems more confident this year because it is her second summer

This external confirmation matters to Fallon.

### Beat 6 — Charlie Watches Fallon Relax in Real Time
Charlie notices the exact moment Fallon believes it all the way down.

Not just because Lÿsa Mandy says she is okay.
Because Fallon can now see that she is okay.

Charlie does not need to explain the moment. He just stays beside Fallon and lets the proof do its work.

### Beat 7 — Lÿsa Mandy as Host, Not Just Child
One of the strongest versions of this scene makes Lÿsa Mandy briefly feel like the host.

She is the one orienting them.
She knows where to go.
She tells them what comes next.
She decides what they have to see.

That role reversal is a quiet sign of growth.

### Beat 8 — The Parents Day Meal / Visit Beat
If the scene includes food, sitting time, or a quieter stretch, this is where Fallon and Charlie can ask small questions and hear camp details in Lÿsa Mandy's own language.

The scene should not become interrogation. The joy is in listening to her talk about things that are now normal to her.

### Beat 9 — The Goodbye at the End of Parents Day
This goodbye is emotionally different from drop-off.

There can still be tenderness, but the point is not grief. The point is continuity.

Lÿsa Mandy is staying.
Fallon and Charlie are leaving again.
And all three of them now know that this is survivable.

### Beat 10 — Emotional Landing
Afterward, Fallon can say some version of the truth:

that the camp is real now in a way even the letters could not fully convey.

Charlie may answer by naming what was visible from the start: that Lÿsa Mandy was never merely getting through camp; she was living inside it.

## Key Dialogue Possibilities

### Lÿsa Mandy
> "Come on, I want to show you where ballet is first."

> "No, wait, gymnastics first. No — both."

> "I know where everything is now."

### Fallon Glenda
> "She's showing us her place."

> "This is different from being okay. She's hers here."

### Charlie
> "She's not visiting camp anymore. She belongs to it a little."

> "I think this is what you needed to see with your own eyes."

## What This Scene Establishes

1. The six-week French Woods stay includes a meaningful Parents Day reunion.
2. Lÿsa Mandy's second summer is fully successful, not merely tolerated.
3. Ballet, gymnastics, and swimming remain central to her camp identity.
4. Fallon gets visual proof of Lÿsa Mandy's independence and settled joy.
5. Charlie continues to function as the steady reader of Fallon and Lÿsa Mandy alike.
