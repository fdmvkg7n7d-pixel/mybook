# SCENE OUTLINE: THE FIRST NURSING — JANUARY AT MCMASTER

## Placement

**When:** Mid-January 2047. Six weeks after Lÿsa Mandy’s birth.

**Where:** McMaster Children’s Hospital, Hamilton, Ontario. Room Four, NICU.

**Continuity Lead-in:**
- Lÿsa Mandy has spent six weeks in the NICU, growing stronger.
- Fallon has been providing milk through an industrial machine for tube feeding.
- The doctors and lactation consultants have finally cleared Lÿsa to attempt direct nursing.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene documents the first time Fallon Glenda successfully nurses Lÿsa Mandy. It marks the transition from mechanical provision (the milking machine) to direct physical connection. It is the realization of the goal Fallon set for herself on December 1st.

---

## Emotional Core

The scene is about **Release and Reclamation**. After weeks of clinical distance, tubes, and machines, the physical act of nursing collapses the space between mother and child. Fallon’s crying is a release of the trauma, the guilt, and the exhaustion of the hospital stay.

---

## Tone

Intimate, quiet, and deeply emotional. The focus is on the sensory details—skin-to-skin contact, the baby’s latch, and the quiet of the room.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — Preparing the Room
Fallon is in the private nursing chair in Room Four. The monitors are still there, but the NG tube has been removed. The nurse is helping Fallon get into a comfortable position. The air is warm and still.

### Beat 2 — The Attempt
Fallon brings Lÿsa Mandy to her breast. There is a moment of uncertainty—Lÿsa is still small, and Fallon is still learning. The "work" of the past six weeks with the industrial machine has ensured a full supply, but this is different.

### Beat 3 — The Latch
Lÿsa Mandy latches correctly. The sound of her rhythmic breathing and the soft, tiny swallows fills the space. For the first time, there is no syringe, no tube, and no machine.

### Beat 4 — The Release
As the nursing continues, Fallon begins to cry. It isn't a sob of pain, but a quiet, overwhelming overflow of emotion. The months of Rio, the hospital stay, the judgmental doctor, and the fear for her daughter’s life all come to a head.

### Beat 5 — Mike’s Witness
Mike is by the door or the window, watching. He sees his thirteen-year-old daughter finally doing what she was desperate to do. He doesn't interrupt. He recognizes that this is the moment Fallon truly becomes "whole" as a mother.

### Beat 6 — The Conclusion
Fallon leans her forehead against Lÿsa Mandy’s. The baby falls asleep while nursing, anchored to her mother. The transition is complete.

### Beat 7 — The Doctors Tell Fallon She Can Take Lÿsa Home in the Morning
After seeing that Lÿsa Mandy has finally nursed well and settled, the doctors tell Fallon that if the baby remains stable overnight, **she can take her home the next morning**.

- The announcement should land with stunned quiet before emotion.
- Fallon has spent weeks living between monitors, tubes, pumping schedules, and visiting hours. Hearing the words *take her home* feels almost unreal.
- The doctors frame it carefully and clinically — not sentimentally, but with unmistakable kindness. Lÿsa Mandy has done the work. Fallon has done the work. The overnight observation is the last step.
- Fallon realizes that the west wing at Carlisle is no longer only a promise or a Christmas present. It is about to become Lÿsa Mandy's actual home.
- Mike, hearing the doctors say it aloud, understands that the long McMaster chapter is finally ending.
