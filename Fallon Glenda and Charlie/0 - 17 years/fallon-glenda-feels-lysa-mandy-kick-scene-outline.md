# Fallon Glenda Feels Lÿsa Mandy Kick — Scene Outline

> **Scope:** A quiet, intensely emotional moment in the Carlisle mansion when twelve-year-old Fallon Glenda feels Lÿsa Mandy kick clearly for the first time. Until now, the pregnancy has mostly been something frightening happening *to* her body — appointments, nausea, exhaustion, fear, and visible change. The kick makes Lÿsa Mandy real in a new way: not as the shadow of the man who hurt Fallon, but as a small living daughter inside her, reaching the world through movement.
>
> **Time:** Late summer 2045, after the family has returned to Carlisle and Fallon is visibly pregnant, but before the strict October bedrest order.
> **Place:** Fallon Glenda's bedroom or the Carlisle sunroom.
> **Characters present:** Fallon Glenda Key (12), Terri Anne Williams-Key, Mike Key. Optional later beat with Sabrina Bryna, Andrea Jennifer, Ashley Erin, or Patti Anne.
> **Mood:** Startled fear → wonder → grief → maternal attachment → family reverence.

---

## 1. The Quiet Before

- **The setting:** It is evening at the Carlisle mansion. The house has settled into one of its rare quiet pockets — after dinner, before bedtime, with the Ontario summer light fading outside the windows.
- **Fallon alone:** Fallon is lying on her bed or curled in the sunroom chair with pillows around her. She has one hand resting on her abdomen because the pregnancy has become physically impossible to forget. She is not doing it sentimentally at first. She is simply aware of the weight, the tightness, the strange unfamiliarity of her changing body.
- **Her emotional state:** She has been trying all day to be calm. Everyone is kind. Everyone is careful. But the changes in her body still frighten her. There are moments when she feels like her body belongs more to doctors and schedules than to her.

---

## 2. The First Clear Kick

- **The movement:** A small, sudden flutter becomes something unmistakable — a real kick from inside her.
- **Fallon's reaction:** She freezes. For one second she is terrified, because something has happened inside her body that she did not control.

  *"Mum?"*

  Her voice is small enough that Terri Anne hears the fear before she hears the word.

- **Terri Anne enters:** Terri Anne comes immediately, not rushing in panic, but with the quick calm of a nurse and a mother.

  *"What is it, sweetheart?"*

- **Fallon:** She keeps her hand pressed to her abdomen, eyes wide.

  *"Something moved."*

- **Terri Anne's face:** Terri Anne understands instantly. Her expression softens, but she is careful not to make the moment too big too fast. She sits beside Fallon.

  *"That was the baby, love."*

- **The second kick:** As if answering, Lÿsa Mandy moves again — stronger this time. Fallon gasps. Not from pain. From shock.

---

## 3. The Emotional Break

- **The realization:** The kick changes the room. Until now, Fallon has known she is pregnant because adults told her, because doctors showed her, because her body changed. But this is Lÿsa Mandy communicating in the only way she can.
- **Fallon's tears:** Fallon starts crying before she knows why. The tears are complicated — fear, grief, wonder, guilt, tenderness, exhaustion, and the first fragile thread of attachment all arriving together.

  *"She's real,"* Fallon whispers.

  The pronoun may surprise even her. *She.*

- **Terri Anne:** Terri Anne does not correct or over-celebrate. She simply lays her hand over Fallon's hand, asking permission with her eyes before touching.

  *"Yes,"* she says softly. *"She is real."*

- **The hug begins:** The realization is too much for Fallon to hold upright. Her face crumples, and she folds toward Terri Anne. Terri Anne immediately gathers her into both arms — not carefully from a distance, but fully, maternally, the way she has held Fallon through every impossible moment since Rio.
- **Fallon's fear, spoken into Terri Anne's shoulder:**

  *"What if I can't love her right?"*

  This is the deeper fear underneath the moment — not whether the baby is alive, but whether Fallon, still a child herself, can be a mother to someone conceived through trauma.

- **Terri Anne's answer, while holding her:**

  *"Love does not have to arrive all at once. Sometimes it comes one breath at a time. One day at a time. One little kick at a time."*

---

## 4. Mike Comes In

- **Mike at the doorway:** Mike hears Fallon crying and appears at the door. He does not enter until Fallon sees him and nods. Even now, even in his own house, he respects that her body and her room are hers.
- **Terri Anne tells him, still holding Fallon:**

  *"She felt the baby kick."*

- **Mike's reaction:** The words hit him hard. He stands still for a moment, visibly moved, because this is one of the first moments where the baby is not only a medical reality or a legal consequence — she is Fallon's daughter.
- **Mike joins the hug:** Fallon reaches for him before she reaches for his hand to feel the baby. Mike crosses the room immediately and sits on her other side, wrapping one arm around Fallon and Terri Anne together. For a moment there is no attempt to make the scene neat or clinical: Mike and Terri Anne are simply hugging their twelve-year-old daughter while she cries, making their bodies into the first shelter around this new, frightening tenderness.
- **Fallon's invitation:** Only after that first wave passes does Fallon look up and reach for Mike's hand.

  *"Dad?"*

  Mike waits. Fallon guides his hand, carefully, to the side of her abdomen where the movement happened. This is completely her choice.
- **The wait:** Nothing happens for a few seconds. Mike does not move. He barely breathes.
- **The kick:** Lÿsa Mandy kicks again, small but undeniable, against Mike's palm.
- **Mike breaks quietly:** Mike's eyes fill. He looks at Fallon first — always Fallon first — and then down at where his hand rests.

  *"Hello, little one,"* he says, voice rough.

- **Fallon's reaction:** Hearing Mike speak to the baby without hesitation, without disgust, without any shadow of the biological father, breaks something open in Fallon. She cries harder, but it is not panic. It is relief.

---

## 5. Naming the Difference

- **Fallon's fear surfaces:**

  *"You don't hate her?"*

  The question devastates the room because it reveals the secret terror she has not known how to say: that the baby might be blamed for where she came from.
- **Mike's answer:** Immediate. Absolute.

  *"No. Never."*

  A beat.

  *"I hate what he did to you. I will never hate your child."*

- **Terri Anne adds:**

  *"She is not what happened to you, Fallon. She is who is with you now."*

- **Mike:**

  *"And she is yours. That means she is ours."*

- **The emotional center:** This is the first time Fallon begins to separate Lÿsa Mandy from the trauma in a bodily way. The kick becomes proof that the baby is not only a consequence. She is a person.

## 8. Closing Image

- **The final posture:** Fallon lies back against the pillows, exhausted from crying. Terri Anne sits beside her. Mike stays close, one hand holding Fallon's, not touching her abdomen now unless she asks.
- **Fallon's hand:** Her own hand remains over the place where Lÿsa Mandy kicked.
- **The final line:** Fallon whispers, almost to herself:

  *"Hi, Lÿsa Mandy."*

- **Closing image:** The Carlisle mansion at dusk. Outside, the grounds are quiet and green. Inside, a twelve-year-old girl, still frightened and still wounded, has felt her daughter move for the first time — and the family around her has made sure the moment belongs not to shame, but to love.

## 9. Character Work

### Fallon Glenda (12)
- **The shift:** The baby moves from concept to presence. This does not erase Fallon's trauma, but it begins the long process of separating Lÿsa Mandy from the violence that caused the pregnancy.
- **Her fear:** *What if I can't love her right?* is the central question. It lets Fallon be both a child and a mother-in-formation.

### Terri Anne Williams-Key
- **The careful mother:** Terri Anne understands the medical meaning and the emotional danger of the moment. She protects Fallon from being rushed into joy, while still allowing wonder to enter.

### Mike Key
- **The claiming grandfather:** Mike's immediate tenderness toward the baby teaches Fallon that Lÿsa Mandy will not be treated as contaminated by the predator's biology. Mike claims her through love before she is even born.
