# FALLON GLENDA, CHARLIE, AND LŸSA MANDY AT HSC FAIR DAY — SCENE OUTLINE

## 1. SCENE CONTEXT & SETTING
- **Timeline:** Fallon Glenda is 17, Charlie is 18. Lÿsa Mandy is four years old. This takes place after Fallon Glenda and Charlie have started dating, settling into a rhythm as a young, devoted chosen family before their eventual move to Stanford.
- **Location:** The expansive campus of **Hillfield Strathallan College (HSC)** in Hamilton, Ontario. It is the **second weekend in May**. The campus is transformed for their annual "Fair Day"—tents, games, bouncy castles, food stalls, and families everywhere.
- **The Core Emotion:** Unapologetic joy and public visibility. After the deep trauma of Rio and the fierce shielding Fallon required during her pregnancy and early motherhood, this scene highlights her newfound confidence. With Charlie by her side and little Lÿsa Mandy holding their hands, Fallon Glenda is stepping out into the wider, bustling community not with shame, but with glowing pride.

## 2. ARRIVAL AT THE FAIR
- **The Walk In:** Fallon Glenda, Charlie, and Lÿsa Mandy arrive at the HSC campus. Lÿsa Mandy is bursting with toddler energy, mesmerized by the colors, the music, and the sheer scale of the event. 
- **The Dynamic:** Charlie naturally takes on a paternal role, holding Lÿsa Mandy's hand on one side while Fallon holds her other hand. Between them, they swing the laughing toddler over the grassy bumps. 
- **Fallon's Outfit & Comfort:** Fallon is dressed beautifully and comfortably in a **pink summer dress**. There is no hiding, no oversized clothing to conceal herself like there was in Rio. She is completely at ease in her own skin, grounded by the safe presence of Charlie and the fierce love for her daughter.

## 3. NAVIGATING THE FAIR
- **The Bouncy Castle:** Lÿsa Mandy spots a bouncy castle and immediately wants to go. The sensory input of the fair is intense but exciting (though they do not yet know she has Asperger Syndrome, they are highly attuned to her needs). Fallon and Charlie carefully monitor her, ensuring she isn't overwhelmed. Charlie patiently waits in line with her, lifting her up to see over the crowd.
- **Winning the Prize:** At one of the classic carnival game booths, Charlie tries to win a prize for Lÿsa Mandy. He's unpretentious and fully committed, laughing off his misses until he finally wins a medium-sized stuffed animal (perhaps a soft plush companion for "my giraffe"). 
- **The Interaction with Others:** As an HSC student, Charlie runs into his friends and teachers. He introduces Fallon Glenda and four-year-old Lÿsa Mandy to them with absolute warmth and pride. When someone politely asks Charlie if Lÿsa Mandy is his little sister, Charlie smiles warmly, gently correcting them, stating how lucky he is to be with Fallon and her daughter. Fallon watches this exchange, moved by how Charlie constantly affirms their family without hesitation or awkwardness.

## 4. THE ALUMNI PHOTOGRAPH & THE BATHROOM BREAK
- **Stepping Inside:** To escape the midday sun, take a brief rest from the crowds, and take Lÿsa Mandy to the bathroom, the trio steps inside one of the main campus buildings. They walk down a long, quiet corridor lined with framed graduating class photographs of HSC alumni spanning decades. 
- **Finding Mike:** Charlie is pointing out his classrooms, but as they walk, Fallon's eyes scan the older photographs on the wall. She stops in her tracks. There, in one of the past graduating class portraits, is her father, **Mike Key**.
- **A Rare Glimpse:** The discovery is profoundly emotional. Mike's personal yearbooks and childhood photographs were lost in a devastating house fire years ago (which occurred while the family was at McMaster Children's Hospital during Jessica Isabella and Rachel Darcy's separation surgery). Because of that fire, Fallon has seen very few images of her father as a teenager.
- **A Full-Circle Moment:** Seeing her father as a young man—standing in the very halls where Charlie now walks—hits Fallon with a wave of deep, grounding emotion. She lifts Lÿsa Mandy up so the articulate four-year-old can point a finger at "Grandpa Mike" on the wall before they continue down the hall to the restrooms. It beautifully cements the spiritual bond between Mike and Charlie: the father who unconditionally protected Fallon, and the young man who stepped up to love her and her daughter with that exact same steadfastness.

## 5. THE LUNCH BREAK
- **Sensory Break:** After walking the halls, they head back outside. The noise of the fair reaches a peak, and Fallon notices Lÿsa Mandy starting to rub her ears—a subtle cue of sensory overload. Without needing a big discussion, Fallon and Charlie seamlessly transition away from the main midway.
- **Lunch at the Tables:** They find an open spot at one of the tables set up for the fair. They sit down to eat their food together—a hotdog for Lÿsa Mandy, and cheeseburgers for the adults.
- **The Generational Echo:** As Charlie sits beside them, gently helping Lÿsa Mandy with her hotdog, she speaks to him in clear, full sentences about the games and the bouncy castle. She is highly articulate for four years old. Fallon watches him listen to her with complete, serious attention. She is struck again by the profound parallel to her father, Mike. She sees Mike’s teachings reflecting in the way Charlie stays present, calm, and utterly devoted. 

## 6. SCENE RESOLUTION
- **The Walk to the Car:** As the afternoon winds down, Lÿsa Mandy is exhausted. Charlie scoops her up, letting her rest her head on his shoulder. She falls asleep almost instantly.
- **The Handhold:** Fallon walks beside Charlie, carrying the stuffed animal he won. She reaches out and intertwines her fingers with his free hand.
- **Final Image:** The three of them walking back to their car across the HSC grounds—a picture of a fiercely chosen, beautifully grounded family, walking in the open daylight, completely unbroken.
