# SCENE OUTLINE: GOODBYE TO PATTI ANNE AND GAMALIEL

**Characters:** Fallon Glenda (17), Charlie (18), Lÿsa Mandy (4), Patti Anne (28), Aleksander, Gamaliel Galvin (7), and Klára (7).
**Setting:** The dining room and later the front steps of Patti Anne's Vaughan mansion.
**Timeframe:** A quiet evening in June, one month before Fallon, Charlie, and Lÿsa Mandy leave for Stanford in July.

## THE CONTEXT
Fallon Glenda (17) and Charlie (18) are preparing to leave the absolute safety of the Key family orbit to drive cross-country to California in July. Because the actual move next month will be chaotic, Fallon and Charlie bring four-year-old Lÿsa Mandy over to Patti Anne's Vaughan mansion for a quiet, deliberate June dinner to say their real goodbyes. 

Patti Anne (28) and Aleksander are currently raising seven-year-old Gamaliel, and fiercely protecting seven-year-old Klára, who has recently moved into their home to escape her abusive father. 

## THE SCENE

**The Dinner Table at Vaughan:**
*   It is a warm June evening. The two families are seated around the large dining table at Patti Anne's Vaughan mansion. 
*   The dinner is warm and grounded. Four-year-old Lÿsa Mandy sits between Fallon and Charlie, entirely focused on aligning her peas into perfect rows on her plate. 
*   Seven-year-old Gamaliel sits quietly across from them. Next to him is seven-year-old Klára. She is safe here, but still deeply traumatized by her father. She is incredibly nervous around older, unfamiliar teenagers—especially a tall, strong eighteen-year-old boy like Charlie.
*   Charlie recognizes this immediately. Throughout dinner, he deliberately keeps his voice low, his movements predictable, and keeps his hands visible above the table so he doesn't startle the little girl in her new home. 

**After Dinner (The Front Steps):**
*   After dinner concludes, the family moves out onto the sprawling front steps of Patti Anne's mansion as Fallon, Charlie, and Lÿsa Mandy prepare to head back to Carlisle. 
*   Charlie stands near their car in the driveway, giving the sisters space. Lÿsa Mandy sits on the bottom step, focused on the rhythmic ticking of Charlie's watch, which he took off and handed to her.

**The Sisterly Goodbye (Patti Anne):**
*   Patti Anne is twenty-eight years old, eleven years older than Fallon. She was already a teenager when Fallon was born, and she was an adult anchor of the family during the years when Fallon's trauma hit. 
*   Patti Anne doesn't give a generic farewell. She steps forward on the steps of her own home and pulls Fallon into a fierce, grounding hug. 
*   *"You don't have to carry any of the ghosts with you,"* Patti Anne tells her quietly, ensuring only Fallon can hear. *"Leave them here. Let Dad and I guard them. You just go build your life."*
*   Fallon nods against her older sister's shoulder, absorbing the matriarchal permission to finally be completely free.

**The Nephew Goodbye (Gamaliel):**
*   Fallon pulls back and looks down at Gamaliel. The dynamic between them is unique—she is his aunt, but he is seven years old, only ten years her junior. 
*   Gamaliel is a quiet, highly observant seven-year-old. He is already showing the early signs of the immense discipline and loyalty he will carry into adulthood. 
*   He steps forward and hugs her waist. *"Are you coming back for Christmas?"* he asks seriously. 
*   *"We'll try, Gamy. If not, you'll have to come visit us in California,"* Fallon promises softly. 

**Klára's Goodbye:**
*   Standing slightly behind Gamaliel on the steps is Klára. Despite Charlie's careful behavior at dinner, she is still physically shrinking back, overwhelmed by the farewells. 
*   Fallon knows that specific fear better than anyone in the world. Instead of forcing Klára to hug her or say a loud goodbye, Fallon simply crouches down so she is smaller, giving Klára space. She offers the seven-year-old a soft, understanding smile, silently communicating that she is safe here. 

**Saying Goodbye to Lÿsa Mandy:**
*   Gamaliel walks down to the bottom step where Lÿsa Mandy is sitting. Even at seven, he doesn't coddle the four-year-old or use a high-pitched voice—he intuitively knows Lÿsa Mandy responds best to calm, steady patterns. He simply taps the toe of her shoe twice, a small, familiar rhythm between them. *"See you, Lÿsa."* 
*   *"Bye, Gamy,"* Lÿsa Mandy replies, focused on the ticking of the watch. 
*   Klára stays safely near Patti Anne's side, watching the quiet, respectful way the Key family treats each other. 

**The Departure:**
*   Charlie opens the back door of the car. He shakes seven-year-old Gamaliel’s hand like a man, which Gamaliel deeply respects, and gives Patti Anne a warm hug. He offers Klára a gentle wave from a safe distance.
*   Fallon buckles Lÿsa Mandy into her car seat and gets into the passenger side. 
*   As Charlie puts the car in gear and drives away from Patti Anne's mansion into the evening, Fallon looks back in the side mirror. 
*   She sees Patti Anne, Gamaliel, and Klára standing together on the steps of the Vaughan estate. She sees her older sister actively protecting a new little girl who needs a fortress, realizing with profound peace that when July arrives, she is finally ready to leave her own fortress behind to build a family of her own.

## WHY THIS SCENE MATTERS
*   **The Shared Trauma:** Having Fallon recognize Klára's fear of Charlie, and having Charlie deliberately moderate his body language to keep Klára safe, beautifully connects Fallon's past trauma with Klára's current reality. 
*   **Patti Anne's Role:** It reinforces Patti Anne as the secondary matriarch/anchor who explicitly gives Fallon permission to leave her trauma behind, while showing Patti Anne already taking on the role of protector for the next generation. 
*   **Generational Loyalty:** Seven-year-old Gamaliel's interaction with Lÿsa Mandy shows how deeply the next generation of the family naturally accommodates neurodivergence from a young age.
