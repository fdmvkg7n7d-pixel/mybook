# SCENE OUTLINE: FALLON GLENDA AND LÿSA MANDY ATTEND CHARLIE'S GRADUATION FROM HILLFIELD STRATHALLAN COLLEGE

**Characters:** Charlie (18), Fallon Glenda (17), Lÿsa Mandy (4–5), Mike Key, Terri Anne Williams-Key, Charlie’s parents (optional, names TBD), other Hillfield Strathallan students and families.
**Setting:** The grounds and ceremony space of **Hillfield Strathallan College** in Hamilton, Ontario — the outdoor graduation ceremony on the lawn, followed by the reception under tents.
**Timeframe:** The **second Saturday in June**, weeks before the Stanford move.

## THE CONTEXT
Charlie is graduating from Hillfield Strathallan College, the private school in Hamilton where he has completed his secondary education. He is eighteen, his February birthday already passed, and he is weeks away from driving west to Stanford with Fallon and Lÿsa Mandy. Fallon Glenda (17) and Lÿsa Mandy (4–5) attend the ceremony as his family. For Fallon, this is a public declaration of their future. For Lÿsa Mandy, it is a long, crowded, sensory event she must navigate with her blocks and her routines. For Charlie, it is the moment he walks across a stage knowing that the two people waiting for him are the family he has chosen.

## THE SCENE

**Arrival at Hillfield Strathallan:**
*   The morning is bright and warm. Cars line the driveway and the lawn in front of the school is already dotted with folding chairs and families in summer dresses and blazers. Fallon has dressed carefully — an ivory blouse, a pink skirt, white tights. She looks young and deliberate, like someone who knows she is about to be watched.
*   Mike Key and Terri Anne Williams-Key have come too, but they hang back slightly. They know this day belongs to Fallon and Charlie as much as it belongs to Charlie’s parents. They give Fallon and Lÿsa Mandy the front seats in their section.
*   Lÿsa Mandy walks between Fallon and Charlie, holding one hand of each. She is wearing a small blue dress and has brought her canvas tote of building blocks. She will not sit through the ceremony without them. Charlie has also given her his watch to hold — the one with the steady, audible ticking she finds grounding. She turns it over in her free hand as they walk, counting the rhythm against her palm. Fallon has also packed a small notebook, a juice box, and a pair of noise-reducing headphones in case the crowd becomes too much.

**Finding Their Seats:**
*   The ceremony is outdoors on the school lawn. Rows of white folding chairs face a temporary stage decorated with the school’s colours. Fallon chooses seats near the end of a row, close to an aisle, so she can take Lÿsa Mandy out quickly if she needs to.
*   Lÿsa Mandy sits on the chair for approximately three seconds before deciding the folding chair is wrong. She climbs onto Fallon’s lap and begins unpacking her blocks onto the seat beside them. Fallon lets her. The woman in the next row gives them a look. Fallon meets it calmly and then looks away. She has stopped apologizing for Lÿsa’s needs in public.
*   Mike and Terri Anne sit behind them. Mike leans forward and whispers, *“You’re doing fine.”* Fallon nods, but she does not relax. She is aware that she is the youngest mother in this crowd, and that people will assume Lÿsa is her sister.

**The Ceremony:**
*   The graduates process in. Charlie is among them, wearing the school cap and gown. He is taller than several of the boys around him. He scans the crowd as he walks and finds Fallon almost immediately. He smiles — not the polite smile for the crowd, but the real one he saves for her. Fallon smiles back, feeling the heat rise in her cheeks.
*   Lÿsa Mandy looks up from her blocks. She sees Charlie and says, clearly and loudly, *“Charlie!”* Several people turn. Fallon gently puts her hand on Lÿsa’s shoulder. *“Yes, that’s Charlie. We’re going to watch him walk across the stage.”* Lÿsa Mandy nods and returns to her blocks, but she keeps one eye on the stage.
*   The speeches begin. Lÿsa Mandy tolerates them for several minutes, then begins to fidget. Fallon puts the headphones on her and gives her a juice box. Lÿsa Mandy drinks it slowly, holds Charlie’s watch against her ear, and builds a small wall of blocks on the chair. The ticking of the watch travels through the headphones and the rhythm of the blocks. It is not disruptive. It is her way of being present.

**Charlie’s Name:**
*   When Charlie’s name is called, Fallon straightens. Lÿsa Mandy looks up from her blocks. Mike Key applauds loudly, and Terri Anne takes a photograph.
*   Charlie walks across the stage to receive his diploma. He shakes the headmaster’s hand. He turns to face the crowd. For a fraction of a second, his eyes find Fallon and Lÿsa Mandy. He does not wave — it is too formal a moment — but he inclines his head slightly, a private acknowledgment that belongs only to them.
*   Lÿsa Mandy stands up on the chair and waves both arms. *“Charlie! Charlie got a paper!”* Fallon laughs, the woman in the next row smiles despite herself, and Charlie’s face breaks into a grin as he walks off the stage.

**The Reception:**
*   After the ceremony, the families gather under tents for refreshments. Charlie finds Fallon and Lÿsa Mandy before he goes to his own parents. Lÿsa Mandy is still holding his watch. He kneels down so he is eye-level with her and gently takes the watch back, slipping it onto his own wrist. Lÿsa Mandy watches the movement of his hands, satisfied that the ticking is where it belongs.
*   *“Did you see me?”* he asks. Lÿsa Mandy nods. *“You got a paper,”* she says. *“I did,”* Charlie says. *“And then I’m going to get more papers at Stanford. But first we have a big trip.”*
*   Fallon hands him a small gift she has been holding — a leather bookmark engraved with a compass rose and the words *“Go forward.”* Charlie turns it over in his hands. *“I thought I was the one graduating,”* he says. Fallon shrugs. *“You are. This is just so you don’t forget where you’re going.”*
*   Charlie tucks the bookmark into his gown pocket. He stands up and kisses her forehead, right there in the tent, in front of his classmates and their parents. Fallon does not pull away. She is not hiding anymore.

**Mike and Terri Anne’s Moment:**
*   Mike Key approaches Charlie with Terri Anne. Mike shakes his hand like a man, then pulls him into a brief, firm hug. *“You did well,”* Mike says. *“And more importantly, you know what you are walking toward.”* He glances at Fallon and Lÿsa Mandy. Charlie nods.
*   Terri Anne hugs Charlie and whispers something in his ear. Whatever she says makes Charlie’s eyes soften. He nods again. When she pulls back, she touches Fallon’s cheek and says, *“You picked a good one.”* Fallon nods, because she knows it is true and because she is still too overwhelmed to speak.

**Lÿsa Mandy’s Goodbye to the School:**
*   Lÿsa Mandy has finished her juice box and is building a small tower of blocks on the grass under one of the tents. Charlie kneels beside her and builds the next level for her, placing the block exactly where she indicates.
*   *“We’re going to California soon,”* he says. *“You, me, and your mom.”* Lÿsa Mandy looks at him. *“And the blocks?"* she asks. *“And the blocks,”* Charlie confirms. *“We will bring the blocks.”*
*   Lÿsa Mandy nods and puts the last block on top of the tower. It stands for two seconds and then falls. She does not get upset. She simply starts again. Charlie helps her.

**The Departure:**
*   The afternoon light begins to soften. Charlie walks Fallon and Lÿsa Mandy to Mike’s car. He will stay for the rest of the reception with his parents, but he wants this last moment with them first.
*   Fallon buckles Lÿsa Mandy into her car seat. Charlie stands at the open door. Lÿsa Mandy holds up one of her blocks to him. *“For Stanford,”* she says. Charlie takes it — a small green block — and puts it in his pocket with the bookmark.
*   Fallon stands up and faces him. She signs slowly — a sign she has been practicing with Sabrina — *“Good luck.”* Then she adds, *“I’m proud of you.”*
*   Charlie kisses her, brief and soft, in the parking lot of his school, with his cap still on and her parents in the front seat. When he pulls back, he says, *“Next graduation is yours. And then we’re going to have a lot of ceremonies in California.”*
*   Fallon gets into the car. As Mike pulls away, she looks back at Charlie standing on the lawn in his graduation gown, holding a green block in his hand. She understands, suddenly, that this is what a chosen family looks like: a boy on a lawn with a child’s block in his pocket, waving goodbye to the daughter he has already decided is his.

## WHY THIS SCENE MATTERS

*   **Public Declaration:** Charlie’s graduation is the first public, formal event where Fallon and Lÿsa Mandy are present as his family. It marks the moment they stop being a secret or a curiosity and become a unit.
*   **Lÿsa Mandy’s Inclusion:** Lÿsa is not expected to behave like a typical four-year-old at a ceremony. She has her blocks, her headphones, her juice box. The scene shows Fallon and Charlie meeting her needs without shame.
*   **Mike and Terri Anne’s Approval:** Mike’s handshake and Terri Anne’s whispered words confirm that Charlie has been accepted by Fallon’s parents not just as a boyfriend, but as the future father of their granddaughter.
*   **The Bookmark and the Block:** The engraved bookmark from Fallon and the green block from Lÿsa become Charlie’s talismans for the next stage. He carries them to California in his pocket.
*   **Fallon’s Growth:** She no longer hides in oversized clothes or apologizes for her presence. She sits in the front row of her partner’s graduation with their daughter on her lap and meets the world’s eyes.
