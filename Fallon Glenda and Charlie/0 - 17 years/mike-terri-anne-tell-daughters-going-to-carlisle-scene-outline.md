# Mike and Terri Anne Tell the Daughters They Are Going to Carlisle — Scene Outline

> **Scope:** A few days after the disclosure, the doctor's appointment, and the police statement. Mike and Terri Anne gather the daughters who are home in Rio and tell them the family is returning to Carlisle — because of Fallon Glenda's health. They do not tell the full picture yet. They tell enough. Fallon is not in the room when it begins. She comes in before it ends.
>
> **Time:** ~May 5–6, 2045. A few days after May 2. Fallon Glenda is twelve. The twins — Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige — are five months old.
> **Place:** The Key family's living room or main gathering room in the Rio de Janeiro residence. Evening, after dinner — the time Mike and Terri Anne have always chosen for the conversations that require everyone present.
> **Characters present:** Mike Key, Terri Anne Williams-Key, Kiley Rose (16), Sabrina Bryna (14), Andrea Jennifer (10), Ashley Erin (8). Fallon Glenda (12) enters partway through. Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige are present — in a bassinet or held.
> **Mood:** The particular weight of a family gathering that the children know, before a word is said, is not ordinary.

---

## 1. The Days Between

- **The days since May 2:** The household has been quiet in a way it is not usually quiet. Not unhappy — just careful. Mike and Terri Anne have moved through the Rio house with the particular deliberateness of parents who are managing something large and not yet ready to name it to everyone. They have been present — meals made, routines kept, the younger girls' days held in their usual shape — but there is a quality to their attention that the children feel without being able to articulate.
- **Fallon:** She has slept. Long, deep, exhausted sleeps — the body recovering from a night of no sleep and a day of three impossible things. She has been quieter than usual, still wearing her oversized clothes, still keeping close to the house. But she is eating. She is present. She comes to the table. She holds Pandora Paige when Kiley Rose offers her. These are small things. They matter.
- **Kiley Rose:** She has not asked. She has watched Fallon carefully — the way Kiley Rose watches things she is not yet sure how to approach — and she has kept the twins fed and the younger girls settled and she has not pushed. She knows something happened. She does not yet know what. She is sixteen and she has learned, from her own night on the patio, that some things come when they are ready.
- **The younger girls:** Sabrina Bryna (14) has noticed the change in Fallon and the weight in her parents and has said nothing, watching. Andrea Jennifer (10) knows something is different but cannot name it. Ashley Erin (8) moves through the household with the particular obliviousness of an eight-year-old who is protected, by age, from the full weight of adult weather.
- **The gathering:** Mike tells them after dinner — *come and sit down, all of you* — in the tone that is not a request. They know this tone. They have heard it before — when Jessica Isabella was burned, when Sabrina Bryna's diagnosis was explained, when the family has needed to sit together and be told a truth. They come and they sit.

---

## 2. The Room Before Fallon Arrives

- **The arrangement:** The girls settle — Kiley Rose with Pandora Paige in her arms, Brandi Jessica in the bassinet nearby. Sabrina Bryna on the sofa. Andrea Jennifer and Ashley Erin close together, Ashley Erin's shoulder against Andrea Jennifer's arm the way younger children press toward older ones when something feels serious. Mike and Terri Anne stand, or sit facing them. The room arranges itself the way the Key family always arranges itself for hard things: everyone included, no one at a distance, no one told to go to their room.
- **Where Fallon is:** She is in her bedroom. She knows the gathering is happening. Mike knocked on her door before calling the others and told her quietly: *"We're going to talk to your sisters. You don't have to be there. But you can come in whenever you want. It's your choice."* Fallon nodded. She stayed in her room. For now.
- **Mike opens:** He does not ease into it. The Key family rule — no child is excluded from the truth just because telling it requires care — means he speaks plainly, but plainly does not mean completely. There are things Fallon has the right to tell in her own time, in her own way. Tonight is not that night. Tonight is what needs to be said to move the family forward.

  *"I need to tell you all something. We are leaving Rio. We're going home to Carlisle — all of us, soon. Within the next week or two."*

  A beat. He lets that land.

  *"The reason we are going is Fallon's health. Fallon is not well. She needs care that we can manage better at home, with her own doctors, with her own support around her. That is what matters right now."*

---

## 3. The Girls React

- **Kiley Rose:** She is the first to understand that *not well* is covering something larger. She looks at her father's face and reads it the way she has always read his face — carefully, completely. She does not ask what is wrong with Fallon. She asks:

  *"Is she going to be okay?"*

  Mike:

  *"She is going to be okay. Yes. But she needs us around her, and she needs to be home."*

  Kiley Rose nods. She holds Pandora Paige a little more closely. She does not push further. She understands — from her own night, from the way her parents held what she said — that there are things that belong to Fallon and are not hers to pull out of the room.

- **Sabrina Bryna:** Mike signs to her simultaneously as he speaks — the same fluency he has always given her, the same insistence that she is inside every conversation. Her face is still and reading. She signs back: *"How sick?"* Mike signs: *"She is going to be okay. We are taking care of it."* Sabrina Bryna looks at him for a long moment. Then she nods — a slow, serious nod that says she is filing this carefully and will watch.

- **Andrea Jennifer (10):** She is old enough to hear *Fallon's health* and feel the specific fear that comes when a sibling is unwell.

  *"What's wrong with her?"*

  Terri Anne, gently:

  *"That's something Fallon will be able to share with you herself, when she is ready. Right now what we need you to know is that she needs us — all of us — and we are going home to give her that."*

  Andrea Jennifer accepts this. She is the observer — she stores it, she does not push.

- **Ashley Erin (8):** She looks at Terri Anne with the directness of an eight-year-old:

  *"Is Fallon going to die?"*

  The room stills. Terri Anne does not flinch.

  *"No, sweetheart. Fallon is not going to die."*

  Ashley Erin:

  *"Promise?"*

  Terri Anne:

  *"I promise."*

  Ashley Erin nods, satisfied in the way only an eight-year-old can be satisfied by a promise, and leans back against Andrea Jennifer.

---

## 4. Fallon Comes In

- **The moment:** The door to the hallway opens quietly. Fallon stands in the doorway in her oversized clothes, her hair loose, her face still carrying the particular tiredness of the past few days. She has been listening from the hallway — not intentionally at first, and then intentionally, standing with her back against the wall and hearing her family talk about her health and her coming home.
- **The room sees her:** Kiley Rose first — she always sees things first. Then the others. Nobody speaks. Nobody tells her to come in or stay back. They wait.
- **Fallon comes in.** She crosses the room and sits down on the sofa beside Sabrina Bryna. Sabrina Bryna does not say anything. She moves slightly to make room and then, without announcement, puts her arm around Fallon's shoulders. Fallon stiffens for a fraction of a second — and then leans into it.
- **The room, now complete:** All five sisters present. Mike and Terri Anne. The twins. The whole household, gathered.
- **Mike, to Fallon:** He looks at her — just at her, directly.

  *"You don't have to say anything."*

  Fallon shakes her head slightly. Then:

  *"I know you're leaving because of me."*

  She says it to the room. Not ashamed — or trying not to be. Just naming it.

  *"I'm sorry."*

- **The room answers her:** Not Mike. Not Terri Anne. Kiley Rose:

  *"Don't."*

  Quiet. Firm. Not unkind — the voice of a sixteen-year-old who has spent her own sleepless night and her own day of impossible things and who knows, from the inside, what it feels like to apologise for something that is not your fault.

  *"Don't apologise for that."*

  Fallon looks at her. Kiley Rose looks back. Something passes between them — sister to sister, the particular understanding of two people who have each, in the past week, been held by the same parents in the same kind of darkness. Fallon does not know exactly what Kiley Rose's night was. Kiley Rose does not know exactly what Fallon's was. They do not need to.

- **Ashley Erin:** She gets up from beside Andrea Jennifer, crosses the room, and sits down on Fallon's other side. She does not say anything. She just sits there — eight years old, small, entirely certain that this is where she should be. Fallon looks at her. Something in her face softens almost imperceptibly.

---

## 5. What Mike Tells Them About Carlisle

- **The practicalities:** Mike lays it out clearly — the Key family does not hide logistics any more than it hides truth.
  - They will leave Rio within the week or two. The house will be arranged, belongings packed, travel organised.
  - They are going to the Carlisle mansion — home. Fallon's doctors in Ontario will be brought in immediately.
  - The girls' homeschooling continues without interruption — Mike and Terri Anne will ensure that. Leaving Rio does not mean falling behind.
  - Rio is not gone forever. The family may return. But right now, Carlisle is where they need to be.
- **What he does not say:** He does not explain what happened. He does not name the swimming teacher. He does not say the word *pregnant.* Those are Fallon's words to say, in Fallon's time, to her sisters. Tonight is about the move. Tonight is about closing around her.
- **Terri Anne:** She adds one thing — quietly, looking at all five sisters:

  *"What Fallon needs from each of you right now is exactly what you are already doing. Being here. Being her sisters. Nothing more complicated than that."*

---

## 6. After — The Room Settling

- **The gathering loosens:** Not abruptly — the Key family does not end these moments with a clap of the hands and *right, off to bed.* They stay in the room together for a while. Someone gets a glass of water. Ashley Erin stays pressed against Fallon's side. Sabrina Bryna's arm stays around her shoulders.
- **Kiley Rose and Mike:** At some point Kiley Rose passes Pandora Paige to Terri Anne and catches Mike's eye across the room. She gives him a look that is not quite a question — more an acknowledgment. *I understand more than you said. I trust you with the rest.* Mike holds her gaze for a moment. Then he nods. Once. It is enough.
- **Fallon:** She does not cry in this room. She has cried everything out. She sits between Sabrina Bryna and Ashley Erin and she is very quiet and she is not alone and that is what this room was for.
- **Andrea Jennifer:** She moves to sit on the floor near Fallon's feet — not touching, just near. She picks up a book and opens it but does not read it. She is just there. Present. The observer, choosing proximity over distance.
- **Brandi Jessica:** She makes a small sound from the bassinet. Fallon hears it — she always hears the twins now, the way people hear things they have been listening for. She looks at the bassinet. Then she looks at Kiley Rose.

  *"Can I hold her?"*

  Kiley Rose:

  *"Of course."*

  Fallon gets up and lifts Brandi Jessica carefully — with the particular care she has always had with the babies, which is the care of a girl who knows what it means to carry something fragile. She sits back down between her sisters with Brandi Jessica against her chest. The baby settles immediately.

- **Closing image:** The Key family in the Rio living room. Mike standing near the window, the city behind him. Terri Anne with Pandora Paige. Fallon on the sofa with Brandi Jessica, Sabrina Bryna's arm still around her, Ashley Erin still pressed to her side, Andrea Jennifer on the floor nearby. Kiley Rose across the room, watching. The Rio night outside — alive and indifferent and already almost behind them. They are going home.

## 7. Character Work

### Mike Key
- **What he tells and what he doesn't:** He tells them enough — Fallon's health, Carlisle, we are going home. He keeps what belongs to Fallon for Fallon. This is the Key family rule of truth applied with precision: radical honesty does not mean removing Fallon's agency over her own story.
- **The nod to Kiley Rose:** Across the room, at the end. He sees that she understands more than was said. He trusts her with that. She trusts him with the rest.

### Terri Anne Williams-Key
- **Her one addition:** *"What Fallon needs from each of you right now is exactly what you are already doing."* She names it so they know they are already doing the right thing. She does not give them tasks. She confirms their instincts.
- **The promise to Ashley Erin:** *"I promise."* She makes it without hesitation because it is true and because Ashley Erin is eight and needs to hear it said plainly.

### Kiley Rose (16)
- **"Don't."** The most important word she says in this scene. She does not let Fallon apologise. She does not know exactly what Fallon has been through — but she knows enough, from her own week, to recognise the shape of someone apologising for something that is not their fault. She stops it immediately.
- **Passing Pandora Paige:** She gives up her anchor — the baby she has been holding through the whole conversation — so she can be more fully present in the room. That is its own kind of statement.

### Sabrina Bryna (14)
- **The arm:** She moves to make room for Fallon the moment she comes in, and then she puts her arm around her. No words. Just the arm. This is everything.
- **Her question to Mike:** *"How sick?"* Signed, not spoken. Direct. She needs the truth in her own language and she asks for it.

### Andrea Jennifer (10)
- **The floor:** She moves to sit on the floor near Fallon's feet. Not touching. Just near. She opens a book she doesn't read. This is her mode of love — proximity, quiet, no demands. She is already the writer. She is already learning that sometimes you show up and you stay and that is the whole offering.

### Ashley Erin (8)
- **The question and the promise:** *"Is Fallon going to die?"* — the most direct question in the room, asked by the person least equipped to manage ambiguity. Terri Anne answers it directly. Ashley Erin accepts the promise and moves across the room to sit beside Fallon. That is her whole arc in this scene: she asks the question, she receives the answer, and then she goes and sits with her sister.

### Fallon Glenda (12)
- **Coming in:** She chose to come in. She was given the choice — *you don't have to* — and she came. That matters.
- **"I know you're leaving because of me. I'm sorry."** She names it herself. She is twelve and she is trying not to let guilt take over, and it is hard, and Kiley Rose stops it before it gets any further.
- **Holding Brandi Jessica:** At the end of the scene, she asks to hold the baby. Not Pandora Paige — Brandi Jessica. The one whose name was built from gratitude. Fallon holds her and the baby settles and something in the room settles with it.

## 8. Thematic Motifs

- **The gathering:** Mike and Terri Anne have always gathered their children for the hard things. Jessica Isabella's burns. Sabrina Bryna's diagnosis. Now this. The ritual of *come and sit down* is the family's way of saying: *you are all inside this, none of you are left out, we face it together.*
- **Fallon's apology stopped:** Kiley Rose stopping Fallon's apology is the scene's emotional pivot. It is also a quiet echo — Kiley Rose knows what it is to apologise for something that is not your fault. She learned that in Jessica Isabella's bedroom, at eleven, touching the scars. She is not going to let Fallon do what she did.
- **The arm and the pressing-in:** Sabrina Bryna's arm. Ashley Erin pressed against Fallon's side. Andrea Jennifer on the floor nearby. The sisters do not need to be told what to do. They already know. They close in.
- **Brandi Jessica:** Fallon asking to hold the baby at the end. The baby settling immediately. Something about a five-month-old who does not know anything except warmth and heartbeat — who does not know about swimming teachers or police stations or Carlisle or any of it — is the most restoring thing in the room.
- **Going home:** Rio has been the place where the worst thing happened. Carlisle is home. The family is moving toward home. That is not nothing.
