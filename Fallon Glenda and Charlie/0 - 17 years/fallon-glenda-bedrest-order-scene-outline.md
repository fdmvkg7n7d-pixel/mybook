# Fallon Glenda on Bedrest — Scene Outline

> **Scope:** October 2045. Fallon Glenda is entering the third trimester of her pregnancy. Because she is only twelve years old, her body is under immense strain. Dr. Aris orders her on strict bedrest for the final two months to ensure both her safety and the baby's. The scene focuses on the psychological toll of being confined to her bed, and how the family rallies around her to ensure the bedrest feels like a cocoon rather than a prison.
>
> **Time:** October 2045. Two months before her December 1st due date.
> **Place:** Fallon Glenda's bedroom in the Carlisle mansion.
> **Characters present:** Fallon Glenda (12), Mike Key, Terri Anne Williams-Key, Dr. Aris, Sabrina Bryna (14), Andrea Jennifer (10), Ashley Erin (8).
> **Mood:** Clinically serious but deeply swaddled in family love.

---

## 1. The Bi-Weekly Appointment

- **The secure entrance:** As always, the bi-weekly appointment at McMaster Hospital is handled via the invisible corridor. However, this time, the examination takes longer than usual.
- **The medical reality:** Dr. Aris finishes her examination and speaks directly to Fallon, with Mike and Terri Anne flanking her. Dr. Aris explains that because Fallon’s body is still developing, the physical demands of the final trimester are becoming too dangerous. To prevent premature labor and protect Fallon’s health, she must go on strict bedrest for the remaining two months.
- **The rule:** Fallon is allowed to get up to use the bathroom, but otherwise, she must remain horizontal in bed. No stairs, no walking around the estate, no visiting the stables.
- **Mike’s nod:** Mike doesn’t argue or ask for a second opinion. He immediately nods, shifting his logistical brain to how he will convert the Carlisle mansion around this new restriction.

## 2. The Return to Carlisle

- **The psychological hit:** On the drive home, Fallon is quiet. The exhaustion of the pregnancy is already heavy, but the idea of being trapped in her room for sixty days feels terrifying. It reminds her too much of hiding in her room in Rio.
- **The elevator:** When they arrive at the Carlisle mansion, Mike does not make Fallon walk. The mansion is equipped with an elevator, and Mike uses it to bring her smoothly up to her bedroom floor. It is a physical manifestation of his promise to remove every possible strain from her body.

## 3. Transforming the Room

- **Not a prison:** Mike and Terri Anne immediately set to work ensuring that her bedroom does not feel like a sickroom or a cage. 
- **The setup:** Mike brings in a mini-fridge, stacks of books, a specialized mattress, and extra pillows. Terri Anne sets up a comfortable chair right beside the bed, establishing that Fallon will rarely be left alone.
- **The sibling mandate:** Mike gathers Sabrina Bryna, Andrea Jennifer, and Ashley Erin in the hallway. He issues a new mandate: *Fallon cannot come downstairs to the family, so the family is moving upstairs to Fallon.*

## 4. The First Evening on Bedrest

- **The new normal:** That evening, the younger sisters burst into Fallon's room. They don't tiptoe. 
- **The sleepover:** Ashley Erin drags her blankets in and makes a pallet on the floor next to Fallon's bed, declaring she is sleeping there tonight. Andrea Jennifer brings her homework and sets up at Fallon's desk. Sabrina Bryna sits at the foot of Fallon's bed, reading a book.
- **The physical touch:** Terri Anne brings up a tray of dinner for all of them. While they eat, Ashley Erin rests her hand on Fallon's heavily pregnant stomach, perfectly unbothered by the medical severity of the situation. 
- **The exhale:** Fallon watches her sisters turn her room into a chaotic, messy, loud sleepover. The panic of being isolated dissolves. She is trapped in bed, but she is trapped with her family.

---

## 5. Character Work

### Fallon Glenda (12)
- **The Fear of Isolation:** Bedrest triggers her memories of isolating herself in Rio out of shame. The family's immediate, loud response is necessary to prove she isn't being quarantined; she is being protected.

### Dr. Aris
- **The Direct Communicator:** She does not soften the medical reality. She tells Fallon exactly what is required and why, treating her with the clinical respect she deserves.

### Mike Key
- **The Physical Anchor:** Using the elevator to bring her upstairs is a literal manifestation of his promise to handle the logistics so she doesn't have to bear the strain. He immediately redesigns the household to center around her bedroom.

### The Younger Sisters
- **The Antidote to Isolation:** By moving their lives into her bedroom, the younger sisters ensure the bedrest is just a change of location, not a change in family dynamics.

---

## 6. Thematic Motifs

- **The Cocoon:** The bedrest order forces the ultimate narrowing of Fallon's physical world. The mansion was the first cocoon; her bedroom is now the innermost layer.
- **Carrying the Weight:** Mike using the Carlisle elevator to bypass the stairs physically represents the Key family ethos of stepping in to carry the burdens their children cannot.
- **Chaos as a Shield:** The younger sisters turning the strict medical quarantine into a loud sleepover strips the situation of its tragedy, replacing it with ordinary love.
