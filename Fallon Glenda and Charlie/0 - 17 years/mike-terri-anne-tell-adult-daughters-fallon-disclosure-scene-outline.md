# Mike and Terri Anne Tell the Adult Daughters — Fallon Glenda's Disclosure — Scene Outline

> **Scope:** The morning of May 3, 2045. The day after the disclosure, the doctor's appointment, and the police statement. Mike and Terri Anne make three calls — to Patti Anne in Vaughan, to Jessica Isabella and Emma together in Halifax, and to Rachel Darcy in Charlottesville. They tell each of them everything. These are adults. They deserve the full truth. Fallon has given her parents permission to tell her older sisters. She does not want them to hear a partial version.
>
> **Time:** May 3, 2045. Morning in Rio — which means it is early morning in Ontario and Virginia, and still the middle of the night briefly before they wait for a decent hour.
> **Place:** Mike and Terri Anne's bedroom in the Rio de Janeiro residence. The same room where Fallon stood at the door yesterday morning.
> **Characters present (in Rio):** Mike Key, Terri Anne Williams-Key.
> **Characters reached:** Patti Anne Key (22, Vaughan); Jessica Isabella Key (20) and Emma Fritz (20, Halifax); Rachel Darcy Key (20, Charlottesville).
> **Mood:** The steadiness required of parents who have to say the worst thing twice more, and then once more after that.

---

## 1. Before the Calls — The Decision

- **Fallon's permission:** Before Mike and Terri Anne make a single call, they ask Fallon. They go to her room in the morning — she is awake, she has slept but not enough — and Mike sits on the edge of her bed and tells her that her older sisters deserve to know the full truth, and that he and Terri Anne would like to tell them today. Not the younger girls yet — that is still Fallon's to give when she is ready. But Patti Anne, Jessica Isabella, Rachel Darcy, and Emma are adults. They will want to know. They will want to be there for her.

  Fallon is quiet for a moment.

  *"Will they be angry?"*

  Mike:

  *"At you? No. Never."*

  Another pause.

  *"Okay,"* she says. *"Tell them."*

- **The order:** Mike and Terri Anne decide together — Rachel Darcy first, because she is alone in Charlottesville and they do not want her to hear it from anyone else first. Then Jessica Isabella and Emma together in Halifax — they are a unit and the news should reach them as one. Then Patti Anne, who is the eldest, who will need to know so she can begin preparing for Fallon's arrival in Ontario.
- **The room:** They sit together in their bedroom — the same room where Fallon knocked on the door yesterday morning. Mike's phone. Terri Anne beside him. They make the calls together. This is not a thing either of them should carry into a phone alone.

## 2. The First Call — Rachel Darcy (Charlottesville)

- **The time:** Early morning in Virginia — Mike waits until it is a reasonable hour before he dials. Rachel Darcy picks up quickly. She is already awake — she has always been an early riser.
- **Her voice:** Bright at first, the way it is when she sees her parents' number and has no reason yet to brace herself. *"Dad? Is everything okay?"*
- **Mike:** *"We need to tell you something about Fallon. We want you to sit down."*
  The brightness changes. She sits down.
- **The telling:** Mike and Terri Anne tell her everything — calmly, completely, in order. The swimming teacher. What he did. The weeks Fallon carried it alone. The sleepless night. The bedroom door. The doctor's appointment. The police statement. The pregnancy confirmed. All of it.
- **Rachel Darcy during the telling:** She does not speak much. She makes sounds — the particular sounds of someone receiving information that keeps arriving in waves, each one larger than the last. At some point: *"She's twelve."* Not a question. Just saying it out loud, the way you say something when you need it to be real in the air.
  Terri Anne: *"Yes. She is twelve."*
- **Antoine:** He is there — Rachel Darcy is living with him. Mike and Terri Anne can hear her telling him quietly, in the background, to give her a moment. The sound of a door closing.
- **What Rachel Darcy asks:** She asks the same question Kiley Rose asked the night before — *is she going to be okay?* And then: *"Can I call her?"* Mike: *"Give her a day or two. She is very tired. But yes — when she is ready, she will want to hear from you."*
- **The end of the call:** Rachel Darcy's voice is thick. She does not cry on the call — she holds it. But before she hangs up she says: *"Tell her I love her. Tell her right now, today."* Mike: *"We will."*

## 3. The Second Call — Jessica Isabella and Emma (Halifax)

- **Why together:** Jessica Isabella and Emma are a unit — they live together, they parent Samira Chaka and Sahara Hannan together, they navigate everything together. Telling Jessica Isabella without Emma in the room would mean Jessica Isabella carrying it alone for the minutes before she could tell Emma. Mike and Terri Anne will not do that to either of them.
- **The call:** Emma answers — she sees the Rio number and calls Jessica Isabella over before she even says hello. Both of them on the phone, four-year-old twins somewhere in the background of the Halifax apartment.
- **Mike tells it:** The same way he told Rachel Darcy — calmly, completely, in order. Everything.
- **Jessica Isabella during the telling:** She goes very quiet. The particular quiet of Jessica Isabella, who has always processed the hardest things by going still first. When Mike finishes she is silent for a long moment.

  Then: *"She knocked on your door."*

  Not a question. She is seeing it — Fallon standing at their parents' bedroom door in the dark of the Rio morning, the same door Jessica Isabella has known her whole life as the most private door in the house.

  *"She knocked on your door and you let her in."*

  Terri Anne: *"The moment we heard the knock."*

  Jessica Isabella: *"Good."* Quiet. Absolute. The same word Fallon used at the police station. *Good.*

- **Emma:** She speaks after Jessica Isabella — her voice careful and warm and already thinking forward. She asks about the pregnancy, the medical support, what Fallon needs. The practical questions of someone who loves well and knows that love sometimes looks like logistics.

  And then, quietly: *"The twins are here if Fallon ever wants to talk to someone who — "*

  She stops herself. What she means is: Samira Chaka and Sahara Hannan were born from something Fallon's situation rhymes with — not the same, but related. Emma does not finish the sentence. Mike and Terri Anne understand it anyway.

- **Samira Chaka or Sahara Hannan:** Somewhere in the background of the call, one of the four-year-old twins says something — a small voice, entirely unaware — and for a moment the Halifax apartment is just an apartment with two mothers and two small children and an ordinary morning. Mike and Terri Anne hear it. They let it sit for a second. Then they continue.
- **The end of the call:** Jessica Isabella: *"We'll come to Carlisle when you get back. Tell us when."* Terri Anne: *"We will."*

## 4. The Third Call — Patti Anne (Vaughan)

- **Patti Anne:** She is 22 and she has a one-year-old. She has been the eldest of eight her whole life. She has been the one who steps up, who holds things together, who takes the practical piece of whatever the family needs. She answers the phone in the particular way of a young mother — slightly distracted, one ear on Gamaliel Galvin in the background.
- **When Mike says *sit down*:** She sits down immediately. She has heard that voice before. She knows what it means.
- **The telling:** Mike tells her the same way he told the others. Everything, in order, complete.
- **Patti Anne during the telling:** She does not go quiet the way Rachel Darcy and Jessica Isabella did. She listens and she asks questions — sharp, practical questions, the questions of the eldest who is already thinking about what needs to happen:
  - *"The police have the statement?"* Mike: *"Yes. Yesterday."*
  - *"And the teacher — has he been arrested yet?"* Mike: *"It is in motion."*
  - *"What does Fallon need when she gets to Carlisle?"* Terri Anne takes this one — she lists it, carefully, the medical and emotional needs, the appointments that will need to be arranged, the things the family will need to hold around Fallon.
  - Patti Anne is writing things down. Mike and Terri Anne can hear a pen.
- **The moment she stops being practical:** Somewhere in the middle of her questions, Patti Anne stops. There is a pause — longer than the others. And then:

  *"She was alone with this. For weeks."*

  Her voice is different in that sentence. Not the eldest-in-charge voice. Something younger. Something that is just a sister.

  *"She was twelve years old and she was alone with it."*

  Terri Anne: *"Not anymore."*

  Another pause. Then Patti Anne takes a breath — audible, deliberate — and comes back.

  *"Okay. Tell me what you need from me."*

- **Gamaliel Galvin in the background:** At some point during the call, Gamaliel makes a sound — a one-year-old's demand for attention, uncomplicated and immediate. Patti Anne says quietly, away from the phone: *"One minute, baby."* And then back: *"Sorry. Go ahead."* The ordinary life continuing around the worst news.
- **The end of the call:** Patti Anne: *"I'll have the Vaughan house ready for anything you need on the way through. And tell Fallon — "*  She stops. Reconsiders. *"I'll tell her myself. When she's ready."* Mike: *"She will be."*

## 5. After the Calls — Mike and Terri Anne Alone

- **The room after:** They have made three calls. They have told the truth three times in the space of a morning — completely, without softening, to the people who could receive it fully. It is done.
- **What it cost:** They sit in the bedroom for a moment — the same bedroom, the same light, the room that yesterday morning held Fallon and her crying and the beginning of all of this. It is quieter now. The calls are finished. The older girls know.
- **Mike:** He sets his phone down on the bed. He does not say anything for a moment. Terri Anne sits beside him. They are not performing anything for anyone right now — there is no one to be steady for, no face to manage, no child in the room who needs them to hold it together.
- **Terri Anne:** She puts her head on Mike's shoulder. He puts his arm around her. They sit like that for a while — two parents who have spent the last twenty-four hours holding everyone else up, taking a moment to hold each other instead.
- **What they do not say:** They do not say *we should have known sooner.* They do not say *how did we miss it.* Those thoughts exist — they will exist for a long time — but this is not the moment for them. This moment is for being glad that Fallon knocked. Being glad she said it. Being glad it is moving, now, in the direction of justice and care and the family closing in.
- **Closing image:** Mike and Terri Anne in their bedroom. The Rio morning outside — the city going about itself, indifferent and alive. Three phone calls made. Three adult daughters who now know the full truth and are already turning toward Fallon from their different cities, their different lives, their different kinds of love. Patti Anne with her pen and her list. Rachel Darcy with Antoine's hand on her shoulder. Jessica Isabella and Emma in Halifax with four-year-old twins who do not know anything except that their mothers have gone quiet. All of them, from different distances, already moving closer.

## 6. Character Work

### Mike Key
- **Saying it three times:** He told the truth in the bedroom. He held it at the clinic. He held it at the police. He said it again to Rachel Darcy, to Jessica Isabella and Emma, to Patti Anne. Six times in two days, he has either received or delivered the hardest information of this family's recent life. He does it because Fallon needs it done and he is her father and this is what fathers do.
- **After the calls:** He sets his phone down and he lets Terri Anne put her head on his shoulder and he does not say anything. That is the truest thing in this section.

### Terri Anne Williams-Key
- **Her head on his shoulder:** After holding everyone up for twenty-four hours, she takes thirty seconds to be held herself. This is not weakness. This is what keeps her able to keep going.
- **To Emma's unfinished sentence:** She understands what Emma was going to say without Emma finishing it. She receives it and moves on without making Emma name it. That is grace.

### Patti Anne (22)
- **The pen:** She is already writing things down before Mike has finished speaking. That is who she is — the eldest, the one who loves by doing, by organising, by making sure the practical pieces are in place. The moment she stops being practical — *she was alone with this, for weeks* — is the scene's glimpse of the sister underneath the eldest.
- **"I'll tell her myself. When she's ready."** She does not send a message through her parents. She will speak to Fallon directly. That is Patti Anne.

### Jessica Isabella (20)
- **"She knocked on your door."** She sees it immediately and names it — the specific image of Fallon at the bedroom door. It matters to her. The door. The knock. The opening. She understands, from her own history with guilt and being held and being received, exactly what that moment cost Fallon and what it meant that the door opened.
- **"Good."** The same word Fallon used at the police station. One syllable. Absolute.

### Emma Fritz (20)
- **The practical questions and the unfinished sentence:** Emma loves by thinking forward and by offering what she has. The unfinished sentence — *the twins are here if Fallon ever wants to talk to someone who —* — is Emma offering the particular understanding of someone who has lived alongside a story that rhymes with Fallon's. She does not finish it because she does not need to. Mike and Terri Anne understand. That is enough.

### Rachel Darcy (20)
- **"She's twelve."** The most devastated sentence in the calls. Not a question. Just the fact, said aloud, because she needs it to be real. She is a dancer — she understands bodies, the particular vulnerability of a young body, what it means to be twelve and to have your body become the site of someone else's choice.
- **"Tell her I love her. Tell her right now, today."** She cannot get to Rio. She cannot sit beside Fallon. This is what she can give — the instruction, the urgency, the love sent through her parents' hands.

## 7. Thematic Motifs

- **The full truth for adults:** Mike and Terri Anne do not soften it, edit it, or protect the older daughters from it. They are adults. They deserve to know completely. This is the Key family rule of radical truth applied to the next generation — the daughters who are now old enough to receive it fully.
- **Fallon's permission:** She said *tell them.* That matters. This is not Mike and Terri Anne deciding for her — it is Fallon choosing to let her adult sisters in. Her agency, even here.
- **Three calls, three receptions:** Rachel Darcy goes quiet and holds it and says *she's twelve.* Jessica Isabella names the door. Patti Anne writes things down and then stops being practical for one sentence. Three sisters, three kinds of love, three ways of receiving the same truth.
- **"She knocked on your door."** Jessica Isabella's line. The bedroom door is the image that carries the whole scene — the most private door in the house, knocked on by a twelve-year-old who had been awake all night. The older sisters hear it and understand what it meant.
- **Mike and Terri Anne, after:** They sit together in the room where it all started. They let themselves be held by each other. The calls are done. The truth is out. The family is closing in from three different cities. That is the whole point.
