# Fallon Glenda — The Doctor's Appointment — Pregnancy Confirmed — Scene Outline

> **Scope:** The same day as the bedroom disclosure. Mike and Terri Anne take Fallon Glenda to the doctor in Rio de Janeiro. Fallon is numb from exhaustion and crying — she moves through the morning in a daze, holding herself together by not thinking too far ahead. When the doctor confirms the pregnancy, it becomes real in a way the whole sleepless night could not make it. The word lands and she falls apart again — and Mike and Terri Anne are right there.
>
> **Time:** May 2, 2045 — the same morning as the bedroom disclosure, a few hours later. Fallon Glenda is twelve years old.
> **Place:** A medical clinic in Rio de Janeiro. The waiting room, the examination room.
> **Characters present:** Fallon Glenda, Mike Key, Terri Anne Williams-Key, the doctor.
> **Mood:** Numb exhaustion → the clinical reality of a waiting room → the word confirmed → falling apart → held.

---

## 1. Leaving the House

- **After the bedroom:** The household is stirring by the time the three of them come out of Mike and Terri Anne's room. The younger sisters — Sabrina Bryna (14), Andrea Jennifer (10), Ashley Erin (8) — are beginning their morning. Kiley Rose (16) is up with the twins. The house has the ordinary morning noise of a large family.
- **What Mike and Terri Anne do not say:** They do not explain to the other girls where they are going. Terri Anne tells them simply that she and Mike are taking Fallon to an appointment and that Kiley Rose is in charge until they are back. Kiley Rose, still raw from her own night, looks at Fallon's face and does not ask questions. She just nods.
- **Fallon getting ready:** She moves through it mechanically — shoes, a hair tie, the oversized jacket she has been wearing for weeks. She does not eat anything. Terri Anne does not push her. She hands her a glass of water and watches her drink it.
- **In the car:** The three of them. Mike driving. Terri Anne in the back seat with Fallon, their shoulders touching. Rio outside the windows — the city already fully alive, indifferent and bright. Fallon watches it go past without really seeing it. She has been awake all night. She has cried until she was empty. She is running on something that is not quite strength — more like the absence of anything left to spend.
- **What Fallon is thinking:** Not about the appointment. Not about what the doctor will say. She is keeping her mind very close to the present moment — the seat beneath her, Terri Anne's shoulder against hers, the sound of the engine. If she thinks further ahead than the next few minutes, something enormous begins to press on her from the inside. She has learned, in the past few weeks, to stay very close to now.

---

## 2. The Waiting Room

- **Arriving:** Mike parks. The three of them walk in together — Mike on one side, Terri Anne on the other, Fallon between them. She is small between her parents. She looks younger than twelve.
- **The waiting room:** Ordinary. Chairs along the wall. A low table with magazines. The particular quiet of a medical waiting room — other people with their own reasons for being here, not looking at each other. Fallon sits between Mike and Terri Anne. She stares at the floor.
- **The numb:** She is not thinking about what the doctor will say. She is not thinking about anything very much. The exhaustion has flattened everything — the night has wrung her out and left her operating in a strange, muffled quiet. She answers when spoken to. She moves when asked to move. She is present and she is not present.
- **Mike's hand:** At some point in the waiting room, without announcement, Mike puts his hand over hers where it rests on her knee. He does not squeeze. He does not say anything. He just puts his hand there and leaves it. Fallon looks down at it. She does not pull away. She turns her hand over so her palm is up and holds his hand the way she held it when she was very small, and Mike closes his fingers around hers and that is how they wait.
- **Terri Anne:** She has already spoken to the clinic by phone — this is not a cold walk-in. She has told them what they are coming for. The doctor who sees Fallon has been briefed on the context: a twelve-year-old, a predatory adult, a suspected pregnancy. The appointment is handled with that knowledge.

---

## 3. The Examination Room

- **Being called in:** The nurse calls Fallon's name. The three of them stand. Fallon's hand tightens on Mike's for one second — just one — and then she releases it and walks forward.
- **Mike in the room:** He is present for the appointment at Fallon's invitation — she did not ask him to wait outside. She wants both of them there. He stands against the wall — not between Fallon and the doctor, never interfering with the clinical space — but visible, present, a fixed point she can look at if she needs to.
- **Terri Anne:** She sits beside the examination table, close enough to touch Fallon's arm. She translates when needed — the medical language, the clinical steps — into something a twelve-year-old can follow. She has done this in professional settings many times. She does it now for her daughter.
- **The doctor:** Warm, careful, and direct. They speak to Fallon first — not just to her parents. They explain each step before they do it. They ask for Fallon's consent before each part of the examination. The room is handled with the particular care of a clinician who understands exactly what this child has been through and what she needs from this space: control. Agency. To be asked, not managed.
- **The examination:** Clinical and necessary. Terri Anne holds Fallon's hand throughout. Mike does not look away from Fallon's face — not at the equipment, not at the doctor. Only at his daughter. Keeping her anchored. Keeping her seen.

---

## 4. The Confirmation

- **The moment:** The doctor looks at the results — the test, the ultrasound image on the screen — and turns to Fallon. They do not bury it in clinical language. They say it plainly, because Fallon deserves plainness:

  *"Fallon. You are pregnant."*

- **The numb holding:** For one long moment, nothing happens. Fallon sits on the edge of the examination table and looks at the doctor and the word goes in but does not immediately land. She has known — she has suspected, she has feared, the sleepless night was full of this exact word — and yet hearing it said in a clinical room by a doctor in the bright Rio morning is something entirely different from suspecting it in the dark.
- **Then it lands:** Her face crumples. Not loudly — not the full-body crying of the bedroom. This is quieter and in some ways worse: the tears that come when a person has already cried everything out and the body finds more anyway. Her shoulders fold inward. Her hands go to her face.

  *"Okay,"* she says. Into her palms. Barely audible.

  Just that. *Okay.* The word of someone who does not know what else to say. The word of a twelve-year-old trying to hold a fact that is too large for her hands.

- **Terri Anne:** She is already moving — already beside her, already with both arms around her before the second tear falls. She does not say *it's going to be okay.* She does not say anything yet. She just holds her and lets the fact settle at whatever speed it needs to.
- **Mike:** He crosses the room in two steps. He does not crowd the space Terri Anne has made — he comes to Fallon's other side and crouches to her level and puts one hand on her back. The three of them are close. Fallon between her parents, the way she has always been safest.

  After a moment, Mike speaks. Very quietly. The same voice he used in the bedroom, the same steadiness he chose over his grief:

  *"We have you. Do you hear me? We have you."*

  Fallon nods into Terri Anne's shoulder. She cannot speak. She nods.

- **The doctor:** They give the family space — stepping back, giving them the room, not rushing to the next clinical point. They have done this long enough to know when a room needs to breathe.

---

## 5. What the Doctor Explains

- **When Fallon is ready:** Terri Anne signals — a small nod to the doctor — and they return to the clinical conversation. Gently. In stages.
- **What is covered:**
  - How far along the pregnancy is — giving the family a timeline.
  - What the next medical steps are — specialist referral, monitoring, prenatal care beginning immediately.
  - The particular considerations for a twelve-year-old's body carrying a pregnancy — the risks, the monitoring required, what Fallon will need.
  - The confirmation that this pregnancy is the result of abuse — noted formally in the medical record, which becomes part of the legal trail Mike's contacts will use.
- **Fallon during this:** She listens. She does not understand everything. Terri Anne translates, quietly and precisely, as she goes. Fallon asks one question — only one:

  *"Will it hurt?"*

  Terri Anne answers her honestly:

  *"Some of it will. We will be with you for all of it."*

  Fallon nods. That is enough for now.

- **Mike:** He listens to every word the doctor says and stores it all. His mind is doing two things simultaneously — absorbing the medical reality for Fallon's sake, and noting every clinical detail that will matter when he speaks to his OPP contacts. The medical record. The timeline. The documented evidence. He says nothing about the second track. That is not for this room.

---

## 6. Leaving the Clinic

- **Walking out:** The three of them walk back through the waiting room. Other people with their own reasons for being here, not looking. The ordinary world, going about itself.
- **Fallon between her parents:** She is exhausted beyond what sleep will fix — the kind of exhaustion that lives in the bones after a night of no sleep and a morning of too much truth. She walks slowly. Mike and Terri Anne match her pace.
- **Outside:** The Rio sun is full now — mid-morning, bright and warm and entirely indifferent to what has just happened in that clinic. The city moves around them.
- **Fallon stops on the pavement outside:** Just for a moment. She stands still and looks at the street — the traffic, the colour, the ordinary Rio morning — and something passes across her face that is not quite grief and not quite relief. It is the expression of someone who has just been given a fact they cannot put down, and who is realising that they are going to have to carry it forward from here.
- **Mike:** He stands beside her. He does not rush her. When she is ready, he puts his arm around her shoulders — not guiding, just there — and they walk to the car.

---

## 7. The Ride Home

- **The car, again:** Terri Anne in the back with Fallon. Mike driving. Rio outside. The same route, in reverse.
- **Fallon's question:** Somewhere on the ride home — quietly, looking out the window:

  *"Are you going to tell the others?"*

  Mike and Terri Anne exchange a glance in the mirror.

  Terri Anne:

  *"We are going to tell them together. When you are ready. Not before."*

  Fallon considers this.

  *"Okay,"* she says again. The same word. Still too large. Still the only one she has right now.

- **What she does not ask:** She does not ask about the swimming teacher. She does not ask what Mike is going to do. She does not ask about the future past today. She is keeping her mind very close to now — the seat, the window, Terri Anne's shoulder, the sound of the engine. Just now. Just this.
- **Closing image:** The car moving through Rio. Mike's eyes in the rearview mirror — finding Fallon, holding a moment, then back to the road. Terri Anne's hand over Fallon's in the back seat. Outside: the city, the water, the colour of Brazil in the morning. Inside: a twelve-year-old girl and the two people who will not leave her side for a single step of what comes next.

## 8. Character Work

### Fallon Glenda (12)
- **The numb:** She has been awake all night and cried herself hollow in the bedroom and now she is moving through the morning on something below strength. The numbness is not detachment — it is the body's mercy, the way exhaustion softens the edges of things that would otherwise be unendurable.
- **When it lands:** The word *pregnant* said out loud in a clinical room by a doctor is different from the word feared in the dark. She knew and she did not know. Now she knows. Her only word is *okay* — the word of someone trying to hold something too large.
- **Her one question:** *Will it hurt?* It is the most honest question in the scene. Not *what happens next,* not *what did I do wrong,* not *why me.* Just: will it hurt. And Terri Anne answers it honestly, which is the only answer Fallon could have received.

### Terri Anne Williams-Key
- **The nurse and the mother:** She prepared the clinic in advance. She translated the clinical language for her daughter in real time. She held Fallon's hand through the examination and was already moving before the second tear fell. She answers the hard question honestly rather than reassuringly. She is both things at once, perfectly.
- **Her promise:** *"Some of it will. We will be with you for all of it."* It is the most Terri Anne sentence in the scene — honest, direct, complete. No false comfort. Just presence.

### Mike Key
- **The two tracks:** His mind runs two simultaneous operations throughout — absorbing everything for Fallon's sake, and noting everything that will matter when he makes the call. He does not let the second track surface in the room. It is entirely invisible. Fallon sees only the first track: her father, present, crouched at her level, telling her *we have you.*
- **"We have you."** Three words. The simplest thing he could say. The most complete.
- **His eyes in the mirror:** The closing image of the scene. He checks on her the way he always checks on her — the rearview mirror version of the crouch, the beach, the Capoeira studio door. He always finds her face first.

## 9. Thematic Motifs

- **The word *okay*:** Fallon says it twice — once when the confirmation lands, once when Terri Anne tells her they will tell the others together. It is not acceptance. It is not resolution. It is a twelve-year-old's way of saying: *I am still here. I am still trying.* It is enough.
- **The hand in the waiting room:** Mike's hand over Fallon's, and then Fallon turning her palm up to hold it the way she did when she was small. She is still small. She is twelve. This is allowed.
- **The rearview mirror:** Mike finds her face in the mirror on the way home. He has always found her face first. This is who he is.
- **The city, indifferent:** Rio outside every window — bright, alive, going about itself. The world does not pause for what is happening inside this car, this clinic, this family. It never does. The Key family moves through it anyway, together.
- **Will it hurt:** The question every child asks when they do not yet have the vocabulary for what they are really asking. What they are really asking is: *am I going to be alone in the hard parts?* Terri Anne's answer — *we will be with you for all of it* — answers both questions at once.
