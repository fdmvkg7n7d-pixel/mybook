# SCENE OUTLINE: RACHEL DARCY SAYS GOODBYE TO FALLON GLENDA

**Characters:** Fallon Glenda (17), Charlie (18), Lÿsa Mandy (4–5), Rachel Darcy (mid-20s).
**Setting:** The kitchen of the Carlisle mansion, late morning. The rest of the family has scattered to other rooms, leaving Rachel Darcy and Fallon at the kitchen island with a mug of tea and a mess of packing tape.
**Timeframe:** Late June, days before the Stanford move.

## THE CONTEXT
Rachel Darcy has traveled from Philadelphia to say goodbye to Fallon before the move to California. She is the sister who makes hard things funny and funny things sharp. Her goodbye is the antidote to the heavier goodbyes — the one that lets Fallon laugh before she cries.

## THE SCENE

**The Kitchen Island:**
*   Fallon is sitting on the kitchen floor taping shut a box of books. Rachel Darcy comes in, sits down on the floor beside her, and picks up the tape gun.
*   *“You’re doing it wrong,”* Rachel Darcy says.
*   Fallon looks at her box. *“It’s taped.”*
*   *“It’s taped like a person who is trying to keep their feelings inside. You need an H-pattern. Two strips across, one down the middle. Structural integrity.”* Rachel Darcy demonstrates on the next box with theatrical precision.
*   Fallon laughs, and the sound surprises both of them. Rachel Darcy grins. *“There it is. I was starting to think you’d forgotten how.”*

**The Map of Diners:**
*   Rachel Darcy pulls a folded piece of paper from her back pocket. It is a hand-drawn map of the Route 66 stretch, covered in her messy handwriting and small drawings of pie slices, coffee cups, and a dinosaur statue.
*   *“I made you a guide,”* she says. *“Best diners, worst motels to avoid, the place in Oklahoma with the pancakes that will change your life, and the gas station in Arizona where you should absolutely not use the bathroom.”*
*   Fallon unfolds it. The map is ridiculous and detailed and full of Rachel’s voice. *“You did this for me?”*
*   *“I did it because you’re about to drive across the country with a five-year-old and a boy who thinks he knows what he’s doing. You need pie. Multiple pies. I have annotated the pies.”*

**Charlie and the Warning:**
*   Charlie walks into the kitchen looking for the tape gun. Rachel Darcy looks up at him from the floor.
*   *“Sit down, Charlie,”* she says. *“I need to threaten you, and it’s more effective at eye level.”*
*   Charlie sits, wary but amused. Rachel Darcy points at him with the tape gun.
*   *“If you make her unhappy, I will find you. I have resources. I have a credit card. I have absolutely no sense of proportion.”*
*   Charlie nods seriously. *“I believe you.”*
*   *“Good. Now help me tape this box.”*
*   The three of them sit on the kitchen floor taping boxes together. It is the most ordinary moment of the whole farewell, and it is exactly what Rachel Darcy intended.

**The Serious Beat:**
*   When Charlie leaves to find Lÿsa Mandy, Rachel Darcy’s tone shifts. She stops being funny.
*   *“You know what I admire about you?”* she asks. *“You became a mother at thirteen in the worst possible way, and you never became hard. You could have. You had every right. But you stayed soft. That is not weakness. That is a kind of strength I don’t have.”*
*   Fallon looks at her. *“You’re soft too.”*
*   Rachel Darcy shakes her head. *“I’m sharp. You’re soft. We need both in this family. Go to California and be soft where the world tells you to be hard.”*

**The Farewell and Good Luck:**
*   They stand up, dusting off their knees. Rachel Darcy pulls Fallon into a fierce, short hug and then holds her at arm’s length.
*   *“Farewell and good luck, Fallon Glenda,”* she says. *“I will miss you. I will also send you terrible postcards. You are not allowed to move without a forwarding address.”*
*   Fallon nods, smiling through tears. *“I love you.”*
*   *“I know. Same. Now go cry somewhere private. I can’t handle it when you cry.”* Rachel Darcy turns away and starts taping another box, but her hands are not quite steady.

## WHY THIS SCENE MATTERS

*   **Rachel Darcy’s Defence Mechanism:** She uses humour to make the unbearable bearable. The scene lets her be funny and then reveals the tenderness underneath.
*   **The Route 66 Map:** The map is a practical gift and a comic one, but it is also a way of staying present in Fallon’s journey. Rachel cannot drive to California with her, so she sends her own voice ahead.
*   **Charlie’s Initiation:** Rachel Darcy’s threat is real and funny, but it also establishes that Charlie has been accepted into the family orbit — he is being warned, which means he is being taken seriously.
*   **Softness as Strength:** Rachel Darcy names Fallon’s survival as a form of softness rather than hardness. This reframes Fallon’s trauma not as something that should have made her tough, but as something she survived while remaining capable of tenderness.
