# Fallon Glenda's First Medical Appointment at Carlisle — Scene Outline

> **Scope:** A few days after the family’s arrival back in Canada. This scene covers Fallon Glenda's first prenatal appointment on Canadian soil. While Mike considered bringing the team to the mansion, the necessity of specialized pediatric-obstetric equipment requires them to go to McMaster Children's Hospital in Hamilton. However, Mike ensures the visit is handled with the utmost security and privacy, bypassing public waiting rooms entirely.
>
> **Time:** Early June 2045. A few days after arriving back in Ontario. Fallon Glenda is twelve.
> **Place:** McMaster Children's Hospital, Hamilton, Ontario (a highly secure, private wing/room).
> **Characters present:** Fallon Glenda (12), Mike Key, Terri Anne Williams-Key, and Dr. Aris (a trusted, highly discreet Ontario specialist).
> **Mood:** Controlled, fiercely private, and profoundly anchoring. The transition from crisis survival to long-term medical care, managed with Mike Key's relentless protective logistics.

---

## 1. Leaving Carlisle

- **The morning routine:** The household is awake, but it is moving with the quiet, deliberate rhythm that has become the new normal since Rio. 
- **The environment:** Outside, the Ontario spring is in full bloom. The air is cool. 
- **Fallon’s state:** Fallon is wearing soft, comfortable clothes—no longer the heavy oversized armor she needed in Brazil, but she is still visibly nervous about leaving the estate. 
- **The drive to Hamilton:** Mike drives. Terri Anne sits in the back with Fallon. The drive from Carlisle to McMaster Children's Hospital in Hamilton is short, but the physical transition from the mansion to a medical facility brings a spike of anxiety for Fallon, echoing the terrifying drive in Rio.

## 2. Arrival at McMaster Children's Hospital

- **The logistical shield:** Mike does not park in the main garage or walk his twelve-year-old daughter through the crowded public lobby. Through his vast resources and prior coordination, they are directed to a secure, private entrance.
- **Bypassing the public:** They are met immediately by a hospital coordinator who takes them straight to a private suite in the pediatric obstetrics wing. There is no waiting room. There are no other patients looking at them. Mike’s money and influence have created an invisible, frictionless corridor through the hospital.
- **The private suite:** The room is clinical but warm, designed for high-risk pediatric cases. Fallon sits on the edge of the examination table. Terri Anne is immediately at her side.

## 3. The Appointment

- **The Request:** Before the examination begins, Dr. Aris politely asks if Fallon would like privacy, giving her the option to have her parents step out. Fallon immediately looks at Mike. Her voice is small but absolutely certain: *"Stay. Please."* Mike does not hesitate. He simply nods. 
- **Dr. Aris:** Dr. Aris—a trusted pediatric obstetrician whom Mike has vetted extensively—is warm, professional, and entirely un-shocked. She speaks directly to Fallon, maintaining the clinical respect established in Rio but adding the warmth of long-term care.
- **The physical proximity:** Mike stays exactly where he was asked to be. He stands near the door, his presence a silent, solid wall between Fallon and the outside world. Terri Anne sits right beside Fallon on the examination table, holding her hand.
- **The examination:** It is comprehensive, utilizing the specialized equipment at McMaster that couldn't be brought to the mansion. It focuses on establishing a baseline for her ongoing care in Canada. 
- **The heartbeat:** Using the hospital's advanced Doppler, Dr. Aris finds the fetal heartbeat. The sound fills the quiet room. 
- **Fallon’s reaction:** In Rio, the ultrasound was a terrifying confirmation of trauma. Here, in the safety of McMaster, surrounded by the invisible shield her father has built, the sound of the heartbeat is still overwhelming, but it lands differently. Fallon looks at Terri Anne. The tears that fall are not from sheer terror, but from the immense weight of the reality she is now carrying.

## 4. The Medical Plan

- **Dr. Aris speaks to Fallon:** She outlines what the next few months will look like. She explains how Fallon’s body will change and what they will do to keep her safe and healthy. 
- **The Bi-Weekly Schedule:** Because Fallon is only twelve years old, her body is still developing, making this an extremely high-risk pediatric pregnancy. Dr. Aris informs them that standard prenatal scheduling will not be enough. Fallon will need to be seen **bi-weekly** (every two weeks) to closely monitor both her health and the baby's development.
- **The assurance:** Dr. Aris makes it clear that despite the rigorous schedule, Fallon is in control. *“We are going to do this together, and you tell me when you need to stop, pause, or ask a question.”*
- **Mike’s interjection:** Mike asks only about logistics. He confirms with Dr. Aris that every single bi-weekly appointment will follow this exact protocol: the secure entrance, the invisible corridor, no waiting rooms, and absolute privacy. He guarantees that whatever resources McMaster needs to accommodate this schedule, he will provide. Dr. Aris confirms it will be done.

## 5. The Return Home

- **Leaving the hospital:** They exit the same way they entered—invisible to the public. 
- **The shift:** The appointment marks a distinct shift in the narrative. The crisis of the Rio discovery is over; the reality of the long road ahead has officially begun.
- **The car ride back:** In the car heading back to Carlisle, the atmosphere is heavy but grounded. Mike looks at Fallon in the rearview mirror. 
- **Fallon’s exhale:** As the heavy iron gates of the Carlisle mansion open to let them back inside, Fallon lets out a deep breath. She survived leaving the cocoon because her family brought the cocoon with them.

## 6. Character Work

### Mike Key
- **The Facilitator:** While he couldn't keep her inside the mansion for this visit, his wealth and influence are used entirely to build an invisible corridor. He ensures Fallon never sees a waiting room and never feels the public gaze. 

### Terri Anne Williams-Key
- **The Translator:** She continues her role of sitting right beside Fallon, anchoring her physically during the drive and the examination. 

### Fallon Glenda (12)
- **The Shift:** Hearing the heartbeat at McMaster is a turning point. It moves the pregnancy from an abstract nightmare in a foreign country to a concrete reality. Surviving the trip out of the mansion and back proves that her family's protection extends beyond their property line.

### Dr. Aris
- **The Safe Professional:** She is calm, discreet, and empowers Fallon by speaking directly to her and giving her agency over the examinations.

---

## 7. Thematic Motifs

- **The Invisible Corridor:** The contrast between the terrifying public exposure in Rio and the frictionless, utterly private movement through a major hospital in Canada highlights Mike Key's protective power.
- **The Heartbeat:** In contrast to the visual shock of the ultrasound in Rio, the auditory reality of the Doppler at McMaster forces the family to face the future, but they do it together, enclosed in safety.
- **The Gates Closing:** The recurring image of the Carlisle gates closing behind them at the end of the scene solidifies the estate as the ultimate safe harbor.
