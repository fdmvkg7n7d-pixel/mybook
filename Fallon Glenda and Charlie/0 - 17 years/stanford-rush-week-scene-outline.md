# FALLON GLENDA AND CHARLIE ATTEND STANFORD RUSH WEEK — SCENE OUTLINE

## 1. SCENE CONTEXT & SETTING
- **Timeline:** Third week in August. Fallon Glenda is 17 (turning 18 in December), Charlie is 18. Lÿsa Mandy is four years old. They have just completed their cross-country road trip and moved into their new home in **San Francisco** to begin their freshman year at Stanford.
- **Location:** Split between the bustling, energetic Greek rows/campus event spaces at **Stanford University** (about an hour south), and Fallon and Charlie's new home in **San Francisco**.
- **The Core Emotion:** The vital balance between their deeply adult responsibilities and their youth. Because Fallon became a mother at thirteen, she missed out on typical teenage recklessness. This scene highlights the Key family intentionally giving her and Charlie the space to just be eighteen-year-old college freshmen for the day.

## 2. THE HANDOFF
- **The Family Shield Flies North:** Terri Anne, along with Fallon's younger sisters Andrea Jennifer and Ashley Erin, were already in California. They had been down in Los Angeles where Ashley Erin was filming for the television show *This Land*. They make the short trip up to San Francisco to help the young family settle into their new home.
- **The Push Out the Door:** It is Monday, the official start of Stanford's Rush Week and freshman orientation. Fallon is hesitant to leave Lÿsa Mandy in the chaos of unpacking the San Francisco house, but Terri Anne is firm and deeply loving. She practically pushes Fallon and Charlie out the door.
- **Terri Anne's Instruction:** Terri Anne tells them both, *"For today, you are not parents. You are college freshmen. Take the drive down to Palo Alto, go to the events, meet your classmates, and do not call me unless someone is in the hospital. Be back by this evening."* 

## 3. MEANWHILE: THE BABYSITTERS IN SAN FRANCISCO
- **The Aunties in Charge:** While Fallon and Charlie are down at Stanford, the perspective shifts briefly to the San Francisco home. Andrea Jennifer and Ashley Erin (the two youngest Key sisters) are having a blast with their four-year-old niece. 
- **The Outing to Pier 39:** Instead of staying in, Terri Anne takes Andrea Jennifer, Ashley Erin, and Lÿsa Mandy out to explore the city. They go to the famous **Pier 39 at Fisherman's Wharf**. Lÿsa Mandy is captivated by the barking sea lions lounging on the docks, the bustling crowds, and the smell of the ocean.
- **The Generational Dynamic:** Terri Anne treats them all to a classic San Francisco lunch—hot clam chowder served in sourdough bread bowls while they watch the sea lions. She watches her two youngest daughters (who are young teenagers) hold hands with her four-year-old granddaughter as they explore the wooden pier. Because Lÿsa Mandy is so articulate and observant, she has hilarious, deadpan conversations with Andrea Jennifer and Ashley Erin about their time on the *This Land* set in LA. 
- **The Evening Wind-Down:** After the long outing at the pier, they head back to the San Francisco home. Terri Anne orders pizza for dinner, and the girls turn the half-unpacked living room into a cozy hangout spot to eat.
- **The Underlying Love:** It shows the incredible, unbroken support of the Key family. They didn't just wave goodbye in Ontario; they mobilized to ensure Fallon and Charlie could start their university journey properly, taking over child-rearing so seamlessly that it just feels like a fun family outing.

## 4. NAVIGATING RUSH WEEK
- **The Daytime Events:** Fallon and Charlie walk the campus during the daytime rush events. It is a whirlwind of informational booths, student groups, and eager freshmen trying to find their place. The California sun is bright, and the energy is high.
- **Meeting Classmates:** They attend a few meet-and-greets and strike up conversations with a few new classmates. In keeping with the Key family rule of unapologetic honesty, when asked which dorm they are in, Fallon and Charlie casually explain that they commute from their home in San Francisco because they are raising their four-year-old daughter, Lÿsa Mandy. 
- **The Contrast:** Their classmates are stunned but immediately impressed by how grounded they are. For Fallon and Charlie—who have navigated parenthood and built a deeply committed life together—it is humorous and sweet to watch the other freshmen panicking about being away from home for the first time. They find typical freshman anxieties endearing but completely foreign. 
- **Charlie's Perspective:** As they walk across the beautiful campus, Charlie looks at Fallon. He didn't know her when she was pregnant in Rio; he met her when she was already a fiercely protective mother to three-year-old Lÿsa Mandy in Carlisle. Seeing her now—seventeen, stepping onto a university campus as a brilliant, capable young woman without the immediate, visible weight of motherhood on her shoulders for a few hours—moves him deeply. He gets to see the "college freshman" version of the girl he loves.

## 5. SCENE RESOLUTION
- **The Return Home:** It is evening. The sun is setting as Fallon and Charlie make the drive back up to the city and quietly unlock the door to their San Francisco home. They are carrying a few flyers and free campus merchandise.
- **The Quiet House:** They find Terri Anne reading on the couch, while Andrea Jennifer, Ashley Erin, and Lÿsa Mandy are all tangled up asleep on the living room floor, completely exhausted from their long day walking around Pier 39.
- **The Goodnight Kiss:** Fallon and Charlie quietly walk over and kneel down to gently kiss Lÿsa Mandy's forehead goodnight without waking her. Charlie wraps an arm around Fallon's waist as they look at their daughter, safely surrounded by her grandmother and aunts.
- **The Landing Image:** Leaving the living room, Fallon and Charlie head into Lÿsa Mandy's bedroom, seamlessly stepping back into their roles as parents. Together, they lay out Lÿsa Mandy's plaid Sacred Heart uniform for tomorrow morning. Fallon leans her head against Charlie's shoulder as they look at the little uniform, exhausted, happy, and profoundly grateful. They successfully did "college" for the day, but they are perfectly content being home.
