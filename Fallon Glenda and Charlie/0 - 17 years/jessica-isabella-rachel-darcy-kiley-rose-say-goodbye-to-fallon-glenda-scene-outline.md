# SCENE OUTLINE: JESSICA ISABELLA, RACHEL DARCY, AND KILEY ROSE SAY GOODBYE TO FALLON GLENDA

**Characters:** Fallon Glenda (17), Charlie (18), Lÿsa Mandy (4–5), Jessica Isabella (mid-20s), Rachel Darcy (mid-20s), Kiley Rose (~21), Mike Key, Terri Anne Williams-Key (optional background).
**Setting:** The Carlisle mansion in Ontario — the kitchen, the back terrace, and the long driveway where Fallon’s packed car waits. A deliberate, pre-departure family gathering before the July road trip to Stanford.
**Timeframe:** A late June afternoon, days before Fallon, Charlie, and Lÿsa Mandy leave for California.

## THE CONTEXT
Fallon Glenda (17) is days away from driving west with Charlie (18) and Lÿsa Mandy (4–5). The three oldest sisters who have already left the family orbit have come back to Carlisle to say goodbye in person: **Jessica Isabella** from Halifax, **Rachel Darcy** from Philadelphia, and **Kiley Rose** from New York City. Each sister once lived inside the same fortress; each now lives in a different city. Their return is not ceremonial — it is the family’s way of ensuring that Fallon does not leave without every older sister having had the chance to release her properly.

This scene is the older sisters’ collective farewell. It is not about the trauma of Rio, though the memory of it lives under everything. It is about the family acknowledging that Fallon is the next one to become a new address on the map.

## THE SCENE

**Arrival at Carlisle:**
*   The late June afternoon is warm and golden. The Carlisle mansion is already busy with packing, boxes, and the controlled chaos of a household preparing to send a daughter across a continent. Lÿsa Mandy’s building blocks are stacked in a corner of the kitchen in a perfect, colour-sorted wall. Her suitcase sits open nearby, half-filled.
*   Three cars have arrived at intervals through the afternoon: Jessica Isabella from the airport, Rachel Darcy from the train station, Kiley Rose from the city. They have not come together by accident. They have coordinated this. The three of them are standing on the back terrace when Fallon comes out with a tray of lemonade, pretending to be busy so she does not have to face the moment.
*   Mike Key and Terri Anne are present but not centre-stage. They understand that this goodbye belongs to the sisters. Mike is in his study with the door open; Terri Anne is folding laundry upstairs, listening.

**The Sisters on the Terrace:**
*   Jessica Isabella is the eldest of the three, composed, maternal, and accustomed to being the one who holds the family together from a distance. She is married, with her own household in Halifax, and she carries the calm authority of a sister who has already crossed the threshold Fallon is about to cross.
*   Rachel Darcy is sharp, funny, and deeply loyal. She has come from Philadelphia with a small gift she will press into Fallon’s hand at the last possible second. She is the sister who makes hard moments bearable by refusing to treat them like funerals.
*   Kiley Rose is the youngest of the three, closest in age to Fallon, and the one who was most present during the Rio years. She remembers the phalanx. She remembers holding Fallon’s hand on the plane. She remembers what it cost to be the sister who stood in front. Her goodbye is the heaviest because she and Fallon have the shortest distance between their ages and the longest shared history of protection.

**The Reunion:**
*   Fallon sets down the lemonade tray. Jessica Isabella steps forward first and pulls her into a long, steady hug. *“You’re smaller than I remember,”* Jessica says quietly, which is not true, but is what she always says when she has not seen a sister in months and the distance has suddenly caught up with her.
*   Rachel Darcy is next, wrapping her arms around Fallon from the side and squeezing hard. *“You’re going to California, kid. You’re going to wear sunglasses and forget what winter feels like and you’re going to be insufferable about it.”* Fallon laughs, finally, and the tension breaks.
*   Kiley Rose does not hug her right away. She stands back, looking at Fallon with the same assessing expression she had in Rio when she was deciding whether Fallon was strong enough to walk through an airport. Then she steps in and holds her for a long moment, saying nothing. When she pulls back, she signs — a habit she picked up from being around Sabrina and the younger sisters: *“Ready?”* Fallon nods.

**Lÿsa Mandy and the Older Aunts:**
*   Lÿsa Mandy comes out onto the terrace holding Charlie’s hand. She has her blocks in the small canvas tote, and she sets them down on the stone floor to sort before she will acknowledge anyone.
*   Jessica Isabella crouches down to her level and waits. She does not rush. Lÿsa Mandy, once her blocks are sorted, looks up and says, with the formality of a child who has rehearsed: *“Hello, Aunt Jessica.”* Jessica Isabella’s face softens completely.
*   Rachel Darcy produces a small bag of perfectly shaped acorns she collected on the train from Philadelphia and arranges them on the terrace beside Lÿsa’s blocks. Lÿsa Mandy considers this addition, then begins to build a wall using the acorns as decorative caps. Rachel Darcy watches, satisfied.
*   Kiley Rose sits on the terrace step near Lÿsa Mandy and simply stays close. She does not ask for attention. Lÿsa Mandy, after several minutes of parallel building, leans against her aunt’s knee for one second, then goes back to her blocks. It is the highest trust Kiley Rose could ask for.

**Charlie and the Older Sisters:**
*   Charlie stands at the edge of the terrace, respectful of the sister circle. Jessica Isabella goes to him first. She shakes his hand firmly and says, *“Take care of her. Not because she can’t take care of herself — because she shouldn’t always have to.”* Charlie nods. *“I know.”*
*   Rachel Darcy corners him with a grin. *“I’m going to like you, Charlie. I can already tell. But if you hurt her, I will fly to California and ruin your life with paperwork.”* Charlie laughs, then realizes she is not entirely joking. *“Understood.”*
*   Kiley Rose approaches Charlie last. She does not say much. She looks at him the way she looked at him in the kitchen at the bowling-alley date — the same measuring gaze. Then she says, *“You stayed. That was the hard part. Keep staying.”* Charlie nods, and she nods back. It is the only approval he needs.

**The Real Conversation (Terrace Table):**
*   The five of them sit around the wrought-iron terrace table — the three older sisters, Fallon, and Charlie. Lÿsa Mandy is on the flagstones nearby, building a low wall of blocks and acorns. Mike and Terri Anne have not come down; they are letting the sisters have this room.
*   Jessica Isabella speaks first. *“When I left for Halifax, I thought I was leaving the family behind. I wasn’t. I was just stretching it. You’re about to do the same thing.”*
*   Rachel Darcy adds, *“The family is going to feel smaller without you in the house. But it’s not smaller. It’s just in more places.”*
*   Kiley Rose is quiet for a moment. Then she says, *“You were the one I worried about the most. In Rio. On the plane. At McMaster. You were the one I thought might not make it out whole. And now you’re the one going farthest.”* She reaches across the table and touches Fallon’s hand. *“Don’t you dare make me right about worrying. Go be happy.”*
*   Fallon looks at each of them. Jessica Isabella, steady and wise. Rachel Darcy, fierce and funny. Kiley Rose, the protector who is finally learning to let go. She says, *“I’m scared I won’t know how to be happy without all of you right here.”*
*   Jessica Isabella answers, *“You already know. You just haven’t had the room to practice.”*

**The Farewell and Good Luck:**
*   The sun is beginning to lower. The packed car waits in the driveway. Lÿsa Mandy has finished her wall and is standing beside it, looking at the adults with the solemnity of a child who knows something is ending.
*   The three older sisters stand together in front of Fallon. They have planned this, or perhaps it is simply what sisters do when one of them walks forward.
*   Jessica Isabella signs first — slowly, because she is less fluent than Sabrina but has learned enough for this: *“Farewell and good luck.”*
*   Rachel Darcy signs next, her hands less graceful but her expression unmistakable: *“Farewell and good luck.”*
*   Kiley Rose signs last, her hands steady and certain: *“Farewell and good luck, Fallon Glenda.”*
*   Fallon signs it back to them: *“Farewell and good luck.”* Her hands shake slightly. No one comments on it.
*   Charlie stands beside her, holding Lÿsa Mandy’s hand, and repeats the sign as best he can. The three older sisters smile at him — the first real collective acceptance of him as part of Fallon’s future.

**The Gifts:**
*   Jessica Isabella gives Fallon a small compass on a thin chain. *“So you always know which way is home.”*
*   Rachel Darcy gives her a folded piece of paper with a hand-drawn map of the best diners on the Route 66 stretch, annotated in Rachel’s messy handwriting. *“You’re going to need pie. Trust me.”*
*   Kiley Rose gives her a photograph — not the family portrait, but a small, candid picture of Fallon holding Lÿsa Mandy for the first time in the NICU, taken by Kiley Rose herself. On the back, Kiley has written: *“You were already a mother. You were already enough. You were already home.”* Fallon cannot look at it for long without crying, so she tucks it into the notebook Sabrina gave her.

**The Departure:**
*   Mike and Terri Anne come down to the driveway now. The goodbye widens to include everyone, but the centre remains the sisters. Fallon hugs Jessica Isabella, Rachel Darcy, and Kiley Rose one last time. Each hug is different — Jessica’s is long, Rachel’s is fierce, Kiley’s is the shortest and most painful.
*   Charlie buckles Lÿsa Mandy into her car seat. Lÿsa Mandy holds up one of her acorn-capped blocks to the window as a goodbye. The three aunts wave back.
*   Fallon gets into the passenger seat. As Charlie starts the car, she looks back at the three sisters standing on the Carlisle driveway with her parents behind them. They are all raising their hands in the same sign: *“Farewell and good luck, Fallon Glenda.”*
*   The car pulls away. Fallon opens the notebook Sabrina gave her and places the compass, the map, and Kiley’s photograph inside. She closes it and holds it in her lap. The family is no longer a single address. It is a map, and she is adding California to it.

## WHY THIS SCENE MATTERS

*   **The Three Faces of Leaving:** Jessica Isabella represents wisdom and maternal steadiness; Rachel Darcy represents humour and fierce loyalty; Kiley Rose represents the Rio protector who is finally letting go. Together they give Fallon three different permissions to leave.
*   **The Farewell and Good Luck Ritual:** The phrase becomes a formal family blessing in this scene. The three older sisters give it to Fallon together, establishing it as the ritual for any sister leaving home. This connects back to the Sabrina goodbye and forward to future leavings.
*   **Charlie’s Acceptance:** The three older sisters test and accept Charlie in three different ways. By the end of the scene, he is not just Fallon’s boyfriend — he is the person the older sisters have entrusted with their sister.
*   **Lÿsa Mandy’s Inclusion:** Lÿsa is included in the family ritual through her blocks, the acorns from Rachel Darcy, and the wave from the car window. She is part of the leaving, not just luggage.
*   **The Family as a Map:** The scene explicitly reframes the scattered family — Halifax, Philadelphia, New York City, and now California — not as a broken circle but as an expanding map. The family’s love is not diminished by distance; it is stretched across it.
