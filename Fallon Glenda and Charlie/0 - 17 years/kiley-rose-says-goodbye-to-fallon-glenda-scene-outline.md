# SCENE OUTLINE: KILEY ROSE SAYS GOODBYE TO FALLON GLENDA

**Characters:** Fallon Glenda (17), Charlie (18), Lÿsa Mandy (4–5), Kiley Rose (~21).
**Setting:** The private indoor pool at the Carlisle mansion, early morning. The water is still, the room is humid and quiet, and the rest of the house is asleep.
**Timeframe:** Late June, the morning before the Stanford move.

## THE CONTEXT
Kiley Rose has come down from New York City to say goodbye to Fallon. Of all the sisters, Kiley Rose was the most present during the Rio years — the one who helped build the fortress around Fallon, the one who held her hand on the plane, the one who understood what it meant to be the shield. Her goodbye is the hardest because she and Fallon have the shortest distance between their ages and the longest shared history of protection.

## THE SCENE

**The Pool at Dawn:**
*   Fallon wakes early and goes to the indoor pool. She has not been able to sleep. The water is calm, reflecting the ceiling lights in long, steady stripes. She sits at the edge of the pool in her swimsuit, her feet in the water, not swimming yet.
*   Kiley Rose appears in the doorway in sweatpants and a hoodie. She does not ask if Fallon is okay. She sits down beside her, takes off her own socks, and puts her feet in the water too. They sit in silence for a while, watching the water move around their ankles.

**What the Pool Means:**
*   Fallon was a swimmer in Rio. The water was supposed to be her place, and then it became complicated. She has not talked about it much since the family returned to Carlisle. Kiley Rose knows this without being told.
*   *“You’re going to California,”* Kiley Rose says finally. *“There will be water there. Pools. The ocean. You do not have to be afraid of it. You do not have to love it either. You just have to know it belongs to you again.”*
*   Fallon looks at the water. *“I don’t know if I remember how to swim without thinking about it.”*
*   Kiley Rose nods. *“Then you will relearn. You have time. You are seventeen. You have a whole life in which the water can just be water.”*

**The Gift:**
*   Kiley Rose pulls a small envelope from her hoodie pocket and hands it to Fallon. Inside is a photograph — a candid shot, slightly out of focus, of Fallon holding Lÿsa Mandy for the first time in the NICU. Fallon is thirteen, exhausted, wearing a hospital gown, and looking at the baby with an expression of pure, terrified wonder.
*   On the back of the photograph, Kiley Rose has written: *“You were already a mother. You were already enough. You were already home.”*
*   Fallon cannot look at it for long. She folds the envelope and tucks it inside the notebook Sabrina gave her.
*   Kiley Rose says, *“I took that picture. I was the first person to see you look at her like that. I wanted you to remember what I saw.”*

**Charlie and the Permission to Let Go:**
*   Charlie appears in the doorway holding Lÿsa Mandy, who is still half-asleep. He stops when he sees the two sisters at the edge of the pool. He starts to back away, but Kiley Rose waves him over.
*   *“Sit,”* she says. *“You should hear this too.”*
*   Charlie sits on a pool chair with Lÿsa Mandy curled against his chest. Kiley Rose looks at him and then at Fallon.
*   *“I have been protecting her since she was twelve,”* Kiley Rose says. *“I built a wall around her. I checked every room. I watched every stranger. That was my job. But it is not my job anymore.”*
*   She looks at Charlie. *“It is yours now. Not because you are a man. Not because you are older. Because you chose her. And you chose Lÿsa. That is the only reason you get to stand in front of her.”*
*   Charlie nods. *“I know.”*
*   Kiley Rose takes a breath. *“Then I’m going to try to stop standing in front of her. It will take me a while.”*
*   Fallon reaches over and takes Kiley Rose’s hand. They do not say anything. The water moves around their feet.

**The Farewell and Good Luck:**
*   The house is starting to wake up. Lÿsa Mandy climbs down from Charlie’s lap and walks over to the edge of the pool. She sits between Fallon and Kiley Rose and puts her feet in the water too, copying them exactly.
*   Kiley Rose signs to her — a small, checking-in sign she learned from Sabrina: *“Okay?”* Lÿsa Mandy nods.
*   Kiley Rose turns to Fallon. She does not hug her right away. She looks at her for a long moment, the way she used to look at her in Rio when she was deciding whether Fallon was strong enough to walk through a crowd.
*   Then she pulls Fallon into a hard, brief hug and whispers, *“Farewell and good luck, Fallon Glenda.”*
*   Fallon whispers back, *“Farewell and good luck.”*
*   Kiley Rose pulls back, wiping her face with the sleeve of her hoodie. *“Go to California. Be a doctor. Be happy. Let him love you. I will be here when you come back.”*
*   She stands up, touches Lÿsa Mandy’s head once, and walks out of the pool room without looking back. Fallon stays at the edge of the water, holding the envelope, watching her sister go.

## WHY THIS SCENE MATTERS

*   **Kiley Rose’s Release:** The Rio protector is learning to let go. This is the emotional climax of her arc with Fallon — she has to accept that the fortress she built is no longer hers to guard.
*   **The Water as Symbol:** The pool scene revisits Fallon’s identity as a swimmer without reenacting the trauma. It gives her permission to reclaim the water on her own terms, in her own time.
*   **The Photograph:** The NICU photo is the most personal gift because it captures the moment Fallon became a mother. Kiley Rose gives it back to her as proof that she was already enough.
*   **Charlie’s Assumption of the Role:** Kiley Rose does not test Charlie the way the others do. She simply transfers the guardianship to him. This is the moment the older sister formally steps back and the partner steps forward.
*   **Lÿsa Mandy at the Water:** The image of Lÿsa Mandy sitting between her mother and her aunt, putting her feet in the water, mirrors the next generation inheriting the family’s rituals of comfort and courage.
