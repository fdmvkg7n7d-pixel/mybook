# SCENE OUTLINE: JESSICA ISABELLA SAYS GOODBYE TO FALLON GLENDA

**Characters:** Fallon Glenda (17), Charlie (18), Lÿsa Mandy (4–5), Jessica Isabella (mid-20s).
**Setting:** The back terrace of the Carlisle mansion, early evening. The rest of the household is inside, giving the two sisters the terrace.
**Timeframe:** Late June, days before the Stanford move.

## THE CONTEXT
Jessica Isabella has traveled from Halifax to say goodbye to Fallon before the move to California. She is the eldest of the three sisters who have already left home, and she carries the particular authority of someone who has already crossed the threshold Fallon is about to cross. This is a private goodbye, not a family performance.

## THE SCENE

**The Terrace, Alone:**
*   Fallon has been avoiding the goodbyes by staying busy — packing, checking lists, moving Lÿsa’s blocks from one corner to another. Jessica Isabella finds her on the terrace and does not announce herself. She simply sits down in the chair across from Fallon and waits.
*   Fallon eventually stops pretending to sort papers. She sits down too. The evening is warm and the crickets are loud enough to fill the silence.

**Jessica Isabella’s Opening:**
*   Jessica Isabella speaks first. She does not offer small talk. *“I came back because I wanted to be the one to tell you that leaving is not the same as running away. I needed someone to tell me that. I’m telling you now.”*
*   Fallon looks at her hands. *“I feel like I’m abandoning everyone.”*
*   Jessica Isabella shakes her head. *“You are not abandoning us. You are extending us. Halifax, Philadelphia, New York, Toronto, and now California. We are becoming a map. That is not a failure. That is what we were supposed to become.”*

**The Gift:**
*   Jessica Isabella reaches into her bag and pulls out a small leather jewellery pouch. Inside is a thin silver chain with a tiny compass pendant. She places it in Fallon’s palm and closes her fingers over it.
*   *“This is not so you can find your way back,”* Jessica says. *“It is so you always know which direction you are going. That is different.”*
*   Fallon fastens the chain around her neck. It sits just above the neckline of her shirt. She does not take it off.

**Lÿsa Mandy and the Aunt:**
*   Lÿsa Mandy comes out onto the terrace holding two of her blocks. She stops at the threshold and looks at Jessica Isabella. She does not approach until Jessica Isabella says, calmly, *“Hello, Lÿsa. I like your blocks.”*
*   Lÿsa Mandy walks over and places one of the blocks in Jessica Isabella’s hand — a green one, the colour she only gives to people she has decided are safe. Jessica Isabella accepts it as if it is a jewel.
*   *“I will come visit you in California,”* Jessica Isabella tells her. *“And you will show me your new room.”* Lÿsa Mandy nods once and returns to the kitchen.

**The Farewell and Good Luck:**
*   Jessica Isabella stands and pulls Fallon into a hug. It is long and steady, the way a mother hugs a child she is sending into the world. When she pulls back, she holds Fallon’s face in both hands.
*   *“Farewell and good luck, Fallon Glenda,”* she says. *“You have already survived the hardest thing. The rest is just geography.”*
*   Fallon nods, unable to speak. Jessica Isabella kisses her forehead and walks back into the house without looking back, because she knows Fallon needs to cry without an audience.

## WHY THIS SCENE MATTERS

*   **Jessica Isabella’s Authority:** As the eldest sister who has already left home, she is the only one who can credibly tell Fallon that distance is not abandonment.
*   **The Compass Gift:** The compass is not sentimental; it is practical. It gives Fallon permission to move forward rather than simply find her way back.
*   **Lÿsa Manda’s Inclusion:** The green block is a four-year-old’s acceptance ritual, and Jessica Isabella receives it with gravity. This shows how the next generation is already being folded into the family’s language of trust.
*   **The Family as a Map:** Jessica Isabella’s line about the family becoming a map reframes the scattered sisters as a deliberate expansion rather than a dispersal.
