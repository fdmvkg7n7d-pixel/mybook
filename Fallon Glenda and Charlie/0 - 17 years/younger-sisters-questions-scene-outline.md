# The Younger Sisters' Questions — Scene Outline

> **Scope:** A few weeks after arriving at the Carlisle mansion. Mike Key has taken the younger girls—Sabrina Bryna (14), Andrea Jennifer (10), and Ashley Erin (8)—to the mansion's private indoor pool for a swim while Fallon Glenda rests. After their swim, sitting on the warm pool deck, the girls turn to Mike with a barrage of unfiltered questions about Fallon's recently disclosed pregnancy. This scene captures the deeply endearing, innocent curiosity of the younger sisters, and Mike's steady, honest parenting as he answers them without exposing the trauma.
>
> **Time:** Early Summer 2045. A few weeks after returning to Ontario.
> **Place:** The private indoor pool at the Carlisle mansion.
> **Characters present:** Mike Key, Sabrina Bryna (14), Andrea Jennifer (10), Ashley Erin (8).
> **Mood:** Honest, slightly chaotic, deeply heartwarming, and grounding.

---

## 1. The Pool Deck

- **The setting:** It is a rainy summer afternoon in Carlisle. The family's indoor pool is a place of high energy and normal childhood activity. Sabrina Bryna, Andrea Jennifer, and Ashley Erin have just finished swimming. They are sitting on the edge of the heated pool deck, the ambient temperature of the room keeping them perfectly warm as they kick their legs in the water.
- **Mike's presence:** Mike is sitting in a deck chair nearby, keeping a watchful eye on them. The atmosphere is relaxed. Fallon is resting upstairs; this is a pocket of normalcy for the younger girls.
- **The pivot:** The girls have recently been told by Fallon that she is pregnant. Because they are young, their reaction is not one of adult horror, but of intense, unfiltered curiosity. Without warning, they pivot their attention from the pool to their father.

## 2. The Barrage of Questions

- **Ashley Erin (8) — The Direct Interrogator:** Ashley Erin begins firing off the most direct, innocent questions possible to Mike.
  - *"Dad, is Fallon having twins like Kiley Rose?"*
  - *"Is it going to be a boy or a girl?"*
  - *"Can it sleep in my room when it gets here?"*
  - *"Did she eat a seed? How did the baby get in there?"* 
- **Andrea Jennifer (10) — The Logistical Observer:** Andrea Jennifer is the quiet thinker. Her questions are highly practical, directed at Mike as the ultimate organizer of the family.
  - *"Is she still going to do homeschooling with us?"*
  - *"Where is the baby going to sit at the kitchen table? Do we need a new chair?"*
  - *"Does this mean Fallon can't ride horses this summer?"*
- **Sabrina Bryna (14) — The Emotional Core:** Sabrina Bryna does not ask about logistics. She watches Mike’s face. She signs, slowly and deliberately: *"Is Fallon scared?"*

## 3. Mike’s Answers

- **The steady father:** Mike does not shut down the questions or tell them they are too young to understand. He treats their curiosity with absolute respect. He answers them one by one, keeping his tone calm and factual.
- **Answering Ashley Erin:** He tells Ashley Erin that Fallon is only having one baby, they don't know if it's a boy or a girl yet, and that the baby will likely sleep near Fallon for a while. 
- **The biological intercept:** When Ashley Erin asks *how* the baby got there, Mike smoothly intercepts. He gives a brief, age-appropriate answer about how babies grow, completely shielding the younger girls from the horrific reality of the swimming teacher. He holds the trauma so they don't have to.
- **Answering Andrea Jennifer:** He confirms Fallon will still do school with them. He smiles at the chair question and promises Andrea Jennifer they will absolutely get a new chair for the kitchen table. He admits Fallon won't be able to ride horses for a while, but she can still visit the stables.
- **Answering Sabrina Bryna:** Mike looks at his fourteen-year-old daughter. He doesn't sugarcoat it. He signs back honestly: *"Yes. A little bit. But she is brave, and we are all going to help her."*

## 4. The Protective Mandate

- **The instruction:** Once the logistical and biological questions are answered, Mike leans forward, resting his elbows on his knees. He gives the girls a very clear, simple instruction regarding their sister.
- **Normalcy as a shield:** *"Your job,"* Mike tells them, *"is exactly what you're doing right now. You treat her like your sister. You don't tiptoe around her. You ask her to play, you talk to her, and if she's tired, you let her rest."*
- **The agreement:** The girls nod. They understand practical instructions. They don't view the pregnancy as a fragile crisis; they view it as a family project they are now part of.

## 5. The Dinner Table

- **Later that evening:** The family gathers for dinner at the large Carlisle kitchen table. Fallon, having rested, comes downstairs. She is still carrying a quiet, residual anxiety about how her body will change and how her sisters will view her.
- **The instruction in action:** The younger girls immediately put Mike's mandate into practice. They do not tiptoe. They do not look at her with pity. 
- **The hug:** Before Fallon even sits down, Ashley Erin runs over and throws her arms around Fallon’s waist in a tight, uncomplicated hug. Andrea Jennifer follows, hugging her from the side. Sabrina Bryna walks over, puts a hand on Fallon's shoulder, and smiles warmly. 
- **The grounding:** It is not a solemn, tragic hug. It is the ordinary, chaotic physical affection of a large family. For Fallon, who was terrified of being touched or seen, the unhesitating, normal hugs of her sisters dismantle the last lingering pieces of her shame.

## 6. The Closing Image

- **The return to childhood:** The serious conversation at the pool has completely dissolved into the warmth of the evening. At the table, Ashley Erin loudly demands to know what they are having for dessert.
- **The exhale:** Mike and Terri Anne share a quiet look across the table. They are carrying the crushing weight of the international legal battle and Fallon's medical care, but right here, at the kitchen table, their younger daughters are doing exactly what they needed them to do: being normal, loving sisters.

## 6. Character Work

### Mike Key
- **The Ultimate Filter:** Mike masterfully filters the horrific reality of Fallon's situation into something his 8, 10, and 14-year-old daughters can safely process. He answers their questions honestly without ever exposing them to the darkness of the Rio trauma.
- **The Family General:** He gives them a clear "job" (treat her normally), deputizing them in Fallon's recovery process.

### Ashley Erin (8)
- **The Innocent Catalyst:** Her complete lack of filter highlights her absolute trust in Mike to answer her questions. Her pivot back to wanting to do a cannonball shows that she is secure and unburdened.

### Andrea Jennifer (10)
- **The Pragmatist:** Her concern is how the family's physical and daily logistics will adapt. She doesn't see a tragedy; she sees a need for a new chair at the table, trusting Mike to provide it.

### Sabrina Bryna (14)
- **The Silent Empath:** Her single question (*"Is Fallon scared?"*) cuts to the emotional truth of the situation, and she trusts Mike to give her a straight answer.

---

## 7. Thematic Motifs

- **The Pool as Safe Space:** The pool at Carlisle is a place of childhood joy, contrasting sharply with the trauma Fallon experienced with the swimming teacher in Rio. Here, Mike reclaims the water as a safe environment for his younger daughters.
- **The Kitchen Table/Family Logistics:** Andrea Jennifer asking where the baby will sit reinforces the Key family ethos: every child, no matter how they arrive, has a permanent, unshakeable place at the family table.
- **Normalcy as a Shield:** Mike instructing the girls to simply treat Fallon normally shows how the Key family uses ordinary routine as a powerful psychological buffer against trauma.
