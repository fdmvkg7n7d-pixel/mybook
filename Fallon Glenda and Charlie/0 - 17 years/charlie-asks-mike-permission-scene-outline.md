# SCENE OUTLINE: CHARLIE ASKS MIKE FOR FALLON'S HAND

**Characters:** Charlie (18), Mike Key, and (briefly) Lÿsa Mandy (4).
**Setting:** Mike's private home office at the Carlisle mansion.
**Timeframe:** Shortly before Fallon (17) and Charlie (18) embark on their cross-country road trip to Stanford. 

## THE CONTEXT & LIVING SITUATION
*   Fallon Glenda (17), Charlie (18), and little Lÿsa Mandy (4) are preparing to leave the immense protective bubble of the Carlisle mansion for Stanford University. 
*   Charlie is currently **living with the Key family at Carlisle**. When his own parents rejected his relationship with a young teen mother and her child, Mike and Terri Anne did not hesitate: they took him in. Charlie has been living under their roof, proving his steadfast loyalty every single day.
*   Before they pack the car for their road trip to California, Charlie knows he needs to speak to Mike. He wants to marry Fallon. He knows they are incredibly young by normal standards, but Fallon’s life hasn't been normal, and Charlie's devotion to her and her daughter is absolute. He wants to leave for Stanford not just as her boyfriend, but with a formalized commitment to her father that he will protect her.

## THE SCENE

**The Home Office:**
*   Charlie finds Mike exactly where he hoped to: sitting at his desk in his private home office at the Carlisle mansion, working quietly at his computer monitors.
*   Fallon is out of the room (perhaps upstairs packing). Four-year-old Lÿsa Mandy is nearby on the plush office rug, completely hyper-focused on lining up a complex sequence of wooden blocks, organizing them by a pattern only she understands. (At this stage, the family does not yet know she has Asperger Syndrome; she is simply Lÿsa Mandy, with her own intense, specific way of moving through the world).

**The Invitation to Sit:**
*   Mike looks up from his computer screens, immediately registering the eighteen-year-old's nervous but resolute posture in the doorway. He takes his hands off the keyboard and gives Charlie his full attention. 
*   *"Come in, Charlie. Sit down."* 
*   Charlie crosses the room and takes a seat across from the patriarch's heavy desk. Mike isn't just his girlfriend's billionaire father anymore; Mike is the man who gave him a home when his own parents turned him away.

**The Pitch & The Debt:**
*   Charlie doesn't prevaricate. He looks Mike in the eye and tells him exactly why he is there: he intends to ask Fallon Glenda to marry him, and he intends to formally adopt four-year-old Lÿsa Mandy as his own daughter. 
*   Charlie speaks plainly. He addresses the obvious hurdle right away: *"I know we're young, sir. I know I'm only eighteen and she's seventeen. But she’s had to live a whole lifetime already, and so have I since the day I met her."*
*   He also acknowledges what Mike has done for him. *"You took me in when my own family didn't want me. You let me be part of this family. I want to make it permanent."*
*   Charlie promises the one currency Mike values above all else: physical and emotional presence. 
*   *"I know I can't build her a fortress like you did. But when we go to Stanford, I promise you, I will never leave the room. When it's hard, when the anxiety comes back, when Lÿsa Mandy needs more than we know how to give... I will stay. I will always stay."*

**The Religious Requirement (Greek Orthodox):**
*   Charlie then lays out one specific, non-negotiable requirement for the marriage. Despite his estrangement from his parents, his own faith and heritage remain foundational to who he is. 
*   He tells Mike that both Fallon Glenda and Lÿsa Mandy are required to change their religion and be baptized into the **Greek Orthodox** faith. 
*   Mike does not flinch at this. In fact, Mike respects it—he values men who have deep structural roots and core convictions. 

**The Silence and The Patriarch's Assessment:**
*   Mike does not answer immediately. He lets a heavy, deliberate silence stretch across the office. He looks at Charlie, evaluating the eighteen-year-old boy sitting before him. 
*   Mike thinks about the bowling date two years ago. He thinks about the hours Charlie has spent just sitting with Fallon when she was overwhelmed. He thinks about how Charlie has never once flinched at the trauma Fallon carries, nor the complexities of raising a child with such intensely specific needs. 

**The Interruption (The 4-Year-Old Anchor):**
*   Before Mike speaks, little Lÿsa Mandy (4) stops her block-stacking on the rug. She pads over to where the two men are sitting. She doesn't seek out Mike; she walks straight to Charlie.
*   She immediately sits down squarely on Charlie's right leg. 
*   Without missing a beat or breaking eye contact with Mike, Charlie instinctively adjusts his posture to support her. He naturally unclahses his watch from his wrist and hands it to her—he knows the weight and ticking of the watch soothes her—resting his other hand protectively on her back. Anchoring Lÿsa Mandy is simply an automatic reflex for him.

**The Blessing:**
*   Mike watches this seamless interaction. His severe expression finally breaks into a quiet, deeply moved smile. Seeing an eighteen-year-old boy instinctively and flawlessly anchor his granddaughter is exactly what he needed to see.
*   Mike leans forward, resting his massive hands on his desk. 
*   *"You don't need to build her a fortress, Charlie. That was my job. Your job is to be the home she comes back to."*
*   Mike gives Charlie his absolute, unwavering blessing, including his full support for the Greek Orthodox conversion. 

**The Logistics (The Mike Key Way):**
*   Once the emotional weight settles, Mike immediately shifts into his practical, patriarchal mode. 
*   He tells Charlie that whenever they decide to actually have the wedding, Key Corporation will handle the security and logistics. 
*   More importantly for right now, he smoothly mentions that his lawyers will help draw up the formal adoption papers for Lÿsa Mandy. He wants Charlie legally recognized as her father before they establish their life in California.

**The Departure & The Goodbye:**
*   Charlie stands up, feeling the immense relief of having passed the final test. He shakes Mike's hand—a firm, equal handshake across the desk between two men who love the same woman. 
*   Charlie seamlessly bends down and scoops Lÿsa Mandy up into his arms, carrying her against his chest. 
*   Before they leave the room, Charlie looks down at the four-year-old he is holding. *"Say goodbye to your grandfather, Lÿsa."*
*   Lÿsa Mandy looks at Mike from the safety of Charlie's arms. *"Goodbye, Grandpa."* (A small, deliberate acknowledgment). 
*   *"Goodbye, Lÿsa Mandy,"* Mike replies softly. 
*   As Charlie carries Lÿsa Mandy out of the office, Mike turns back to his computer monitors, knowing with absolute certainty that when his seventeen-year-old daughter leaves for Stanford, she will be safe. 

**Finding Fallon Glenda:**
*   Carrying Lÿsa Mandy securely against his chest (the four-year-old still clutching his watch), Charlie navigates the halls of the Carlisle mansion to find Fallon.
*   He locates her in one of the family sitting rooms. They are not packing in this room; it is a quiet domestic moment. Fallon is sitting with her mother, Terri Anne, and her two younger sisters, Andrea Jennifer (15) and Ashley Erin (13). 
*   They are all drinking bowls of Terri Anne's freshly whisked matcha green tea. They are discussing the impending move to San Francisco. Andrea Jennifer and Ashley Erin are completely familiar with California (Ashley Erin spends extensive time in Los Angeles filming *This Land*), so their conversation is less about tourist questions and more about the logistical reality of Fallon establishing her own household on the West Coast.
*   Charlie pauses in the doorway for a moment, just watching the seventeen-year-old woman he is going to marry. 

**The Proposal:**
*   Charlie doesn't wait for a perfectly staged romantic sunset. Their love has always been built in the middle of real, complicated life. He walks into the sitting room, still holding four-year-old Lÿsa Mandy in his arms. 
*   The conversation lulls as he approaches. Terri Anne looks at Charlie, sees the absolute certainty in his posture, and immediately knows where he just came from. She calmly sets her matcha bowl down on the low table.
*   Charlie stands in front of Fallon. He doesn't drop to one knee—he is holding their daughter, which is exactly how it should be. 
*   He speaks directly and honestly to Fallon in front of her mother and sisters. He tells her that he just spoke with her father. He tells her that Key Corporation lawyers are drawing up the papers for him to legally adopt Lÿsa Mandy. 
*   He then brings up the structural requirement of his faith: they must be baptized Greek Orthodox, which involves full physical immersion in water. They agree that this will take place in **San Francisco** in a **private ceremony** once they settle into their new life.
*   Fallon processes the physical reality of this. For a survivor of a water-based trauma in Rio, stepping into a baptismal font is a massive, terrifying ask. She also knows the religious tradition: young children like Lÿsa Mandy are baptized naked. Knowing her daughter's intensely specific sensory needs, Fallon realizes that the water and exposure could terrify the four-year-old. 
*   In a moment of staggering maternal courage, Fallon makes an immediate decision about their future San Francisco baptism. To ensure Lÿsa Mandy is calm, comfortable, and doesn't feel alone, Fallon states that she will not wear the adult baptismal robe. She will be baptized completely naked alongside her daughter in that private ceremony. She chooses to face her deepest trauma entirely exposed, stripping away her armor so her child will feel perfectly safe.
*   Then, holding her daughter, Charlie asks her to marry him. 
*   Fallon is deeply moved by the lack of pretense and the profound security of his promise. She doesn't hesitate. She sets her own tea down, stands up, and wraps her arms around both Charlie and Lÿsa Mandy, saying yes.
*   Andrea Jennifer and Ashley Erin immediately burst into excited, loud teenage celebrations, abandoning their tea to hug them. 
*   Terri Anne watches with a quiet, fierce pride, seeing her traumatized daughter fully stepping into a joyful, secure future. 
*   As the excitement settles slightly, Terri Anne steps forward. She gently touches her daughter's arm and offers exactly what they need in that moment: *"I have her."* Terri Anne offers to take full care of Lÿsa Mandy for the rest of the evening, explicitly clearing the path for Fallon and Charlie to retreat to their bedroom and celebrate privately, just the two of them, before the massive reality of Stanford begins.

**The Next Morning (The Private Kitchen):**
*   The following morning, four-year-old Lÿsa Mandy wakes up to a comforting, grounding sensory detail: the smell of homemade blueberry pancakes.
*   She wanders out of her room and into the private kitchen of Fallon and Charlie's suite within the Carlisle mansion. 
*   Charlie is already awake and standing at the stove, cooking the pancakes from scratch. It is a quiet, beautiful morning-after moment—showing Charlie immediately stepping into the domestic, caretaking role of a father and husband, building a warm, anchoring home for them even before they pack the car for California. 

## WHY THIS SCENE MATTERS
*   **The Office Setting:** Bringing Charlie into Mike's private workspace highlights that Mike takes Charlie seriously as a man, an equal, and a permanent fixture in the family.
*   **Adoption & Faith:** It formally establishes Charlie's intent to legally adopt Lÿsa Mandy and introduces the Greek Orthodox conversion, cementing his own roots into their new family unit.
*   **Maternal Sacrifice & Healing:** Fallon choosing to be baptized naked to comfort Lÿsa Mandy is the ultimate triumph over her Rio trauma. She reclaims her body and the water not for herself, but to be an absolute shield for her daughter.
