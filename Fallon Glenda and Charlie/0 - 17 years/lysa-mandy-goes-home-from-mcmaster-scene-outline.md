# SCENE OUTLINE: LŸSA MANDY GOES HOME FROM MCMASTER

## Placement

**When:** The morning after Lÿsa Mandy's first successful direct nursing session and overnight observation, in January.

**Where:** McMaster Children's Hospital, Hamilton, Ontario → Carlisle mansion, Ontario.

**Continuity Lead-in:**
- Lÿsa Mandy has spent weeks in the NICU after her difficult birth.
- Fallon Glenda has just successfully nursed her for the first time.
- The doctors told Fallon the day before that if Lÿsa Mandy remained stable overnight, she could take her home in the morning.
- Fallon has already been given the Christmas promise of the **west wing at Carlisle** as home for her and Lÿsa Mandy.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene documents the discharge morning: the moment Fallon Glenda stops being a hospital mother moving between incubator glass, pumping schedules, and visiting rules, and becomes a mother bringing her daughter through the front door of the home waiting for her.

---

## Emotional Core

The scene is about **release into real life**. The crisis is not erased, but the hospital finally loosens its grip. Fallon does not merely survive the NICU chapter; she carries Lÿsa Mandy out of it.

---

## Tone

Tender, fragile, exhausted, and quietly triumphant.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — The Morning Check
- Fallon wakes after a thin, anxious night. Even though the doctors told her the night before that Lÿsa Mandy could go home if she stayed stable, Fallon does not fully believe it until morning rounds.
- The room is different now: no sense of crisis, but intense vigilance.
- A nurse checks:
  - overnight vitals,
  - weight,
  - temperature,
  - breathing,
  - feeding tolerance after the first direct nursing,
  - discharge readiness.
- Fallon watches every face, trying to read the answer before anyone says it aloud.

### Beat 2 — The Doctors Confirm It
- The doctors come in and tell Fallon that Lÿsa Mandy did well overnight.
- They officially clear her to go home.
- The language is clinical, but to Fallon it sounds almost unreal.

Possible line from the doctor:

> *"She had a good night. You can take your daughter home today, Fallon."*

- Fallon goes still before she reacts. She has spent so long bracing for more waiting that the absence of delay shocks her.
- She may cry immediately, or simply nod because the feeling is too large for words.

### Beat 3 — Mike Handles the Practical World
- Mike takes over the logistics the way he always does:
  - discharge paperwork,
  - prescriptions or follow-up instructions,
  - feeding guidance,
  - return precautions,
  - the car seat,
  - the timing of getting Fallon and Lÿsa Mandy out without chaos.
- His steadiness matters because Fallon is emotionally flooded.
- He does not make the moment bureaucratic; he quietly removes every obstacle between Fallon and the door.

### Beat 4 — Fallon Dresses Lÿsa Mandy to Leave
- Fallon helps dress Lÿsa Mandy in her going-home clothes.
- The tiny outfit matters because it is not hospital-issued and not for an incubator. It is for the outside world.
- Fallon may say Lÿsa Mandy's name over and over while dressing her, as if naming her helps reality settle.
- This is the first time Fallon dresses her daughter for home rather than for a ward.
- If desired, the **toy giraffe** from Christmas reappears here: Fallon tucks it beside the blanket or tells Lÿsa Mandy quietly that now she really gets to bring her giraffe home.

### Beat 5 — Saying Goodbye to the NICU
- Fallon looks around the room that has held so much fear:
  - monitors,
  - the chair where she pumped,
  - the place where she first held Lÿsa Mandy,
  - the space where she first nursed her.
- A nurse or doctor may say something kind and practical, acknowledging the work Fallon has done.

Possible line from a nurse:

> *"You did not miss a day. She knows your voice."*

- Fallon does not need a speech. The emotional point is that the staff now see what Mike insisted on from the beginning: she is the mother.

### Beat 6 — The Car Seat
- Mike carries the car seat out when the time comes, or helps secure it while Fallon stays close.
- Fallon walks beside him rather than behind him. This matters visually: she is not being escorted away from the hospital as a child; she is leaving it as Lÿsa Mandy's mother.
- The ordinary mechanics of departure — straps, blankets, elevator doors, winter air — feel enormous because they were once unimaginable.

### Beat 7 — The Ride to Carlisle
- The drive is quiet.
- Fallon keeps looking back at Lÿsa Mandy, listening for every tiny sound.
- Mike drives more carefully than he has ever driven anything.
- The world outside the window feels changed simply because Lÿsa Mandy is in it now.
- Fallon realizes that for the first time, there is no return trip scheduled back to the NICU that day.

### Beat 8 — Arrival at Carlisle
- Carlisle is waiting.
- Terri Anne has the west wing ready:
  - a prepared nursery,
  - clean blankets,
  - a crib or bassinet,
  - somewhere for Fallon to rest and nurse,
  - the extra room for whichever family member is helping overnight.
- The homecoming should be warm but controlled. No one swarms Fallon.
- The younger sisters may be waiting under clear instructions to be gentle and quiet.
- Fallon crosses the threshold carrying or following the car seat and understands that the room promised to her at Christmas now has its child in it.

### Beat 9 — The First Settling-In
- Fallon sits down with Lÿsa Mandy in the west wing and feels the silence of home after weeks of hospital noise.
- The silence may frighten her for a moment because she is used to monitors, then soothe her once she realizes Lÿsa Mandy is simply breathing beside her.
- If desired, Fallon nurses her again at home, completing the transition from hospital success to domestic life.
- Terri Anne checks on both of them. Mike lingers in the doorway or sets down the final bag and lets the room belong to Fallon.

### Beat 10 — Closing Image
- Fallon places Lÿsa Mandy in the crib or keeps her sleeping on her chest for a long while, unwilling to set her down yet.
- The **toy giraffe** is now finally allowed to stay beside the baby.
- Closing image options:
  - Fallon in the nursing chair in the west wing, Lÿsa Mandy asleep against her, no machines in sight.
  - Mike setting the empty car seat down by the nursery door and pausing, overwhelmed.
  - Terri Anne adjusting a blanket and then stepping back, letting Fallon have the room.
  - Fallon whispering to Lÿsa Mandy: *"You're home, my giraffe."*
