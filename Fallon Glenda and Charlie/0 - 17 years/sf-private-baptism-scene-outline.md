# SCENE OUTLINE: THE PRIVATE BAPTISM IN SAN FRANCISCO

**Characters:** Fallon Glenda (18), Charlie (19), Lÿsa Mandy (4), Mike Key, Terri Anne, Andrea Jennifer (16), Ashley Erin (14), Charlie's cousins (the sponsors), and the Greek Orthodox Priest.
**Setting:** A beautiful, dimly lit Greek Orthodox Church in San Francisco. 
**Timeframe:** Shortly after arriving in California and settling into their new life.

## THE CONTEXT
Following through on the promise Charlie made to Mike Key back at the Carlisle mansion, Fallon and four-year-old Lÿsa Mandy are being baptized into the Greek Orthodox Church. To accommodate Fallon's severe trauma surrounding water (from her assault in Rio) and Lÿsa Mandy's sensory needs, Charlie has arranged for a strictly private ceremony. The massive church is entirely empty except for their core family—Mike, Terri Anne, Andrea Jennifer, Ashley Erin, and Charlie's cousins who have agreed to act as their Orthodox sponsors (Godparents).

## THE SCENE

**The Empty Sanctuary & The Family:**
*   The scene opens inside the cavernous, quiet space of a San Francisco Greek Orthodox Church. The air smells faintly of beeswax and incense. 
*   Mike, Terri Anne, Andrea Jennifer, and Ashley Erin are standing in the pews, watching with quiet, profound respect. Mike is watching Charlie, deeply satisfied that the boy is honoring both his faith and his daughter.
*   Charlie stands near the large baptismal font with two of his cousins, who have remained loyal to him despite his parents' rejection. They are serving as the official Koumbaroi (sponsors) for Fallon and Lÿsa Mandy.

**The Preparation (Stripping the Armor):**
*   The priest says the initial prayers of exorcism and acceptance of the faith. 
*   Then comes the physical reality of the sacrament. Fallon is terrified. Her body remembers the water in Rio. Her body remembers the vulnerability of a swimming pool. But she looks at her four-year-old daughter. 
*   True to her promise in the Carlisle sitting room, Fallon does not hide. In front of her father, her mother, her sisters, and her future husband, she simply undresses herself, and she undresses Lÿsa Mandy. 
*   She stands completely naked beside the font, holding her naked daughter against her chest. She is not ashamed. She is a mother acting as an absolute, physical shield for her child. Her family watches not with shock, but with absolute awe at her strength.

**The Font & The Water:**
*   They step into the warm water of the baptismal font together. 
*   The sensory shock of the water hits Lÿsa Mandy, and she immediately starts to panic—but Fallon holds her skin-to-skin, murmuring softly, completely neutralizing the child's sensory overload with her own steady, familiar heartbeat. 
*   Charlie watches from the edge of the font. He sees exactly what it is costing Fallon to be in that water, and his eyes are filled with absolute reverence for his future wife.

**The Triple Immersion:**
*   The priest understands the gravity of the moment and moves with gentle, deliberate care. 
*   *“The servant of God, Lÿsa Mandy, is baptized in the name of the Father. And of the Son. And of the Holy Spirit.”* 
*   Fallon helps guide Lÿsa Mandy through the three immersions, keeping her close so the water feels like an embrace rather than a shock.
*   Then, it is Fallon’s turn. *“The servant of God, Fallon Glenda...”*
*   Fallon takes a breath and submerges herself completely, three times. Under the water, the ghosts of Rio wash away. She reclaims the water. It is no longer a place of violation; it is the place where she formally joins her life to Charlie’s and secures her daughter's future. 

**The White Towels & The Chrismation:**
*   As soon as they step out of the font, Charlie is right there waiting. He wraps massive, warm white towels tightly around both of them, pulling them into a fierce, protective embrace. 
*   Right there by the font, they are dried and dressed in pure white garments—a small white dress for Lÿsa Mandy, and a simple, flowing white dress for Fallon.
*   They return to the priest for Chrismation. He anoints their foreheads, eyes, ears, chests, hands, and feet with Holy Myrrh, sealing them with the Holy Spirit. Lÿsa Mandy is fascinated by the smell of the myrrh.

**The Walk Around the Font:**
*   Holding lit white candles, Charlie, Fallon, Lÿsa Mandy, and Charlie's cousins walk in a circle around the font three times, symbolizing their first steps in their new, unified life. 
*   Lÿsa Mandy insists on holding Charlie's hand with her free hand. Mike watches his daughter walk around the font, radiating a fierce, quiet pride.

**The Celebration:**
*   Once the ceremony concludes, the heavy, holy silence of the church gives way to warm family celebration. 
*   Mike and Terri Anne take the entire family—Fallon, Charlie, Lÿsa Mandy, Andrea Jennifer, Ashley Erin, and Charlie's cousins—out to a beautiful, private dinner at a restaurant in San Francisco to celebrate the baptism. 
*   At dinner, the two families blend seamlessly. Charlie's cousins are welcomed into the Key family orbit, and Charlie sits beside his newly baptized future wife and daughter, having successfully built the foundation of their new life.
*   **Conquering Trauma:** It is the ultimate physical culmination of Fallon’s healing from Rio. She reclaims her nakedness and the water out of pure love for her daughter.
*   **The Follow-Through:** It shows Charlie honoring his faith and Fallon honoring her promise to Mike and Charlie. 
*   **Building Their Own Family:** Stripped of the billionaires and mansions, this quiet, holy moment in San Francisco solidifies the new nuclear family they are building on the West Coast.
