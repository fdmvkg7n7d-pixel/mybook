## Scene Beats

- The scene begins in their Oregon State University dormitory room or just after leaving it.
- Lÿsa Mandy and Samantha have been assigned to the same dormitory room, which has made their friendship deepen through ordinary shared routines.
- The weather is not Hawaiian.
- It is Oregon: cooler, wetter, greener, and far from the bright reef water where they first met.
- They are comfortable enough with each other to talk without performing.
- Their friendship already has a rhythm.

### Beat 2: The Hawaii Detail

- Something brings up Hawaii.
- It may be a winter-break conversation, a childhood vacation story, a photograph, a dolphin-related topic, a computer wallpaper, or a casual mention of Aulani.
- Lÿsa Mandy says she went to Aulani as a little girl in December.
- Samantha says she did too.
- At first, the overlap feels like coincidence.
- Then the details start becoming too specific.

### Beat 3: Rainbow Reef

- One of them mentions **Rainbow Reef**.
- The other immediately reacts.
- They both remember snorkeling there.
- They both remember tropical fish, warm pool deck, bright sun, and the strange quiet of communicating underwater.
- One of them mentions a bright yellow tang fish.
- The other remembers that exact fish or that exact moment.
- The conversation slows down because both of them feel the recognition approaching before they can prove it.

### Beat 4: The Little Girls

- Lÿsa Mandy remembers a confident little girl who swam over to show her a fish.
- Samantha remembers a serious, observant little girl snorkeling with her parents.
- Lÿsa remembers Fallon and Charlie nearby.
- Samantha remembers being there with her own parents.
- They begin comparing ages, dates, and details.
- They realize they are describing each other.
- They were the little girls at Rainbow Reef.

### Beat 5: Nearly Thirteen Years Ago

- They count backward.
- The memory was in December.
- It was nearly thirteen years ago.
- The phrase lands oddly because childhood memories usually feel either yesterday or impossibly far away.
- Lÿsa Mandy says it out loud: nearly thirteen years ago December.
- Samantha repeats it or sits with it.
- They both understand that the timeline is real.
- They met as children and then found each other again at Oregon State.

### Beat 6: The Feeling of Recognition

- The realization makes the room feel briefly unreal.
- Lÿsa Mandy may become very quiet because memory and pattern matter deeply to her.
- Samantha may laugh first because the coincidence is too large to hold seriously all at once.
- Then the laughter softens.
- They are not strangers who became friends by chance only.
- They are also a childhood echo returning.
- The recognition gives their friendship a new layer.

### Beat 7: What They Remember Differently

- They discover that they remember different parts of the same day.
- Lÿsa remembers the water, the fish, the feeling of being overwhelmed and then steadied by a friend in the reef.
- Samantha remembers showing the fish, the warmth, and the ease of swimming beside someone who did not need too much talking.
- Lÿsa remembers Fallon and Charlie letting the day unfold gently.
- Samantha remembers her parents and the relaxed island feeling.
- Their memories do not match perfectly.
- That makes the recognition more real, not less.

### Beat 8: Why It Matters

- Lÿsa Mandy understands that Samantha was part of one of her earliest Hawaii memories.
- Samantha understands that Lÿsa was not only a university friend who happened to love the ocean.
- They had once shared a small, wordless childhood moment in the water.
- Now they are sharing a dormitory room without having known the older connection.
- The realization connects Oregon State to Hawaii.
- It connects childhood to adulthood.
- It connects the future zoologist studying dolphins to the little girl who watched fish at Rainbow Reef.
- It connects Samantha’s technical, logical mind to a coincidence too beautiful to reduce to data.

### Beat 9: Calling or Telling the Parents

- They may decide to tell Fallon and Charlie.
- Lÿsa knows Fallon will remember the Aulani trip.
- Charlie may remember Samantha because of the way Lÿsa talked about the little girl afterward.
- Samantha may want to ask her own parents if they remember the Key family or the girl at Rainbow Reef.
- The adults’ memories can confirm the edges later.
- In this scene, the girls do not need full confirmation to know what they have found.

### Beat 10: The First Emotional Shift

- The realization does not instantly turn friendship into romance.
- It does make the relationship feel more significant.
- They become more careful with each other for a moment.
- They are holding a shared past neither of them knew they had.
- Lÿsa Mandy may say that Hawaii always felt important to her.
- Samantha may say that maybe it was important before they knew why.
- The line should feel gentle, not fated in a heavy-handed way.

### Beat 11: Final Image

- The final image is Lÿsa Mandy and Samantha sitting together at Oregon State with the December Aulani memory between them.
- Outside, Oregon is cool and wet.
- In their minds, Rainbow Reef is bright and warm.
- Nearly thirteen years ago, they were little girls pointing at fish in Hawaii.
- Now they are university students realizing they had already met.
- The future is not fully visible yet.
