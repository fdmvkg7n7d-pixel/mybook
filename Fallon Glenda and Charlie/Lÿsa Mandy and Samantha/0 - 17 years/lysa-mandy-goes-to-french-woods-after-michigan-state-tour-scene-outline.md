## Core Situation

- Lÿsa Mandy has just completed three major university tours.
- The tours have given her too much information to hold loosely.
- Because of her Asperger Syndrome, she needs structure, sequence, and criteria to make a major decision.
- French Woods gives her that structure.
- Camp is familiar: cabins, schedules, classes, counselors, meals, and repeatable routines.
- The departure is harder this year because Rebecca Brazilia and Charlie Janya finally understand that French Woods means Lÿsa Mandy will be gone for the whole summer.
- Inside that familiar rhythm, Lÿsa Mandy can sort the tours into a linear decision.
- Oregon State begins to become the clear choice.

## Scene Beats

### Beat 1: Leaving Michigan State

- The Michigan State tour ends.
- Lÿsa Mandy is tired from the sequence of campuses, questions, travel, and sensory input.
- She has notes from OSU, USC, and Michigan State.
- She has brochures, maps, answers, impressions, and questions still running in her mind.
- Fallon, Charlie, and Piper do not ask her to make the decision immediately.
- They know that would be too much.
- They remind her that French Woods is next.
- French Woods becomes the place where the decision can settle.

### Beat 2: Travel Toward French Woods

- The travel from Michigan State toward French Woods gives Lÿsa Mandy a transition space.
- She sits with her notes close by.
- She may organize the papers by school.
- She may put sticky notes on each packet.
- She may rewrite the same categories across three pages so the comparison becomes manageable.
- Fallon watches this without interrupting.
- Charlie handles the practical logistics.
- Piper helps keep the emotional atmosphere steady.

### Beat 3: The Three-School Stack

- Lÿsa Mandy creates or reviews a three-school stack.
- OSU has Behavior & Ecology answers, a quieter rhythm, water-adjacent possibility, and a pathway toward dolphin research.
- USC has opportunity, California proximity, intensity, and a large urban energy.
- Michigan State has research strength, animal science seriousness, scale, and distance.
- Lÿsa Mandy is not ranking them by prestige.
- She is ranking them by fit.
- Fit means whether her mind and body can function there every day.

### Beat 4: Rebecca and Charlie Janya Realize What French Woods Means

- Before or during the French Woods drop-off, Rebecca Brazilia and Charlie Janya become very emotional.
- They are about five years old.
- In earlier years, Lÿsa Mandy going away to camp may have felt vague or temporary to them.
- This year, for the first time, they understand that she will be away for the whole summer.
- The realization lands suddenly.
- Rebecca Brazilia may ask how many sleeps Lÿsa Mandy will be gone.
- Charlie Janya may ask whether Lÿsa Mandy is coming back before dinner, then understand from the adults’ faces that the answer is no.
- Both twins start crying or clinging to Lÿsa Mandy.
- Fallon and Charlie comfort the twins without making Lÿsa Mandy responsible for fixing their sadness.
- Piper helps keep the departure calm and practical.
- Lÿsa Mandy is moved by their reaction, even if the intensity of it is difficult for her to process.

### Beat 5: Arrival at French Woods

- Lÿsa Mandy arrives at French Woods with the university tours still alive inside her.
- French Woods is familiar in a way the campuses are not.
- She knows the rhythm of arrival.
- She knows where to put things.
- She knows how camp sounds and smells.
- She knows that the schedule will hold her.
- The familiarity helps her nervous system quiet down.
- The twins’ tears are still with her, but the camp structure gives the sadness edges.
- Fallon, Charlie, and Piper see the shift in her almost immediately.

### Beat 6: Camp Structure Returns

- French Woods gives Lÿsa Mandy a daily structure.
- Meals happen at known times.
- Activities have names and places.
- Counselors know how to move campers through transitions.
- The camp rhythm does not solve the university decision, but it gives her a container for it.
- Lÿsa Mandy can think because the day has edges.
- She can compare because the rest of the environment is not constantly changing.

### Beat 7: Behavior & Ecology Notes

- Lÿsa Mandy returns to her Behavior & Ecology notes from the OSU tour.
- She writes down the answers she was given.
- Biology.
- Ecology.
- Evolution.
- Animal behavior.
- Statistics.
- Field methods.
- Research ethics.
- Graduate preparation for dolphin work.
- She compares those answers to what USC and Michigan State gave her.
- OSU’s answers remain the clearest for the kind of zoologist she wants to become.

### Beat 8: Fallon, Charlie, and Piper Leave Her With the Process

- Fallon, Charlie, and Piper do not force a conversation before leaving or stepping back.
- They know Lÿsa Mandy needs time.
- Fallon tells her she can let the decision become orderly.
- Charlie reminds her that she does not have to call it final until it is final.
- Piper says something practical and grounding about noticing which place lets her think.
- Lÿsa Mandy hears all three comments and stores them.
- The adults leave her with structure rather than pressure.

### Beat 9: Camp Friends and the Question

- A counselor or camp friend may ask about the university tours.
- Lÿsa Mandy explains them in her own linear way.
- She does not say “I loved OSU” first.
- She says OSU answered the Behavior & Ecology questions most clearly.
- She says USC was interesting but too fast.
- She says Michigan State was serious but too large in a way she is still thinking about.
- Saying it out loud helps her hear the decision forming.

### Beat 10: The Linear Decision

- Lÿsa Mandy lays out the criteria again.
- Which school gives the clearest path toward studying dolphins?
- Which school gives the clearest Behavior & Ecology answers?
- Which campus feels predictable enough for daily life?
- Which environment lets her build routine instead of constantly recovering from the environment?
- Which school gives her independence without making her feel unanchored?
- Oregon State comes first in the sequence.
- The conclusion is not sudden.
- It is the result of the line reaching its end.

### Beat 11: Lÿsa Mandy Chooses Oregon State

- At French Woods, after processing the tours, Lÿsa Mandy chooses Oregon State University.
- She may write it down first before saying it aloud.
- Writing it makes it real in a manageable way.
- She does not yet know Samantha will attend Oregon State too.
- This choice belongs to Lÿsa Mandy’s own reasoning, her own questions, and her own future.
- Oregon State is not chosen because of romance or coincidence.
- Oregon State is chosen because the linear decision leads there.

### Beat 12: Telling Fallon and Charlie

- Lÿsa Mandy tells Fallon and Charlie that Oregon State is her choice.
- The conversation may happen by phone, video call, letter, or after a camp visiting moment.
- Fallon listens without overreacting.
- Charlie is proud but steady.
- Piper may be present or included later.
- They understand that Lÿsa Mandy has not been vague or impulsive.
- She has done the work of choosing.
- They respect the process.

### Beat 13: Final Image

- The final image is Lÿsa Mandy at French Woods with her university notes organized and Oregon State written as the answer.
- Camp continues around her.
- The cabins, meals, and activities remain ordinary.
- The decision has become clear inside the structure that helps her think.
- She is sixteen.
- She has chosen Oregon State.
- She still does not know that Samantha is on her own separate road to the same place.
