# SCENE OUTLINE: LŸSA MANDY’S SCHOOL DROPOFF BEFORE THE AIRPORT

## Placement

**When:** The morning after Lÿsa Mandy’s first day at Sacred Heart School.  
**Where:** Fallon and Charlie’s San Francisco home, the drive to Sacred Heart School, the school drop-off area, then the transition toward the airport.  
**Characters Present:** Lÿsa Mandy, Fallon Glenda, Charlie, Terri Anne Williams-Key, Andrea Jennifer, Ashley Erin.  
**Lÿsa Mandy’s age:** Four.  
**Context:** Terri Anne, Andrea Jennifer, and Ashley Erin have been staying with Fallon, Charlie, and Lÿsa Mandy during the Stanford/San Francisco transition. Terri Anne has helped with Lÿsa Mandy while Fallon and Charlie handled Stanford University Rush Week, and she has bought them a second vehicle so they can manage separate Stanford schedules and Sacred Heart drop-offs/pickups. This morning, Terri Anne, Andrea Jennifer, and Ashley Erin must leave for Los Angeles because of Ashley Erin’s filming schedule. Fallon and Charlie are taking them to the airport, so the three women come along to Lÿsa Mandy’s school drop-off first.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene establishes:

- The next-morning goodbye after the first school-day evening.
- How the family folds a difficult transition into an ordinary routine instead of turning it into a dramatic farewell.
- Terri Anne, Andrea Jennifer, and Ashley Erin seeing Lÿsa Mandy in her new Sacred Heart life before they leave.
- Fallon and Charlie functioning as young parents with real logistical responsibilities: school drop-off, airport run, Stanford schedules, and the new second vehicle.
- Lÿsa Mandy’s need for predictability even when the morning contains a goodbye.

---

## Emotional Core

**A goodbye hidden inside a routine.**

The family does not stage a huge emotional farewell at the house because that would overwhelm Lÿsa Mandy before school. Instead, the goodbye is built into the school drop-off rhythm: uniform, backpack, car ride, school entrance, small kisses, simple words, then forward motion.

Terri Anne, Andrea Jennifer, and Ashley Erin know they are leaving. Fallon and Charlie know the house will feel different when they return from the airport. Lÿsa Mandy may understand the facts more than the emotional scale: they are going to Los Angeles because Ashley Erin has filming.

---

## Tone

Tender, practical, controlled, and bittersweet. The scene should feel like a family protecting a four-year-old’s nervous system by keeping the morning ordinary on purpose.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — Morning With Suitcases

The morning begins earlier than usual. Terri Anne, Andrea Jennifer, and Ashley Erin’s suitcases are already by the door, but not placed in the center of the room where they would dominate Lÿsa Mandy’s attention.

Fallon helps Lÿsa Mandy into her Sacred Heart uniform again. After yesterday, the uniform is slightly less unfamiliar, but still sensory-heavy. Fallon keeps the routine calm: same order, same socks, same shoes, same backpack.

Charlie checks the car plan. The second vehicle Terri Anne bought has already changed the shape of their life: it means Fallon and Charlie can manage Stanford and Sacred Heart without panic. This morning, it also makes the airport run feel possible.

### Beat 2 — Breakfast Without Making It Too Big

Breakfast is quiet and simple. Terri Anne does not say, “We’re leaving today” over and over. Andrea Jennifer keeps her voice low. Ashley Erin is warm but careful, resisting the urge to turn every look at Lÿsa Mandy into a sentimental moment.

Lÿsa Mandy may ask one precise question:

> “Are you going to the airport before lunch?”

Ashley Erin answers plainly:

> “Yes. We have to go to Los Angeles because I have filming.”

No one makes the answer more complicated than that.

### Beat 3 — Loading the Car

Charlie loads the luggage while Fallon keeps Lÿsa Mandy near the doorway and away from the bustle. Terri Anne notices this without needing to be told and helps keep the path clear.

Andrea Jennifer carries one smaller bag. Ashley Erin checks that she has what she needs for filming, but when she sees Lÿsa Mandy watching, she slows down and smiles instead of rushing.

The morning has two purposes now:

1. take Lÿsa Mandy to school;
2. take Terri Anne, Andrea Jennifer, and Ashley Erin to the airport.

Fallon and Charlie make the order very clear for Lÿsa Mandy.

### Beat 4 — The Drive to Sacred Heart

Everyone rides to Sacred Heart together. Lÿsa Mandy sits in her car seat, holding a grounding object — Charlie’s watch, a favorite block, or another small item.

The adults do not fill the car with chatter. The city moves past the windows. Lÿsa Mandy may comment on one concrete thing from yesterday: the school bell, her cubby, the smell of her teacher’s leather shoes, or the place where she stood in line.

Terri Anne listens as if every small detail matters, because to Lÿsa Mandy, it does.

### Beat 5 — Arrival at School

Sacred Heart is busy again: cars, parents, uniforms, backpacks, voices. Fallon and Charlie keep Lÿsa Mandy at the quieter edge of the flow, just as they did the day before.

Terri Anne, Andrea Jennifer, and Ashley Erin step out too, but they do not crowd her. They are there to see her off, not to turn the school entrance into a family spectacle.

Lÿsa Mandy’s uniform ties her visually to the other girls. Terri Anne sees it and understands the generational weight of the moment. Andrea Jennifer notices how carefully Lÿsa Mandy studies the movement of the other children. Ashley Erin looks proud, but keeps her expression soft.

### Beat 6 — The Goodbye at the School Door

The goodbye is short by design.

Fallon kneels first, fixes one small detail of Lÿsa Mandy’s collar, and tells her the pickup plan.

> “Daddy or I will be here after school. Same place.”

Charlie crouches beside them and reinforces the routine.

> “Same door. Same backpack. Same us.”

Then Terri Anne says goodbye in a way that is affectionate but not overwhelming.

> “I’m going to Los Angeles today. I love you. You can tell me about the bell next time.”

Andrea Jennifer offers one concrete anchor.

> “I’ll remember that your cubby is near the door.”

Ashley Erin keeps her goodbye simple because of the filming schedule and the school morning pressure.

> “I have to go film now. I’ll see you after Los Angeles.”

Lÿsa Mandy may not hug everyone. That is okay. She might touch Terri Anne’s hand, lean briefly into Fallon, or simply nod. The adults accept her goodbye exactly as she gives it.

### Beat 7 — Lÿsa Mandy Goes In

Lÿsa Mandy walks into school. She may look back once, not dramatically, but to confirm that everyone is where they said they would be.

Fallon gives a small wave. Charlie keeps his hand visible. Terri Anne, Andrea Jennifer, and Ashley Erin stay still until Lÿsa Mandy has fully entered, so she does not have to process movement behind her.

The school door closes.

### Beat 8 — The Adult Emotion Breaks Through Quietly

Only after Lÿsa Mandy is inside does the adult emotion show.

Fallon exhales. Charlie puts a hand on her back. Terri Anne looks at the closed school door for one extra second, proud and aching at the same time.

Ashley Erin may whisper:

> “She looked so little walking in.”

Andrea Jennifer answers softly:

> “And she still did it.”

Terri Anne turns them gently toward the car. There is no time to collapse into the feeling. They still have an airport to reach.

### Beat 9 — Transition to the Airport

They get back into the car. The energy changes: the Lÿsa Mandy portion of the morning is complete, and now the adults must move into the airport goodbye.

Fallon and Charlie are quiet at first. The second vehicle, the Stanford schedules, the school routine, and the airport run all sit together as evidence that their new life has truly begun.

Terri Anne may reassure them:

> “You have the car. You have the schedule. You have each other. You can do this.”

The scene ends with the car pulling away from Sacred Heart toward the airport, leaving Lÿsa Mandy safely at school and carrying the rest of the family toward Los Angeles.

## What This Scene Establishes

1. Terri Anne, Andrea Jennifer, and Ashley Erin do not leave from the house before Lÿsa Mandy’s school day; they accompany Fallon and Charlie to Sacred Heart because Fallon and Charlie are taking them to the airport afterward.
2. Lÿsa Mandy’s goodbye is protected by the structure of school drop-off.
3. The family respects that a four-year-old may process departure through facts, objects, and routine rather than emotional speeches.
4. Terri Anne’s support is both emotional and practical: childcare during Stanford Rush Week and the second vehicle.
5. Ashley Erin’s filming schedule creates the need for the Los Angeles departure.
6. Fallon and Charlie’s new adult life is shown through logistics, not exposition: school, car seats, suitcases, Stanford, airport timing.
7. The scene follows naturally from the first-evening-back-from-school outline and gives the next-morning goodbye its own focused moment.
