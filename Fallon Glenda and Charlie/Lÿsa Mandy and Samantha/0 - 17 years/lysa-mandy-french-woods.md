# Lÿsa Mandy at French Woods

## Purpose

This file gathers the developing canon around **Lÿsa Mandy Key** and her time at **French Woods**.

It exists because French Woods is becoming an ongoing part of her branch rather than a one-off honeymoon logistics note.

---

## Core Canon

- **Character:** Lÿsa Mandy Key
- **Parents:** Fallon Glenda Key and Charlie
- **Placement in timeline:** During Fallon Glenda and Charlie's **Amsterdam summer honeymoon**, Lÿsa Mandy is away at **French Woods**.
- **Age:** **Eight years old**.
- **Important note:** This is her **second summer / second season** at French Woods. She is returning to a place she already knows.
- **Length:** **Two sessions totaling six weeks**.

That detail matters emotionally. Fallon Glenda would not be able to relax fully if Lÿsa Mandy were being sent somewhere unfamiliar, frightening, or unstable. The fact that French Woods is already known to Lÿsa Mandy gives the honeymoon moral and emotional permission.

---

## Why French Woods Fits Lÿsa Mandy

French Woods suits Lÿsa Mandy especially well because it overlaps with the activities she genuinely loves:

- **Ballet**
- **Gymnastics**
- **Swimming**

Importantly, she does **not** lean more strongly toward ballet than gymnastics, or vice versa. She loves **both equally**, which helps French Woods feel especially well-matched to her because it does not force her into a single mode of expression.

This makes the camp feel like a natural extension of who she already is rather than an imposed summer solution.

---

## Character Logic

Lÿsa Mandy is not at camp merely to be "occupied."

She is there because French Woods offers the kind of structured, expressive, skill-based environment that can genuinely work for her:

- movement with discipline
- repetition with progress
- clear skill-building
- room for joy without chaos being the only mode
- a sense of competence in her own body

Ballet gives her elegance, ritual, and form.
Gymnastics gives her physical confidence and precision.
Swimming gives her familiarity, regulation, and freedom.

Together, those three activities create a summer space that feels vivid and active but still grounded.

---

## Returning-Summer Note

Because this is a **returning French Woods summer**, Lÿsa Mandy already knows some combination of:

- the rhythm of camp days
- how cabins work
- what it feels like to be away and then settle in
- that she has activities there she genuinely looks forward to
- that camp is a place where she has already succeeded before

This should reduce the sense of rupture.

The emotional tone is not:

> "She is being sent away while her parents leave."

It is:

> "She is going back to a place that already belongs to her summer life."

---

## Relationship to the Amsterdam Honeymoon

French Woods is part of what makes the Amsterdam honeymoon possible.

It gives Fallon and Charlie:

- rare adult privacy
- peace of mind
- a believable reason Fallon can emotionally unclench enough to travel
- confidence that Lÿsa Mandy is not just supervised, but happily engaged in something meaningful to her

This is especially important for Fallon Glenda. Because of her history, she does not separate easily from the people she loves unless she believes they are truly safe.

Because Lÿsa Mandy is at camp for **two sessions totaling six weeks**, the story also allows room for important later beats: **Fallon Glenda and Charlie visit her at French Woods on Parents Day**, and **after the second session they take her to New York City to see a ballet performance**. That matters structurally. The camp stay is not written as a total disappearance from one another, but as a successful separation followed by reunion inside settings that continue to honor what the summer has deepened in her.

## Tone Notes for Future Scenes

Scenes involving Lÿsa Mandy at French Woods should probably lean toward:

- competence
- delight in chosen activities
- specific routines and favorite parts of the day
- a sense of earned familiarity
- letters, phone calls, or updates that reassure Fallon and Charlie
- movement-based joy rather than abstract sentimentality

Good scene textures might include:

- ballet class or rehearsal language
- chalk, leotards, practice clothes, wet hair, pool smell, bun pins
- the physical vocabulary of bars, mats, stretching, and swim time
- the pride of doing something "again" rather than for the first time
