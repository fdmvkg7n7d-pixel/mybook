# SCENE OUTLINE: DEPARTURE FROM NEW YORK CITY AND RETURN TO SAN FRANCISCO — PREPARING FOR SCHOOL

## Placement
**When:** Day 6 (departure day) of the six-day New York City vacation, extending through the flight home and the first days back in San Francisco.  
**Where:** Hotel in New York City → Flight to San Francisco → Home in San Francisco.  
**Lÿsa Mandy’s age:** Eight.  
**Context:** This closes the New York City vacation arc and transitions the family back into their regular life in San Francisco, with a focus on preparing for the upcoming school year at Sacred Heart School.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene establishes:
- A graceful, unhurried ending to the New York trip.
- The emotional completion of the French Woods + New York summer arc.
- The shift from vacation mode to the grounded rhythm of home and school.
- Fallon and Charlie beginning to prepare Lÿsa Mandy (and themselves) for the new school year.

---

## Emotional Core

The emotional core is **gentle closure and forward movement**.

The family leaves New York carrying the memories of the ballet night, backstage experience, baseball game, and Central Park day. There is no sadness in the departure — only a quiet sense that the summer has been beautiful and complete. The return to San Francisco marks the beginning of a new chapter: school, routine, and the continuation of Lÿsa Mandy’s growth.

---

## Tone

Calm, affectionate, slightly reflective, and quietly hopeful. The scene should feel like the soft landing after a full, rich summer.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — Final Morning in New York
The family wakes slowly at the hotel. There is no rush. They have a final breakfast together, perhaps ordering room service one last time. Lÿsa Mandy is quieter than usual, holding the small souvenirs from the trip (the pink rose from the ballet, the signed program, her new baseball cap).

They pack without urgency. Lÿsa Mandy carefully folds her ballet dress and places it in her suitcase.

### Beat 2 — The Emotional Landing at the Hotel
Before leaving the room, Lÿsa Mandy stands for a moment looking out the window at the city. She says something simple and childlike about what she will remember most.

Fallon and Charlie listen without turning it into a big moment. They simply acknowledge her feelings.

### Beat 3 — Departure from New York
They take a car to the airport. The drive through the city feels different now — familiar rather than dazzling. Lÿsa Mandy watches the buildings go by with a peaceful expression.

At the airport, they move through security and boarding with the easy rhythm of a family that has traveled together many times.

### Beat 4 — The Flight Home
On the plane, Lÿsa Mandy sits between Fallon and Charlie. She alternates between looking out the window and flipping through the signed program from the ballet. She falls asleep for part of the flight, the baseball cap still on her head.

Fallon and Charlie speak quietly about the trip and what awaits them in San Francisco — school supplies, uniforms, new routines.

### Beat 5 — Arrival in San Francisco
They land in the evening. The air feels different — cooler, familiar. Lÿsa Mandy perks up as they collect their bags and head to their car or a waiting driver.

The drive home through the Bay Area is quiet and comfortable. Lÿsa Mandy points out familiar landmarks with sleepy satisfaction.

### Beat 6 — Arriving Home
They pull up to their home in San Francisco. The house feels welcoming after the trip. Lÿsa Mandy runs inside first, then slows down, suddenly aware that the adventure is over.

They unpack slowly. The ballet dress is hung in her closet. The baseball cap is placed on her dresser. The small gifts from New York are arranged on her nightstand.

### Beat 7 — First Evening Back
They have a simple dinner at home. Lÿsa Mandy talks about the trip in pieces — the boat in Central Park, the loud cheers at the baseball game, the dancers backstage. Fallon and Charlie listen and ask gentle questions.

Before bed, Fallon helps Lÿsa Mandy lay out her school uniform for the first time in weeks. The sight of the Sacred Heart plaid feels grounding.

### Beat 8 — Beginning School Preparations
Over the next few days, the family settles into school-prep mode:

- Shopping for new school supplies and any needed uniform pieces.
- Reviewing the school calendar and Lÿsa Mandy’s class schedule.
- Re-establishing morning and bedtime routines.
- Lÿsa Mandy practicing her ballet positions in her room, mixing the discipline from French Woods with the memory of the New York performance.

Fallon feels a quiet satisfaction watching her daughter move between vacation memories and the steady rhythm of home.

### Beat 9 — Emotional Completion
One evening, a few days after returning, Lÿsa Mandy says something that shows she has integrated the entire summer:

> “I think French Woods and New York were the best parts of summer. But I’m ready for school now.”

Fallon and Charlie exchange a look of quiet pride.

The summer arc — French Woods, New York City, and the return home — feels complete.

## Key Dialogue Possibilities

**Lÿsa Mandy**
> “I’m going to remember the boat the most. And the dancers.”
> “Can I wear my new baseball cap to school on the first day?”
> “I think French Woods and New York were the best parts of summer. But I’m ready for school now.”

**Fallon Glenda**
> “We’re home now. And we get to keep everything we did this summer.”
> “It’s time to get ready for a new year.”

**Charlie**
> “You grew a lot this summer, kiddo.”

---

## What This Scene Establishes

1. The New York vacation ends with grace and emotional completion rather than abruptness.
2. The family successfully transitions from summer adventure back into the structure of school life.
3. Lÿsa Mandy carries the experiences of French Woods and New York forward as part of who she is becoming.
4. Fallon and Charlie are present and intentional as they guide their daughter into the new school year.
5. The summer arc reaches a natural, satisfying close.
