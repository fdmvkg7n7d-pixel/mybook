# SCENE OUTLINE: LŸSA MANDY'S SECOND SUMMER AT FRENCH WOODS

## Placement

**When:** Summer, during Fallon Glenda and Charlie's Amsterdam honeymoon.

**Where:** French Woods.

**Lÿsa Mandy's age:** Eight.

**Camp status:** This is her **second summer / second season** at French Woods, spanning **two sessions totaling six weeks**.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene establishes that French Woods is no longer an experiment or a parental gamble. It is a place Lÿsa Mandy already knows, likes, and can re-enter with real confidence.

It also anchors three key pieces of canon:

1. **She is eight years old.**
2. **This is her second summer there.**
3. **She loves ballet and gymnastics equally, with swimming as a third major joy.**

---

## Emotional Core

The scene is about the difference between **being sent away** and **returning somewhere that already belongs to you**.

For Lÿsa Mandy, French Woods should feel familiar enough to be grounding and exciting enough to feel like a real summer of her own.

For Fallon Glenda, this is the moment that makes the honeymoon emotionally possible: she can let go because Lÿsa Mandy is not frightened, lost, or newly displaced.

---

## Tone

Warm, competent, lightly emotional, and confidence-building.

The scene should not feel wrenching.
It should feel earned.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — Arrival at Camp
Fallon Glenda and Charlie bring Lÿsa Mandy to French Woods.

Because this is her second summer, the arrival energy is completely different from a first-time drop-off. Lÿsa Mandy recognizes the grounds, the flow of camp check-in, and the fact that this place already exists inside her memory.

### Beat 2 — Recognition Instead of Fear
A counselor, staff member, or even another returning camper recognizes Lÿsa Mandy.

This matters. The scene pivots here from logistics to belonging. Lÿsa Mandy is not just entering a program; she is re-entering a world where she already has shape.

### Beat 3 — The Activities That Make the Place Hers
Very quickly, the narrative should show why French Woods fits her so well.

She lights up most around:

- ballet
- gymnastics
- swimming

Importantly, she does **not** have to choose between ballet and gymnastics emotionally. She loves both equally. That balance should be part of her character texture.

### Beat 4 — Fallon Watches the Difference
Fallon Glenda notices the difference between anxious separation and confident return.

Lÿsa Mandy is not clinging. She is not being brave through misery. She is already turning toward camp life — toward movement, routine, and the pieces of the summer that belong to her.

This is the beat where Fallon begins to unclench.

### Beat 5 — Charlie as the Steady Reader of the Room
Charlie notices Fallon noticing.

He does not force the moment. He simply stays near, reads both of them correctly, and helps mark the transition as safe. If Fallon needs a quiet reassurance, he gives it without making the scene larger than it is.

### Beat 6 — The Goodbye
The goodbye should be loving but not melodramatic.

Lÿsa Mandy can hug them, promise to write or call, and then turn back toward camp with real momentum. The emotional point is that she has a summer life here.

### Beat 7 — The Parents Leave
Once Fallon and Charlie are back in the car or walking away, Fallon can finally admit that the second-summer part changed everything.

If it had been Lÿsa Mandy's first time, Fallon might never have been able to settle enough to enjoy the honeymoon. But this is different. This is return, not rupture.

## What This Scene Establishes

1. French Woods is a recurring part of Lÿsa Mandy's branch.
2. The second-summer detail is emotionally crucial.
3. Ballet and gymnastics are equal loves, not ranked interests.
4. Swimming remains part of her physical and emotional ease.
5. Fallon Glenda's ability to take the honeymoon depends in part on seeing Lÿsa Mandy securely belong somewhere.
