# Scene Outline: Fallon and Piper Put a Big Tent in the Living Room Before Lÿsa Mandy Leaves

## Overview

- **Characters:** Lÿsa Mandy Key, Fallon Glenda Key, Piper, Charlie, Rebecca Brazilia Key, Charlie Janya Key.
- **Location:** Fallon Glenda and Charlie’s San Francisco home, living room.
- **Timing:** After Lÿsa Mandy’s Michigan State University tour and before she leaves for French Woods.
- **Status:** New scene outline.

---

## Placement

- Lÿsa Mandy is sixteen years old.
- She has finished touring her three zoology university options: Oregon State University, USC, and Michigan State University.
- After the Michigan State tour, she is preparing to leave for French Woods.
- Rebecca Brazilia and Charlie Janya are about five years old.
- The twins are beginning to understand that Lÿsa Mandy will be away for the whole summer.
- Before Lÿsa leaves, Fallon Glenda and Piper put a big tent in the living room for the girls.
- Piper is Fallon Glenda’s romantic girlfriend.
- This scene happens before the French Woods departure scene.

---

## Core Situation

- Fallon and Piper realize the younger girls need a concrete way to be close to Lÿsa before she leaves.
- Rebecca Brazilia and Charlie Janya are too young to hold the whole idea of summer-long separation calmly.
- A big living-room tent gives the girls a private, playful, contained place to be together.
- The tent becomes a family-made bridge between home and camp.
- Lÿsa Mandy gets one last night of sister closeness before French Woods.
- Rebecca and Charlie Janya get one last ordinary-feeling memory before the goodbye becomes real.

---

## Scene Beats

### Beat 1: Fallon Notices the Tension

- Fallon notices that Rebecca Brazilia and Charlie Janya are hovering around Lÿsa Mandy more than usual.
- The twins are asking repeated questions about French Woods.
- They ask how long Lÿsa will be gone.
- They ask whether she will come back before dinner.
- They ask whether she will sleep in a cabin every night.
- They ask whether she will forget the house.
- Fallon understands that the twins are starting to realize that Lÿsa will be away for the whole summer.
- The realization is too big for five-year-old hearts.

### Beat 2: Piper Suggests the Tent

- Piper sees the same emotional pattern Fallon sees.
- Piper suggests making the living room into a tent night for the girls.
- The idea is practical and tender.
- It gives the twins something to look forward to instead of only something to dread.
- Fallon immediately understands the value of it.
- A tent is a contained space.
- A tent is a pretend camp.
- A tent is a way to practice separation by creating closeness first.

### Beat 3: Building the Big Tent

- Fallon and Piper build a big tent in the living room.
- They use blankets, sheets, cushions, chairs, clips, and whatever safe supports make the tent large enough for the girls.
- The tent is big enough for Lÿsa Mandy, Rebecca Brazilia, and Charlie Janya to fit inside comfortably.
- It is not perfect.
- That is part of why it feels real.
- Piper handles some of the practical construction.
- Fallon adjusts the inside so it will feel soft, predictable, and safe for Lÿsa Mandy’s sensory needs.
- The living room changes from an ordinary room into a small indoor camp.

### Beat 4: The Girls Discover It

- Rebecca Brazilia and Charlie Janya see the tent and become immediately excited.
- Their fear about Lÿsa leaving is interrupted by the concrete joy of the tent.
- Lÿsa Mandy studies the structure before entering.
- She checks where the openings are.
- She checks how much light comes in.
- She checks the blankets and the floor space.
- Fallon and Piper let her inspect it without rushing her.
- Once Lÿsa understands the tent, she goes inside with her sisters.

### Beat 5: Inside the Tent

- The girls bring pillows, blankets, stuffed animals, books, and small treasures into the tent.
- Rebecca Brazilia and Charlie Janya want Lÿsa close to them.
- They may press against her, sit beside her, or arrange themselves so she cannot leave without them noticing.
- Lÿsa Mandy accepts more closeness than she usually might because she understands this is their way of saying they will miss her.
- The tent makes the closeness manageable.
- It has edges.
- It has a ceiling.
- It has a doorway.
- It has rules the girls can understand.

### Beat 6: Talking About French Woods

- The twins ask more questions from inside the tent.
- They ask whether French Woods has tents.
- They ask whether Lÿsa will sleep in a cabin.
- They ask whether she will write letters.
- They ask whether she can send drawings or maps.
- Lÿsa answers in her precise way.
- She explains cabins, activities, schedules, meals, counselors, and letters.
- She tells them she will come home after the summer.
- The twins listen because inside the tent, the answers feel less frightening.

### Beat 7: Fallon and Piper Watch From Outside

- Fallon and Piper sit nearby, outside the tent.
- They do not intrude.
- They listen enough to know the girls are okay.
- Fallon is emotional watching Lÿsa be both a child leaving for camp and an older sister comforting younger sisters.
- Piper is moved by the way the tent is working.
- Charlie may pass through, check on everyone, or bring water or snacks, but he lets the tent remain the girls’ space.
- The adults understand that the tent is doing something words could not do alone.

### Beat 8: A Tent Snack

- Fallon and Piper bring the girls a simple living-room tent snack.
- The snack should be easy, familiar, and not messy enough to make the tent stressful.
- The girls eat inside or at the tent opening.
- Rebecca and Charlie Janya treat the snack like a camp feast.
- Lÿsa Mandy likes that the food has a clear place and a clear purpose.
- The tent becomes a ritual rather than a distraction.

### Beat 9: The First Softening

- After the questions and snack, the twins become calmer.
- They are still sad.
- They still do not want Lÿsa to go.
- But they now have a memory of being together before she leaves.
- Lÿsa sees that the tent has helped them.
- She may quietly tell Fallon or Piper that the tent was a good idea.
- Piper receives that without making a big deal of it.
- Fallon stores the sentence away because praise from Lÿsa is precise and meaningful.

### Beat 10: The Sleepy Tent

- The girls begin to get sleepy inside the tent.
- Rebecca Brazilia and Charlie Janya fight sleep because sleeping means the night will end and Lÿsa leaving will be closer.
- Lÿsa Mandy may read to them or list the French Woods schedule one more time.
- The repetition soothes all three girls.
- The living room becomes quiet around the tent.
- Fallon and Piper dim the lights.
- The tent glows softly in the room.

### Beat 11: Middle-of-the-Night Check

- In the middle of the night, Fallon Glenda and Piper come downstairs to check how the girls are doing.
- The living room is quiet.
- The big tent is still standing.
- The blankets have shifted, but the tent has held.
- Fallon and Piper move carefully so they do not wake anyone.
- They look inside the tent.
- Lÿsa Mandy is asleep on her back or slightly turned inside the nest of blankets.
- Rebecca Brazilia and Charlie Janya are asleep on top of Lÿsa Mandy’s body.
- The twins have settled across her as if their bodies needed to keep her from leaving before morning.
- Lÿsa Mandy is sleeping peacefully under them, accepting the weight of her little sisters in the safe, ordinary way of family sleep.
- The sight is beautiful.
- Fallon Glenda becomes very emotional.
- Piper becomes very emotional too.
- They do not speak at first.
- They simply stand there and take in the image of the three sisters holding each other through the coming goodbye.

### Beat 12: Fallon and Piper’s Quiet Emotion

- Fallon understands that Rebecca Brazilia and Charlie Janya are not only sleeping.
- They are clinging to the last night before Lÿsa Mandy leaves for French Woods.
- Piper sees the same thing.
- Piper’s idea of the tent has become something deeper than a distraction.
- It has given the girls a place to arrange their love physically.
- Fallon may wipe her eyes or cover her mouth so she does not make a sound.
- Piper stays close beside her.
- The adults are careful not to disturb the beautiful sight.
- They let the girls sleep exactly as they are.
- The moment tells Fallon and Piper that the tent did what it needed to do.

### Beat 13: Final Image

- The final image is the big tent in the living room in the middle of the night.
- Lÿsa Mandy is still home for one more night.
- Rebecca Brazilia and Charlie Janya are asleep on top of her body, held by her even in sleep.
- Fallon and Piper stand outside the tent, very emotional at the beautiful sight.
- French Woods is coming.
- For tonight, the living room is camp enough, and Lÿsa Mandy is still within reach.

## Emotional Notes

- The tent should feel practical, loving, and sensory-aware.
- Fallon and Piper should be shown as a coordinated adult support pair.
- Piper’s role should be warm and useful, not decorative.
- Rebecca Brazilia and Charlie Janya’s emotions should be age-appropriate.
- Lÿsa Mandy should not be forced to manage the twins’ feelings, but she can participate in comforting them in her own precise way.
- The middle-of-the-night image of the twins sleeping on top of Lÿsa Mandy should be beautiful, innocent, and familial.
- Fallon and Piper becoming emotional should show that they understand the girls are holding onto each other before the summer separation.
- The tent is not a cure for separation anxiety.
- It is a container for one last night of closeness before French Woods.
