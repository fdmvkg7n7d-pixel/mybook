# SCENE OUTLINE: GETTING READY FOR UNIVERSITY — FALLON GLENDA AND CHARLIE REFLECT WHILE SETTLING BACK IN SAN FRANCISCO

## Placement
**When:** Within the first week or two after returning from New York City to San Francisco.  
**Where:** The family home in San Francisco.  
**Lÿsa Mandy’s age:** Eight.  
**Context:** This scene takes place alongside school-preparation activities for Lÿsa Mandy and serves as a quiet bridge between the summer adventures and the family’s ongoing life in the Bay Area. It focuses on Fallon and Charlie preparing for (or reflecting on) their own university path at Stanford.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene establishes:
- Fallon and Charlie actively reconnecting with their university identities after the summer.
- A tender, age-appropriate way of sharing their past with Lÿsa Mandy.
- The practical and emotional preparation involved in balancing parenthood with higher education.
- A sense of continuity between the family’s history at Stanford and their present life.

---

## Emotional Core

The emotional core is **shared history and forward momentum**.

As the family settles back into San Francisco routines, Fallon and Charlie begin the quiet work of preparing for the upcoming university term (or reflecting on their time there). Lÿsa Mandy witnesses this process, gaining a deeper understanding that her parents have dreams and responsibilities beyond being her mother and father.

---

## Tone

Intimate, reflective, slightly nostalgic, and grounded. The scene should feel like the family is gently stepping back into their adult lives while keeping Lÿsa Mandy included.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — Evening at Home
A few days after returning from New York, the family is home after a day of school-supply shopping for Lÿsa Mandy. The house is calm. Lÿsa Mandy is playing quietly in her room while Fallon and Charlie sit at the kitchen table with folders, laptops, and old notebooks spread out.

### Beat 2 — The University Documents
Fallon and Charlie are going through Stanford-related materials:
- Course registration information
- Old syllabi or reading lists
- Financial aid or scholarship documents
- Photos from their early days at Stanford (including some with a very young Lÿsa Mandy)

They speak in low voices, occasionally laughing at old memories.

### Beat 3 — Lÿsa Mandy Joins Them
Lÿsa Mandy comes into the kitchen in her pajamas, curious about the papers. Fallon invites her to sit on her lap. Lÿsa Mandy picks up an old photo of Fallon and Charlie on the Stanford campus with five-year-old Lÿsa Mandy between them.

### Beat 4 — Gentle Storytelling
Fallon and Charlie share small stories in an age-appropriate way:
- How hard it was at first to balance classes with a young child.
- The friends they made.
- The late-night study sessions.
- How they supported each other through difficult semesters.

They keep the tone light and warm, not overwhelming.

### Beat 5 — Lÿsa Mandy’s Questions
Lÿsa Mandy asks practical questions:
- “Did you have to wear uniforms like Sacred Heart?”
- “Was it scary?”
- “Did you miss me when you were at school?”

Fallon and Charlie answer honestly but gently.

### Beat 6 — Practical Preparation
They show Lÿsa Mandy their class schedules and explain that university is like a very big, very serious version of school. Fallon mentions that she is working toward becoming a doctor. Charlie shares what he is studying.

Lÿsa Mandy listens seriously, beginning to understand that her parents have big goals.

### Beat 7 — The Emotional Layer
At one point, Fallon looks at a photo of herself at seventeen with baby Lÿsa Mandy and becomes quiet for a moment. Charlie reaches over and squeezes her hand. They share a brief, wordless look of recognition about how far they have come.

Lÿsa Mandy notices but does not interrupt.

### Beat 8 — Including Lÿsa Mandy
Fallon asks Lÿsa Mandy if she would like to help organize the papers or pick a new notebook for herself to “study” alongside them. Lÿsa Mandy takes the task seriously and helps sort papers into piles.

This small act makes her feel part of their world.

### Beat 9 — Closing the Evening
Before bed, Fallon tucks Lÿsa Mandy in and says:

> “University is a long road. But we’re walking it together — you, me, and Daddy. Just like we walked through New York together.”

Lÿsa Mandy nods, understanding more than she can yet put into words.

### Beat 10 — Emotional Landing
Later that night, Fallon and Charlie sit together on the couch. They talk quietly about the upcoming term, the balance of parenting and studying, and how grateful they are to be doing it as a team. The New York trip feels like a beautiful chapter that has now closed, and university life feels like the steady next step.

## Key Dialogue Possibilities

**Lÿsa Mandy**
> “Was it scary being so far from home?”
> “Can I come to your university one day?”
> “Did you really do homework every night?”

**Fallon Glenda**
> “We were very young. But we had each other, and we had you. That made it easier.”
> “University is like school, but bigger and harder. We’re going to work very hard this year.”

**Charlie**
> “Your mom is going to be a doctor one day. That’s why she studies so much.”
> “We’re lucky we get to do this together.”

---

## What This Scene Establishes

1. Fallon and Charlie are actively preparing for (or reflecting on) their university journey while raising Lÿsa Mandy.
2. Lÿsa Mandy is gently included in her parents’ adult world and goals.
3. The family’s history at Stanford is treated as an ongoing, living part of their story.
4. The return from New York transitions smoothly into the grounded, ambitious rhythm of Bay Area life.
5. The scene reinforces the themes of partnership, ambition, and family support.
