# SCENE OUTLINE: LŸSA MANDY'S FIRST GYMNASTICS LESSON AT THE PRESIDIO YMCA & PIER 39 CELEBRATION

## Placement

**When:** A Saturday morning and afternoon during Fallon Glenda and Charlie's undergraduate years at Stanford University.
**Where:** The **Presidio YMCA Gymnastics Center** in **San Francisco, California**, followed by a celebratory trip to **Pier 39**.
**Characters Present:** Lÿsa Mandy (approx. age 4–5), Fallon Glenda, Charlie, youth gymnastics coach (Coach Maya), and a small group of fellow four- and five-year-old beginners and their parents.
**Context:** Lÿsa Mandy has recently started attending Sacred Heart School in San Francisco (`lysa-mandy-first-day-sacred-heart-scene-outline.md`). Curious, active, and deeply sensitive to her surroundings, she has expressed interest in structured physical movement. **Fallon and Charlie do not know that Lÿsa Mandy has Asperger Syndrome at this age.** They simply know their daughter: how she takes in the world, how she processes noise and space, and how much she thrives when given clarity, structure, and advance notice. While Fallon Glenda is studying pre-med at Stanford, **Charlie is studying Pre-Law and Public Policy with a focus on Child Advocacy**—an academic path directly inspired by his protective devotion to Fallon and his formal adoption of Lÿsa Mandy. Together, they have enrolled her in her very first beginner gymnastics class at the renowned Presidio YMCA, celebrating afterward with a trip to famous **Pier 39** where they walk her through the upcoming week's schedule.

## Scene Purpose

This scene establishes:

- **The origin of Lÿsa Mandy's canonical love for gymnastics**, which will later anchor her equal devotion to "ballet and gymnastics both" during her summers at French Woods.
- **Charlie's academic identity at Stanford**: Officially establishing that while Fallon pursues medicine, **Charlie studies Pre-Law, Public Policy, and Child Advocacy**, aligning his university coursework with his instinctive, steadfast protection of vulnerable children and families.
- **The San Francisco geography & family rituals**: Connecting her lessons at the **Presidio YMCA Gymnastics Center** to her beloved waterfront rituals at **Pier 39** (`friday-pier-ritual-outline.md`).
- **Parental attunement without clinical labels**: Because Fallon and Charlie **do not know she has Asperger Syndrome**, their careful guidance through the noisy gymnasium, the sensory whirlwind of Pier 39, and their proactive scheduling previews demonstrate natural, deeply observant parental instinct.
- **Lÿsa Mandy's distinct physical focus**: Contrasting the chaotic energy of typical four-year-olds with Lÿsa Mandy's deliberate, highly concentrated approach to spatial balance and physical grip.
- **The partnership between Fallon and Charlie**: Coordinating Stanford University commutes across law seminars and pre-med labs alongside childcare routines while keeping Lÿsa Mandy grounded and informed.

## Emotional Core

**Finding grounded strength in the gymnasium and reassuring structure at the pier.**

For four/five-year-old Lÿsa Mandy, entering a cavernous gymnasium can feel intense and loud. Because Fallon and Charlie don't have medical terminology or an Asperger diagnosis to frame her behavior, their patience at the edge of the mat is pure devotion. After watching her conquer the balance beam and low bars, traveling together to **Pier 39** provides sensory comfort through warm clam chowder and chocolate mint ice cream. While sitting by the water, Fallon and Charlie gently lay out the concrete roadmap for Monday morning school drop-off and afternoon pick-up, balancing Charlie's pre-law coursework and Fallon's medical labs around what matters most: ensuring Lÿsa Mandy feels secure, informed, and deeply loved well before the new week begins.

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — Preparing at the San Francisco Home

Saturday morning begins in Fallon and Charlie's San Francisco home. Fallon helps Lÿsa Mandy put on her very first gymnastics leotard—a soft, tagless leotard carefully chosen because Fallon has noticed Lÿsa Mandy dislikes itchy seams or rough fabrics. Charlie braids or ties her hair back securely. 

Lÿsa Mandy looks at her reflection, feeling the tightness and stretch of the fabric compared to her Sacred Heart plaid uniform. She asks precise questions about what the Presidio YMCA looks like, whether the floor is hard or soft, and how high the bars are. Without knowing anything about Asperger Syndrome, Fallon and Charlie naturally answer with concrete, detailed reassurance, knowing from daily experience that clarity makes their daughter feel safe.

### Beat 2 — Arrival at the Historic Presidio YMCA

They drive into San Francisco's Presidio, passing through groves of towering eucalyptus trees and historic red-brick buildings under a crisp morning sky. As they step inside the **Presidio YMCA Gymnastics Center**, the sensory environment shifts dramatically: the smell of chalk mats, bright overhead lights, the springy acoustics of echoing shouts, and children running across foam blocks.

Sensing Lÿsa Mandy's momentary hesitation as she takes in the noise and scale of the gymnasium, Charlie kneels down beside her while Fallon holds her hand. Having no clinical diagnosis in mind, they simply read their daughter's body language. Charlie's coursework in child advocacy reinforces his natural patience; he and Fallon do not push her out onto the floor. Instead, they stand with her near the observation benches for several minutes, pointing out the spring floor, the low wooden balance beam, and the foam pit, giving her natural space to observe until she nods ready.

### Beat 3 — Meeting Coach Maya and Step-On

Coach Maya, an energetic and structured youth coach, calls the beginner class over to the blue circle mat. Lÿsa Mandy walks out onto the spring floor for the first time. She pauses at the edge, pressing her bare feet into the carpeted foam, fascinated by the give and rebound beneath her toes. 

When Coach Maya leads warm-up stretches and basic tucks, Lÿsa Mandy observes intently for three seconds before executing the movements with serious, laser-focused precision—not just copying the motion, but testing how her balance shifts with each stretch.

### Beat 4 — The Low Balance Beam

The class moves to the low balance beam (raised only a few inches off the mat). While many of the other four-year-olds giggle, rush across, or jump off haphazardly, Lÿsa Mandy approaches the beam as an exact spatial puzzle. 

She places one foot carefully in front of the other, her arms extended horizontally. Her face is a picture of fierce concentration. From the parent seating area, Fallon leans into Charlie's shoulder, watching with open admiration as Lÿsa Mandy walks the entire length of the beam without faltering or rushing, finding profound calm in the exact alignment of her steps.

### Beat 5 — The Junior Uneven Bars & The Joy of Grip

Next, Coach Maya guides the children to the junior hanging bar. Coach Maya lifts Lÿsa Mandy up to grip the wooden rail. The moment Lÿsa Mandy's hands close around the chalky bar, her expression shifts from concentration to pure physical exhilaration.

Hanging by her arms, she pulls her knees up into a neat tuck, feeling the solid, undeniable strength of her own muscles against gravity. When Coach Maya counts to five and prepares to lift her down, Lÿsa Mandy holds on for two extra seconds, reluctant to let go of that feeling of direct, powerful bodily grounding.

### Beat 6 — The Spark of Equal Devotion

Class concludes with a final stretch and stamp on the hand from Coach Maya. Lÿsa Mandy trots back across the spring floor to where Fallon and Charlie are waiting, her cheeks flushed with pride. 

Charlie scoops her up into a hug, asking what she thought of the bars and the beam. Lÿsa Mandy looks at her chalky hands and declares with certainty that gymnastics makes her feel "strong and bright"—the foundational spark that will soon pair with ballet to form her lifelong insistence that she loves **ballet and gymnastics both equally**.

### Beat 7 — Pier 39 Celebration & Monday Schedule Briefing

Leaving the Presidio YMCA, Fallon and Charlie promise a special celebration and drive Lÿsa Mandy along the scenic waterfront directly to bustling **Pier 39**. Stepping out into the salty Pacific mist and the lively noise of Fisherman's Wharf, Fallon and Charlie naturally form a protective physical buffer around Lÿsa Mandy as they walk down to K-Dock to watch the barking sea lions piled on the wooden floats.

To celebrate her very first gymnastics lesson, they take her for their favorite family waterfront feast: steaming clam chowder served inside a warm, crusty sourdough bread bowl, followed by scoops of refreshing chocolate mint ice cream (`friday-pier-ritual-outline.md`). While Lÿsa Mandy enjoys her ice cream overlooking the bay, Fallon and Charlie calmly walk her through the schedule for Monday. Because they intuitively know how much peace Lÿsa Mandy draws from knowing what comes next, they clearly outline how their university schedules shape the routine: **Before Charlie heads down to Palo Alto for his pre-law seminar, he will drive Lÿsa Mandy and walk her into her Sacred Heart classroom on Monday morning. And when the afternoon school bell rings, Fallon Glenda—finishing her early biology lab—will be waiting right outside her classroom door to pick her up.** Lÿsa Mandy methodically repeats the schedule back ("Charlie for morning drop-off, Fallon for afternoon pick-up"), her shoulders visibly relaxing as the predictability of the upcoming week locks into place.
