# SCENE OUTLINE: LŸSA MANDY AT THE NEW YORK METS VS TORONTO BLUE JAYS GAME (Day 4)

## Placement
**When:** Afternoon / Evening of Day 4 of the six-day New York City vacation.  
**Where:** Citi Field, Queens, New York.  
**Lÿsa Mandy’s age:** Eight.  
**Context:** This day follows the emotional high of the ballet night and backstage experience on Day 3. It provides a bright, energetic contrast.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene establishes:
- A lively, all-American (with a Canadian twist) family outing.
- Lÿsa Mandy’s first major professional sports experience.
- A joyful, high-energy day that balances the elegance of the ballet.
- The Blue Jays connection as a nod to the family’s Ontario roots.

---

## Emotional Core

The emotional core is **shared excitement and belonging**.

After the refined, almost dreamlike ballet night, this game is loud, colorful, and physical. Fallon and Charlie want Lÿsa Mandy to experience the full spectrum of New York — from the hushed awe of the theater to the roaring energy of a ballpark. The Blue Jays angle gives the day a gentle “home team” feeling even while far from Ontario.

---

## Tone

Lively, colorful, playful, slightly overwhelming in a fun way, and warmly family-oriented. The scene should feel like pure summer joy.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — Morning Recovery
After the late ballet night, the family has a slow morning at the hotel. Lÿsa Mandy is still processing the previous evening but is clearly excited about today’s plan. Fallon and Charlie keep the tone light and anticipatory.

### Beat 2 — Travel to Citi Field
They take the subway or a car service to the stadium. Lÿsa Mandy watches the city change outside the window. The energy shifts from the quiet elegance of the ballet to the growing buzz of game day.

### Beat 3 — Arrival at the Ballpark
Citi Field is huge, loud, and full of color. Lÿsa Mandy’s eyes go wide at the scale — the giant screens, the crowds, the smell of hot dogs and popcorn. Fallon buys her a small Mets or Blue Jays cap as a souvenir.

### Beat 4 — Finding Their Seats
They settle into their seats with a good view of the field. Lÿsa Mandy is given a program and a small foam finger or pennant. She studies everything with serious concentration, the same way she studied the ballet program the night before.

### Beat 5 — The Game Begins — Mets vs Blue Jays
The game starts with the national anthem and the roar of the crowd. Lÿsa Mandy jumps a little at the first loud cheer. Fallon and Charlie explain the basics gently as the innings progress.

Key moments for Lÿsa Mandy:
- The crack of the bat
- The big screen replays
- The wave moving through the stands
- The seventh-inning stretch
- Cheering for both teams (especially when the Blue Jays are up, because of family roots)

### Beat 6 — Food and Treats
Hot dogs, popcorn, Cracker Jacks, and soda become part of the experience. Lÿsa Mandy tries everything and declares the hot dog “the best thing she’s ever eaten at a game” (even though it’s her first).

### Beat 7 — Fallon and Charlie’s Experience
Fallon enjoys watching Lÿsa Mandy’s reactions more than the game itself. Charlie explains a few rules in simple terms and cheers along with her. The three of them form a small, happy island in the sea of fans.

### Beat 8 — Emotional High Point
At some point in the game — perhaps during a big play or when the crowd does the wave — Lÿsa Mandy throws her arms in the air and cheers with full abandon. It is the opposite of the still, focused way she watched the ballet. Fallon catches the moment and smiles.

### Beat 9 — The End of the Game
Whether the Mets or Blue Jays win, the family leaves on a high note. Lÿsa Mandy is tired but glowing, her new cap slightly crooked on her head. She carries her foam finger like a trophy.

### Beat 10 — Ride Back to the Hotel
On the way back, Lÿsa Mandy talks nonstop about her favorite moments — the loudest cheer, the best play, the taste of the hot dog. Fallon and Charlie let her narrate the entire evening.

## Key Dialogue Possibilities

**Lÿsa Mandy**
> “That was so loud! Did you see how far the ball went?”
> “Can we get another hot dog? The first one was the best.”
> “I think I like baseball now.”

**Fallon Glenda**
> “You cheered louder than anyone in our row.”
> “We can come to another game sometime if you want.”

**Charlie**
> “Blue Jays or Mets — which team are you rooting for next time?”

## What This Scene Establishes

1. The trip offers a full range of experiences — elegant art and rowdy sports.
2. Lÿsa Mandy is capable of moving between very different worlds with delight.
3. The Blue Jays connection keeps a thread of “home” alive even in New York.
4. Fallon and Charlie actively create joyful, memory-making days for their daughter.
5. The energy of Day 4 serves as a bright counterpoint to the ballet night.
