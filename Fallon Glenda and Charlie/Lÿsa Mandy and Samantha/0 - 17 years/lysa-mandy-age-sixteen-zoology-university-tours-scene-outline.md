# Scene Outline: Lÿsa Mandy Chooses Three Zoology University Tours

## Overview

- **Characters:** Lÿsa Mandy Key, Fallon Glenda Key, Charlie, Piper, Calida Dawn / Rebecca Brazilia / Charlie Janya referenced as needed, university tour guides/advisors.
- **Locations:** San Francisco; Oregon State University; University of Southern California; Michigan State University.
- **Timing:** When Lÿsa Mandy is **sixteen years old**.
- **Academic Focus:** Zoology / animal science / pre-zoology path, with Lÿsa Mandy’s long-term interest in studying dolphins.
- **Status:** New scene outline.

---

## Placement

- Lÿsa Mandy is sixteen.
- She is old enough to begin thinking seriously about university.
- Her long-term interest is zoology, especially dolphins.
- She chooses three universities to tour for a possible zoology degree path:
  - **Oregon State University | OSU**
  - **University of Southern California | USC**
  - **Michigan State University**
- Fallon Glenda, Charlie, and Piper take Lÿsa Mandy to these universities.
- Piper is Fallon Glenda’s romantic girlfriend.
- The universities are visited in the order Lÿsa Mandy lists them: Oregon State University first, University of Southern California second, and Michigan State University third.
- After the Michigan State tour, Lÿsa Mandy goes to **French Woods**.
- This scene sets up Lÿsa Mandy eventually attending **Oregon State University first**, where she is assigned to the same dormitory room as Samantha.
- Lÿsa Mandy and Samantha do not know yet that they are both going to attend Oregon State University.

---

## Core Situation

- Lÿsa Mandy is not choosing a university randomly.
- She is choosing possible futures connected to animals, water, science, and the careful observation that has always shaped her mind.
- Fallon and Charlie support the process with structure and patience.
- Piper accompanies them as part of the support system for the tours.
- Piper is Fallon Glenda’s romantic girlfriend, and her presence is treated as part of the family’s loving adult support structure.
- The tours help Lÿsa Mandy compare how each campus feels in her body and mind, not only how each school looks on paper.
- The decision leads toward Oregon State, but this scene should let all three tours matter.
- Because of her Asperger Syndrome, Lÿsa Mandy makes the decision in a linear, step-by-step way rather than by vague feeling alone.

---

## Scene Beats

### Beat 1: Lÿsa Mandy Names the Three Schools

- Lÿsa Mandy sits with information about zoology programs, animal sciences, marine life, and university paths.
- She is sixteen and serious.
- She has already begun thinking about dolphins not only as a special interest, but as a possible life’s work.
- She names three universities she wants to tour.
- Oregon State University.
- University of Southern California.
- Michigan State University.
- Fallon and Charlie listen carefully.
- They do not make the choice for her.
- They help her turn the list into an actual travel plan.

### Beat 2: Why These Three

- Lÿsa Mandy explains what interests her about each school.
- Oregon State feels connected to the Pacific Northwest, marine science, biology, and a quieter kind of focus.
- USC feels close to family geography, California, opportunity, and a larger urban world.
- Michigan State feels like a serious animal-science and research environment with a strong campus identity.
- Lÿsa Mandy does not evaluate them through prestige only.
- She wants to know where she can think clearly.
- She wants to know where animals, science, and routine can fit together.

### Beat 3: Fallon, Charlie, and Piper Prepare the Tours

- Fallon, Charlie, and Piper plan the tours with Lÿsa Mandy’s needs in mind.
- Piper is Fallon Glenda’s romantic girlfriend, and she is included naturally in the planning.
- They build clear itineraries.
- They visit the universities in the order Lÿsa Mandy listed them: OSU, USC, then Michigan State.
- They avoid too many surprises.
- They leave enough time between campus events for food, quiet, rest, and processing.
- Charlie helps with logistics: routes, hotel details, parking, timing, and backup plans.
- Fallon helps Lÿsa Mandy prepare questions.
- Piper helps keep the trip emotionally and practically steady.
- The adults understand that university tours are exciting, but also sensory-heavy and emotionally loaded.

### Beat 4: Oregon State University Tour

- They tour Oregon State University first.
- The campus feels different from San Francisco.
- The air, rain, trees, paths, and quieter rhythms matter to Lÿsa Mandy.
- She pays attention to science buildings, animal-related pathways, biology advising, and any mention of marine science connections.
- She has many questions about **Behavior & Ecology**.
- She asks whether a zoology path can focus on how animals behave in their environments.
- She asks whether ecology means only ecosystems or whether it also means the relationships between animals, food, movement, sound, water, and social groups.
- She asks how someone who wants to study dolphins should prepare.
- The advisor explains that Behavior & Ecology would require a strong foundation in biology, ecology, evolution, animal behavior, statistics, field methods, and research ethics.
- The advisor explains that dolphin work usually requires patience, data collection, observation, marine mammal knowledge, and often graduate study.
- The advisor explains that undergraduates can prepare by taking relevant courses, seeking research or field experience, learning data analysis, and building relationships with faculty and marine/animal research programs.
- Lÿsa Mandy writes the answers down carefully because the answers make the future feel less imaginary.
- Fallon watches her daughter become focused in a way that feels familiar and new.
- Charlie notices that Lÿsa Mandy is not overwhelmed in the same way she sometimes is in busier places.
- Piper notices that Lÿsa Mandy keeps returning to the idea of water, animals, and observation.

### Beat 5: Processing Oregon State

- After the Oregon State tour, they give Lÿsa Mandy time to process.
- She does not immediately declare it her choice.
- She lists details.
- She talks about paths, buildings, weather, quiet, students, and the feeling of being able to build a routine there.
- She also repeats the Behavior & Ecology answers back to herself: biology, ecology, animal behavior, statistics, field methods, research ethics, and graduate preparation for dolphin work.
- Fallon and Charlie do not rush her.
- Piper may ask one simple clarifying question that helps Lÿsa Mandy organize her thoughts.
- Oregon State becomes the first campus that feels like it could hold her future.

### Beat 6: University of Southern California Tour

- They tour the University of Southern California second.
- USC feels louder, warmer, more urban, and more intense.
- The campus has energy, movement, and opportunity.
- Lÿsa Mandy is interested, but she has to work harder to filter the noise and pace.
- She asks about biology, animal behavior, research, internships, and pathways that might support zoology or marine mammal work.
- The California location makes the school feel close to family and familiar systems.
- That familiarity is both comforting and complicated.
- Lÿsa Mandy has to decide whether familiar geography helps her grow or keeps her too close to old patterns.

### Beat 7: Processing USC

- After USC, Lÿsa Mandy needs quiet.
- She may like parts of it very much.
- She may also feel that the campus energy is too fast for how she thinks best.
- Fallon helps her separate admiration from fit.
- Charlie reminds her that liking a school is not the same as needing to live there.
- Piper helps write down Lÿsa Mandy’s impressions before they blur.
- USC remains a serious option, but not an automatic one.

### Beat 8: Michigan State University Tour

- They tour Michigan State University third.
- The campus feels large, green, and research-oriented.
- Lÿsa Mandy pays attention to animal science, biology, zoology-related coursework, research opportunities, and the seriousness of the academic environment.
- Michigan State feels strong and structured.
- It also feels far in a different way than Oregon.
- Lÿsa Mandy studies how the campus handles scale.
- She wants to know whether a large place can still give her a predictable rhythm.
- Fallon watches Lÿsa Mandy ask more confident questions by the third tour.

### Beat 9: Processing Michigan State

- After Michigan State, Lÿsa Mandy has three sets of impressions.
- Oregon State: quieter, water-adjacent, routine-friendly, animal-science/marine-life possibility, and the clearest answers to her Behavior & Ecology questions.
- USC: opportunity, California, intensity, proximity to family systems.
- Michigan State: research strength, animal science seriousness, scale, distance.
- Lÿsa Mandy begins comparing the schools in her own language.
- She talks about where she could breathe.
- She talks about where she could study dolphins someday.
- She talks about where she could be independent without being unanchored.
- Because of her Asperger Syndrome, she starts organizing the comparison into a linear sequence of criteria instead of leaving it as a swirl of impressions.

### Beat 10: The Adults Let Her Own the Choice

- Fallon, Charlie, and Piper do not tell her which school to choose.
- They each give observations only when asked.
- Fallon sees the emotional and medical-developmental side of fit.
- Charlie sees the logistics and whether the school can support her daily life.
- Piper sees the moments when Lÿsa Mandy’s attention becomes easiest and most alive.
- The adults agree on the most important thing: Lÿsa Mandy has to own the choice.

### Beat 11: After Michigan State — Going to French Woods

- After the Michigan State tour, Lÿsa Mandy goes to **French Woods**.
- The transition from university tours to camp matters.
- The tours have shown her possible adult futures.
- French Woods returns her to a known rhythm of cabins, schedules, activities, counselors, and summer structure.
- She carries Oregon State, USC, and Michigan State in her notes while entering the familiar world of camp.
- Fallon, Charlie, and Piper understand that French Woods gives her time to let the university impressions settle.
- Lÿsa Mandy does not have to decide everything in the car after Michigan State.
- She can let the decision become orderly inside the structured space of French Woods.

### Beat 12: Lÿsa Mandy’s Linear Decision

- Lÿsa Mandy makes the decision in a linear way because of her Asperger Syndrome.
- She does not choose Oregon State by impulse.
- She lines up the criteria one by one.
- First, she considers the clearest zoology and Behavior & Ecology answers.
- Second, she considers whether the campus rhythm feels predictable enough for her to function.
- Third, she considers whether the environment gives her access to animals, water, observation, and future dolphin work.
- Fourth, she considers whether she can be independent without being unanchored.
- Fifth, she considers whether the school feels like a place where she can build a routine instead of constantly recovering from the environment.
- Oregon State comes out first in her sequence.
- USC and Michigan State remain serious schools, but they do not line up as cleanly with the way her mind needs to choose.
- She also does not know that Samantha will attend Oregon State.
- Samantha’s OSU path is separate until move-in.
- Lÿsa Mandy chooses Oregon State because the linear decision leads there.
- Fallon notices that once the sequence is complete, Lÿsa Mandy becomes calmer.
- Charlie respects the clarity of the process.
- Piper understands that the decision is not cold; it is how Lÿsa Mandy makes a large future manageable.

### Beat 13: Final Image

- The final image is Lÿsa Mandy with notes, brochures, campus maps, and her own careful impressions from all three tours as she heads to French Woods after Michigan State.
- Oregon State University.
- University of Southern California.
- Michigan State University.
- She is still sixteen.
- The decision is made through her linear process.
- Oregon State is the school she chooses.
- Her future has become more visible because the sequence finally makes sense.
- Lÿsa Mandy does not know that Samantha is on her own separate road to the same university.
