# SCENE OUTLINE: THE FIRST HOLD — CHRISTMAS AT MCMASTER

## Placement

**When:** December 25, 2046 (Christmas Day). Twenty-four days after Lÿsa Mandy's birth.

**Where:** McMaster Children’s Hospital, Hamilton, Ontario.

**Continuity Lead-in:**
- Lÿsa Mandy was born on December 1st (Fallon's 13th birthday).
- She has been in the NICU for over three weeks due to birth complications and Fallon's young age.
- Fallon has been visiting every day, but has only been allowed to touch the baby through the incubator portholes.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene documents the first time thirteen-year-old Fallon Glenda is allowed to hold her daughter. It establishes the "Kangaroo Care" anchor and Mike's role as the fierce protector of Fallon's maternal dignity against institutional prejudice.

---

## Emotional Core

The scene is about the clash between **Institutional Judgment** (the doctor seeing a child) and **Maternal Reality** (Fallon being the mother). It highlights the "soft yell" of Mike Key—his low-frequency authority used to shield his daughter's right to her own child.

---

## Tone

Tense and clinical at the start; explosive and protective in the middle; quiet and sacred at the end.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — The Arrival at McMaster
Mike (or Terri Anne) takes Fallon Glenda to see Lÿsa Mandy. It is Christmas morning, and the hospital is quiet. This is the day the doctors have finally promised that Fallon can hold the baby.

### Beat 2 — The Parking Lot Split
Mike tells Fallon to go on ahead while he parks the car. Fallon walks into the hospital alone, her thirteen-year-old frame small in her winter coat, driven by the urgency of twenty-four days of waiting.

### Beat 3 — The Confrontation at the Door
At the door of the NICU ward, a doctor stops Fallon. He does not believe she is a mother; he thinks she is too young and tells her she doesn't belong on the restricted ward. He treats her like a child wandering where she shouldn't be.

### Beat 4 — The Argument
Fallon Glenda argues with the doctor, her voice shaking with anger and desperation as she tries to assert her identity. The doctor is dismissive, his prejudice against her age acting as a physical barrier to her daughter.

### Beat 5 — Mike’s Intervention
Mike steps out of the elevator and sees Fallon upset and cornered. He comes to her rescue immediately. 

### Beat 6 — The "Soft Yell"
Mike "softly yells" at the doctor—a low, vibrating intensity that carries absolute authority. He asserts that Fallon has every right to be on the ward and that her age does not diminish her status as Lÿsa Mandy's mother.

### Beat 7 — Entering the Room
The doctor steps aside. Mike guides the upset Fallon into the ward, reaffirming her dignity as they walk toward Room Four.

### Beat 8 — The First Hold
In the quiet of the NICU, the nurse places Lÿsa Mandy against Fallon's chest. The physical weight of the baby anchors Fallon's motherhood.

## What This Scene Establishes

1. **The Kangaroo Care Anchor:** The physical start of the lifelong bond between Fallon and Lÿsa.
2. **Mike’s Mentorship:** Further establishes Mike's role in protecting the "unseen" mothers of the family.
3. **Institutional Conflict:** Shows the reality of being a thirteen-year-old mother in a clinical setting.
