# SCENE OUTLINE: THE MILKING MACHINE — PROVIDING FOR LŸSA

## Placement

**When:** Early December 2046. A few days after Lÿsa Mandy’s birth.

**Where:** McMaster Children’s Hospital, Hamilton; later the Carlisle Mansion.

**Continuity Lead-in:**
- Lÿsa Mandy is in the NICU. Due to her "little stomach" and the complications of her early arrival, she is unable to nurse directly from Fallon.
- Fallon is thirteen and struggling with the feeling of helplessness that comes with a NICU stay.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene establishes the "Nursing Anchor" mentioned in Fallon’s profile. It shows Mike and Terri Anne’s practical, high-resource support—providing Fallon with an industrial-grade milking machine (hospital-grade breast pump) so she can provide for her daughter when direct nursing is impossible.

---

## Emotional Core

The scene is about **Agency through Provision**. Fallon cannot hold her baby yet, but she can feed her. The "industrial milking machine" becomes a tool of maternal power. It also highlights Mike and Terri Anne’s "no-expense-spared" protective style.

---

## Tone

Clinical, determined, and practical. There is a shift from Fallon’s initial frustration to a sense of purpose.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — The Context of the Tube
Fallon sits by Lÿsa Mandy's incubator. She watches the nurses at McMaster Children's Hospital administer milk through a nasogastric (NG) tube. Lÿsa’s stomach is so small and her system so fragile that she cannot coordinate nursing. The hospital is currently providing donor milk or specialized formula through the tube, but the medical goal is to transition to Fallon's own milk.

### Beat 2 — The Emotional Gap
Fallon feels a profound sense of distance. She watches the "hospital milk" go through the tube and feels like an observer in her own daughter's life. She wants to be the one providing the milk that goes through that tube.

### Beat 3 — The Industrial Solution
Mike and Terri Anne arrive at the hospital. They have seen the struggle. Instead of just a standard consumer pump, they have used their resources to purchase an industrial-grade, hospital-standard milking machine—the best in the world.

### Beat 4 — The Delivery to the Mansion
Mike has a second unit installed in Fallon’s suite at the Carlisle mansion, and a portable industrial unit for her private room at the hospital. He explains that if she wants to do this, she will have the best tools available.

### Beat 5 — Learning the Machine
Fallon looks at the machine—chrome, clinical, and powerful. It’s intimidating. Terri Anne sits with her, helping her set it up. There is no shame, only the practical reality of motherhood. 

### Beat 6 — The First Bottle
The first time Fallon successfully produces milk using the machine. It’s a tiny amount—just enough for a syringe or a small tube feed—but it changes everything. 

### Beat 7 — The Provision
Fallon watches the nurse administer the milk to Lÿsa Mandy. For the first time, Fallon feels like she is "doing" something. The machine isn't just a medical device; it's her way of being present when she can't be holding her.

## What This Scene Establishes

1. **The Nursing Anchor:** The literal start of the extended breastfeeding bond that lasts three years.
2. **Resource-Driven Protection:** How Mike and Terri Anne use wealth to solve the practical problems of trauma and recovery.
3. **Fallon’s Resilience:** Her willingness to do the "work" of motherhood (pumping every three hours) at age thirteen.
