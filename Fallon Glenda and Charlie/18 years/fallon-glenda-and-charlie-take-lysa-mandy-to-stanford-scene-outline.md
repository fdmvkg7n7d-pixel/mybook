# SCENE OUTLINE: FALLON GLENDA AND CHARLIE TAKE LŸSA MANDY TO STANFORD

## Placement
**When:** Shortly after returning from New York City, during the university preparation period.  
**Where:** Stanford University campus (bookstore, registrar/administrative buildings, and surrounding areas).  
**Lÿsa Mandy’s age:** Eight.  
**Context:** Fallon Glenda and Charlie are preparing for the new university term. They bring Lÿsa Mandy with them because they prefer to include her rather than arrange separate care.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene establishes:
- Fallon and Charlie’s choice to bring Lÿsa Mandy along as part of their normal family rhythm.
- Lÿsa Mandy being included in her parents’ university world as a natural extension of their life together.
- A contrast between the luxurious New York vacation and the grounded, everyday routines of their life in the Bay Area.
- The family’s preference for staying together rather than separating for practical errands.

---

## Emotional Core

The emotional core is **intentional family inclusion**.

Fallon and Charlie are not creating a special outing — they are simply doing what they need to do and choosing to bring Lÿsa Mandy with them because that is how they prefer to move through their days. The scene shows quiet togetherness and the way they weave their university responsibilities into family life.

---

## Tone

Warm, calm, affectionate, and matter-of-fact. The scene should feel natural and grounded.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — The Decision at Home
In the kitchen or living room, Fallon and Charlie discuss their plan for the day. They need to go to Stanford to buy textbooks and pick up their official timetables. One of them mentions the option of arranging care for Lÿsa Mandy. They quickly decide against it.

> “Let’s just take her with us. She’ll be fine.”

Lÿsa Mandy, who has been listening from the doorway, perks up.

### Beat 2 — The Drive to Stanford
They drive south from San Francisco to Stanford. Lÿsa Mandy sits in the back with a small backpack containing a book, a snack, and her new baseball cap from New York. The mood in the car is calm but focused — this is not a fun outing; it is an errand.

### Beat 3 — Arriving on Campus
They park and walk across campus. Lÿsa Mandy holds Fallon’s hand. The campus feels familiar to Fallon and Charlie but still large and slightly overwhelming to Lÿsa Mandy. She stays close.

### Beat 4 — Bookstore
Their first stop is the Stanford Bookstore. Fallon and Charlie each have lists of required textbooks. They move efficiently through the aisles, comparing prices and editions.

Lÿsa Mandy walks beside them, occasionally touching the spines of books or looking at the displays. She is quiet and well-behaved, understanding that this is not playtime. Fallon occasionally explains what a certain book is for in simple terms.

### Beat 5 — Picking Up Timetables
They go to the administrative building to collect their official class timetables. While waiting in line, Lÿsa Mandy sits on a bench and reads her book or watches the students passing by. Fallon and Charlie take turns holding her hand so she doesn’t get lost in the crowd.

When they finally receive their printed timetables, they stand together comparing schedules, quietly discussing who will handle pickup and drop-off on which days.

### Beat 6 — Lÿsa Mandy’s Experience
Lÿsa Mandy is not bored, but she is clearly in “good girl” mode. She does not complain. She helps carry one of the lighter bags of books. At one point she asks:

> “Is this where you go every day?”

Fallon answers honestly but gently.

### Beat 7 — Small Breaks
They take one short break for water or a snack from a campus café. They sit on a bench and let Lÿsa Mandy rest her legs. The conversation is practical — they talk about which classes overlap, who will study when, and how they will manage Lÿsa Mandy’s Sacred Heart schedule alongside their own.

### Beat 8 — The Drive Home
On the way back to San Francisco, Lÿsa Mandy falls asleep in the back seat with a textbook balanced on her lap (one of Fallon’s lighter ones). Fallon and Charlie speak quietly in the front about the term ahead.

There is no grand reflection — just the quiet satisfaction of having accomplished what they needed to do as a family.

### Beat 9 — Emotional Landing
That evening at home, Lÿsa Mandy helps unpack the new books onto the kitchen table. She places her baseball cap next to the stack of textbooks. The New York trip already feels like a distant, sparkling memory compared to the steady reality of this day.

Fallon and Charlie feel the weight of the upcoming term, but also the quiet strength of doing it together — with Lÿsa Mandy beside them.

2. Lÿsa Mandy is included in their university life out of necessity, not indulgence.
