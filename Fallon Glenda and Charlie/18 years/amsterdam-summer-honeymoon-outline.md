## Canon Setup

## Lÿsa Mandy Placement

During the honeymoon, **eight-year-old Lÿsa Mandy is away at French Woods for her second summer / second season**, staying for **two sessions totaling six weeks**.

That detail matters.

She is **not** being sent somewhere unfamiliar for the first time so Fallon and Charlie can slip away. She is returning to a place she already knows and enjoys, especially because it suits her love of:

- **ballet**
- **gymnastics**
- **swimming**

This gives the honeymoon moral and emotional permission. Fallon Glenda, especially, would not truly relax if Lÿsa Mandy were frightened, destabilized, or newly displaced.

---

## Key Emotional Choice

The most important scene choice is this:

**The major trust-and-boundaries conversation happens in the hotel room *before* Fun4Two, not after.**

Why this is the stronger version:

- It lets Fallon say the hard thing before silence hardens around it.
- It allows Charlie to prove, in advance, that desire does not outrank care.
- It makes the later scenes feel like confirmation and debrief, not first-response damage control.

---

## Scene Function of the Fun4Two Material

At Fun4Two, Fallon and Charlie **go apart by mutual agreement for part of the evening** and later reconnect.

The emotional point is **not** shock, betrayal, or collapse.
The emotional point is:

- spoken boundaries
- mutual courage
- return
- reassurance
- marriage-deepening honesty

The after-scene should be comparatively brief.
The **morning-after** scene should be soft, reflective, and confirming.

---

## Character-Specific Reading Note

This material should always remember who these two are:

- **Fallon Glenda** is direct, grounded, and shaped by survival; she does better with truth than with vagueness.
- **Charlie** is patient, warm, and unshowy; his strength is that he stays.
- The emotional success of the honeymoon depends less on glamour than on whether the two of them keep choosing one another in clear language.
