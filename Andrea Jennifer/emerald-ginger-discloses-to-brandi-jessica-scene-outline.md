# EMERALD GINGER DISCLOSES TO BRANDI JESSICA

## Scene Outline — Beats, Dialogue Cues, and Thematic Notes

**POV:** Emerald Ginger (with brief shifts to Brandi Jessica's perception)
**Tone:** Hushed, frightened, finally safe — the unburdening
**Location:** Brandi Jessica and Jamal's home, **Las Vegas, Nevada** — a few days after Emerald Ginger arrives (see `emerald-ginger-runs-to-brandi-jessica-and-jamal-scene-outline.md`)
**Ages:** Emerald Ginger is **~17**, now into the pregnancy; Brandi Jessica is a settled young adult (~21–22, with Jamal). They are **first cousins**.
**Time in saga:** [TBD — same anchor as the runaway scene; Brandi's UNLV years, ~2065–2067.]

## CONTENT NOTE

This is the **telling**, not the event. **What happened to Emerald Ginger — the cause of the pregnancy — is kept entirely off-page and non-graphic.** The disclosure itself is rendered through restraint: the reader understands the weight and the truth without any depiction or detail of the abuse. Emerald Ginger is a minor; the focus is on her being **believed, held, and protected**. Specifically, the identity of the perpetrator can be conveyed without graphic content — the horror is in the fact, not the description.

---

## BEFORE THE SCENE — WHAT HAS SHIFTED

*A few days of safety have changed what is possible.*

- Emerald Ginger has been in the Las Vegas house long enough to sleep, eat, and breathe. Brandi and Jamal have asked nothing (per the runaway scene's Beat 4). The not-asking is exactly what makes telling possible.
- Brandi has already promised: *"You don't have to tell me anything... but you're safe here."* That promise is the ground Emerald Ginger now stands on.
- Brandi has kept her word from the runaway scene's Beat 5 — she has let Andrea Jennifer know only that Emerald Ginger is **alive and safe**, nothing more. Emerald Ginger knows this. It is part of why she trusts her.

**Thematic note:** This rhymes with Emma "reaching" in Jessica's profile — the survivor who learned to need less finally asking to be seen. Here, the child who ran finally speaks.

---

## BEAT 1 — THE APPROACH

*Emerald Ginger chooses the moment. It must be hers.*

- A quiet, low-stakes setting — late, the house still, maybe Jamal deliberately gives them space (or is out), so it's just the two cousins. Brandi is doing something ordinary with her hands (tea, braille, the dishes) — present but not staring, which for a blind cousin is literal and for Emerald Ginger is a relief: *no one is watching her face.*
- Emerald Ginger circles it. Starts and stops. Brandi doesn't rush her, doesn't finish her sentences.
- **Dialogue cue (Brandi, gentle):** "Whatever it is, it can't get out of this room unless you let it. Take your time."

---

## BEAT 2 — THE TELLING (OFF-PAGE CORE)

*The truth comes out. Rendered with restraint.*

- Emerald Ginger tells her. **Not all of it — enough.** (Echo the runaway scene's language: *"Not all of it. Enough."*)
- **On the page:** fragments, silences, the things she can't say spoken around rather than through. The reader assembles the truth from her fear and from Brandi's dawning understanding — *not* from explicit narration.
- The two hard facts that land, without graphic detail:
  - She is pregnant.
  - **The father is Fachnan** — her mother's partner, the father of her three younger half-siblings.
- **Brandi's perception (POV shift):** She can't see her cousin's face, so she reads the whole thing through breath and tremor and the long pauses. When the name lands, Brandi goes very still — the same stillness as Jamal at the door. She understands the size of it instantly: this is not a teenage romance gone wrong. This is a grown man and a child. *This is abuse.*

---

## BEAT 3 — THE RESPONSE THAT MATTERS

*Believed. No cross-examination. The weight transferred.*

- Brandi does **not** ask "are you sure," does not ask what she did, does not defend Fachnan, does not protect the family's image. She believes her instantly.
- **Dialogue cue (Brandi):** "I believe you. None of this is yours. Not one piece of it. You hear me?" *(Deliberate echo of Andrea Jennifer's line in the police-call beat — the family speaks with one voice.)*
- Brandi reaches for her — finds her arm, then pulls her in — the way she did at the door. Physical safety as the answer to spoken fear.
- **Emerald Ginger's interior:** The terror she's carried since the third month — that telling would make it *real*, would burn everything down — meets something she didn't expect: she said it, and the world did not end. Brandi is still here. Still holding her.

---

## BEAT 4 — THE PROMISE AND THE TURN TOWARD HOME

*Brandi names what has to happen — without taking Emerald Ginger's agency.*

- Brandi is honest: this cannot stay a secret in this room. Not because Emerald Ginger did anything wrong — because a child is not safe and a crime has been committed, and the people who love her have to be able to protect her.
- But she keeps Emerald Ginger at the center of it:
- **Dialogue cue (Brandi):** "Here's what I won't do: I won't carry this alone and I won't make you carry it alone either. We tell your mom. Together. I'll be right there — on the phone, on a plane, however you need me. And then your mom is going to do exactly what a mother should do. But you will never be alone in a room with this again. That part is over."
- Optional: Brandi gently raises that there may be a baby to think about, and a decision that is **Emerald Ginger's** to make — handing her agency, not prescribing.

---

## BEAT 5 — THE BRIDGE

*Set up the next scene without resolving it.*

- They agree to call Andrea Jennifer. Maybe not tonight — but soon, and together. Emerald Ginger asks Brandi to be with her when she tells her mother.
- **End image:** Emerald Ginger lighter by exactly the weight of one secret — not healed, but no longer alone. Brandi's hand still on her arm. The phone, dark, waiting on the table — the same object that will define the next beat.
