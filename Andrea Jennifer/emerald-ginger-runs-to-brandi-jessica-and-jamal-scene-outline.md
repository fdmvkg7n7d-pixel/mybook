**Tone:** Frightened, then sheltering — fear meeting unconditional safety

## CONTENT NOTE

This scene is the **flight and the shelter** — fear, running, and being taken in. The cause of the pregnancy is **deliberately kept off the page**; it is never depicted, narrated, or detailed here. This outline focuses on what the saga does best: a frightened child being **noticed, believed, and protected**.

## BEFORE THE SCENE — WHAT EMERALD GINGER CARRIES TO LAS VEGAS

*Establish, briefly, the weight she is running with — without naming it.*

- Emerald Ginger is **three months pregnant** and terrified. She has told no one. She is the eldest of Andrea Jennifer's children — the responsible one, the first — and the secret she carries feels like it would burn the whole family down if she spoke it.
- She has **already run** (see the runaway beat): left the note (*"I'm okay. Don't look for me. I'm sorry"*), gone out the side door before dawn. She is not running *to* a plan — she is running *from* a house she cannot stay in.
- Why Brandi Jessica: she is **family but not a parent** — close enough to trust, far enough that telling her doesn't detonate Emerald Ginger's whole world. And Las Vegas is genuinely **far** from the family's New York/Halifax/LA orbit — a believable distance to disappear into.
- Emerald Ginger does **not** intend to tell Brandi the truth when she arrives. She just wants a door that will open. The telling, if it comes, comes later.

**Thematic note:** This rhymes with the saga's recurring pattern of the family taking in the child who has nowhere safe to go — Mike and Terri Anne taking in Emma; Patti Anne fostering Klára. The Key family "does nothing alone," and here a *cousin*, not an elder, is the one who opens the door.

## BEAT 1 — THE DOOR AT MIDNIGHT

*Arrival. The traveled-too-far exhaustion of a frightened kid who made it.*

- Late. Emerald Ginger has come by bus / cheap flight, little money, phone off so she can't be tracked or talked out of it. She stands on Brandi and Jamal's doorstep in a strange desert city.
- **Jamal answers** — because Brandi can't see who's knocking. He goes very still when he recognizes her.
- **Dialogue cue (Jamal, careful, over his shoulder):** "Brandi. It's Emerald Ginger."

**Brandi's perception (brief POV shift):** She reads the situation the way she reads everything — through what she hears. The hour. The silence on the step. The way Jamal said the name. She knows *something is wrong* before anyone says a word.

---

## BEAT 2 — NO INTERROGATION

*Brandi's defining move: shelter first, questions never (not yet).*

- Brandi comes to the door, hand trailing the wall, and finds her cousin — first her arm, then her shoulders — and pulls her inside.
- She does **not** ask why she's there, how she got there, what happened, or whether their family knows. She asks none of it.
- **Dialogue cue (Brandi):** "Okay. You're here. Come inside. We'll figure out the rest in the morning."
- Jamal moves into quiet host-mode: closes the door, gets water, a blanket, makes up a place to sleep. Two steady adults, no alarm, no demands.

**Thematic note:** Brandi's whole life has taught her how to be met with patience instead of panic (Julia waiting half a block behind on the Manhattan Drops; Kiley refusing to coddle but never abandoning). She gives her cousin exactly what she herself was always given: *room*.

---

## BEAT 3 — THE LONG EXHALE

*The body lets go once it's finally safe.*

- For three months Emerald Ginger has been holding her breath — since the side door, since before that. In the quiet of a strange kitchen, with a cousin who isn't asking anything, the holding finally cracks. She cries, or shakes, or simply goes boneless with relief. No words required.
- Brandi doesn't fill the silence. (Beat: she is one of the only people in the family who knows how to *not* fill a silence.) She just keeps a hand on her cousin so Emerald Ginger knows she's not alone in the dark.
- **Optional detail unique to canon:** Brandi can't see her cousin's face, so she reads the whole thing through touch and breath — the tremor in the shoulders, the hitch in the breathing — and understands more from that than sight would have told her.

---

## BEAT 4 — THE UNSPOKEN UNDERSTANDING

*Brandi senses the size of it without being told.*

- Brandi gathers, from the hour and the distance and the way her cousin is coming apart, that this is **not** a small thing — not a fight with a friend, not boredom, not a whim. Something that drove a 17-year-old across the country in the night.
- She does not push. She offers the one thing that matters most:
- **Dialogue cue (Brandi):** "You don't have to tell me anything tonight. Or ever, if you don't want to. But you're safe here. You can stay as long as you need. Nobody's making you go anywhere."
- **Emerald Ginger's interior:** The first loosening of the secret's grip — not because she's told it, but because for the first time someone has said *you're safe* and she believes it.

---

## BEAT 5 — THE QUESTION OF HOME (THE HOOK)

*Set up the disclosure and the protective arc to come — without resolving it here.*

- The unavoidable shadow: **Andrea Jennifer.** Emerald Ginger's mother is, by now, frantic (she found the note). Brandi knows the family will be searching. She has to hold two truths at once: her cousin needs to *not be made to go back yet*, and the people who love her need to know she's alive.
- A gentle, honest negotiation — Brandi does **not** seize control or call Andrea Jennifer behind her cousin's back. She names the bind plainly and keeps Emerald Ginger's agency at the center.
- **Dialogue cue (Brandi):** "Here's the only thing I won't do — I won't let your mom think you're dead. We don't have to tell her anything else. Not why, not where you'll go next. Just that you're with me and you're okay. Can we give her that much? And the rest — the rest is yours, and we'll do it when you're ready."
- **End on the threshold, not the resolution:** Emerald Ginger nods, or doesn't yet — but the door to the disclosure has opened. This is the bridge to the next beats: *she tells Brandi the truth → Brandi helps her tell Andrea Jennifer → Andrea Jennifer immediately calls the police.*
