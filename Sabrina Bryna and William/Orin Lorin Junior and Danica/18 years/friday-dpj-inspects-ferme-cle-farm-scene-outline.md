# Friday — DPJ Inspects Ferme Clé Farm — Scene Outline

## Scene Information

- **Characters:**
  - **Danica (Xochitl)** — 16 years old. Living at Ferme Clé after leaving her parents’ home at the beginning of Christmas break. Back at BCS this week, fragile but steadier.
  - **Orin Lorin Junior** — 19 years old. Second-year McGill student, biology and chemistry, pre-medical track. Rearranged his Friday so he can be present for the DPJ home visit.
  - **Mme Claire Desrosiers** — DPJ youth-protection worker. Francophone Quebecer, early forties, practical, observant, professional. Warm enough not to frighten Danica, but not casual; she is there to assess safety.
  - **Madame Hélène Bélanger** — Ferme Clé chef. Late fifties, Francophone Quebecer. Briefly onstage or referenced; her kitchen presence helps show the farm is staffed and functional.
  - **Martin Gagnon** — Ferme Clé farm-office / operations staff member. Possible driver for Danica when Orin is unavailable. Shows the farm truck with the Ferme Clé logo.
- **Offstage / referenced:** Dr. Whitfield, Ms. Renée Lapointe, Mme Beaudoin, Danica’s parents, the SQ officers, Sabrina Bryna and William, BCS.
- **Location:** Ferme Clé — front drive, kitchen, living room, spare bedroom, Orin’s bedroom doorway, bathrooms, farm office, barn, rabbit area, vehicle area.
- **When:** **Friday afternoon, around four o’clock**, after Danica’s first week back at BCS and after her Wednesday meeting with Ms. Renée Lapointe. Danica has just come from school and is **still wearing her BCS uniform**. The DPJ visit was anticipated since the police visit.
- **Tone:** Serious, procedural, controlled, quietly tense. The farm is beautiful, but this is not a tour for pleasure. It is an inspection of whether Danica is safe there.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene brings the DPJ into Ferme Clé physically. The worker inspects the property, confirms Danica’s sleeping arrangements, assesses the household structure, asks Orin direct questions, speaks to Danica privately, and determines whether Ferme Clé can remain a safe temporary placement while the file develops.

The scene should show:

1. Orin’s transparency.
2. Danica’s anxiety about being taken away.
3. The farm as stable, staffed, and orderly.
4. Clear separation between Danica’s room and Orin’s room.
5. Practical systems for school transportation and supervision.
6. The DPJ worker neither romanticizing nor demonizing Orin — she assesses facts.

---

- She had a police nightmare Tuesday morning at six o’clock; Orin rushed from his cornflakes to help ground her.

- Today is the planned DPJ farm inspection / home visit.

# Scene Outline

## Beat 1: Waiting for the DPJ Worker

Friday afternoon, around four o’clock. Ferme Clé is clean but not staged. Orin has not tried to make the farm look unrealistically perfect; he has simply made sure nothing is chaotic.

Danica has just come back from BCS and is **still wearing her uniform**: skirt, blouse, tie, blazer with the crest, tights, and the boots that still do not quite belong with the uniform but have become part of this week. At the door, she removes her boots carefully before stepping onto the wooden floor. Part of it is practical — she does not want to track snow and slush into the house — and part of it is habit from her Japanese upbringing. Shoes come off at the entrance. Even here, even now, that custom remains in her body.

She is nervous and keeps touching the cuff of her blazer, the way she does when she is trying to stay composed.

Orin is calm, but he is not pretending the visit does not matter. His McGill books are stacked on the kitchen counter because he rearranged his day for this.

Possible dialogue:

> “What if she says I can’t stay?”
>
> “Then we ask what the next step is.”
>
> “That isn’t an answer.”
>
> “It’s the honest one.”

He does not promise what he cannot control.

> “I can promise you this: we tell the truth. All of it. The truth is our strongest position.”

That echoes Dr. Whitfield’s earlier warning.

---

## Beat 2: Mme Claire Desrosiers Arrives

A government vehicle or modest sedan comes up the long drive. Mme Claire Desrosiers steps out with a shoulder bag, clipboard, and winter coat. She looks around before she even reaches the door:

- the main house,
- the barn,
- the driveway,
- the distance from the road,
- the staff vehicles,
- the lighting,
- the general condition of the property.

Orin opens the door before she has to knock twice.

> “Mme Desrosiers?”
>
> “Oui. Direction de la protection de la jeunesse.”
>
> “Orin Lorin Junior.”

He does not over-explain. He lets her enter.

Danica’s boots are already off by the door, placed neatly to one side so the snow does not touch the wooden floor. Claire notices, but she does not comment or make it strange. She understands enough about Danica’s Japanese customs to recognize the gesture for what it is: respect for the home, not nervousness or performance.

Claire stamps her own boots carefully on the outside mat and stays on the entry rug with her clipboard and shoulder bag in hand. Her attention remains on the visit itself, not on correcting Danica’s customs.

Danica stands slightly behind Orin, then makes herself step forward.

> “Bonjour, madame.”
>
> “Bonjour, Danica.”

Mme Desrosiers says her name without surprise, which helps. She is expected. She is not a raid.

---

## Beat 3: Opening Conversation at the Kitchen Table

They sit at the kitchen table first. Hélène may have left tea, water, or simple biscuits, but she does not hover.

Mme Desrosiers explains the visit plainly.

> “I am here because the police referred the file after locating you here. My job today is not to punish anyone. My job is to assess whether you are safe, whether your basic needs are being met, and what needs to happen next.”

She looks at both of them.

> “I will speak to you together. I will speak to each of you separately. I will look at where Danica sleeps. I will look at the common areas, exits, bathrooms, and transportation arrangements. I may ask uncomfortable questions. If I do, it is because I need answers for the file, not because I have already decided what those answers mean.”

Orin nods.

> “Understood.”

Danica nods more slowly.

---

## Beat 4: Basic Needs Checklist

Mme Desrosiers starts with the practical questions.

### For Danica

- Is she eating regularly?
- Does she have clothing?
- Does she have her school uniform?
- Is she attending BCS?
- Does she have access to medication, toiletries, menstrual products, and privacy?
- Does she have a phone or a way to contact emergency services?
- Does she feel physically safe in the house?
- Does she know how to leave the house if she needs help?

### For Orin

- Who owns the property?
- Who lives here?
- Who works here?
- What staff are present during the day?
- Who has keys?
- Who drives Danica to and from school?
- Does Orin understand that Danica is sixteen and still a minor?
- Has there been any sexual contact?
- Is Danica free to call the DPJ, police, BCS, or friends?

Orin answers directly, even when the questions are uncomfortable.

> “She has her own room.”
>
> “She is not locked in.”
>
> “She can call BCS, the DPJ, the police, or her friends.”
>
> “I understand she is sixteen.”
>
> “I understand the age difference matters.”
>
> “I am not trying to become her legal guardian.”
>
> “I am trying to make sure she is not unsafe while the adults and the court process catch up.”

Mme Desrosiers writes that down.

---

## Beat 5: Inspecting the Spare Bedroom

Danica leads the way to the spare bedroom.

The room is simple but comfortable:

- bed,
- clean sheets,
- quilt or comforter,
- dresser drawers now partly filled with clothes Orin bought her,
- BCS uniform hanging neatly,
- schoolbooks stacked on the desk or chair,
- toiletries in a small basket,
- possibly the Christmas-present shirt folded where she can see it.

Mme Desrosiers does not touch Danica’s private things without asking.

> “May I open the closet?”
>
> “Yes.”

She checks that Danica has a real sleeping space, not a symbolic one staged for inspection.

She notices signs of actual use:

- worn slippers or socks,
- school papers,
- a hairbrush,
- laundry basket,
- the BCS agenda,
- maybe the folded safety plan from Ms. Lapointe in her blazer pocket or on the desk.

> “You sleep here?”
>
> “Yes.”
>
> “Every night?”

Danica hesitates because of Monday night.

> “Usually. Monday I fell asleep on the couch.”

Mme Desrosiers looks at her, then at Orin.

Orin answers before it becomes suspicious.

> “She fell asleep studying. I put a quilt over her and left her there. I did not wake her.”

Danica adds softly:

> “He was at the other end of the couch doing chemistry.”

Mme Desrosiers writes it down without comment.

---

## Beat 6: Inspecting Orin’s Room and Boundaries

Mme Desrosiers asks to see Orin’s bedroom, or at least its location relative to Danica’s.

Orin opens the door himself.

The room is clearly his:

- McGill books,
- organic chemistry notes,
- biology textbook,
- clothes,
- laptop,
- maybe a framed family photo,
- no sign that Danica sleeps there.

Mme Desrosiers checks distance, doors, privacy, and whether the arrangement matches what they have said.

She may ask:

> “Does Danica ever sleep in this room?”
>
> “No.”

She turns to Danica.

> “Is that true?”
>
> “Yes.”

This is not accusatory in tone, but it is necessary.

---

## Beat 7: Bathroom, Laundry, and Household Function

Mme Desrosiers asks to see the bathroom Danica uses, the laundry area, and the kitchen.

The goal is to establish that Danica can live there as a student, not merely hide there.

Details:

- towels available,
- toiletries,
- laundry access,
- clean kitchen,
- food in the fridge,
- cereal boxes including cornflakes,
- Hélène’s note or Danica’s thank-you note may be visible.

Mme Desrosiers notices Danica’s thank-you note to Hélène if it is still displayed or mentioned.

> “You wrote this?”
>
> “Yes. She made dinner Monday.”
>
> “That was kind.”

The note matters because it shows a developing household rhythm and Danica responding to care.

---

## Beat 8: Staff and Supervision

Mme Desrosiers asks about staff.

Orin explains:

- Ferme Clé is owned by Sabrina Bryna and William as an investment property.
- They do not live there.
- The farm has professional barn staff.
- Hélène cooks and lives over the carriage house or nearby.
- Martin Gagnon handles farm operations / office matters and can drive if arranged.
- Staff are employees, not family substitutes.
- Orin is the resident family member on the property.

Mme Desrosiers may ask to meet one staff member briefly.

Hélène can appear at the kitchen doorway, wiping her hands on a towel.

> “Bonjour.”

She confirms simply:

- Danica has been eating.
- Danica is polite.
- Danica wrote her a thank-you note.
- Hélène is present many mornings and evenings but does not supervise Danica as a guardian.

Hélène does not oversell the situation. That makes her credible.

---

## Beat 9: Inspecting the Barn and Grounds

They put on coats and boots and go outside.

The inspection includes:

- the path from the house to the barn,
- exterior lighting,
- driveway access,
- distance to the road,
- farm vehicles,
- barn entrance,
- staff presence,
- any hazards Danica should avoid.

Orin is clear:

> “Danica does not work in the barn. She can visit with me or staff present. She is not responsible for the horses or rabbits.”

This matters. Danica is not being exchanged for labor or expected to earn her keep.

Inside the barn:

- horses in stalls,
- staff moving hay or feed,
- rabbits in their area,
- the smell of clean straw and winter animals,
- staff greeting Orin normally,
- Danica recognized but not stared at.

Mme Desrosiers watches how people react to Danica. She is looking for whether Danica seems hidden, displayed, controlled, or simply present.

Danica relaxes a little in the barn because the animals are not asking questions.

---

## Beat 10: The Ferme Clé Logo Truck

Outside near the vehicle area, Orin shows the farm truck used for school pickups when he has class.

The truck has the **Ferme Clé logo** on the door or side panel — the same logo as the sign by the gate.

Possible logo description:

- a stylized key crossing a horse silhouette,
- the words **FERME CLÉ** in dark green or black,
- perhaps a small rabbit motif beneath the lettering.

Orin explains the pickup system:

> “If I cannot pick Danica up because of McGill, she knows the driver’s name before the end of the school day. BCS knows the driver’s name. The truck has the farm logo. If any detail does not match, she goes back inside the school and calls me or the office.”

Mme Desrosiers asks Danica:

> “Do you understand that plan?”
>
> “Yes.”
>
> “Do you feel comfortable refusing to get in if something is wrong?”

Danica hesitates, then says:

> “If I’m inside the school, yes.”

Mme Desrosiers nods.

> “Then the plan is: you wait inside the school until the vehicle and driver are confirmed. Not outside alone.”

This improves the plan and aligns with Ms. Lapointe’s school safety plan.

---

## Beat 11: Private Conversation with Danica

Mme Desrosiers asks Orin to step away so she can speak with Danica privately.

Orin does so immediately.

He does not look wounded. He does not argue. He does not ask to stay.

That matters.

Danica and Mme Desrosiers sit either in the kitchen, the spare bedroom with the door open, or the counselling-like corner of the living room. The location should feel private but not closed in.

Mme Desrosiers asks direct questions:

- Do you want to remain here today?
- Are you afraid of Orin?
- Has Orin threatened you?
- Has Orin told you not to speak to anyone?
- Has Orin touched you in any way you did not want?
- Do you have access to a phone?
- Do you want contact with your parents right now?
- Do you understand that the DPJ may need to speak with your parents?
- Do you understand this is temporary unless there is a legal arrangement?

Danica answers:

> “I want to stay here.”
>
> “I am not afraid of him.”
>
> “He asks before touching me.”
>
> “He tells me to tell the truth.”
>
> “I do not want to go home right now.”

She may mention the police nightmare:

> “I dreamed the police came back and took me.”

Mme Desrosiers answers carefully:

> “My job is not to surprise you. If the plan changes, you will be told. You may not like every step, but you should not be ambushed.”

---

## Beat 12: Private Conversation with Orin

Then Danica steps out, and Mme Desrosiers speaks to Orin alone.

Her tone becomes more direct.

> “You understand why this arrangement raises questions.”
>
> “Yes.”
>
> “You are nineteen. She is sixteen. You are romantically involved?”

Orin does not evade.

> “Yes.”

Mme Desrosiers lets that sit.

> “Then I need you to understand something clearly. Your good intentions do not erase the power imbalance. Money, housing, transportation, age, and her estrangement from her parents all matter.”

Orin accepts it.

> “I understand.”

> “Do not isolate her. Do not become her only adult. Do not make her dependent on your approval to remain safe.”

> “I won’t.”

> “Good. Then prove it structurally, not emotionally. School counsellor. DPJ. Legal aid. Clear transportation plan. Separate bedroom. Staff aware enough to be useful but not invasive. No secrets from professionals.”

Orin nods.

> “That’s what I want too.”

She studies him.

> “Wanting it is not the same as doing it.”
>
> “Then I’ll do it.”

---

## Beat 13: The Preliminary Assessment

Mme Desrosiers brings them back together at the kitchen table.

She does not make a grand declaration. She gives a preliminary assessment.

> “Based on what I have seen today, I do not have an immediate safety concern that requires removing Danica from this property today.”

Danica exhales so sharply she almost shakes.

Mme Desrosiers continues:

> “That does not mean the file is closed. It does not mean this arrangement is legally settled. It means that for today, while the DPJ continues the assessment, Ferme Clé is an acceptable temporary location.”

She lists conditions:

1. Danica continues attending BCS.
2. BCS has a verified pickup plan and emergency contact process.
3. Danica has direct access to the DPJ worker’s phone number.
4. Danica’s bedroom remains separate.
5. Orin cooperates fully with DPJ and legal aid.
6. Any contact from Danica’s parents is reported.
7. No one lies about Danica’s location to professionals involved in the file.
8. A follow-up meeting will be scheduled.

Danica nods through tears.

Orin nods once, serious.

---

## Beat 14: Legal Aid and Next Steps

Mme Desrosiers asks about legal aid / emancipation.

Danica says she wants to learn more.

Mme Desrosiers gives or confirms contact information and explains next steps plainly:

- DPJ assessment continues.
- Parents will be contacted or interviewed in a controlled way.
- Danica may speak with legal aid about simple emancipation.
- The court process is not instant.
- Danica should not expect one visit to solve everything.

Possible line:

> “Today is not the end. It is the first written step that says where you are and what you are asking for.”

Danica holds onto that.

---

## Beat 15: Mme Desrosiers Leaves

Orin walks Mme Desrosiers to the door. Danica stays by the kitchen table or follows to the foyer.

At the door, Mme Desrosiers says to Danica:

> “If you are afraid, call me. If you change your mind, call me. If anyone pressures you not to call me, call me immediately.”

She gives Orin a look when she says the last part. He accepts it.

> “Understood.”

The DPJ car goes down the long driveway.

The house is quiet again, but not the same quiet as before. The farm has been inspected. The reality has been written down.

---

## Beat 16: Conversation After Claire Leaves

Danica and Orin stand in the foyer after the door closes. Through the front window, they watch Claire Desrosiers walk to her car, start the engine, and drive slowly down the long gravel driveway.

Neither of them speaks until the DPJ car has passed the gate.

Danica is still in her BCS uniform, blazer buttoned, tie slightly crooked from the long day. Her boots are still neatly placed by the door where she removed them earlier so she would not bring snow onto the wooden floor.

For a moment, she just stares at the closed door.

> “She said I can stay today.”

Orin answers carefully.

> “Yes. Today.”

Danica turns her head toward him.

> “You keep saying the careful part.”
>
> “Because the careful part keeps us safe.”

She looks down at her socked feet on the wooden floor.

> “I wanted her to say forever.”
>
> “I know.”
>
> “I wanted her to say I never have to go back.”
>
> “I know.”

Orin does not rush to fill the silence. He lets the disappointment be real. Claire’s assessment was good news, but it was not the kind of good news a frightened sixteen-year-old wants. It was procedural. Temporary. Conditional.

Danica swallows.

> “She looked in my closet.”
>
> “She asked first.”
>
> “I know. I’m not angry.”
>
> “Okay.”
>
> “It just felt strange. Like proving I exist here.”

Orin nods.

> “That is what today was.”
>
> “Proving I exist?”
>
> “Proving you have a real bed, real clothes, real schoolbooks, real food, real ways to call people. Proving this is not hiding.”

Danica looks toward the hallway where her spare bedroom is.

> “And proving I don’t sleep in your room.”

Orin’s face stays steady.

> “Yes.”

She waits for him to make it softer. He does not, because softening it would make it less true.

> “Did that bother you?” he asks.

Danica thinks before answering.

> “No. Not exactly. I knew she had to ask.”
>
> “She did.”
>
> “But when she asked me alone, it felt like maybe if I answered wrong, everything would disappear.”

Orin’s voice lowers.

> “You answered the truth.”
>
> “What if the truth is still not enough?”
>
> “Then we keep telling it until it is written in enough places that people have to deal with it.”

That almost makes her smile, but not quite.

They move from the foyer into the kitchen. The house feels too quiet after the inspection, as if every room is still holding its breath. Orin gets two glasses of water and sets one in front of Danica at the kitchen table.

Danica sits. She does not take off the blazer yet.

> “Claire said you shouldn’t be my only adult.”

Orin sits across from her, not beside her.

> “She’s right.”

Danica looks up sharply.

> “You agree with her?”
>
> “Yes.”
>
> “That doesn’t mean I don’t trust you?”
>
> “No. It means I don’t want your safety to depend on whether I can be everywhere at once.”

She absorbs that.

> “Dr. Whitfield.”
>
> “Yes.”
>
> “Ms. Lapointe.”
>
> “Yes.”
>
> “Claire.”
>
> “Yes.”
>
> “Hélène?”
>
> “Hélène too, in the ways that are appropriate.”
>
> “Martin, if he picks me up?”
>
> “Yes. With the office knowing, the truck logo, the driver’s name — all of it.”

Danica looks at the water glass.

> “I don’t like needing so many people.”
>
> “I know.”
>
> “At home, needing people was dangerous.”

Orin does not argue with that. He simply says:

> “Here, needing people is how we make sure no one person has too much power.”

That sentence lands between them.

Danica touches the edge of her tie.

> “She asked if I was afraid of you.”
>
> “She should have.”
>
> “I said no.”
>
> “Good.”
>
> “She asked if you tell me not to talk to people.”
>
> “Good.”
>
> “You keep saying good.”
>
> “Because those are the questions she is supposed to ask.”

Danica studies him.

> “It doesn’t make you angry?”
>
> “No.”
>
> “Why?”
>
> “Because if the system is going to let you stay here, it has to be allowed to question me. If I resent the questions more than I care about your safety, then I’m not safe.”

Danica’s eyes fill at that, but she does not cry yet.

> “I hated when she left.”
>
> “Why?”
>
> “Because while she was here, somebody official knew. And then she left, and it was just us again.”

Orin pushes her water a little closer.

> “It is not just us anymore.”

Danica shakes her head once, not disagreeing exactly.

> “It feels like it.”
>
> “Then we make it feel less like it. We call the people when we need them. We keep the pickup plan. You go to Ms. Lapointe. Claire comes back. Dr. Whitfield has the file. We do not let this become a secret house.”

Danica finally takes a sip of water.

> “A secret house.”
>
> “That’s what we are avoiding.”
>
> “But I like the house.”
>
> “So do I.”
>
> “I don’t want the house to feel like a file.”
>
> “It won’t always.”

A pause.

> “But today it did.”
>
> “Yes.”

That answer is the one that lets her cry. She puts her elbows on the kitchen table and covers her face. The crying is not loud. It is the delayed crying of someone who held herself together for a professional adult, for the inspection, for the bedroom questions, for the barn, for the truck, for the preliminary assessment.

Orin does not cross the room immediately. He lets her have the first moment of it privately.

Then he asks:

> “Do you want me closer?”

Danica nods without lifting her face.

He moves to the chair beside her, not touching until she reaches first. She reaches for his hand. He gives it.

> “I’m tired of being assessed,” she says into her hands.
>
> “I know.”
>
> “I’m tired of being brave in rooms.”
>
> “I know.”
>
> “I want to take off my uniform.”
>
> “Then take off your uniform.”
>
> “I want Hélène’s soup.”
>
> “I can ask if there is soup.”
>
> “I want no one to ask me anything else today.”
>
> “Then no one here asks you anything else today unless it is necessary.”

She lowers her hands. Her face is wet.

> “Is dinner necessary?”
>
> “Yes.”
>
> “Is homework necessary?”
>
> “Unfortunately, yes. But not immediately.”
>
> “Is talking about court necessary?”
>
> “Not tonight.”

Danica nods. That is enough.

Then she says, smaller:

> “Can we make pizza?”

Orin looks at her.

> “For dinner?”
>
> “Yes. Not order it. Make it.”

After a day of being inspected, assessed, and written down, making something with their hands feels different. It feels like choosing the evening instead of having the evening happen to them.

> “We can make pizza.”
>
> “And watch a movie?”
>
> “In the home theater?”
>
> “Yes.”
>
> “Then pizza and a movie.”

The scene moves into a softer closing sequence:

- Danica goes to the spare bedroom to change out of her BCS uniform.
- Her boots remain by the door, placed neatly where she left them.
- Claire’s car is gone from the driveway.
- Orin checks the kitchen for pizza dough, sauce, cheese, and toppings.
- If Hélène has left dough or ingredients, it becomes another quiet sign of household care.
- Danica comes back wearing her **Christmas-present shirt** and **baby blue pyjama bottoms**, face washed, hair tied back or left loose.
- They make the pizza together: flour on the counter, sauce spread unevenly, cheese scattered, toppings negotiated without pressure.

Possible small dialogue:

> “Too much cheese?” Danica asks.
>
> “No such thing after a DPJ inspection.”

Orin does not make the joke too big. It is just enough to let her breathe.

When the pizza is ready, they take it to the home theater instead of eating formally at the table. The home theater should feel cozy rather than extravagant in this moment: dark room, soft seats, a blanket, the blue light of the screen, pizza slices on plates or a tray between them.

They choose a movie that does not require emotional work — something familiar, gentle, funny, or adventurous without being frightening. The point is not the film itself. The point is that Danica gets an evening where nobody questions her, nobody assesses her, nobody asks her to prove anything.

Orin sits beside her but gives her space. If she leans against him, it is her choice. If she only sits under the blanket eating pizza, that is enough.

Closing image:

Danica in her Christmas-present shirt and baby blue pyjama bottoms, no uniform, no blazer, no inspection voice. Pizza cooling on the tray. A movie playing in the home theater. Orin beside her, quiet and present. The farm has passed inspection for one day. One day is not forever — but it is enough for homemade pizza, a movie, and breathing without answering questions.

# What This Scene Establishes

1. **The DPJ has physically inspected Ferme Clé.** The property is no longer only described by Orin and Danica; it has been seen by a professional.
2. **Danica’s room is real and separate.** The inspection confirms she has her own space and basic needs are met.
3. **Orin’s room and boundaries are checked.** The age difference and relationship are addressed directly.
4. **The farm staff matter structurally.** Hélène and Martin help show the household is not a hidden two-person isolation.
5. **The Ferme Clé logo truck becomes part of official pickup safety.** The DPJ worker refines the plan: Danica waits inside BCS until driver and vehicle are verified.
6. **Danica is privately interviewed.** Her choice to stay is recorded as her own statement, not Orin’s claim.
7. **Orin is warned about dependency and power imbalance.** He accepts that care must be proven structurally.
8. **Ferme Clé is accepted as a temporary location for now.** The file remains open; this is not a legal resolution.
9. **Legal aid / emancipation remains the next thread.** The inspection supports but does not replace the legal process.
