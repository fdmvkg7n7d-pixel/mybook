# Danica’s Parents Show Up at BCS — Scene Outline

## Scene Information

- **Characters:**
  - **Danica (Xochitl)** — 16 years old. Back at Bishop’s College School after leaving home. Trying to function as a student while the DPJ file remains open.
  - **Danica’s Mother** — controlled, formal, angry beneath politeness. Used to being obeyed and to institutions responding to parental authority.
  - **Danica’s Father** — quieter at first, but aligned with the mother. His silence can feel as threatening as her words.
  - **Mme Solange Beaudoin** — BCS front-office secretary. Calm, protective, procedural.
  - **Dr. Margaret “Maggie” Whitfield** — Dean of Student Affairs. Handles the parents directly.
  - **Ms. Renée Lapointe** — school counsellor. Supports Danica during and after the confrontation.
  - **Geneviève Lavoie** — Danica’s protective friend.
  - **Chloe Mackenzie** — Danica’s warm, anxious friend.
- **Offstage / referenced:** Orin Lorin Junior, Claire Desrosiers from the DPJ, the SQ officers, BCS teachers, Ferme Clé staff.
- **Location:** Bishop’s College School, Sherbrooke — main office, hallway, student-services office, possibly a classroom.
- **When:** **Monday during the students’ lunch period**, the first school day after the Saturday Saint-Viateur bagels tradition. Danica has had one quiet weekend morning with Orin and the new bagel ritual; now, during lunch at BCS, her parents escalate from phone calls to appearing in person.
- **Tone:** Tense, controlled, institutional protection. Danica is frightened, but the school does not collapse around her.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene externalizes the threat Danica has been fearing since she returned to BCS: her parents physically come to the school.

The scene should prove that the BCS safety plan matters. Danica does not have to manage her parents at the school door. Mme Beaudoin, Dr. Whitfield, and Ms. Lapointe act as the adult structure around her.

The emotional center is this:

> Danica’s parents arrive expecting parental authority to open every door. Instead, the school becomes a locked door Danica does not have to hold shut by herself.

---

## Continuity to This Point

- Danica left home after her parents made racist remarks in Japanese about Orin.
- Police located her at Ferme Clé and referred the file to the DPJ.
- Dr. Whitfield kept Danica enrolled and did not call her parents.
- Ms. Lapointe created a safety plan with Danica, including what to do if her parents came to school.
- Claire Desrosiers inspected Ferme Clé and accepted it as a temporary safe location for now.
- Danica and Orin created a Saturday Saint-Viateur bagels tradition.
- Sabrina Bryna and William remain unaware of Danica.
- Danica’s parents have already called BCS twice during Christmas break. Now they escalate from phone calls to appearing in person.

# Scene Outline

## Beat 1: Lunch With Geneviève and Chloe

The scene begins on **Monday during the students’ lunch period**, the first school day after the Saturday Saint-Viateur bagels outing with Orin.

Danica is in the dining hall or student lunch area with **Geneviève** and **Chloe**. She is wearing her BCS uniform — blazer with crest, blouse, tie, skirt, tights — and trying to have an ordinary lunch.

The ordinary details matter:

- lunch trays,
- winter coats hanging nearby,
- students talking too loudly,
- Geneviève complaining about an assignment,
- Chloe asking whether Danica did the reading,
- Danica eating slowly because school food still feels strange after Hélène’s dinners and Saint-Viateur bagels.

For a moment, Danica is not thinking about her parents. She is telling Geneviève and Chloe about Saturday morning — not every intimate detail, but enough:

> “We went to Saint-Viateur.”
>
> “For bagels?” Chloe asks.
>
> “With cream cheese.”
>
> Geneviève says, “Good. That is a correct use of Saturday.”

The normality is interrupted when the **office comes to the dining hall**. Mme Beaudoin herself, or a trusted office runner sent directly by her, appears at the entrance to the lunchroom and scans the tables until she finds Danica.

They do **not** announce the reason in front of other students. The adult walks to Danica’s lunch table and keeps their voice low.

> “Danica, Ms. Lapointe would like to see you in student services. Bring your things, please.”

Danica’s stomach drops.

Geneviève notices immediately. Chloe does too.

> “I’ll come,” Geneviève says.
>
> “No,” the adult says gently. “Ms. Lapointe is waiting for Danica.”

Danica gathers her tray and bag with hands that have gone cold. Her face changes before she can stop it — the colour draining, eyes bright, mouth pressed tight because she is trying not to cry in the dining hall. She is very emotional and very nervous, and Geneviève sees it immediately.

> “Danica?” Chloe says softly.
>
> “I’m okay,” Danica says, but her voice shakes badly enough that no one believes it.

She leaves the dining hall with the office adult, clutching her bag too tightly against her side.

---

## Beat 2: Ms. Lapointe Meets Her in the Hallway

Ms. Lapointe is already in the hallway before Danica reaches student services. That is deliberate. Danica does not have to walk into uncertainty alone.

> “Danica.”
>
> “What happened?”

Ms. Lapointe answers plainly. No euphemism.

> “Your parents are here.”

Danica freezes.

Before the panic can take over completely, Ms. Lapointe adds the next fact.

> “The school has called the police.”

Danica’s eyes widen.

> “The police?”

After the nightmare on Tuesday morning, the word hits her hard. Her breathing goes shallow and fast.

Ms. Lapointe keeps her voice low and steady.

> “Yes. Not to arrest you. Not to take you away. We called because your parents came in person, there is an open DPJ file, and we need everyone safe and documented.”
>
> “Are they going to make me go with them?”
>
> “No. Danica, listen to me: you are safe.”

Ms. Lapointe says it slowly, making sure Danica hears every word.

> “You are safe at school. You are with me. Dr. Whitfield is handling the front office. Mme Beaudoin knows the plan. The police are being called to help keep the situation controlled, not to take you away.”
>
> “No one is taking you from student services by surprise.”

Then Ms. Lapointe gives the immediate instruction.

> “You are not going to the front office. You are coming with me.”

Danica looks toward the direction of the main office.

> “Did they see me?”
>
> “No.”
>
> “Are they asking for me?”
>
> “Yes.”
>
> “Are you going to make me talk to them?”
>
> “No.”

That word should land hard: **No.**

Ms. Lapointe repeats the safety-plan phrase:

> “Adults handle adults.”

Danica almost cries right there.

---

## Beat 3: The Parents in the Front Office

Cut to the main office.

Danica’s parents are at the front desk. They are well-dressed, controlled, and furious in the way people are furious when they are trying to sound reasonable.

Danica’s mother speaks first.

> “We are here for our daughter.”

Mme Beaudoin remains seated, hands folded near the keyboard.

> “Dr. Whitfield has been notified that you are here. Please have a seat.”

> “We do not need to have a seat. We need to see Danica.”

Mme Beaudoin’s voice does not change.

> “Dr. Whitfield will speak with you.”

The mother tries the institutional-authority route:

- they are Danica’s parents,
- Danica is sixteen,
- they pay tuition,
- the school has no right to hide her,
- the school is interfering with parental authority,
- they know she has been attending classes.

Mme Beaudoin gives nothing away beyond procedure.

> “Dr. Whitfield will speak with you.”

This is Mme Beaudoin’s strength: repetition as protection.

---

## Beat 4: Dr. Whitfield Takes Control

Dr. Whitfield comes out of her office and does not invite the parents deeper into the school.

She keeps them in the administrative area.

> “Mr. and Mrs. [Surname], I’m Dr. Whitfield, Dean of Student Affairs. You may speak with me.”

Danica’s mother says:

> “We want our daughter brought here now.”

Dr. Whitfield does not say whether Danica is in the building.

> “I understand that you are requesting to speak with your daughter. Given the open youth-protection involvement, I will not be arranging that meeting without coordination with the DPJ.”

The father speaks now, controlled:

> “She is a minor.”

> “Yes,” Dr. Whitfield says. “And because she is a minor with an open DPJ referral, the school will follow the safety plan currently in place.”

The mother’s politeness begins to crack.

> “Safety plan? Against us?”

Dr. Whitfield does not take the bait.

> “For Danica.”

---

## Beat 5: Danica in Ms. Lapointe’s Office

Meanwhile, Danica sits in Ms. Lapointe’s office, in the chair closest to the door.

She is shaking.

Ms. Lapointe gives her simple options:

> “You can sit. You can stand. You can text Orin if you want. You do not have to decide anything else right now.”

Danica asks:

> “Can they take me?”

Ms. Lapointe answers carefully.

> “Not from this office by surprise.”

Danica catches the carefulness.

> “Careful part.”
>
> “Yes. Careful part. The DPJ has to be contacted. Dr. Whitfield is handling the front office. You are not alone with them.”

Danica takes out her phone and texts Orin.

Possible text:

> My parents are at BCS. I’m with Ms. Lapointe. Dr. Whitfield is handling it.

Orin may be in class or lab. He does not answer instantly. That delay matters because Danica has to experience the school supporting her even before Orin responds.

Ms. Lapointe says:

> “Breathe first. Phone second.”

Danica almost laughs, but cannot quite.

---

## Beat 6: Geneviève and Chloe Find Out

Back in class, Geneviève and Chloe understand enough from Danica being pulled out and the teacher’s face.

Depending on pacing, they may ask to go to the washroom and find Ms. Lapointe’s office, or they may wait until later.

Recommended version: they do **not** rush the office. The teacher quietly tells them Danica is safe with Ms. Lapointe. This prevents the scene from becoming chaotic.

Geneviève whispers:

> “Her parents.”

Chloe looks sick.

The teacher gives them a look that says: not now.

This shows that Danica’s friends are protective, but the adults are keeping the school environment controlled.

---

## Beat 7: The Parents Push Harder

In the main office, Danica’s mother escalates.

Possible lines:

> “You are hiding a child from her family.”

> “We have the right to know where she is.”

> “This school has no authority to interfere in our home.”

> “She has been manipulated by an older boy.”

That last line matters because it is partly a weapon and partly a real institutional concern. Dr. Whitfield cannot dismiss it casually.

Dr. Whitfield answers with measured precision:

> “Any concerns about exploitation or manipulation should be provided to the DPJ worker assigned to the file. The school will cooperate fully with youth protection. What I will not do is produce a student for an unscheduled confrontation in the front office.”

The mother says:

> “Then call her here and let her decide.”

Dr. Whitfield says:

> “No.”

A clean, professional no.

---

## Beat 8: Calling Claire Desrosiers

Dr. Whitfield or Ms. Lapointe calls Claire Desrosiers at the DPJ.

This can happen offstage or onstage.

Onstage option:

Dr. Whitfield steps into her office and calls Claire while the parents wait under Mme Beaudoin’s watch.

> “Mme Desrosiers, this is Margaret Whitfield at BCS. Danica’s parents are here in person requesting access to her. Danica is in student services with Ms. Lapointe. We are not arranging contact without your direction.”

Claire’s side may not be heard, but Dr. Whitfield’s responses establish the plan.

> “Yes.”
>
> “No, she is not alone with them.”
>
> “Yes, we can keep her in student services.”
>
> “Understood.”

Dr. Whitfield returns to the parents.

> “The DPJ has been notified. They will contact you directly. The school will not facilitate a meeting today.”

---

## Beat 9: Danica Sees Them — Or Almost Does

For emotional intensity, Danica may glimpse her parents through interior glass, down a hallway, or from behind Ms. Lapointe’s office door.

Use this carefully. The school should not accidentally expose her unnecessarily, but a glimpse can make the fear embodied.

Possible version:

Ms. Lapointe walks Danica to a quieter room farther from the office. At the end of the hallway, Danica sees her mother’s profile through the glass panel of the main office door.

Her mother is standing very straight. Her father is beside her. They look exactly like themselves.

Danica stops breathing.

Ms. Lapointe steps slightly between Danica and the view.

> “Eyes on me.”

Danica cannot.

> “Danica. Eyes on me.”

Finally Danica looks at Ms. Lapointe.

> “They came.”
>
> “Yes.”
>
> “I knew they would.”
>
> “And now the plan is happening.”

That line is important: the feared event has occurred, and the safety plan is actually functioning.

---

## Beat 10: Orin Responds by Text

Orin finally replies.

Possible text:

> Stay with Ms. Lapointe. Do not go to them. I am leaving now if you want me there.

Danica stares at the message.

Ms. Lapointe asks:

> “What did he say?”

Danica shows her.

Ms. Lapointe says:

> “Do you want him here?”

Danica wants to say yes immediately. But she remembers Claire saying Orin cannot be her only adult. She remembers the school is already handling it.

> “Not yet.”

She texts:

> Stay at McGill for now. They are handling it. I’ll tell you if I need you.

This is a major growth beat. Danica chooses to let the school adults protect her without summoning Orin into the crisis immediately.

Orin responds:

> Okay. I’m here. Text me every ten minutes or when you can.

---

## Beat 11: The Parents Leave Without Seeing Her

The parents do not get what they came for.

They may leave angry, controlled, humiliated, or threatening further action.

Danica’s mother says to Dr. Whitfield:

> “This is not finished.”

Dr. Whitfield answers:

> “I understand.”

The father may say very little, but before leaving he looks around the office as if memorizing every face.

Mme Beaudoin watches them go.

The front door closes.

No one relaxes immediately.

---

## Beat 12: Dr. Whitfield Comes to Danica

Dr. Whitfield comes to Ms. Lapointe’s office herself.

She does not stand over Danica. She sits.

> “They have left.”

Danica’s first response is not relief but disbelief.

> “They left?”
>
> “Yes.”
>
> “Without me?”
>
> “Yes.”

Danica starts crying then. Not loudly. The delayed crying of someone whose worst fear came into the building and was not allowed to take her.

Dr. Whitfield says:

> “You did exactly what you were supposed to do. You stayed with Ms. Lapointe. You did not go to the front office. You let adults handle adults.”

Danica says:

> “I didn’t do anything.”

Ms. Lapointe answers:

> “Sometimes not doing the dangerous thing is doing the thing.”

---

## Beat 13: Practical Aftermath

The adults make the rest of the day practical.

Options:

1. Danica stays in student services until pickup.
2. Danica returns to class if she wants, but only if she genuinely wants to.
3. Geneviève and Chloe are allowed to sit with her briefly at lunch or after class.
4. BCS contacts Claire with a written summary.
5. Pickup is moved to a more secure plan: Danica waits inside the main office, not outside.
6. If a Ferme Clé truck comes, the driver’s name and logo are verified before she exits.

Recommended version: Danica does **not** go back to normal class immediately. She stays in student services for a period, then Geneviève and Chloe are allowed to come sit with her for ten minutes.

---

## Beat 14: Geneviève and Chloe Visit

Geneviève enters first, fierce and pale. Chloe follows, already crying.

Geneviève says:

> “They came?”

Danica nods.

Chloe sits beside her and takes her hand.

> “Did they see you?”
>
> “No. Not really.”
>
> “Good.”

Geneviève says, low:

> “Adults handled adults?”

Danica nods again, and this time she almost smiles.

> “Adults handled adults.”

This lets the phrase become shared among the girls, not only something Ms. Lapointe says.

---

## Beat 15: Pickup at the End of the Day

At the end of the day, Danica does not wait outside. She waits inside the main office or student services.

Depending on Orin’s schedule, either:

### Option A: Orin picks her up himself

He arrives visibly shaken but controlled. He does not storm in. He does not make the school’s handling about his fear.

He thanks Dr. Whitfield and Ms. Lapointe.

> “Thank you for not making her face them.”

Dr. Whitfield says:

> “That was never the plan.”

### Option B: Martin picks her up in the Ferme Clé logo truck

This reinforces the safety plan. The office verifies:

- driver name,
- vehicle,
- Ferme Clé logo,
- pickup authorization.

Danica gets into the truck only after every detail matches.

Recommended version: **Orin picks her up himself** if his schedule allows, because the emotional aftermath needs their conversation at Ferme Clé. If he cannot, Martin picks her up and Orin meets her at home later.

---

## Beat 16: Closing Image

Danica leaves BCS through a controlled pickup, not through a confrontation.

Her parents came to the school.

They did not get her.

The safety plan held.

Closing line option:

> “The door her parents expected to open had stayed closed — not because Danica held it shut, but because for once, adults had stood on her side of it.”

---

# What This Scene Establishes

1. **Danica’s parents escalate from phone calls to physical presence.** The threat becomes real and visible.
2. **BCS protects Danica procedurally.** Mme Beaudoin, Dr. Whitfield, and Ms. Lapointe do what the safety plan said they would do.
3. **The phrase “adults handle adults” pays off.** Danica does not have to manage her parents.
4. **Danica chooses not to summon Orin immediately.** This shows growth and reinforces that Orin is not her only support.
5. **The DPJ becomes more involved.** Claire is notified that the parents appeared in person.
6. **Geneviève and Chloe remain protective friends.** They support Danica without turning the school into chaos.
7. **Pickup procedures tighten.** Danica waits inside and verified transportation becomes mandatory.
