# SCENE OUTLINE: MONDAY EVENING — ORIN PICKS DANICA UP, CHEF'S DINNER AT FERME CLÉ, SYLLABI ON THE SOFA

> **Filename note:** still `monday-evening-danica-orin-saint-viateur-bagels-scene-outline.md` for repo continuity, but the Saint-Viateur bagel trip has been removed from this scene. They drive straight east from BCS to Ferme Clé, where the chef has dinner waiting. Rename when convenient — suggested: `monday-evening-danica-orin-chefs-dinner-syllabi-scene-outline.md`.

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:**
  - **Danica (Xochitl)** — 16 years old. End of her first day **back at BCS after the Christmas break**, in her new uniform. She is finishing the autumn semester — these are the classes she was already enrolled in before the break, the classmates she sat with before the break, the teachers she already knew. She has been with Geneviève and Chloe at lunch. She has her agenda with the rest of the autumn-semester schedule in it.
  - **Orin Lorin Junior** — 19 years old. **Second-year McGill University student, studying biology and chemistry on the pre-medical track.** Intends to apply to medical school. Today was also **his** first day back at McGill — but for him it is the start of a **new** term (the winter semester, with new syllabi and new courses). Danica already knows about the doctor plan; they have talked about it many times in the weeks since they met in mid-November.
  - **Madame Hélène Bélanger** — Ferme Clé's chef. Late fifties, Francophone Quebecer, warm and unfussy. Lives in a small apartment over the carriage house; comes in to cook for whoever is at the farm. Has dinner waiting in the kitchen for both Orin and Danica tonight. (Briefly onstage in absence — her plated dinner and her handwritten note are the scene of her presence; not given a speaking line in this draft.)
- **Offstage / referenced:** Geneviève Lavoie, Chloe Mackenzie, Dr. Whitfield, Mme Beaudoin, Ms. Renée Lapointe, the DPJ worker, Sabrina Bryna and William (back in Vaughan, Ontario — the Orlando trip ended over the weekend because the younger children's winter term has started), Danica's parents (still no contact since the school calls)
- **Location:** BCS pickup point in Sherbrooke → the car on the autoroute (Highway 10, eastbound back toward the farm) → **Ferme Clé**: kitchen for the chef's dinner, then the living room sofa for the syllabi
- **When:** **Monday evening — two weeks after Danica left her parents' home.** Her first day back at BCS after the two-week Christmas break, finishing her autumn semester. Same day as the BCS office meeting and the classroom hug with Geneviève and Chloe. Also Orin's first day back at McGill, starting his winter semester.
- **Status:** New scene outline. Carries the in-car material that was previously Beat 9 of the Monday-morning office-meeting outline, plus a chef's dinner at home and a syllabi-on-the-sofa exchange.
- **Tone:** Tired in a good way. The first hard thing of the day worked — for both of them. They are spending the evening together at the farm, eating a meal somebody else cooked, in pyjamas, looking at each other's term ahead.

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

Reunite Danica and Orin at the end of their two parallel first days back — hers at BCS finishing the autumn semester, his at McGill starting the winter one — and let the morning's victory finally land. The in-car exchange about Dr. Whitfield not calling her mother, the gratitude about the uniform, and Orin's gentle closing of the IOU thread all live in the drive home.

Then: a quiet supper made by the chef, Danica changing into her Christmas-present shirt and pink pyjama bottoms, and the two of them on the sofa with Orin's printed syllabi — biology and chemistry — between them. This is the first scene where they are both *students* again at the same time, sharing what their respective weeks ahead actually look like.

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: The Pickup

Orin drives back from Montreal to Sherbrooke at the end of the afternoon. He parks at the agreed pickup point — close to BCS but not at the gate. Danica is there with her agenda tucked under her arm and the bookstore bag from this morning. She gets in. She closes the door. She lets her shoulders drop for the first time since the alarm went off this morning.

> "Hi."
>
> "Hi. How was it?"
>
> "Long. Good. Long."

He waits. She doesn't volunteer more, not yet. She is exhausted. He doesn't push.

> "Home?"
>
> "Home."

He starts the car. He turns east, toward the farm.

### Beat 2: The Drive East (Highway 10)

The autoroute toward the farm in the early dark. Snow on the shoulders. The cab of the car is warm.

Danica is the one who finally speaks.

> "She didn't call my mother."
>
> "No, she didn't."
>
> "My mother called *her*. Twice."
>
> "I know. I heard from the chair by the window."

A pause.

> "Dr. Whitfield said 'emergency-replacement protocol.'"
>
> "I heard that too."

Danica laughs — once, short, more like a breath out than a laugh. Then she starts crying, not hard, just steadily. Orin doesn't say anything. He doesn't reach for her. He keeps both hands on the wheel and lets her.

After a few kilometres she wipes her face with the cuff of her uniform blazer and tells him about lunch.

> "Geneviève and Chloe — I gave them the new parts today. The police on Saturday. The DPJ on Friday. The meeting with Dr. Whitfield this morning. They already knew most of the rest — I called them from your phone the first night, remember, and we've been on the phone basically every other day for two weeks. But it was different in person. Geneviève swore at the table. Chloe held my hand on top of the table the whole time and didn't let go until the bell. Geneviève said *'tu nous appelles à n'importe quelle heure, Danica, n'importe quelle heure'* — call us any hour, any hour. Like she hadn't already been saying it for two weeks."
>
> "Good."

A small pause.

> "Chloe asked if she could meet you. Eventually. Not tomorrow. Just — eventually."
>
> "Whenever you want."

Another pause.

> "Thank you. For the uniform."
>
> "You're welcome."
>
> "It was a lot of money."
>
> "It wasn't. Not for me. Don't make it into a thing — let me do this. Let me do this every time I can."

She looks at him in profile. She doesn't argue. She just nods, once.

### Beat 3: Danica's Day, Briefly

She tells him about the rest of her day in the way exhausted teenagers actually tell things — out of order, half a sentence at a time, switching between English, French, and the bits of Japanese she has from her parents' kitchen. Orin follows all three. He has French from Quebec, Japanese from his four years at Upper Canada College and his McGill courses, and English from everything else.

- The first hallway between the front office and her homeroom. The eyes — but only because she walked in halfway through first period, not because anyone was surprised to see her. Everybody in homeroom was already back from the break too; she was just the one who came in late. The teacher said *"Bon retour, Danica,"* the same *"bon retour"* she had said to every other student that morning, and that was that.
- Math class: a quiz on the material from before the break — the standard back-from-Christmas refresher quiz. Everyone in the class was rusty after two weeks off, but Danica had also lost her textbook on Friday night two weeks ago and hadn't been able to review the way she normally would. She did what she could. The teacher, noticing, came by her desk afterwards and said quietly that if she wanted to redo it for a better grade she could come in at lunch on Friday. She said *merci.*
- French: easy. They were on *Les Belles-Sœurs.* She had read it. *"C'était facile,"* she says, half-shrugging. *"Pour une fois."*
- World history: the teacher pulled her aside after class to ask if she was alright. She said yes. He said *"si tu as besoin de quoi que ce soit."* She said *merci, monsieur.*
- Lunch with Geneviève and Chloe. (See Beat 2.)
- Last period — chemistry. The teacher handed out the lab schedule for the rest of the autumn semester. She has labs every Tuesday and Thursday until the end of January.

At one point, halfway through telling him about the math quiz, she switches into Japanese for half a sentence — *「お母さんがいたら、もう一度勉強できたのに」* (if my mother had been there, I could have studied again) — and then catches herself and finishes the sentence in English. Orin doesn't react to the switch. He doesn't translate it back to her. He just keeps listening.

He says, at the end, in English:

> "First day back is the worst one. You got through the worst one."

### Beat 4: Home

They turn up the long gravel driveway. The barn is lit; the staff already have the horses in. Lights are on in the kitchen window.

When Danica opens the front door, she smells dinner. **Madame Hélène Bélanger** has cooked: **chicken breast in a plum sauce, a baked potato each, cauliflower, carrots**, with a side **salad — lettuce, onions, tomatoes** — in the wooden bowl on the kitchen table. There is a covered plate at each of their usual places and a covered serving dish in the middle. A note in Hélène's careful Francophone hand: *"Poulet à la sauce aux prunes. Réchauffer 5 min si nécessaire. Salade dans le frigo. Bonne soirée. — H."*

Danica stands in the doorway with the bookstore bag in her hand and starts to cry again, quietly, for no reason she can name. Orin sets the bag down and rests his hand on her shoulder for a second, no more.

> "Go change. I'll heat the plates."

### Beat 5: She Changes

She goes to the spare bedroom and changes out of the uniform. She hangs it carefully on the back of the door — first time. Then she puts on the shirt Orin gave her for Christmas — the shirt she wore on the first night, the one he kept and washed and wrapped and gave back to her on Christmas morning — and a pair of soft **pink pyjama bottoms**. Nothing else. Her feet bare on the wood floor.

She comes back to the kitchen looking very young and very held-together at the same time.

Orin sees her in the shirt and the pink pyjama bottoms and doesn't say anything about it. He just sets her plate down at her place.

### Beat 6: The Chef's Dinner

They eat. The chicken comes apart at the touch of the fork. The plum sauce is sweet but not heavy. The potato is hot through. The carrots and cauliflower have been roasted with a little butter. The salad is plain and cold and exactly right. Danica eats more than she has eaten in days. They talk a little, mostly about small things. The way the snow looked off Highway 10. The way the front office at BCS smells like the laminator.

Halfway through, Danica says, in French:

> *"Il faut que j'écrive une carte de remerciement à Hélène demain matin."*
>
> Orin, in French: *"Elle va aimer ça."*

When they're done they leave the plates stacked at the sink — Hélène will come through in the morning — and they take their water glasses with them to the living room. Danica grabs her schoolbag on the way.

### Beat 7: Two People Doing Homework on the Sofa

They settle onto the sofa at opposite ends, both with their work in their laps. Orin gets his bag from the front hallway and pulls out two printed syllabi — biology, chemistry — both stapled at the corner, both already creased once from being folded in his jacket pocket. He also pulls out his first organic-chem problem set.

Danica pulls out her own things: the math textbook (the makeup quiz Friday), the chemistry lab schedule, the *Les Belles-Sœurs* paperback for French, and a new notebook she bought this morning at the BCS bookstore.

They look at each other once across the sofa, both of them with paper in their laps, both of them in school clothes of a kind — his McGill hoodie, her Christmas-present shirt and pink pyjama bottoms — and they both, very briefly, smile.

> "Show me the syllabi first," she says. "Then I have to do math."
>
> "Deal."

He hands her the chemistry one first.

> "Organic two. Professor Sauvé. Problem set every week — first one due in eight days. Midterm second week of February. Final exam in the middle of April."

She reads it carefully. Her face does a small thing at *"30% of final grade."*

> "This is a lot."
>
> "It is. It's also doable. I did organic one in the fall — same professor, same format. I know the rhythm."

He hands her the biology one.

> "Bio. Labs Tuesday afternoons, lecture Monday and Wednesday morning. Lab partners assigned by the end of this week. Field trip to the McGill Gault Nature Reserve in March if the snow cooperates."

She reads that one slowly too.

> "What does 'cell biology II' actually mean? Like, what does the test ask you?"

He laughs, a little.

> "It asks me to draw mitochondrial membranes from memory and label every transport protein."
>
> "Okay."
>
> "Yeah."

She hands the syllabi back. He puts them on the coffee table and opens the problem set. She opens her math textbook.

For the next forty minutes they work in parallel without talking. Orin scratches out organic chemistry mechanisms in pencil on engineering paper. Danica works through the quiz material she missed before the break — half-remembered identities, the sine rule, a problem involving a ladder against a wall that takes her three tries. Once she leans over and reads a line of his pencil work over his arm; she has no idea what it says; she leans back. Once he glances at her ladder problem; he doesn't say anything. The lamp is on. The house is quiet. The snow outside is doing nothing.

She finishes the practice quiz. She closes the textbook on her finger.

> "Friday."
>
> "Friday."
>
> "You moved everything for Friday."
>
> "I moved bio lab to the Tuesday section. Chem I can self-study. It's fine."
>
> "Orin."
>
> "It's fine. I am not going to be a worse doctor in eight years because I sat with you on a Friday in January. That's not how any of this works."

She looks at him. She doesn't argue. She just nods, once.

She opens *Les Belles-Sœurs.* He goes back to his problem set. Neither of them gets up.

### Beat 8: Danica Falls Asleep on the Couch

The last part of the evening is not dramatic. It is simply exhaustion finally winning.

The two syllabi are on the coffee table. The math textbook is closed on the sofa cushion. *Les Belles-Sœurs* is open in Danica's lap, one hand still resting on the page as if she means to keep reading. Orin's organic-chem problem set is spread across his end of the sofa, pencil marks and reaction arrows covering the engineering paper.

Danica is curled at the opposite end of the sofa in the Christmas-present shirt and pink pyjama bottoms. Her bare feet are tucked under her. Her eyes close once, open, close again. She tries to read the same line a third time and does not make it to the end.

Orin notices only when the room has been quiet too long.

He looks over and sees that she has fallen asleep with the paperback still open against her knees.

For a moment he does nothing. He lets the house be quiet around them: the lamp, the snow-dark windows, the dishes stacked at the sink for Hélène, the bookstore bag still on the floor by the front door, the uniform hanging carefully on the back of the spare bedroom door.

Then he sets his pencil down.

He does not make a joke. He does not tease her for falling asleep. He does not touch her carelessly. He simply reaches for the quilt folded over the arm of the sofa and lays it over her, careful not to wake her too abruptly.

For one quiet second, he stands there looking down at her — the girl who got through her first day back, the girl who trusted the house enough to fall asleep in the living room. Then he bends and softly kisses her forehead. It is brief, gentle, and protective, the kind of kiss that asks nothing from her sleeping body and gives only reassurance.

Danica does not wake.

Orin leaves her there.

He closes *Les Belles-Sœurs* around a bookmark or scrap of paper so she does not lose her place, then sets the book on the coffee table beside the syllabi. He gathers the math textbook and her notebook into a neat pile. He turns the lamp down low instead of switching it off completely, so she will not wake in darkness if she opens her eyes later.

Then he returns to his end of the sofa with the first problem set of his term — organic chemistry, due in eight days.

He works quietly while she sleeps under the quilt at the other end of the couch.

The final image is the low lamp beside him, Danica asleep under the quilt in the Christmas shirt and pink pyjama bottoms, the spare-bedroom door still open down the hall, and the quiet understanding that falling asleep on the couch was not a mistake. It was the first ordinary accident of a shared home.

## WHAT THIS SCENE ESTABLISHES

1. **Today was both their first days back — but asymmetric.** Danica is *continuing* her autumn semester at BCS (same classes, same teachers, same desks she had before the break). Orin is *starting* his winter semester at McGill (new syllabi for second-year bio and chem on the pre-medical track). The asymmetry is real and on the page.
2. **The homeroom and the rest of BCS already knew Danica.** When she walked in at mid-morning, the class had recognized her instantly — they had sat with her before Christmas. The teacher's *"Bon retour, Danica"* covered it without ceremony.
3. **Geneviève and Chloe already knew about Orin** from the mid-November meeting in the Montreal Underground City. Telling them at lunch was filling in what happened *to* him and her, not introducing him. Geneviève swore. Chloe held Danica's hand on top of the table all of lunch. Geneviève's line: *"Tu nous appelles à n'importe quelle heure, Danica, n'importe quelle heure."*
4. **Day-of-school details are now on the page:** math quiz, French (*Les Belles-Sœurs*), world history teacher checking in, chemistry lab schedule through end of January.
5. **No Montreal bagel trip.** They drive straight home from BCS to Ferme Clé.
6. **Madame Hélène Bélanger, the Ferme Clé chef, has dinner waiting.** Tonight's meal: chicken breast in plum sauce, baked potatoes, cauliflower, carrots, and a side salad of lettuce, onions, and tomatoes — plated, covered, with a French handwritten note signed *— H.* The farm has staff; tonight they need them. Danica decides to write Hélène a thank-you note tomorrow, in French.
7. **Danica changes into the Christmas-present shirt and pink pyjama bottoms.** The shirt is the one she wore on the first night, which Orin kept and gave back to her on Christmas morning (per the Christmas scene outline). Wearing it tonight, on the sofa with his syllabi between them, is its own small landing point — *she is here, she is staying, this is what she puts on when the school day is done.*
8. **The IOU thread closes here, not in the bookstore.** Orin's *"Don't make it into a thing — let me do this every time I can"* lands in the car on the autoroute.
9. **The syllabi land here, not at the door.** What was previously deferred to "Tuesday night" now happens on the sofa tonight, after dinner.
10. **They study side by side on the sofa for forty minutes without talking** — Orin on his first organic-chem problem set, Danica on her makeup math quiz and then *Les Belles-Sœurs*. The first time in two weeks they have both been *students* in the same room at the same time. The image is the scene's emotional anchor: a pre-medical student and a high-school sophomore doing homework together in a quiet house with the snow doing nothing outside.
11. **Danica falls asleep on the couch.** She is still holding *Les Belles-Sœurs*, still wearing the Christmas-present shirt and pink pyjama bottoms, and exhaustion finally overtakes her. Orin covers her with a quilt, softly kisses her forehead, and does **not** wake her. He leaves her asleep at the other end of the sofa while he works quietly on his organic-chem problem set. The gesture reads as ordinary domestic tenderness, protection, and restraint.
12. **Orin has rearranged his Friday for the DPJ home visit.** Bio lab moved to the Tuesday section; chem self-study. *"I am not going to be a worse doctor in eight years because I sat with you on a Friday in January."* His McGill term runs through to a **mid-April final exam in organic chemistry** — the scaffolding she will be hearing him plan around for the next three months.
12. **The conversation moves between English, French, and Japanese.** Danica is mixed — Anglo schooling at BCS, French as the language of Quebec and her classroom day, Japanese from her parents' kitchen. Orin follows all three (English from everything else, French from Quebec, Japanese from his four years at UCC and his McGill courses — the same Japanese her parents did not know he had). The scene has Danica slipping into Japanese for half a sentence about her mother and Orin not reacting, not translating, just letting it be.
13. **Two weeks** since Danica left home — the length of the Christmas break.
