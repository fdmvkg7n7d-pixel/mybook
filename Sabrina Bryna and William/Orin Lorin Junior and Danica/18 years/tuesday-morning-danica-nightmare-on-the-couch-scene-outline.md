# Tuesday Morning — Danica Screams in Her Sleep on the Couch — Scene Outline

## Scene Information

- **Characters:**
  - **Danica (Xochitl)** — 16 years old. The morning after her first day back at BCS. She fell asleep on the living room couch the night before while reading *Les Belles-Sœurs*.
  - **Orin Lorin Junior** — 19 years old. Second-year McGill student on the pre-medical track. He covered Danica with a quilt the night before, kissed her forehead, and did not wake her.
- **Offstage / referenced:** Danica’s parents, Dr. Whitfield, Mme Beaudoin, Geneviève, Chloe, Madame Hélène Bélanger, the DPJ worker.
- **Location:** Ferme Clé living room, early Tuesday morning.
- **When:** Tuesday morning before school, the morning after the Monday evening couch scene.
- **Tone:** Sudden fear breaking into domestic quiet; protective, careful, restrained.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene shows that Danica’s first successful day back at school did not erase the trauma of leaving home. Her body is still carrying fear. Orin’s response is immediate, protective, and disciplined: he rushes to her side, but he does not overwhelm her, grab her carelessly, or make the moment about himself.

The scene should establish that Ferme Clé is safe, but safety is still something Danica’s nervous system has to learn.

# Scene Outline

## Beat 1: Six O'Clock Breakfast

It is **six o'clock in the morning**, not fully light yet. The house is quiet in the blue-gray hour before sunrise. The farm has not fully woken, though the barn lights are beginning to come on outside and one or two workers may already be moving in the distance.

Danica is still asleep on the living room couch under the quilt. One hand has slipped out from beneath it. Her face is turned toward the back cushions. She looks younger asleep than she does awake.

Orin is in the kitchen eating breakfast. He is already dressed for the day in jeans, socks, and a McGill hoodie, sitting at the kitchen table with a bowl of cornflakes, a spoon, a mug of tea or coffee, and his organic chemistry notes open beside him. He has come down early because he wants the morning to be calm before Danica has to leave for BCS.

Her school does not start until **around nine o'clock**, so there are still three hours before the first bell. In theory, this should be a quiet, manageable morning: breakfast, shower, uniform, drive to Sherbrooke, school.

The house feels ordinary:

- cold morning outside,
- warm kitchen light,
- the quilt over Danica on the couch,
- school things ready,
- Orin eating breakfast before the day begins,
- the first school morning after the first day survived.

Then Danica screams.

---

## Beat 2: The Scream

The sound is raw and terrified, not a startled cry but a full scream from sleep.

She jerks under the quilt. Her hands clench. Her breath catches hard in her chest. She says something half-formed — English, French, and Japanese tangling together because the nightmare has reached below language.

Possible fragments:

> “No—”
>
> “Don’t—”
>
> “I’m not going back—”
>
> “やめて—”
>
> “Please—”

Orin reacts instantly.

His chair scrapes back from the kitchen table. He drops his spoon into the bowl, leaves the cornflakes untouched, and rushes from the kitchen into the living room to Danica’s side.

---

## Beat 3: Orin Reaches Her Side

Orin kneels beside the couch, close enough for her to hear him but not climbing over her or trapping her. His first instinct is to touch her, but he checks himself. She is still asleep, still inside the nightmare. He knows that waking someone from panic by grabbing can make the fear worse.

His voice is low, steady, and immediate.

> “Danica. Danica, it’s Orin.”
>
> “You’re at Ferme Clé.”
>
> “You’re safe.”
>
> “It’s Tuesday morning.”
>
> “You’re on the couch. I’m right here.”

She thrashes once under the quilt. Her eyes are still closed.

> “No, no, no—”

He puts one hand on the edge of the couch where she can feel the movement without being seized.

> “Danica. Listen to my voice. You’re not there. You’re here.”

---

## Beat 4: Permission Before Touch

Danica gasps awake, but not fully. Her eyes open without understanding the room. She tries to sit up too fast and nearly tangles herself in the quilt.

Orin lifts both hands slightly, visible to her.

> “It’s me. I’m not going to touch you unless you want me to.”

This matters. The scene should show his restraint as much as his urgency.

Danica looks around, disoriented:

- the couch,
- the quilt,
- the low lamp,
- the coffee table,
- the snow-light in the windows,
- Orin kneeling beside her.

Her breathing is still too fast.

> “I was—”
>
> “I know.”
>
> “I thought—”
>
> “I know.”

He does not ask her to explain the nightmare immediately. He does not demand details.

---

## Beat 5: Grounding Her

Orin guides her back to the present with simple facts.

> “Tell me five things you can see.”

She cannot answer at first.

He makes it easier.

> “Look at the coffee table. What’s on it?”

She swallows.

> “My book.”
>
> “Good. What else?”
>
> “Your papers.”
>
> “Good.”
>
> “My notebook.”
>
> “Good.”

Her breathing begins to slow.

He keeps his voice calm.

> “What can you feel?”

She clutches the quilt.

> “The quilt.”
>
> “Good. What else?”
>
> “The couch.”
>
> “Good.”

A pause.

> “What can you hear?”

She listens.

> “The heater.”
>
> “Yes.”
>
> “You.”
>
> “Yes. I’m here.”

---

## Beat 6: The Forehead Kiss Echo

Danica notices the quilt fully for the first time. She looks down at it, then at him.

> “You didn’t wake me.”

Orin answers honestly.

> “No.”

> “I slept here all night?”
>
> “Most of it.”

She is embarrassed for half a second, but the fear has not completely left her face.

> “I’m sorry I screamed.”
>
> “Don’t apologize for being scared in your sleep.”

She looks at him for a long moment, still shaken.

If she remembers or half-remembers the forehead kiss, keep it delicate. She may not mention it directly. She may simply touch her forehead once, confused by the residue of comfort.

Orin does not make the kiss into a topic. He lets it remain what it was: a quiet act of reassurance, not a claim.

---

## Beat 7: The Choice to Be Held

Danica’s breathing is steadier now, but she is still trembling.

Orin asks before offering comfort.

> “Do you want me to sit beside you?”

She nods.

He sits on the floor beside the couch first, still giving her space.

After a moment, she whispers:

> “Closer.”

He moves carefully onto the edge of the couch, not under the quilt, not crowding her. He keeps his body turned so she can move away if she needs to.

Danica leans toward him. Not dramatically. Just enough that her shoulder touches his arm.

He lets her decide the contact.

If she reaches for his hand, he gives it. If she leans against him, he lets her. If she only sits close, he accepts that too.

The important emotional rule: Orin does not take comfort from her fear. He gives steadiness to it.

---

## Beat 8: The Police Dream

Danica tells him only one piece of the dream at first.

> “The police came again.”

Orin stays still.

> “Here?”
>
> “Yes.”

She looks toward the front door as if she expects to hear knocking.

> “They came back. The same way. Early. Before school. I was on the couch and I couldn't get up fast enough. They said I had to go back. They said because I slept here, because I was still here, because I didn't go home when they told me to—”

Her breathing catches again.

> “And this time they took me.”

Orin does not correct the dream with legal explanations. This is not the moment for a DPJ lecture or emancipation details. He knows the real police visit, the DPJ process, and the Friday home visit are all tangled together inside her fear.

He says only:

> “You’re here.”
>
> “Nobody is taking you from this couch this morning.”
>
> “It is six o'clock. School is not until around nine. We have time.”
>
> “We will handle today one piece at a time.”

Danica keeps looking at the door.

Orin adds, still calm:

> “If anyone knocks, I answer it. Not you. And we do not open the door to fear. We open it to facts.”

---

## Beat 9: Hélène’s Kitchen Sounds

From the kitchen or the back hallway, there may be a small sound — Hélène arriving quietly, a door opening, water running, a pan being set down. She may have heard the scream but does not intrude immediately.

This can show that the household is alive and staffed, but respectful.

Possible note from Hélène later:

> *J’ai préparé du thé. Pas besoin de parler si vous ne voulez pas. — H.*

Translation: *I made tea. No need to talk if you do not want to.*

This gives Danica another adult presence without forcing an interaction.

---

## Beat 10: Getting Up

Because it is only **six o'clock** and school starts **around nine**, the morning does not have to become frantic. Danica sits wrapped in the quilt for a few minutes while her breathing settles.

Orin does not decide for her. He asks.

> “Do you want to go today?”

Danica is silent at first, then nods.

> “On time?”
>
> “I think so.”
>
> “Okay. On time.”
>
> “But slowly.”
>
> “Slowly.”

After another minute, Danica pushes the quilt back. Her legs are unsteady when she stands, not because she is injured but because the nightmare has left her body weak.

> “I’m going to the bathroom.”
>
> “Okay.”

Orin does not follow her. He stays where he is until she is safely moving down the hall, giving her privacy and dignity after the fear of waking.

The spare-bedroom door is still open. Her uniform is still hanging carefully on the back of it from the night before.

---

## Beat 11: Cornflakes With Orin

When Danica comes back from the bathroom, her face is washed and her hair is still sleep-messy. She has not changed yet. She returns to the kitchen instead of the bedroom.

Orin has not thrown away his breakfast. The bowl of cornflakes is still on the table, softened from sitting in milk after he dropped the spoon and rushed to her. He pushes that bowl aside and gets a fresh bowl down for her.

> “Cornflakes?”
>
> “Yes.”

He pours her a bowl. Then he makes himself another one too, because the first has gone soggy.

They sit at the kitchen table eating cornflakes together in the early morning quiet. It is not a grand breakfast. It is cereal, milk, spoons, and the ordinary sound of two people eating after a frightening thing has passed.

Danica eats slowly at first. Then, once her stomach understands that the fear is over, she eats more normally.

The smallness of the meal matters: cornflakes are not comfort in a dramatic way. They are comfort because they are simple and repeatable. They make the morning real again.

---

## Beat 12: The Thank-You Note to Hélène

After breakfast, Danica notices the note from **Madame Hélène Bélanger** from the night before — the dinner note signed *— H.* The memory of the chicken in plum sauce, baked potatoes, cauliflower, carrots, and salad returns to her.

She says, in French:

> *“Il faut que j’écrive une carte de remerciement à Hélène.”*

Orin nods.

> *“Elle va aimer ça.”*

Danica takes out one of her new notebooks or a small card from the school supplies bought at the BCS bookstore. She writes a short thank-you note to Hélène before getting dressed for school.

Possible text of the note:

> *Madame Hélène,*
>
> *Merci beaucoup pour le dîner hier soir. Le poulet à la sauce aux prunes était merveilleux, et la salade aussi. Après ma première journée de retour à l’école, c’était exactement ce dont j’avais besoin.*
>
> *Merci de prendre soin de nous.*
>
> *Danica*

English sense:

> Madame Hélène, thank you very much for dinner last night. The chicken with plum sauce was wonderful, and so was the salad. After my first day back at school, it was exactly what I needed. Thank you for taking care of us. Danica.

The note shows that Danica is not only being cared for; she is beginning to answer care with care.

---

## Beat 13: The Afternoon Pickup

While Danica is finishing the note, Orin remembers the practical shape of the day.

He speaks carefully, not wanting the change to sound like abandonment.

> “I need to tell you something about this afternoon.”

Danica looks up.

> “I have class at four o’clock at McGill.”

A pause.

> “I can take you to BCS this morning, but I can’t pick you up this afternoon myself. One of the staff will come for you in one of the farm trucks.”

Danica absorbs that. After the police dream, any change in pickup plans has the potential to feel unsafe.

Orin sees it immediately and gives her facts before fear can fill the space.

> “The truck will have the Ferme Clé logo on the side.”
>
> “You will know who is driving before they arrive.”
>
> “I’ll give you their name.”
>
> “They will come to the agreed pickup point.”
>
> “If anything feels wrong, you go back inside BCS and call me. Or Dr. Whitfield. Or Mme Beaudoin.”
>
> “You do not get into any car unless you are sure.”

Danica nods slowly.

> “The farm logo?”
>
> “Yes. Ferme Clé on the door. Same logo as the sign by the gate.”

This helps. It gives her something visible to look for that is not only a stranger’s face.

> “Who?”

If a specific staff member has not yet been named, keep it general for now:

> “I’ll ask one of the house staff after breakfast and tell you before we leave.”

Or, if the story wants a named driver:

> “I’ll ask Martin from the farm office. You met him on Saturday during the barn tour.”

Danica nods again, steadier this time.

> “Okay.”
>
> “And I’ll be reachable before class and after class.”
>
> “At four.”
>
> “At four.”

The point is not that Orin is unavailable. The point is that he is building a reliable system around her so she is not dependent on one person being everywhere at once.

Danica looks at him for a moment after he finishes explaining it. The morning is still sitting in her body — the scream, the dream, the fear of police at the door — but now there is also cereal, a thank-you note, the Ferme Clé logo, and a plan.

She steps closer and kisses Orin Lorin Junior on his **right cheek**.

> “Thank you.”

The kiss is quick and grateful, not theatrical. Orin does not make it larger than it is. He simply receives it, a little still for a second, then nods.

> “You’re welcome.”

Danica turns toward the hallway.

> “I’m going to get ready for school.”
>
> “Okay. I need to get ready for McGill too.”

They both move into the practical part of the morning: **Danica getting ready for BCS**, Orin getting ready for **McGill**. She goes to the spare bedroom to change into her BCS uniform. Orin remains in the kitchen for a moment beside the empty cereal bowls and Hélène’s thank-you note, letting the morning settle before he starts clearing breakfast and gathering his McGill books.

---

## Beat 14: Closing Image

The morning has fully become morning now.

Danica’s thank-you note for Hélène rests on the kitchen table beside the empty cereal bowls. Orin’s original soggy cornflakes sit pushed aside, evidence of how fast he ran when she screamed. Her uniform waits down the hall. School starts around nine. Orin’s four o’clock class waits at the far end of the day. This afternoon, a Ferme Clé truck with the farm logo on the side will come to the agreed pickup point.

The nightmare has passed, but it has left its shape behind.

Orin does not pretend everything is fine.

He gives Danica facts, breakfast, time, and a plan.

---

# Key Emotional Points

1. **Danica’s trauma is physical, not only verbal.** She can be brave at school and still scream in her sleep the next morning.
2. **Orin responds immediately but carefully.** He rushes to her side, but his care is controlled and consent-aware.
3. **He grounds her instead of interrogating her.** He does not force the story of the nightmare out of her.
4. **The couch becomes a symbol of safety.** She fell asleep there by accident; she woke there terrified; he helped make it safe again.
5. **The scene keeps Orin protective without making him possessive.** His steadiness is the point.
6. **The morning creates a realistic school decision.** After a nightmare, Danica may go late, stay home, or ask to see the counsellor.
