# Saturday Morning — Saint-Viateur Bagels with Cream Cheese Tradition — Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Timing:** Saturday morning, March 17, 2077 (Case 1 Chronology — the morning immediately following the Friday arrival of Orin's family).
  - *Context:* This is the morning after the high-stakes foyer confession, the introduction in the kitchen, the swimsuit-free pool swim, and the cozy pyjamas Québécois dinner. Danica has survived the "big reveal" and is officially embraced by Orin's family.
- **Location:** Ferme Clé (the horse farm in Quebec), transitioning to Saint-Viateur Bagels in Montreal, and returning to the Ferme Clé kitchen.
- **Core Characters:**
  - **Orin Lorin Junior** — 19 years old, second-year McGill pre-med student.
  - **Danica** — 17 years old (having turned seventeen on Valentine's Day). She is finishing her BCS autumn-semester classes under youth protection (DPJ) supervision.
  - **Sabrina Bryna Key** — 43 years old. Orin's adoptive mother; she **runs Key Corporation** and communicates primarily in ASL; she has Cerebral Palsy, but **she does not need anything to help her walk** and possesses complete physical independence.
  - **William** — Sabrina's husband and Orin's blood uncle; premier **child ophthalmologist** at SickKids, calm, deeply paternal.
- **Sibling Presence (Traveling Group from Vaughan):**
  - **Taylor Raven Key** (14), **Sun-Young "Liz" Key** (14), **Roberta Katherine Key** (8), and **William Timothy Junior Key** (8).
- **Explicitly Absent Sibling:**
  - **Carlota Delicia Key** (23) — living independently in Orlando with her partner Keith and their nine-month-old daughter Madeleine Jaycee Holiday Key.
- **Tone:** Warm, domestic, ordinary, and deeply restorative. The transition from survival into established ritual.

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene establishes Orin and Danica's cherished Saturday morning tradition—**bagels with cream cheese from Saint-Viateur Bagels**—and integrates it into the family's first morning at the farm. It shows the emotional payoff of the Friday reveal: Danica wakes up for the first time without the crushing weight of secrecy, and she is warmly and unconditionally welcomed into the family's cozy, domestic morning routine. It also highlights their shared family activities and Sabrina's intimate, loving support.

---

## SCENE ARC & BEATS

### Beat 1: Waking Without Secrecy
- Danica wakes up later than usual in her bedroom at Ferme Clé. For the first morning in three months, there is no fear of discovery, no dread of an unannounced visit, and no burden of hiding. Her relationship with Orin and her residency at the farm are officially known and supported by his parents.
- She lies still and listens to the house. The quiet mansion is alive with warm family sounds: the soft clinking of coffee cups, the laughter of the younger siblings downstairs, and the familiar, uneven but independent footsteps of Sabrina Bryna walking down the hallway without any crutches or canes.
- Danica comes downstairs to the kitchen still wearing her cozy sleepwear. 

### Beat 2: The Saturday Bagel Run
- In the kitchen, Orin is already dressed, preparing to head out. Sabrina Bryna and William are sipping coffee at the island, enjoying the peaceful morning.
- Orin explains their Saturday morning tradition to his parents: ever since Danica's first stressful week, they have made it a repeatable ritual to drive to Montreal and pick up fresh, wood-fired bagels from **Saint-Viateur Bagels**.
- Danica, wanting a quiet moment for the two of them to debrief after the high-stakes Friday events, asks to go with him: *"Can I come?"*
- Orin smiles: *"Of course. Saturday is our day."*
- Danica goes to change into comfortable Saturday clothes: jeans, a soft sweater, wool socks, and her winter coat, checking her boots at the entrance.

### Beat 3: The Drive & The Debrief (The West Keys Confession)
- Orin and Danica drive toward Montreal in the winter morning, the heater hum warming the car. 
- For the first time, leaving the farm does not make Danica anxious. She looks at the snow-covered fields and talks with Orin about how incredibly warm his parents were. 
- Orin reassures her that Sabrina and William’s questions in the study were born out of deep, protective care for her safety and legal status, not suspicion. Danica expresses her immense gratitude for the Key family's embrace.
- As the conversation turns toward his own past, **Orin explains the history behind the framed photograph on his dresser that Danica had asked him about earlier**.
- Danica had noticed the photo of **four people—his father, his mother, his baby sister, and a younger Orin Lorin Junior**—and gently asked him who they were. That quiet question serves as the catalyst for his confession now.
- Orin opens up and confesses his deepest, most painful trauma to her, telling her that **he has always felt an overwhelming, crushing weight of survivor's guilt because he was out of the house when his parents and baby sister died** in the devastating May 2073 house fire.
- He explains that when the fire broke out, his North Atlanta High School class was away on **a school trip to the West Keys** (Florida Keys). The school called his teacher on the trip to deliver the devastating news, and the teacher had to pull fifteen-year-old Orin aside right there on the beach.
- He returned to Atlanta only to find his entire household gone in an instant. This silver-framed photograph on his dresser is the only physical piece of his family he has left—Sabrina Bryna and William had that digital photo on **William's iPhone**, and they lovingly **made/printed that physical picture from William's iPhone** so he would have them with him forever.
- This unbearable guilt—the agony of having survived simply because he was away on a school trip—is what rewired his entire personality, turning him from a loud child into the quiet, intensely protective, and watchful young man he is today. He explains that this is why he could never walk away from Danica when she was cut off from home, because he knows the absolute terror of a family vanishing in a single night.
- Danica is deeply moved by this raw, tragic vulnerability. Choking back her own tears, she reaches across the console and tightly clasps his hand, offering him the silent, steadfast comfort of someone who understands exactly what it means to carry a loud silence on the inside.
- Having shared this heavy truth, they talk more quietly, transitioning to lighter matters: bagels, the upcoming organic chemistry labs, and how many tubs of cream cheese they need to buy to feed a family of eight.

### Beat 4: The Saint-Viateur Miracle
- They arrive at the warm, bustling Saint-Viateur Bagels shop in Montreal. The air is alive with the smells of wood fire, sesame, poppy seed, and hot dough.
- They stand in the fast-moving line together. Orin buys a massive quantity—dozens of hot sesame and poppy seed bagels, along with several tubs of plain cream cheese—knowing his younger siblings will eat them like a storm.
- The paper bag is hot in Danica's hands as they walk back to the car. That warmth represents something repeatable, safe, and entirely theirs.

### Beat 5: Returning to the Pyjamas Table
- They return to Ferme Clé and carry the steaming paper bags into the kitchen. 
- The entire family is waiting for them. Sourcing the family's cozy, relaxed lifestyle, **everyone is just wearing their pyjamas around the massive dining room table**—Sabrina, William, the four younger children, Orin, and Danica. 
- They open the bags, releasing a cloud of wood-fired steam. The table is instantly a beautiful, chaotic canvas of domestic bliss:
  * Orin slicing the hot bagels,
  * Danica spreading cream cheese thickly for the twins,
  * Roberta and William Jr. (8) bickering playfully over who gets the bagel with the most sesame seeds,
  * Taylor (14) and Liz (14) enjoying their breakfast in their soft pyjamas, completely comfortable around Danica.
- Sabrina Bryna (who runs Key Corporation) signs to Danica with deep, maternal warmth, while Orin translates: *Orin told us this is your Saturday tradition. It is a very good tradition. From now on, whenever we are here, Saturday is bagel morning.*
- William (the child ophthalmologist) smiles and nods, passing another cream cheese: "Bagels before schoolwork. That is a rule we can support."

### Beat 6: Horseback Riding in the Heated Barn
- After their warm, relaxing breakfast at the dining table, the family transitions into their Saturday activities.
- **William and Orin Lorin Junior take the four children (Taylor, Liz, Roberta, and William Jr.) horseback riding in Ferme Clé's warm, heated barn**, introducing them to the farm's spectacular indoor riding arena.
- Sourcing the family's deeply ingrained, body-positive and natural values, **William allows the children to take their t-shirts off** in the warm barn. Riding completely shirtless and free in the heated arena, the children laugh, ride, and bond with their father/uncle and older brother in an atmosphere of pure, comfortable naturalism.

### Beat 7: The Kitchen Nook Study & Sabrina's Quiet Support (The Apology)
- Meanwhile, inside the peaceful house, **Danica sits at the kitchen nook, diligently doing her homework** to finish her assignments for Bishop's College School (BCS) before Monday.
- **Sabrina Bryna sits with her at the kitchen nook, quietly watching and supporting Danica as she studies.**
- Sponsoring her physical and clinical comfort, Sabrina is **drinking her Matcha Powdered Green Tea, using thickening powder** (thickener) to ensure she can swallow completely safely and easily despite her CP spasticity.
- Sabrina watches Danica's intense focus with immense pride and affection. To communicate with her directly while Danica is still actively learning ASL, **Sabrina Bryna opens her iPad and types out a warm, highly encouraging message, turning the screen to show Danica: *"I'm very impressed with your work routine."***
- Sourcing Sabrina's formidable status as the executive leader who actively **runs Key Corporation**, this specific praise carries massive personal and professional weight for Danica.
- **Reading the words on the iPad, Danica becomes very emotional.** After months of surviving on high alert, leaving her family, and carrying the silent pain of her parents' racist rejection, the sudden, immense validation from Orin's mother breaks through her reserve. She begins to cry—tears of overwhelming relief, healing, and gratitude.
- **Sabrina Bryna reaches across the kitchen nook and gently takes Danica's trembling hands in her own.** With absolute maternal tenderness, Sabrina holds her hands firmly, letting the warmth of her palms ground the young girl.
- Clutching Sabrina’s hands tightly, **Danica quietly and tearfully tells her how incredibly sorry she is for her parents' racist and hateful views against Orin Lorin Junior.** Through her tears, Danica speaks with fierce, protective loyalty, wanting his mother to know how deeply unfair the rejection was, and she confesses a raw, intimate truth: *"I am so, so sorry for what my parents said to him in our living room. Orin did absolutely nothing to deserve that. He is a very excellent, honorable man. On the very first night we came here, when I had just walked out of my house with nothing and was so terrified, **I threw myself at him**. I was so vulnerable, and I tried to kiss him and wanted to sleep with him. But Orin gently pushed me away. He told me he would be taking advantage of me if we did anything that night. He gave me his own shirt and housecoat to sleep in and put me safely in the guest bedroom. He has been the most amazing, honorable, and respectful boyfriend in the world. **He has already told me the whole story, too**—about Atlanta, the school trip to the West Keys, and his baby sister. He trusted me with it, and I know how much pain he carries behind his eyes."*
- Sabrina absorbs this fierce, loving defense of her son. Hearing Danica confess that she had **thrown herself** at Orin in her panic, and how Orin had honored her boundaries—and **realizing that Orin had trusted Danica enough to share the whole story of his family's loss and his West Keys survivor's guilt**—Sabrina is deeply moved. She recognizes the profound, steel-like integrity of the boy she raised, and she holds immense respect for Danica's brave, raw honesty in sharing such a vulnerable truth. Reaching up to gently wipe Danica's tears, Sabrina signs slowly and clearly so Danica can follow: *You do not need to apologize for their hatred. It is not your burden to carry. We know exactly who Orin is, and we see how deeply you love him. That is all that matters. You are safe here, and you are part of our family now.*

### Beat 8: The Sibling Return & The Barn Smell Shower
- **Just as Sabrina and Danica are finishing this deep, private, and emotional bonding conversation, the sibling return begins.**
- While Orin remains at the stables to finish tending to the horses, **William is walking back to the mansion from the heated barn with fourteen-year-old sisters Taylor Raven and Sun-Young**.
- However, the eight-year-old twins, **Roberta Katherine and William Timothy Junior, have run far ahead—and William is too late to catch or stop them before they enter the mansion.**
- **Roberta and William Jr. burst straight through the kitchen door and into the kitchen nook, arguing loudly—and they are covered in horse poop!** Sourcing the shirt-free permission William gave them in the heated barn, **the twins are just wearing their jeans**, running in completely shirtless and bare-chested with the wet manure smeared across their skin. 
- Sponsoring their twin rivalry, they are bickering loudly and pointing fingers about how one of them accidentally pushed the other into a fresh manure pile while Orin was saddling the horses, their messy, shirtless, and poop-covered twin energy immediately filling the quiet kitchen nook and cutting through the heavy emotional tension with a hilarious burst of ordinary family chaos.
- Just as they burst in, **William, Taylor Raven, and Sun-Young walk in through the kitchen door behind them**, with William sighing in exasperated parental defeat at being too late to intercept the twins before they brought the stable mess onto the hardwood floors.
- Sibling introductions and warm, laughter-filled moments are shared at the nook. 
- Sighting the trail of wet manure they are starting to leave, **Danica immediately steps forward and authoritatively orders the twins to stop walking right in their tracks**, because she sees the horse poop is getting onto the clean kitchen floor! The twins halt in shock, completely silenced by her firm, caring, big-sister authority.
- **William, Taylor Raven, and Sun-Young go straight to the bathroom** to wash and clean up from their own barn visit.
- Turning to Sabrina and William, **Danica warmly offers to take Roberta Katherine and William Timothy Junior to the swimming pool's dedicated bathroom herself and wash them.** 
- Sabrina Bryna and William are deeply touched, impressed, and filled with maternal and paternal pride at her immediate, loving initiative to take care of her new younger siblings. William offers his heartfelt thanks, and Sabrina signs her complete, warm approval.
- **Danica leads the shirtless, poop-covered twins straight to the swimming pool's dedicated bathroom.** This bathroom is equipped with a spacious shower and is functionally designed with dual access to serve **both the indoor and outdoor swimming pools**. Danica lovingly gets them completely washed, cleaned up, and refreshed there.
- The scene closes with Danica returning to the kitchen, laughing softly with Orin (who has just walked in from the barn) and the parents about the twins' bickering, her face flushed but glowing with a profound sense of peace and true, unconditional belonging. She has found her ultimate sanctuary.
