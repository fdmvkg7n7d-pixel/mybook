# Orin Lorin Junior and Danica — The Saturday Bagel Tradition — Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Timing:** Saturday morning, Mid-January 2077 (Case 1 Chronology — shortly after Danica's winter term at BCS has begun).
  - *Context:* Orin is 19 years old; Danica is 16 years old (having turned seventeen on Valentine's Day). This is their weekly Saturday bagel tradition, happening in Orin's bedroom at Ferme Clé.
- **Location:** Orin Lorin Junior's bedroom at Ferme Clé (the horse farm in Quebec).
- **Core Characters:**
  - **Orin Lorin Junior** — 19 years old, second-year McGill pre-med student.
  - **Danica** — 16 years old. Day student at Bishop's College School (BCS).
- **Offstage / referenced characters:** Orin's parents, his baby sister Clara, Sabrina Bryna, and William.
- **Tone:** Intimate, cozy, vulnerable, and deeply emotional.

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene coordinates the first time Orin Lorin Junior opens up about his tragic past to Danica. It establishes their intimate Saturday morning bagel ritual occurring in his bed, uses the framed photo of his family on his dresser as the catalyst, and details his painful confession about his parents' and baby sister's death in the Atlanta house fire while he was away on a school trip to the West Keys. It serves as the direct emotional foundation for their relationship's mutual trust and Danica's later disclosure to Sabrina Bryna in March 2077.

---

## SCENE ARC & BEATS

### Beat 1: Bagels in Bed
- A cold Saturday morning in mid-January 2077. Outside Ferme Clé, the winter wind whistles, but Orin's bedroom is a warm, quiet haven.
- **Orin and Danica are eating their Saint-Viateur bagels and cream cheese in his bed**, propped against pillows and surrounded by warm blankets. The paper bag sits open on a wooden tray, releasing hot, wood-fired steam.
- **Danica is wearing her Christmas-present shirt** (the oversized shirt he gave her on Christmas morning), **a pair of white boyshorts pyjamas**, and **nude nylons** underneath. Orin is casually dressed in his McGill hoodie.
- They share a warm, sleepy-eyed conversation about how much cream cheese Danica likes (enough to make the bagel feel soft) and Orin's playful ranking of the bagels' sesame coverage. They enjoy the quiet, ordinary peace of a Saturday morning with no school bells or official appointments.

### Beat 2: The Framed Photo on the Dresser
- As Danica reaches over to set her plate down, her eyes catch a framed **photograph sitting on Orin's dresser** between his watch-tray and **a picture of Danica wearing her Bishop's College School (BCS) uniform, which Orin had taken on the Tuesday after the Monday she returned to school**.
- The photograph shows **four people** standing on a sun-bleached boardwalk under a bright, Southern sky: a laughing father and mother, a younger Orin smiling proudly in a North Atlanta High School t-shirt, and a baby girl with a pink bow in her sparse hair.
- Danica notices the baby’s face tilt is identical to Orin’s. She gently asks him about it: *"Orin? Who are they? There are four of you. One of them is you."*

### Beat 3: The Atlanta House Fire Confession (May 2073)
- A heavy, sudden silence settles over the room. Orin sets his bagel down. He looks at the framed photograph, his dark eyes holding a solemn weight.
- He stands up, walks completely independently to the dresser, and looks at the photo before speaking in a low, quiet voice. He tells her: *"My mother, my father, and my baby sister, Clara. It was taken in April 2073. A month before the fire."*
  - Orin returns to sit on the edge of the bed beside her, his hands clasped over his knees, and **he confesses his deepest, most painful trauma and his crushing survivor's guilt**:
    * When the devastating house fire occurred in May 2073, he wasn't even in Atlanta. 
    * His high school class at North Atlanta High School was away on **a school trip to the West Keys** (Florida Keys).
    * The school called his teacher on the trip to deliver the devastating news, and the teacher had to pull fifteen-year-old Orin aside on the beach.
    * He returned to Atlanta to find his entire household—his parents and his baby sister—gone in an instant.
    * The framed photograph on his dresser is the only physical piece of his family he has left—Sabrina Bryna and William had that digital photo on **William's iPhone**, and they lovingly **made/printed that physical picture from William's iPhone** so Orin would have them with him forever.
- Orin explains that this survivor's guilt—the agony of having survived because he was away on a school trip—rewired his personality, turning him from a loud child into the quiet, watchful, and intensely protective young man he is today. He tells her this is why he could never walk away from her when she fled her parents' house, because he knows what it means to lose everything in a single night.

### Beat 4: Danica's Emotional Breakthrough
- **Danica becomes very emotional upon hearing this raw, tragic vulnerability.** The dam holding back her own trauma—her parents' cold, racist rejection in Sherbrooke, the fear of the police, and the stress of her DPJ file—completely breaks.
- She leans forward, burying her face in his chest, weeping tears of pure relief, healing, and gratitude in his arms.
- Orin wraps his strong arms around her, holding her tightly in his bed. He strokes her back through the soft cotton of her Christmas-present shirt, while she curls her knees up in her white boyshorts and nude nylons.

### Beat 5: Quiet Japanese Intimacy
- Sitting together in his bed while the winter wind whistles outside, Orin begins to comfort her in **Japanese**—their chosen, private language of intimacy, which they use when English feels too blunt:
  * Orin: *"大丈夫だよ (It's okay). 君は一人じゃない。僕がここにいる。ずっとここにいる。(You are not alone. I am here. I will always be here.)"*
  * Danica (clutching his shirt): *"ありがとう、オリン。あなたは、私が出会った中で最も素晴らしく、誇り高い人です。(Thank you, Orin. You are the most wonderful, honorable person I have ever met.)"*
- The scene closes with them holding each other in a tight, unbreakable embrace in his bed, the silver-framed photograph of the four people in Atlanta looking out over the quiet room as a silent witness to their shared sanctuary.
