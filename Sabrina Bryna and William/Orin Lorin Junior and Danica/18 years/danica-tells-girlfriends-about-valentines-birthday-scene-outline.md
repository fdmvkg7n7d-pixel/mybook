# Danica Tells Her Girlfriends About Her Valentine's Day Birthday — Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Timing:** Wednesday lunch, February 15, 2077 (Case 1 Chronology — the day after her seventeenth birthday and Valentine's Day).
  - *Context:* This is the afternoon immediately following the first night Danica and Orin slept together at Ferme Clé. Danica is back at Bishop's College School (BCS) in her uniform, sitting in the dining hall.
- **Location:** The dining hall at Bishop's College School (BCS), Sherbrooke, Quebec.
- **Characters:**
  - **Danica** — 17 years old (having turned seventeen yesterday). Day student at BCS; glowing, carrying a peacefulness she hasn't held in fifty-seven days, wearing Orin's thin silver chain under her uniform collar.
  - **Geneviève Lavoie** — Danica's protective, sharp, and fiercely loyal best friend at BCS; day student, bilingual Francophone.
  - **Chloe Mackenzie** — Danica's warm, romantic, and highly supportive best friend at BCS; day student, Anglo-Quebecer.
- **Offstage / referenced characters:** Orin Lorin Junior, Hélène, the farm's Osaka-trained chef, and Danica's parents.
- **Tone:** Lighthearted, warm, giggly, deeply relational, and celebratory.

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene serves as the lighter, peer-level counterpart and emotional payoff to the intense intimacy of the Valentine's Day / seventeenth birthday chapter. It shows Danica, who is normally guarded and quiet, leaning into her peer support network at BCS. By sharing her romantic victory, her elaborate surprise, and the dissolution of her separate-bedroom boundary with Geneviève and Chloe, Danica transitions from a traumatized, hiding runaway into a happy, loved teenager who is supported by an unbreakable circle of friends.

---

## SCENE ARC & BEATS

### Beat 1: Lunch at the Corner Table
- Danica sits down at their usual corner table in the BCS dining hall, carrying her lunch tray. 
- Geneviève and Chloe are already there, and they immediately freeze, looking at her. They notice a physical change in her posture before she even speaks: the low-level defensive tension she has carried for fifty-seven days has vanished, replaced by a radiant, soft calm.
- Chloe’s eyes drop instantly to Danica's open uniform collar, catching the delicate glint of the thin silver chain resting against her collarbone. 
- Chloe gasps, dropping her fork: *"Danica. What is that?"*

### Beat 2: The Direct Interrogation
- Geneviève leans across the table, her eyes narrowing with sharp, protective curiosity: *"We've been waiting for you all morning. How was yesterday? Did Orin make it a real seventeenth, or do I need to drive to Montreal and have a word with him?"*
- Danica smiles—a real, uninhibited, and blushing smile—and touches the silver chain. *"He gave me this. And he made me a Japanese breakfast. Miso soup, rice, and a soft-cooked egg, just like my mother used to."*
- Chloe clutches her hands to her cheeks: *"Oh my god, he cooked for you? In his pyjamas?"*
- Danica nods, her blush deepening: *"But... he didn't plan the rest of the night. I did. I planned a surprise for him."*
- This completely stuns both girls. Geneviève sets her sandwich down, utterly captive: *"You planned something? Danica, tell us everything right now."*

### Beat 3: Unpacking the Clues & The Red Dress
- Danica leans in close, their lunch trays forgotten, and walks them through her afternoon:
  * She tells them about writing the **six handwritten clues** and hiding them throughout the farmhouse while Orin was in Montreal at McGill.
  * She confesses that she wore **the red backless dress, nude nylons, and red heels** she had secretly bought on their Montreal shopping trip three weeks ago and hidden in her closet.
  * Chloe squeals, drawing a sharp look from a nearby prefect, while Geneviève mutters: *"A backless dress on a horse farm. Danica, you are a complete genius."*

### Beat 4: The Osaka Chef & The Strawberry Shortcake
- She explains where the clues led Orin: to the finished loft in the warm barn.
- She tells them about her meeting with the farm's cook: how she discovered he had spent two years studying culinary skills in **Osaka, Japan**, and how he prepared a perfect Japanese dinner of grilled fish and pickles for them.
- She shares the emotional centerpiece: how the chef had baked a traditional Japanese strawberry shortcake for her dessert, the exact cake her mother used to order.
- She describes the magic of sitting at the table in her red dress, eating home-cooked food, and how they sat talking for hours—Orin opening up about his Atlanta house fire and his West Keys survivor's guilt, and her telling him her funny childhood stories.

### Beat 5: The Rose Blossoms & The Confession
- Danica's voice drops to a soft, giggly whisper as she reaches the bedroom part of the loft:
  * She confesses that she had **scattered pink rose blossoms all over his bed and the floor**, and how she led him by the left hand inside.
  * She tells them, with absolute, warm certainty, that **they slept together for the first time**, officially dissolving the eight-week-old boundary between their rooms.
- Chloe lets out a muffled, ecstatic scream into her napkin, while Geneviève proudly raises her juice glass: *"To Orin, the most honorable man in Quebec, and to you, the luckiest girl in this school."*

### Beat 6: The Gas Station Card & The Circle of Trust
- Danica shares the sweetest, most unexpected detail: Orin was so undone by her clues that on his drive home from McGill, he stopped at a highway gas station to buy her a second card.
- She recites his words from memory: *"I'm saying it again because you asked the chef to make dinner in a barn loft on your own birthday and I am never going to get over that."*
- At the end of the lunch period, the bell rings. Danica, Geneviève, and Chloe stand up, hugging each other tightly. 
- Danica touches the silver chain beneath her collar, realizing that while her biological mother didn't call, she is surrounded by the absolute, unbreakable warmth of her new family and her protective friends.
