# Scene Outline: Sun-Young Receives Her Adoption Papers on Christmas Day

## Placement

**When:** Christmas Day in Sao Paulo, when **Sun-Young is sixteen years old**. The adoption papers have come through shortly before Christmas. For this Sao Paulo trip, **Taylor Raven and Sun-Young are sixteen**, **Roberta Katherine and William Timothy Junior are ten**, **Orin Lorin Junior is twenty-one**, and **Danica is eighteen**.

**Where:** **Sao Paulo**, during the family Christmas Day gathering. They are not at the Vaughan mansion for this scene.

**Characters:** Sun-Young "Liz", Sabrina Bryna, William, Taylor Raven, Roberta Katherine, William Timothy Junior, **Orin Lorin Junior** (21), **Danica** (18), **Adelisa** (Keith's stepsister), **Carlota Delicia**, **Keith**, **Madeleine Jaycee**, and **Caesarius** (Adelisa's son, who is deaf).

---

## Scene Purpose

1. Establish that when **Sun-Young is sixteen years old**, her adoption papers come through.
2. Show **Sabrina Bryna and William giving Sun-Young the adoption papers on Christmas Day**.
3. Make the adoption official emotionally as well as legally: Sun-Young is no longer only the foster child in the family story.
4. Let Sun-Young become very emotional when she sees the papers.
5. Show Sabrina Bryna and William giving her the truth in a way she can keep: paperwork that says what the household has already lived.
6. Establish that this Christmas Day adoption-papers scene happens in **Sao Paulo**, with Adelisa, Carlota Delicia, Keith, Madeleine Jaycee, and Caesarius present.
7. Establish who Adelisa and Caesarius are: **Adelisa is Keith's stepsister; Caesarius is her son; Adelisa and Caesarius live with Carlota Delicia and Keith because Adelisa has Juvenile Arthritis; Caesarius is deaf too.**
8. Include Sabrina Bryna and William asking Sun-Young if she noticed that they did **not** give the Canadian Border Service Agency a letter from her worker to cross the border; Sun-Young tells them she did not notice.
9. Establish that **after opening their presents, William and Keith take the children swimming in the Atlantic Ocean**.
10. Lock the Sao Paulo trip ages for Sabrina Bryna and William's children: **Taylor Raven and Sun-Young are sixteen**, and **Roberta Katherine and William Timothy Junior are ten**.
11. Establish that **Orin Lorin Junior and Danica accompany them to Sao Paulo** because **Orin Lorin Junior is twenty-one** and **Danica is eighteen**.
12. Establish that the children can be **shirtless** while swimming, written as practical, body-positive, and non-sexualized.
13. Establish that while the children are swimming, **Carlota Delicia, Sabrina Bryna, and Adelisa prepare Christmas dinner**.

---

## Core Emotional Throughline

Sun-Young has already been living as Sabrina Bryna and William's daughter. The family has already treated her as theirs. But foster care teaches a child that love and law can be different rooms, and sometimes the locked room wins.

On Christmas Day, Sabrina Bryna and William give her proof that the locked room has opened.

The adoption papers are not just forms. To Sun-Young, they are the end of a question she has been afraid to ask too loudly: *Can I stay?* When she sees them, she becomes very emotional because the answer is suddenly visible in writing.

Sabrina Bryna and William also give her a quiet retrospective proof: they ask if she noticed that, when they crossed the border for this Sao Paulo trip, they did **not** give the Canadian Border Service Agency a letter from her worker. Sun-Young tells them she did not notice. That is the point. They did not make the border crossing scary or ceremonial; the law had already caught up, so there was no worker letter to hand over.

## Scene Structure

### Beat 1: Christmas Morning With a Secret in Sao Paulo

- The family is in **Sao Paulo** for Christmas Day.
- The room is in Christmas motion: gifts, food, noise, siblings moving through the rooms, wrapping paper, travel clutter, and the strange sacred chaos of the Key family holiday happening away from the Vaughan mansion.
- Sabrina Bryna and William know the adoption papers have come through.
- **Orin Lorin Junior and Danica accompany them to Sao Paulo**. Orin Lorin Junior is **twenty-one**, and Danica is **eighteen**.
- **Adelisa, Carlota Delicia, Keith, Madeleine Jaycee, and Caesarius are with them** for the Christmas Day gathering.
- The scene should identify the relationships clearly:
  - **Adelisa is Keith's stepsister.**
  - **Caesarius is Adelisa's son.**
  - **Adelisa and Caesarius live with Carlota Delicia and Keith because Adelisa has Juvenile Arthritis.**
  - **Caesarius is deaf too**, so Sabrina Bryna's ASL and the family's visual language matter in the room in another generation.
- They have kept the timing careful. They are not making Sun-Young wait for belonging; they are choosing Christmas Day because the papers arriving near Christmas makes the day impossible not to mark.
- The papers are placed in a folder / envelope / box that looks ordinary enough not to frighten her.

### Beat 2: Sun-Young Opens It

- Sabrina Bryna and William give the papers to Sun-Young together.
- Because Sabrina Bryna is deaf and communicates in ASL, the moment can include both signed and spoken language:
  - Sabrina signs clearly and slowly;
  - William speaks and / or signs with her;
  - Caesarius, who is deaf too, is part of the visual-language texture of the room even if he is too young to understand the legal meaning;
  - the point is that Sun-Young receives the message in the language of the family, not as a legal lecture.
- Sun-Young opens the envelope or folder.
- At first she may not understand what she is seeing.
- Then she sees her name, Sabrina Bryna's name, William's name, and the word **adoption** / legal wording that makes the truth clear.

### Beat 3: The Emotional Hit

- Sun-Young becomes very emotional when she sees the adoption papers.
- The emotion may come as:
  - stunned silence;
  - shaking hands;
  - tears;
  - looking from the papers to Sabrina and William as if asking whether it is real;
  - trying to read the same line over and over.
- She is not emotional because she doubted their love every day. She is emotional because the papers answer the oldest foster-child fear: there is no one coming to move her away from this family.

### Beat 4: Sabrina Bryna and William Explain

- Sabrina Bryna and William tell her plainly:
  - the papers came through;
  - she is adopted;
  - she is their daughter;
  - she belongs here permanently;
  - this has been true in their hearts, and now the law has caught up.
- Sabrina's ASL should be intimate and clear. She does not overcomplicate the moment.
- William keeps his voice steady and warm.
- They do not ask Sun-Young to be grateful. They let her be overwhelmed.

### Beat 5: The Border-Crossing Detail

- After Sun-Young has seen the adoption papers, Sabrina Bryna and William gently ask her a question.
- They ask whether she noticed that, when they crossed the border for the Sao Paulo trip, they did **not** give the **Canadian Border Service Agency** a letter from her worker.
- Sun-Young tells Sabrina Bryna and William that she **didn't notice that**.
- This is a quiet emotional reveal:
  - before the adoption, international travel would have required worker / foster-care permission paperwork;
  - for this trip, the adoption had come through;
  - Sabrina Bryna and William were not traveling with her as a temporary foster placement requiring a worker's travel letter;
  - they were traveling with their daughter.
- Sabrina and William do not make Sun-Young feel foolish for not noticing.
- They are glad she did not notice, because a border crossing should not be the place where a child has to panic about whether she belongs.
- The missing worker letter becomes one more proof that the adoption is real.

### Beat 6: The Family Holds the Moment

- **Taylor Raven is sixteen** and may be emotional too because she was the child who first found Sun-Young at SickKids and understood that she "didn't have a flamingo."
- **Roberta Katherine and William Timothy Junior are ten**. They may not understand the legal weight, but they understand that this is a big family moment.
- **Orin Lorin Junior and Danica are present** too, having accompanied the family to Sao Paulo.
- **Adelisa, Carlota Delicia, Keith, Madeleine Jaycee, and Caesarius are present** in the Sao Paulo Christmas room, witnessing the family become legally what it has already been emotionally.
- Adelisa's presence should carry her exact context without taking over Sun-Young's scene: she is **Keith's stepsister**, she has **Juvenile Arthritis**, and she and her deaf son Caesarius live with Carlota Delicia and Keith because that household is accessible, safe, and supportive.
- Caesarius being deaf too creates a quiet echo with Sabrina Bryna; do not over-explain it, but let the room's signing / visual attention feel natural.
- No one turns Sun-Young's tears into a performance.
- The family gives her space to look at the papers, hold them, hug them, or hand them back and reach for Sabrina / William instead.

### Beat 7: Sun-Young's Response

- Sun-Young may ask a small, devastating question:

> I can stay?

- Sabrina and William answer immediately.
- The answer should be plain and absolute:

> Yes. You are staying. You are our daughter.

- The papers become something she can hold when the feeling is too big.

### Beat 8: The Hug

- Sabrina Bryna and William hug Sun-Young.
- If Sabrina's CP is physically present in the moment, William may anchor the hug by positioning himself so Sabrina can hold Sun-Young without strain.
- Sun-Young may fold into Sabrina first, William second, or into both of them at once.
- The hug should feel like a legal fact becoming a body fact: the family holding the child the papers now name.

### Beat 9: Christmas Continues, Changed

- Christmas does not stop forever. Eventually someone laughs, a younger sibling asks about a present, food needs attention, Madeleine Jaycee or Caesarius needs something, or wrapping paper is everywhere.
- The children continue opening their presents after the adoption-papers moment has been held.
- But everything after this moment is different for Sun-Young.
- She is not a foster child watching Christmas happen in a house that might not remain hers.
- She is Sabrina Bryna and William's adopted daughter on Christmas Day.

### Beat 10: After Opening Presents — William and Keith Take the Children Swimming

- **After opening their presents, William and Keith take the children swimming in the Atlantic Ocean.**
- The children can be **shirtless** while they swim. This is practical beach/ocean comfort and part of the family's body-positive ease; keep it non-sexualized and ordinary.
- The Atlantic Ocean swimming beat gives the children a physical release after the enormous emotion of the adoption papers.
- William and Keith handle the Atlantic Ocean swimming logistics together.
- The children can include Sun-Young, Taylor Raven, Roberta Katherine, William Timothy Junior, Madeleine Jaycee, and Caesarius as age-appropriate.
- Because **Caesarius is deaf too**, William and Keith keep visual attention and signing / clear gestures part of the Atlantic Ocean swimming supervision.
- While William and Keith take the children swimming, **Carlota Delicia, Sabrina Bryna, and Adelisa prepare Christmas dinner**.
- Adelisa's Juvenile Arthritis matters in the kitchen too: she helps in ways that are comfortable for her joints, sits when she needs to, and is not expected to stand or carry heavy things through a flare.
- Carlota and Sabrina can handle the heavier / more mobile kitchen tasks while Adelisa contributes from a comfortable position.
- The kitchen and ocean beats run in parallel: the children are being held safely in the Atlantic Ocean while the mothers / adult women prepare the Christmas meal.
- Because **Adelisa has Juvenile Arthritis**, the scene should not make her responsible for Atlantic Ocean swimming logistics or chasing children on the beach.
- The Atlantic Ocean swimming scene should not replace the emotional adoption beat; it is the way Christmas keeps moving after the family has absorbed the legal truth.
- For Sun-Young, swimming in the Atlantic Ocean after the papers is different from swimming before them: she enters the water as Sabrina Bryna and William's adopted daughter.

### Beat 11: Closing Image

- Possible closing image:
  - Sun-Young sitting with the adoption papers in her lap, crying into Sabrina Bryna's shoulder.
  - Taylor Raven sitting close by, finally seeing that the girl without a flamingo has one forever.
  - Carlota Delicia, Keith, Adelisa, Madeleine Jaycee, and Caesarius present in the Sao Paulo Christmas room as the papers become real; Adelisa is Keith's stepsister, Caesarius is Adelisa's deaf son, and they live with Carlota and Keith because Adelisa has Juvenile Arthritis.
  - William putting the papers safely back into their folder after Sun-Young has touched them enough to believe them.
  - Sabrina signing **daughter** or **family** while Sun-Young watches through tears.
  - Later, after presents, William and Keith leading the shirtless children toward the Atlantic Ocean, with Sun-Young carrying the certainty of the papers with her.
  - Carlota Delicia, Sabrina Bryna, and Adelisa in the kitchen preparing Christmas dinner while the swimming happens.
