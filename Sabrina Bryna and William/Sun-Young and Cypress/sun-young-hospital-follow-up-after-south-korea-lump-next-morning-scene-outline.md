# Scene Outline: Next-Morning Hospital Follow-Up After Sun-Young Finds the Lump

## Placement

**When:** The **next morning** after the family returns to **Sabrina Bryna and William's mansion** from the South Korea trip. Taylor Raven and Sun-Young "Liz" are **ten years old**.

**Where:** Begins at **Sabrina Bryna and William's Vaughan mansion**, then moves to **Toronto Sick Children's Hospital / SickKids**.

**Characters:** Sabrina Bryna, William, Taylor Raven, Sun-Young "Liz", Devon Smythson, Roberta Katherine, William Timothy Junior, and William's hospital colleague.

**Childcare / Overnight Support:** **Devon Smythson has her own bedroom at Sabrina Bryna and William's mansion**. She does not need to leave; she remains at the mansion and watches the twins, Roberta Katherine and William Timothy Junior, while Sabrina Bryna and William take Taylor Raven and Sun-Young to the hospital.

---

## Scene Purpose

1. Continue the South Korea lump / swollen lymph node medical thread the morning after the family returns home.
2. Establish that **Devon Smythson has her own bedroom at the mansion** and does not need to leave; she remains there and watches the twins so Sabrina Bryna and William can both go with Sun-Young.
3. Show **Sabrina Bryna and William taking Taylor Raven and Sun-Young to the hospital** together.
4. Preserve Taylor Raven as Sun-Young's emotional support after Taylor held Liz's right hand during the bedroom check in Seoul and after **Taylor Raven and Sun-Young slept together for comfort** at the mansion.
5. Reinforce that Sun-Young is a **Leukemia survivor**, so William takes the lump seriously and follows the guidance from the colleague he alerted on the **twentieth day of the South Korea trip**.
6. Keep Sun-Young's foster-child fear visible: she is still nervous that medical trouble might make Sabrina Bryna and William stop wanting her.
7. Let Sabrina Bryna and William reassure her again that they still want her as their foster daughter because they love her so much.
8. Do **not** resolve the diagnosis in this scene unless a later scene requires it; this is the hospital-arrival / evaluation scene.

---

## Continuity Lead-In

- In South Korea, at Sabrina Bryna's Seoul apartment, Sun-Young found a lump / swollen lymph node.
- William asked Sun-Young if he could check the lump.
- They were in the girls' bedroom.
- Taylor Raven was in the room holding Liz's right hand.
- The scene was very emotional.
- William hugged Sun-Young to calm her down.
- Sun-Young was terrified that Sabrina Bryna and William might not want to be her foster parents anymore because of the medical scare.
- William reassured her that he and Sabrina Bryna still wanted her to be their foster daughter because they loved her so much.
- William quickly called one of his colleagues for urgent medical guidance on the **twentieth day of the South Korea trip**, so the colleague already knew about the emergency before the family returned to Vaughan.
- On Day 21, the family returned to Sabrina Bryna and William's mansion.
- **Devon Smythson has her own bedroom at the mansion**, so she does not need to leave; she remains there that night and the household has support.
- **Taylor Raven and Sun-Young sleep together** for comfort after the emotional medical scare. This is a child comfort / sister-support beat: they are ten years old, scared, and trying not to be alone.
- This new scene begins the **next morning**.

---

## Core Emotional Throughline

Sun-Young is not only afraid of the lump. She is afraid of what the lump could mean for belonging.

Leukemia taught her that bodies can betray children. Foster care taught her that homes can disappear. The hospital follow-up combines both fears.

Sabrina Bryna and William respond by making the logistics of the night and morning prove the opposite: Devon has her own bedroom at the mansion and does not need to leave, Taylor Raven and Sun-Young are allowed to sleep together for comfort, both parents come with her to the hospital, and Taylor Raven comes too because Liz needs her. No one treats Sun-Young as a problem to be managed away from the family. They treat her as a daughter they are staying with.

## Scene Structure

### Beat 1: Morning at the Mansion

- The house is quiet after the long flight home from South Korea.
- The luggage is not fully unpacked.
- **Devon Smythson slept in her own bedroom at the mansion** overnight, so the household had support and she did not need to leave.
- **Taylor Raven and Sun-Young slept together** for comfort after the emotional medical scare.
- The emotional weight from the girls' bedroom scene in Seoul still hangs over the family.
- Sun-Young wakes up nervous and watchful beside / near Taylor Raven.
- Sun-Young is wearing **pink shorts and a white T-shirt** for the hospital morning.
- Taylor Raven stays near her, protective and tired.
- Sabrina Bryna and William have already decided they are both going to the hospital with Sun-Young.
- Taylor Raven is going too because she was present during the lump check and because Liz needs her.

### Beat 2: Devon Smythson Is Already at the Mansion / Takes Over the Twins

- **Devon Smythson has her own bedroom at Sabrina Bryna and William's mansion**. She does not need to leave; she is already part of the household support system and watches the twins.
- The twins are **Roberta Katherine and William Timothy Junior**.
- Devon keeps the morning ordinary for them:
  - breakfast;
  - clean clothes;
  - playroom / nursery routine;
  - calm explanations that Mommy, Daddy, Taylor, and Liz have a hospital appointment.
- Devon does not make the morning dramatic in front of the twins.
- Sabrina Bryna trusts Devon with the twins, consistent with Devon staying on as nanny after the Carlos split.
- This allows both Sabrina Bryna and William to go to the hospital with Sun-Young instead of one parent staying home.

### Beat 3: Sun-Young's Fear Comes Back

- As the adults prepare to leave, Sun-Young becomes visibly nervous.
- Her fear is not only medical.
- She is afraid Sabrina Bryna and William might decide they do not want to be her foster parents anymore if the lump means she is sick again.
- She may say it plainly or ask in a small voice:

> If it is bad, do I still get to stay?

- Taylor Raven immediately tightens her grip on Liz's hand or moves closer to her.
- Taylor is frightened too, but her instinct is to protect Liz.

### Beat 4: William and Sabrina Bryna Reassure Her

- William gets down to Sun-Young's level so she does not feel lectured over.
- Sabrina Bryna signs clearly and stays physically close.
- William tells Sun-Young the reassurance again:

> We still want you to be our foster daughter.

> You are not too sick for us to love.

> Sabrina and I love you so much.

- Sabrina Bryna reinforces the same message in ASL:
  - Sun-Young belongs with them;
  - the hospital does not change that;
  - the medical scare is something the family faces with her, not a reason to send her away.
- This reassurance should happen before they leave the mansion, so Sun-Young is not carrying the question alone in the car.

### Beat 5: Leaving the Mansion

- Devon remains at the mansion with Roberta Katherine and William Timothy Junior because she has her own bedroom there and does not need to leave.
- The twins may wave from the doorway / window or be kept distracted by Devon depending on tone.
- Sabrina Bryna and William take **Taylor Raven and Sun-Young** to the car.
- Sun-Young is still in her **pink shorts and white T-shirt**.
- Taylor Raven and Sun-Young can sit beside each other in the back seat with regular seatbelts.
- They can hold hands in the car, with Taylor keeping physical contact so Liz does not feel alone.
- William is careful not to speak in frightening medical shorthand.
- Sabrina Bryna watches Sun-Young's face and signs small reassurance when Liz looks toward her.

### Beat 6: Car Ride to SickKids

- The car ride is quiet.
- Sun-Young may ask whether William's colleague thinks it is Leukemia again.
- William does not lie, but he does not frighten her with speculation.
- He explains that the reason they are going to the hospital is because checking is how they keep her safe.
- Taylor Raven stays with Liz emotionally; the girls sit beside each other and hold hands during the ride.
- Sabrina Bryna's presence matters because this is not only William-the-doctor managing a case; this is both parents bringing their foster daughter for care.

### Beat 7: Arrival at the Hospital

- They arrive at **Toronto Sick Children's Hospital / SickKids**.
- This location carries history:
  - Sun-Young was treated there for Leukemia;
  - Taylor Raven first met Sun-Young there;
  - William works there.
- William has to shift carefully between doctor knowledge and parent role.
- He is not casual just because he knows the building.
- He is protective because this is Sun-Young.
- Sabrina Bryna keeps communication accessible through ASL / visual attention.

### Beat 8: William's Colleague Meets Them

- William's colleague is expecting them because William called quickly on the **twentieth day of the South Korea trip**. The colleague already knows about the emergency and is ready for the follow-up.
- The colleague treats Sun-Young gently and directly, not as a chart being handed from one doctor to another.
- Sun-Young is wearing **pink shorts and a white T-shirt**; the colleague explains what needs to happen before examining the lump.
- William lets the colleague lead the medical evaluation so he can stay present as Sun-Young's foster father.
- This matters emotionally: Sun-Young sees that William is not distancing himself from her by turning her into a case.
- Taylor Raven can stay in the room and holds Sun-Young's left hand.

### Beat 9: Initial Evaluation

- Possible hospital steps:
  - vitals;
  - physical exam of the lump / swollen lymph node;
  - because Sun-Young is wearing a **white T-shirt**, she can take her T-shirt off to let the doctor check the lump; she does not need to wear a hospital gown; keep this clinical and consent-based;
  - bloodwork such as CBC / differential as appropriate for Leukemia follow-up;
  - **ultrasound of the lump / swollen lymph node**;
  - review of Sun-Young's Leukemia history and survivorship concerns.
- Keep the tone focused and careful.
- The doctor asks / explains before Sun-Young removes her T-shirt. Sabrina Bryna and William stay present as her foster parents. Taylor Raven stays in the room and holds Sun-Young's left hand.
- The **ultrasound looks bad**, which raises the urgency without creating a final diagnosis in this scene.
- Do not give a final diagnosis in this outline unless a later continuity decision requires it.
- The point is that William takes the lump seriously and gets Sun-Young evaluated quickly.

### Beat 10: Conversation Outside the Examination Room

- After the ultrasound, William's colleague asks to speak with William **outside the examination room** because the lump looks bad on the ultrasound.
- William and his colleague step out of the examination room for the conversation.
- Sabrina Bryna stays with Sun-Young and Taylor Raven so the girls are not left alone.
- Taylor Raven remains beside Sun-Young and holds Sun-Young's left hand while they wait.
- The colleague tells William plainly that the lump / swollen lymph node looks bad on ultrasound and needs urgent follow-up.
- The colleague says Sun-Young is required to have **surgery sooner than later** because of what the ultrasound shows.
- The colleague is **checking the schedule** for the soonest surgical opening / appropriate surgical slot.
- William hears the medical seriousness but stays controlled because Sun-Young is already terrified.
- This conversation should raise stakes without giving a final diagnosis yet.
- William's role splits again: he understands the medical danger, but he also has to return to the room as Sun-Young's foster father, not as a frightened doctor.

### Beat 11: William Tells the Family Surgery Is Required

- William returns to the examination room where Sabrina Bryna, Sun-Young, and Taylor Raven are waiting.
- He tells the family plainly and gently that the ultrasound means Sun-Young is required to have **surgery sooner than later**.
- He does not present the surgery as optional or distant; it is necessary and needs to happen soon.
- He also tells them his colleague is **checking the schedule** now.
- Sabrina Bryna stays close to Sun-Young and makes sure she can follow what is being said.
- Taylor Raven keeps holding Sun-Young's left hand.
- Sun-Young is frightened, but William frames the surgery as the next step in taking care of her, not as proof that she is unwanted or too much trouble.
- William and Sabrina Bryna again reinforce that this does not change whether they want her as their foster daughter.
- The scene still does not name a final diagnosis; the immediate canon is that the ultrasound looks bad and surgery is required soon.

### Beat 12: Taylor Raven as Support

- Taylor Raven remains emotionally central.
- She is not the patient, but she is Liz's support person.
- Taylor Raven stays in the room and holds Sun-Young's left hand during the exam, including while Sun-Young takes off her white T-shirt so the doctor can check the lump.
- Taylor may be scared of the hospital environment because of her own medical anxiety, but she stays because Liz needs her.
- This reinforces the sister bond that began when Taylor first found Sun-Young at SickKids.

### Beat 13: Waiting Room / Family Holding Pattern

- After the initial steps, the family waits.
- Sabrina Bryna sits close to Sun-Young.
- William stays calm but cannot hide that he is serious.
- Taylor Raven leans against Liz or holds her hand.
- Sun-Young may ask again if she is still wanted.
- Sabrina Bryna and William answer again without impatience.
- They understand that one reassurance does not erase foster-care fear.

### Beat 14: Devon at the Mansion Parallel Beat

- Brief cutaway / note:
  - Devon keeps Roberta Katherine and William Timothy Junior safe and occupied at the mansion.
  - The twins' day remains calm because Devon is watching them.
  - This parallel beat prevents the scene from forgetting the younger children while keeping the hospital focus on Sun-Young.
- Devon may receive a short update from Sabrina Bryna or William that they are still at the hospital and the twins are fine.

### Beat 15: Closing Image

- Possible closing image:
  - Sun-Young sitting between Sabrina Bryna and William at SickKids, with Taylor Raven pressed close beside her.
  - Taylor's hand wrapped around Sun-Young's left hand.
  - William and his colleague just outside the examination room after the ultrasound looks bad, speaking quietly before William returns to Sun-Young.
  - Sabrina Bryna signing **love** or **stay** to Sun-Young while they wait.
  - At the mansion, Devon keeps Roberta Katherine and William Timothy Junior in their morning routine, protecting the household's calm while the older girls are at the hospital.
