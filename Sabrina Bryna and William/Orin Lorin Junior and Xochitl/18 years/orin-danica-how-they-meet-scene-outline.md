# SCENE OUTLINE: ORIN LORIN JUNIOR & DANICA — THE UNDERGROUND SANCTUARY (HOW THEY MEET)

**Characters:** Orin Lorin Junior (age 19, student at McGill University), Danica (age 16, day student at Bishop’s College School), Geneviève and Chloe (Danica's girlfriends from BCS), Danica's parents.
**Setting:** Montreal, Quebec — The sprawling Underground City (RÉSO) food court during early Christmas shopping, transitioning to weekend phone calls between Sherbrooke and Montreal, Danica's family living room in Sherbrooke, Ferme Clé, and the Saturday phone call to Vaughan cancelling the Key Corporation plane pickup.
**Timeframe:** Mid-November through the weekend before Christmas Day. Orin is nineteen; Danica is sixteen.
**Theme:** Finding sanctuary beneath the surface; shared stillness in a bustling food court; building anticipation through distance; confronting familial prejudice in the living room; choosing absolute solidarity and mutual devotion over parental approval and luxury family holiday plans.

---

## THE CONTEXT

Nineteen-year-old Orin Lorin Junior is attending McGill University in Montreal. While Orin studies in Montreal during the academic term, he has the opportunity to live at a sprawling horse farm located between Montreal and Sherbrooke, known as **Ferme Clé**. Sabrina Bryna and William bought Ferme Clé ten years ago as an investment property. Orin is given the freedom to live at this farm, finding immense peace in its wide-open rural sanctuary. 

Orin's twelve-year-old sister Sun-Young "Liz" (along with the rest of his family) is not in the picture during his daily routine in Quebec because she is living full-time with Sabrina Bryna and William in Vaughan, Ontario. For the upcoming Christmas holidays, the entire family is planning to gather in Orlando, Florida. Sabrina Bryna and William are still in Vaughan, and they are supposed to fly over to Montreal in the **Key Corporation plane** to pick Orin Lorin Junior up before continuing on to Florida.

Across the province in Sherbrooke, sixteen-year-old Danica attends the elite Bishop’s College School (BCS) as a day student. Her family originally hails from Japan, and Danica carries a quiet, graceful composure, navigating the vibrant traditions of Canadian independent school life. While Danica is connected to her heritage, she isn't fully fluent in Japanese, though she can understand a little bit.

## THE OUTLINE

### Part One: Mid-November in the Montreal Underground City (RÉSO)

- It is the **middle of November** in Montreal. The crisp autumn chill has set in above ground, and early holiday displays are just beginning to appear in the sprawling Underground City (RÉSO).
- Orin Lorin Junior, nineteen, is walking near the Eaton Centre food court, dressed comfortably in his favorite crimson **McGill University hoodie**, taking a much-needed break from his university coursework.
- Danica, sixteen, is in Montreal for the day. Her mother has driven her into the city from Sherbrooke for an early Christmas shopping excursion.
- While her mother attends to errands on the upper levels, Danica is scheduled to meet two of her close girlfriends from Bishop’s College School, **Geneviève** and **Chloe**, for an afternoon of shopping and lunch.
- Arriving a bit ahead of their agreed meeting time, Danica grabs a table right near the entrance of the underground food court and begins eating a tray of hot french fries while waiting for her friends.

### Part Two: The Encounter, The McGill Hoodie & The Stolen French Fries

- Orin notices Danica sitting near the entrance of the food court. He spots the distinct crest of her Bishop's College School bag, but it is Danica herself—her calm, graceful composure amidst the bustling food court—that catches his eye. He finds her deeply interesting.
- Recognizing someone waiting alone, Orin approaches her table. His crimson **McGill University hoodie** immediately puts her at ease—an undeniable sign of established local respectability and safety from a nearby university student.
- Orin strikes up a polite, friendly conversation in English. He does not speak Japanese to her; instead, they connect purely through genuine curiosity, natural charm, and immediate mutual intrigue.
- As Danica sits eating, the conversation flows with an unexpected, effortless familiarity. In a moment of charming, spontaneous warmth that completely breaks any lingering stranger tension, Orin reaches across the table and playfully **steals some french fries from Danica's tray**. Danica laughs, her quiet reserve melting away. Beneath the rushing city, they establish an immediate island of genuine connection and shared joy.
- When **Geneviève** and **Chloe** finally rush up to join her with enthusiastic greetings, Orin politely excuses himself, but not before exchanging contact numbers with Danica.

### Part Three: The Weekend Calls (Mid-November to December)

- Following their chance encounter in mid-November, Orin and Danica do not immediately meet up in person. Instead, they establish a cherished, dedicated routine: **they call each other every weekend**.
- With Danica living in Sherbrooke with her parents and attending Bishop's College School during the week, and Orin managing his rigorous academic schedule at McGill University in Montreal, their weekend phone calls become the highlight of their respective weeks.
- Over the phone, their connection deepens profoundly. Orin shares his quiet academic dedication, while Danica shares her life in Sherbrooke. Despite their three-year age difference (nineteen and sixteen), they share a remarkable emotional maturity. Across the distance, their regular weekend calls become a steadfast, irreplaceable emotional sanctuary.

### Part Four: The Sherbrooke Living Room & Cancelling the Key Corp Plane (The Weekend Before Christmas Day)

- After over a month of building intense anticipation through their weekend calls, **they finally see each other again in person on the weekend before Christmas Day**.
- Orin is supposed to be picked up in Montreal by Sabrina Bryna and William in the Key Corporation plane for the holiday trip to Orlando, but before his departure, Danica invites him over to her family home in Sherbrooke for dinner to meet her parents.
- Staying nearby at **Ferme Clé**—the horse farm located between Montreal and Sherbrooke that Sabrina Bryna and William bought ten years ago as an investment property—Orin makes the short drive to her house for the evening.
- Orin arrives looking sharp and polite. However, as they gather in the **living room before dinner**, a severe underlying tension emerges. Danica's parents harbor deep-seated racial prejudice, specifically exhibiting strong racism against Black people.
- Assuming that Orin only speaks English because he has never spoken Japanese during his interactions with Danica, her parents openly utter four nasty, disparaging sentences in Japanese right there in the living room.
- While Danica isn't fully fluent in Japanese, she understands enough to catch the cruel, racist meaning of what her parents are saying to her boyfriend. She becomes incredibly emotional, horrified and heartbroken by her parents' behavior.
- Orin maintains his quiet, steadfast composure and resolute moral dignity. Turning to her parents, he lets them know in flawless Japanese that he understood every single word. He informs them that when he was attending high school, he studied Japanese for four years.
- Seeking to diffuse their prejudice with dignity and established respectability, Orin Lorin Junior lets Danica's parents know exactly where he has been living for sixteen months (at Ferme Clé), because he doesn't want them to think racist thoughts about him.
- **However, they say another thing that is racist.** Their willful ignorance proves uncompromising.
- Overwhelmed with emotion and deeply mortified by what her parents said to Orin, Danica makes a radical, decisive choice right then and there. She refuses to stay in that house. She decides to leave with Orin Lorin Junior instead of living in her parents' home. 
- **She doesn't pack anything.** Without taking a single bag or possession, she walks right out the front door into the quiet winter night with Orin, choosing absolute solidarity over her old life.
- Orin drives Danica directly back to Ferme Clé. (For the detailed exploration of her arrival, the mansion tour, and the Friday night sleeping attire, see `ferme-cle-mansion-tour-scene-outline.md`).
- Because she left immediately without packing anything, **Danica can't travel out of Canada without a passport.** She cannot leave the country for Orlando.
- Facing the prospect of flying to Florida alone, Orin Lorin Junior makes his own profound sacrifice: **he decides to stay with Danica.** She decides to stay with Orin Lorin Junior at Ferme Clé.
- **On Saturday**, Orin calls Sabrina Bryna and William, who are still in Vaughan preparing to fly over to Montreal in the Key Corporation plane to pick him up. Orin informs them he is staying in Quebec. To protect Danica's privacy and avoid a painful explanation of her parents' prejudice, he makes an excuse. 
- **His excuse:** He tells them that a close friend needs him because his friend's parents died. Because Orin himself was orphaned at sixteen in a devastating house fire, Sabrina Bryna and William immediately understand the profound, sacred weight of this duty. They offer their full blessing for him to remain behind, cancelling the Montreal stop for the Key Corporation plane.
- Forgoing the luxury trip to Orlando, Orin and Danica remain together in the peaceful winter sanctuary of Ferme Clé. (For the detailed exploration of their Saturday morning breakfast and barn tour, see `ferme-cle-bagels-and-barn-tour-scene-outline.md`).

## WHY THIS SCENE MATTERS

- **It establishes a highly specific, iconic Canadian autumn geography.** Setting the initial meeting in Montreal's Underground City (RÉSO) food court during a mid-November shopping excursion roots the romance in rich, authentic regional lore.
- **The McGill hoodie serves as a crucial visual anchor.** Orin wearing his McGill hoodie instantly bridges the initial stranger barrier, projecting safety, academic dedication, and local comfort to a sixteen-year-old student waiting for her friends.
- **The stolen french fries add essential human warmth.** Orin playfully stealing french fries from Danica's tray injects a vital spark of flirty, charming spontaneity, transforming a polite encounter into the beginning of a genuine romance.
- **It introduces rich supporting friendships.** Giving Danica two named girlfriends, Geneviève and Chloe from Bishop's College School, adds wonderful social texture to her life as a day student.
- **The pacing builds beautiful romantic tension.** Having them call each other every weekend without seeing one another in person until the weekend before Christmas Day creates a profound, mature emotional foundation before their grand holiday reunion.
- **The living room confrontation is a masterclass in dramatic execution.** Having the parents utter four specific nasty sentences in Japanese in the living room before dinner creates an explosive catalyst. Orin revealing that he studied Japanese for four years in high school, and explaining where he has been living for sixteen months so they won't think racist thoughts about him, brilliantly demonstrates his dignity and intelligence. The parents' refusal to listen—saying another racist thing—pushes the scene to its necessary breaking point.
- **Danica's emotional breakthrough defines her character.** Highlighting that Danica isn't fully fluent but understands enough to be deeply emotional over what her parents said to her boyfriend gives her agency. Her definitive choice to walk out immediately with Orin without packing anything, rather than living in her parents' home, establishes her immense loyalty and courage.
- **The Key Corporation plane and Saturday call elevate the corporate wealth lore.** Establishing that Sabrina Bryna and William are still in Vaughan and are supposed to fly over to Montreal in the Key Corporation plane to pick him up perfectly frames the immense scale of the family's resources. Orin cancelling his pickup on Saturday with the excuse that a friend needs him because his friend's parents died perfectly echoes Orin's own tragic backstory, ensuring his adoptive parents completely understand and support his decision to stay.
