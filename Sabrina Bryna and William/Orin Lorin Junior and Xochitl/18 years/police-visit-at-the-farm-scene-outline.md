# Police Visit at the Farm — Scene Outline

## Placement

**When:** The next morning (Saturday) after Danica arrives at the farm.  
**Location:** Sabrina Bryna and William’s horse and rabbit farm.  
**Focus:** The police arriving because Danica’s parents reported her missing, and their separate interviews with Orin and Danica.

---

## Characters

- **Danica (Xochitl)** — 16 years old.
- **Orin Lorin Junior** — 19 years old.
- **Officer Bouchard** — Male police officer.
- **Officer Tremblay** — Male police officer.
- **Officer Roy** — Female police officer.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1: The Police Arrive

The next morning, three police officers arrive at the farm in a marked cruiser. It is a quiet Saturday morning. The officers knock on the front door.

Orin answers the door. Officer Bouchard and Officer Tremblay stand in front, while Officer Roy stands slightly behind them.

> “We’re looking for Danica,” Officer Bouchard says. “Her parents reported her missing last night.”

Orin remains calm.

> “She’s here. She’s safe.”

### Beat 2: Separate Interviews

The officers explain that they need to speak with both Orin and Danica separately. Officer Bouchard takes Orin into the living room for questioning, while Officer Tremblay and Officer Roy speak with Danica in the kitchen.

Danica is still wearing Orin’s shirt and housecoat over her ivory tights. She looks tired but composed.

### Beat 3: Danica Shows Officer Roy Where She Slept

During her interview, Officer Roy asks Danica where she slept the night before. Danica stands up and leads her down the hallway to the spare bedroom.

She opens the door and shows Officer Roy the bed, which is still unmade.

> “I slept here,” Danica says quietly. “Alone.”

Officer Roy nods and makes a note. She does not press further on the matter.

### Beat 4: Orin’s Interview

In the living room, Officer Bouchard asks Orin a series of questions about how Danica came to be at the farm. Orin answers honestly and calmly, explaining that Danica left her parents’ house after they made racist remarks about him.

He does not become defensive, but he is clear that Danica came with him willingly.

### Beat 5: The Officers Leave

After the interviews, the three officers confer briefly outside. Officer Roy returns to the door and speaks to both Orin and Danica.

> “We’ll file our report. For now, it looks like she’s here of her own free will. We’ll be in touch if we need anything else.”

The officers leave the farm. Once the cruiser drives away, the house falls quiet again.

### Beat 6: Breakfast Request

After the police leave, Danica and Orin stand in the kitchen. Danica looks at him and speaks softly.

> “Can you go to Saint Viateur Bagels and get some bagels with cream cheese? For our breakfast?”

Orin nods without hesitation.

> “Of course. I’ll be back soon.”

He grabs his keys and heads out to buy the bagels.

---

## Emotional Notes

- This scene adds external pressure and legal tension to Danica’s decision to leave home.
- Danica remains calm and cooperative during the interview.
- Orin handles the situation with maturity and honesty.
- The separate interviews and Danica showing her room help establish that nothing inappropriate occurred.
