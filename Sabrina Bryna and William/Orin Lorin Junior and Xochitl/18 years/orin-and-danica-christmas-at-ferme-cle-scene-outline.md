# SCENE OUTLINE: ORIN LORIN JUNIOR AND DANICA'S CHRISTMAS AT FERME CLÉ

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Orin Lorin Junior, Danica
- **Offstage / referenced characters:** Sabrina Bryna, William, Carlota Delicia, Keith, Madeleine Jaycee, Roberta Katherine
- **Location:** Ferme Clé, between Montreal and Sherbrooke
- **When:** Christmas Eve through Christmas Day, after Orin has told his family he is staying behind
- **Status:** New scene outline
- **Tone:** Quiet, tender, improvised, bittersweet, intimate

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene shows Orin and Danica building a real Christmas together at Ferme Clé under difficult circumstances: secrecy, family absence, guilt, and new attachment. It should feel modest but deeply meaningful.

---

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: The Setup
- This is Danica's first Christmas away from her parents.
- This is Orin's first Christmas missing the Orlando family gathering by choice.
- Orin feels guilty about the lie he told, but he still knows he did the right thing by staying.

### Beat 2: Making a Holiday Anyway
- They create Christmas out of what is available: food, the farm, winter quiet, maybe a small tree or improvised decorations.
- The emotional point is not luxury but intentionality.

### Beat 3: Danica Finds Roberta Katherine's Beadwork Box
- Somewhere in the house, Danica finds **Roberta Katherine's beadwork box**.
- Using the materials, she makes **Orin a beadwork necklace** for Christmas.
- The gift matters because it is handmade, thoughtful, and created from what she finds in his family's world.

### Beat 4: Orin's Gift to Danica
- Orin gives Danica **the shirt she wore on the first night** as her Christmas gift.
- The gift is emotionally loaded rather than expensive.
- The shirt has become the material memory of her first night of refuge, restraint, safety, and care at Ferme Clé.

### Beat 5: Christmas Day Call
- Orin calls the family on Christmas Day.
- The call should carry warmth and longing.
- He still feels guilty, but the call confirms he has not been cut off by the choice he made.

### Beat 6: The Emotional Center
- By the end of the day, the holiday feels real.
- Not easy, not uncomplicated, but real.
- Ferme Clé becomes not just a refuge from a bad night, but a place where a shared life is beginning.

---

## WHAT THIS SCENE ESTABLISHES

1. Orin feels guilty, but still knows he did the right thing.
2. Danica actively makes Christmas with him rather than just receiving rescue.
3. The beadwork necklace and first-night shirt become foundational objects in their relationship.
4. The Christmas Day family call keeps the Orlando branch emotionally present.
5. Ferme Clé becomes a true holiday home for them.
