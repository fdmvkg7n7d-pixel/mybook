# SCENE OUTLINE: MONDAY MORNING — DANICA'S BCS OFFICE MEETING AND BOOKSTORE VISIT

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:**
  - **Danica (Xochitl)** — 16 years old. Day student at BCS. Has no uniform, no schoolbooks, no student ID. In civilian clothes.
  - **Orin Lorin Junior** — 19 years old. **Second-year McGill University student, biology and chemistry, pre-medical track.** Ferme Clé is his rural sanctuary, not a job (the barn has its own staff). Wealthy — Key Corporation family. **Today is also the first day of his McGill winter term**; he has cleared his morning lectures and a planned syllabus pickup so he can drive Danica in, walk in with her for moral support, wait in the front office during the meeting, and accompany her to the bookstore. His afternoon class is still on; he will go to McGill in the afternoon and come back to pick Danica up at end of day.
  - **Mme Solange Beaudoin** — BCS front-office secretary. Francophone Quebecer, long-tenured. Recognizes Danica by sight.
  - **Dr. Margaret "Maggie" Whitfield** — BCS **Dean of Student Affairs**. Anglo-Quebecer. Calm, decisive, has seen youth-protection situations before. Keeps a kettle in her office.
  - **Mr. Daniel Côté** — BCS bookstore manager. Bilingual, practical, doesn't ask intrusive questions.
  - **Ms. Renée Lapointe** — BCS school counsellor. Francophone Quebecer, mid-thirties, bilingual. Dr. Whitfield names her by the end of the meeting so Danica will recognize the name when Ms. Lapointe calls later in the week. She does not appear onstage in this scene.
  - **Geneviève Lavoie** — Danica's close girlfriend from BCS. Francophone Quebecer, day student, sharp and protective. Established in *Orin & Danica — How They Meet*; one of the two friends Danica called from Orin's iPhone the night she left home, and on the phone with her many times in the two weeks since.
  - **Chloe Mackenzie** — Danica's close girlfriend from BCS. Anglo-Quebecer, day student, warmer and more demonstrative. The other of the two friends Danica called Friday night, and on the phone with her constantly since.
- **Offstage / referenced:** Danica's parents (who called the school over the break), the SQ officers (Bouchard, Tremblay, Roy), the DPJ worker, Sabrina Bryna and William (back in Vaughan, Ontario — the Orlando trip ended over the weekend because the younger children's winter term has started; still not informed about Danica)
- **Location:** Bishop's College School (BCS), Sherbrooke, Quebec — main office building, the Dean of Student Affairs' office, and the campus bookstore
- **When:** **Monday morning**, first day back at school after the **two-week Christmas break** — Danica left her parents' home on the Friday night the break began, the police visit was the next morning, and tomorrow is now today. The Friday DPJ home visit is four days away. Danica is finishing her **autumn semester** at BCS (same classes she had before the break). For Orin it is also the first day of his McGill **winter** term.
- **Status:** New scene outline
- **Tone:** Quietly miraculous. The thing Danica has been afraid of for two weeks turns out to be survivable — not because the world is gentle, but because two specific adults at BCS choose to be decent.

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

This is the scene that ends the bubble. Danica walks into the BCS office expecting the worst — that her parents will be called before she sits down, that she'll be turned away, that she'll lose her place as a day student. Instead, the secretary recognizes her, the Dean welcomes her back, and within an hour she has a new uniform on her arm and a counsellor appointment on her calendar.

The scene is **not** a fairy tale — Dr. Whitfield is professional, not sentimental, and there are conditions and forms. But the conditions are workable, and the welcome is real.

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: The Drive In

Monday morning. The barn staff have been in for hours already; Orin is up later than them, dressed, and has cleared his McGill morning — first day of the winter term — to be free for the whole morning with Danica. His afternoon organic chemistry class is still on. They eat almost nothing. Danica is in the civilian outfit she laid out the night before: plain sweater, dark trousers, the boots she walked off her parents' porch in. The small bag with the legal aid pamphlet is on her lap.

Orin drives. The forty minutes pass mostly in silence. As they get close to Sherbrooke, Danica says, quietly:

> "Don't drop me two blocks away. Please come in with me."

Orin nods. He had already decided he would, but he lets her ask.

> "I won't sit in the meeting. I'll be in the front office. You'll be able to see me through the door."
>
> "Okay."

He parks in the visitor lot. They walk to the main office building together. He opens the door for her. She walks in first.

### Beat 2: Mme Beaudoin at the Front Desk

The front office is warm, fluorescent, smells like the laminator. **Mme Solange Beaudoin** is at the desk — sixties, glasses on a beaded chain, a cardigan over her shoulders. She looks up, and her face does a small thing: recognition, then concern, then a deliberate professional smoothing-over. She has seen Danica every morning for two years.

> "Danica. *Bonjour, ma belle.* You're back."

Danica's throat closes for a second. She manages:

> "*Bonjour*, Mme Beaudoin. I need to speak with the Dean of Student Affairs, please. Dr. Whitfield. It's — it's not about my schedule."

Mme Beaudoin looks at Danica's clothes, then at Orin standing one pace behind her, then back at Danica. She does not ask. She picks up the phone.

> "*Un instant.*"

She speaks briefly into the receiver in French, low. She hangs up.

> "She'll see you now. Go on through. *Tu peux laisser ton ami ici*, he can wait — there are chairs by the window."

Orin sits where she points. Danica catches his eye once before she goes through the inner door. He nods. She goes.

### Beat 3: Dr. Whitfield's Office

**Dr. Margaret Whitfield** — "Maggie" to colleagues, "Dr. Whitfield" to students — stands when Danica comes in and gestures to the chair across from her desk, not the formal chair, the soft one by the window. There is a kettle on the credenza. There is a box of tissues on the desk that is clearly placed, not decorative.

> "Danica. Sit down. Take your coat off if you'd like. Would you like tea?"
>
> "No. Thank you. I — I'd like to just tell you, before I lose my nerve."
>
> "Go ahead."

Danica delivers the speech she rehearsed. She gets through most of it without breaking:

- She left her parents' home a week ago Friday.
- She is safe. She is staying with a friend who is an adult.
- The Sûreté du Québec came to where she is staying the morning after she left. They confirmed she is safe and referred the file to the DPJ.
- There is an open DPJ file. A worker is coming to do a home visit on Friday.
- She is sixteen. She knows she is a minor under Quebec law. She has been given information about simple emancipation under the Civil Code and may pursue it.
- She does not want to give the address of where she is staying.
- She wants to know what BCS needs from her to continue as a day student.

Dr. Whitfield listens without interrupting. When Danica finishes, Dr. Whitfield is quiet for a moment, then says:

> "Thank you for coming in and telling me directly. That took courage. I want to say three things before anything else. First: you are still a student here. Nothing you just told me changes that. Second: I am not going to call your parents this morning. I want you to hear that clearly. I am not. Third — and you need to know this — your parents called the school during the break."

Danica's whole body goes still.

Dr. Whitfield continues, calmly, no edge in her voice:

> "Your mother called the main office twice. Once the Monday after Christmas, once again on the Thursday. She spoke to Mme Beaudoin both times. She said you had run away and asked whether the school knew where you were. Mme Beaudoin's answer, which was the correct answer, was that the school could not discuss a student's situation by telephone and that any concerns should be brought to the Dean of Student Affairs in person. I have a note about both calls on my desk. I read them this morning before you walked in."

Danica's eyes fill. She does not cry, but her hands curl in her lap.

> "I want you to know what the school has done and not done, so there are no surprises. The school has not told your parents anything. The school has not confirmed that you are enrolled, that you were here before the break, or that you are expected back. We have said nothing. We will continue to say nothing until you and the DPJ tell me otherwise. Do you understand?"
>
> "Yes."
>
> "Are you alright to keep going?"
>
> "Yes."

### Beat 4: The Conditions, Plainly

Dr. Whitfield then walks Danica through what BCS *does* need, in the order it matters:

1. **An address for the school's records.** Not necessarily the address where Danica is sleeping — Dr. Whitfield understands the distinction. A mailing address that BCS can use for transcripts, report cards, and any official correspondence. Danica can use a post office box, the DPJ worker's office, or a lawyer's address. They will sort it out together; it does not have to be today.
2. **A contact adult on file** for emergencies during the school day. Again, this does not have to be a guardian, and it does not have to be her parents. The DPJ worker can serve as the contact while the file is open. If Danica eventually has a lawyer for the emancipation petition, that works too.
3. **Coordination with the DPJ.** Dr. Whitfield will, with Danica's permission, speak to the DPJ worker directly — not to disclose anything Danica hasn't already disclosed, but so that the school and the youth-protection file are not working at cross-purposes. Danica nods.
4. **A counsellor referral.** Dr. Whitfield is referring Danica to **Ms. Renée Lapointe**, one of the BCS school counsellors. Dr. Whitfield names her deliberately and writes the name down on a slip of paper for Danica to take with her, so that when Ms. Lapointe calls later in the week — Dr. Whitfield says probably **Wednesday** — Danica will recognize the name and not be ambushed by an unfamiliar voice. *"Renée is good. She's bilingual; you can speak French or English with her, whichever you want. She's not going to try to fix anything. She's there so you have one more person in this building who knows what's going on and whose office you can walk into."* Danica can choose to take the referral or not, but Dr. Whitfield is strongly recommending it. Danica takes the slip of paper.
5. **The uniform and materials.** Dr. Whitfield asks, almost gently: *"You don't have your uniform with you, do you?"* Danica shakes her head. Dr. Whitfield nods. *"We'll go to the bookstore from here. The school has an emergency-replacement protocol — it covers students in exactly this kind of situation. You won't pay full retail today; we'll work out the rest later."*

Danica almost laughs — the relief of it. *Emergency-replacement protocol.* The phrase is so bureaucratic, and so kind.

### Beat 5: One More Question

Before they leave the office, Dr. Whitfield asks one more question. She asks it carefully.

> "The friend who is an adult, the one you came in with. Is he the person you're staying with?"

Danica hesitates. Then:

> "Yes."

Dr. Whitfield nods. She does not ask his name. She does not ask the address. She makes a note on her own pad — not the file — and looks back up.

> "Okay. I'm not going to ask anything else about him today. The DPJ will. When they do, tell them the truth. Don't protect him by lying about who he is. The truth is your strongest position, in the school and in court. Yes?"
>
> "Yes."

### Beat 6: Back Through the Front Office

Danica walks back out into the front office. Orin is in the same chair by the window where she left him. He stands up the moment the door opens. He is looking for the answer in her face before she speaks.

She gives him a small nod and a smaller smile. He exhales.

Dr. Whitfield comes out behind her, professional and warm.

> "Mme Beaudoin, would you call down to the bookstore and let Mr. Côté know we're coming? Emergency-replacement, full kit. Uniform, gym, agenda, the calculator if he has one in stock."

Mme Beaudoin picks up the phone again. Her face is doing the same small thing it did the first time, only this time the third stage is something like satisfaction.

Dr. Whitfield turns to Orin.

> "You came in with her. Good. Come with us to the bookstore. She'll need a hand carrying things."

Orin says, simply:

> "Thank you."

### Beat 7: The Bookstore

The campus bookstore is in the basement of the next building over. **Mr. Daniel Côté** — early forties, glasses, sleeves rolled — is already pulling sizes when they walk in. He greets Danica by name, doesn't comment on her clothes, doesn't ask anything.

The next twenty minutes are practical:

- **Uniform.** Skirt, blouse, blazer with crest, tie, school sweater. Two of each shirt; Danica protests once and Dr. Whitfield says "Two." Tights — three pairs.
- **Gym kit.** Shorts, t-shirt, school sweatshirt, runners (Mr. Côté measures her foot).
- **Agenda.** The official BCS one. Mr. Côté writes the new term's dates on the inside front cover.
- **A basic graphing calculator.** They have one left in the case. Mr. Côté puts it in the pile.
- **A folder of forms** — the winter-term photo consent, the emergency-contact form, the transcript-address form. Dr. Whitfield says: *"Fill these in this week. Bring them back Friday. Leave the parent-signature lines blank for now — we'll talk about who signs them after the DPJ visit."*

At the counter, Mr. Côté quietly rings everything through at the emergency-replacement rate. The total appears on the little screen.

Danica looks at it and the colour leaves her face. She has nothing. No purse, no wallet, no debit card, no cash — all of that is in her bedroom at her parents' house, with everything else she didn't have time to take. She has known this since Friday night and she has been pushing it to the back of her mind for two weeks. Now it is on a small grey screen in front of her.

She opens her mouth. Nothing comes out.

Orin, beside her, already has his credit card in his hand. He doesn't make a thing of it. He just hands the card to Mr. Côté.

> "I've got it."

Danica turns to him and starts to say something — *I'll pay you back* — and he shakes his head, once, gently, before she can finish the sentence.

> "Danica. No. There is nothing to pay back. Let me do this. Please."

He says it quietly, but he says it as a closed door, not an open one. Mr. Côté taps the card. The transaction goes through without ceremony. He hands Orin the receipt and tucks a second copy into the bag.

Orin carries the larger of the two bags. Danica carries her uniform on its hanger, draped over her arm like something precious.

### Beat 8: Out the Office Door

Dr. Whitfield walks them as far as the main office door. She says, to Danica:

> "You can take the rest of today if you need it. Come back tomorrow in uniform. Your counsellor will reach out by Wednesday. The DPJ worker has my direct line."

Danica hesitates, then:

> "I think — I'd rather stay. For my classes. If I leave now I'll just sit at home all day thinking about this. Could I — change into the uniform now? And go say hello to Geneviève and Chloe first? Just for a minute. They've been worried."

Dr. Whitfield does not pause.

> "Of course. Both. There's a changing room down the hall to your left. Geneviève and Chloe are in homeroom — I'll let the teacher know you're coming in for a minute. After that, go to your scheduled classes. I'll have your schedule for the term printed and waiting at the front desk at the end of the day."

To Orin, just before he steps away — quietly, so Mme Beaudoin doesn't hear:

> "You walked her in. That was the right thing. Keep doing the right things."

Orin nods, once.

### Beat 8b: The Classroom

Danica changes in the small bathroom off the front office — skirt, blouse, tie, blazer, tights. The boots she walked off her parents' porch in are now the wrong shoes for the uniform, but they will do. She comes out and Orin almost doesn't recognize her for a second; she looks like a BCS student again. She looks like herself again.

He waits by the office. She goes alone to her homeroom.

She knocks once on the door and opens it. The teacher — already alerted by Mme Beaudoin — nods to her without making it a thing. The class is mid-period. Heads turn.

**Geneviève** sees her first. Her face does the same small thing Mme Beaudoin's did — recognition, then concern, then relief — except Geneviève does not bother to smooth it over. She is up out of her chair before Danica has crossed the threshold. **Chloe** is half a second behind her.

Danica meets them at the front of the classroom and hugs them both at once — one arm around each, the way she has hugged them since their first year. Geneviève holds on tighter than Chloe. Chloe is the one who cries — even though they have been on the phone every other day for two weeks, even though she heard Danica's voice on Friday night and again on the Sunday and again on New Year's Eve, *seeing her* is different. The phone doesn't have shoulders.

They don't say much. The classroom is watching. Danica says, quietly, into the side of Geneviève's head:

> "I'm okay. I came back. I'll tell you the rest at lunch — the police, the DPJ, the meeting just now."
>
> Geneviève, fierce, low: *"Tabarnak,* Danica."
>
> Chloe, wet, in English: "You're in *uniform.*"

Danica almost laughs.

> "I know."

She steps back. Squeezes each of their hands once. She is staying. They will see her at lunch.

She turns to the homeroom teacher and says *"Merci, madame,"* and goes to her own desk.

### Beat 8c: Saying Goodbye to Orin

Before going to her classes, Danica walks back down the hall in uniform. Orin is standing exactly where she left him by the front office. He does not ask how it went. He sees her face and he knows.

> "I'm staying. For the rest of the day."
>
> "Good."

She tells him she'll text him when she's ready to be picked up after last period. He says he'll be there.

She hands him the bag of civilian clothes — he'll take it back to the farm. He keeps the bookstore bag with anything not needed today. She keeps the uniform she is wearing and the agenda.

> "Are you going to McGill?"
>
> "Yes. First day of term — I have organic chem at one. I'll come back for you at the end of the day."
>
> "Okay."

He goes out the main office door alone. Danica watches him through the glass for a moment, then turns and walks to her first class of the winter term in her brand-new uniform.

## WHAT THIS SCENE ESTABLISHES

1. BCS — through Mme Beaudoin and Dr. Whitfield specifically — chooses to be decent. The institution itself isn't magical; two specific people are.
2. **Danica's parents called the school over the break.** Her mother called twice, spoke to Mme Beaudoin both times, and the school correctly said nothing. Danica now knows her parents are actively looking for her through institutional channels. This is the first external sign that her parents have not simply gone quiet.
3. Danica keeps her day-student status. The conditions (mailing address, contact adult, DPJ coordination, counsellor, forms) are real but workable.
4. The **emergency-replacement protocol** exists at BCS. Danica is not the first student in this position. That detail matters: she is not anomalous.
5. The school counsellor is named in this scene: **Ms. Renée Lapointe**, who will call Danica probably on Wednesday. Dr. Whitfield writes the name on a slip of paper and gives it to her, so Ms. Lapointe will not be an unfamiliar voice on the phone.
6. Orin enters the BCS office for the first time. He waits in the front office for the meeting and then goes onto campus for the bookstore. He does not lie about his role; he also does not volunteer it. Dr. Whitfield, in private, acknowledges what he did and gently warns him to keep doing the right things.
7. **Orin pays for everything at the bookstore** with his credit card. Danica has nothing — no purse, no wallet, no card, no cash; all of that is still in her bedroom at her parents' house. The moment is small and unceremonious. Orin is wealthy (Key Corporation family) and the cost is genuinely nothing to him; when Danica starts to say *I'll pay you back*, he closes the door on it gently and firmly: *"There is nothing to pay back. Let me do this every time I can."* This is the first time he has openly used his financial position on her behalf, and it is also the first time he has asked her to let him.
8. **Danica reconnects with Geneviève and Chloe in person** in their homeroom, in her new uniform, before she goes to first period. She has been on the phone with them every other day for two weeks — first from Orin's iPhone on Friday night (per *Danica Arrives at the Farm*) and many times since — so they have not been out of contact. But seeing her is different. The hug is the scene's emotional release — short, public, witnessed by the homeroom.
9. **Danica chooses to stay at BCS for the rest of the day** rather than go home with Orin. She tells Geneviève and Chloe she will tell them everything at lunch. The day will end with Orin picking her up after his McGill class — *that* part of the day is its own scene (see closing of Beat 8c).
10. The Friday DPJ home visit is now also on Dr. Whitfield's radar. The school and the youth-protection file will, with Danica's permission, talk to each other.
