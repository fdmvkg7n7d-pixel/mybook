# SCENE OUTLINE: ORIN LORIN JUNIOR & DANICA — BAGELS & BARN TOUR AT FERME CLÉ

**Characters:** Orin Lorin Junior (age 19), Danica (age 16), Sabrina Bryna Key and William (off-scene telephone presence in Vaughan), Ferme Clé farm workers.
**Setting:** Ferme Clé — the grand horse farm located between Montreal and Sherbrooke (kitchen and working barn), St. Viateur Bagels in Montreal, and Vaughan, Ontario (telephone link).
**Timeframe:** Saturday morning through Saturday afternoon on the weekend before Christmas Day, immediately following the 7:00 AM police departure.
**Theme:** Grounding in local culinary traditions; navigating corporate aviation wealth and family obligations; integrating into a working rural community; the natural, mature progression of shared living space.

---

## THE CONTEXT

Following a tense 7:00 AM police inquiry at Ferme Clé—where Officers Bouchard, Tremblay, and Roy conducted separate interviews and verified the pristine separation of the bedrooms before departing with Officer Roy leaving Danica her business card—the peaceful winter quiet returns to the horse farm mansion.

Sixteen-year-old Danica has officially made the brave decision to stay with nineteen-year-old Orin Lorin Junior at Ferme Clé rather than returning to her parents' toxic home in Sherbrooke. However, because she left immediately without packing anything, Danica lacks a passport to leave Canada. 

Meanwhile, back in Vaughan, Ontario, Sabrina Bryna and William are preparing for their family Christmas trip to Orlando, Florida. They are scheduled to fly to Montreal in the **Key Corporation plane** to pick Orin Lorin Junior up before continuing south. Orin must navigate this high-stakes family travel plan while providing comfort and long-term grounding for Danica at Ferme Clé.

---

## THE OUTLINE

### Part One: St. Viateur Bagels & The Breakfast Question (Saturday Morning)

- Following the departure of the police, Orin Lorin Junior turns to Danica in the quiet kitchen and gently asks her what she wants to eat for their breakfast.
- Danica smiles, her post-investigation relief palpable, and tells him she wants bagels **with cream cheese**.
- Seeking to provide the ultimate comforting regional tradition, Orin leaves Ferme Clé and drives out to **St. Viateur Bagels** in Montreal to pick up fresh, wood-fired hot bagels and tubs of rich cream cheese.
- He returns to the farm, setting up a warm, informal breakfast table by the massive kitchen windows overlooking the snowy paddocks. They share a deeply comforting morning meal, the hot bagels and cream cheese cutting through the exhaustion of the early morning police visit.

### Part Two: The Saturday Call to Vaughan & The Key Corp Plane

- Over their bagel breakfast, Orin addresses his impending holiday travel obligations. Sabrina Bryna and William are still in Vaughan, Ontario, packing for Florida and preparing to fly over to Montreal in the **Key Corporation plane** to pick him up.
- Facing the prospect of leaving Danica behind without a passport, Orin Lorin Junior decides to stay with Danica. She decides to stay with Orin Lorin Junior at Ferme Clé.
- Orin places the Saturday phone call to Sabrina Bryna and William in Vaughan to inform them he will be staying behind in Quebec. To protect Danica's privacy and avoid a painful explanation of her parents' prejudice, he makes an excuse.
- **His excuse:** He tells them that a close friend needs him because his friend's parents died. 
- Because Orin himself was orphaned at sixteen in a devastating house fire, Sabrina Bryna and William immediately recognize the sacred, non-negotiable weight of this duty. They offer their full blessing for him to remain behind, officially cancelling the Montreal stop for the Key Corporation plane and allowing the corporate jet to fly directly from Vaughan to Orlando.

### Part Three: The Barn Tour & Meeting the Workers (Saturday Afternoon)

- With the Key Corporation plane cancelled and their holiday stay officially secured, Orin takes Danica out into the crisp winter air that afternoon.
- **He gives Danica a comprehensive tour of the working barn**, walking her through the pristine timber stalls and heated tack rooms of Ferme Clé.
- Orin introduces Danica to the dedicated farm workers who manage the magnificent horse property on behalf of Sabrina Bryna and William.
- The workers greet Danica with genuine, respectful warmth, treating her not as a temporary guest, but as an established figure within the farm's permanent community.

### Part Four: The Progression of Home

- As they conclude the barn tour and walk back toward the brilliantly lit stone mansion in the winter twilight, Orin and Danica reflect on their future.
- While Danica begins her stay in the grand guest suite across the hall where she slept the night before, **in time, she will move her stuff into Orin Lorin Junior's bedroom**.
- This natural, mature progression transforms Ferme Clé from an investment property bought ten years ago into their true, unshakeable permanent home.

---

## WHY THIS SCENE MATTERS

- **The St. Viateur Bagels breakfast grounds the setting in iconic Montreal culture.** Orin asking Danica what she wants for breakfast, and driving out to St. Viateur Bagels for bagels with cream cheese, roots their morning routine in authentic, world-renowned regional lore.
- **The Key Corporation plane elevates the corporate wealth lore.** Establishing that Sabrina Bryna and William are still in Vaughan and are scheduled to fly over to Montreal in the private Key Corporation plane to pick him up perfectly illustrates the immense scale of the family's resources.
- **The Saturday phone call provides brilliant psychological resonance.** Orin cancelling his pickup in the Key Corporation plane with the excuse that a friend needs him because his friend's parents died perfectly echoes Orin's own tragic backstory, ensuring his adoptive parents completely understand and support his decision to stay.
- **The barn tour integrates Danica into the rural community.** Orin giving Danica a tour of the barn to meet the workers establishes her permanent presence among the staff of Ferme Clé.
- **The bedroom transition seals their long-term future.** Establishing that in time she will move her stuff into Orin Lorin Junior's bedroom provides the perfect, mature long-term payoff to their story.
