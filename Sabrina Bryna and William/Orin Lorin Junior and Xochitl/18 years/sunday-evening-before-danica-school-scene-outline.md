# SCENE OUTLINE: SUNDAY EVENING BEFORE DANICA'S SCHOOL

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Danica, Orin Lorin Junior
- **Offstage / referenced characters:** Danica's school administration, Danica's parents, Sabrina Bryna and William (who do not know about Danica)
- **Location:** Ferme Clé
- **When:** Sunday evening, the night before Danica returns to school after the Christmas break
- **Status:** New scene outline
- **Tone:** Anxious, tender, uncertain, protective

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene shows Danica on the Sunday evening before school, very emotional about what the school will do. She has left home and is now living at Ferme Clé. She does not know whether the school will allow her to continue, whether they will contact her parents, or what consequences her situation will bring. Orin is there with her.

**Key context:** Danica is a **day student** at BCS — she does not board there. The school expects her to be living with her parents or a guardian. Now that she has left home and is living secretly at Ferme Clé, she has no official residence that matches what the school has on file. Sabrina Bryna and William own Ferme Clé, but they do not know about Danica. Orin has been keeping her presence at the farm a secret from them. This means Danica has no adult guardians who can advocate for her at the school, and Orin cannot ask Sabrina Bryna and William for help without revealing Danica.

---

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: Sunday Evening at Ferme Clé
- The weekend is ending.
- The Christmas break is over and school starts tomorrow.
- Danica is at Ferme Clé, where she has been staying since leaving home.
- The quiet of the farm makes the coming school day feel even larger.

### Beat 2: Danica's Emotion
- Danica is very emotional.
- She does not know what her school will do.
- Her situation — having left home, now living secretly at Ferme Clé — may mean BCS contacts her parents, may mean she is not allowed to continue, may mean something she cannot predict.
- She cannot ask Sabrina Bryna and William for help because they do not know she is there.
- Orin cannot involve them without exposing the secret.
- The uncertainty is worse than any specific fear.
- She may cry, or go quiet, or pace, or cling to Orin.

### Beat 3: Orin's Presence
- Orin is there with her.
- He cannot fix what the school will decide, but he can hold the evening with her.
- He may tell her that whatever happens, she will not face it alone.
- His steadiness contrasts with her distress without dismissing it.

### Beat 4: The Night Before
- The scene does not resolve the school question — it cannot, because morning has not come yet.
- The scene closes with Danica and Orin in the uncertainty of the night before.
- She has to go to school tomorrow and find out what happens.
- All she has tonight is the evening, the farm, and Orin.

---

## WHAT THIS SCENE ESTABLISHES

1. Danica's situation at school is a real source of fear and instability.
2. Leaving home has consequences she cannot fully control.
3. Orin is her anchor in the uncertainty.
4. The scene sits in the not-knowing rather than resolving it.
5. Sabrina Bryna and William do not know about Danica, so there is no adult guardian who can advocate for her at school.
