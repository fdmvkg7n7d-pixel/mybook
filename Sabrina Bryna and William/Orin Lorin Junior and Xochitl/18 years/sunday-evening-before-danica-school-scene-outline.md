# SCENE OUTLINE: SUNDAY EVENING BEFORE DANICA'S MONDAY MEETING AT BCS

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Danica (Xochitl), Orin Lorin Junior
- **Offstage / referenced characters:** **Mme Solange Beaudoin** (BCS front-office secretary), **Dr. Margaret "Maggie" Whitfield** (BCS Dean of Student Affairs), BCS school counsellor (to be assigned at the Monday meeting), BCS bookstore staff, Danica's parents, the SQ officers (Bouchard, Tremblay, Roy), the DPJ worker, Sabrina Bryna and William (returning to Vaughan, Ontario today or yesterday — the younger children's winter term starts tomorrow morning — and **deliberately not in the loop**)
- **Location:** Ferme Clé, Quebec — the farm where Orin lives and works alone. **Sabrina Bryna and William own the property as an investment; they do not live there.** They were in Orlando for Christmas with the younger children and are now back (or returning today) to Vaughan, Ontario, because the younger children's winter term starts in the morning.
- **When:** **Sunday evening** at the end of the two-week Christmas break — roughly **thirteen days after** Danica left home, twelve days after the police visit, and the night before her **Monday meeting at the Bishop's College School (BCS) office** in Sherbrooke
- **Status:** Revised — woven with *Danica Leaves Home*, *Danica Arrives at the Farm*, and *Police Visit at the Farm*; corrected so that (a) Danica has no uniform, (b) her plan is a **Monday office meeting**, not attending classes, and (c) Orin is not planning to tell Sabrina Bryna and William about her
- **Tone:** Anxious, tender, uncertain, protective — a quiet night before a hard conversation, not a normal first day back

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

For nearly two weeks Ferme Clé has been a sealed world: Orin, Danica, the horses, the rabbits, the snow. The police came and went; the DPJ has only been a voice on the phone; Sabrina, Bryna, and William have been in Orlando the entire break with the younger children, not knowing Danica exists at the farm. Tonight or yesterday they have flown back to Vaughan, Ontario, so the younger children (Taylor Raven, Sun-Young "Liz", and the twins) can be at school in the morning. Classes also resume across BCS in the morning. Danica is not going to slip into a classroom — she is going to walk into the **BCS office** in civilian clothes and tell administration in person what has happened.

This scene is the last evening before that conversation.

---

## TIMELINE TO THIS POINT

- **Friday night, start of break:** Danica's parents make racist remarks in Japanese, not knowing Orin speaks it. Orin answers in Japanese. Danica leaves with him with **only what she is wearing** — no overnight bag, no uniform, no schoolbooks. *(Danica Leaves Home)*
- **Friday night:** Forty-minute drive to Ferme Clé. Tea, oyakodon, miso soup. Danica kisses Orin; he gently stops it. She sleeps alone in the spare bedroom. *(Danica Arrives at the Farm)*
- **Saturday morning:** Three Sûreté du Québec officers arrive. Officer Roy explains Quebec law honestly — she's a minor until 18, the default is that police return runaways, but she has three real options: go home, accept DPJ intervention, or petition the Superior Court for **simple emancipation** under *art. 168 C.c.Q.* Danica picks option two. The officers leave a legal aid pamphlet and refer the file to the DPJ. *(Police Visit at the Farm)*
- **Saturday morning, after the police leave:** Saint-Viateur bagels.
- **The week between Christmas and New Year's:** The DPJ assigns a worker to the file and phones Orin's mobile during the break. The worker confirms Danica is safe at the farm pending an in-person home visit, which is now **scheduled for Friday**. Danica's parents have been told by the SQ that she is safe but not where she is.
- **Sunday evening, end of the break:** *This scene.*

## KEY CONTEXT (REVISED)

### Danica has no uniform

She left her parents' house on Friday night with only what she was wearing — the outfit from dinner, a coat, no bag. **Everything she owns school-related — uniform, schoolbooks, gym kit, agenda, student ID — is still at her parents' house.** She has not been back. She knows BCS has its own **bookstore** on campus that sells the uniform, but she does not know what tomorrow's conversation will allow her to do about it. Tonight she has no uniform to lay out. She has only the outfit she will wear into the office.

### She is going to the office, not to class

Tomorrow is the first morning of the winter term. Other students will arrive in uniform with their schoolbags, ready to go to homeroom. Danica is not. She is going to:

- Enter through the **main office door**, not the student entrance.
- Speak to the **front-office secretary** first, by name if she remembers it. Ask to see the **Dean of Student Affairs**, not a homeroom teacher or a counsellor at the desk. The Dean is the person who can actually decide things.
- Tell the Dean she has left her parents' home, that she is safe, that she is staying with a friend who is an adult, that the Sûreté du Québec has been to where she is staying, and that there is an open DPJ file.
- Ask what BCS needs from her in order for her to continue as a day student.
- **Not** give the address of the farm. That is the DPJ's information, not the school's.
- **Not** give Orin's name unless she has to.

She has rehearsed parts of this in her head all week. She is choosing to disclose. She is not trying to slip back in unnoticed.

### Orin is **not** planning to tell Sabrina Bryna and William

He has thought about it. He has decided against it. His reasoning, worked out over the last two weeks:

- They are not at the farm. They are in Vaughan, Ontario — they returned from Orlando this weekend so the younger children could start the winter term tomorrow. The farm is a thirteen-hour drive or a chartered Key Corporation flight away from where they actually live. They are not going to walk in the door.
- The farm is their **investment** property, not their residence. He is the one who lives and works there. Whatever happens day-to-day at Ferme Clé is, in practice, his to manage.
- The DPJ file is **Danica's**, not the farm's. The worker on the phone has not asked to speak to the owners.
- If he tells them, he loses control of the situation in a way that does not help Danica.
- He is not lying to them. He is not hiding the farm from them. He is not asking them for anything. He is simply not volunteering information about a minor who is in the middle of an open DPJ file and a possible emancipation petition.

This is a *deliberate* choice. He does not say it to Danica in so many words. He says enough that she understands.

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: The Last Night of the Bubble

The break is ending. The barn is quiet — horses already in for the night, rabbit hutches still. Snow on the fields outside the kitchen window.

Almost two weeks of small evidence that Danica has been here: a second mug in the drying rack, a second pair of slippers by the door, a notepad of her own handwriting on the kitchen table where she has been rehearsing what she will say in the morning. An open library book Orin brought home for her.

The legal aid pamphlet Officer Roy left is on the windowsill. Danica has not opened it. She has not thrown it out.

Danica is in clothes Orin found for her — soft, oversized, his. She has no uniform. She has no real wardrobe at all. Tomorrow she will go into the BCS office in the most presentable civilian outfit she has been able to assemble from this farm in the middle of nowhere.

### Beat 2: The Plan, Said Out Loud

Danica walks Orin through the plan, partly to him and partly to herself. Saying it out loud is part of how she has been rehearsing all week.

- Go in through the **main office door**, not the student entrance.
- Speak to the **secretary** first. Ask, politely and clearly, to see the **Dean of Student Affairs**. Not her homeroom teacher. Not a guidance counsellor at the desk. The Dean is the person who can actually decide things.
- When the Dean is available, tell the Dean: *I have left my parents' home. I am safe. The Sûreté du Québec has been to where I am staying and they know I'm safe. There is an open file with the DPJ. I want to know what BCS needs from me to continue as a day student.*
- Do not give the address of the farm.
- Do not give Orin's name unless she has to.
- If the Dean wants to call her parents in front of her, say no — and say why.
- If the Dean refuses to keep her, ask what the formal process is, in writing, and leave.

She says this clearly, almost flatly. Then she says, much smaller:

> "What if they call my mother before I even sit down?"

Orin doesn't have an answer for that. He says so.

### Beat 3: The Things She Doesn't Have

She lists what she doesn't have, in the same voice. Not self-pitying. Just factual:

- No uniform.
- No schoolbooks.
- No gym kit.
- No agenda.
- No graphing calculator.
- No student ID card.
- No parental signature on the consent form for the winter term photo, which was due before the break.

She is going to walk into the BCS office tomorrow as a sixteen-year-old in borrowed civilian clothes, with **a legal aid pamphlet and her own handwriting on a notepad** as the only paperwork she has.

She mentions, quietly, that the school has a **bookstore** on campus — it sells the uniform, gym kit, textbooks. If the Dean is willing to keep her, she will need to buy everything **tomorrow morning, right after the meeting** — same trip, not a separate visit later. She does not know whether they will let her, whether it requires a parent on file, whether she has the money on her, whether they take debit.

Orin listens. He does not promise her the bookstore will work. He does tell her, simply:

> "If the meeting goes the right way, we walk straight from the office to the bookstore. Same morning. You don't come home in civilian clothes a second time. I'll be there with you."

That sits with her.

She says, almost laughing, almost crying:

> "I look like a runaway."
>
> Orin says, evenly: "You *are* a runaway. The Sûreté du Québec used that exact word. It's not an insult — it's a category in a form. Walk in like you know the category and you've decided what you're doing inside it."

### Beat 4: What Orin Will and Will Not Do

He tells her, in order:

- He will drive her in tomorrow.
- He will **park nearby and walk in with her** — through the main office door, for moral support. He won't sit in the meeting itself unless the Dean asks him to; he'll wait in the front office while she speaks with Mme Beaudoin and then with Dr. Whitfield. But she will not walk through that door alone.
- His phone will be on. If she calls or texts at any point, he comes.
- If the Dean is willing to keep her and the meeting moves to the bookstore, he stays with her for that part too — **the same morning, straight from the office**. She does not have to walk into the bookstore alone with no uniform, and she does not have to come back to the farm and start over in the afternoon.
- About the DPJ — Friday. He has the date. If she's at school that day, he handles the home visit alone. He will tell the worker the truth — about Friday night, about the police visit, about Danica sleeping alone in the spare bedroom, all of it.

He does **not** say he is going to call Sabrina, Bryna, and William. He does not raise their names. When Danica, gently, almost in passing, asks, *"Are you going to tell your aunt and uncle?"* — he answers honestly but briefly:

> "Not right now. They're back in Vaughan — they flew home from Orlando this weekend because the kids start school in the morning. The farm is an investment property to them; the day-to-day is mine. If the DPJ wants to talk to them, that's the DPJ's call, not mine. I'm not lying to them. I'm just not putting you on their plate while you're in the middle of this."

He doesn't ask for her opinion on it. He doesn't ask for permission. He's telling her how he has decided to carry it.

Danica nods, once, and doesn't push.

### Beat 5: The Pamphlet

At some point in the evening, Danica picks up the pamphlet from the windowsill for the first time. She doesn't read it carefully — she turns it over in her hands. The phrase **émancipation simple** is on the front. Inside, a number for legal aid in the Estrie region.

> "If BCS refuses to keep me," she says, "I'll call this number tomorrow."

Orin nods.

> "If BCS keeps you, you can still call it."

She doesn't answer. But she folds the pamphlet and slides it into the small bag she is taking to the office — the only "schoolbag" she has, because her actual schoolbag is at her parents' house.

### Beat 6: The Outfit, Not the Uniform

There is no uniform to lay out. Instead, she lays out the most presentable civilian outfit she can assemble: a plain sweater, dark trousers, the only pair of boots she has (the ones she walked off her parents' porch thirteen days ago). No skirt. No blouse. No tights. No BCS crest anywhere.

The familiarity of the ritual — laying clothes out the night before school — is the only part of tomorrow that resembles a normal first day back. She does it slowly. She doesn't say anything while she does it. Orin lets her have the silence.

### Beat 7: The Night Before

The scene does not resolve the school question. It cannot — morning has not come.

It closes quietly. Danica goes to the spare bedroom alone, as she has every night for thirteen nights. Orin stays in the kitchen for a while longer. He does **not** pick up his phone to look at his aunt and uncle's contact. He has already decided. He sits at the kitchen table with the duplicate pamphlet Officer Roy left him, finishes a cup of cold tea, and turns the light off.

Last image: the kitchen dark, the snow outside, the civilian outfit laid out two rooms away, the pamphlet creased once down the middle, in the small bag that is going to the BCS office in the morning.

## WHAT THIS SCENE ESTABLISHES

1. The two-week Christmas break has been a sealed bubble. Monday's office meeting ends the bubble — by Danica's choice, not by accident.
2. Danica has **no uniform and no school materials**; she left her parents' house with only what she was wearing, and she has not been back. She knows the BCS bookstore exists; whether she will be allowed to use it tomorrow morning is one of the things she does not yet know.
3. Her plan for Monday morning is **disclosure, not attendance** — she is walking into the BCS office in civilian clothes, speaking to the **secretary** first, and asking to see the **Dean of Student Affairs**.
4. If the meeting goes the right way, **the bookstore happens the same morning, straight from the office** — not a separate afternoon trip. She does not go home in civilian clothes a second time.
5. Orin has made a deliberate decision **not** to involve Sabrina, Bryna, and William while Danica's situation is mid-process. He is honest with Danica about that decision but does not put it up for discussion.
6. Orin's agreement to come **onto campus** for the bookstore part — if the meeting gets that far — is the first time he has agreed to step out of the two-blocks-away role. Small, but it matters.
7. The legal aid pamphlet has moved from the windowsill into the small bag Danica is taking to the office — a quiet act of claiming the emancipation option as hers.
8. The **DPJ home visit is pinned to Friday** — it is no longer ambient "sometime this week" pressure. Orin will handle that visit himself if Danica is at school.
