# SCENE OUTLINE: ORIN LORIN JUNIOR CALLS SABRINA BRYNA, WILLIAM, AND CARLOTA DELICIA ABOUT NOT COMING TO ORLANDO FOR CHRISTMAS

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Orin Lorin Junior, Sabrina Bryna, William, Carlota Delicia
- **Offstage / referenced characters:** **Danica** (secretly Orin's girlfriend), **Keith**, and **Madeleine Jaycee** (Carlota and Keith's six-month-old baby)
- **Key context:** Carlota Delicia and Keith work for Walt Disney World. They live in Orlando.
- **Locations:** Orin's location at Ferme Clé [or nearby — TBD]; Sabrina Bryna and William's Orlando home; Carlota Delicia and Keith's Orlando home
- **When:** In the lead-up to Christmas, when **Orin is nineteen (19)**
- **Status:** New scene outline
- **Tone:** Sad, loving, responsible, secretive, mature. The emotional center is Orin protecting Danica and their private situation while finding a believable reason to stay behind.

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene shows Orin Lorin Junior making a painful but morally clear decision: he will **not** come to Orlando for Christmas because he is staying behind with **Danica**, who has left home and cannot travel. But because his family does **not** know he is dating her, he tells them a lie: that **a friend** has lost a parent suddenly and needs him for the holidays.

The scene should do six things:

1. **Show Orin's maturity and loyalty**.
2. **Show that he is protecting Danica's privacy and the secrecy of the relationship.**
3. **Let Sabrina Bryna and William respond as loving parents** to the story he gives them.
4. **Give Carlota Delicia her own separate call**, while making clear that Orin lies to her too.
5. **Show that Christmas in this branch is about people, not attendance.**
6. **Make Orin's absence meaningful rather than logistical.**

---

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

## BEAT 1 — THE REAL SITUATION AND THE COVER STORY

- Orin is nineteen.
- He is secretly dating **Danica**, but his family does not know.
- Danica has left home and is now with him, and she cannot leave Canada for Orlando.
- Orin knows fairly quickly what he wants to do: **he is not going to leave her alone for Christmas.**
- But he also knows he cannot tell his family the truth yet.
- So he constructs a cover explanation: **a friend has lost a parent suddenly and needs him for the holidays.**
- The weight of the moment is twofold:
  - he is making the right choice about where to stay
  - he is doing it through a lie because the truth is not yet available to him

**Purpose of beat:** establish both the moral choice and the secrecy around it.

---

## BEAT 2 — ORIN CALLS SABRINA BRYNA AND WILLIAM

- Orin calls **Sabrina Bryna and William first**.
- This call should feel serious from the beginning.
- He tells them that **a friend's parent has died suddenly** and that he wants to stay with his friend for the holidays instead of coming to Orlando.
- The important thing is that Orin does not sound flaky or casual. He sounds steady, sad, and resolved.
- The lie works emotionally because Orin himself was orphaned young; he knows exactly what kind of grief-duty he is invoking.

### Emotional shape of the call
- Sabrina Bryna and William may feel immediate sadness that he will not be with them for Christmas.
- But neither of them responds selfishly.
- They hear the real issue in the story he gives them immediately: a grieving young person should not be left alone if love can stay.
- Sabrina Bryna's response should carry the emotional intelligence of someone who knows what family crisis does to time and language.
- William's response should be warm, practical, and affirming.

### Core family truth
- They make clear to Orin that **he is doing the right thing**.
- They may tell him that Christmas can move; grief cannot.
- Orin should come away from the call both comforted and slightly burdened, because their blessing is being given to a story that is not quite true.

**Purpose of beat:** let his parents bless the choice, while preserving the painful fact that Orin cannot tell them why he is really staying.

---

## BEAT 3 — ORIN CALLS CARLOTA DELICIA SEPARATELY

- After calling Sabrina Bryna and William, Orin calls **Carlota Delicia separately**.
- This matters because Carlota is not just family-background; she is someone Orin wants to tell directly.
- At this point, **Carlota is living with Keith**, and they have a **six-month-old daughter named Madeleine Jaycee**.
- The emotional texture here should be slightly different from the parent call.
- He may be more informal with Carlota, but he still does **not** tell her the truth.

### What the call does
- Orin repeats the same explanation: **a friend's parent has died suddenly**.
- He tells Carlota he is not coming to Orlando because he wants to stay with his friend through Christmas.
- Carlota understands the story as given.
- Because of her own history and emotional architecture, she is especially capable of understanding that there are moments when presence matters more than plans.
- Her position as a new mother may sharpen her response further: the call reaches her in a household now built around care, attachment, and showing up when someone is fragile.

### Possible household texture
- Keith may be nearby, or Madeleine Jaycee may be in Carlota's arms, asleep, fussing, or needing to be shifted on her shoulder during the call.
- This can make the scene feel more lived-in and deepen Carlota's adulthood without changing the emotional center.

### Possible sibling/cousin tone
- She may tell him not to worry about Orlando.
- She may tell him to stay exactly where he is needed.
- She may say something simple that makes the call land emotionally, not ceremonially.

**Purpose of beat:** give Orin and Carlota a direct relational beat while preserving the secrecy: even Carlota is being told the cover story.

---

## BEAT 4 — ORIN AFTER THE CALLS

- After the calls, Orin is more settled but not uncomplicatedly relieved.
- The family did not make him defend his choice.
- That matters.
- He now knows he can stay behind without carrying an extra layer of family guilt.
- But he is also carrying the quiet weight of having lied to people who would probably have loved him through the truth if he had been ready to give it.
- The emotional center shifts from dread to steadiness, with a residue of secrecy still in it.

**Purpose of beat:** close the scene on support, secrecy, and moral complexity.

---

## WHAT THIS SCENE ESTABLISHES

1. **Orin is loyal and emotionally mature.**
2. **He is willing to protect Danica and their private situation, even through a lie.**
3. **Sabrina Bryna and William know how to respond to grief without making it about themselves.**
4. **Carlota Delicia gets her own relational beat with Orin, but she does not know the truth yet.**
5. **The family's definition of Christmas is flexible when love is needed elsewhere.**
6. **Orin's absence from Orlando becomes a meaningful emotional choice, not just a scheduling change.**
