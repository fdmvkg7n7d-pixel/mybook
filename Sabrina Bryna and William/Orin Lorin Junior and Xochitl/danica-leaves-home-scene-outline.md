# Danica Leaves Home — Scene Outline

## Placement

**When:** Friday evening before Christmas Day.  
**Ages:** Danica (Xochitl) is 16, Orin Lorin Junior is 19.  
**Focus:** Danica inviting Orin to meet her parents, their racist remarks in Japanese, and her decision to leave home with him that night.

---

## Characters

- **Danica (Xochitl)** — 16 years old. Mixed heritage. Does not fully understand Japanese.
- **Orin Lorin Junior** — 19 years old. Black. Studied Japanese for four years in high school.
- **Danica’s Parents** — Racist toward Black people. Speak Japanese when making derogatory comments.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1: The Invitation

On Friday evening, Danica invites Orin Lorin Junior to her house to meet her parents. She is nervous but hopeful. She tells him that her parents are making a wonderful dinner and that she wants them to know him.

Orin agrees, though he senses her nervousness.

### Beat 2: At the Door

Danica meets Orin Lorin Junior at the front door before he can enter. She steps outside and closes the door behind her, keeping her voice low.

> “They’re being weird,” she whispers. “I don’t want you to go inside yet.”

Orin studies her face, sensing something is wrong.

### Beat 3: Racist Remarks

Danica’s parents open the door and begin speaking to each other in Japanese, assuming Orin cannot understand them. They make several racist remarks:

> 「黒人の男を家に入れるなんて、信じられないわ。」  
> *(I can’t believe we’re letting a Black man into the house.)*

> 「ダニカはなぜあんな人と付き合うのかしら。」  
> *(Why is Danica dating someone like that?)*

> 「黒人は信用できないわよ。」  
> *(You can’t trust Black people.)*

> 「うちの娘と結婚させる気なんてないでしょうね。」  
> *(They have no intention of letting him marry our daughter, right?)*

As her parents speak, Danica’s eyes fill with tears. She understands enough Japanese to know they are saying terrible, racist things about Orin. Her face crumples and she starts crying quietly on the doorstep, her shoulders shaking.

Orin Lorin Junior stands beside her, listening to every word. His expression remains calm, but his jaw tightens.

After they finish speaking, Orin replies to them in fluent Japanese:

> 「日本語が分かりますよ。」  
> *(I can understand Japanese.)*

Danica’s parents freeze, their faces turning pale with shock.

### Beat 4: Danica Returns

Danica returns to the living room and notices the tension in the air. She looks at Orin, who meets her eyes but says nothing.

She asks quietly in English:

> “What did they say?”

Orin looks at her for a moment, then answers calmly:

> “They don’t want me here. They said some very racist things about me.”

Danica freezes. She understands enough to know he is telling the truth.

### Beat 5: Danica’s Decision

Danica turns to her parents and speaks in English, her voice shaking with anger:

> “You’re disgusting.”

Her parents look shocked. They try to respond in Japanese, but Danica cuts them off.

She turns back to Orin.

> “Let’s go.”

Without another word, Danica walks to her room, packs a small bag, and returns. She takes Orin’s hand and they leave the house together.

### Beat 6: Leaving Home

As they walk down the driveway, Danica is visibly upset but resolute. Orin walks beside her in silence, still holding her hand.

She speaks quietly:

> “I’m not going back there.”

Orin nods.

> “You don’t have to.”

They continue walking into the night, away from her house.

---

## Emotional Notes

- This scene marks a major turning point in Danica’s life.
- Orin’s ability to understand Japanese becomes crucial.
- Danica’s decision to leave home is driven by her love for Orin and her rejection of her parents’ racism.
- The scene carries heavy emotional weight and sets up their future together.
