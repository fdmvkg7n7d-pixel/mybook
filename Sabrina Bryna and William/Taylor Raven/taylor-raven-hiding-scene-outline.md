# SCENE OUTLINE: THE GHOST IN THE HALLWAY

## OVERVIEW
- **Characters:** Taylor Raven Key (age 4-5), Carlota Delicia Key (age 12-13), Sabrina Bryna Key.
- **Location:** The hallway of the Vaughan mansion, north of Toronto.
- **Timing:** A few days after Carlota’s return from the hospital.

---

## THE SETTING
The Vaughan mansion is large and echoes. The sun is setting, casting long, distorted shadows across the hardwood floors. Taylor Raven is playing with a set of wooden blocks near the top of the grand staircase.

---

## SCENE BEATS

### 1. The Sound of Footsteps
Taylor Raven is stacking her blocks when she hears a sound she isn't used to. It's the sound of heavy, dragging footsteps coming from the guest wing. In this house, people usually move with a certain rhythm—her mother's uneven but familiar gait, William's brisk walk. These footsteps are different. They sound tired.

### 2. The Appearance
Carlota Delicia emerges from the shadows of the hallway. She is wearing an oversized sweatshirt, her hair lank, her eyes staring at nothing. To Taylor, she doesn't look like the "big sister" her mother cried about for years. She looks like a specter.

### 3. The Freeze
Taylor drops a block. It clatters loudly on the floor. 
Carlota stops. She looks at Taylor. There is no smile, no "hello," no sign language. Carlota just watches the small girl with a hollow, unblinking intensity. 

### 4. The Failed Communication
Taylor tries to be brave. She raises her small hands and signs: *HELLO. PLAY?*
Carlota doesn't move. She doesn't sign back. Her hands stay buried in her sweatshirt pockets. She looks at Taylor’s moving hands with what looks like disgust, or perhaps fear. 

Carlota speaks, her voice low and raspy: "Don't do that. Stop doing that."

### 5. The Retreat
Taylor doesn't understand the words, but she feels the coldness in the tone. The lack of signing—the language that means "family" and "safety" to Taylor—makes Carlota feel like an intruder. 

Taylor’s lower lip trembles. She scrambles backward, leaving her blocks behind. She doesn't just walk away; she crawls behind a large decorative urn in the corner of the hallway, pressing her back against the cold porcelain, trying to make herself invisible.

### 6. The Lingering Shadow
Carlota doesn't follow. She stands there for a long minute, looking at the abandoned blocks. Then, she turns and walks back into her room, closing the door with a soft, final *click*. 

Taylor stays behind the urn for a long time, even after the hallway is empty, waiting for her mother to come and find her.

---

## CORE THEMES
- **The Stranger in the House:** The reality that a rescued loved one can be a source of fear.
- **Lost Language as Lost Safety:** For a child raised in a signing household, a non-signing person feels "wrong" or dangerous.
- **The Shattered Family Dynamic:** How trauma ripples down to the youngest members who don't even understand what happened.

---

*This scene establishes the long-term tension between the two sisters.*
