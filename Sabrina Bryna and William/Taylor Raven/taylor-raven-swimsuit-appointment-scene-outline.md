# SCENE OUTLINE: THE SWIMSUIT APPOINTMENT

## OVERVIEW
- **Characters:** Taylor Raven Key (age 4), William (Dad/Doctor), Nurse (background).
- **Location:** Toronto Sick Children's Hospital (SickKids).
- **Timing:** Taylor’s annual check-up, roughly six months after Carlota's return.

---

## THE SETTING
The bustling corridors of SickKids. William is in a unique position—he is both a respected doctor here and a father trying to manage a headstrong four-year-old. He is wearing his lab coat over his shirt, trying to maintain a professional air while carrying a pink glittery backpack.

---

## SCENE BEATS

### 1. The Great Fashion Debate
In the exam room, the nurse hands William a standard-issue, flimsy hospital gown with little blue elephants on it. "For the check-up, Dr. Key," she says with a wink. 

William turns to Taylor. "Alright, Taylor. Time to swap your dress for the gown so we can check your heart and tummy."

Taylor, sitting on the crinkly paper of the exam table, crosses her arms. "No gown. It has holes in the back. I brought my own."

### 2. The Reveal
Taylor reaches into her backpack and pulls out a bright neon-pink one-piece swimsuit with a large ruffle across the chest and a picture of a sunglasses-wearing flamingo on it. 

William sighs, the tired sigh of a man who has already lost this battle. "Taylor, honey, we're at the hospital, not the splash pad in Vaughan."

### 3. The Logic of a Four-Year-Old
Taylor stands up on the exam table, holding the swimsuit like a shield. "The gown is for sick kids, Daddy. You work at SickKids. I am a *swimming* kid. If I wear the swimsuit, the germs will think I'm at the pool and they'll go away."

She adds, with absolute finality: "And the flamingo needs to see the doctor too."

### 4. The Compromise
A few minutes later, the door opens. The attending pediatrician (a colleague of William's) walks in to find Taylor Raven sitting proudly on the table, wearing the neon-pink flamingo swimsuit *over* her underwear, with her socks and sneakers still on. 

William is sitting in the corner, rubbing his temples. 

"Everything alright, William?" the colleague asks, stifling a laugh.

William looks up. "The flamingo is healthy, but we might need a specialist for the ruffles. Proceed with the exam."

### 5. The Professional "Swimming" Patient
Taylor is perfectly behaved for the rest of the appointment. She lets them listen to her heart through the flamingo's head and stands perfectly still for her height measurement, insisting that "swimmers have to be tall to jump off the diving board."

---

## CORE THEMES
- **The Doctor’s Daughter:** Taylor’s comfort and familiarity with her father's workplace.
- **Independence and Humor:** Taylor’s headstrong personality beginning to shine through.
- **A Moment of Levity:** A much-needed break from the heavy atmosphere of the mansion.

---

*This scene highlights the "normal" side of Taylor’s childhood, showing her as a spirited, imaginative child.*
