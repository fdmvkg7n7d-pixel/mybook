# SCENE OUTLINE: THE SECOND OPINION

## OVERVIEW
- **Characters:** Taylor Raven Key (age 4), William (Dad/Specialist), Dr. Aris (Pediatrician).
- **Location:** Exam Room, Toronto Sick Children's Hospital (SickKids).
- **Timing:** Immediately following the "Swimsuit Appointment."

---

## THE SETTING
The atmosphere in the exam room has shifted from lighthearted humor about neon-pink swimsuits to a sudden, heavy stillness. Dr. Aris, a seasoned pediatrician and a close friend of William’s, has gone quiet while examining Taylor’s eyes with an ophthalmoscope.

---

## SCENE BEATS

### 1. The Pause
Dr. Aris is checking Taylor's pupillary reflex. He moves the light back and forth across her eyes, then stops, leaning in closer. He switches the lens on his scope and looks again, his brow furrowed. 

William, leaning against the counter, notices the change in his colleague's posture immediately. The "dad" part of him stays still, but the "specialist" part of him feels a cold jolt of adrenaline.

### 2. The Request
Dr. Aris stands up and clicks off his light. He doesn't look at Taylor; he looks directly at William. 

"William," Dr. Aris says, his voice carefully neutral—the 'professional' voice they both use when they see something they don't like. "I’m seeing a bit of an unusual reflection in the left fundus. It’s likely just a fluke of the light, but... would you mind taking a look? For a second opinion?"

### 3. The Professional Mask
William’s heart hammers against his ribs, but his face remains calm for Taylor. "Of course," he says. 

He walks over to the exam table. Taylor is still wearing her flamingo swimsuit, her small legs dangling. She looks up at him, sensing the shift in the room. The silence of the two men, the way they aren't looking at her, and the bright light of the scope make her stomach twist. "Is the flamingo okay, Daddy?" she asks, her voice small and trembling.

"The flamingo is perfect, honey," William says, his hands steady as he takes the scope from Dr. Aris. "I just want to see what the flamingo sees."

### 4. The Flight
As William leans in with the bright light, the pressure of the moment breaks Taylor's nerve. She doesn't just pull away—she bolts. 

Before William can get the scope to her eye, Taylor scrambles off the table. Her sneakers squeak on the linoleum as she ducks under Dr. Aris’s arm and bursts through the exam room door. 

"Taylor! Wait!" William shouts, dropping the professional mask. 

### 5. The Chase through SickKids
Taylor sprints down the hallway of the pediatric wing, a flash of neon-pink flamingo and ruffles against the white hospital walls. She isn't just running from the light; she’s running from the "sick kid" feeling she tried so hard to avoid. 

William gives chase, his lab coat flapping behind him. He rounds the corner near the nurse's station, dodging a gurney. He finds her huddled behind a large colorful statue of a bear in the lobby, her knees pulled to her chest, sobbing into the flamingo’s sequins.

### 6. The Specialist’s Gentleness
William kneels beside her, ignoring the curious looks from staff and parents. He doesn't scold her. He simply sits on the floor next to her. 

"I'm sorry, Taylor. I know it's scary," he signs and speaks simultaneously. "But I need to be the doctor for one minute so I can be Daddy for the rest of the day. Can we do it here? By the bear?"

Taylor looks at him, her face tear-streaked. She nods slowly. Right there in the lobby, under the giant bear, William performs the second opinion exam. 

### 7. The Verdict
William pulls back. He breathes through his nose, focused, clinical. He sees the "unusual reflection" Aris mentioned. He adjusts the focus, his trained eye mapping the anatomy of his daughter's retina.

"It’s not a tumor," William says, his voice loud enough for Aris (who has caught up) to hear. His voice is steady, but there’s a faint tremor of relief in his hands. "It’s a persistent fetal vasculature—a small remnant of a blood vessel that didn't fully regress. It’s benign, but it’s causing a slight blurring in that quadrant."

### 8. The Aftermath
The tension leaves the room like air escaping a balloon. William picks Taylor up and holds her tight. She wraps her arms around his neck, the neon-pink ruffles pressed against his white coat. 

"All done, Taylor," William whispers. "The flamingo is brave. Let's go home."

---

## CORE THEMES
- **Medical Anxiety:** The physical "flight" response to the clinical environment.
- **Parenthood over Professionalism:** William’s willingness to perform an exam on a lobby floor to comfort his child.
- **The Contrast of Light and Dark:** The bright neon swimsuit vs. the "shadow" the doctors saw in her eye.

---

*This scene adds a layer of medical tension to Taylor's childhood and reinforces William's expertise.*
