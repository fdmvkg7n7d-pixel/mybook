## FAMILY CONTEXT

Taylor Raven is the eldest of the children Sabrina Bryna shares with William. For the first few years of her life, she is the youngest child in the house while her older half-sister Carlota Delicia is missing. Her world is quiet, safe, and revolves around her mother and William, but it is also shaped by the search and by the photos of Carlota on the mantle.

## KEY LIFE EVENTS

### The Swimsuit Appointment & Diagnosis (Age 4)
During her annual check-up at SickKids, Taylor refused to wear a hospital gown, insisting on her pink flamingo swimsuit. During the exam, her pediatrician flagged an anomaly in her eye. Her father, William, had to perform a second opinion exam. Overwhelmed by the tension, Taylor bolted from the exam room and hid in the hospital lobby. William eventually completed the exam behind a statue of a bear, diagnosing her with **PFV**. This event solidified her distrust of medical environments.

### The Meeting with Sun-Young (Age 5)
Despite her medical anxiety, Taylor's curiosity led her to sneak away from her father's office at SickKids. She navigated the staff elevators and found her way onto an inpatient floor, where she met a lonely patient named **Sun-Young**. Taylor's immediate bond with the girl and her report to William about Sun-Young's lack of "a flamingo" (a family) was the catalyst for Sun-Young eventually being fostered, adopted at age sixteen, and raised as a Key.

### South Korea Trip with Sun-Young (Age 10)

When Taylor Raven and Sun-Young are **ten years old**, **William takes them to South Korea**. For Taylor, the trip is a sister adventure with Liz and a father-daughter travel milestone with William. For Sun-Young, it is a Korean-heritage homecoming. During the trip, **Sun-Young finds a lump / swollen lymph node**, and Taylor sees immediately that Liz is frightened in a way ordinary travel nerves cannot explain because Sun-Young is a Leukemia survivor. They are in the girls' bedroom at Sabrina's Seoul apartment. William asks Sun-Young if he can check the lump; Taylor Raven stays in the room and holds Liz's right hand during the check. The scene is **very emotional** for Taylor: she is scared for Liz, protective, and tearful, but she keeps holding Liz's hand so Liz is not alone. Sun-Young is very nervous that Sabrina Bryna and William will not want to be her foster parents anymore. After the check, **William hugs Sun-Young to calm her down** while Taylor stays close and keeps hold of Liz's right hand. William reassures Sun-Young that he and Sabrina Bryna still want her to be their foster daughter because they love her so much. William treats the discovery as serious, quickly calls one of his colleagues on the **twentieth day of the South Korea trip**, and arranges medical follow-up. That night at the mansion, **Devon Smythson has her own bedroom at the mansion and does not need to leave**; **Taylor Raven and Sun-Young sleep together for comfort** after the emotional medical scare. The next morning, **Devon Smythson watches the twins** while Sabrina Bryna and William take Taylor Raven and Sun-Young to **SickKids / Toronto Sick Children's Hospital**; Taylor goes as Liz's support. At SickKids, the ultrasound looks bad, and William and his colleague have a conversation outside the examination room. William tells the family that Sun-Young is required to have surgery sooner than later; his colleague is checking the schedule. ### The Kidnapping of Carlota Delicia
Taylor Raven was approximately one year old when Carlota was kidnapped — too young to truly remember her older half-sister before the abduction, but old enough for Carlota's absence to become part of the architecture of the house. She grew up through several formative years defined by a "missing person"—photos of Carlota on the mantle, tense adult conversations, and a mother who was often distracted by grief and the search.

When Carlota returns after forty-two months, Taylor Raven (age 4-5) finds herself **paralyzed by fear** around her older half-sister. 

To Taylor, Carlota is a stranger who looks like the girl in the photos but acts like a ghost. The "new" Carlota is quiet, guarded, and carries an aura of the "sleazy bar in Tijuana" and the trauma of her father's abuse. Taylor is frightened by:
*   **The Silence:** Carlota’s refusal to sign (the language the rest of the household uses).
*   **The Darkness:** The heavy, traumatic energy Carlota brought back with her.
*   **The Unpredictability:** Taylor often hides behind furniture or retreats to her room when Carlota enters a room.
