# SCENE OUTLINE: SABRINA BRYNA AND WILLIAM'S CONVERSATION AFTER ORIN LORIN JUNIOR'S CHRISTMAS CALL

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Sabrina Bryna, William
- **Characters:** Sabrina Bryna, William, Roberta Katherine, William Timothy Junior, Taylor Raven, Sun-Young
- **Offstage / referenced characters:** Orin Lorin Junior, Danica (unknown to them as Orin's girlfriend), the rest of the Orlando Christmas household
- **Location:** Vaughan, Sabrina Bryna and William's home, their bedroom
- **When:** In the lead-up to Christmas, immediately after Orin tells them he is not coming
- **Status:** New scene outline
- **Tone:** Quiet, parental, grief-literate, reflective

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene shows Sabrina Bryna and William privately processing Orin's call after he tells them that a friend needs him because the friend's parent died suddenly. The scene is about how loving parents absorb difficult news without making it about themselves.

---

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: The Call Ends
- Orin's call has just ended.
- Sabrina Bryna and William sit with the absence before they speak.
- The first feeling is sadness that he will not be with them in Orlando.

### Beat 2: They Believe Him
- They take his explanation seriously.
- Because Orin himself was orphaned young, the story lands with particular force for both of them.
- Neither parent responds with suspicion or resentment.

### Beat 3: Private Parental Reading
- William may say that Orin would not stay behind lightly.
- Sabrina Bryna, with her deep emotional intelligence, understands that whether or not the full story is simple, the need behind it is real.
- The important point is that they trust his moral seriousness.

### Beat 4: Their Conclusion
- They agree he is doing the right thing by staying where he is needed.
- They let themselves miss him without trying to pull him back.
- A moment of parental sadness, trust, and blessing.

### Beat 5: Roberta Katherine and William Timothy Junior Interrupt
- The door opens and Roberta Katherine and William Timothy Junior come into their parents' bedroom arguing.
- They are arguing about the seats on the Key Corporation plane — who gets which seat, who sat where last time, who called it first.
- The plane is supposed to fly them from Toronto to Montreal to get Orin Lorin Junior, and then on to Orlando, to be at Carlota Delicia and Keith's mansion for Christmas.
- Now that Orin is not coming, the Montreal stop no longer makes sense, which makes the seat argument even more charged — the plan has changed and the kids feel it.
- Then Taylor Raven and Sun-Young come into the bedroom too, asking Sabrina Bryna and William about their summer dresses for Christmas Day.
- The bedroom is now full — the arguing kids, the girls with their dress question, Sabrina Bryna and William still sitting with what they just heard about Orin.
- Sabrina Bryna and William shift immediately from the quiet weight of Orin's call into parenting mode.
- The contrast is sharp: the deep private sorrow about Orin gives way to the loud, present demands of four younger children.
- Sabrina Bryna and William handle it all with their usual steady calm, but the earlier conversation about Orin sits behind their eyes.
- The scene ends with the household continuing — the missing son held quietly inside while the present children are tended to.

---

## WHAT THIS SCENE ESTABLISHES

1. Sabrina Bryna and William respond as true parents, not offended hosts.
2. Orin's orphan history shapes how they hear his excuse.
3. The family can hold absence without turning it into punishment.
4. Roberta Katherine and William Timothy Junior are present and active in the Orlando household.
5. Sabrina Bryna and William carry their worry about Orin privately while continuing to parent the younger children.
