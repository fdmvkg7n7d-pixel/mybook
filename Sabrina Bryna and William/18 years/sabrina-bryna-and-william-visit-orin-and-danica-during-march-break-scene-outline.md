- Sabrina Bryna and William arrive in March during the children's break.
- The visit has emotional weight because Christmas was handled at a distance.

### Beat 2: Seeing the House as Their House
- Ferme Clé no longer feels like Orin's solitary sanctuary alone.
- Danica's presence is visible in practical, domestic ways.

### Beat 3: Sabrina Bryna and William Reading the Truth of It
- Whether or not every Christmas lie has been unpacked yet, the emotional reality is now visible.
- The adults can see that this is not temporary drift. It is becoming a life.

### Beat 4: Danica and the Parents
- Danica has to receive Sabrina Bryna and William not as abstract parental authorities but as the people who raised Orin.
- Their warmth and seriousness matter.

### Beat 5: The Visit Lands
- By the end of the visit, some major threshold has been crossed: Orin and Danica are not just surviving there. They are living there.
