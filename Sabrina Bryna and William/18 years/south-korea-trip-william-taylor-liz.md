# SOUTH KOREA TRIP — William, Taylor Raven & Sun-Young "Liz"

---

## AGE / CANON PLACEMENT

**When:** Taylor Raven and Sun-Young are **ten years old**. William takes them to **South Korea**. They can take the **Key Corporation plane** for the trip. This is Sun-Young's Korean-heritage homecoming trip and a father-daughters travel milestone.

**Medical thread:** During this trip, **Sun-Young finds a lump / swollen lymph node**. Because she is a childhood **Leukemia survivor**, William takes the discovery seriously and treats it as a relapse scare until doctors prove otherwise. The check happens in the girls' bedroom at Sabrina's Seoul apartment: William asks Sun-Young if he can check the lump, and Taylor Raven is in the room holding Liz's right hand during the check. The moment is **very emotional** for all three of them — Sun-Young is frightened, Taylor Raven is trying to be brave for Liz, and William has to stay calm as both father and doctor. Sun-Young is also very nervous that Sabrina Bryna and William will not want to be her foster parents anymore because of the medical scare. After the check, **William hugs Sun-Young to calm her down** while Taylor Raven stays close and keeps hold of Liz's right hand. William reassures Sun-Young that Sabrina Bryna and William still want her to be their foster daughter because they love her so much.

## 💡 SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS

### For Taylor Raven
- **Medical Anxiety:** No hospitals planned. Her PFV doesn't need intervention but William monitors. Her neon-pink flamingo swimsuit is her comfort object — she wears it at every beach.
- **Sensory Breaks:** Seoul and Busan are loud. Sabrina's apartment is her sanctuary — quiet, familiar, hers. She retreats there when the city gets too big.
- **Growth Arc:** On Day 1, she hides behind William in crowded Myeongdong. By Day 20, she's confidently leading the way through Hongdae. Korea changes her.

### For Sun-Young "Liz"
- **Heritage:** Born in Korea, raised in Canada. This trip is a quiet homecoming. She doesn't talk about it, but Taylor notices her staring longer at things — the mountains, the ocean, the tangerine trees.
- **The Temple Moment:** At Seokguram Grotto in Gyeongju, standing before the ancient Buddha, she feels something she can't name. She survived. She's here. That's enough.
- **Post-Leukemia / Lump Discovery:** Sun-Young is a childhood **Leukemia survivor**. During the South Korea trip, she finds a lump / swollen lymph node. The discovery turns the homecoming into a relapse scare. The scene happens in the girls' bedroom at Sabrina's Seoul apartment: William asks Sun-Young if he can check the lump, Taylor Raven stays in the room holding Liz's right hand, and William takes her seriously, **quickly calls one of his colleagues on the twentieth day of the trip**, and makes sure she is evaluated. This is a **very emotional** bedroom scene, with Sun-Young frightened, Taylor Raven protective and tearful, and William balancing fatherly fear with medical calm. Sun-Young is very nervous that Sabrina Bryna and William will not want to be her foster parents anymore. After the check, **William hugs Sun-Young to calm her down** while Taylor keeps holding Liz's right hand, and William reassures her that he and Sabrina Bryna still want her to be their foster daughter because they love her so much. The hot springs on Days 14 and 18 remain nods to her healing journey, but the trip now also carries the fear that healing may not be finished.
- **Food:** The kimchi queen. She tries everything. Taylor calls her "the bravest eater in Korea."

### For William
- **Anchoring the trip:** He's a pediatrician on vacation. He doesn't check in with any hospital. For 21 days, he's just Dad.
- **ASL as secret language:** In crowded streets, he and the girls use ASL to communicate — a private bond in a foreign land.
- **The watching father:** He watches Taylor come out of her shell. He watches Liz reconnect with her roots. By Day 21, he's quieter than usual — changed by what he witnessed.
