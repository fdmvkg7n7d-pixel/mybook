## ORIGIN — HOW SHE COMES TO THE KEY FAMILY

Sun-Young's entry into the Key family is entirely due to **Taylor Raven**. 

Sun-Young was a patient at **Toronto Sick Children's Hospital (SickKids)**, where William works. She was being treated for **Juvenile Breast Carcinoma**, an exceptionally rare diagnosis for a child. At the time, she was a ward of the state with no family visitors. Five-year-old Taylor Raven snuck onto Sun-Young's inpatient floor and befriended her. Taylor was struck by Sun-Young's loneliness and told William that Sun-Young "didn't have a flamingo" (Taylor's way of saying she lacked a family and the joy that came with it).

Moved by his daughter's bond with the girl and Sun-Young's isolated situation, William and Sabrina Bryna began the process of becoming her foster parents. Sun-Young, later nicknamed **Liz**, was eventually integrated into the family as their **fifth child**.

## FAMILY CONTEXT

Sun-Young grows up in the Vaughan mansion as part of the household. Her siblings include three biological children of Sabrina and William (Taylor Raven, and twins Roberta Katherine and William Timothy Junior), Orin Lorin Junior (William's orphaned nephew, who joins when she and Taylor are 14), and Carlota Delicia (Sabrina's daughter from her first relationship). Although she was the youngest for a brief time before the twins were born, she remains a central part of the older sibling group with Taylor and Orin.
