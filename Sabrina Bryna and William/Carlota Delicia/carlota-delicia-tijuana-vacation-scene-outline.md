# CARLOTA DELICIA — TIJUANA VACATION SCENE OUTLINE

## Overview

**Title:** The Return  
**Ages:** Carlota Delicia and Keith, both 21  
**Duration:** 12 days total  
**Structure:**  
- **Days 1–5:** Tijuana (emotional pilgrimage and reclamation)  
- **Days 6–12:** East coast of Mexico (recovery, rest, and reconnection)  

**Framing:** Publicly described to friends and family as “a vacation to Tijuana.” In reality, it is a deeply emotional pilgrimage of reclamation, grief, and healing, followed by a restorative week on Mexico’s east coast. Keith knows every detail of Carlota’s past and is present solely to support her throughout.

**Core Emotional Theme:**  
A 21-year-old woman confronting the place where her childhood was stolen at age nine, held and witnessed by the man who knows everything and refuses to let her face it alone — followed by gentle healing and rebuilding together on the Caribbean coast.

---

## Scene Structure

### TIJUANA (Days 1–5)

#### Day 1 – Arrival & First Night
- They fly into Tijuana International Airport in the late afternoon.
- Carlota is quiet during the taxi ride to their hotel in the Zona Río district.
- Keith holds her hand the entire ride. He doesn’t try to fill the silence.
- First night: They stay in. Carlota barely touches the room-service food. She sits on the balcony staring at the city lights and eventually whispers, “I was nine when I first walked these streets at night.”
- Keith simply replies, “I’m here,” and sits with her until she’s ready to go inside.

#### Day 2 – The First Corner
- Carlota leads Keith to the exact street corner where she was first forced to work at nine years old.
- She stands frozen for several minutes. Keith stands slightly behind her, one hand resting gently on her back so she knows he’s there.
- She begins to cry — not the quiet tears of an adult, but the raw, shaking sobs of a child who never got to grieve.
- Keith steps forward, wraps both arms around her from behind, and holds her while she breaks. He murmurs, “You were just a little girl. It was never your fault.”

#### Day 3 – The Bar (They Do Not Enter) + Ceremony of Release
- Carlota brings Keith to the bar where she was most frequently taken as a child.
- They stop across the street. Carlota’s hands are shaking so badly she has to clutch Keith’s arm to stay upright.
- She whispers, voice breaking: “I was nine the first time they made me go inside. I didn’t even know what was happening.”
- Keith’s eyes fill with tears, but he stays completely still, letting her lead.
- Carlota stares at the entrance for a long time. Her breathing becomes ragged. She begins to hyperventilate.
- “I can’t go in,” she chokes out. “I can’t. I won’t.”
- Keith gently pulls her into his chest, shielding her view of the building. “You never have to go in again. Never. I’ve got you.”
- She sobs against him for several minutes, her whole body trembling. Keith holds her tightly, one hand cradling the back of her head, the other rubbing slow circles on her back.
- Eventually she pulls away just enough to look up at him, tears streaming. “You’re the only person who knows exactly where this is. And you’re still here.”
- Keith kisses her forehead. “I’m not going anywhere.”
- They do not approach the bar any closer. They never enter.

- As they walk away, Carlota’s steps slow. She leads Keith in silence to a stretch of beach not far from the bar.
- She stops at a particular spot in the sand, staring at the water.
- Her voice is barely audible: “This is where my father let the wolves have me.”
- Keith’s face goes pale. He reaches for her hand but waits.
- Carlota’s knees buckle. She sinks to the sand, shaking violently.
- “He brought me here when I was nine. Told me to wait. Then he left. The men came. He never came back.”
- She breaks completely — guttural, animal sounds of grief and horror. Keith drops to his knees beside her and pulls her into his lap, wrapping his entire body around her like a shield.
- He rocks her as she sobs, tears streaming down his own face. “I’m so sorry. I’m so, so sorry, my love. You were just a baby.”
- They stay there for a long time, Carlota clinging to him while the waves crash nearby — the same waves that once witnessed her greatest betrayal and pain.
- Eventually, still trembling, she whispers, “I needed you to see it. I needed someone to know exactly where it happened.”
- Keith presses his forehead to hers. “I see it. I know. And I will never leave you.”

- Later that evening, on the beach, they hold the ceremony of release. Carlota’s scream into the wind is even more raw after seeing the bar and the place of her father’s betrayal — a sound of pure, childlike anguish. Keith holds her through all of it.

#### Day 4 – Staying In Bed
- After the intensity of the previous day, they wake up and decide together: they are not leaving the room.
- They stay in bed all day — curtains drawn, phones on silent, the world shut out.
- They hold each other for hours. Sometimes they talk. Sometimes they’re silent. Keith strokes her hair while she rests her head on his chest.
- In the quiet of the afternoon, Carlota’s voice is small but clear:
  > “Do you think… we could ever have a child? Together?”
- Keith is quiet for a moment, then answers without hesitation, his voice thick with emotion:
  > “Yes. I want that with you. Whenever you’re ready — or if you’re never ready — I still want a life with you. But if you want to be a mother… I want to be the father of your child.”
- Carlota cries softly against him. She tells him she’s terrified she’ll never be able to be a good mother because of what was done to her. Keith holds her tighter and whispers:
  > “You would be the most loving mother in the world. And I would be right there with you. Every single day.”
- They stay in bed for the rest of the day, talking about the possibility of a future child — not making any decisions, just letting the idea exist between them in safety. By evening, Carlota falls asleep in his arms, her hand resting over his heart.

#### Day 5 – Final Day in Tijuana & Departure
- Carlota asks Keith to take one last photo of her standing on the same corner where everything began.
- In the photo she is no longer crying. She looks directly at the camera, Keith’s hand visible holding hers.
- They fly out that evening to the east coast of Mexico.

### EAST COAST OF MEXICO (Days 6–12) – Recovery & Reconnection

#### Day 6 – Arrival & First Breath
- They arrive on the Caribbean coast (likely Cancún or Tulum area) in the evening.
- Their hotel is simple and private, right by the water.
- Carlota sleeps deeply for the first time since arriving in Mexico. Keith stays awake for a while just watching her rest.

#### Day 7 – The Ocean
- They spend the entire day at the beach.
- Carlota lets herself float in the warm Caribbean water for the first time as an adult without fear.
- Keith swims beside her, never more than a few feet away.
- That night she tells him, “The water here doesn’t feel like it’s trying to take anything from me.”

#### Day 8 – Gentle Exploration
- They rent a scooter and explore small cenotes and quiet beaches.
- Carlota begins taking photos — not of landmarks, but of small beautiful things: flowers, shells, Keith’s hand in hers.
- She starts a new journal entry titled “After Tijuana.”

#### Day 9 – Deep Rest
- A full day of doing almost nothing.
- They lie in hammocks, read, and nap.
- Carlota cries once — soft, healing tears — and Keith simply holds her without asking why.

#### Day 10 – Reclaiming Joy
- Carlota suggests they go dancing at a small beach bar.
- She dances with Keith for the first time in years, laughing when he spins her.
- Later she says, “I didn’t know I could still feel this light.”

#### Day 11 – The Conversation
- In the evening they sit on the beach and talk about the future.
- Carlota brings up marriage for the first time since the trip began.
- Keith listens, then tells her he will wait as long as she needs — he’s not going anywhere.

#### Day 12 – Departure & Return Home
- They take one final sunrise walk along the water.
- Carlota collects a small handful of shells to bring back to Ontario.
- On the flight home she falls asleep with her head on Keith’s shoulder, holding the shells in her hand.

---

## Keith’s Role Throughout

- He never leaves her side unless she explicitly asks for space.
- He carries tissues, water, and the small bag of items for the ceremony.
- He reminds her multiple times: “You don’t have to be strong for me. I can carry it with you.”
- He cries with her on several occasions.
- Every night he asks the same gentle question: “What do you need right now?”

## Emotional Notes

- This trip is not about “moving on.” It is about **bearing witness** — both Carlota bearing witness to her own past and Keith bearing witness to her pain.
- The most powerful moments are often the silences.
- Carlota’s healing does not end with this trip, but this pilgrimage marks a major turning point: the moment she stops running from the city that broke her and begins to take pieces of herself back.

---

## Aftermath (Future Scenes)

- Upon returning to Ontario, Carlota begins writing more openly in her journal.
- She and Keith have their first serious conversation about marriage.
- Carlota starts considering whether she wants to eventually share parts of her story publicly to help other survivors.
