## PAST & NOTABLE EVENTS

### Life in Tijuana (Ages 9–18)
Carlota Delicia was forced into prostitution in Tijuana at only nine years old after being trafficked following the death of her mother. For nine long, devastating years she endured unimaginable exploitation on the streets and in bars. The trauma of those years carved deep, lasting wounds into her body and soul. At eighteen she finally escaped with the help of a social worker and made her way to Canada, carrying the weight of everything she had survived.

### Escape and Rebuilding
She arrived in Ontario with almost nothing. Through community programs and her own determination she finished high school, began therapy, and started to rebuild a sense of self-worth.

## The Return to Tijuana (Age 21)
At twenty-one, Carlota Delicia and Keith make the deliberate, emotionally charged decision to return to Tijuana together. This is not a vacation in the traditional sense but a **pilgrimage of reclamation and closure**.

### Purpose of the Trip
- Visit the exact corners, alleys, and bars where a nine-year-old Carlota was forced to work
- Hold an intensely emotional private ceremony of release (letters to her younger self, flowers, prayers, and the burning of symbolic items)
- Reclaim the city on her own terms with Keith beside her as living proof that she survived and is loved
- Possibly connect with local organizations fighting child trafficking, in the hope that her story can help protect other little girls

Keith’s steady, informed presence is central to every part of the trip. He is her anchor.

### Emotional Stakes
For Carlota, returning is an almost unbearable emotional weight. She was only nine years old when her childhood was stolen in that city. Walking those same streets at twenty-one with Keith beside her is both an act of defiance and a raw confrontation with the terrified little girl she once was. The trip forces her to face the full horror of what happened to her as a child — something she has only begun to speak about in therapy. Keith understands this is the most vulnerable she has ever allowed herself to be, and he is determined to hold space for every tear, every silence, and every moment of rage or grief that surfaces.

### Vacation Framing
They frame the journey outwardly as “a vacation to Tijuana” to friends and family, but both understand its deeper purpose. They plan to stay for ten days, mixing difficult visits with moments of normalcy (beach time, good food, quiet evenings).
