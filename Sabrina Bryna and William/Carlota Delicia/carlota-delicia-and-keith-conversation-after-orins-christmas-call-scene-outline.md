# SCENE OUTLINE: CARLOTA DELICIA AND KEITH'S CONVERSATION AFTER ORIN LORIN JUNIOR'S CHRISTMAS CALL

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Carlota Delicia, Keith
- **Offstage / referenced characters:** Orin Lorin Junior, Madeleine Jaycee Holiday Key
- **Key context:** Carlota Delicia and Keith work for Walt Disney World. Their home in Orlando is connected to their employment there.
- **Location:** Carlota and Keith's home in Orlando
- **When:** In the lead-up to Christmas, immediately after Orin's separate call
- **Status:** New scene outline
- **Tone:** Domestic, intimate, thoughtful

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene gives Carlota and Keith a private conversation after Orin's call. Carlota is nursing six-month-old Madeleine Jaycee Holiday Key. The scene should feel lived-in, domestic, and perceptive.

---

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: The Call Ends
- Carlota has just gotten off the phone with Orin.
- She is nursing Madeleine Jaycee Holiday Key while Keith is nearby.
- The room is quiet in the way rooms with babies often are: full, soft, interrupted.

### Beat 2: She Repeats the Story
- Carlota tells Keith that Orin is not coming because a friend's parent died suddenly and he wants to stay with the friend.
- Keith receives the story seriously.

### Beat 3: Carlota's Reading
- Carlota believes Orin means what he says about needing to stay.
- She may also feel, without being able to prove it, that there is more under the surface.
- Not deceit in a malicious sense — simply that Orin sounded like someone carrying more than one thing at once.

### Beat 4: Keith's Role
- Keith helps her name the difference between fact and intuition.
- He may say that whether or not Orin told the whole story, the moral center is obvious: he is staying where he believes love is required.

### Beat 5: Madeleine Jaycee Holiday Key in the Middle of It
- Carlota's nursing of Madeleine Jaycee Holiday Key deepens the scene's emotional register.
- Holding an infant sharpens her sense that showing up matters more than plans.
- The scene closes with Carlota hoping Orin is where he needs to be, even if she suspects there is more she has not been told.

---

## WHAT THIS SCENE ESTABLISHES

1. Carlota and Keith have a thoughtful adult household.
2. Carlota reads Orin with more sensitivity than a surface conversation might suggest.
3. Madeleine Jaycee Holiday Key's presence gives the scene embodied domestic warmth.
