# SCENE OUTLINE: CARLOTA DELICIA IS TOLD SHE IS HIV-POSITIVE

*Post-recovery medical workup at SickKids. Carlota is twelve or thirteen years old, home from Tijuana for a matter of weeks. Mike Key is dead — he died when Gamaliel Galvin and Klára were sixteen, years before this scene. The room is shaped by that absence.*

---

## SETTING

- **Location:** A private consultation room at **Toronto Sick Children's Hospital (SickKids)**. Not an exam room with a paper-covered table — a room with upholstered chairs, a low table, a box of tissues already on it because someone always cries in here.
- **Time:** Late morning, a few weeks after the police-assisted recovery from Tijuana. Long enough for the bloodwork to come back. Not long enough for any of the rest of the trauma to have settled.
- **Outside the window:** Toronto. Carlota has been home from Mexico for about a month. The light is wrong to her — too northern, too pale. Tijuana lighting is still what her eyes expect when she wakes up.

---

## IN THE ROOM

- **Carlota Delicia Key** — twelve or thirteen. Hearing. Not signing. Wearing clothes Sabrina Bryna bought her in the first week back, none of which quite fit yet.
- **Sabrina Bryna Key** — Carlota's mother. Deaf. Cerebral palsy. Has barely let Carlota out of her sight since the airport in Tijuana.
- **William** — Sabrina's partner. Child ophthalmologist at this hospital. Today he is not the doctor in the room — he is Carlota's stepfather. He has handed off the conversation to a colleague on purpose, so that Carlota can hear it from someone who is not also family.
- **Dr. [TBD]** — the pediatric infectious-disease specialist coordinating Carlota's case. Calm. Has done this before. Has not done it like this before.
- **An ASL interpreter** — a hospital staff interpreter who knows Sabrina Bryna from previous visits with William. Sits where Sabrina can see her clearly.

**Notably absent:** **Mike Key**, who would have been in this room if he were alive. He is not. Sabrina feels it. So does William, who attended Mike's funeral years ago and has been one of the steady male presences in the family since.

---

## BEAT 1 — BEFORE THE DOCTOR COMES IN

Carlota and Sabrina sit on the same small couch, not touching. Carlota has not initiated physical contact with her mother since the airport. She does not pull away, either. She just does not start.

William sits across from them. The interpreter sits at an angle so she can sign for Sabrina without standing between mother and daughter.

Sabrina signs, slowly, *"Whatever the doctor says, we will deal with it together."*

The interpreter speaks the words aloud, softly, in case Carlota wants to hear them.

Carlota looks at her mother's hands. She does not sign back. She has not signed since she came home. Carlos broke that.

She nods, once. It is the most she has given Sabrina since Tijuana. Sabrina takes it the way someone takes a thimble of water in a desert.

---

## BEAT 2 — THE DOCTOR SITS DOWN

Dr. [TBD] comes in with a tablet, sits down, sets the tablet aside immediately. (William coached her: *do not deliver this through a screen.*)

She introduces herself in three sentences. She tells Carlota that she has the results. She tells Carlota that she is going to use clear words, and she will pause as much as Carlota needs.

She looks at Sabrina, and the interpreter, and back at Carlota. She says:

> *"Carlota, the bloodwork shows that you have HIV. That means there is a virus in your blood. You got it from one of the men who hurt you. It was not your fault, and it is not a punishment."*

She pauses.

The interpreter signs it for Sabrina.

Sabrina's hands are still in her lap. Her face does not move. She has spent forty-two months preparing to hear worse. This is not worse. This is one of the things on her list.

---

## BEAT 3 — WHAT CARLOTA HEARS

Carlota does not cry. She has not cried in front of anyone since the airport.

She hears *virus.* She hears *one of the men.* She hears *not your fault.*

She thinks of the bar in Tijuana. She thinks of the men whose faces she can and cannot remember. She thinks: *of course.* She has been waiting for a piece of news like this. She had already decided, on the plane home, that her body was probably broken in ways she did not yet know about. Now one of those ways has a name.

She says, in spoken English, the first long sentence she has spoken to anyone in this country since coming back:

> *"Am I going to die?"*

The doctor does not flinch. (William coached her on this, too: *she will ask. Have the answer ready.*)

> *"Not from this. Not anymore. We have medicine that works really well. You will take it every day for the rest of your life, and if you do, the virus in your blood becomes so small that the tests can't find it. We call that 'undetectable.' People who are undetectable live full lives — they go to school, they have jobs, they have partners, they have children if they want to. You can do all of those things."*

Carlota turns this over. *The rest of your life.* It is the first sentence anyone has said to her, in any language, that assumes she is going to have one.

---

## BEAT 4 — SABRINA SIGNS

Sabrina has been holding her hands still in her lap throughout. Now they move.

She signs to Carlota — not through the interpreter, directly to her daughter — *"You are not alone in this. Not now. Not ever."*

It is the first thing she has signed *to* Carlota (rather than *near* Carlota) since the airport. She does not expect a sign back. She does not need one.

Carlota watches her mother's hands. She understands. She has not lost the language — only the practice of it. The vocabulary is still in her, sealed in a box she has not yet been able to open.

She does not sign back.

But she leans, just slightly, into her mother's shoulder. The first contact she has initiated in a month.

Sabrina does not move. She is afraid that moving will end it.

---

## BEAT 5 — THE EMPTY CHAIR

Late in the conversation, while the doctor is explaining the medication schedule (a single pill, once a day, with food), Sabrina glances at the chair to William's right. It is empty.

If Mike were alive, he would be in it.

He would be the one asking the practical questions — *what's the brand name, what are the side effects, who do we call in an emergency, what about her school records.* He would be the one Sabrina could lean on when the doctor's voice got too clinical. He would be the one taking notes on the back of an envelope because he never trusted hospital pamphlets.

Mike is not here. He died when Gamaliel and Klára were sixteen, years ago now. By the time this diagnosis lands, the family has had to learn how to absorb crises without him.

William sees Sabrina look at the chair. He says nothing about it. He takes out a small notebook and starts writing down everything the doctor is saying. Not because he needs the notes — he already knows the protocol — but because someone has to do what Mike would have done, and he can be that person today.

Sabrina sees him do it. She doesn't sign anything. She just exhales.

---

## BEAT 6 — LEAVING THE ROOM

The doctor walks them out as far as the elevator. She tells Carlota she will see her in two weeks for the first follow-up. She tells her she can call any time, with any question, even small ones, even at night.

In the elevator going down, the three of them stand in a triangle. William, Sabrina, Carlota.

Carlota says, in spoken English, looking at the floor:

> *"Don't tell the cousins yet. Don't tell anyone yet. Please."*

William says: *"That's yours to tell. We won't tell anyone without asking you first."*

Sabrina signs the same thing. Carlota watches her mother's hands and nods.

They walk out into the Toronto afternoon. Carlota gets into the back seat of William's car. Sabrina gets in beside her — not in front, beside her — and Carlota does not move away.

---

## WHAT THIS SCENE ESTABLISHES

- Carlota's HIV diagnosis is **confirmed and named** in the saga.
- The medication regimen and **U=U (Undetectable = Untransmittable)** framework is introduced — important for the long-term Carlota/Keith arc.
- Carlota's autonomy over her own disclosure is established early: *"That's yours to tell."* This sets up later beats — telling Keith on the tenth date, telling (or not telling) the broader Key family.
- **Mike's absence** is felt, named (in narration), and processed. The empty chair is the saga acknowledging that the patriarch who would have steered this conversation is gone, and that William has stepped into a version of that role.
- The first small, unconscious **physical reconnection** between Sabrina and Carlota happens here — a lean of a shoulder. The first **sign directed to** Carlota, even without a sign back. These are seeds for the eventual "Return of the Signs" arc.
