## KEY CHILDHOOD EVENTS

### Event 1 — The Nanny Incident (Age 3)

At **age three**, Carlota Delicia witnesses her father, Carlos Reyes, kissing her nanny, **Devon Smythson**. She tells Sabrina Bryna what she saw. Carlos denies it; Sabrina trusts her daughter. Sabrina throws Carlos out of her Vaughan mansion. The relationship between Sabrina Bryna and Carlos ends.

### Event 2 — The Kidnapping (Age 9 to ~12/13)

At **age nine**, Carlota Delicia is kidnapped by her father, Carlos Reyes, during a scheduled visitation. He takes her to **Mexico City**, Mexico.

She is gone for **forty-two months** — three and a half years.

During this time:
- Carlos keeps her in Mexico City.
- He **breaks her ASL** — she stops using it entirely.
- He **exploits her at a sleazy bar in Tijuana**, Mexico.
- During this exploitation, she **contracts HIV.**

She is found at approximately **twelve or thirteen years old**. Her cousin **Pandora Paige Key** (Kiley Rose's daughter, ~20) is in Tijuana. Pandora notices Carlota in the sleazy bar. She recognizes her. Pandora does **not** approach or introduce herself — she secretly photographs Carlota and sends the image to Sabrina Bryna. That photograph is the first proof in forty-two months that Carlota is alive.

**Sabrina Bryna and William fly down to Tijuana.** With the help of the **police**, they bring Carlota Delicia home.

Carlos is arrested in Mexico City and imprisoned there.

## POST-KIDNAPPING & RECOVERY

Carlota Delicia returns home at approximately **twelve or thirteen years old**. 

### The Language Barrier
The most immediate and painful consequence of the kidnapping is the **destruction of her ASL**. Carlos forced her to stop signing to distance her from her mother. Upon her return, she is a hearing girl in a household where the primary language is one she no longer speaks. This creates a profound emotional wall between her and Sabrina Bryna.

### The Reintegration Process
*   **Medical & Trauma Care:** Carlota undergoes extensive therapy to process the exploitation in Tijuana and the trauma of the kidnapping. The medical workup on her return also confirms she is **HIV-positive** — contracted during the Tijuana exploitation. She is started on **antiretroviral therapy (ART)** immediately. With consistent treatment her viral load becomes and remains **undetectable** (U=U: untransmittable). Her infectious-disease care is coordinated through William's SickKids network. The diagnosis is one more thing the kidnapping took from her — and one more thing she learns to live with rather than be defined by.
*   **The Siblings' Welcome:** Her five siblings (Taylor Raven, Roberta Katherine, William Timothy Junior, Orin Lorin Junior, and Sun-Young "Liz") have to get to know a sister who is effectively a stranger. They are protective but struggle with the "new" Carlota who is guarded and quiet.
*   **The Sibling Tension:** **Taylor Raven** is specifically frightened of Carlota. The younger sister's fear and avoidance add to Carlota's sense of isolation, making her feel like an outsider in her own family home.
*   **Schooling:** She is enrolled at **Everest Academy** (130 Racco Parkway, Thornhill, Vaughan) — a K–12 holistic private school. The individualized, whole-child approach helps her catch up on the years of education she missed or that were disrupted while in Mexico. If needed, **Villanova College** (King City) is identified as a secondary option. **It is in her grade eight class at Everest Academy that she meets Keith Holiday.** After high school, she attends the **University of Central Florida (UCF) — Rosen College of Hospitality Management** in **Orlando, Florida**, majoring in **Hospitality Management** with the goal of working at **Walt Disney Imagineering**. It is a fresh start in a new city, far from the memories of Vaughan and Mexico — and Keith makes the move with her, enrolling in the same program.

## UNIVERSITY YEARS — UCF / ROSEN COLLEGE / IMAGINEERING

### The Move to Orlando
Carlota and Keith arrive in Orlando together after high school. Sabrina Bryna and William have already purchased and furnished the **Orlando mansion** for them. It is private, secure, and quiet — everything a dormitory could not be for someone with Carlota's history.

### Same School, Same Major
Both Carlota and Keith are enrolled at the **University of Central Florida**, specifically the **Rosen College of Hospitality Management** (the campus dedicated to hospitality, theme park, and attraction management — located near International Drive in Orlando, in close proximity to the Disney parks). They share the **same major** so that they can study together, attend the same classes, and aim for the same career path.

### Separate Bedrooms
Although they live together, **Carlota and Keith sleep in separate bedrooms.** This is Keith's choice, made out of respect for Carlota. After everything she survived in Tijuana, he is determined never to be the person who pushes her, surprises her, or assumes anything about what she is ready for physically. He has told her plainly:

> *"My door is open. I'm not coming into your room. If you ever want to come into mine, you can — when you want, and only when you want. And if you never do, that's okay too."*

It is a quiet, ongoing act of love. The separate bedrooms are not distance — they are safety.

### The Imagineering Internship Interviews
Both Carlota and Keith apply for **Walt Disney Imagineering** internships through the program. Imagineering is the most competitive and creatively demanding division at Disney — the team that designs and engineers the parks, rides, shows, and resorts.

They are **very nervous** in the lead-up to the interviews:
- It is the first major professional bar either of them has ever had to clear.
- For Carlota, an interview means sitting in a small room with strangers asking her questions about herself — a setting that can pull her straight back into trauma memory.
- For Keith, the nerves are about Carlota as much as himself: he wants her to get in, and he wants them to get in *together*, so that the dream they built in their grade eight classroom does not split apart now.

The nights before the interviews, neither of them sleeps well. Keith stays in his bedroom. Carlota stays in hers. In the morning, they get dressed, eat breakfast together at the kitchen island, and drive to Imagineering side by side.

## HOW HE MEETS CARLOTA — GRADE EIGHT → GRADE TEN

Keith and Carlota are in the **same grade eight class at Everest Academy** in Thornhill. By that point, Carlota has been home from Mexico for roughly two years. She is still guarded, still quiet, still re-learning who she is.

Keith doesn't keep his distance. He doesn't push, either. He just **stays nearby** — sits at the same lab table, partners with her on group projects, lends her a pen and never asks for it back. Slowly, she lets him in.

For roughly two years they are **just friends**. Keith is in no hurry. He does not ask her out in grade eight, or grade nine. He waits — partly because he can read her, partly because that is simply who he is.

They **start dating at the end of their grade ten year.**

### The Tenth Date
On their **tenth date**, Carlota tells him everything: the forty-two months, the broken ASL, the sleazy bar in Tijuana and what was done to her there — **and that she is HIV-positive**. She tells him because if Keith is going to be in her life, he has to know all of it — and if knowing makes him leave, better that he leave now.

Keith listens. He doesn't flinch. He doesn't ask intrusive questions. He tells her, simply, that **what happened to her does not define her**, and that he is not going anywhere. He stays.

## PERSONALITY & VALUES

- **Steady.** Calm, soft-spoken, dependable. He does what he says he will do.
- **Respectful — almost to a fault.** He has internalized very deeply what was done to Carlota in Tijuana. He has resolved he will **never** be a man who takes anything from her — never a presence she has to brace against, never a door she has to lock.
- **Patient.** He operates on a long timeline. He is willing to wait years for things most teenage boys would not wait weeks for.
- **Creative.** Drawn to design, storytelling, and how things work — which is why Imagineering, not finance or hotel management, is the dream.
- **Loyal.** Once he is in, he is in. The tenth-date conversation cemented him; he has not wavered since.

## THE LIVING ARRANGEMENT IN ORLANDO — SEPARATE BEDROOMS

Carlota and Keith share the Orlando mansion. They eat together, study together, watch movies together, drive to campus together. **But they sleep in separate bedrooms** — Keith's decision, made out of love and respect:

- He will **never** go into Carlota's bedroom uninvited. Not to wake her up, not to bring her something, not for any reason. Her room is hers.
- If she is ever ready for that part of the relationship, **she will come to him.** On her own feet, on her own timing, on her own terms.
- He has told her this plainly so that she never has to wonder.
- If she never crosses the hallway, that is also fine. He is not waiting impatiently. He is just *there*.

> *"My door is open. I'm not coming into your room. If you ever want to come into mine, you can — when you want, and only when you want. And if you never do, that's okay too."*

For Carlota — whose entire experience of male desire before Keith was her father's exploitation in Tijuana — this is the first time a man's wanting has come wrapped in the words *"only when you want."* It is, in its own quiet way, part of her healing.

## UCF / ROSEN COLLEGE / IMAGINEERING

Keith chose UCF's Rosen College because Carlota was going there, and he chose the same major so they could share classes, share assignments, and stay on the same career path. He is genuinely interested in the field — but he made his choice with her in mind.

Both apply for **Walt Disney Imagineering** internships. They are **very nervous**. For Keith, the nerves are doubled:
1. He wants the internship for himself — it is the dream he and Carlota have been talking about since grade eight.
2. He wants Carlota to get it more than he wants it for himself. The idea that they might be split — one of them in, one of them out — is the version of the future he cannot bear.

The night before the interviews, he barely sleeps. He stays in his bedroom. Across the hallway, in hers, Carlota also barely sleeps. In the morning, they get dressed, eat breakfast at the kitchen island, and drive to Imagineering together.
