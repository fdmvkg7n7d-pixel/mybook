# SCENE OUTLINE: THE DECISION TO ADOPT

**Characters:** Ashley Erin Key, Georgi Dimitrov.
**Setting:** Their shared home (likely Toronto). A quiet, intimate evening.
**Timeframe:** **Ashley Erin and Georgi are thirty-one.** They have been together for seventeen years; they are settled in their careers and their marriage.
**Theme:** Legacy, intentionality, and the expansion of love.

---

## THE CONTEXT

At thirty-one, Ashley Erin and Georgi have reached a plateau of stability. Ashley Erin is an established icon in the fashion world; Georgi is a successful business professional with his MBA. They have the financial means, the emotional maturity, and a relationship that has already survived the greatest tests of distance and time.

They have always spoken of "marriage, kids, the whole thing," but for years, that conversation was a distant horizon. Now, the horizon has arrived.

This is not a decision born of a biological clock or societal pressure, but of a shared realization: they have an abundance of love and stability that they are ready to offer to children who need it. For Georgi, the idea of adoption is intuitive—he knows the value of being chosen and the power of a stable home. For Ashley Erin, it is about creating a sanctuary of unconditional acceptance, the same kind of sanctuary Georgi has been for her since she was thirteen.

---

## THE OUTLINE

### Part One: The Quiet Opening

- The scene begins in the mundane intimacy of their home—perhaps cooking dinner together or winding down after a long day.
- The conversation starts not as a "big talk," but as a series of small observations. A comment about a niece or nephew, a story about a child they saw in the park, or a reflection on their own childhoods.
- There is a comfortable silence between them—the kind of silence that only comes from seventeen years of knowing exactly what the other is thinking.
- Georgi is the one to bring it into focus. He doesn't ask *if* they want children; he asks *how* they want to become parents.

### Part Two: The "Why" of Adoption

- They discuss the choice of adoption.
- Georgi speaks about the concept of "chosen family." He reflects on his own experience as an immigrant—of arriving in a place where he was a stranger and being welcomed in by Ashley Erin and her family. He wants to be that welcoming force for a child.
- Ashley Erin speaks about the "Haden wound" and the safety Georgi provided. She expresses a fierce desire to be the parent who ensures a child never feels "wrong" or "too much" or "a problem." She wants to build a home where a child's identity is celebrated as a strength, not a target.
- They acknowledge the challenges: the bureaucracy, the emotional toll, the unknowns of biological histories. But they do so with a shared confidence. They are a team; they have always been a team.

### Part Three: The Commitment

- The conversation shifts from the theoretical to the practical.
- They talk about the "idea" of their future children—not specific traits, but the *feeling* of the home they want to create.
- The decision is sealed not with a grand gesture, but with a quiet, mutual agreement.
- "Let's do it," one of them says. "Let's start the process."
- The scene ends with a sense of profound anticipation. The "whole thing" they promised each other as teenagers is finally beginning.

---

## WHY THIS SCENE MATTERS

- **It establishes the intentionality of their parenthood.** Their children are not an accident or a default; they are a choice made from a place of abundance and stability.
- **It ties back to the core themes of the relationship.** The decision to adopt is an extension of the "radical relational steadiness" and "unflinching acceptance" that defines Georgi and Ashley Erin.
- **It sets the stage for the adoption timeline.** This decision at thirty-one provides the four-year runway (the research, the agency applications, the home studies) that leads to the adoption of the twins at thirty-five.
- **It reinforces their partnership.** The way they navigate this decision—with transparency, mutual respect, and a shared vision—mirrors the way they will eventually navigate the challenges of raising five children.
