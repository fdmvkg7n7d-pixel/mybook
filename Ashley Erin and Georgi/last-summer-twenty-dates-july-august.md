# SCENE OUTLINE: THE LAST SUMMER — TWENTY DATES (JULY–AUGUST)

**Characters:** Ashley Erin Key (17 — turns eighteen in October), Georgi Dimitrov (17 — turned seventeen in April). Supporting: Raya, the Key sisters (cameos), Dr. Stoyan Dimitrov, Dr. Veska Dimitrova, Mike and Terri Anne Key.
**Setting:** Toronto and its edges — the city, the lake, the islands, the places that are theirs.
**Timeframe:** **Early July through late August.** The eight weeks between graduation/hotel (late June) and Georgi's departure for London (late August). They know the clock is ticking. Every date counts.

---

## THE CONTEXT

Georgi leaves for London at the end of August. They have known this since the acceptance letter arrived. They have two months — July and August — and they are determined to spend them together.

This is not a countdown. They are not saying goodbye every day. They are building a summer that will have to last — a store of memories to draw on when the ocean is between them and the time difference makes everything harder. Twenty dates in eight weeks. Some are elaborate. Most are ordinary. All of them matter because the person you love is about to be five thousand miles away, and you are seventeen, and you don't know when you'll see him again.

**Tone:** Sweet, urgent, shadowed. The reader knows what's coming at the end of August. They don't talk about it much. They don't have to.

---

## JULY — DATES 1 THROUGH 12

### Date 1: The Toronto Islands (Early July, first weekend after graduation)

- They take the ferry across the harbor. A whole day on the islands — the beach, the bike paths, the view of the skyline from the other side.
- They rent bikes. Georgi is faster; she yells at him to slow down. He doesn't. She catches up anyway.
- They sit on the beach and eat ice cream that melts too fast. She gets sand in her hair. He doesn't help.
- On the ferry back, the sun is setting behind the city. She leans against the railing. He stands behind her, arms around her waist. They don't say anything. The skyline does the talking.
- **Why it matters:** The first date of the last summer. The city from the other side. A whole day with no schedule, no parents, no *This Land* — just them.

### Date 2: The Night Market (Early July)

- A street festival or night market — crowded, loud, anonymous. The kind of place where no one is looking for a celebrity.
- They eat food from too many stalls. She makes him try something he's never had. He makes a face. She laughs.
- They run into someone from UCC — a casual acquaintance of Georgi's — and Ashley Erin is introduced simply as *my girlfriend.* No surname, no explanation. The acquaintance doesn't recognize her. She loves it.
- **Why it matters:** Anonymity. The gift of being nobody in a crowd.

### Date 3: The Recording Studio (Mid-July)

- Ashley Erin takes him to the recording studio where she's working on music. She plays him something new on guitar — a song she's been writing. She's nervous. She's always nervous playing for him, even after four years.
- Georgi listens. He doesn't say *it's good* right away. He listens to the whole thing, and then he says: *The bridge. Do the bridge again.*
- She does. He nods. *Better.*
- She records a rough take with him in the booth — just a voice memo on her phone, his quiet presence in the background. She'll listen to it a hundred times after he leaves.
- **Why it matters:** The music arc — Ashley Erin as an artist. Georgi as her first and best audience. A recording she'll keep.

### Date 4: The Aquarium (Mid-July)

- A callback to one of their early dates — dates 2 through 9 of the first year, which included a trip to the aquarium.
- Now they're older. Now they've done everything. But the aquarium is the same — the blue light, the slow drift of jellyfish, the tunnel where the sharks glide overhead.
- He stands in the tunnel longer than she expects. She watches him watch the fish. His nursing mind, already turning toward the body, toward life, toward the small fragile things.
- *What are you thinking about?* — *Nothing. Everything.*
- **Why it matters:** A callback to the early dating runway. The aquarium as a place of stillness. Georgi's quiet — the thing she'll miss most.

### Date 5: The Rooftop (Mid-July)

- Georgi finds a rooftop. Not a fancy one — a friend of a friend, a low building with a fire escape and a view of the city. He brings a blanket and a portable speaker and two bottles of something cold.
- They lie on the blanket and watch the sky go dark. The stars are faint above the city light, but they're there.
- He plays music from his phone — the playlist she made him for Valentine's Day, the one he played guitar to after the burned dinner. The same songs, two years later.
- *We should do this in London,* she says. Then catches herself. London. He won't be here. There won't be a rooftop.
- He doesn't let the silence sit. *We will. I'll find one. I'll send you a picture.*
- **Why it matters:** The first time London is spoken aloud on a date. The shadow that falls and is pushed back.

### Date 6: The Double Date with Raya (Mid-July)

- Raya insists. She has watched them since she pulled Georgi into her kitchen at thirteen. She has earned the right to third-wheel one more time.
- They go bowling. Raya is terrible. Georgi is surprisingly competitive. Ashley Erin keeps score and mocks both of them.
- At some point Raya pulls Ashley Erin aside — bathroom, snack bar, whatever — and says something real. *He's going to be okay, you know. And so are you.*
- Ashley Erin: *I know.*
- Raya: *Do you?*
- Ashley Erin: *No.*
- Raya hugs her. The same kitchen, four years later — but the kitchen is a bowling alley now, and the hug is the same.
- **Why it matters:** Raya's recurring role as the matchmaker / truth-teller. Acknowledgment that this is hard.

### Date 7: The Pool at the Key Mansion (Late July)

- A hot July day. Ashley Erin swims — she's one of the Key sisters who swims, a detail planted in her profile — and Georgi sits on the edge with a book.
- She does laps. He reads. Occasionally she splashes him. He pretends to be annoyed.
- Terri Anne brings them lemonade. She doesn't stay — she just sets the glasses down and disappears. She has known about the London departure since Georgi told them in April. She doesn't need to say anything. The lemonade says it.
- At some point Georgi puts the book down and gets in the pool. He doesn't swim — he just stands in the shallow end, water up to his chest, and she swims over to him. They kiss in the water. The sun is hot. The summer is half gone.
- **Why it matters:** The Key mansion as home. Terri Anne's quiet presence. The swimming detail paid off.

### Date 8: The Bookstore — Again (Late July)

- Another callback. The used bookstore from their fourteenth date — the one where they wandered separate aisles and bought each other's picks.
- This time Georgi buys her a book about London. Not a guidebook — a novel, or a history, or something that makes the city feel less like a rival and more like a place.
- She buys him a copy of the Bulgarian-English dictionary — a joke, because she has his original on her nightstand, and he needs a new one. He laughs. He actually laughs — the full, unguarded laugh that belongs only to her.
- **Why it matters:** The dictionary callback. London acknowledged through a gift — a way of saying *I'm letting you go, but I'm sending something with you.*

### Date 9: The Long Walk (Late July)

- No destination. No plan. They walk from his neighborhood to hers — or as far as they can get before their feet hurt. Hours of city streets, talking about nothing and everything.
- They talk about the future. Not the big future — not *marriage, kids, the whole thing* (they did that in the hotel room). The near future: what his first week in London will be like. What her senior year will be like. The winter formal without him. Her graduation without him.
- *You'll come back for my graduation, right?* — *I'll try.* — *Try harder.* — *I will.*
- **Why it matters:** The practical future. The logistics of love across an ocean. The first real conversation about how this will work.

### Date 10: The Cinema — A Reversal (Late July)

- A deliberate echo of the first date — the movie theatre, the dark, the armrest. But everything is different now.
- Back then they were fourteen and fifteen, terrified of ruining the friendship. Now they are seventeen, and they have spent one night in a hotel room, and the armrest is not a barrier anymore.
- They sit in the back row. She holds his hand from the opening credits. He doesn't let go.
- Afterward they don't talk about the movie. They talk about the first time — how nervous they were, how the hand-hold in the dark was the most terrifying thing either of them had ever done.
- *Would you do it again?* — *The movie?* — *The first date. All of it.* — *In a heartbeat.*
- **Why it matters:** The first-date callback. Full circle. The recognition that they would choose each other again.

### Date 11: The CN Tower (Early August)

- Tourist Toronto. They have both lived here for years and neither has done the CN Tower. They decide to be tourists for a day.
- The glass floor terrifies her. He walks across it casually, just to annoy her. She refuses to follow.
- At the top, looking out over the city — the lake, the islands, the sprawl of neighborhoods — she tries to memorize the view. This is her city. His city. Their city. In a few weeks it will just be hers.
- *You can see everything from up here,* she says. — *Not everything.* — *What can't you see?* — *London.*
- **Why it matters:** The city from above. The acknowledgment that he is leaving, spoken from the highest point in Toronto.

### Date 12: The Sick Day — Reversed (Early August)

- Georgi gets sick. A summer cold, nothing serious. Ashley Erin shows up at the Dimitrov apartment with soup — soup she made herself, not the Key chef, not Terri Anne. She has been practicing.
- It's a deliberate echo of the sick day when they were fourteen, when she was sick and he brought his mother's soup and Terri Anne noticed him for the first time.
- Now she sits on the edge of his bed. He is feverish and grumpy and embarrassingly grateful. Dr. Veska checks on them and finds Ashley Erin feeding her son soup, and her expression — complicated, proud, a little sad — says everything.
- *You didn't have to come.* — *You came when I was sick.* — *That was four years ago.* — *So?*
- **Why it matters:** The sick-day callback. The role reversal. Ashley Erin taking care of him before he leaves — proving she can.

## AUGUST — DATES 13 THROUGH 20

### Date 13: The Kensington Market Afternoon (Early August)

- Kensington Market on a Sunday. Vintage shops, street musicians, the smell of spices and incense from a dozen different cuisines. They wander without aim.
- She buys a ridiculous hat. He buys a used vinyl record he's never heard of. They eat empanadas on a curb.
- A street musician is playing guitar — badly. Georgi nudges her. *You're better.* She rolls her eyes, but she's smiling.
- **Why it matters:** The ordinary. The kind of afternoon you don't remember until you're five thousand miles away and suddenly you can smell the spices.

### Date 14: The Drive-In Movie (Early August)

- An actual drive-in, somewhere outside the city. Georgi borrows his father's car. They park in the back row.
- The movie is beside the point. They sit in the back seat with the windows down and the summer air coming in, and they talk through the entire film.
- It is not like the first-date movie — that was silence and held breath. This is comfortable, known, easy. The difference between the beginning of something and the middle.
- **Why it matters:** Another movie callback, but looser, warmer. The comfort of being three years in.

### Date 15: The Scarborough Bluffs (Mid-August)

- A day trip to the Bluffs — the white cliffs, the turquoise water, the view that doesn't look like it belongs in Toronto.
- They climb down to the beach. The water is cold. She goes in; he stays on the sand and shakes his head at her. *You're missing out.* — *I'm fine.*
- She comes out shivering. He wraps her in a towel. They sit on the sand and watch the sailboats.
- A long silence. Then, quietly: *Two weeks.*
- She doesn't answer. She leans into him. The water was cold. The sun is warm. Two weeks is not enough.
- **Why it matters:** The first spoken countdown. Nature as witness. The stillness before the goodbye.

### Date 16: The Key Family Dinner (Mid-August)

- Mike and Terri Anne host a dinner for Georgi — not a farewell party, just a dinner. Patti Anne is there. Kiley Rose. Fallon Glenda. Maybe Andrea Jennifer. The sisters who have watched this boy since he was fourteen and Ashley Erin's weird new friend.
- It is warm and loud and exactly what the Key family does. Mike doesn't make a speech — Mike Key doesn't make speeches. But he shakes Georgi's hand at the end of the night, and the grip is the same grip he gave him in April, and Georgi understands.
- Terri Anne hugs him — the same full, maternal hug. *You come back to us,* she says. Not a question.
- Ashley Erin watches her family say goodbye to him, and it occurs to her that they are not saying goodbye to her boyfriend. They are saying goodbye to *Georgi,* who is his own person to them, who belongs to this family on his own terms.
- **Why it matters:** The Key family's formal farewell. Georgi as family, not just Ashley Erin's plus-one. Mike's handshake as bookend to the April permission scene.

### Date 17: The Dimitrov Dinner (Mid-August)

- The mirror of the Key dinner. Ashley Erin at the Dimitrov apartment — the same table, the same food, the same slippers on the kitchen floor.
- Dr. Veska doesn't fuss as much this time. She is too sad to fuss. She sits next to Ashley Erin at dinner and holds her hand under the table.
- Dr. Stoyan tells stories about Georgi as a child in Bulgaria — the time he tried to "operate" on a stuffed animal, the time he announced at age six that he would be a doctor like his parents. Georgi is mortified. Ashley Erin memorizes every word.
- After dinner, Dr. Veska gives Ashley Erin something — a small Bulgarian icon, or a piece of jewelry, or a family recipe written in her own hand. Something that says *you are family, and we will see you again, and this is not goodbye.*
- **Why it matters:** The Dimitrovs' formal farewell to Ashley Erin. The gift from Dr. Veska. The stories of Georgi as a child — origins of the nurse he will become.

### Date 18: The First Time Place — Raya's Kitchen (Mid-August)

- Raya's parents are away. Raya invites them over — just the three of them, in the kitchen where they met. It is not a party this time. It is a pilgrimage.
- They sit at the same table. Raya makes the same snacks. The kitchen is smaller than Ashley Erin remembers. Or she is bigger.
- Raya tells the story from her side: how she saw Georgi walk in, jet-lagged and terrified, and knew exactly who to introduce him to. How she watched them find each other and thought: *okay. That's done.* How she's been waiting for them to figure it out ever since.
- *You knew?* — *Everyone knew. You two were the last to know.*
- They stay late. The kitchen is the same. Nothing is the same.
- **Why it matters:** The origin point revisited. Raya closes the loop on her role as matchmaker. Full circle — from meeting to goodbye in the same room.

### Date 19: The Hotel — Again (Late August, a few days before departure)

- They book the same hotel. Not the same room — they don't need it to be the same room. But the same hotel, the same quiet street, the same night clerk who doesn't recognize her.
- This time there is one bed. They don't discuss it. It's just the room they booked.
- They do not reenact the first time. That night was its own thing — the nerves, the newness, the tears. Tonight is different. Tonight is the last time before the ocean. Tonight is not discovery; it is memorization.
- They are slower. Quieter. Every touch says *remember this.*
- Afterward, they do not order room service. They lie in the dark and talk — about London, about her senior year, about the visits they will plan and the calls they will schedule and the life they are going to build when he comes back.
- *This is not the end of us,* she says. — *No.* — *Promise.* — *Promise.*
- **Why it matters:** The hotel callback. The arc from first time to last time before London. The shift from discovery to memorization. The promise.

### Date 20: The Last Day — The Airport Eve (The day before departure)

- They do not plan anything. They have been planning for eight weeks. The last day is for whatever happens.
- They end up at the Key mansion, in her room, doing nothing. She plays guitar. He reads. They order pizza. They watch a movie on her laptop. They fall asleep on her bed, fully dressed, the way they used to when they were younger and didn't know what they were doing.
- Mike and Terri Anne do not disturb them. The mansion is quiet. The clock is ticking.
- At some point in the night, she wakes up and he is already awake, watching her. *Creepy,* she says. — *You love it.* — The slow, unguarded smile. *Yeah. I do.*
- The same exchange as the morning after the hotel. But now it means something else — not *we just did this for the first time* but *we are about to be apart for the longest time.*
- **Why it matters:** The last ordinary day. The inversion of the hotel morning-after — now the *creepy/you love it* exchange is loaded with the coming goodbye. The Key mansion as the last place they fall asleep together before London.

## THE AFTER: THE TWENTY DATES IN RETROSPECT

- These twenty dates are the bridge between the hotel (June) and the airport (late August).
- When Georgi is in London — when the time difference is five hours and the calls are scheduled and she is sleeping alone — she will go back through these eight weeks like a rosary. The islands. The rooftop. The Bluffs. The sick day. The second hotel. Twenty beads on a string.
- He will do the same. In a London flat, in a city that doesn't know him yet, he will close his eyes and remember the CN Tower, the drive-in, the night she fed him soup. The photo-booth strip pinned to his corkboard. The Bulgarian-English dictionary on her nightstand, five thousand miles away.

---

## WHY THIS SUMMER MATTERS

- **It is the last sustained stretch of ordinary life before London.** After August, everything changes — the ocean, the time difference, the years of long distance. These eight weeks are the last time their relationship exists in the same city, the same rhythm, the same easy access. The reader should feel the sweetness and the shadow in equal measure.
- **It deepens the physical relationship beyond the first time.** The second hotel is not a reenactment — it's an evolution. From discovery to memorization. From *what are we doing* to *remember this.*
- **It pays off every callback available:** the first-date movie (date 10), the aquarium (date 4), the bookstore (date 8), the sick day (date 12), the hotel (date 19), the winter-formal alcove banter (the *creepy/you love it* on date 20). The saga is built on echoes, and this summer is an echo chamber.
- **It integrates both families into the goodbye.** The Key dinner (date 16) and the Dimitrov dinner (date 17) bookend each other — his family and hers, both saying farewell, both claiming the other's child as their own.
- **Raya closes her loop.** The matchmaker who started it all gets to see the arc complete — from kitchen at thirteen to kitchen at seventeen, with the whole love story in between.
- **It creates the emotional storehouse for the long-distance arc.** When they are apart, these twenty dates are what they will draw on. The reader who remembers the islands, the Bluffs, the second hotel will understand exactly what is being survived.

---

## STRUCTURAL NOTES

- Not all twenty dates need to be full scenes. Some (the islands, the Bluffs, the second hotel, the Raya kitchen, the two family dinners) deserve full treatment. Others (the night market, the Kensington afternoon, the drive-in) can be shorter beats or part of a montage.
- The summer can be written as a continuous sequence with the airport departure as its final scene, or as a standalone "Last Summer" chapter that ends with Georgi walking through security.
- The tone should be warm and sunlit with a growing shadow — the reader knows August 31st is coming, and every beautiful moment is edged with the knowledge that it is finite.
