# SCENE OUTLINE: THE UCC WINTER FORMAL (10th DATE)

**Characters:** Ashley Erin Key (15 — she turned fifteen in October), Georgi Dimitrov (15 — he turned fifteen in April), background presence of UCC students and faculty.
**Setting:** Upper Canada College's winter formal — a school gymnasium or hall decorated for the season, with the particular energy of a private-school dance: awkward, earnest, a bit overdone.
**Timeframe:** **December, five months after their first date.** Georgi is 15, Ashley Erin is 15. They have been together since July — about five months, nine previous dates. This is their tenth date, and the first formal / school dance they attend together as a couple.

---

## THE CONTEXT

By December, Ashley Erin and Georgi have been a named couple for five months. They have had nine dates — a mix of movies, coffee shops, walks, a trip to the aquarium, a day at the CNE, and quiet afternoons at each other's houses. The initial nerves of the first date have settled into something comfortable and known. They are **that couple** among their friends — the one that everyone already thinks of as a unit.

The UCC winter formal is Georgi's school dance, not Ashley Erin's. She is the guest. This matters: for once, she is entering **his world** rather than the other way around. At UCC, she is not the Key heir or the star of *This Land* — she is simply *Georgi's girlfriend*, and for a girl who is rarely just anybody's anything, that ordinariness is its own kind of gift.

**The tenth-date milestone:** Ten dates in five months is a deliberate pace — not rushing, but steady. By the tenth date, they have moved past the surface. They have had their first real argument (and survived it). They have learned each other's moods, silences, and tells. The winter formal is a celebration of that — a marker that says *this is real, this is lasting, this is ours.*

## THE LEAD-UP

### Georgi Asks Her (A Few Weeks Before)
- Georgi asks Ashley Erin to the winter formal in his usual plainspoken way. Not a grand gesture — just: *there's a dance at my school in December. Do you want to go with me?*
- She says yes. He is visibly relieved. She teases him about it.
- Optional beat: He mentions he's never been to a school dance before — in Bulgaria, that wasn't really a thing. She tells him she's never been to one as a guest, only as the person people want photos of. They agree to be each other's firsts.

### The Dress / Preparation
- Ashley Erin does not make a production of the preparation. She picks a dress — something she feels good in, not something for the cameras. Maybe she asks one of her sisters for advice (Kiley Rose or Fallon Glenda — the ones who won't make it weird).
- Georgi rents a suit or borrows one. He is conscious of the cost — his parents are still struggling — but Ashley Erin, who knows him, makes sure the conversation never dwells on it.
- Optional texture: Dr. Veska takes a photo of Georgi in his suit before he leaves. She sends it to the family group chat. Georgi is mortified. Ashley Erin, when she sees it, thinks it's the sweetest thing.

## THE SCENE

### Arrival
- They arrive together — not in a limousine (Georgi would never) but in a car arranged by the Keys. Ashley Erin has deliberately kept it low-key: a nice car, not a spectacle.
- Walking in, Ashley Erin feels the shift immediately: for once, she is not the centre of attention. A few students recognize her, but most are absorbed in their own dates and dresses and nerves. She is, blissfully, **just another date.**
- Georgi, by contrast, is in his element — not because he's popular, but because this is *his* school, *his* turf, and he gets to share it with her. He leads her in with a hand on her back, steady and sure.

### The Dance Floor
- They are not natural dancers. Neither of them is the type to own a dance floor. The early songs are awkward — the swaying, the not-quite-knowing-where-to-put-hands, the laughing at their own incompetence.
- This awkwardness is the point. They are not performing for anyone. They are just two teenagers being bad at dancing together, and it is perfect because it is *theirs.*
- Beat: A slow song comes on. They find their rhythm. It is not smooth, but it is close. Ashley Erin rests her head on Georgi's shoulder. For a minute, she forgets that anyone else is in the room.

### The Punch Bowl / Conversation Beat
- A break from the dance floor. They stand at the edge of the room with cups of bad punch, watching the crowd.
- A UCC student — someone Georgi knows — approaches. The conversation is ordinary: *hey, you're Georgi's girlfriend, right? Cool.* No mention of the show, no mention of the Key name. Ashley Erin realizes with a start that this is the first time she has been introduced as *someone's girlfriend* rather than as *herself.*
- She likes it.

### The Moment
- At some point in the night, they find a quiet corner — a hallway, an alcove, a spot away from the music. The conversation turns reflective.
- Georgi, in his plain way: *I'm glad you came.*
- Ashley Erin: *I'm glad you asked.*
- A beat where they both recognize how far they've come since Raya's kitchen. Not said aloud, but felt.
- Optional: The first *I love you* could live here — or not. If it's too early, the scene should still hold the weight of the unspoken thing. If it feels right, this is a natural place for it: quiet, unobserved, completely theirs.

### The End of the Night
- The dance winds down. They spill out into the cold December air, still warm from the heat of the gym.
- Georgi walks her to the car. They stand there a moment longer than they need to, breath fogging in the cold.
- A kiss — or a near-kiss — that is warmer and more certain than the one on the first date. They know each other better now. The shyness has softened into something else.
- She drives home. He watches the car go. The night is ordinary and monumental in exactly the same measure.

## WHY THIS SCENE MATTERS

- **It shows their relationship five months in.** The reader sees the comfortable ease that has grown since the first date — the inside jokes, the shared silences, the way they move around each other. It is not the nervous first date; it is the steady, happy couple.
- **It flips the power dynamic.** For once, Ashley Erin is the guest in someone else's world. She is not famous here; she is *Georgi's date.* This is a rare and precious thing for her, and the scene should honour that.
- **It grounds the dating timeline.** The first date (July) establishes the relationship; the winter formal (December) shows it thriving. The reader trusts that these two are building something real.
- **It lets Georgi be the host.** At the Key family Christmas, he was the guest navigating her world. Here, he is the one who belongs. He leads, he introduces, he holds the familiar ground. It is good for him, and good for them.
- **It marks the tenth date.** Ten dates in five months is a steady, unhurried pace. The winter formal feels earned — a celebration of a relationship that has been built carefully, one ordinary date at a time.
