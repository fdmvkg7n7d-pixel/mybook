# SCENE OUTLINE: ASHLEY ERIN MEETS GEORGI (RAYA'S BIRTHDAY PARTY)

**Characters:** Ashley Erin Key (13), Georgi Dimitrov (14 — his birthday in April makes him the older of the two by about six months), Raya (— Georgi's cousin, and the actor who plays Ashley Erin's character's *sister* on *This Land*). Background: Raya's parents; Georgi's parents, **Dr. Stoyan Dimitrov** and **Dr. Veska Dimitrova**.
**Setting:** Raya's family home in Toronto — a warm, modest, lived-in house, not a mansion. A backyard or rec-room birthday party for a thirteen-year-old.
**Timeframe:** The year **Georgi's family immigrates to Toronto from Bulgaria.** Roughly twenty years before Ashley Erin and Georgi's first adoption (at thirty-five). This is the origin point of their entire love story.

## THE CONTEXT

Ashley Erin Key — youngest of the eight Key sisters, transgender girl, already working as a teen actor — stars on the television show ***This Land***. Her closest co-star is a girl her own age named **Raya**, who plays her character's **sister** on the show; cast together at ten (via a chemistry read — see the casting-call scene outline), they have become real, off-screen sisters-in-spirit.

Importantly, **Ashley Erin's transgender identity is one of the central points of *This Land*** — she plays a trans character, openly and publicly, in a celebrated, affirming, groundbreaking portrayal. By the time of this party she is therefore **publicly out as trans**: a young actor known and praised for representation. This matters for the scene, because it means Georgi is meeting not just a famous, wealthy girl, but a girl the whole country is already *talking about* — and he treats her like none of that is the point.

Raya has a cousin, **Georgi**, whose family is **moving to Toronto from Bulgaria.** Georgi's parents are both doctors back home; they are uprooting an entire professional life to start over in a new country, and Georgi — fourteen years old, just turned in April — is about to land in a brand-new city, a brand-new school (**Upper Canada College**), and a brand-new language-of-belonging, knowing **not a single person in Ontario.**

So Raya does something small and enormous: for **her own birthday party**, she invites both her cousin and her castmate, with one quiet goal in mind — *Georgi should not arrive in this country knowing no one.* She wants to hand him at least one friend before his first day of school. She is not matchmaking for romance; she is matchmaking for **safety**. The romance is an accident of two good kids in the same kitchen.

Crucially: **Georgi is not an actor and wants nothing to do with show business.** He is the one person at this party who is not impressed by anybody.

## THE SCENE

**Arrival — the outsider:**
*   Georgi arrives at Raya's house jet-lagged and overwhelmed — fresh off the upheaval of the move, still translating the room in his head, hyper-aware that everyone here already knows everyone else. He sticks close to the edges. He is polite, watchful, quiet.
*   The party is full of Raya's friends — including, conspicuously, people from *This Land*. There is a low hum of "industry" in the room that Georgi neither recognizes nor cares about. To him they're just loud teenagers.

**Raya makes the introduction:**
*   Raya pulls Georgi off the wall and brings him straight to Ashley Erin. Her introduction is deliberately stripped of all the things that would normally precede Ashley Erin into a room.
*   She does **not** say *"this is Ashley Erin Key"* (the wealthiest family in the world) and she does **not** say *"this is the girl from the show."* She says something like: *"Georgi — this is my friend Ashley Erin. Ashley Erin — this is my cousin Georgi. He just moved here. He doesn't know anybody yet."*
*   And then — being thirteen and having a party to host — Raya is gone again, off to her other guests. She has done the one thing she came to do.

**The kitchen — two kids who don't need anything from each other:**
*   Ashley Erin and Georgi drift to the quietest corner of the loud house — the kitchen, the back step, the edge of the yard. Somewhere off to the side.
*   What strikes Ashley Erin immediately is what Georgi *doesn't* do. He doesn't clock her surname. He doesn't ask about the show. He doesn't get that flicker people get when they realize who her family is — or that *other* flicker people get when they realize she's the trans girl from *This Land* they've read about. He has no idea he's supposed to be impressed, or careful, or curious-in-that-way, and so he simply… talks to her. Asks her actual questions. Listens to the answers.
*   For Ashley Erin — who lives at the intersection of immense fame, immense wealth, and a very public trans identity, and who has already learned how people perform around all three — this is startling and disarming. **To Georgi she is just the friendly girl Raya wanted him to meet.** (Whether Georgi already vaguely knows she's trans from Raya, or simply never treats it as a topic, the effect is the same: it is a non-issue to him, and that is exactly the point.)
*   For Georgi, Ashley Erin is the first person in this entire country to make him feel like less of a stranger. She asks him about Bulgaria. About what he left. About his parents. She doesn't flinch at his accent or his unfamiliarity; she's curious, not condescending.

**The honest, unglamorous details:**
*   Their conversation is small and real, not cinematic. Georgi tells her his parents are doctors back home — and that here, they're starting over, that it's complicated, that nobody seems to count what they already did. (An early, unguarded admission of the **re-credentialing struggle** — said plainly, without self-pity, the way a kid reports a fact he hasn't yet learned to be ashamed of.)
*   He mentions he's starting at UCC and doesn't know a soul. Ashley Erin, without making it a big production, basically appoints herself: *then now you know me.*
*   It is the kind of conversation where time disappears. Two kids discovering that the other one is easy to talk to.

**The quiet center of the whole saga:**
*   The emotional core of the scene is this: **Georgi sees Ashley Erin as herself** — not as a Key, not as a celebrity, not as the youngest of eight, and (because none of it has come up and none of it matters to him) not as anything other than the person in front of him. He likes *her.*
*   This is the antidote, planted years early, to the wound that **Haden** will later inflict (mocking Ashley Erin — see `Andrea Jennifer/Andrea Jennifer.md`). Long before anyone is ever cruel to her, the saga quietly establishes the boy who never will be. The reader who knows what's coming feels the weight of it; the kids in the kitchen do not.

**Raya checks back in:**
*   Later, Raya circles back through the kitchen and finds the two of them still talking, having migrated through three topics and lost the party entirely. She doesn't make a thing of it. She just notes it — a small, satisfied flicker — and moves on. *Mission accomplished: Georgi has a friend.*
*   (Saga note: this is the seed of Raya's recurring role as "the matchmaker who started it all" — a beat the saga can pay off years later at the wedding, or in making her godmother to one of the five children.)

**The goodbye — keeping in touch:**
*   The party winds down. Georgi's parents — Dr. Stoyan and Dr. Veska Dimitrov(a) — come to collect him, tired from the thousand logistics of immigration, gently warm with their son, exchanging a few careful words with Raya's family. (Optional grace note: a brief, kind beat between the exhausted immigrant parents and the room — they are good people carrying a heavy season.)
*   Before he leaves, Georgi and Ashley Erin make the small, momentous arrangement that kids their age make: they'll talk, they'll hang out, she'll show him around, he'll have someone at school events / on weekends.
*   Neither of them frames it as anything more than *I made a friend today.* But the saga frames it as the first page of a twenty-year story that ends in marriage and five adopted children.

## WHY THIS SCENE MATTERS

*   **It is the origin of the entire Ashley Erin & Georgi relationship** — childhood sweethearts who meet at thirteen and never really leave each other's lives.
*   **It defines who Georgi is to Ashley Erin from the very first minute:** the one person who sees her as herself, outside her fame, her family, and the very public conversation about her being trans. That ordinariness is the whole point of him — and the saga's deliberate counterweight to Haden's later cruelty. The country celebrates her; her sister's boyfriend will later mock her; Georgi, from minute one, just *likes her.*
*   **It introduces Raya** (the actor who plays Ashley Erin's on-screen sister on *This Land*, and Georgi's cousin) as the kind, low-key matchmaker who set everything in motion — a recurring thread the saga can revisit. There's a quiet symmetry the saga can lean on: the girl who plays Ashley Erin's *sister* is the one who brings her the boy she'll marry.
*   **It grounds Georgi's immigrant story** — the move from Bulgaria, his doctor parents facing the re-credentialing struggle, the brand-new start at UCC — and shows how being the outsider is exactly what lets him meet Ashley Erin without the usual distortions of her name.
*   **It keeps the saga's signature contrast intact:** the wealthiest girl in the world and the new-arrival son of two underemployed immigrant doctors, meeting in an ordinary kitchen, with neither one needing anything from the other.
