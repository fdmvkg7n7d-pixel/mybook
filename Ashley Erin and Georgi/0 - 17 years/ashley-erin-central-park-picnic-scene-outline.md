# SCENE OUTLINE: CENTRAL PARK PICNIC (DAY 2)

*Part of the seven-day New York trip. This warm, bonding scene takes place on the morning and early afternoon of Day 2, before the family returns to the Connecticut mansion to prepare for the evening performance of The Lion King.*

---

## CANON BACKDROP & PLACEMENT

- **Day:** Day 2 of the trip
- **Time:** Morning through early afternoon
- **Location:** Central Park, Manhattan
- **Characters Present:**
  - Ashley Erin Key (7)
  - Andrea Jennifer Key (8)
  - Terri Anne Williams-Key
- **Tone:** Very warm, relaxed, and bonding

---

## SCENE PURPOSE & THEMATIC CORE

This scene gives the three of them quiet, private time together in one of New York’s most iconic places. It emphasizes sisterly closeness, maternal care, and the girls’ excitement about the special evening ahead.

---

## SCENE OUTLINE — BEATS & PROSE CUES

### Beat 1 — Arrival in Central Park

The private car drops them off near the southeastern entrance of Central Park. Terri Anne carries a large, elegant picnic basket prepared by the mansion staff. Andrea Jennifer holds Ashley Erin’s hand as they walk into the park.

The morning light filters through the trees. The park feels peaceful and green.

### Beat 2 — Finding the Perfect Spot

They walk for a while before choosing a lovely, slightly secluded spot near a large tree with dappled sunlight. Terri Anne spreads out a thick, soft blanket. Andrea Jennifer helps unpack the basket while Ashley Erin watches the squirrels.

Inside the basket: sandwiches cut into small triangles, fresh fruit, lemonade in a thermos, and a small box of cookies.

### Beat 3 — The Picnic

The three of them sit together on the blanket. Terri Anne pours lemonade while the girls eat. The conversation is light and affectionate.

Andrea Jennifer points out birds and tells Ashley Erin funny stories about the squirrels. Ashley Erin laughs and leans against her sister.

Terri Anne watches them with quiet pride, occasionally brushing a strand of hair from Ashley Erin’s face or adjusting Andrea Jennifer’s collar.

### Beat 4 — Quiet Conversation

At one point, Ashley Erin looks up at her mother and sister and says softly:

> “This is really nice. Just the three of us.”

Terri Anne smiles and replies warmly:

> “It is. Sometimes the best parts of a trip are the quiet moments in between everything else.”

Andrea Jennifer adds, grinning:

> “And we still get to see *The Lion King* tonight in our special dresses.”

Ashley Erin’s eyes light up at the reminder.

### Beat 5 — Heading Back

After a couple of hours, they pack up the basket. Before leaving, they take a short walk along one of the paths so the girls can stretch their legs. They hold hands — Andrea Jennifer on one side of Terri Anne, Ashley Erin on the other.

They return to the car and head back to the Connecticut mansion to rest and prepare for the evening.
