# SCENE OUTLINE: ARRIVING AT THE ONE (SAN FRANCISCO TO LOS ANGELES)

## Placement

**When:** Directly following the Sacred Heart drop-off/airport morning (see [`lysa-mandy-school-dropoff-before-airport-scene-outline.md`](../../Fallon%20Glenda%20and%20Charlie/L%C3%BFsa%20Mandy%20and%20Samantha/0%20-%2017%20years/lysa-mandy-school-dropoff-before-airport-scene-outline.md)), the same day — **a Wednesday**. Fallon and Charlie have just dropped Terri Anne, Andrea Jennifer, and Ashley Erin at San Francisco International Airport following Lÿsa Mandy's school drop-off.
**Where:** A short flight from **San Francisco International Airport (SFO)** to **Los Angeles International Airport (LAX)** aboard one of **Key Corporation's four private planes**, then the drive up into Bel Air, arriving at **The One** — Mike and Terri Anne's mansion, purchased when Patti Anne was fourteen months old (see [`mike-terri-anne-buy-the-one-bel-air-scene-outline.md`](../../Mike%20and%20Terri%20Anne/mike-terri-anne-buy-the-one-bel-air-scene-outline.md)).
**Characters Present:** Terri Anne Williams-Key, Andrea Jennifer, Ashley Erin. Staff at The One (**Evelyn Thorne, the household manager, and Marcus Vance, the security lead**, greeting them). Mike Key is not present for the arrival — he is in New York City visiting **Kiley Rose and Julia**.
**Ages:** Fallon Glenda is 17 (Stanford freshman year); Andrea Jennifer is about 15–16; Ashley Erin is about 13–14, mid-run on *This Land*, returning to Los Angeles for a block of filming.
**Filming Block:** **Fourteen weeks** of *This Land* production. This is not a quick in-and-out trip — tonight is the beginning of a long, settled-in chapter at The One, one that will fold Stanford visits, Sacred Heart holidays, and ordinary weeks of Andrea Jennifer's own life into the rhythm of Ashley Erin's shoot schedule.
**Schooling & Training:** Terri Anne continues homeschooling Andrea Jennifer and Ashley Erin **at The One** for the length of the stay, just as she would at Carlisle — the mansion has a schoolroom set up for exactly this purpose. Terri Anne personally teaches every subject in their curriculum, ensuring their education travels with the family rather than pausing for it. Alongside filming, Ashley Erin is also **taking ballet lessons under Marat Daukayev in Los Angeles** during this block, fit around her shoot schedule.
**Context:** This is a routine leg of the family's ordinary logistics — Ashley Erin's filming schedule pulls Terri Anne and Andrea Jennifer back to Los Angeles from the San Francisco stay where they helped Fallon and Charlie through Stanford Rush Week and Lÿsa Mandy's Sacred Heart start. The One is not a novelty to any of them; it is simply where the family stays in Los Angeles, the way Carlisle is home in Ontario.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene establishes:

- The domestic, unglamorous reality of the family's use of The One: it functions as a **tool-house** for Ashley Erin's filming commitments, not a spectacle.
- The contrast between the ordinary, tender morning in San Francisco (a four-year-old's school routine) and the scale of the Bel Air estate they land back inside a few hours later.
- Andrea Jennifer and Ashley Erin's easy, lived-in familiarity with the house — this is their normal, not a tourist's wonder.
- Ashley Erin's shift from "sister/aunt on a family morning" back into "working actor" as filming resumes.
- Terri Anne's steady, unchanged parenting register whether she is in a four-year-old's classroom or a mansion with a private theater — the house does not change who she is.
- Andrea Jennifer's guitar as a living thread of her ongoing music arc — carried with her, played for family rather than for an audience, and specifically offered as a gift to soften Ashley Erin's pre-filming tension. She has been studying classical guitar for **eight years** at this point, since around age seven or eight, which is why pieces like **Asturias** and **Canción y Danza** are fully under her fingers rather than newly learned. Her taste runs classical: these pieces, plus her own quiet classical-guitar arrangement of **Imagine** (John Lennon).
- The pool at The One as a site of quiet family intimacy rather than spectacle, echoing (without repeating) its earlier significance in the house's founding purchase scene.
- The continuity of the girls' homeschooling wherever the family goes: Terri Anne runs their lessons **at The One** for the length of this fourteen-week stay, exactly as she would in Carlisle, Paris, or Cap d'Agde.
- Ashley Erin's life in Los Angeles as more than just filming: she is also **taking ballet lessons under Marat Daukayev** during this block, one more thread of ordinary structure woven around the show.

---

## Emotional Core

**Two very different scales of home, held by the same steady hands.**

The morning began small: a uniform, a backpack, a school bell. The day ends large: security gates, a motor court, a house the size of a small hotel. Terri Anne, Andrea Jennifer, and Ashley Erin move through both with the same unbothered ease, because the Key family's rule — from `mike-terri-anne-buy-the-one-bel-air-scene-outline.md` — has already been set: *it is only a house if children can be loved in it.* The One passes that test the same way Fallon and Charlie's cramped, half-unpacked San Francisco apartment did: by being a place where the people inside it are known.

---

## Tone

Warm, low-key, a little wry about the scale of the house next to the smallness of the day's earlier goodbye. Grounded rather than awed.

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — The Flight

The flight from **SFO** to **LAX** is short — under two hours — aboard one of the **four private planes in the Key Corporation fleet**, the kind of ordinary-to-them logistics that make this trip no different from any other family errand. Terri Anne uses the time practically: checking Ashley Erin's call sheet for the next day (an **eleven o'clock call**, mercifully civilized compared to some of her earlier shoot days) and running the mental math on the **fourteen-week** production schedule ahead of them — how the San Francisco visits will need to be spaced out, when Andrea Jennifer's own commitments will need to be worked around it, how long a stretch of Los Angeles life this really is. She lets Andrea Jennifer have the window seat because she never gets tired of watching the California coastline unspool beneath the wing.

Ashley Erin is quieter than usual, already downshifting out of "aunt at Sacred Heart drop-off" mode and into "actor going back to set" mode. She reviews a few pages of revised sides on her tablet. Andrea Jennifer notices and doesn't fill the silence; she has watched her younger sister do this enough times to know it is not distance, just preparation.

### Beat 2 — Landing and the Drive Up

They land at LAX's private aviation gate. A Key family driver — familiar, unremarked-upon, part of the furniture of their lives — is waiting right at the gate to meet them. The drive up into Bel Air is quick, and the transition from freeway to the quiet, gated hush of the neighborhood is its own small ritual: windows up, the noise of the city falling away.

Andrea Jennifer cracks the same joke she always makes as the gates of The One come into view: something about how the house still doesn't look real, even though she's been coming here since she was a little girl. Ashley Erin, still half in call-sheet mode, laughs anyway, because the joke isn't really about the house — it's about coming back to something familiar together.

### Beat 3 — Arrival at The One

The car pulls into the motor court. The house's scale registers the way it always does — glass, water visible somewhere past the entry, the sky deck line against the hills — but none of the three women pause to take it in the way a first-time guest would. This is simply where they stay in Los Angeles.

A member of the household staff greets them by name at the door and takes the bags. Terri Anne asks after small, specific things — whether the kitchen has the groceries she requested, whether the outdoor kitchen by the pool is stocked for the evening, whether the schoolroom has been opened up and aired out — treating the enormous house exactly the way she would treat the Carlisle kitchen: as infrastructure for the people currently living inside it. There's no paper script sitting around to lose or forget; Ashley Erin's sides live on her tablet, the same one she was reading from on the flight. There's no need to arrange a production driver either; with an eleven o'clock call, the Keys' own driver will simply take Ashley Erin to the studio in the morning — and, on other days, to her lessons with Marat Daukayev.

### Beat 4 — Settling In

Andrea Jennifer heads for the room that has unofficially become "hers" whenever the family stays at The One — she knows which hallway, which door, without needing to be shown. Before she does anything else, she pulls her guitar case out from where it travels with her, the one she hasn't opened in days because filming and San Francisco have eaten the time for it. 

Ashley Erin goes over her lines once more before dinner, sitting cross-legged on her bed in a room that is, underneath the scale of the house around it, just a teenage girl's room: a mirror ringed with cards and photos. During this time, she makes a quick, focused call to **Raya**. The conversation is a mix of childhood-friend ease and professional shorthand, centered on a specific upcoming scene in *This Land*. They troubleshoot a beat in the script—discussing the emotional pivot of the scene and how their characters' sibling bond should land—before Raya reminds her that "the director is probably already stressing about our chemistry read memories." Before hanging up, Raya shares a piece of news: she's decided to join the same ballet academy as Ashley Erin, meaning they'll be training under Marat Daukayev together. It's an exciting overlap of their professional and personal growth, adding another layer to their shared journey. It's a brief bridge back into her working life, grounding her in the collaboration that defines the show.

Terri Anne checks in on both of them in turn — not hovering, just present — the same way she would in any house the family has ever lived in. On her way past, she glances into the schoolroom the household staff has already opened up: textbooks unpacked, the same curriculum they'd be working through at the Carlisle kitchen table, waiting for morning. Fourteen weeks is long enough that lessons cannot simply stop; they will happen here instead, the way they happened in Paris, in Cap d'Agde, in every other place the family's life has ever taken them. She makes a mental note, too, of Ashley Erin's ballet schedule with Marat Daukayev pinned to the schoolroom corkboard alongside the call sheet — one more thing threaded into the fourteen weeks, alongside filming and lessons.

### Beat 5 — The Swim and Sauna

Before dinner, the three of them head out to the pool to wash off the residue of travel and the tension of the day's transitions. In keeping with the family's natural comfort with their bodies and the private sanctuary of the estate, they swim undressed. The water is a shock of cool clarity against the warm Bel Air air. For Andrea Jennifer and Ashley Erin, this is a ritual of resetting—shedding the clothes of the "outside world" (the airport, the car, the professional expectations) to return to the baseline of being simply daughters and sisters. 

They float and talk in the water, the immense architecture of The One looming around them, but the focus is entirely on the quiet, physical presence of one another. There is no self-consciousness, only the easy, unburdened intimacy of a family that views nudity not as a statement or a spectacle, but as the most honest way to exist within their own walls.

After the swim, they move into the estate's sauna, continuing the ritual of shedding the day's stress in the heavy, enveloping heat. The transition from the cool water to the warm wood and steam further deepens the sense of relaxation and familial bond. It's a space of quiet conversation and shared breath, a final physical and mental cleansing that leaves them feeling grounded and restored. This sequence of shared vulnerability and ease serves as the final emotional bridge before the working rhythms of the fourteen-week block truly begin.

### Beat 6 — The Evening

Dinner is eaten outside, at the pool's permanent patio set — a standing outdoor dining table and an attached outdoor kitchen built right into the pool deck, used exactly this way any time the family is in residence, rather than the grand dining hall, which the family has long since decided is for entertaining, not for a Wednesday night. The water is lit from below, glowing pale blue against the dark, and the Los Angeles evening is warm enough to eat outside in shirtsleeves. Terri Anne, Andrea Jennifer, and Ashley Erin talk about the San Francisco morning: Lÿsa Mandy's questions at the school door, the sound of her teacher's leather shoes, the fact that she "still did it."

As the evening settles, the conversation turns to the sheer scale of the lives they lead—the private jets, the homes in different cities, the professional support for Ashley Erin's career and Andrea Jennifer's music. There is a moment of genuine, unprompted gratitude; both girls take a moment to thank Terri Anne, acknowledging the immense opportunities that she and Mike have created for them. It isn't about the money, but the freedom and the belief their parents have in their individual callings. Terri Anne accepts the thanks with her usual quiet grace, reminding them that the opportunities are only meaningful if they use them to become the best versions of themselves.

Andrea Jennifer has brought her guitar out to the pool with them. Between courses, without much ceremony, she picks it up and plays for Ashley Erin and Terri Anne — not an original, but a small set of the classical pieces she loves and returns to again and again, the fruit of **eight years of study**: **Asturias**, its restless, rippling opening giving way to something more haunted and slow in the middle; then a **Canción y Danza**, the song half and the dance half, the shift between them audible even to non-musicians; and finally, quieter than either, her own classical-guitar arrangement of **Imagine** by **John Lennon**, stripped of its piano and its words, just melody and fingerpicked chords in the pool light. None of it wobbles the way beginner's pieces do — eight years in, her hands simply know where to go. Nothing is rehearsed or performed for an audience — this is just what she plays for the two people in the world she doesn't have to be good in front of. Ashley Erin, who has spent the whole flight and the whole drive half-inside her call sheet, finally softens all the way, leaning back in her deck chair and closing her eyes to listen. Terri Anne watches her two daughters — one playing, one listening — with the pool light moving on the water beside them, and thinks that this, not the house behind them, is the actual reason they keep coming back.

Ashley Erin doesn't rush off to bed the way she would before a pre-dawn call — an eleven o'clock start means there's still a real evening to have together, and the song stretches the evening a little longer than it might have otherwise. Eventually she does run her lines once more before turning in, because the version of her that exists at The One tonight is already, quietly, becoming the version who will be on set tomorrow.

### Beat 7 — The Closing Image

Terri Anne is the last one up. She walks back out past the pool, now dark and still, and thinks, briefly, of the five-day stay years ago with fourteen-month-old Patti Anne — the stay that decided this house was worth keeping. It has held true ever since: the house has never once outgrown the family living in it. Whatever its size, it still only matters because of who is asleep inside it tonight — and, tonight, because of three quiet classical pieces played poolside for an audience of two.

She turns off the last light and goes to bed.
