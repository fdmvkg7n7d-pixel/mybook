# SCENE OUTLINE: ASHLEY ERIN REVIEWS HER FIRST PHOTOGRAPHS WITH VALERIE STERLING

*Direct follow-up to Ashley Erin’s first New York City profile photoshoot at age seven. This scene takes place later the same day (or the next morning) when Valerie Sterling brings the contact sheets and selected images for review with Ashley Erin, Terri Anne, and Andrea Jennifer.*

---

## CANON BACKDROP & PLACEMENT

- **Placement:** Immediately follows the first photoshoot (age seven, ~2043).
- **Location:** A private meeting room at Sterling Management or a quiet corner of the photography studio.
- **Characters Present:**
  - **Ashley Erin Key** (7)
  - **Terri Anne Williams-Key**
  - **Andrea Jennifer Key** (8)
  - **Valerie Sterling**
- **Key Elements:** Six different outfits, makeup application, hair styling, and Ashley Erin’s explicit comfort with changing in front of the photographer.

---

## SCENE PURPOSE & THEMATIC CORE

This scene deepens the trust between Ashley Erin and Valerie while showing Ashley Erin’s growing professional confidence and bodily autonomy. It also reinforces Valerie’s role as a protective, respectful agent who prioritizes the child’s comfort.

By the end of the scene:
1. Valerie presents the photographs from the six outfits.
2. Ashley Erin sees herself professionally photographed for the first time.
3. The makeup and hair styling process is shown as gentle and professional.
4. Ashley Erin openly states she is comfortable being photographed and changing in front of the photographer.
5. She asks Valerie if this is okay / if Valerie minds.
6. Valerie responds with clear affirmation and professional boundaries.

---

## SCENE OUTLINE — BEATS & PROSE CUES

### Beat 1 — The Review Meeting

Valerie Sterling arrives with a large portfolio and a tablet. She spreads out contact sheets and selected prints across a table in the meeting room. Six distinct looks are represented:

1. Simple white button-down shirt and jeans (natural, everyday)
2. Soft pastel sweater dress
3. Tailored blazer with trousers (more structured)
4. Flowy summer dress
5. Classic black turtleneck and skirt
6. Light blue button-up with shorts

Terri Anne and Andrea Jennifer sit on either side of Ashley Erin. Ashley Erin leans forward, eyes wide as she sees the images.

### Beat 2 — Makeup and Hair Styling (Flashback / Description)

Valerie explains the process while they review:

> “We kept everything very light and natural. Just a touch of concealer, a little mascara, and soft lip color. Elena wanted you to still look like you — just polished.”

Ashley Erin touches her own face lightly, remembering.

> “The makeup artist was really nice. She let me watch in the mirror the whole time. And then they brushed my hair out. It felt different — smoother.”

Andrea Jennifer adds proudly:

> “She looked so pretty. Like a real model.”

### Beat 3 — Ashley Erin Speaks About Comfort

Ashley Erin looks up at Valerie seriously.

> “Ms. Sterling… I wanted to tell you something. I was really comfortable the whole time. Getting my picture taken, and even when I had to change outfits. I didn’t mind that the photographer was there. I felt safe.”

She pauses, then continues with quiet confidence:

> “I know some kids might feel shy about changing in front of other people, but I didn’t. I was okay with it. I liked that everyone was professional and nice.”

Valerie listens carefully, giving Ashley Erin her full attention.

### Beat 4 — Ashley Erin Asks the Question

Ashley Erin tilts her head slightly and asks directly, her voice calm and straightforward:

> “Ms. Sterling… does it bother you if I keep changing in front of different people? Like, if there are different photographers or different people on set, and I have to change outfits in front of them — would you mind if I’m comfortable doing that?”

She looks at Valerie with open, honest eyes, genuinely wanting to know the professional boundary.

### Beat 5 — Valerie’s Response

Valerie’s expression softens with clear respect.

> “Ashley, thank you for telling me that. It’s very important that you feel comfortable, and it’s also important that you feel safe enough to say exactly how you feel.”

She continues gently but firmly:

> “You being comfortable is a good thing. It means the team did their job well — they made the environment respectful. And no, I don’t mind at all. What matters is that *you* decide what feels right for you. As long as you’re comfortable and everyone stays professional, that’s what we want.”

Valerie adds one clear boundary:

> “If there ever comes a time when you *don’t* feel comfortable, you can always tell me or your mom, and we’ll stop immediately. No questions asked.”

### Beat 6 — Closing the Review

Ashley Erin nods, visibly relieved and proud.

Terri Anne places a hand on her daughter’s shoulder.

Andrea Jennifer grins and points at one of the photos:

> “This one is my favorite. You look like you’re about to tell someone a secret.”

Ashley Erin laughs softly.

Valerie closes the portfolio gently.

> “These are going into your book today. You did beautifully, Ashley Erin.”
