# SCENE OUTLINE: THE FRIENDSHIP YEAR

**A year of scene beats between Raya's birthday (July — Georgi 14, Ashley Erin 13) and the first date (next July — Georgi 15, Ashley Erin 14).**

---

## OVERVIEW

This year is the **foundation.** Before the romance has a name, before the first date, before anything is spoken — this is the year Ashley Erin and Georgi simply *become each other's person.* The reader sees the friendship grow in real, unglamorous increments: texts, car rides, stolen hours between her filming schedule and his UCC classes, the slow accumulation of inside jokes and shared silences. No single beat is dramatic. The drama is the aggregate — the way a year of small, consistent choices turns two strangers in a kitchen into two people who belong to each other.

**Thematic throughline:** Georgi is the one person who sees Ashley Erin as *herself* — not a Key, not a headline, not a cause. Every beat in this outline should reinforce that. And Ashley Erin is the one person who makes Georgi feel like he belongs in a country where nothing yet fits.

**Structural note:** These are not all full scenes. Some are short beats (a sentence, a moment); some could grow into full scenes. They are presented in loose chronological order across the year.

---

## SUMMER (July–August)
*The first two months. Georgi's family has just arrived. The friendship is brand-new and fragile.*

### 1. The Text That Starts Everything
- The night after the party, one of them texts the other. Small and ordinary: *hey, it's [name].* A confirmation that the connection in the kitchen wasn't just party chemistry.
- Who texts first? Either works — but the content should be low-stakes. A question about something they talked about. A follow-up to a joke. The first thread of what becomes a daily conversation.

### 2. The Tour Guide Beat
- Ashley Erin shows Georgi around Toronto on a summer afternoon — not the tourist spots, but *her* Toronto: the diner where the Key family goes, the park she likes, the record store. She is the expert-for-a-day; he is the grateful outsider.
- Beat: He asks about a landmark. She realizes she's never actually looked at it before — she's lived here her whole life, taken it for granted. Seeing it through his eyes makes it new.
- Optional texture: She points out where she filmed a scene for *This Land.* He hasn't seen the show. She's charmed by this.

### 3. The First Time He Meets a Key Sister
- A casual run-in with one of Ashley Erin's older sisters — likely Kiley Rose or Fallon Glenda, the less-intimidating ones. The sister gives Georgi a once-over, clocks that he's *not* industry, *not* rich-kid-showy, and files that information away.
- Georgi is polite but not nervous. The sister reports back to the family: *Ashley Erin's new friend seems normal.* This is, in Key-family terms, high praise.
- Beat: After the sister leaves, Ashley Erin braces for Georgi to ask about the family wealth/fame. He doesn't. She exhales.

### 4. The Bulgarian Dinner (Optional Full Scene)
- Ashley Erin is invited to the Dimitrov family home for dinner. A small, crowded apartment — nothing like the Key mansion. Dr. Stoyan and Dr. Veska are warm, formal in that immigrant-parent way, proud of their son and nervous about their son's famous friend.
- Culture texture: Bulgarian food (banitsa, shopska salad, something grilled). The parents speak Bulgarian to each other and careful English to Ashley Erin. Georgi translates the odd word, embarrassed but fond.
- Meaning: Ashley Erin sees where Georgi comes from — the modest home, the hardworking doctor parents who left everything for him. She loves him more for it, not less.

### 5. The First Time He Sees Her on Set (Optional Full Scene)
- He visits the *This Land* set once, out of curiosity. Watches her work. Sees the transformation — the nervous kid from the diner becomes a focused, professional actor.
- Afterward, he tells her something honest: *you're good.* Not *you're famous,* not *I saw you on TV.* Just: she's good at what she does. This means more to her than any review.

## FALL (September–November)
*School starts. The friendship deepens from convenience to genuine closeness.*

### 6. The First Day of School Check-In
- Georgi starts at UCC. Ashley Erin texts him before school — a good-luck message. He texts her after — a report on how it went (fine, but he didn't know anyone).
- Beat: She offers to hang out that weekend. He accepts. The friendship is becoming a routine.

### 7. The Car-Pool / Driver Beat
- Mike and Terri Anne's driver (or a family assistant) drops Ashley Erin at UCC events or picks her up near Georgi's neighbourhood. A running background detail: the wealthiest family's car in the modest part of town, and Ashley Erin in the back seat talking to Georgi on the phone.
- Optional: One time the driver makes a casual comment — *he's the Bulgarian boy, right? He seems nice* — and Ashley Erin realizes her family is already keeping tabs. She's not annoyed. It's just the reality of being a Key.

### 8. The *This Land* Airing Beat
- The show premieres or a new season airs. Ashley Erin is nervous — not about reviews, but about *people she knows* watching her play a version of her own experience. She texts Georgi: *don't watch it.* He texts back: *too late.*
- He watched. He thought it was good. He doesn't make it weird. That's the whole beat.

### 9. The Homework / Quiet Hangout
- They do homework together in a coffee shop or at the Dimitrov kitchen table. No drama. Just two teenagers complaining about assignments, sharing earbuds, existing in the same space.
- A beat where Ashley Erin realizes she's happiest when nothing is happening — just *being* with him.

### 10. The First Major Fight / Disagreement
- A small, realistic conflict between them — not a romance fight, a *friendship* fight. Maybe he cancels plans because his parents need help with something immigration-related. Maybe she's tired and short with him after a long day on set. Maybe there's a misunderstanding over a text.
- The resolution: an honest apology, no grand gesture. They learn that their friendship can survive small cracks. This becomes important for later, when bigger things hit.

### 11. Halloween Beat
- Ashley Erin's Halloween plans: she's either working (set) or avoiding parties where people will want pictures. Georgi doesn't do Halloween in the Bulgarian way — it's new to him.
- They end up watching a horror movie at his place, not dressed up, eating candy. Dr. Veska checks on them with tea. It's quiet and perfect.

## WINTER (December–February)
*The friendship is now established. They are each other's default person.*

### 12. The Key Family Christmas Event (Optional Full Scene)
- Ashley Erin invites Georgi to a Key family holiday gathering. He meets multiple sisters at once — the full force of seven older Key women. It's overwhelming.
- Patti Anne (the eldest) sizes him up. Andrea Jennifer is warm. Jessica Isabella and Rachel Darcy are curious about whether he's "more than a friend." Mike Key shakes his hand and asks one pointed question — *what do you want to do with your life?* Georgi says *nursing.* Mike nods. That's it. The approval is subtle but real.
- Georgi handles it all with steady, plainspoken grace. The sisters report back: *he's fine. Keep him.*

### 13. The Winter Break Lull
- A stretch of days where neither has obligations. Ashley Erin is off-set for the holidays. Georgi's parents have a rare week off from survival jobs.
- They spend multiple days in a row together — long walks in the cold, a day at the Key mansion pool (she swims; he sits on the edge and reads), an afternoon at the Dimitrov apartment helping Dr. Veska bake.
- The comfort of being around someone without needing to *do* anything.

### 14. The New Year's Eve Beat
- Not at a party. They watch fireworks from somewhere low-key — a rooftop, a park, a window. Neither says *happy new year* dramatically; they just say it.
- A quiet acknowledgment that they've known each other for almost six months, and life is better with the other person in it. Neither says that part out loud.

### 15. The Sick Day Beat — Terri Anne Notices
- Ashley Erin gets sick (a cold, exhaustion from filming). Georgi shows up with soup made by his mother and a DVD. He sits on the end of her couch, not hovering, just present.
- Terri Anne comes in to check on them. She and Georgi exchange a few words. Terri Anne, who has watched eight daughters grow up, recognizes something in the way he looks at Ashley Erin — the quiet steadiness, the way he doesn't perform concern, he just *is* concerned. She says nothing to them. But later, in the kitchen with Mike, she mentions it: *that Bulgarian boy. He's good for her.* Mike grunts in acknowledgment — which, from Mike Key, is a full endorsement.

### 16. The Bullying / UCC Beat
- Georgi faces a small incident at UCC — a comment about his accent, his name, his family's situation. Nothing catastrophic, but the quiet cruelty of teenagers in a private school.
- He doesn't tell Ashley Erin at first. She finds out through Raya (who hears from another cousin). She's furious on his behalf. He shrugs it off — *it's not a big deal.*
- But it *is* a big deal, because Ashley Erin knows exactly what it feels like to be mocked for something you can't change. She doesn't push him to talk. She just makes sure he knows she's on his side. The friendship deepens through shared understanding of what it means to be *the different one.*

---

## SPRING (March–May)
*The year is aging. The friendship is approaching its natural hinge point.*

### 17. Georgi's 15th Birthday (April)
- Ashley Erin plans something small but thoughtful. Not lavish (he'd hate lavish). Maybe a homemade gift, a book she thinks he'd like, a playlist.
- His parents make a Bulgarian celebration dinner. Ashley Erin is included like family. Dr. Veska slips her a piece of birthday cake to take home.
- Beat: Georgi's birthday is in April, Ashley Erin's is in October. In a few months, she'll turn 14 and he'll already be 15 — the gap between them will feel a little wider, a little more real.

### 18. Mike Notices (Spring)
- Mike Key, who is not a hovering father but has a quiet, almost supernatural awareness of his daughters' lives, mentions Georgi in an offhand way to Terri Anne. *That boy's at the house a lot.*
- Terri Anne: *Yes. He is.*
- A beat of silence. Mike: *He good?*
- Terri Anne, with absolute certainty: *He's good.*
- Mike doesn't need to say more. The conversation is over. Georgi has been vetted by the parents — not through an interrogation, but through the simple, unshowy fact that he has spent a year being kind to their youngest daughter, and they have watched.

### 19. The First Time Someone Assumes They're Together
- A stranger or acquaintance sees them together and assumes they're dating. Both of them say *no, we're just friends* — but there's a beat of silence before or after where neither of them is sure that's true anymore.
- The question hangs in the air. Neither picks it up. But it's been asked, and they both heard it.

### 20. The *This Land* Season Wrap Beat
- Ashley Erin's season of *This Land* wraps. There's a small cast party. She brings Georgi. Raya is there — delighted to see her handiwork (the introduction) paying off.
- Raya corners Georgi at some point: *so?* He plays dumb. She rolls her eyes. *Whenever you're ready.*
- Beat: Ashley Erin sees Raya and Georgi talking and feels a sudden, unnamed flutter of… something. She doesn't name it yet either.

### 21. The End-of-School-Year Beat
- They both finish their school years. Georgi has survived his first year in Canada. Ashley Erin has balanced filming and school. They are both tired and both relieved.
- A beat where they sit outside somewhere — a park bench, the edge of a driveway — and just exist in the late-spring warmth. The conversation drifts to summer plans. Neither says it, but both are thinking about what the summer will mean.

---

## EARLY SUMMER (June – early July)
*The final weeks before the first date. The friendship is at full strength. Something is about to change.*

### 22. The *Almost* Beat
- A moment where they almost say something — or almost do something — and pull back. Not a dramatic almost-kiss (save that for the first date). A smaller *almost*: almost says *I don't want to just be your friend,* or almost reaches for a hand, or almost stays a little too long in a hug.
- They both feel it. They both don't say it. But the knowledge that something has shifted is now in the room, and neither of them can un-know it.

### 23. Raya's Next Birthday (July) — The Push
- A second Raya birthday party — one year after the first. Full circle. This time Georgi knows everyone. This time he's not the outsider. This time Ashley Erin is the familiar face in the crowd, not the stranger.
- Raya, the architect of the whole thing, watches them interact all night. They are easier together than anyone else in the room. She pulls Georgi aside and asks, directly: *are you ever going to ask her out?*
- Georgi, cornered and finally ready: *I'm working on it.*
- Raya: *Work faster.*

### 24. Georgi Asks Her Out (Immediately Before the First Date)
- A day or two after Raya's party, Georgi works up the nerve. Not a grand confession — just an ordinary question, delivered with extraordinary courage.
- He finds her alone — maybe on her front step, maybe after a quiet afternoon together — and says it plainly, the way he says everything: *Hey. Do you want to go see a movie? Like — a date?*
- Ashley Erin, who has been waiting for this without letting herself admit she was waiting: *I thought you'd never ask.*

---

## WHY THIS YEAR MATTERS

- **It earns the first date.** When they finally call it a relationship, the reader has watched it grow across a full year of small, real moments. The first date isn't a beginning; it's a naming of something that already exists.
- **It builds the Georgi contrast.** Through this year, the reader sees — scene by scene — that Georgi never once makes Ashley Erin's transness, fame, or wealth into a *thing.* He's not performing acceptance; he just doesn't care about the things that make everyone else treat her differently. This makes Haden's later cruelty land harder.
- **It grounds the immigrant story.** Georgi's first year in Canada is also his first year with Ashley Erin. The two threads are braided together — she is not just his first love, she is his first *friend* in a new country. That gives the relationship a weight it wouldn't have otherwise.
- **It lets Raya be the matchmaker in the background.** She set this in motion. She checks in on it. She gently nudges. Her payoff at the wedding/godmother arc is earned because she's present across this entire year, even in small beats.
- **It shows Mike and Terri Anne watching.** Across the year, both parents independently clock Georgi — Terri Anne through the sick-day visit, Mike through his quiet observation. They don't interfere. They don't interrogate. They just watch and confirm to each other: *he's good.* Their quiet approval is the foundation that makes the first date feel safe as well as thrilling.

## AFTER THE FIRST DATE — THE DATING RUNWAY (July–December)

*Five months, nine dates, and a relationship that settles into something steady and known.*

### A Note on Dates 2–9
These are not all outlined as full scenes, but the reader should feel their accumulation. Between July and December, Ashley Erin and Georgi go on roughly eight more dates — a mix of low-key hangouts and deliberate outings:

- **Date 2:** A walk through the distillery district. They hold hands for the first time since the movie theatre — deliberate, not accidental.
- **Date 3:** She takes him to a recording studio where she's working on a song. He watches her play guitar. It's the first time she's played for anyone, and she's nervous. He listens. That's all.
- **Date 4:** A double-date with Raya and someone she's seeing — a chaotic, loud, fun night that ends with Raya giving them a knowing look that says *I told you so.*
- **Date 5:** A rainy afternoon at the Key mansion. They build a fire, read, barely talk. The comfort of silence.
- **Date 6:** He cooks her Bulgarian food — the kind his mother makes. It is not perfect. She eats it like it is.
- **Date 7:** A night market or street festival — crowded, loud, anonymous. They get lost in the crowd together.
- **Date 8:** A study date that turns into a nap on the couch. They wake up tangled. Neither moves.
- **Date 9:** A real conversation about their futures. She talks about acting, *This Land*, whether she'll do it forever. He talks about nursing, about what it means to care for people the way his parents wanted to. Neither has answers. Both feel safe enough to say *I don't know.*

By the time the winter formal arrives in December, they have done the slow, quiet work of becoming a real couple. The formal is their 10th date — a marker of five months of steady, consistent choosing each other.

## THE NEW YEAR — JANUARY AND FEBRUARY DATES

*After the holidays — three dates in January, two in February, then Valentine's Day.*

### January: Three Dates

**Date 11 — Ice Skating (Early January)**
- A classic winter date. Neither of them is good at skating. They hold hands, stumble, laugh. It is cold and perfect.
- Afterward, hot chocolate somewhere small and warm. Georgi's cheeks are red from the cold. Ashley Erin tells him he looks like a tomato. He tells her she looks like a tomato with better hair.

**Date 12 — The Art Gallery (Mid-January)**
- A quiet afternoon at the Art Gallery of Ontario. They wander without a plan, stopping at whatever catches their eye.
- Georgi pauses at a landscape that reminds him of Bulgaria — not dramatically, just a small, quiet beat. Ashley Erin notices. She doesn't say anything. She just stands next to him.
- They spend twenty minutes in the gift shop arguing about which postcard is funniest. The answer is the one with the angry cat.

**Date 13 — Board Games at the Key Mansion (Late January)**
- A low-key night in. Board games, takeout, the fire going. Mike and Terri Anne are upstairs or out — the house is theirs for the evening.
- Georgi is surprisingly competitive. Ashley Erin is surprisingly bad at the game they're playing. It devolves into laughter and cheating and accusations.
- A quiet moment at the end of the night: the game abandoned, the fire low, the two of them on the floor. No one says *I love you* yet. But it is close.

### February: Two Dates + Valentine's Day

**Date 14 — The Bookstore (Early February)**
- A used bookstore in Toronto. They wander separate aisles and reconvene with books to show each other. Georgi picks up a Bulgarian cookbook. Ashley Erin picks up a collection of poetry she's never heard of.
- They buy each other's picks. They read them aloud to each other on the walk home. This is their love language now.

**Date 15 — The Arcade (Mid-February, a few days before Valentine's)**
- A retro arcade — skee-ball, air hockey, a photo booth. They are aggressively average at every game.
- The photo booth strip: four frames of them making faces, laughing, Georgi mid-eye-roll at one of her jokes. Ashley Erin will keep this in her wallet for years.
- They walk home in the cold, still buzzing from sugar and neon lights. Georgi says, almost casually: *So what are we doing for Valentine's Day?*
- Ashley Erin, who has been planning it for weeks: *I'm cooking you dinner.*

## VALENTINE'S DAY — THE BURNED DINNER

### The Plan
- Ashley Erin wants to do something real. Not a restaurant, not a reservation, not the Key chef. She wants to cook for him — herself — for the first time.
- She has never done this before. She is a Key; there has always been someone to cook. But she wants to give him something that costs nothing but effort.
- She picks a recipe. Something simple, or so she thinks. Pasta, maybe. A sauce. A vegetable. Nothing complicated.

### The Attempt
- She starts early, alone in the Key kitchen. The sisters have been warned to stay away. Mike and Terri Anne are upstairs, giving her space.
- It goes wrong immediately. She misreads a measurement. The pan is too hot. The garlic burns. The sauce separates. She tries to salvage it and makes it worse.
- The kitchen smells like smoke. She is standing over a ruined pan, defeated.

### Terri Anne Saves the Dinner
- Terri Anne comes downstairs — not because she was called, but because a mother always knows. She takes one look at the scene and does not laugh. She does not say *I told you so.*
- She rolls up her sleeves. *Okay. Let's fix this.*
- Together, they start over. Terri Anne guides her — not taking over, but teaching. How to control the heat. When to add the garlic. What a good sauce looks like.
- It is not a perfect meal, but it is edible. More than edible — it is *theirs.* Ashley Erin's first real dinner, saved by her mother, made with her own hands.

### The Dinner
- Georgi arrives. The table is set — not extravagantly, but with care. Candles. The slightly lopsided pasta. A vegetable that is slightly overdone.
- Ashley Erin tells him the truth: *I burned the first one. My mom helped.*
- Georgi, who has never cared about perfection: *It's perfect.*
- He eats it like it is the best thing he has ever had. It is not. But it is made for him, and that is what matters.
- They sit at the table longer than the food lasts. Talking. Laughing. The burned first attempt becomes a story they will tell for the rest of their lives.

### The Small Gift
- Georgi did not buy her anything expensive. He could not. Instead, he brings his guitar — he plays in a band at UCC, but he has never played for her before. A song he wrote or learned, something that says what he cannot yet say in words.
- He plays it for her after dinner. It is not perfect — a missed chord here, a hesitation there — but it is *for her*, and that is everything.
- Ashley Erin, who has been playing guitar for years, who has performed on stages, who knows what good music sounds like — this is the best performance she has ever heard. She knew he played in a band, but hearing him play for *her* is different.
- She gives him the playlist she made, the one she has been adding to all year. The songs that remind her of their first year together.
- Neither gift costs money. Both are priceless.

---

## WHY THIS RUN MATTERS

- **It shows them in different seasons.** The winter dates — skating, galleries, bookstores, arcades — are different from the summer dates. They are learning to be together in the cold, in the dark, in the quiet.
- **It deepens their domestic life.** The board games, the cooking, the fire — they are building a shared interior world, not just going on outings.
- **The burned dinner is a turning point.** Ashley Erin tries to do something she has never done, fails, and learns that failure is not the end. Georgi loves her anyway. Terri Anne is there to help without judgment. It is the first time Ashley Erin has cooked for anyone she loves, and it is imperfect, and that is exactly right.
- **It sets the Valentine's Day rhythm.** From here on, Valentine's Day is not about expensive gifts. It is about effort, imperfection, and the story they will tell.
