# SCENE OUTLINE: THE LION KING EVENING PERFORMANCE & MEETING THE CAST (DAY 2)

*Part of the seven-day New York trip. This warm, bonding scene takes place on the evening of Day 2. After the Central Park picnic, the family returns to the Connecticut mansion to rest and prepare before attending the evening performance of The Lion King. Thanks to Mike Key’s powerful connections, they have excellent seats and backstage access to meet the cast after the show.*

---

## CANON BACKDROP & PLACEMENT

- **Day:** Day 2 of the trip
- **Time:** Evening performance
- **Location:** Minskoff Theatre, Broadway
- **Show:** *The Lion King*
- **Characters Present:**
  - Ashley Erin Key (7)
  - Andrea Jennifer Key (8)
  - Terri Anne Williams-Key
- **Tone:** Very warm, excited, and bonding
- **Special Elements:** The girls wear their very special dresses. Backstage meet-and-greet arranged through Mike’s connections.

---

## SCENE PURPOSE & THEMATIC CORE

This scene captures a magical, once-in-a-lifetime experience for the two young sisters. It emphasizes family joy, the excitement of Broadway, and the warmth of the evening as a special treat arranged by their father’s influence.

---

## SCENE OUTLINE — BEATS & PROSE CUES

### Beat 1 — Getting Ready at the Mansion

Back at the Connecticut mansion, the afternoon is spent resting and preparing. Terri Anne helps both girls get ready in their very special dresses — elegant, age-appropriate formal dresses chosen specifically for this occasion.

Ashley Erin’s dress is a soft, shimmering gold with delicate details. Andrea Jennifer’s is a rich emerald green. Both girls are excited and a little nervous.

Terri Anne takes photos of them together in the grand foyer before they leave.

### Beat 2 — Arrival at the Theatre

The private car drops them off at the Minskoff Theatre. The evening lights of Broadway are glowing. Because of Mike’s connections, they are escorted directly to their seats — excellent orchestra seats with a clear view of the stage.

The theatre is buzzing with energy. Ashley Erin and Andrea Jennifer hold hands as they walk down the aisle, their eyes wide with wonder.

### Beat 3 — The Performance

The lights dim and the show begins. The girls are completely captivated by the music, costumes, and spectacle of *The Lion King*. 

During “Circle of Life,” Ashley Erin leans forward in her seat, completely enchanted. Andrea Jennifer squeezes her hand during emotional moments. Terri Anne watches both of her daughters with a soft, proud smile.

Throughout the performance, the girls occasionally whisper to each other or point at something amazing on stage, but they remain respectful and fully immersed.

### Beat 4 — Backstage Meet-and-Greet

After the final curtain call, a stage manager escorts the three of them backstage. Because of Mike Key’s connections, they are given special access to meet several cast members.

They meet the actors playing Simba, Nala, and Rafiki. The performers are warm and kind, crouching down to the girls’ level. One actress compliments their beautiful dresses. Another asks them which song they liked best.

Ashley Erin is a little shy at first but lights up when the actor playing Simba tells her she has a very pretty smile. Andrea Jennifer proudly introduces herself and her sister.

Terri Anne stays close, gently guiding the girls and thanking the cast members.

### Beat 5 — The Ride Home

On the car ride back to the Connecticut mansion, the girls are still buzzing with excitement. They talk about their favorite parts of the show, the costumes, and how nice the cast members were.

Ashley Erin rests her head on Terri Anne’s arm and says quietly:

> “Tonight was the best night ever.”

Terri Anne kisses the top of her head.

Andrea Jennifer adds with a big smile:

> “I can’t believe we got to meet them. Dad is the best.”

They return to the mansion tired but happy, still in their special dresses.
