## BEFORE THE SCENE — THE TRIP

She is nervous. She is ready.
- **Mike looks at his daughter.** He sees her excitement. He sees her nervousness. He sees the young woman she is becoming — the model, the actress, the person who is stepping into the world on her own terms. He is proud. He is protective. He is here.

- **She models.** She moves through the snow, through the cold, through the high altitude. She poses. She walks. She turns. She looks at the camera. She is a model. She is doing what she was born to do. She is in the Andes, in the winter, in the snow, and she is beautiful, and she is professional, and she is becoming the person she is meant to be.
- **Mike watches.** He stands off to the side, in his own coat, in the cold, watching his daughter work. He watches her become someone else in front of the camera — someone confident, someone powerful, someone who knows exactly who she is. He watches her and he is proud. He watches her and he is grateful. He watches her and he thinks about how far she has come — from the little girl who was assigned male at birth, from the child who knew she was different, from the teenager who transitioned, to the young woman who is now modeling winter clothes in the Andes mountains of Chile. - **Dialogue cue (Photographer, to Ashley Erin):** "That's it. That's the one. You were incredible today."
