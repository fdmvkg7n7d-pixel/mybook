# SCENE OUTLINE: THE CASTING CALLS (ASHLEY ERIN AUDITIONS FOR *THIS LAND*)

**Characters:** Ashley Erin Key (10); roughly **fifty transgender girls** at the first call; **Raya** (also ~10, for the final call — already cast as the role of Ashley Erin's character's *sister*); Mike Key and Terri Anne Key (parents, in the waiting room); the *This Land* **executive producer** and **casting manager**.
**Setting:** A casting studio / audition space in Toronto — a waiting area full of girls and parents, and an audition room beyond a closed door.
**Timeframe:** **Ashley Erin is ten years old.** This is roughly three years *before* she meets Georgi at thirteen — by which point she is an established star on *This Land*. This is the **origin of her acting career.**

## THE CONTEXT

***This Land*** is being cast, and the production is doing something deliberate and rare: for its central role — a transgender girl — it has put out an **open casting call specifically for transgender girls.** It will not cast a cisgender actor in the part. The search runs as **three casting calls** — three rounds, narrowing the field each time:

1. **First call — the open audition:** roughly **fifty trans girls**, all around the same age, all reading for the same part.
2. **Second call — the shortlist:** the field is cut down; deeper reads, more of the creative team in the room.
3. **Third call — the final / chemistry read:** down to the last contenders, paired with an already-cast actor to test how they play together on screen.

For ten-year-old **Ashley Erin Key**, this is enormous on two levels at once:
*   It is her **first real audition process** — the doorway to everything her acting life will become.
*   And the first call is the first time she has ever been in a **room full of girls like her.** She has grown up the deeply loved youngest of eight sisters in a family that has never once made her transness a problem — but she has never been one of *fifty*. The casting call is, accidentally, the first time she sees how many of her there are in the world.

She is **very nervous.** That nervousness — and how she carries it through three rounds — is the spine of the scene.

## THE SCENE

### CALL ONE — The open audition (fifty girls)

*   Ashley Erin and her parents arrive to a packed waiting area: fifty girls, fifty sets of nerves, fifty families. Sides clutched in small hands; lips moving over lines; a few girls pacing, a few too quiet.
*   The first thing that hits Ashley Erin isn't the competition — it's the **recognition.** She has never been in a room where *everyone* is like her. For a beat it steadies her (*I'm not the only one*); for another it terrifies her (*they're all so good — what makes me special?*).
*   **Mike and Terri Anne — the right kind of parents.** They are *not* stage parents. The wealthiest man in the world sits in a folding chair because his ten-year-old wants to act, and his only job today is to keep her steady. Mike kneels to her level and gives her the thing she actually needs — not *"you'll get it"* (a promise he can't keep), but the truer thing: that whatever happens behind that door, she is already exactly who she is supposed to be, and no casting director can give her that or take it away. Terri Anne does the practical mothering — water, a hand on the back, the lines one more quiet time.
*   Ashley Erin goes in, reads, and — once she's *being someone* — the nerves convert to focus. She is good not because she's polished but because she's **honest**, and the role is one she doesn't have to reach for.
*   She makes the first cut.

### CALL TWO — The shortlist

*   A smaller waiting room, fewer girls, a heavier quiet — the ones who are left are the real contenders, and everyone knows it.
*   More of the creative team is in the room now. The read is deeper: a real scene, maybe a redirect ("again, but try it like ___"), the first test of whether she can take direction, not just deliver lines. Ten-year-old Ashley Erin, nervous but increasingly hooked on the work, rises to it.
*   Mike and Terri Anne are again in the waiting room, not the audition room — present, not pushing.
*   She makes the second cut. Now it's down to the final few.

### CALL THREE — The final (chemistry read with Raya)

*   The third call is different. This time the room is **just two people from the production: the executive producer and the casting manager.** No big panel, no crowd. It's the closest, most decisive room — and the smallest.
*   And this time Ashley Erin is **not reading alone.** Waiting in the room is **Raya** — another girl their age who has **already been cast as Ashley Erin's character's *sister*** on *This Land*. The final test is a **chemistry read**: the producers need to see whether these two play like sisters on screen.
    *   *(Real-world note: Raya is also Georgi's cousin — but Ashley Erin doesn't know that yet, and won't for three more years. Here they are simply two kids being asked to pretend to be sisters. See the meeting-Georgi scene outline.)*
*   The scene-within-the-scene: the two girls read a sibling scene together. Whatever nerves Ashley Erin walked in with, something clicks between her and Raya almost immediately — the easy, bickering, fiercely loyal shorthand of sisters. The executive producer and casting manager watch it land. Ashley Erin, who has seven real sisters, knows *exactly* how to be one; Raya matches her. They are, instantly, believable.
*   This is the moment the role is really won — not on a monologue, but on the spark between two ten-year-olds who will, in fact, become real lifelong friends.

### THE OUTCOME

*   Ashley Erin comes out to her parents not knowing how it went (you never do). The relief of *it's over*; the agony of *now we wait.*
*   The verdict comes later (off-page or in a short coda): **she books it.** Out of the fifty who started, after three calls, the role is hers — and *This Land* begins, with **Raya cast as her on-screen sister.**

## WHY THIS SCENE MATTERS

*   **It is the origin of Ashley Erin's entire acting career** — the three-round audition that lands her *This Land*, the show that, three years later, puts her in the room where she meets Georgi (see `ashley-erin-meets-georgi-at-rayas-birthday-scene-outline.md`).
*   **It establishes the Ashley Erin–Raya bond at its root:** they are cast as *sisters*, and the chemistry read is literally what wins Ashley Erin the part. Their real friendship grows out of playing siblings — which makes Raya, years later, exactly the kind of person who would make sure her cousin Georgi met Ashley Erin.
*   **It shows the casting as principled:** *This Land* sought a real trans girl for a trans role, which is *why* the first waiting room holds fifty trans girls — and why Ashley Erin's eventual, celebrated portrayal carries weight.
*   **It gives Ashley Erin a first encounter with community** — the first time she's one of many, not the only one.
*   **It reinforces the Key family ethic** through Mike and Terri Anne: not stage parents, just present ones; the same truth-and-safety love that runs through the whole saga, here in miniature — and, in hindsight, the memory that Mike was there in a folding chair for her very first audition carries weight after his later death.
