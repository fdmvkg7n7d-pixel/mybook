# SCENE OUTLINE: ASHLEY ERIN & GEORGI'S FIRST CHRISTMAS AND NEW YEAR'S AS A COUPLE

**Characters:** Ashley Erin Key (15), Georgi Dimitrov (15), Terri Anne Williams-Key, Mike Key, the seven Key sisters (varying degrees of presence), Dr. Stoyan Dimitrov and Dr. Veska Dimitrova (referenced).
**Setting:** The Key family mansion — decorated for the holidays, the full Key Christmas experience.
**Timeframe:** **Christmas Eve, late December, about two weeks after the UCC winter formal.** Georgi and Ashley Erin have been together for about five and a half months. This is their first Christmas as a couple — and their first time sharing a holiday together.

---

## THE CONTEXT

By Christmas, Georgi and Ashley Erin have been a couple for nearly six months. They have had ten dates — including the milestone winter formal — and have settled into the easy rhythm of people who know each other deeply. They have survived their first fight, learned each other's silences, and become *that couple* in both their worlds.

But Christmas means family. And this year, there is a quiet, unspoken understanding: **Ashley Erin stays with Mike and Terri Anne on Christmas Day.** She is fifteen, she is the youngest, and she is not ready to leave her parents on Christmas morning. Not yet. Maybe not ever.

So they share **Christmas Eve** — Georgi comes to the Key mansion for the evening, participates in the Key family traditions, and then goes home to his own family for Christmas Day. It is not both families — it is just **Christmas Eve together**, and that is enough.

---

## THE LEAD-UP

### The Question of Plans (Mid-December)
- A conversation in the days after the winter formal: *what are we doing for Christmas?*
- Ashley Erin: *I want to spend Christmas Day with my parents. I'm sorry.*
- Georgi, without hesitation: *Don't be sorry. Of course you do.*
- The resolution: Christmas Eve together at the Key mansion. Georgi goes to the Dimitrov apartment on Christmas morning. They will miss each other on the day, but they will have the night before.

### The Gift Struggle
- Georgi has no money for an extravagant gift. This is a source of quiet anxiety for him — not because Ashley Erin would care, but because he *wants* to give her something that shows how much she means to him.
- He settles on something handmade or deeply personal — a Bulgarian tradition, a letter, a playlist of songs that remind him of their year together.
- Ashley Erin, who knows him, keeps her gift to him simple and thoughtful — not expensive, but carefully chosen. A book he mentioned once. A sweater in his favourite colour. Something that says *I listen to you.*

---

## CHRISTMAS EVE — THE KEY FAMILY MANSION

### Arrival
- The Key mansion is transformed for Christmas — trees in multiple rooms, garlands, the smell of Terri Anne's signature candles. It is warm, loud, and a little overwhelming.
- Georgi has been to the mansion before (the Christmas gathering last year as her friend, plus casual visits), but this time is different: he is not Ashley Erin's *friend* anymore. He is her *boyfriend*, and the sisters notice.
- He arrives with a gift in hand, slightly nervous, immediately folded into the chaos.

### The Key Christmas Traditions
- A few distinctive Key-family traditions: the youngest daughter (Ashley Erin) places the star on the main tree. Mike reads from something — a story, a poem, a small speech. There is a specific holiday dish Terri Anne makes that only appears once a year.
- Georgi watches all of this with quiet attention. He is not a passive observer — he helps, he participates, he lets himself be folded into the noise.
- Beat: One of the sisters (Patti Anne, probably) hands him a glass of something and says: *welcome to the family.* It's said lightly, but it lands heavily. He belongs here now.

### The Whisper Beat
- At some point in the evening, Ashley Erin finds Georgi standing alone for a moment — overwhelmed, not unhappily. She comes up beside him.
- *Too much?*
- Georgi: *No. Just... a lot. Good a lot.*
- She takes his hand. He stays.

### Gift Exchange
- The Key family gift exchange is extravagant — not in a gaudy way, but in the way of a family that has everything and gives generously. Georgi feels the weight of his modest gift for Ashley Erin.
- But when she opens it — the handmade thing, the letter, the playlist — her reaction is genuine and moved. It is the gift she will remember, not the expensive ones.
- Beat: Mike Key gives Georgi a gift. Something practical and kind — a tool, a book, a thing that says *I see you.* Georgi doesn't know what to do with the gesture. Mike just nods. Mike Key does not give gifts to just anyone. Georgi understands what this means.

### The Bulgarian Gift Beat
- Ashley Erin gives Georgi his gift privately, away from the Key-family spectacle. Something personal — the book, the sweater, the thing that says *I listen to you.*
- She also has something for his parents — a small gift she asks him to take home to Dr. Stoyan and Dr. Veska. A bottle of something, a nice throw, a gesture that says *I see them too.*
- Georgi is moved by the thought. He doesn't say much. He doesn't have to.

### The End of the Night
- The evening winds down. The sisters drift off to their own rooms or partners. Mike and Terri Anne settle into the living room. The house is quieter now.
- Georgi and Ashley Erin find a moment alone — a hallway, a couch, the edge of the night. They sit in the quiet, letting the evening settle.
- Georgi, quietly: *I should go. My parents are expecting me in the morning.*
- She nods. She knows. She doesn't want him to leave, but she knows.
- He leaves. She watches him go. The Key mansion feels a little bigger without him.

## CHRISTMAS DAY

### The Two Christmases
- **Morning — The Key Mansion:** Ashley Erin wakes up in her childhood home. She has Christmas morning with Mike and Terri Anne — the quiet, unshowy version of the Key family Christmas, just the three of them. No sisters, no chaos. Coffee, stockings, the warmth of being the youngest and still, in some ways, their little girl.
- **Morning — The Dimitrov Apartment:** Georgi wakes up to Bulgarian Christmas bread and his mother's cooking. He tells his parents about the night before. Dr. Veska asks about Ashley Erin. Georgi says she's good. She sends her love. Dr. Veska smiles.

### The Missing Beat
- At some point on Christmas Day, Ashley Erin texts Georgi: *Merry Christmas.*
- He texts back: *Merry Christmas. Miss you.*
- She reads it twice. She misses him too.
- But she is where she needs to be. And so is he. That is the shape of their first Christmas.

---

## NEW YEAR'S EVE — TWO FAMILIES TOGETHER

### The Invitation
- Sometime between Christmas and New Year's, Mike and Terri Anne extend an invitation to the Dimitrovs: New Year's Eve at the Key mansion.
- The gesture is deliberate. Mike and Terri Anne have seen how much Georgi means to Ashley Erin. They have vetted him quietly across the year. Now they want to meet his parents properly — not in a rushed pickup or drop-off, but as equals, as family.
- Dr. Stoyan and Dr. Veska are nervous. The Key mansion is not their world. But they accept — because their son loves this girl, and because the Keys have been kind.

### The Arrival
- New Year's Eve. The Dimitrovs arrive at the Key mansion — Dr. Stoyan in his best suit, Dr. Veska carrying a Bulgarian dish she made herself, Georgi caught between pride and embarrassment.
- Mike meets them at the door. He shakes Dr. Stoyan's hand firmly. He thanks Dr. Veska for the dish. No grand speeches. Just the quiet dignity of two fathers recognizing each other.
- Terri Anne welcomes Dr. Veska into the kitchen — the universal language of mothers. Dr. Veska is nervous, but Terri Anne puts her at ease.

### The Parents' Conversation
- Mike and Dr. Stoyan find themselves in the living room with drinks. The conversation is careful at first — work, immigration, the re-credentialing struggle. Mike listens. He asks real questions. He does not perform sympathy; he offers respect.
- Dr. Stoyan, quietly: *We had a good life in Bulgaria. We left so he could have a better one.*
- Mike: *I know what it means to build something for your children.*
- A beat of silence between two very different fathers who understand each other perfectly.
- In the kitchen, Terri Anne and Dr. Veska find their own rhythm — cooking, tasting, the easy exchange of women who have both raised large families in very different circumstances.

### The Kids' Beat
- Ashley Erin and Georgi watch their parents interact from across the room. It is surreal and wonderful.
- Georgi, quietly: *Your parents invited mine.*
- Ashley Erin: *I know.*
- Georgi: *This is... a lot.* Pause. *Good a lot.*
- She takes his hand. They watch their two worlds slowly, awkwardly, beautifully merge.

### The Midnight Countdown
- The whole group — Mike and Terri Anne, Dr. Stoyan and Dr. Veska, the Key sisters who are present, Ashley Erin and Georgi — gathers for the countdown.
- The clock hits midnight. Cheers. Glasses clink. Dr. Veska wipes her eyes. Dr. Stoyan shakes Mike's hand again, longer this time.
- Georgi and Ashley Erin find their moment in the middle of it all — a kiss, warm and certain, surrounded by both families.
- Georgi, against her ear: *Happy new year.*
- Ashley Erin: *Happy new year.*

### The Overnight
- As the night winds down, Mike speaks up: the roads are bad — snow, ice, the kind of December night where driving is a risk. He insists the Dimitrovs stay. Not a suggestion. A quiet, firm offer.
- Dr. Stoyan hesitates. He does not like accepting charity. But Dr. Veska puts a hand on his arm. *Stoyan.* And he nods.
- The Key mansion has more than enough room — guest rooms prepared without fuss, towels laid out, the quiet efficiency of a household that is used to hosting.
- Georgi and Ashley Erin exchange a look. Neither of them expected this. Neither of them is sorry.

### The Morning After
- New Year's Day. The Dimitrovs wake up in the Key mansion — a world away from their small apartment. Dr. Stoyan is the first one up, unsure of the kitchen, until Terri Anne finds him and makes him coffee.
- Breakfast is warm and slightly awkward in the best way. Two families finding their footing over eggs and coffee.
- Dr. Veska, quietly, to Ashley Erin: *Your parents are good people.*
- Before they leave, Mike shakes Dr. Stoyan's hand one more time. No speeches. Just a grip that says *you are welcome here anytime.*

### The Quiet Echo
- Later, when the Dimitrovs have gone home, Georgi texts Ashley Erin: *My dad said your dad is a good man.*
- She texts back: *My dad said the same about yours.*
- They have done something hard — brought two families together — and it worked. The Key mansion and the Dimitrov apartment are not so different after all. Both are full of people who love fiercely.

## THE AFTER

### The Debrief (Boxing Day or Later)
- They see each other again a day or two after Christmas. Not a date — just a walk, a coffee, a chance to compare notes.
- Ashley Erin: *Was it okay? The Key family Christmas?*
- Georgi: *It was a lot.* Pause. *Good a lot.*
- She tells him about Christmas morning with her parents — the quiet version, just the three of them. He listens. He likes knowing that part of her too.
- They talk about next year — not seriously, but as a shape on the horizon. *Maybe next year we figure out Christmas Day too.*
- For now, Christmas Eve together is enough.

## WHY THIS SCENE MATTERS

- **It brings the two families together.** Mike and Terri Anne's New Year's Eve invitation — and their insistence that the Dimitrovs stay overnight rather than drive home on bad roads — is their deliberate, quiet way of saying *we accept him, and we accept them.* The overnight stay makes it real: they are not guests passing through. They belong here.
- **It shows Mike and Terri Anne's quiet approval.** They do not make grand speeches. They do not need to. Inviting the Dimitrovs to the Key mansion for New Year's Eve is the gesture that says everything.
- **It gives the Dimitrovs a place in the Key world.** Dr. Stoyan and Dr. Veska have spent the year feeling like outsiders in a new country. Being welcomed into the Key mansion — by Mike Key himself, who sees another father who built everything for his children — is a small, profound act of belonging.
- **It respects Ashley Erin's place in her family.** She is fifteen. She is the youngest. She is not ready to leave her parents on Christmas morning, and she does not have to. The story honours that — she can be in a serious relationship and still need to be Mike and Terri Anne's daughter on Christmas Day.
- **It deepens Georgi's integration into the Keys.** The sisters accepting him, Mike giving him a gift, the offhand *welcome to the family* — these are small steps toward him becoming a Key in everything but name.
- **It establishes their holiday rhythm.** Christmas Eve together as a couple, Christmas Day apart with their own families, New Year's Eve as two families together. They are learning how to navigate the holidays — close when they can be, trusting when they can't, and building bridges when it matters most.
