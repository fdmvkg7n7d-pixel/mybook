# Jemahr Pierce Kupchenko: Reaction to *The Jungle Girl*

## THE REACTION

Jemahr Pierce — age **9–10** at the time of *The Jungle Girl*'s production and release, identical twin to Mikkel James — does not discover the controversy alone. He is told. Mikkel James reads the headline first and storms in, and Jemahr Pierce hears the words "bad rating" and "exploitation" and feels something drop inside his chest before his mind catches up. He is nine years old. He is a twin. His first job is to match his brother's frequency. His second job is to mean it.

He means it. He means it more than he expected. He is not the first to yell — that is Mikkel James's role, the twin who leads, the one who speaks first. Jemahr Pierce is the one who amplifies. When Mikkel James says "they're wrong," Jemahr Pierce says "they're stupid and they're wrong." When Mikkel James says "they didn't watch it," Jemahr Pierce says "they don't deserve to watch it." He is the echo that adds sharper edges. He is the second voice that makes the first voice feel less alone.

But Jemahr Pierce is also the one who notices things Mikkel James does not. He notices Jordan Lisa's face when she comes home from set — tired, sometimes quiet, sometimes humming the jungle song. He notices that she is smaller than the movie makes her look. He notices that the jungle was hot, that she was stung by something, that there was a day she cried and their mother held her in a canvas chair and no one filmed it. He notices these things and he does not say them. He stores them. He builds a private case file in his mind: *She was safe. She was loved. She was tired but she was happy. I saw it. They did not see it.*

The yelling happens everywhere Mikkel James yells, because Jemahr Pierce does not let his brother stand alone. But Jemahr Pierce's yelling is different. Mikkel James yells because he is angry. Jemahr Pierce yells because he is **frightened** — frightened that if he does not yell loudly enough, the world will believe the lies, and the lies will hurt Jordan Lisa, and he will not be able to stop it. He yells to cover the fear. He yells to build the wall. He yells because he is nine and he has no other tools.

At school, when a teacher gently asks the twins to "stop disrupting class with their opinions about a movie," Jemahr Pierce is the one who says, "It's not a movie. It's my sister." This is the sentence he repeats. He does not say *The Jungle Girl* is good. He does not say the critics are wrong. He says: *It's my sister.* That is the only argument he has. That is the only argument he needs.

The pillow fort is his idea. Mikkel James wants to fight. Jemahr Pierce wants to hide. He suggests the fort not as a retreat but as a **sanctuary** — a place where Jordan Lisa can be the monkey-saver, where they can be the protectors, where the critics do not exist. He finds the best pillows. He arranges the laptop at the exact right angle. He brings juice boxes and Jordan Lisa's stuffed monkey from the set. When they are all inside, he does not yell. He watches his sister's face as she watches herself on the screen, and he memorizes her smile, and he stores it next to the other things he has stored: *She was happy. I saw it. They did not see it.*

## KEY LINES / DIALOGUE BEATS

- *To Mikkel James, after the first headline:* "They're stupid and they're wrong. And if they come near her, I'll — I'll —" (He does not finish. He does not know what he will do. But he means it.)
- *To a teacher who asks them to stop discussing the movie in class:* "It's not a movie. It's my sister."
- *To Jordan Lisa, inside the pillow fort:* "You don't have to watch the jungle part. We can skip to the monkey. The monkey part is the best part. You can skip anything you want."
- *To Ashley Erin, quietly, when she thanks him for being gentle with Jordan Lisa:* "I'm not gentle. I'm just not loud. The fort is loud. It has all the pillows. That's loud enough."
- *To Georgi, when Georgi tries to explain that critics are "doing their job":* "Their job is wrong. Their job should be seeing what she did. She climbed a tree. I can't even climb the rope in gym. Their job is wrong."

## EMOTIONAL BEATS

1. **The twin ignition:** Mikkel James tells him. He does not need to process. He matches frequency immediately. But beneath the matching, he feels the fear — the fear that Jordan Lisa has been hurt and they did not stop it.
2. **The witnessing:** He is the one who remembers the private set moments. He builds a private archive of proof. He does not speak it aloud. He uses it to fuel his certainty.
3. **The amplification:** He yells everywhere Mikkel James yells, but his yelling is different — sharper, more precise, more afraid. He is the one who adds words like "stupid" and "don't deserve." He is the one who makes the anger feel like a fortress.
4. **The sanctuary:** The pillow fort is his design. He builds it with care, with intention, with the knowledge that fighting is only one kind of protection. The other kind is a place where Jordan Lisa can simply be safe and watched and loved.
5. **The quiet watching:** Inside the fort, he does not yell. He watches Jordan Lisa watch herself. He stores her smile. He makes a promise he cannot yet articulate: *I will remember this. I will remember that you were happy. And if anyone ever makes you forget, I will remind you.*
