## TIER 1 — QUESTIONS THAT GATE OTHER SCENES

These are questions where, until they're answered, several downstream scenes can't be written without guessing.

### Mike's death — the moment itself

- [x] **Where does Mike's heart attack happen?** Home, in his study at the mansion, in his reading chair.
- [x] **Who is with him when it happens?** He is alone when the attack begins, but Terri Anne finds him immediately after he collapses and performs CPR.
- [x] **How is he found?** Terri Anne hears his collapse from down the hall. There is a 911 call, a window of CPR, and the OPP arrive with silent sirens out of respect.
- [x] **Who is told first, and in what order?** Terri Anne first; then Patti Anne as the eldest/anchor; then the other seven daughters in birth order. Patti Anne drives to Sabrina Bryna's house to tell her in-person in ASL, preserving the family rule of truth in the child's language.
- [x] **The interval between his death and his funeral** — The 3-5 days between are spent with all eight daughters and their families moving into the mansion. The days are characterized by a heavy physical silence, highlighting Sabrina’s linguistic isolation without Mike to lead ASL. *(See `Mike and Terri Anne/mike-key-death-scene-outline.md`.)*

### Carlota Delicia's HIV — disclosure beyond Keith

- [ ] **When (in what year/her age) is she officially diagnosed?** The diagnosis scene puts it at ~12-13, right after recovery, but the exact sequence (police rescue → flight home → SickKids workup → result) deserves a clearer timeline.
- [ ] **Who, outside the immediate household and Keith, knows?** Specifically:
  - Does **Patti Anne** know? She is Sabrina Bryna's eldest sister and the family anchor.
  - Does **Terri Anne** know? (She is the surviving matriarch.)
  - Do any of the **other six aunts** know?
  - Do any of **Carlota's five half-siblings** know — especially Taylor Raven, who is already frightened of her?
- [ ] **How does Carlota choose what to disclose to Keith's parents (the dentists)?** They will eventually meet her. Does Keith tell them? Does Carlota? Does she choose not to?
- [ ] **Disclosure at UCF / Rosen College / Walt Disney Imagineering** — there is no legal obligation, but the saga should decide whether she ever proactively tells anyone in Orlando.

### Gamaliel and Klára — the pre-cruise disclosure to Patti Anne and Aleksander

- [x] **When and where does Klára have the conversation?** Before the cruise, Klára talks with her psychologist, then with Patti Anne and Aleksander in her bedroom, with Willow on her bed. Frightened that her social worker will remove her, she confesses the relationship.
- [x] **Is Aleksander present for it, or does Patti Anne do it alone first?** Both are present in Klára’s bedroom, providing solid, unified protection.
- [x] **What is Klára's reaction?** Guided by her therapist, she moves from paralyzing fear to active trust. When she is told that because they aren't blood-related she is safe, she experiences profound relief.
- [x] **What is the social worker plan that comes out of that conversation?** Patti Anne and Aleksander handle the social worker directly, ensuring there are no secrets. They invite Gamaliel in for a deep, family-wide conversation, giving their emotional blessing and offering help with their Prague plans. *(See `Patti Anne and Aleksander/Gamaliel Galvin and Klara/pre-cruise-disclosure-scene-outline.md`.)*

### The Prague academy — the financial mechanics

- [ ] **When is the twenty-five million actually transferred?** Three plausible moments:
  1. Soon after the offer (held in trust until Klára turns 18)
  2. At graduation
  3. At the moment the Prague property is purchased
- [ ] **What trust structure is used?** Canadian trust law; cross-border implications for moving funds to the Czech Republic; Klára's foster-child status (which ends at 18) and how that interacts with receiving a large gift.
- [ ] **Does Patti Anne ever ask Terri Anne for any of it?** (No — but the saga should at least know that "no" is the answer.)
- [ ] **Do the other seven daughters ever learn the exact figure?** Or do they only learn that *"Mom gave them something to start with"*?
- [ ] **Is there a parallel gift or arrangement for any of Mike and Terri Anne's other grandchildren?** Twenty-five million is enormous; it matters whether this is unique to Gamaliel and Klára or whether Terri Anne is restructuring her estate generally.

## TIER 3 — QUESTIONS ABOUT THE EIGHT DAUGHTERS' GRIEF

The funeral scene treats each daughter as separately upset. None of those eight griefs have actually been written out as scenes.

- [ ] **Sabrina Bryna** — Although all her sisters are fluent in ASL (due to Mike and Terri Anne's insistence) and surround her with hands in motion during the pre-funeral days, the first weeks and months after his death must still carry a unique linguistic and emotional void. Mike was her original, default ASL partner, the first parent who signed to her simply because he was her father. The daily absence of his hands, his specific signs, and his warm physical presence leaves a quiet space in her world. There is a powerful scene here to explore this transition.
- [ ] **Patti Anne** — the eldest, the social/legal daughter Mike chose. The funeral eulogy implies she keeps it together publicly. What does private Patti Anne look like in the months after? Does her bipolar disorder destabilize?
- [ ] **Jessica Isabella and Rachel Darcy** — the separated twins. They lean into each other during the Mass *the way they did before they were separated.* That body-memory beat deserves its own scene.
- [ ] **Kiley Rose** — already carries the kitchen-accident guilt. Mike was one of the people who told her it wasn't hers. Without him, does that guilt come back?
- [ ] **Fallon Glenda, Andrea Jennifer, Ashley Erin** — the three youngest daughters are sketched less, in part because their families are less developed in the keybook. Each one is owed a moment.
