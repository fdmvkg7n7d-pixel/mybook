### Choosing to Run for Prime Minister (PM) of Canada
- **Concept**: The ultimate political peak within the family's home country as a Key family member or spouse (such as an established MP or a prominent national leader) makes the monumental decision to **run for Prime Minister (PM) of Canada**.
- **Narrative Arc**: Moving from local riding representation to the intense, nation-wide arena of a federal leadership race. The candidate must win their party's leadership and then barnstorm across all ten provinces and three territories—navigating the bilingual French and English media, intense televised debates, national security briefs, and the pressure of a unified national campaign.
- **Plot Points**:
  - The emotional and strategic decision-making process behind closed doors in Vaughan: weighing the immense cost to their family's cherished privacy against their conviction to serve Canada.
  - Barnstorming the country: from the Maritime fishing ports to the prairies of Saskatchewan and the mountains of British Columbia, balancing national policy with the family's deep-rooted Ontario home base.
  - The bilingual national debate nights, facing fierce media scrutiny and political opponents while maintaining their quiet moral dignity, intelligence, and family unity.
- **Themes**: High-level national service, bilingual and multicultural leadership, the sacrifice of private peace for the highest public trust, and the strength of the family unit under national scrutiny.

### Running for Member of Parliament (MP) in the Canadian House of Commons
- **Concept**: A high-stakes, locally grounded campaign in the family's home country. A Key family member (perhaps a Vaughan-based sibling or spouse) launches a campaign to represent their community as a **Member of Parliament (MP) in the Canadian House of Commons** in Ottawa.
- **Narrative Arc**: Navigating the specific, highly interactive structure of Canadian parliamentary politics. The candidate must transition from the private family wealth to a rigorous, door-to-door retail campaign, building trust with local voters in their riding (such as Vaughan-Woodbridge or a neighboring Ontario constituency) while being swept up in the broader currents of a federal election campaign.
- **Plot Points**:
  - Launching the nomination race and the general election campaign in their local riding, organizing campaign offices, and coordinating with party leadership.
  - The tactical realities of "retail politics" in Canada: knocking on doors in Vaughan, attending local multi-cultural festivals, and participating in heated all-candidates debates in suburban Ontario.
  - The balance between local representation and national duty: traveling to Ottawa for parliamentary sessions, caucus meetings, and committee work while keeping the family and home base in Vaughan steady and loved.
- **Themes**: Grassroots democracy, community responsibility, the transition from family privilege to public representation, and serving the nation in the House of Commons.

### Buying Buffalo Central Terminal
- **Concept**: A massive, high-profile acquisition and restoration project taken on by the Key family. They purchase the historic **Buffalo Central Terminal**—the grand, 14-story Art Deco masterpiece built in 1929 by the New York Central Railroad in Buffalo, New York, which sat vacant/degraded for decades.
- **Narrative Arc**: Leveraging the family's unfathomable wealth and influence to fund a complete structural and historical restoration of the terminal. The project transforms the monumental concourse and iconic tower from a symbol of industrial decline into a vibrant, high-tech transportation hub, cultural center, or a stunning corporate/philanthropic estate.
- **Plot Points**:
  - The negotiations and acquisition of the massive property on Buffalo's East Side, working with local restoration groups and historical societies.
  - The architectural and structural challenges of restoring the grand passenger concourse, massive Art Deco details, and the 14-story office tower.
  - The symbolic transition: using their wealth to breathe life back into a monument of the golden age of railroading.
- **Themes**: Restoration, legacy, preserving history, and the power of philanthropic enterprise.

### Restoring the East Wing and the Rose Garden of the White House
- **Concept**: A massive, prestigious historical preservation and design project funded or led by the Key family (such as a multi-million-dollar philanthropic grant from the Key Foundation, or a later-generation architect/designer like Carlota being selected by the White House Historical Association) to coordinate a complete historical and structural restoration of **the East Wing and the iconic Rose Garden of the White House** in Washington, D.C.
- **Narrative Arc**: Navigating the high-stakes, highly secure, and politically sensitive arena of federal preservation. In the saga's lore, during the administration of **President Donald Trump**, both the East Wing and the iconic Rose Garden had undergone controversial, non-historic alterations (including the highly criticized 2020 Rose Garden renovation) that heavily compromised their architectural integrity, natural beauty, and classic Federal-style neoclassical aesthetic. The future restoration project is initiated and overseen in collaboration with **Chloë (known as Chlo0235)**, **Paris**, and **Brayden**, who are currently residing in the White House. This project involves stripping away those modern, non-historic disruptions—including rebuilding the Rose Garden back to its classic, lush JFK-era design (replanting the historic crabapple trees and restoring its rich, natural floral tapestry)—and completely rebuilding the East Wing to restore its pristine, original neoclassical grandeur, while seamlessly blending it with modern security, accessibility, and climate control.
- **Plot Points**:
  - The high-profile announcement in Washington, working alongside **Chloë (Chlo0235)**, **Paris**, **Brayden**, the First Lady, the White House Historical Association, and the Commission of Fine Arts.
  - The architectural and structural challenges of restoring the grand, neoclassical spaces and the historic Family Theater, actively removing the controversial, non-historic changes left behind from the Trump administration under the close coordination of **Paris and Brayden** inside the Executive Mansion.
  - The botanical and landscaping challenge of restoring the Rose Garden, working alongside **Chloë (Chlo0235)** to source historic records, replant the iconic crabapple trees, and recreate the lush, vibrant floral designs of Bunny Mellon's original 1962 layout.
  - The emotional centerpiece: a grand, exclusive opening gala in the restored East Colonnade and the pristine, blooming Rose Garden hosted by **Chloë, Paris, and Brayden**, showcasing the family's dedication to preserving the cultural, architectural, and natural heritage of the nation's capital.
- **Themes**: Historic preservation, national legacy, public service, botanical restoration, and the intersection of family artistry with the highest levels of federal prestige.
