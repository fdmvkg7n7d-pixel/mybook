# Keybook Saga: Medical Encyclopedia

This document tracks the specific medical conditions, disabilities, and rare diagnoses featured in the Key family saga.

## CANCER & ONCOLOGY

### Retinoblastoma (RB)
- **Specialist:** William (SickKids)
- **Affected Character:** Brandi Jessica Key only
- **Unaffected Twin:** Pandora Paige Key never had retinoblastoma. Screening found no tumors or retinal disease, and she required no treatment.
- **Description:** A rare form of eye cancer that almost always affects young children.
- **Genetic Link:** Brandi carries a constitutional pathogenic *RB1* variant that current canon establishes arose after the twins separated embryologically and is not present in Pandora. Brandi’s biological children follow the relevant testing and screening protocol.
- **Treatment:** Brandi required bilateral enucleation because of her aggressive disease.

### Leukemia
- **Affected Characters:** Sun-Young "Liz" Key
- **Description:** Sun-Young is a survivor of childhood Leukemia.
- **Treatment / Care:** She was treated through SickKids oncology care as a child.
- **Survivorship Needs:** Long-term follow-up after treatment, with careful attention to unexplained symptoms, including lumps / swollen lymph nodes, fatigue, bruising, fever, or other possible relapse warning signs.
- **Significance:** Leukemia is the reason Sun-Young was hospitalized at SickKids when she met Taylor Raven. When Sun-Young is ten and William takes her and Taylor Raven to South Korea, **Sun-Young finds a lump / swollen lymph node** during the trip. They are in the girls' bedroom at Sabrina's Seoul apartment. William asks Sun-Young if he can check the lump; Taylor Raven is in the room holding Liz's right hand. The moment is **very emotional**: Sun-Young is very nervous that Sabrina Bryna and William will not want to be her foster parents anymore. After the check, **William hugs Sun-Young to calm her down** while Taylor stays close, and William reassures Sun-Young that he and Sabrina Bryna still want her to be their foster daughter because they love her so much. The scene creates a relapse-scare thread that William takes seriously: he quickly calls one of his colleagues on the **twentieth day of the South Korea trip** and routes the situation into medical follow-up. That night at the mansion, **Devon Smythson has her own bedroom at the mansion and does not need to leave**; **Taylor Raven and Sun-Young sleep together for comfort** after the medical scare. The next morning, **Devon Smythson watches the twins** at Sabrina Bryna and William's mansion while Sabrina Bryna and William take Taylor Raven and Sun-Young to **SickKids / Toronto Sick Children's Hospital**. At SickKids, the ultrasound looks bad, and William and his colleague have a conversation outside the examination room. William tells the family that Sun-Young is required to have surgery sooner than later; his colleague is checking the schedule.

## NEUROLOGICAL & PHYSICAL DISABILITIES

### Cerebral Palsy (CP)
- **Affected Characters:** Sabrina Bryna Key
- **Description:** A group of disorders that affect a person's ability to move and maintain balance and posture.
- **Impact in Saga:** Sabrina's gait is affected, and her physical limitations are often highlighted during high-stress events (like the search for Carlota).

### Turner Syndrome (Mosaic Form)
- **Affected Characters:** Kiley Rose Key
- **Description:** A genetic condition in females caused by a missing or partially missing X chromosome.
- **Impact in Saga:** Kiley Rose faced a high-risk pregnancy with her twins, Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige, as spontaneous pregnancy is rare in women with Turner Syndrome.

## SENSORY DISABILITIES

### Deafness
- **Affected Characters:** Sabrina Bryna Key
- **Language:** American Sign Language (ASL).
- **Theme:** The "Broken Language" arc with Carlota Delicia explores the trauma of losing access to a shared sensory language.

### Blindness
- **Affected Characters:** Brandi Jessica Key
- **Cause:** Aggressive bilateral Retinoblastoma resulting in enucleation at age seven.
- **Adaptive Tools:** Braille, white cane, and her Labernese guide dog, **Burden**.

## CARDIAC

### Massive Myocardial Infarction (Heart Attack)
- **Affected Characters:** **Mike Key** (cause of death)
- **Description:** A massive heart attack — a sudden, large-scale interruption of blood flow to a major portion of the heart muscle, causing rapid tissue death and often immediate cardiac arrest.
- **Significance in Saga & Chronic History:** Mike Key, the patriarch, had managed a chronic heart condition (coronary artery disease and stable angina) for **twenty years**, quietly carrying a small glass vial of **sublingual nitroglycerine pills** in his pocket wherever he went. The family (and especially Terri Anne) had grown accustomed to this routine—it was a known, managed constant, which made his sudden death even more shocking.
- **The Event:** At age sixteen of Gamaliel and Klára, Mike suffers a massive, catastrophic myocardial infarction that nitroglycerine cannot reverse. He dies suddenly—no long illness, no anticipated decline, no chance to say goodbye over weeks. This shapes the whole post-Mike arc: Terri Anne becomes the surviving matriarch overnight; the family is forced to absorb both *Mike is here* and *Mike is gone* in the same breath. *(See `Mike and Terri Anne/Mike Key.md`.)*

## INFECTIOUS DISEASE

### HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus)
- **Affected Characters:** Carlota Delicia Key
- **Cause / Significance in Saga:** Contracted during the **forty-two months** Carlos Reyes exploited Carlota at a sleazy bar in Tijuana. Diagnosed during the medical workup after her recovery at approximately twelve or thirteen years old.
- **Management:** **Antiretroviral therapy (ART)**, started immediately on diagnosis and continued for life. With adherence, her viral load is **undetectable**, which under the medical consensus of **Undetectable = Untransmittable (U=U)** means the virus cannot be transmitted sexually.
- **Care Team:** Coordinated through **William**'s SickKids network and a pediatric/adult infectious-disease specialist team.
- **Disclosure:** Keith knows — Carlota told him on their tenth date along with the rest of her history. Beyond family, disclosure is on her terms; HIV status is legally protected in employment (relevant to her Walt Disney Imagineering career goal).
- **Thematic Role:** One more thing the kidnapping took from her, and one more thing she learns to live with rather than be defined by — a parallel to the broken-ASL arc with Sabrina Bryna.
