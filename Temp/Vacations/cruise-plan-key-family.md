# THE KEY FAMILY CRUISE — PLANNING NOTES

*A family trip for Jessica Isabella, Emma, and the twins (Samira Chaka & Sahara Hannan, age 10).*
*Home base: Halifax, Nova Scotia. Travelers: 4 (2 adults, 2 children age 10).*

> Note: This is a research/planning document, not a booking. Actual fares, dates, and cabins must be confirmed and booked directly with the cruise line or a travel agent. Prices and sailings change constantly.

---

## THE GEOGRAPHY PROBLEM (AND SOLUTION)

Halifax is a port cruise ships **visit**, not one they regularly **depart from**. So the family has two realistic embarkation strategies:

- **Drive or short-fly to Boston** (~11 hr drive / ~1.5 hr flight) — the closest major cruise port. No onward flight needed for the cruise itself.
- **Fly to Florida** (Port Canaveral / Miami / Fort Lauderdale) — more travel, but unlocks Disney and the widest Caribbean choice.

**Passports:** Even on "closed-loop" US sailings where US citizens may not need one, a **Canadian family will need valid passports** for everyone, including the twins. Check expiry dates early — children's passports are shorter-validity.

---

## OPTION 1 — BERMUDA FROM BOSTON (easiest from Halifax)

- **Why it fits:** No flying required; drive to Boston. Ship docks 2–3 days at King's Wharf (Royal Naval Dockyard) so the family isn't rushed — pink-sand beaches, snorkeling, calm water, easy shore time for 10-year-olds.
- **Typical length:** 7 nights round-trip from Boston.
- **Lines/ships seen for 2026:** Norwegian (e.g., *Norwegian Gem*) runs Boston–Bermuda sailings.
- **Best season:** May–October (warm, calm seas).
- **Kid appeal:** Beaches, forts, glass-bottom/snorkel tours; ship kids' club for ages 9–11.

## OPTION 2 — CARIBBEAN / BAHAMAS FROM BOSTON (warm + activity-packed)

- **Why it fits:** Still sails from Boston (no cruise-flight), but goes tropical.
- **Example:** Royal Caribbean *Voyager of the Seas* — 7-night from Boston with stops in the Bahamas, Dominican Republic, US Virgin Islands, and Puerto Rico.
- **Kid appeal:** Big-ship features (rock wall, FlowRider surf simulator, waterslides, Adventure Ocean kids' club). Royal Caribbean is widely rated the best line for tweens.

## OPTION 3 — DISNEY FROM FLORIDA (best for kids, worth the flight)

- **Why it fits:** The gold standard for families with kids. Requires flying to Port Canaveral.
- **Example:** *Disney Magic* — 3-, 4-, 5-, or 7-night Bahamas/Caribbean sailings from Port Canaveral, including Disney's private island **Castaway Cay**.
- **Kid appeal:** Oceaneer Club (ages 3–10 — the twins just make the cutoff), Pirate Night with fireworks at sea, AquaMouse/AquaDuck water rides on newer ships, character meets, Broadway-style shows.
- **Faith note:** Disney and most major lines offer Catholic Mass / interdenominational services on board, especially on Sundays and holidays — a fit for this Catholic family.

## BONUS / IN-CANON OPTION — CANADA & NEW ENGLAND (round-trip Boston, stops in Halifax)

- 7-night Canada & New England sailings (e.g., Royal Caribbean *Brilliance of the Seas*) call at Portland (ME), Saint John (NB), and **Halifax** — Peggy's Cove, the waterfront, fresh seafood.
- A charming (if funny) idea for a Halifax family: sail from Boston and "arrive home from the sea." Best in **September–October for fall foliage**.

## MONEY-SAVERS TO LOOK FOR

- **Kids Sail Free / 3rd & 4th guest free** promotions — Royal Caribbean (kids 12 & under as 3rd+ guest, sailings 4+ nights, blackout dates apply), Norwegian (3rd/4th guest free on select sailings), Holland America, MSC, Carnival. With two 10-year-olds as guests 3 & 4, these can cut the trip cost significantly. Taxes/port fees still apply per child.
- **Book early for 2026 peak weeks** (holidays, Easter, summer) — they sell out and price up.
- **One cabin for four:** Look for family staterooms that sleep 4 (e.g., Royal Caribbean Family Ocean Balcony; Holland America oversized quad staterooms; NCL family cabins).
