# 14-Day Saona Island Vacation Plan

## Best Overall Structure

**Recommended split:**

- **Days 1–7:** Bayahibe / Dominicus
  For Saona Island, beach time, snorkeling, diving, and Cotubanamá National Park.
- **Days 8–10:** La Romana / Casa de Campo area
  For Altos de Chavón, Chavón River, and Catalina Island.
- **Days 11–14:** Punta Cana or Santo Domingo
  Choose Punta Cana for more beach/resort relaxation, or Santo Domingo for culture, history, and food.

If Saona Island is the main goal, stay closest to **Bayahibe** for the first week.

---

## Day 1 — Arrival in the Dominican Republic

**Best airport options:**

- **La Romana Airport** — closest if available.
- **Punta Cana Airport** — most common and convenient for many flights.
- **Santo Domingo Airport** — good if you want to add city/history time.

Transfer to **Bayahibe or Dominicus**.

Spend the afternoon settling in:

- Check into your hotel or resort.
- Walk the beach.
- Have an easy dinner.
- Confirm your Saona Island tour for Day 2 or Day 3.

**Overnight:** Bayahibe/Dominicus

---

## Day 2 — Easy Bayahibe Beach Day

Keep the first full day relaxed.

Do:

- Swim at Bayahibe Beach.
- Walk around Bayahibe village.
- Buy sunscreen, snacks, cash, and anything needed for tours.
- Watch the sunset by the waterfront.

Optional:

- Short snorkeling trip.
- Beach massage.
- Dinner at a local seafood restaurant.

**Overnight:** Bayahibe/Dominicus

---

## Day 3 — Classic Saona Island Tour

Take your first **full-day Saona Island tour**.

Typical stops:

- Speedboat or catamaran from Bayahibe.
- Natural Pool sandbar.
- Starfish viewing — do not touch or remove them from the water.
- Saona beach lunch.
- Swimming, relaxing, and photos.
- Return by catamaran or speedboat.

Choose a **small-group tour** if possible. It will feel less crowded than the big party boats.

**Overnight:** Bayahibe/Dominicus

---

## Day 4 — Recovery Day in Dominicus

After the long Saona day, make this a slow day.

Do:

- Relax at Playa Dominicus.
- Read, swim, and nap.
- Have a long lunch.
- Enjoy your resort or beach club.

Optional:

- Sunset drinks.
- Easy evening walk.

**Overnight:** Bayahibe/Dominicus

---

## Day 5 — Cotubanamá National Park / Caves

Explore the natural area around Bayahibe.

Possible activities:

- Cotubanamá National Park visit.
- Padre Nuestro cave and spring area.
- Short hiking trail.
- Birdwatching or photography.
- Guided eco-tour.

This gives you a break from boats while still staying close to nature.

**Overnight:** Bayahibe/Dominicus

---

## Day 6 — Snorkeling or Diving Day

Bayahibe is a good base for snorkeling and diving.

Options:

- Beginner scuba dive.
- Certified dive trip.
- Reef snorkeling tour.
- Wreck dive if certified.
- Private snorkel boat.

If you do not dive, do a gentle snorkel tour and spend the afternoon on the beach.

**Overnight:** Bayahibe/Dominicus

---

## Day 7 — Second Saona Trip: Mano Juan Focus

For a 14-day trip, it is worth doing Saona twice, but differently.

For the second visit, choose a tour that includes **Mano Juan**, the small village on Saona Island.

Focus on:

- Local village life.
- Sea turtle conservation, depending on availability.
- Quieter beach areas.
- Less crowded parts of the island.

This should feel different from the classic beach/natural-pool route.

**Overnight:** Bayahibe/Dominicus

---

## Day 8 — Transfer to La Romana / Altos de Chavón

Move from Bayahibe/Dominicus to **La Romana or the Casa de Campo area**.

Visit **Altos de Chavón**:

- Cobblestone streets.
- Art galleries.
- Chavón River views.
- Amphitheater.
- Restaurants.

Go in the late afternoon if possible so you can enjoy golden-hour views and dinner there.

**Overnight:** La Romana / Casa de Campo area

---

## Day 9 — Catalina Island Day Trip

Take a day trip to **Catalina Island**.

This is a great contrast to Saona:

- Good snorkeling.
- Clear water.
- Beach time.
- Boat ride from the La Romana area.
- Often a little less intense than the big Saona tours.

Bring:

- Reef-safe sunscreen.
- Snorkel gear if you have your own.
- Waterproof phone case.
- Cash for tips.

**Overnight:** La Romana / Casa de Campo area

---

## Day 10 — Chavón River / Relaxed Resort Day

Choose one option.

### Option A — Chavón River Adventure

Do:

- Boat ride on the Chavón River.
- Kayaking or paddle activity.
- Scenic views and photography.

### Option B — Relaxed Resort Day

If you are staying somewhere nice:

- Pool.
- Spa.
- Beach club.
- Long dinner.

This day is intentionally flexible.

**Overnight:** La Romana / Casa de Campo area

---

## Day 11 — Transfer to Punta Cana or Santo Domingo

Now choose your final base.

### Option 1: Punta Cana

Best if you want:

- All-inclusive resort.
- White-sand beach.
- Easy final days.
- Nightlife or excursions.

### Option 2: Santo Domingo

Best if you want:

- History.
- Museums.
- Colonial architecture.
- Restaurants and city energy.

For a beach-heavy trip, choose **Punta Cana**.
For a more balanced trip, choose **Santo Domingo**.

---

# If You Choose Punta Cana: Days 11–14

## Day 11 — Transfer to Punta Cana + Resort Evening

Arrive, check in, and enjoy the resort.

Do:

- Beach walk.
- Pool.
- Easy dinner.
- Early night.

**Overnight:** Punta Cana

---

## Day 12 — Punta Cana Beach Day

Relax at:

- Bávaro Beach.
- Arena Gorda.
- Juanillo Beach, if accessible.

Optional:

- Spa treatment.
- Catamaran cruise.
- Beach club.

**Overnight:** Punta Cana

---

## Day 13 — Adventure Day

Choose one:

- Scape Park.
- Hoyo Azul cenote.
- Ziplining.
- Buggy tour.
- Indigenous Eyes Ecological Reserve.
- Catamaran/snorkeling tour.

**Overnight:** Punta Cana

---

## Day 14 — Final Morning + Departure

Have breakfast, swim one last time, and transfer to the airport.

If flying from Punta Cana, this is the easiest ending.

---

# If You Choose Santo Domingo: Days 11–14

## Day 11 — Transfer to Santo Domingo

Check into a hotel in or near the **Colonial Zone**.

Evening:

- Walk the Colonial Zone.
- Dinner in a historic courtyard restaurant.

**Overnight:** Santo Domingo

---

## Day 12 — Colonial Zone History Day

Visit:

- Alcázar de Colón.
- Catedral Primada de América.
- Parque Colón.
- Calle Las Damas.
- Museo de las Casas Reales.

Evening:

- Rooftop bar or live music.

**Overnight:** Santo Domingo

---

## Day 13 — Los Tres Ojos + City Food Day

Do:

- Los Tres Ojos National Park.
- Mercado Modelo or local shopping.
- Dominican food tour or café hopping.

Try:

- Mangú.
- Tostones.
- Sancocho.
- Mofongo.
- Fresh tropical juices.

**Overnight:** Santo Domingo

---

## Day 14 — Departure

Breakfast, final walk, and airport transfer.

---

# Recommended Final Itinerary

Best balanced version:

- **Days 1–7:** Bayahibe/Dominicus — main Saona Island base.
- **Days 8–10:** La Romana — Altos de Chavón, Catalina Island, river/resort.
- **Days 11–14:** Santo Domingo — history, food, culture, and a strong contrast after many beach days.

This gives the vacation more depth than only beach resorts.

---

# Suggested Budget for 14 Days

Approximate per person, excluding international flights.

## Budget

- Guesthouses/simple hotels: $50–$90/night.
- Shared tours: $60–$120 each.
- Food/local transport: $30–$60/day.
- Total estimate: **$1,300–$2,200**.

## Mid-Range

- Beach hotels/resorts: $120–$250/night.
- Small-group tours: $100–$180 each.
- Food/transport: $70–$140/day.
- Total estimate: **$2,500–$4,500**.

## Luxury

- High-end resorts/villas: $300–$800+/night.
- Private tours/charters: $400–$1,200+.
- Private transfers, fine dining, and spa.
- Total estimate: **$5,000–$10,000+**.

---

# Key Tips

- Stay in **Bayahibe/Dominicus** for Saona, not Punta Cana, if Saona is the priority.
- Do **two Saona visits**: one classic beach/natural-pool tour, one quieter Mano Juan/local-focused tour.
- Avoid touching starfish.
- Bring cash for tips and small purchases.
- Book private or small-group tours if you dislike crowds.
- Keep rest days between boat days.
- Use private transfers if you want comfort and reliability.

Final rhythm:

**Beach → Saona → Rest → Nature → Snorkel/Dive → Saona again → Culture → Catalina → City or resort finish.**
