# SCENE OUTLINE: MIKE & TERRI ANNE TEACH THE BIBLE — HOMESCHOOLING MORNING

**Characters:**
- **Mike Key**
- **Terri Anne Williams-Key**
- **Patti Anne Key** (~7–8)
- **Jessica Isabella Key** & **Rachel Darcy Key** (~5–6, the identical twins)
- **Kiley Rose Key** (~3–4, with Turner Syndrome)
- **Aldoma** (the nanny, present in the background)

**Setting:** The master bedroom or the kitchen table at the Key house. Morning light. The family is gathered after breakfast, the day's first lesson ready to begin.

**Timeframe:** The middle homeschooling years (~2029–2030). The girls have been homeschooled for several years now. The routine is well-established. Faith is woven into their daily life — not as a separate subject, but as the air they breathe.

---

## SYNOPSIS

This scene shows how Mike and Terri Anne teach the Bible to their daughters as part of their homeschooling — not as a formal lesson with tests and memorization, but as a living conversation. The girls learn scripture through reading, storytelling, and family discussion. This is the soil in which Patti Anne's later recitation of Psalm 91:11 is rooted.

---

## THE SCENE

### Beat 1 — The morning settles into learning

- Breakfast is finished. Aldoma clears the dishes quietly. The kitchen table is wiped clean.
- Terri Anne brings out a large, leather-bound family Bible — worn at the edges, with a ribbon marker. It has been in Mike's family for generations.
- Patti Anne sees it and shifts in her chair, curious. The twins stop their quiet twin-play and look up. Kiley Rose is on Mike's lap, still sticky from breakfast.
- Terri Anne sets the Bible in the center of the table. She does not open it yet. She lets it sit there, a presence.
- Mike says, simply: *"Let's start with what we already know. Who remembers the story of David and Goliath?"*

### Beat 2 — The girls share what they remember

- Patti Anne raises her hand — she has outgrown calling out. *"David was a shepherd boy. Goliath was a giant. David killed him with a sling and a stone because everyone else was too scared."*
- Rachel Darcy adds, almost in the same breath: *"Goliath was a Philistine."*
- Jessica Isabella nods firmly: *"And David trusted God."*
- Kiley Rose, too young to follow the full story, says: *"Giant!"* and holds her arms wide. Mike laughs and hugs her.
- Terri Anne smiles. *"Good. You remember the story. Now let's read it the way God gave it to us."*

### Beat 3 — Terri Anne reads from the Bible itself

- She opens to 1 Samuel 17 and reads aloud. Her voice is steady and warm, not performative. She lets the words speak:
  > *"And the Philistine said unto David, Am I a dog, that thou comest to me with staves? And the Philistine cursed David by his gods."*
- She pauses to explain an unfamiliar word. She does not rush.
- When she reaches the part where David says *"Thou comest to me with a sword, and with a spear, and with a shield: but I come to thee in the name of the LORD of hosts,"* she slows down and lets the weight of it settle.
- The girls are quiet. Even Kiley Rose, sensing the shift, stops fidgeting.

### Beat 4 — Mike connects it to their lives

- Mike takes over after the reading. He speaks to them at the table the way he speaks to them about everything: honestly, without condescension.
- *"David wasn't the strongest. He wasn't the biggest. He wasn't the one the army chose. But he knew something the army forgot: that God was with him. That's why he won."*
- He looks at each girl in turn. *"You will face giants too. Not giants with swords. Giants that look like fear, or loneliness, or people who tell you you're not enough. The question isn't whether you're strong enough. The question is whether you remember who is with you."*
- Patti Anne is listening with the serious attention she has had since she was small. The twins exchange a look — they understand in their twin way. Kiley Rose reaches for the Bible and pats the leather cover. Mike lets her touch it.

### Beat 5 — The twins ask about their bodies

- Rachel Darcy, looking at the Bible, asks softly: *"Does God love us even though we were stuck together?"*
- The room shifts. This is the kind of question that homeschooling makes possible — the kind a child would never ask in a classroom full of strangers.
- Terri Anne answers without hesitation: *"God knit you together in my womb, my love. Every part of you — even the parts the doctors had to separate — was made on purpose. He loves you exactly as you are, and He loves you exactly as you are becoming."*
- Jessica Isabella adds, quieter still: *"Even our scar?"*
- Mike answers this time. *"Especially your scar. It's the place where God's healing showed up. It's not a mark of brokenness. It's a mark of being made whole."*
- The twins are still. Then Rachel Darcy says, barely above a whisper: *"Like David's sling."*
- Mike nods. *"Like David's sling. Something small that God used for something big."*

### Beat 6 — The book of Psalms is introduced

- Terri Anne gently turns the Bible to the center. The ribbon marker falls open at the Psalms.
- *"There's a whole book in here called Psalms. They are songs and prayers written by people who loved God — people who were happy, scared, angry, thankful. Every feeling you've ever had, someone in the Psalms has already said it to God."*
- She reads a verse from Psalm 23 — the one the girls will recognize from bedtime:
  > *"The LORD is my shepherd; I shall not want."*
- The girls join in, reciting from memory. They know this one. They have heard it a hundred times.
- Then Terri Anne reads a verse they do not know yet — Psalm 56:3:
  > *"What time I am afraid, I will trust in thee."*
- She closes the Bible softly. *"That one is short enough for even the littlest among us to remember. Let's practice it together."*
- They say it together, the four girls' voices layering over each other: *"What time I am afraid, I will trust in thee."*
- Kiley Rose, on Mike's lap, says the last three words: *"Trust in thee."* Mike kisses the top of her head.

### Beat 7 — A seed is planted

- Patti Anne, who has been quiet through the Psalm reading, speaks up. She is seven or eight now — old enough to think ahead.
- *"Mommy, can I learn more Psalms? A whole one? By heart?"*
- Terri Anne meets her eyes. *"Yes, my love. You can learn any Psalm you want. We'll work on it together."*
- Patti Anne nods, already deciding which one. (She does not say it aloud yet, but she has already chosen. She has heard Psalm 91 somewhere — perhaps at Mass, perhaps from her grandmother — and the words have lodged in her chest like a seed waiting for the right season to grow.)
- Mike watches his eldest daughter, seeing something in her face that he recognizes but cannot name. He says quietly: *"You let us know which one you pick, okay? We'll learn it with you."*
- Patti Anne smiles — small, certain — and nods.

### Beat 8 — Closing the lesson

- Aldoma appears with a tray of water and small cups. She sets it down and says, in French: *"La Parole de Dieu est douce comme l'eau."* (The Word of God is sweet like water.)
- The girls sip their water. The lesson does not end with a bell or an assignment. It ends naturally, the way all their lessons end: the Bible closes, the chairs push back, and the morning continues.
- Terri Anne catches Mike's eye over the girls' heads. She does not say anything. She does not need to. This is exactly what they promised each other years ago — the table, the truth, the faith woven in. It is happening.

## THEMATIC RESONANCE

- **Faith as air, not a subject:** The Bible is not taught as a textbook. It is read, discussed, and connected to the girls' actual lives — their fears, their bodies, their questions.
- **The table as the classroom:** The same kitchen table where Mike and Terri Anne make business decisions, where the family eats, where hard truths are spoken — it is also where scripture is opened.
- **Homeschooling as protection:** The twins' question about their scar could not have been asked in a school. The family's educational choice creates the safety for these conversations.
- **Patti Anne's Psalm 91:** This scene plants the seed. She chooses a Psalm to memorize. By the time Terri Anne is rescued from the kidnapping, she has learned it — and it becomes the verse she offers her mother.
- **Each child, individually:** Mike and Terri Anne answer each daughter at her own level — Kiley Rose with simple words and touch, the twins with theology about their bodies, Patti Anne with the invitation to choose her own Psalm.
- **The generational Bible:** The old, worn Bible connects the girls to their father's family history. Faith is not new in this house. It has been passed down.

- **Psalm 91:11 in the homecoming scene:** This is where Patti Anne's knowledge of the Psalms begins. When she recites *"For he shall give his angels charge over thee"* to Terri Anne after the kidnapping, she is drawing on what she learned at this table.
