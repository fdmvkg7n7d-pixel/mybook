# SCENE OUTLINE: THE MORNING AFTER — TERRI ANNE WAKES IN MIKE'S ARMS

**Characters:**
- **Terri Anne Williams-Key**
- **Mike Key**

**Setting:** The master bedroom at the Carlisle mansion. Early morning. Soft grey light filters through the curtains. The house is still quiet — the girls are not yet awake, and Sabrina Bryna has not yet stirred for her first feeding.

**Timeframe:** The morning after the Blue Room visit — seven weeks and one day after Sabrina Bryna's birth.

---

## THE SCENE

### Beat 1 — Waking

- Terri Anne wakes slowly. The room is dim, the light soft and grey through the curtains. She is warm — wrapped in Mike's arms, her back pressed against his chest, his arm draped across her waist.
- For a moment, she does not move. She does not want to break the spell. She lies still, feeling his breath against her hair, the slow rise and fall of his chest against her back.
- She remembers the Blue Room. The crying. The floor. The robe being wrapped around her shoulders. She remembers thinking she would feel ashamed this morning.
- She does not. She feels tired. Hollowed out in a good way. Like something that needed to drain has finally drained.

### Beat 2 — Mike wakes

- She feels Mike shift behind her. His arm tightens slightly around her waist — a reflex, not yet fully awake.
- She does not turn. She says, her voice soft and morning-rough: *"You still holding me?"*
- His voice, low and warm against her hair: *"I'm never letting go."*
- She smiles. A small one, but real.

### Beat 3 — What he says to her

- Mike props himself up on one elbow, looking down at her. The grey light falls across her face. She looks tired. She looks beautiful. She looks like someone who survived something and is still here.
- He reaches out and brushes a strand of hair from her face. His hand lingers on her cheek.
- He says: *"You are the most powerful woman I have ever known."*
- Terri Anne's eyes meet his. She does not look away.
- He continues, his voice low and certain: *"You were taken. You were hurt. You carried our baby through all of it. You gave birth. You are still standing. Do you understand how extraordinary that is?"*
- She says, barely a whisper: *"I don't feel extraordinary."*
- He says: *"That is what makes you extraordinary. You don't know how strong you are. But I do. I see it every day."*

### Beat 4 — Gorgeous

- He is quiet for a moment. His thumb traces her cheekbone.
- He says: *"And you are the most gorgeous woman I have ever seen."*
- She almost laughs. *"Mike. I have stretch marks. I have a scar on my foot. I haven't slept through the night in seven weeks."*
- He does not laugh. He meets her eyes and says, with absolute seriousness: *"I know. And you have never been more beautiful to me than you are right now."*
- Terri Anne's eyes well up — not the deep crying of last night, but a softer emotion, something close to relief.
- She says: *"You really mean that."*
- He says: *"I have never meant anything more."*

### Beat 5 — The morning begins

- Somewhere down the hall, Sabrina Bryna makes a small sound — not a cry yet, but the pre-cry rustle that means she will be hungry soon.
- Mike looks toward the door, then back at Terri Anne.
- He says: *"I'll get her."*
- She says: *"Are you sure?"*
- He says: *"You stay here. Rest. I'll bring her to you."*
- He kisses her forehead — slow, deliberate — and gets out of bed.
- Terri Anne watches him disappear through the doorway. She lies back against the pillows, looking at the ceiling.
- The house is waking up. Sabrina Bryna's cry becomes definite. Mike's footsteps pad down the hall.
- Terri Anne puts her hand on her chest, where something that was clenched for seven weeks has finally loosened.

---

## THEMATIC RESONANCE

- **The aftermath of vulnerability:** Last night she fell apart in the Blue Room. This morning she is held and named — powerful, gorgeous, seen. The two are connected. She had to fall apart to hear this.
- **Mike's steady love:** He does not try to fix her. He names what he sees. That is the gift.
- **"I'll get her":** The smallest act of love. He takes the first duty of the morning so she can rest. After a night of being vulnerable, she gets to be cared for.
