## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene outlines the emotional telephone call when **Terri Anne Key calls her eighteen-year-old daughter Fallon Glenda and Charlie to tell them about Brandi Jessica’s retinoblastoma diagnosis**. 

Currently living in San Francisco, Fallon, Charlie, and five-year-old Lÿsa Mandy are **at the airport preparing to fly to Hawaii for a magical Christmas vacation.** The call introduces a heartbreaking conflict: Fallon Glenda’s fierce desire to rush to New York to stand with her sister Kiley during this medical crisis, contrasted with her and Charlie's deep protective love for Lÿsa Mandy (who has Asperger's and intense sensory needs), who is completely excited and dressed up for her dream trip to Hawaii.

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: The Airport Terminal — SFO to Hawaii Excitement
- It is the afternoon of December 23, 2051, at San Francisco International Airport (SFO). 
- Fallon Glenda (18) and Charlie (18) are standing near the boarding gate with **five-year-old Lÿsa Mandy**, who is practically vibrating with childlike holiday excitement. 
- They are **taking Lÿsa Mandy to Hawaii for Christmas** on Oahu—a dream vacation they planned months ago. Lÿsa Mandy is wearing her bright summer clothes under her jacket, holding her toy, completely hyper-focused on the boarding passes and the airplanes outside the giant glass window.
- Fallon and Charlie watch her with deep, maternal and paternal pride, holding hands, feeling a quiet sense of peace in their new California life.

### Beat 2: The Ringing Phone
- Fallon's phone rings. Seeing her mother's caller ID, she smiles and answers, expecting a routine holiday check-in.
- But Terri Anne's voice on the other end is heavy and thick with tears. 
- Terri Anne delivers the crushing news from New York: **six-year-old Brandi Jessica has just been diagnosed with advanced Bilateral Retinoblastoma** during her nine o'clock check-up. The tumors are highly aggressive, and her enucleation (eye surgery) is scheduled for **January**.

### Beat 3: The Heartbreaking Conflict
- Fallon Glenda goes completely pale, her hand trembling against the phone. She looks across the boarding gate at Lÿsa Mandy, who is happily tracing the outline of an airplane on the glass, oblivious to the tragedy.
- Fallon’s first instinct as a sister is to cancel everything immediately:

> “Mom, we’re coming to New York. We’ll change our tickets right now. We need to be with Kiley and Julia.”

- But Terri Anne, speaking with soft, unyielding maternal wisdom, stops her. She points out Lÿsa Mandy's intense sensory needs and how much this Hawaii trip means to her:

> “No, Fallon. Sweetheart, listen to me. Lÿsa Mandy has been talking about Hawaii for months. She is five years old, she's packed, and she is standing at the gate. If you cancel this trip now, it will terrify and destabilize her. Brandi's surgery isn't until January. You take your daughter to Hawaii. Give her the magical Christmas she deserves. We have Mike, Sabrina, Andrea, and Ashley here at the townhouse. You stand by your daughter first.”

### Beat 4: Charlie's Steady Support
- Fallon hangs up, tears spilling over her cheeks as she turns to Charlie. 
- Charlie immediately senses the shift, steps in close, and wraps his arms around her, shielding her from the crowded terminal. She whispers the devastating news about Brandi's diagnosis and her mother's instruction to go to Hawaii.
- Charlie looks at five-year-old Lÿsa Mandy, then looks at Fallon with his signature quiet, unshowy steadiness—the man who always stays:

> “Your mother is right, Fallon. We can't break our promise to Lÿsa Mandy. We will take her to Hawaii, we will make it beautiful and safe for her, and we will fly straight to New York the moment we get back. We will support Kiley from there every single day. I've got you.”

### Beat 5: Closing Image — Boarding the Plane
- The gate agent announces: *"Now boarding for Honolulu."*
- Lÿsa Mandy runs over, grabbing Fallon's hand with absolute, pure excitement: *"Mom! It's time! We're going to the beach!"*
- Fallon wipes her tears, forces a bright, loving smile for her daughter, and squeezes Lÿsa Mandy’s small hand tightly. 
- As they walk down the jet bridge, Fallon holds Charlie's hand, looking back at the city. Their hearts are heavy with the clinical shadow hanging over New York, but surrounded by the quiet courage of their partnership, they step onto the plane to give their daughter her Christmas miracle, completely united.
