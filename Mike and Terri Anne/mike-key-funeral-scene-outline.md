# SCENE OUTLINE: THE FUNERAL & CELEBRATION OF LIFE OF MIKE KEY

*Mike Key dies when Gamaliel Galvin and Klára are sixteen years old. All eight of his daughters attend the funeral. They are all upset. At the celebration of his life, Gamaliel and Klára dance a ballet piece for him.*

---

## TIMING WITHIN THE SAGA

- **Gamaliel and Klára are sixteen** — within the last year of their secret relationship as foster siblings still under Patti Anne's roof.
- **Patti Anne** is approximately thirty-seven (Gamaliel was born when she was twenty-one).
- **Carlota Delicia** is a young child — *years before* her kidnapping at age nine, and many years before her recovery from Tijuana and HIV diagnosis. She is at this funeral as a little girl, holding Sabrina Bryna's hand. She will not remember it clearly. Sabrina will.
- **Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige** are roughly twelve or thirteen at this time (Pandora is born December 25, 2044; the funeral lands a few years before the kidnapping arc, when Pandora is ~16-17).
- This is **one year after** Mike walks in on Gamaliel and Klára kissing and tells Klára *"you are not losing this family."* He died with that protection already given. Gamaliel and Klára know that.

---

## TWO-PART STRUCTURE

Per Catholic Key family practice (Patti Anne is a practicing Catholic; the family's religious anchor is Catholic), the day has two distinct movements:

1. **The Funeral Mass** — formal, liturgical, the church.
2. **The Celebration of Life** — held later that day at the **Mike and Terri Anne mansion** (the same mansion where Patti Anne picks up Gamaliel on weekends). A community-and-family gathering. Speeches. Photos. Food. And, this time, a ballet.

---

# PART ONE — THE FUNERAL MASS

## SETTING

A Catholic church in the Toronto/Vaughan area. The eight daughters fill the first two pews on the family side, with their partners, children, and Terri Anne. The casket is closed. The church is full — Mike's OPP contacts are in the back; Devon Smythson the nanny is somewhere in the middle; teachers, neighbours, family friends spill into the standing-room aisles.

## THE EIGHT DAUGHTERS

All eight Key sisters attend. None of them is okay. Each one is upset in a way that fits her.

| # | Sister | How she is upset |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Patti Anne** (~37) | The eldest. The one Mike chose to raise as his own when she was not biologically his. She holds it together publicly — eldest sister, family anchor — but her hands shake when she takes Communion. She has Gamaliel (16), Klára (16, foster), and the twins Palmer Peter and Salena Sarai (~3) with her. Aleksander has his arm around her the entire Mass. |
| 2 | **Jessica Isabella** | The daughter Mike sat down and explained the kitchen accident to. Sits beside Emma. Cannot look at the casket. |
| 3 | **Rachel Darcy** | Jessica's twin. Sits on Jessica's other side. They lean into each other the way they did before they were separated — a body-memory thing. Antoine is beside Rachel. |
| 4 | **Kiley Rose** | The daughter who was pushed out of the way of the boiling water. She has carried that guilt her whole life and Mike was one of the people who told her it was not her fault. Julia holds her hand. Brandi Jessica (~13, blind, with Burden the Labernese) and Pandora Paige (~13) sit in front of her. |
| 5 | **Sabrina Bryna** | The deaf daughter Mike always signed to. **The only person in the family who has signed to her by default her whole life is in the casket.** She watches the priest deliver the homily through an ASL interpreter the family hired for the service. William is beside her. Carlota Delicia, a small child, is on Sabrina's lap, holding her mother's hand the entire Mass. Sabrina is undone in a way that does not look like anyone else's undoing — quiet, still, a tear that just keeps moving. |
| 6 | **Fallon Glenda** | One of the younger daughters who grew up with Mike's evenings, his easy presence at the kitchen table. Charlie is beside her. She cries in the way of someone who has never had to bury a parent before and does not have a procedure for it. |

| 8 | **Ashley Erin** | The youngest. Transgender. Mike was the father who used her chosen name from the moment she gave it to him. Georgi sits beside her. Ashley wears the suit she would have worn to thank Mike for that — but he is the one being thanked, and he is not here. |

**Terri Anne** sits in the front pew between Patti Anne and Jessica Isabella. The widow. She does not weep performatively. She has been Mike's wife for a very long time and her grief is private and structural — it goes all the way down.

## THE GRANDCHILDREN

There are many. The pews behind the daughters are full of them. The notable ones present:

- **Gamaliel Galvin and Klára** (16, 16) — Patti Anne's. Both in formal black. They sit together but do not sit too close — they are still hiding their relationship from everyone except Mike, who knew and is gone. They share one piece of information at this funeral that no one else in the room shares: *the only adult in this family who knew about them was the man being buried.* They hold that fact between them through the entire Mass without looking at each other.
- **Carlota Delicia** — Sabrina's young daughter, on Sabrina's lap.
- **Brandi Jessica** with **Burden** (guide dog) — Kiley Rose's daughter. Burden is the only dog in the church. Mike loved that dog.
- **Pandora Paige** — Kiley Rose's other daughter. Has her camera in her bag and does not take it out, not once, because *not every moment is photographed*. This is one of the things Mike taught her.
- All of Rachel Darcy's seven children, Jessica Isabella and Emma's children, Andrea Jennifer's children, Ashley Erin and Georgi's children, Fallon Glenda and Charlie's children. The Key family is large and Mike is the man who made it so.

## THE EULOGIES — THE DAUGHTERS AND PARTNERS

**All eight Key daughters, along with four of their partners (Aleksander, Julia, Charlie, and Georgi), eulogize Mike.** Terri Anne insisted that the voices who knew and loved Mike best should shape the service. No daughter or partner is skipped. They speak in an order that lets their voices build, from the lectern, one after the other. The Mass is longer than usual because of this. No one minds.

The twelve eulogies — each carrying one specific, true thing they wanted said out loud about the patriarch:

1. **Patti Anne** (eldest, ~37). Speaks under five minutes. The daughter Mike *chose* to raise as his own. Talks about a man who never made her feel she was anyone's second choice. Closes by thanking him for *"showing the whole family how to tell hard truths to children in the language each child could understand — ASL for Sabrina, plain words for the youngest, full sentences for the oldest. He never thought any of us were too small for the truth. He thought we were exactly the right size for it, every time."*

2. **Aleksander** (Patti Anne's husband). Speaks of Mike not just as a father-in-law, but as his own primary father figure and mentor in family leadership. *"Mike taught me that being a father isn't about blood or authority. It’s about being a solid, unyielding shield for the people you love. He showed me how to co-anchor a household with Patti, and he gave us his quiet, steady strength every single day. I am the husband and father I am today because Mike Key showed me how."*

3. **Jessica Isabella.** Speaks about the night of the kitchen accident — the night Mike sat the family down and explained, in age-appropriate words, what had happened to her and to Kiley Rose. *"He was the first adult I ever saw treat the truth as a kindness. He gave it to us the way you'd hand a child something hot — carefully, with both hands, never pretending it wasn't hot."* She has to stop twice. Rachel Darcy reaches for her hand from the pew when she sits down.

4. **Rachel Darcy.** Jessica's twin. Speaks for less than two minutes. Says she will not try to add to what her twin just said — they grew up sharing one body and they will share this eulogy too. Instead she talks about Mike at the ballet — the only father in the audience who knew the difference between an arabesque and an attitude, because he had asked, and listened to the answer, and remembered. *"My father did not know ballet when I was three. He knew it by the time I was five. He learned it because I loved it. That was always how he learned things."*

5. **Kiley Rose.** Speaks about the morning Mike sat her down — years after the kitchen accident — and told her the guilt she had carried since she was small was not hers to carry. *"He didn't tell me I shouldn't feel it. He told me feeling it didn't make it true. That is a different kind of permission."* She is unable to finish the sentence she had prepared about Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige. She sits down.

6. **Julia** (Kiley Rose's partner). Stands to speak beside Kiley, holding her hand. *"I want to thank Mike for giving Kiley Rose her freedom. For years, she carried a heavy, unfair weight on her shoulders, and Mike was the one who lifted it off. He told her she was allowed to be happy. Because of his gift to her, our family exists in light, not in the shadow of an accident. Mike protected Kiley's heart so that she could love our children, Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige, with a free spirit."*

7. **Sabrina Bryna.** Signs her eulogy in **ASL**. The same hospital interpreter who will be in the room years later for Carlota's HIV diagnosis stands beside her and voices it for the hearing congregation. Sabrina signs slowly. The church is silent in the particular way a church is silent when people are *watching* a language they don't know.
   - *"My father did not learn ASL because I was deaf. He learned ASL because he was my father. There is a difference. The first one is a duty. The second one is a love letter. Every word he ever signed to me was the second one."*
   - She does not cry while signing. Her hands are steady. William, beside her in the pew, is the one who weeps. Carlota Delicia — small, on Sabrina's lap, watching her mother's hands — does not understand the words yet but understands the shape. She is too young to know it, but this is the eulogy she will carry into the room where her own daughter is later diagnosed, and the empty chair will belong to the man Sabrina is signing about now.

8. **Fallon Glenda.** Speaks about Mike at the kitchen table — the ordinary man, the one who made breakfast, the one who was just *there* in the way fathers in television are not. *"He didn't have to be extraordinary for us. He was already there. That was the extraordinary part."* She uses Charlie's handkerchief before she goes up.

9. **Charlie** (Fallon Glenda's husband). Charlie speaks with a quiet, unpretentious warmth, describing his relationship with Mike. *"I married Fallon Glenda when we were teenagers, and I took her three-year-old daughter, Lÿsa Mandy, as my own. I was terrified of being a father to a daughter who didn't share my blood, but Mike Key became my mentor. He showed me, through how he loved and raised Patti Anne, exactly how to love Lÿsa Mandy. Mike didn't need to dominate a room to lead it. He would make coffee, slide a mug across the counter, and show me that fatherhood is about staying in the room, making sure the mugs are full and the doors are unlocked. He gave me my daughter, and he showed me how to keep her."*

10. **Andrea Jennifer.** Speaks about Mike teaching her to drive — a small, specific memory, told without metaphor. The patience. The lack of yelling. The hand on the dashboard when she stopped too hard. *"My father taught me how to use a brake without making me feel like a danger. I have looked for that in every man since, and the only ones I have trusted are the ones who could do it too."*

11. **Ashley Erin** (youngest). Transgender. Wears the suit she would have worn if she'd been thanking Mike. Speaks of the absolute freedom Mike gave her.
   - *"My father used my name from the day I gave it to him. He did not ask why. He did not pause. He did not require an explanation. He just used it, the way you use a word you have known your whole life. He did not give me the name. He gave me the freedom not to defend it. That was his gift."*
   - She cries when she sits down beside Georgi.

12. **Georgi** (Ashley Erin's partner). Georgi speaks last, bringing the service to its final crescendo. *"I want to thank Mike Key for showing the world what a father's love actually looks like. When Ashley Erin gave him her name, Mike didn't hesitate. He didn't demand she defend her identity; he celebrated it. As her partner, I watched Mike give Ashley the ultimate gift of safety under his roof. He showed me what unconditional love means, and I promise to love Ashley with that same unyielding, fierce protection for the rest of my life."*

After the twelfth eulogy, the church is silent for a long count. The priest steps forward and gives the homily that, somehow, no longer needs to do very much. The daughters and partners have done the work. The casket is in front of them. Their father has been said.

After the eighth eulogy, the church is silent for a long count. The priest steps forward and gives the homily that, somehow, no longer needs to do very much. The daughters have done the work. The casket is in front of them. Their father has been said.

# PART TWO — THE CELEBRATION OF LIFE

## SETTING

**The Mike and Terri Anne mansion** — the family home. Catered. Tables of photographs of Mike at every age. A piano in the great room, opened for whoever wants to play (Patti Anne plays classical; she does not play today). The yard is in use because there are too many grandchildren for any one room.

Terri Anne has insisted on this. The funeral is for grief. The celebration of life is for telling Mike's family — all of them — that **he was loved, and that they will keep being a family without him.** This is the version of the day Mike himself would have wanted.

## THE FIRST HOUR

People talk. People eat. People tell Mike stories. A few of his OPP contacts tell stories the daughters have never heard, including one where Mike refused to take credit for a cold case he'd quietly pushed someone to reopen. The eight daughters circulate. Terri Anne sits in Mike's chair — on purpose — and people come to her in turn.

## THE BALLET

About ninety minutes in, **Patti Anne** stands up by the piano and asks for everyone's attention.

> *"Mike's two oldest grandchildren want to do something for him. Please make space in the front room."*

Furniture is moved. A cleared space appears — not a stage, just a space, the same way ballet started as something people did in a room before they had theatres. Someone unrolls a length of marley flooring that the Academy lent them. Klára's pointe shoes and Gamaliel's ballet slippers are already laid out. They have rehearsed for this for two weeks, in secret, at Victoria Ballet Academy after hours, with one teacher who knew Mike and asked no questions.

### What They Dance

A pas de deux. Slow. Modern classical — not story-ballet, not Swan-Lake-pretty. **A piece about a man carrying weight he did not have to carry, and putting it down.** That is what Mike did for both of them. That is what they will say without words.

**The Dog's Role in the Dance:** Klára’s Golden Retriever, **Willow**, is on the unrolled marley flooring and participates directly in the ballet (her second dog, Kleopatra, is not born yet). Trained with gentle, absolute precision, Willow sits quietly beside the empty chair where Mike would have sat, serving as a golden anchor framing the performance. During the slow introduction, she walks calmly behind Klára and Gamaliel, then settles into her position flanking the empty chair, her steady, calm eyes following the dancers.

Gamaliel partners Klára with the precision he has been trained for — every lift clean, every catch sure, his hands where they need to be without ever being where they don't. Klára trusts his weight the way she has trusted it at the barre since they were five years old. They have been physical partners longer than they have been anything else, and tonight that is what they can give.

Halfway through, there is a **single lift** in which Klára extends her arm toward the empty chair where Mike would have sat. She holds the position. Gamaliel holds her. Underneath her extended hand, Willow looks up from her spot beside the chair, tracking her movement. **No one in the room misses what it means.**

The piece ends with Gamaliel lowering Klára to the floor and kneeling beside her — not on one knee, both knees, head bowed. They hold the stillness for the full count of ten before standing. Willow stands up, walking over to press her head against Klára and Gamaliel's knees, breaking the silence with the soft, rhythmic patter of her tail on the marley.

There is no music left. The room has been silent for the last seven counts. Someone — it sounds like Kiley Rose — is crying audibly. Sabrina Bryna is signing something to Carlota, who is too small to understand the dance but is watching her mother's hands.

When they stand, they bow — to Terri Anne, not to the room. Terri Anne stands up out of Mike's chair. She crosses the cleared space. She puts a hand on each of their faces. She does not say anything. She does not need to.

### What the Adults See

The adults who know how to watch dancers see two things at once:
1. **The grief.** Two sixteen-year-olds doing the most articulate thing their bodies have ever done, for the man who deserved it.
2. **The relationship.** Gamaliel and Klára are not hiding it in this dance. They cannot. Partnering at this level requires a trust that reads as love to anyone who is paying attention. Several of the aunts — **Rachel Darcy** the dancer especially, but also **Patti Anne** the mother — *see it.* They say nothing today. They will think about it later.

Mike, of course, already knew. That is part of why the dance can exist.

### What Gamaliel and Klára Know

They know that the only adult in this family who was certain about them is gone.
They know that they have just shown several other adults something true.
They know that they have one more year before they leave Patti Anne's roof at eighteen.
They know that they danced for Mike, and that Mike, somehow, in the language he taught all of his family — the language of the truth told in the form each person can understand — has been answered.

---

## AFTER THE BALLET

The room takes a long minute to come back to itself.

**Sabrina Bryna** is the first one to move. She walks across the cleared space — slowly, her cerebral palsy showing more today than usual — and stands in front of Gamaliel and Klára. She does not have a way to thank them in spoken English that she is willing to use in front of so many people. So she signs it. The interpreter from the church is there, and translates:

> *"Mike would have said you both danced like you meant it. He would have said the room felt it. He would have said thank you."*

Gamaliel signs back. He is not fluent — none of the cousins from outside Sabrina's household are truly fluent — but he knows the sign for **thank you**, and the sign for **family**, and he uses them in that order, slowly. Klára watches him do it and then does the same, with her hands shaking.

Sabrina takes both of their hands and holds them. Carlota Delicia, who is small and tired and confused but who has been beside her mother all day, reaches up and signs — *family* — the only one of the three she remembers. Sabrina almost folds in half.

Patti Anne sees it from across the room. Aleksander steadies her.

---

## PRAGUE — WHAT GAMALIEL AND KLÁRA TELL TERRI ANNE

Later in the afternoon, with the rooms quieter and the cousins scattered across the yard, **Gamaliel and Klára find Terri Anne in Mike's study.** She is sitting in his chair — not at the desk; in the reading chair by the window where Mike used to fall asleep with his glasses on his chest. Willow, who is not Mike's dog but who has been to this house enough to know it, is asleep on the rug at her feet (Klára's second dog, Kleopatra, is not born yet).

Gamaliel asks if they can tell her something.

Terri Anne says yes.

They tell her, together — taking turns, finishing each other's sentences the way two people do who have stood at the same barre since they were five years old — that they have decided what they want to do after Victoria Ballet Academy. After graduation at eighteen. After everything.

**They want to open their own ballet academy. In Prague.**

They have been talking about it for almost a year — since before Mike caught them kissing, since before he died. Klára speaks first about the city: she has never been, but she has read everything, she has watched videos of the Czech National Ballet, she knows about the studios in Karlín and the rehearsal halls that smell of old wood. *Prague*, she says, *is a city that takes ballet seriously without making it precious.* Gamaliel speaks about the dream itself: a small school, not enormous, with academics alongside training the way Victoria Ballet Academy did it for them — *so that no kid has to choose, the way no kid should have to choose, between being good at school and being good at this.*

They tell her they wanted Mike to know. They tell her they will tell him in their own way — by going. By building it. By naming a studio after him if she will let them.

Terri Anne listens to all of it. She has not interrupted once. When they finish, she takes Klára's hand and Gamaliel's hand and holds them both — the same way Sabrina held them an hour ago in the front room. She says:

> *"Your grandfather would have started writing down phone numbers before you finished the sentence. He would have known three people in Prague by tomorrow morning."*

She is smiling, and crying, and she does not let go of their hands for a long moment.

She says — and this is the second thing she tells them, the practical thing that arrives after the blessing — *"You're going to need somewhere quiet to plan. And somewhere quiet to rest, when home is too loud."* And then she gives them what becomes the standing arrangement:

**They are welcome to stay over at the Mike and Terri Anne mansion any time they need to.**

Their own rooms, kept for them. Separate rooms, because Klára is still officially a foster child and Terri Anne is not going to be the grandmother who blurs that line in writing. But the rooms are theirs — Klára's at the top of the stairs by the window seat that looks east, Gamaliel's down the hall in what used to be a small study. They can come on weekends. They can come on weekday evenings when the studio runs late and Patti Anne's house is full and they need somewhere to spread choreography notes on a kitchen table without anyone moving them. They can bring Willow. They can come without notice. The door — like every door in this family — is left cracked.

This is not a duty Terri Anne is taking on. It is a continuation. **Mike would have offered the same thing.** Terri Anne is offering it for both of them now.

Gamaliel hugs his grandmother. Klára does not hug easily, but she does today. Willow wakes up, walks over, and leans against Klára's leg.

---

## THE END OF THE DAY

The mansion empties slowly. The youngest grandchildren fall asleep on couches and are carried out. Terri Anne stands at the door and hugs each of her eight daughters as they leave, one at a time, in order, eldest to youngest — Patti Anne, Jessica Isabella, Rachel Darcy, Kiley Rose, Sabrina Bryna, Fallon Glenda, Andrea Jennifer, Ashley Erin. Each hug is long. Each one is the same and not the same.

**Gamaliel and Klára never change out of their ballet outfits all afternoon.** They danced in them, signed *family* in them with Sabrina Bryna in them, told Terri Anne about Prague in them in Mike's study. No one suggests they change. They don't suggest it to each other. The clothes stay on through the whole day.

When Patti Anne's household goes to leave — Patti Anne, Aleksander, the twins, with Gamaliel carrying a sleeping Salena Sarai and Klára carrying the bag of rolled-up marley flooring — **Terri Anne pulls Patti Anne aside** and asks if Gamaliel and Klára can stay over tonight. *"They're exhausted, let them sleep here, I'll drive them home in the morning."* Patti Anne, who has been watching her own son and her own foster daughter all day, understands the real sentence inside the offered one. She says yes. Aleksander takes the keys. The twins are buckled in.

Klára hands the marley bag back through the car window. Gamaliel and Klára stand in the driveway in their ballet clothes, holding the front door open, as Patti Anne's car turns out of the driveway. Then they go back inside.

Klára does not let herself cry until the front door is closed.

## WHAT THIS SCENE ESTABLISHES

- **All eight Key daughters, along with four of their partners (Aleksander, Julia, Charlie, and Georgi), eulogize him.** This highlights how profoundly Mike influenced his daughters and welcomed their partners, establishing his unyielding legacy of protection, love, and acceptance. Each daughter is upset in a way that fits her — eight separate griefs, eight separate truths.
- **Sabrina Bryna's eulogy is delivered in ASL** with the same hospital interpreter who will be in the room years later for Carlota's HIV diagnosis. *"The first one is a duty. The second one is a love letter. Every word he ever signed to me was the second one."*
- **Ashley Erin's eulogy** establishes that Mike used her chosen name from the day she gave it to him, without explanation, without pause.
- **Terri Anne is the widow** at the centre of both gatherings — she insisted that all eight daughters speak.
- **Mike's death is dated to when Gamaliel and Klára are sixteen** — the funeral is also their last shared act with the only adult who knew about them.
- The **ballet piece** by Gamaliel and Klára is now canon: a pas de deux about *carrying weight you did not have to carry, and putting it down.* Includes the single lift toward Mike's empty chair.
- **Several of the aunts notice** Gamaliel and Klára's relationship through the dance — Rachel Darcy especially. The secret will not stay perfectly secret forever; this is a planted seed.
- **Sabrina Bryna's sign to the two of them** at the end is one of the first instances of the family using ASL as the public language of grief and gratitude — Mike's legacy made literal.
- **Carlota Delicia, as a small child, signs *family*** to her cousins. This is *years* before Carlos breaks her ASL and the kidnapping. The audience will not realize, on first encounter, that this is the last fluent sign Carlota will make for many years. It will be remembered.
- **Gamaliel and Klára tell Terri Anne their dream:** to open their own ballet academy **in Prague** after graduation at eighteen. This is the first time anyone in the family hears it. Terri Anne blesses it on the spot.
- **The standing arrangement is established:** Gamaliel and Klára may stay over at the Mike and Terri Anne mansion any time they need to — separate rooms kept for them at the top of the stairs, no notice required, Willow welcome.
- **The standing arrangement is used the same day:** Terri Anne asks Patti Anne to leave Gamaliel and Klára at the mansion for the night. Patti Anne understands the request and says yes. *(See [gamaliel-klara-banana-split-conversation-scene-outline.md](gamaliel-klara-banana-split-conversation-scene-outline.md) for what happens later that night.)*
- **Gamaliel and Klára never change out of their ballet outfits all day** — through the dance, the signing, the conversation with Terri Anne, the goodbye in the driveway, and into the late-night banana split conversation in the kitchen.
