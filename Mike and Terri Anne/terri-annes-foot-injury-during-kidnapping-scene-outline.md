# Terri Anne's Foot Injury During the Kidnapping — Scene Outline

## Setting
- **Time:** Mid-January 2030, during Terri Anne's twentieth week of pregnancy with Sabrina Bryna.
- **Place:** A secondary location used by the kidnappers — possibly a warehouse, a remote house, or a basement in or near Halifax. Cold, dim, unclean. Terri Anne has been held for hours or days.
- **Characters present:** Terri Anne, the kidnappers (number and identity TBD), and Sabrina Bryna in utero.
- **Mood:** Tense, brutal, intimate. The violence is controlled and deliberate, not frenzied. The focus is on Terri Anne's psychological resistance and her protection of the pregnancy.

## 1. The Lead-Up
- Terri Anne has been compliant so far, trying to keep her breathing steady and her body calm for the baby's sake.
- The kidnappers have made demands to Mike or Key Corporation. Mike has not met them quickly enough, or the kidnappers want to escalate to prove seriousness.
- Terri Anne is seated or tied in a chair. Her hands are bound or free. She is cold, hungry, and exhausted, but her mind is fixed on the baby.
- One of the kidnappers carries a knife, a box cutter, or a heavy tool. The weapon is shown to her before it is used.

## 2. The Threat
- The lead kidnapper tells Terri Anne that her husband needs to understand they are not waiting. He makes it clear: this is not about her. It is about Mike. He destroyed someone in a business deal, and now they are going to make him pay by destroying what he loves.
- He looks at her belly — the visible pregnancy at twenty weeks — and then at her feet.
- The choice of the foot is intentional: it will not kill her, but it will disable her, humiliate her, and terrify Mike. It also prevents her from running if she tries to escape.
- Terri Anne understands what is about to happen. She does not beg. She places her hands over her belly and curls forward, shielding Sabrina Bryna as much as she can.
- She speaks — if she speaks — to the baby, not to the kidnappers. *"It's okay. Mommy is here."*

## 3. The Injury
- The kidnappers force her left foot out or remove her shoe. They may cut through the shoe to make it faster.
- The blade enters the arch or outer edge of her left foot, deep enough to sever tendons and damage nerves. The wound is precise, not random.
- Terri Anne screams, then bites the scream back, afraid that the stress and sound will harm the baby. Her silence is more terrible than the scream.
- She bleeds heavily. The kidnappers wrap the foot in a dirty cloth or tape to stop the bleeding enough to keep her alive.
- The pain is immediate and overwhelming. She focuses on her breathing — in through the nose, out through the mouth — the same breathing she used in labor.

## 4. The Aftermath of the Injury
- The kidnappers leave her alone or step back to send the evidence to Mike.
- Terri Anne is shaking, sweating, and nauseous. She checks in with her body: the baby. Is the baby moving? She needs to feel movement.
- She places her hands on her belly and waits. After a long moment, she feels a flutter — Sabrina Bryna is still alive. This is the only thing that matters.
- The dirty wrapping on her foot begins to breed infection almost immediately. The wound throbs. Fever begins to rise.
- Terri Anne, despite the pain, begins to plan. She looks at the room, the door, the window, the bindings. She notes every detail she might need later.

## 5. The Message to Mike
- The kidnappers photograph or video Terri Anne's injured foot, sometimes with her face, sometimes without.
- The message is sent to Mike: proof that they can reach her, proof that the next injury could be worse, proof that time is running out.
- Mike receives the image. His reaction is the turning point of the rescue effort. The kidnapping shifts from a negotiation to a war.
- The image of Terri Anne's bloody foot, attached to the pregnant body of his wife, becomes the thing Mike cannot unsee. It drives every decision he makes until she is home.

## 6. Terri Anne's Internal Monologue
- She thinks of her daughters: Patti Anne managing the house, Jessica and Rachel clinging to each other, Kiley Rose too small to understand.
- She thinks of Mike, who will be frantic, who will blame himself, who will move heaven and earth.
- She thinks of the baby, who has already felt her fear, her scream, her stress. She apologizes to Sabrina Bryna silently, promising to keep her safe.
- She does not pray for herself. She prays for the baby. She prays for Mike to find them. She prays for her foot to hold out until rescue.

## 7. The Rescue Arrives
- The rescue is swift and violent. Mike's people — or law enforcement, or both — breach the location.
- Terri Anne is found slumped in the chair, pale, feverish, her left foot wrapped in a bloody cloth. Her hands are still on her belly.
- The first thing she says, before asking about herself, is: *"The baby. Is she moving?"*
- Mike, if he is present, sees the foot first. He cannot touch her until the medical team clears her. The delay is agony.
- She is carried out on a stretcher, her foot elevated, her belly monitored. The rescue is successful, but the damage is done.

## 8. Thematic Resonance
- **The foot as message and wound:** The kidnappers choose the foot because it is both functional and symbolic. It is the part of the body that carries a person home. Terri Anne's ability to walk home to her children is damaged.
- **Maternal sacrifice:** Terri Anne's first instinct is to shield her belly, not herself. Her body becomes a barrier between violence and her daughter.
- **Controlled violence:** The scene avoids gratuitous gore by focusing on the psychological horror and the precise, deliberate nature of the act.
- **The origin of disability:** The injury triggers the infection and fever that lead to Sabrina Bryna's hypoxic episode. The foot wound is the physical act; the fetal brain injury is the invisible consequence. This connects the crime directly to Sabrina Bryna's future without making her disability a moral punishment.
- **Mike's transformation:** Seeing the image of Terri Anne's foot changes Mike. He becomes more protective, more controlling, and more willing to use his wealth and power to ensure his family's safety.
- **The foot as family symbol:** Years later, Terri Anne's limp and Sabrina Bryna's spastic gait become a shared language of survival. They walk differently for the same reason.

## 9. Sensory Details to Include
- The cold of the floor against Terri Anne's bare right foot.
- The smell of the room: rust, dust, old fabric, blood.
- The sound of her own breathing, deliberately slowed, and the silence after the scream is bitten back.
- The visual of the blade, the shoe, the foot, the blood on the dirty floor.
- The warmth of her own hands on her belly, the only comfort available.
- The flutter of Sabrina Bryna moving inside her, the small proof of life.
