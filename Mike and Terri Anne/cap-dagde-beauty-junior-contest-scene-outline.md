## SCENE OUTLINE — BEATS & PROSE CUES

### Beat 1 — The Overarching Pact Around the Dining Room Table (Morning)
On the warm, sunlit morning of the contest (the first Saturday in August), Terri Anne Williams-Key has a lengthy conversation with her daughters about the contest. They are sitting down around the long dining room table of their private Cap d'Agde villa compound, enjoying fresh French croissants and local Southern peaches.

Terri Anne explains exactly what to expect on the amphitheater stage that afternoon. But before she finishes her explanation, an extraordinary moment of inter-sibling solidarity occurs. Kiley Rose (12), Sabrina Bryna (11), and Fallon Glenda (8)—who have heard or worried about how rigid outside institutional pageants operate—sit forward at the dining room table.

With Sabrina signing fiercely and expressively in ASL, they declare an absolute, non-negotiable inter-sibling pact:
> *"We don't want to be in the contest if Ashley Erin wouldn't be allowed to be in the contest."*

Little Ashley Erin (5), who transitioned in youth, sits wide-eyed on the dining bench. In standard institutional outside sports or pageants, transgender children are frequently met with rigid birth certificate checkpoints, invasive friction, or outright exclusion. Her elder sisters are putting their absolute familial shield completely around her: *Nobody gets left behind. The Key family stands together.*

Terri Anne’s heart swells with profound matriarchal pride. She does not override them or demand institutional compliance. She completely validates their pact, signing to Sabrina and speaking out loud: *"You are exactly right, my formidable girls. We never walk onto a stage that closes its door to your sister."* 

Then, Terri Anne delivers the pure cleanness of Cap d'Agde: *"But here, there are no rigid boxes or societal armor. The organizers already know Ashley is your sister, and she is 100% entered and fully welcomed exactly as she is."*

With their absolute pact honored and their sanctuary confirmed, the sisters finish their peaches and head toward the preparation area in pure, unified joy.

### Beat 6 — Ashley Erin (5): Pure Youth Freedom & The Transphobic Complaint
The final Key entrant bounds out onto the sunlit Mediterranean stage.
* Little **Ashley Erin (5)**—the youngest sister, who transitioned in youth—tumbles out wearing a tiny flower garland in her auburn hair.

* **The Fiduciary Family Shield:** Patriarch Mike Key does not let the sentence linger or strike his five-year-old daughter. He stands up from the absolute center of the front row, his immense physical scale and heavy detective composure instantly commanding the entire amphitheater. Before the judges can even form a stammering response, Mike's deep, resonant voice booms across the stage:
  > *"She is exactly where she belongs. She is my daughter, and she holds this floor with her sisters."*
* Sourcing the naturist village's load-bearing ethos of absolute radical acceptance, the head judge firmly waves the two complaining families away, confirming into the microphone: *"Every child in this village is accepted exactly as they are."* Sourcing their dining room table pact, Kiley, Sabrina, Fallon, and Andrea step right out onto the stage around little Ashley Erin, holding her hands incredibly tightly in an absolute, impenetrable wall of family protection.
