# Mike Calls Jessica Isabella and Emma — Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Jessica Isabella Key (twenty-five years old), Emma Fritz (twenty-five years old), Samira Chaka Key (nine years old), Sahara Hannan Key (nine years old), Mike Key (offstage / on phone)
- **Offstage / referenced characters:** Brandi Jessica Key (six years old), Pandora Paige Key (six years old), Kiley Rose Key (twenty-two years old), Terri Anne Key, Julia, the Key sisters, Andrew
- **Location:** Halifax, Nova Scotia / Halifax International Airport (YHZ) (or their Halifax residence)
- **When:** December 23, 2051, afternoon (A few hours after Brandi Jessica’s 9:00 AM pediatric diagnosis of Bilateral Retinoblastoma in New York, and right before the holiday gathering)
- **Status:** Updated scene outline
- **Tone:** Tender, bittersweet, conflict of family duty vs. childhood magic, protective, sibling solidarity

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene outlines the emotional telephone call when **Mike Key calls his twenty-five-year-old daughter Jessica Isabella and her wife Emma Fritz to tell them about Brandi Jessica’s retinoblastoma diagnosis**. 

Currently living in Halifax, Jessica Isabella and Emma are **preparing to take nine-year-old twins Samira Chaka and Sahara Hannan to Walt Disney World for a magical Christmas vacation.** The call introduces a heartbreaking conflict: Jessica Isabella’s fierce desire to cancel their trip and fly immediately to New York to stand with her sister Kiley during this medical crisis, contrasted with her and Emma's deep, protective love for the nine-year-old twins (specifically Sahara, who has Type 1 diabetes and has worked incredibly hard), who are completely excited and ready for their dream trip to Walt Disney World.

---

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: The Holiday Excitement in Halifax
- It is the afternoon of December 23, 2051, in Halifax, Nova Scotia. 
- Jessica Isabella (25) and Emma (25) are in their home or at the airport preparing to leave with their **nine-year-old twins, Samira Chaka and Sahara Hannan**.
- The twins are vibrating with absolute, pure holiday excitement. They are **taking Samira and Sahara to Walt Disney World in Orlando, Florida for Christmas**—a major milestone trip they have been planning and saving for. 
- Sahara (who has Type 1 diabetes) is carefully checking her insulin pump and CGM, while Samira helps her pack her bags, both laughing and chattering about meeting the Disney characters and swimming in the Florida sun.
- Jessica and Emma watch them with deep, maternal pride, holding hands, feeling a quiet sense of peace.

### Beat 2: The Ringing Phone from New York
- Jessica's phone rings. Seeing her father's caller ID, she smiles and answers, expecting a routine Christmas check-in from New York.
- But Mike's voice on the other end is heavy, low, and laced with absolute, raw paternal pain.
- Mike delivers the crushing news: **six-year-old Brandi Jessica has just been diagnosed with advanced Bilateral Retinoblastoma** during her nine o'clock check-up in New York today. The tumors are highly aggressive, and her enucleation (eye surgery) is scheduled for **January**.

### Beat 3: The Heartbreaking Conflict
- Jessica Isabella goes completely pale, her hand clutching her chest—the place where her skin graft scars lie. She looks across the room at her nine-year-old daughters, who are happily matching their outfits, completely oblivious to the tragedy.
- Jessica’s first instinct as Kiley's older sister (who literally took boiling water on her back to save Kiley's life at age seven) is to drop everything and fly to New York:

> “Dad, we’re coming to New York. We’ll change our flights right now. We need to be with Kiley and Julia.”

- But Mike Key, speaking with absolute, firm paternal command and protective wisdom, stops her:

> “No, Jessica. You are not coming to New York. You are taking Samira and Sahara to Walt Disney World. They have worked so hard this year, and Sahara has managed her diabetes beautifully. If you cancel this trip now, it will break their hearts. Brandi's surgery isn't until January. We have Terri Anne, Sabrina, Andrea, and Ashley here at the townhouse, and Rachel is on her way. You take your daughters to Florida and give them their Christmas magic. You stand by your children first.”

### Beat 4: Sibling Solidarity and Asymmetry
- Jessica hangs up, tears streaming down her face as she leans against Emma. Emma wraps her arms around her, providing her unshakeable, patient support.
- Jessica whispers the devastating news about Brandi's diagnosis and her father's command to go to Florida. She reflects on the profound asymmetry of their family: 

> “Emma, Kiley's daughter is named after me because she was so grateful to be alive to have them. And now... Brandi is going to lose her eyes. I want to be there. I want to hold them.”

- Emma holds her tightly, wiping her tears, her voice steady and calm:

> “We will support Kiley every single day from Florida, Jessica. We will call her, we will send everything they need, and we will fly straight to the New York townhouse in January to stand with them during the surgery. But Mike is right. We cannot break our promise to Samira and Sahara. We must carry them first.”

### Beat 5: Closing Image — Stepping Into the Flight
- Samira and Sahara run over, holding their boarding passes with pure excitement: *"Mom! Emma! It's time! We're going to see Mickey!"*
- Jessica Isabella wipes her tears, crouches down to her daughters' level, and smiles warmly, her maternal shield completely intact. She kisses them both, ensuring her own pain doesn't dim their holiday wonder.
- As they walk toward their gate, Jessica holds Emma's hand tightly, looking south. Their hearts are heavy with the medical shadow over Manhattan, but surrounded by the quiet courage of their partnership, they step forward to give their daughters their Christmas miracle, completely united.
