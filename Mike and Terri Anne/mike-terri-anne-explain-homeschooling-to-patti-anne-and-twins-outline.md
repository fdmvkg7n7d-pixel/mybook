# SCENE OUTLINE: MIKE & TERRI ANNE EXPLAIN HOMESCHOOLING TO PATTI ANNE AND THE TWINS (THE FIRST TRANSPARENCY)

**Characters:** 
- **Terri Anne Williams-Key**
- **Aldoma** (the children’s nanny)
- **Mike Key**
- **Patti Anne Key** (≈4)
- **Jessica Isabella Key** & **Rachel Darcy Key** (≈2, recently separated at McMaster Hospital)

**Setting:** The Carlisle mansion kitchen — early morning, before the girls wake up. Later, the girls appear at the kitchen table in only their underwear.

**Timeframe:** The morning after Mike and Terri Anne’s private decision (~2026). This scene establishes the first deliberate act of the “kitchen-table transparency” rule.

---

## SYNOPSIS

This scene begins with an intimate early-morning conversation between Terri Anne and Aldoma about the homeschooling decision. Aldoma offers to teach the girls French, German, and Polish as part of their home education. Only after this conversation do the girls wake up and join their parents at the table (wearing only their underwear). Mike and Terri Anne then explain the decision to the children, weaving in their own schooling stories while honoring the family’s new commitment to transparency.

---

## THE SCENE

### Beat 1 — Early morning conversation with Aldoma

- It is still early. The house is quiet. Terri Anne is already in the kitchen in a soft robe. Aldoma, the trusted nanny, arrives and begins preparing breakfast. The two women speak in low voices.
- Terri Anne tells Aldoma about the decision she and Mike made the night before: they will homeschool the girls rather than send Patti Anne to Junior Kindergarten.
- Aldoma listens carefully, then offers something valuable: *“If you are keeping the girls home, I can teach them languages. French, German, and Polish. I speak all three fluently. It would be good for them to learn young.”*
- Terri Anne smiles, visibly relieved. *“That would be wonderful, Aldoma. Thank you.”* She adds that they want the girls to learn in a way that feels natural — woven into daily life rather than forced into a rigid schedule.
- Aldoma nods. *“Then we will speak the languages at the table, during play, during bath time. They will not even notice they are learning.”*

### Beat 2 — The girls wake and appear

- A few minutes later, small footsteps are heard. Patti Anne pads into the kitchen first, wearing only her underwear (a simple cotton pair with a small bow). The twins follow shortly after, also in only their underwear, their surgical scars still visible on their bellies from the recent separation at McMaster Hospital.
- The atmosphere is casual and intimate — this is how the family often begins the day when there are no outsiders present. The girls are comfortable and unbothered.
- Patti Anne climbs into her usual chair. The twins are placed in their high chairs. Mike joins them at the table, still in his morning clothes.

### Beat 3 — Setting the tone

- Terri Anne looks at the three girls in their underwear and smiles softly. She does not comment on their state of undress — it is normal and accepted here.
- She begins gently: *“We have something important to talk about this morning. It’s about where you will learn things.”*

### Beat 4 — Patti Anne’s question

- Patti Anne, almost four and very observant, looks at her mother. *“Am I going to school like the big kids?”*
- Mike answers honestly: *“Not right now, sweetheart. We’re going to learn here, at home, together.”*
- Patti Anne frowns a little. *“Why?”*

### Beat 5 — Mike shares his story (age-appropriate)

- Mike keeps his voice gentle. *“When I was a little boy, I went to a big school called Hillfield Strathallan College. It had lots of rules. But my mother had to go on business trips a lot. Sometimes she couldn’t come pick me up when all the other mothers came. I would sit and wait after everyone else left. It made me feel small inside.”*
- He looks at Patti Anne. *“I don’t ever want you or your sisters to feel small like that. I want to be here when you need me.”*

### Beat 6 — Terri Anne shares her story (age-appropriate)

- Terri Anne reaches over and smooths Patti Anne’s hair. *“When I was little in North York, I went to a regular school. But my parents wouldn’t let me join any sport teams. I had to watch my friends play while I sat on the side. It made me feel left out.”*
- She continues: *“My grandmothers made sure I could go to Scouting. That was the one place I felt like I belonged. But when my grandmother died when I was thirteen, that ended too. After that, there was only school and home. No teams. Nothing extra.”*
- She looks at the children. *“I don’t want any of you to feel left out of things that make you strong and happy.”*

### Beat 7 — Aldoma’s contribution is mentioned

- Terri Anne turns to Aldoma, who is still quietly preparing breakfast nearby. *“Aldoma is going to help us too. She’s going to teach you girls French, German, and Polish — right here at home.”*
- Aldoma smiles at the girls. *“We will speak the languages every day. It will be like a game.”*
- Patti Anne’s eyes widen with interest. *“Like a game?”* Aldoma nods.

### Beat 8 — Explaining to the twins (age-appropriate)

- Terri Anne gently touches Jessica’s and Rachel’s bellies where their scars are visible. *“You two had a very big operation at the hospital. You need extra care right now. We want to be the ones who take you to the doctor and help you when you’re tired.”*
- Mike adds softly: *“We are your first teachers. We will learn together every day.”*

### Beat 9 — Patti Anne processes

- Patti Anne looks at her parents, then at her sisters in their underwear, then at Aldoma. *“So… we stay here? With you and Aldoma?”*
- Mike nods. *“Yes. And when you’re ready for something bigger, we will talk about it together. Just like we’re talking now.”*
- Terri Anne adds the most important line: *“No one will ever decide for you without telling you why. That’s our promise.”*

### Beat 10 — The first small ritual

- Mike pushes the simple drawing (house + table + three girls) across the table to Patti Anne. *“This is our school now. This table. This house. And Aldoma will help us make it beautiful.”*
- Patti Anne carefully folds the paper and puts it in her pocket.
- The twins babble happily. One reaches for Terri Anne; the other reaches for Aldoma.

### Beat 11 — Closing the circle

- Before they begin breakfast, Terri Anne says the line that will become family canon:
  > *“From now on, when something important happens, we sit here and talk about it. All of us. Even when you’re little. Even when it’s hard.”*

- Mike adds quietly: *“No more empty classrooms. No more watching from the sidelines.”*

- Aldoma, still at the counter, simply says: *“The girls will learn well here.”*

---

## THEMES EXPRESSED

- **Transparency as love**: The decision is explained openly, even to very young children.
- **Healing parental wounds**: Mike’s loneliness and Terri Anne’s exclusion are directly connected to the choice.
- **Aldoma as extended family**: The nanny is included in the educational vision and language teaching.
- **Casual intimacy**: The girls appearing in only their underwear underscores the safe, trusting home environment.
- **The table as the school**: The physical and emotional center of learning.
