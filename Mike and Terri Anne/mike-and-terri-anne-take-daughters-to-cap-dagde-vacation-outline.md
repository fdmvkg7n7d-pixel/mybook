## SCENE OUTLINE 1 — THE JUNE KITCHEN TABLE DISCLOSURE

### Beat 1 — Gathering the Homeschooled Household at the Carlisle Kitchen Table
Because all eight daughters are actively homeschooled by Mike and Terri Anne, their education does not stop or operate on standard institutional "school terms." Their learning is continuous, deeply holistic, and entirely integrated into their family life. 

On a bright June morning, sunlight cuts through the tall windows of the massive Carlisle mansion kitchen, illuminating the large oak farm table where Terri Anne and Mike have just wrapped up a morning homeschool session.

Mike Key and Terri Anne don't believe in springing massive life shifts on their children without preparation. Per their foundational family rule—*no child is excluded from the truth just because telling it requires more care*—they sit all eight of their daughters down around the kitchen table to explain that for July and August, their overarching homeschool is moving across the Atlantic.

Patti Anne (17) takes her customary seat beside her parents. Fifteen-year-old Jessica and Rachel sit shoulder to shoulder. Kiley Rose (11) sits near Julia (in her future memory). Nine-year-old **Sabrina Bryna**, who navigates **Cerebral Palsy**, sits perfectly where she can see Mike's hands, her posture secured by her custom core-stabilizing ergonomic kitchen chair. With her core beautifully supported, her hands and arms are completely free from spastic strain as she signs in ASL. Little Fallon Glenda (7), Andrea Jennifer (6), and Ashley Erin (5) clamber onto the wooden kitchen benches, wearing their little summer shorts and bare feet.

### Beat 2 — Terri Anne Explains July: A Month in Paris
Terri Anne stands at the head of the table holding an elegant, hand-drawn map of France. She explains the first half of their great summer migration.

For the entire month of **July**, the family will be living in **Paris, France**. 
* She explains that they will reside in a spectacular private penthouse overlooking the Seine (or a grand historic townhome in Le Marais).
* She outlines their daily cultural routines: morning trips to authentic Parisian street markets, purchasing fresh warm baguettes and wheels of Brie; afternoon walking tours through the Louvre and Musée d'Orsay; and evening strolls across stone bridges.
* **Saga Canon Origin:** This deliberate, immersive July month in Paris sows the lifelong intergenerational love for the city that endures decades later when Gamaliel and Klára decide that *"an annual, fixed Disneyland Paris trip for every grade-eight class"* will be an unbreakable tradition for Pražská Baletní Akademie.

### Beat 3 — Mike Explains August: A Month in Cap d'Agde
Mike Key sits forward, resting his massive forearms on the oak table. He signs fluidly in ASL while speaking his deep, reassuring words aloud so Sabrina Bryna and her seven sisters receive the exact same unblemished truth simultaneously.

He explains the second half of their summer migration. For the entire month of **August**, they will travel down from Paris to the Mediterranean coast to stay in **Cap d'Agde**.
* He explains Cap d'Agde in plain, beautiful, non-titillating terms: it is a premier, world-class family village by the sea where clothing is entirely optional.
* He looks directly at fifteen-year-old **Jessica Isabella**. He explains that on Plage Naturiste, every human body is viewed as completely ordinary and unblemished. Nobody stares. Nobody whispers. Her Shriners skin grafts will be completely safe in the Mediterranean sun. Jessica's shoulders visibly drop in profound relief.
* He looks down at young **Ashley Erin** (5). He reassures her that in Cap d'Agde, there are no gendered swimsuits or societal assumptions to navigate. She will simply be a free, unhindered little girl running the warm sand.

The eight sisters break into chattering excitement. The master plan is completely clear, transparent, and settled.

## PHASE 2 — AUGUST IN CAP D'AGDE (BODILY SANCTUARY)

### Days 1–3 — The Mediterranean Relocation
* A private executive train or tinted van convoy carries the family from Paris down through the sun-drenched lavender fields and vineyards of Southern France directly to Cap d'Agde.
* They check into a spectacular, high-walled private compound inside the secure Quartier Naturiste, complete with private heated saltwater swimming pools and a private pathway cutting through the windy dunes to the Plage Naturiste.

### Days 4–14 — Sanctuary Life on Plage Naturiste & Dual Aquatic Play
* **Ordinary Fact:** Families from across Europe playing petanque, reading paperbacks, and building sandcastles in the Mediterranean sun completely unburdened by bodily shame.
* **Canonical Aquatic Life (Sea & Pools):** While they are at Cap d'Agde, swimming is the definitive daily equalizer and central family connection. They split their active aquatic life beautifully between **the Mediterranean Sea** and **the swimming pools**:
  * **1. Swimming in the Mediterranean Sea:** The long golden afternoons are spent diving into the incredibly buoyant, turquoise Mediterranean Sea. Sourcing the Key family's unashamed swimming legacy, all eight sisters swim completely free. Fifteen-year-old **Jessica Isabella** floats peacefully on her back in the gentle swells, feeling the absolute liberation of sea salt on her Shriners hospital burn scars in an unshielded, non-judgmental tide.
  * **2. Swimming in the Swimming Pools:** In the warm European mornings and relaxed late evenings, the family gathers at their spectacular private heated saltwater swimming pools at the villa compound, as well as the magnificent large community pools inside the Cap d'Agde village. The pool is established saga canon as *"shared ground in the Key family"*—here, it is an unblemished hub of ordinary play. Six-year-old **Andrea Jennifer** and five-year-old **Ashley Erin** practice playful underwater handstands and somersaults, fifteen-year-old **Rachel Darcy** performs elegant underwater ballet glides in the deep end, and **Terri Anne** sits on the warm limestone coping supervising the chattering aquatic life.
* **Milestone Event: Cap d'Agde's Beauty Junior Contest (First Saturday in August):** Pinned specifically to **the first Saturday in August**, before the late-night catch scene, the younger daughters are entered into Cap d'Agde's Beauty Junior Contest. In the Quartier Naturiste, this contest is a treasured, wholesome summer tradition—completely devoid of toxic, adultified pageant tropes (no makeup, no artificial flippers, no competitive shaming). It is an unadorned, joyful celebration of natural childhood self-expression, athletic vitality, and unblemished confidence:
  * **Ashley Erin's Pure Triumphant Autonomy:** Sited on the sunlit Mediterranean stage, little **Ashley Erin (5)** walks out in absolute self-determination. With no gendered swimwear barriers or societal assumptions to navigate, she executes playful tumbles across the stage, completely aglow with radical self-acceptance and wildly cheered by her entire family.
  * **Sabrina Bryna's CP Agency & ASL Masterpiece:** Flawlessly accommodating her **Cerebral Palsy**, nine-year-old **Sabrina Bryna (9)** rolls onto the stage in her custom all-terrain beach wheelchair. She executes an extraordinarily sharp, fluid, and expressive ASL poetry piece about the salt sea that completely captivates the European audience and judges.
  * **Kiley, Fallon, and Andrea:** Eleven-year-old Kiley Rose, seven-year-old Fallon Glenda, and six-year-old Andrea Jennifer execute beautiful gymnastics layouts, swimming pantomimes, and artistic recitations.
  * **The Fiduciary Family Anchor Front Row:** From the very front row, Mike Key, Terri Anne, eighteen-year-old Patti Anne (18), and fifteen-year-old twins Jessica and Rachel stand up and applaud wildly, establishing an unshakeable intergenerational shield of parental validation.
* **The ASL Shellwork & Cerebral Palsy Sea Support:** Because walking across dry, shifting dunes and uneven tidal pools is exceptionally difficult for a nine-year-old with **Cerebral Palsy**, Mike and Terri Anne utilize a custom all-terrain beach wheelchair with balloon tires to navigate the Cap d'Agde sand. When Sabrina Bryna (9) wants to enter the water to swim in the Mediterranean Sea or explore the tidal pools, Mike Key personally lifts her out of her beach wheelchair and carries her into the foamy surf. He holds her securely in the chest-deep water so she can float and kick her spastic lower limbs in the weightless, warm salt sea, or sits down in the shallow tide bracing her back against his immense chest so she can converse in fluent ASL.
* **The Origin of Matriarchal Naturism:** Terri Anne relaxing on the shaded teak decking and pool coping, establishing the exact emotional roots that drive her adult move to **Cypress Cove in Orlando, Florida**, decades later.
