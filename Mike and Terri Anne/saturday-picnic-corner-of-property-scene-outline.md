# SCENE OUTLINE: TERRI ANNE'S SATURDAY PICNIC WITH KILEY ROSE

**Characters:**
- **Terri Anne Williams-Key** — mother
- **Kiley Rose Key** (~2 years old, with Turner Syndrome)
- **Any other younger daughters at home** (depending on the year)

**Setting:** The corner of the Key family's property in Carlisle, Ontario — where the tree line meets the open field, under an old maple. The spot is close enough to the house to see it in the distance, far enough to feel like a journey for small legs.

**Timeframe:** The three Saturdays when Mike takes the older girls to their competitions — Jessica Isabella to Ottawa, Rachel Darcy to Mohawk College, and Patti Anne to Vancouver. Kiley Rose is two years old — too young to travel, old enough to feel left behind.

---

## THE SCENE

### Beat 1 — The first Saturday morning (Jessica Isabella goes to Ottawa)

- Saturday morning. Mike and Jessica Isabella have already left for Ottawa. The house is quieter without them. Kiley Rose has been standing at the back door since breakfast, watching the driveway where the car disappeared.
- She is too young to say *I feel left out* but she knows how to feel it. Her small hand presses against the glass. Her blanket is clutched under her arm.
- Terri Anne watches her from the kitchen. She knows what is happening in that small, Turner-syndrome body — the frustration of being too little, the ache of watching older sisters go places she cannot follow.
- She kneels beside Kiley Rose and says, gently: *"Do you want to do something special with me today? Just us?"*

### Beat 2 — Kiley Rose's question

- Kiley Rose turns from the window. Her face is serious — she has always been serious, even as a toddler, as if her body's smallness made her watch the world more carefully.
- She asks: *"Am I a big girl too?"*
- Terri Anne feels the question in her chest. She answers without hesitation: *"You are a big girl. You are my big girl. And big girls get to do special things too."*

### Beat 3 — The idea of the picnic

- Terri Anne does not make a plan. She follows the moment. She goes to the mudroom and pulls out a simple wicker basket. She packs what is in the kitchen: an apple, a piece of bread, a small thermos of water.
- She takes a quilt from the hook by the door — not the good one, the one that is already worn and soft.
- She holds out her hand to Kiley Rose. *"Come on, big girl. We're going on a picnic. Just you and me."*
- Kiley Rose takes her hand. She brings her blanket with her.

### Beat 4 — The walk to the corner

- They walk together across the property. Terri Anne carries the basket in one hand and the quilt under her arm. Kiley Rose's small hand is in her other hand. They walk slowly — the pace of a two-year-old with Turner Syndrome, whose steps are careful and deliberate.
- Terri Anne does not rush her. She does not carry her. She lets Kiley Rose walk the whole way on her own feet.
- She says: *"This is far, isn't it? But you're walking it. That's what big girls do."*
- Kiley Rose does not complain. She keeps walking, her blanket trailing behind her.

### Beat 5 — Reaching the spot

- They reach the corner where the old maple stands. The ground is uneven here — roots breaking through the grass, patches of moss.
- Terri Anne spreads the quilt. She sits down and pats the spot beside her.
- Kiley Rose sits down carefully. She looks around — at the tree, the field, the house in the distance.
- She says: *"It's far."*
- Terri Anne nods. *"It is. And you walked all the way here by yourself."*

### Beat 6 — The picnic

- Terri Anne opens the basket. She takes out the apple, the bread, the thermos. She pours a small cup of water for Kiley Rose.
- They eat simply, sitting on the quilt under the maple. The house is a small shape in the distance. The field stretches around them.
- Kiley Rose holds her bread in both hands. She chews slowly, watching the clouds.
- Terri Anne does not fill the silence with chatter. She lets Kiley Rose have this quiet — the kind of quiet that says: *you are enough. You do not need to perform. You do not need to compete. You just need to be here.*

### Beat 7 — The question about Jessica Isabella

- At some point, Kiley Rose asks: *"Where Jessie go?"*
- Terri Anne answers honestly: *"She went to Ottawa with Daddy for her gymnastics competition. She is doing something brave and hard. And you are doing something brave and hard too — being a big girl and having a picnic all the way out here."*
- Kiley Rose considers this. She takes another bite of bread.
- She says, after a long pause: *"I big girl."*
- Terri Anne's eyes sting. She blinks the feeling away. *"Yes, my love. You are a big girl. You have always been a big girl."*

### Beat 8 — The walk back

- They stay for about an hour. Kiley Rose finishes her bread. She drinks her water. She lies back on the quilt and looks up at the leaves moving in the wind.
- When she is ready, they pack up. Kiley Rose helps — she grabs one corner of the quilt and drags it across the grass while Terri Anne folds the rest.
- They walk back together, the same slow pace. Kiley Rose's hand is in Terri Anne's. Her blanket is tucked under her arm.
- Before they reach the house, Kiley Rose stops. She looks up at Terri Anne.
- *"Tomorrow?"*
- Terri Anne smiles. *"Maybe. We'll see."*

---

## THE REPETITION — SECOND AND THIRD SATURDAYS

### The second Saturday (Rachel Darcy's recital)

- The following Saturday, Mike takes Rachel Darcy to Mohawk College. Kiley Rose is at the door again, watching the car disappear.
- This time, she turns to Terri Anne before Terri Anne can speak. She says: *"Picnic?"*
- Terri Anne laughs softly. *"Yes, my love. Picnic."*
- They walk to the corner again. This time, Kiley Rose leads the way. She knows the path now.
- On the quilt, Kiley Rose asks about Rachel Darcy. Terri Anne tells her she is dancing.
- Kiley Rose tries to stand on the quilt and spin. She wobbles. She sits down. She says: *"Dancing is hard."*

### The third Saturday (Patti Anne's competition)

- The next Saturday, Mike and Patti Anne are flying to Vancouver. Kiley Rose is too young to understand the distance, but she understands the pattern. She is at the mudroom before Terri Anne gets there, holding the basket with both hands.
- *"Picnic!"* she announces. Not a question. A declaration.
- Terri Anne watches her daughter — this small, determined girl with Turner Syndrome, who has turned a single Saturday into a ritual.
- She kneels down and looks at her. *"Yes, Kiley Rose. Picnic. You and me. Every Saturday they go, we go."*
- Kiley Rose nods, satisfied. She takes Terri Anne's hand and pulls her toward the door.

---

## THEMATIC RESONANCE

- **Not a tradition — a response:** Terri Anne does not plan this as a family tradition. She sees her two-year-old at the window, watching her sister leave, and responds with love. The repetition comes from Kiley Rose herself — she asks for it the second time, declares it the third.
- **Kiley Rose's Turner Syndrome:** She is smaller, slower, more deliberate. The walk to the corner is genuinely hard for her. Terri Anne's gift is letting her walk it anyway, without being carried.
- **"Big girl":** The phrase is not about age or size. It is about worth. Terri Anne is telling Kiley Rose that she does not need to be like her sisters to matter.
- **The picnic as equal:** Kiley Rose does not get a competition, a medal, or a stage. She gets a quilt under a tree and her mother's full attention. And that is enough — not as a consolation prize, but as its own kind of richness.

## CONNECTION TO OTHER SCENES

- **The three-Saturday competition sequence:** While Mike takes Jessica Isabella, Rachel Darcy, and Patti Anne to their performance milestones, Terri Anne stays home with Kiley Rose and creates a counter-milestone — not a competition, but a walk, a quilt, an apple, and a mother's full presence.
- **Later homecoming scene (Psalm 91:11):** When Terri Anne comes home from the kidnapping, this early pattern of being held and seen by her mother is part of what Kiley Rose brings to the bedside — the memory of being told she is a big girl, even when she is small.
