# Mike and Terri Anne Tell Kiley Rose and Julia What Happened at French Woods — Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Mike Key, Terri Anne Key, Kiley Rose Key, Julia
- **Onstage animal:** None
- **Locations:** Kiley Rose and Julia's new townhouse — living room / kitchen
- **When:** The evening after Session 3 Visiting Day at French Woods (July 2053). Kiley Rose and Julia have driven back from camp to their new townhouse. Mike and Terri Anne are visiting them there. The twins are still at camp — Sessions run back-to-back, so the girls won't be home until after Session 4.
- **Status:** New scene outline
- **Tone:** Quiet authority, family intel, the weight of what Mike did delivered calmly in a living room, love as a form of precision

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene captures the moment Mike and Terri Anne sit down with Kiley Rose and Julia — at Kiley Rose and Julia's new townhouse, the evening after Visiting Day — and tell them everything that happened with the Hayashis. Kiley Rose sensed something in the car but didn't ask. Now she finds out what her father actually did.

It establishes:
- That Kiley Rose and Julia's new townhouse is their new home — a newer, more personal space than the Manhattan townhouse where the twins grew up. The family has moved. This is where the girls will come home to after Session 4.
- That Mike tells Kiley Rose and Julia about the acquisition of Hayashi Development matter-of-factly — not as a confession, not as a boast, but as a report. This is what happened. This is what I did.
- That Julia and Kiley Rose react differently — Kiley Rose with a mixture of fury at the Hayashis and gratitude for her father, Julia with a sharper, more strategic understanding of what Mike just set in motion.
- That Terri Anne was fully complicit — she knew what Mike was going to do before he did it. She didn't stop him. She approved.
- That the conversation moves from the Hayashi situation to the twins — because for Mike and Terri Anne, the business move and the family protection are the same act.
- That Kiley Rose and Julia realize the full scope of what it means to be a Key — not just the name, but the reach, the resources, and the absolute certainty that the family protects its own.

## BEATS

### Beat 1: The New Townhouse

- It is evening. The summer light is still in the sky but fading. Kiley Rose and Julia's new townhouse is warm and lived-in — not the old Manhattan townhouse where the twins were born and the Christmas party happened, but something newer. A fresh start. A place they chose together after everything. It smells like coffee and the basil Julia grows on the windowsill.
- The townhouse is smaller than the mansion but still generous — three stories, good light, a kitchen that opens onto a living room with comfortable furniture and family photographs on every surface. The twins on the beam. The twins at the pool. The twins as babies. Kiley Rose at their age.
- Mike is in the kitchen making coffee. Terri Anne is setting out mugs. Kiley Rose and Julia sit at the kitchen island, still in their driving clothes from the trip back from French Woods.
- The energy is calm but full. They have things to talk about.

### Beat 2: The Girls First

- Kiley Rose: "They're doing so well. Brandi walked the beam without counting this time. She just walked it."
- Terri Anne: "I saw. She didn't hesitate once."
- Julia: "Pandora told me Brandi introduced herself in Community Service. Told the whole class about the Retinoblastoma. About how you gave back to the hospital."
- Mike sets the coffee down. He listens to this. His face is still, but his eyes are warm.
- Mike: "She said it out loud? In front of everyone?"
- Julia: "In front of everyone. The teacher asked them to introduce each other. Brandi stood up and told them."
- Mike: "Good for her."
- Terri Anne: "That's our granddaughter."
- A pause. They drink their coffee. The townhouse is quiet except for the hum of the refrigerator and the distant sound of traffic.
- Then Kiley Rose puts her mug down.

### Beat 3: Kiley Rose Asks

- Kiley Rose: "Dad. What happened with Mr. Hayashi?"
- Mike looks at Terri Anne. Terri Anne nods once — go ahead.
- Mike: "I bought his firm."
- The words land in the kitchen like a stone in still water.
- Kiley Rose: "What?"
- Mike: "Hayashi Development. Key Corporation is acquiring it. The paperwork has been in motion for six months — we needed the Harrison Street property and buying the whole firm was the cleanest way to get it. The numbers finally made sense. The offer was delivered to his partners this morning."
- Julia: "You bought his company."
- Mike: "I'm acquiring the one asset we need and liquidating the rest. The Harrison Street building stays with Key Corporation. The Westchester parcels, the Greenwich listings, the commercial holdings — all of it goes on the market. His partners are being fairly compensated. His clients will be transitioned. It's a clean transaction."
- Kiley Rose stares at him. Julia's hand has found Kiley Rose's under the counter.
- Mike: "The offer wasn't affected by what happened at camp. The numbers are the same whether he complained about Brandi or he didn't. I'd have made the same acquisition either way."
- A beat.
- Mike: "But I'd be lying if I said it didn't affect the way I told him."

### Beat 4: Kiley Rose's Reaction

- Kiley Rose is quiet for a long time. She stares at her coffee. Julia watches her.
- Kiley Rose: "You told him at Visiting Day. At my daughters' camp. On the day I was supposed to be watching them swim and do yoga and sit on a beam."
- Mike: "Yes."
- Kiley Rose: "And you didn't tell me first."
- Mike: "No."
- Kiley Rose: "Because I would have tried to stop you."
- Mike: "Because you would have worried about how it looked. And I wasn't interested in how it looked. I was interested in what it was."
- A beat. Kiley Rose's jaw is tight. But she is not angry at him. She is angry at the situation — at the Hayashis, at the fact that it came to this, at the fact that her father had to do something at all.
- Kiley Rose: "What did he say?"
- Mike: "He asked if this was because of the camp. I told him the acquisition has been in motion for six months. The Harrison Street building. The numbers."
- Kiley Rose: "And?"
- Mike: "And I told him that while the offer wasn't affected by what he did, the way I was telling him was."
- Julia, quietly: "You made sure he knew."
- Mike: "I made sure he understood the difference between a threat and a fact. A threat is something that might happen. This has already happened."

### Beat 5: Terri Anne's Role

- Kiley Rose looks at her mother.
- Kiley Rose: "You knew."
- Terri Anne does not flinch. She picks up her coffee and takes a sip.
- Terri Anne: "We discussed it before we drove up. I didn't stop him because I didn't want to stop him. That man stood in front of you and told you that your daughter didn't belong in a ballet class. His wife complained to the camp director. His daughters interrupted her in the middle of class. And you handled it. You and Julia handled it beautifully. But your father and I have resources you don't have yet. And one of those resources is the ability to make sure that man understands exactly who he picked a fight with."
- Terri Anne: "Not to hurt him. To inform him. There's a difference."
- She echoes Mike without trying to. It's not a coordinated statement. It's just the way they think — the same language, forty years of marriage, the same moral architecture.
- Julia: "What happens to the Hayashis now?"
- Mike: "Mr. Hayashi can keep a minority stake in the Harrison Street property or cash out. His partners are already on board. The board votes next week. By the end of the quarter, Key Corporation holds the building and the rest of the portfolio is sold. His firm continues to exist in name only."
- Julia: "And the camp?"
- Mike: "The camp is the camp. I didn't buy his firm to punish his daughters. Sloan and Rilee are children. They did what children do when their parents teach them to be afraid of difference. The acquisition is a business decision. The timing is personal. The two things can coexist."

### Beat 6: Julia Processes

- Julia has been quiet through most of this. She is the strategist in the room — she sees angles, consequences, downstream effects. She is also the one who stood between the Hayashis and Brandi on Session 2 Visiting Day. She was the wall.
- Julia: "You're dismantling a man's company because he complained about our daughter at a summer camp."
- Mike: "I'm acquiring a property Key Corporation has needed for six months. The man who owns it happens to be the same man who told my daughter that my granddaughter — who survived cancer — didn't belong in a ballet class. I would have bought the firm either way. But I would have sent a letter and had a broker handle it. Instead, I walked up to him on Visiting Day and told him myself."
- Julia: "Because you wanted him to know it was connected."
- Mike: "Because I wanted him to know that when you pick a fight with a mother at a summer camp, you're not just picking a fight with a mother. You're picking a fight with a family. And families have reach."
- Julia nods slowly. She is not disapproving. She is measuring. Calculating. Understanding the full scope.
- Julia: "The girls don't know."
- Mike: "No. And they don't need to. Brandi Jessica has enough to carry. She's eight years old and she's telling strangers about her cancer and walking balance beams by herself. She doesn't need to know that her grandfather bought a company because someone was cruel to her. She just needs to know that her family shows up."
- Julia: "We showed up."
- Mike: "Yes. You did."

### Beat 7: What the Girls Told Them Today

- The conversation shifts. Kiley Rose and Julia tell Mike and Terri Anne what the girls told them on Visiting Day — things they didn't know. They didn't know what classes the twins were taking this session. The girls chose their own schedules at camp and the mothers found out today.
- Kiley Rose: "We didn't know what they were taking. They changed everything for Session 3. Brandi dropped Ballet as a major. She's doing Songwriting now. Charlie Brown — the musical. And Painting and Drawing. Those are her three majors."
- Mike: "She dropped Ballet?"
- Kiley Rose: "She dropped it as a major. She can still take it as a minor. But her new majors are Songwriting, Charlie Brown, and Painting and Drawing. She chose them herself. She and Pandora asked us at the end of Session 2 if they could change, and we said yes — it's their summer. But we didn't know the specifics until today."
- Julia: "She wrote a song. In Songwriting class. About the family. About the Retinoblastoma. About Partner Yoga during chemo. She hums it during Pointe instead of counting."
- Mike: "Instead of counting?"
- Kiley Rose: "She used to count her steps. Forty-two, eighteen, seven. Now she hums her own song. Julia, tell them what Brandi told us about Community Service."
- Julia: "The teacher asked them to introduce each other. Brandi stood up and said, 'I had Retinoblastoma. It's eye cancer. They removed both of my eyes when I was seven. My mommies gave back to the hospital where I was being treated.' She said it like she was reading a weather report."
- Terri Anne: "She gets that from you."
- Kiley Rose: "She gets that from you. I would have cried. She didn't cry. She just said it."
- Julia: "Pandora's doing Spray Art. She says she's terrible at it but she loves it. Brandi says the paint is everywhere. Pandora says that's the point."
- Mike almost smiles. "That sounds like Pandora."
- Kiley Rose: "And Brandi's taking Gymnastics as a minor now. After Community Service. She walks there by herself."
- Mike: "Good."
- Kiley Rose: "Pandora's majors are Ballet, Gymnastics, and Intermediate Modern. She's doing the same minors as Brandi — Swimming Lessons, Painting and Drawing, Water Recreation, Horse Care, Community Service — plus Spray Art. She wanted to do the same ones as Brandi."
- Terri Anne reaches across the counter and takes Kiley Rose's hand.
- Terri Anne: "She's not just surviving, Kiley. She's living."
- Kiley Rose's eyes fill. She blinks it away.
- Kiley Rose: "I know. And we didn't know any of it until today. They just... did it. They made their choices and they went to class and they didn't wait for us to approve."

### Beat 8: The Quiet After

- The four of them sit in the kitchen of the new townhouse. The coffee has gone cold. The summer light has faded to blue.
- Mike stands up and opens the window. The evening air comes in — warm, New York summer, the sound of a neighbor's sprinkler.
- Mike: "You did the right thing at Session 2. Standing your ground. Not raising your voice. Telling them who you are. I'm proud of you for that."
- Kiley Rose: "I was shaking the whole time."
- Mike: "I know. That's what courage is."
- Terri Anne: "And now you know that if something like this happens again — at camp, at school, anywhere — you have more than your voice. You have us. You have the name. You have the firm. You have whatever you need."
- Julia: "We don't want to need it."
- Terri Anne: "No one does. That's not the point. The point is that it's there. And the people who tried to make your daughter feel like she didn't belong — they understand that now."
- A pause.
- Kiley Rose: "What about Sloan and Rilee?"
- Mike: "They're children. Their parents taught them something wrong. Maybe they'll unlearn it. Maybe they won't. But that's not your fight. Your fight is making sure Brandi Jessica knows she belongs everywhere she chooses to be. And she does. I watched her today. That girl belongs everywhere."
- Kiley Rose nods. Julia puts her arm around Kiley Rose's waist.
- Mike: "Now. Are you two staying for dinner, or are you driving back tonight?"
- Kiley Rose: "We're staying. We're already home. You two are staying for dinner."
- Terri Anne: "Good. I'm making pasta. Mike, you're on sauce."
- Mike: "I'm always on sauce."
- Terri Anne: "Because you're good at it."
- Mike puts on the apron that hangs on the hook by the stove. It has a small embroidered K on the pocket — a gift from one of the daughters, years ago. He starts pulling ingredients from the pantry.
- In the kitchen of Kiley Rose and Julia's new townhouse, Mike Key makes sauce while his wife makes pasta and his daughter and her wife sit at the island and talk about their daughters. The evening is ordinary. The evening is everything.
