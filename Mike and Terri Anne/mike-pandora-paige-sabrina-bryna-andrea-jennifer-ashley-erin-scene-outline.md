# Mike, Pandora Paige, Sabrina Bryna, Andrea Jennifer, and Ashley Erin — Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Mike Key, Pandora Paige Key (six years old), Sabrina Bryna Key (nineteen years old), Andrea Jennifer Key (seventeen years old), Ashley Erin Key (fifteen years old)
- **Offstage / referenced characters:** Brandi Jessica Key (missing), Kiley Rose Key, Julia, Terri Anne Key, Rachel Darcy Key, Haden (Andrea Jennifer's boyfriend), the Police / Detectives
- **Location:** Kiley Rose and Julia's townhouse, New York City
- **When:** December 30, 2051 (The morning and day while Julia is at Key Porn Studios)
- **Status:** New scene outline
- **Tone:** Protective, tender, fiercely familial, the quiet strength of sisters holding the line, the weight of a grandfather's arms, grief made bearable by love

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene outlines **the hours at the townhouse while Julia is at Key Porn Studios on December 30** — and how Mike, Sabrina Bryna, Andrea Jennifer, and Ashley Erin hold the space for Pandora Paige and Kiley Rose.

With Julia gone, the townhouse loses its primary anchor. Pandora Paige, who has not been out of Julia's arms since the kidnapping, is devastated by the separation. **Mike Key steps in as the steadying force** — his big arms, his deep voice, his unyielding presence becoming the substitute weight Pandora needs. But Mike cannot do it alone. He is also coordinating the search, fielding calls from police and private investigators, and holding his own terror for his granddaughter behind a wall of composure.

**Sabrina Bryna, Andrea Jennifer, and Ashley Erin** — the three youngest Key sisters — are in the townhouse too, and they become the quiet infrastructure of survival. They are not the ones making phone calls or answering detective questions. They are the ones holding Pandora when Mike's arms need to be free. They are the ones heating soup, changing wet sheets, sitting on the floor with a six-year-old who will not stop asking for her twin. They are the ones who let Kiley Rose cry in the kitchen without trying to fix it, because sometimes the most loving thing you can do is not look away.

This scene is about the sisters who are not the leads of the crisis but who hold the line anyway — and about Mike, who carries the weight of the entire family on his shoulders while a six-year-old clings to his neck.

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: Julia Leaves — Pandora Screams
- It is early morning, December 30. Julia has to leave for Key Porn Studios. The scene of her departure is outlined in the Pandora-and-Julia scene outline — the screaming, the clinging, Mike taking Pandora into his arms.
- But here, we stay with Pandora after Julia walks out. **Pandora's screaming does not stop.** She screams Brandi's name, Julia's name, her voice raw and cracking, her small body rigid against Mike's chest. She pounds her fists against his shoulder. She is not angry at Mike. She is angry at the universe that keeps taking people away from her.
- Mike holds her through it. He does not shush her. He does not tell her to be brave. He walks slowly through the townhouse, one arm under her, the other cradling her head against his shoulder, and he lets her scream. His jaw is set, his eyes are red, but his body is a wall — she cannot push through him, and she cannot fall.
- **Kiley Rose stands in the kitchen doorway and watches**, her hand over her mouth, her own tears silent. She wants to take Pandora from Mike, but she knows — Pandora is not reaching for her right now. Pandora wants Julia, and Julia is gone, and Mike is the next best thing.
- After twenty minutes, the screaming turns to hitching sobs. After thirty, Pandora is quiet — not because she is calm, but because her voice has given out. She lies against Mike's chest, her face pressed into his neck, her small hands still gripping his collar. She does not speak. She does not let go.

### Beat 2: Sabrina Bryna Takes Over
- Mike needs to take a call from the lead detective. He cannot hold Pandora and coordinate the search at the same time. He looks across the room and finds **Sabrina Bryna**.
- Sabrina is nineteen years old, deaf, living with cerebral palsy, and she has been sitting on the couch since dawn, watching the family fracture around her. She has not been able to do the things her sisters do — she cannot run upstairs, she cannot carry Pandora for hours, she cannot shout across the townhouse. But she can do this.
- Mike carries Pandora to the couch and sits down beside Sabrina. He shifts Pandora gently from his lap toward her. Pandora resists — her hands seize Mike's shirt — but Mike cups her face in his big hands and looks her in the eyes: *"Sabrina's got you, baby. I'll be right in the next room. I promise."*
- Pandora looks at Sabrina. Sabrina signs slowly, clearly, her movements steady despite her CP: *I'VE GOT YOU. I'M RIGHT HERE.* Pandora does not know ASL fluently the way the older cousins do, but she understands Sabrina — everyone understands Sabrina. Her hands are warm and certain.
- Pandora climbs into Sabrina's lap. She is too big for a lap — her legs dangle, her head rests against Sabrina's shoulder — but Sabrina wraps her arms around her and holds on. Her CP makes her grip uneven, one hand tighter than the other, but Pandora does not notice. She just feels arms around her, and that is enough.
- Sabrina holds her and rocks her, and when Pandora starts crying again — quieter now, the exhausted crying of a child who has nothing left — Sabrina signs against her back, the words pressed into her spine: *IT'S OKAY. YOU'RE NOT ALONE. I'VE GOT YOU.* Pandora cannot see the signs, but she feels the hands moving against her back, and the rhythm is its own kind of language.
- Mike steps into the study. The townhouse is quiet except for Pandora's breathing and Sabrina's steady hands.

### Beat 3: Andrea Jennifer and Ashley Erin in the Kitchen
- **Andrea Jennifer (seventeen) and Ashley Erin (fifteen)** are in the kitchen. They have been here since they woke up, moving around each other with the wordless efficiency of sisters who grew up in a house where the kitchen was always the center.
- Andrea Jennifer is heating soup on the stove — the second pot today, because no one ate the first one. She stirs mechanically, her jaw tight, her eyes on the pot. She is not crying. She is holding it together the way the middle sisters do — by doing things. Soup. Coffee. Dishes. Anything to keep her hands moving. Haden has been texting her all morning — *How are you? What can I do? Should I come over?* — and she has answered every message with one word: *Later.* She loves him. She does not have the space for him right now.
- Andrea Jennifer is heating soup on the stove — the second pot today, because no one ate the first one. She stirs mechanically, her jaw tight, her eyes on the pot. She is not crying. She is holding it together the way the middle sisters do — by doing things. Soup. Coffee. Dishes. Anything to keep her hands moving.
- Ashley Erin sits at the kitchen table, her phone face-down, her fingers drumming on the wood. She is fifteen, and the youngest Key sister is not supposed to be in a house where a six-year-old is missing, but no one is sending her home. She belongs here. She is family.
- They do not speak for a long time. The silence between them is not uncomfortable — it is the silence of two sisters who have shared a room, a life, a set of parents, and who do not need words to understand each other's pain.
- Then Ashley Erin says, quietly: *"I keep thinking about what she's eating. Brandi. Is she eating? Who's feeding her?"*
- Andrea Jennifer stops stirring. She does not turn around. Her shoulders tighten. She says: *"Don't."*
- *"I can't help it,"* Ashley Erin whispers. *"She's six, Andrea. She's six and she's out there and—"*
- Andrea Jennifer turns around and crosses the kitchen in three steps. She pulls Ashley Erin into a hug — hard, tight, the kind of hug that says *I am falling apart too but I will hold you first*. Ashley Erin presses her face into Andrea Jennifer's shoulder and breathes.
- Andrea Jennifer says into her hair: *"We are going to find her. Dad is going to find her. And when she comes home, I am going to make her so much soup she'll never want to eat again."*
- Ashley Erin laughs — a broken, wet laugh that is half a sob. It is the first sound of anything close to humor in the townhouse in four days.
- Kiley Rose appears in the doorway. She sees her two youngest sisters holding each other in the kitchen, and something in her chest loosens — not the grief, not the terror, but the loneliness. She is not carrying this alone. Her sisters are here. Her family is here. And they are breaking too, but they are breaking together.

### Beat 4: The Red Dress and Ashley Erin
- Later in the morning, Ashley Erin finds Pandora sitting on the couch with Sabrina, clutching Brandi's red Christmas dress. The dress is bunched up against her chest, her fingers twisted in the fabric.
- Ashley Erin sits down on the floor in front of them. She does not try to take the dress. She does not try to distract Pandora. She just sits there, cross-legged, and looks at her.
- After a long silence, Pandora says: *"Brandi wore this."*
- Ashley Erin nods. *"I know."*
- *"It still smells like her."*
- Ashley Erin reaches out and touches the edge of the dress, very gently. *"Then you keep holding it. As long as you need."*
- Pandora looks at her — this fifteen-year-old aunt who is not so much older than her, who still feels like a cousin, who is sitting on the floor in her socks because the couch is full. And Pandora does something she has not done since the kidnapping: she holds out a corner of the dress to Ashley Erin.
- Ashley Erin takes it. They sit there, holding opposite corners of the red dress, and it is the most sacred object in the townhouse. Sabrina watches them, her hands resting on Pandora's back, and she does not sign anything. Some moments do not need translation.

### Beat 5: Mike's Quiet Collapse
- Midday. Mike has been on the phone for three hours. The detectives have nothing new. The search has stalled. The trail outside the city has gone cold. Every call ends the same way: *"We're doing everything we can."*
- Mike hangs up the phone and sits at the desk for a long time. He does not move. His hands are flat on the table, his head bowed. The composure — the wall he has built for his daughters, for Kiley, for Pandora — cracks, just for a moment, in the empty study where no one can see him.
- His eyes fill. His jaw trembles. He presses his palms into the desk and breathes through it, forcing the wall back up, piece by piece, because if he falls apart, the whole townhouse falls apart. Mike Key is the foundation. Foundations do not crack where people can see.
- But the door is open a crack, and **Andrea Jennifer** sees him. She is walking past with two glasses of water, and she stops. She sees her father — the strongest man she has ever known, the man who threw Haden out of his mansion for mocking her, the man whose hands have held every one of his daughters through every crisis — sitting alone with his head bowed, his eyes wet.
- Andrea Jennifer does not go in. She does not call out to him. She stands in the hallway, holding the two glasses of water, and she lets him have his moment. Because she knows — the way children of strong parents always know — that her father's grief is his own, and that he will put the wall back up before anyone needs it.
- She waits until he straightens his shoulders, wipes his eyes, and picks up the phone again. Then she walks into the study, sets a glass of water next to his hand, kisses the top of his head, and walks out without a word.
- Mike picks up the glass, takes a sip, and makes the next call.

### Beat 6: Feeding Pandora
- Lunchtime. **Andrea Jennifer brings a bowl of applesauce to the living room.** She knows — because Kiley told her — that applesauce is the only thing Pandora will eat. She knows why. She does not ask questions.
- Mike is sitting in the armchair with Pandora on his lap. Sabrina is beside them on the couch, Ashley Erin on the floor at their feet. The whole room is holding its breath.
- Andrea Jennifer hands the bowl to Mike. He takes it with one hand, the other still around Pandora. He brings the spoon to her mouth, and Pandora opens her lips like a baby bird. She does not look at the spoon. She does not look at the food. She just opens her mouth, takes the applesauce, and swallows.
- *"Brandi hates applesauce,"* Pandora whispers.
- Andrea Jennifer, standing behind the chair, rests her hand on the back of Mike's shoulder and says, softly: *"I know, baby. She really does."*
- Mike feeds her the whole bowl, one slow spoonful at a time. Sabrina signs *GOOD JOB* when Pandora finishes, and Pandora does not react, but her grip on Mike's collar loosens slightly — not because she is letting go, but because she has been fed, and for a few minutes, her body feels like it belongs to her again.

### Beat 7: Sabrina Bryna and Mike — Signs in the Quiet
- After Pandora falls asleep on Mike's chest, Sabrina stays on the couch, her eyes on the sleeping child. Mike looks at Sabrina across the room. They do not speak — they sign.
- Sabrina signs: *HOW ARE YOU HOLDING UP?*
- Mike signs back, slowly, his big hands forming the words with the same care he has always used with her: *I'M HOLDING.*
- Sabrina signs: *YOU DON'T HAVE TO HOLD EVERYTHING.*
- Mike pauses. His hands rest on his knees. Then he signs: *SHE'S MY GRANDDAUGHTER. I HOLD WHAT I CAN.*
- Sabrina nods. She does not push. She knows Mike's grief is a load-bearing wall — if it comes down, the whole structure shifts. But she also knows that her father is human, and humans crack.
- She signs one more thing: *I'M HERE. WHATEVER YOU NEED.*
- Mike looks at her — his daughter, nineteen years old, deaf, cerebral palsy, holding a grieving six-year-old on a couch in a crisis — and he signs back: *I KNOW. THAT'S WHY YOU'RE HERE.*
- Ashley Erin, sitting on the floor, watches their hands move and does not interrupt. She does not know ASL as fluently as the older sisters, but she can read enough to understand: her father and her sister are taking care of each other in the language that belongs only to them.

### Beat 8: Pandora Falls Asleep on Ashley Erin — And Wets Herself
- Late afternoon. The light through the windows is grey and thin. Snow is falling.
- Mike is in the armchair, his arms empty for the first time all day, his phone in his hand, a text thread with the lead detective open. No new messages.
- Sabrina is on the couch, her hands still, her eyes closed. She has not let go of the cushion where Pandora was sitting, as if keeping the warmth.
- Andrea Jennifer is in the kitchen, washing the applesauce bowl, the water running over her hands. She is staring out the window at the snow. Her phone sits on the counter with Haden's unread messages.
- Ashley Erin is sitting on the couch beside Sabrina, her hair still damp from the shower, Pandora Paige curled up against her side in clean clothes, holding the red dress. She will not let go until Pandora reaches for it again.
- Then, slowly, Pandora shifts. She slides off Mike's lap and reaches for Ashley Erin. Mike steadies her, and Pandora climbs down to the floor and into Ashley Erin's lap. Ashley Erin wraps her arms around her — this small, shattered girl who is not so much younger than her, who feels more like a sister than a niece — and Pandora curls up against her chest, her fists still gripping the red dress, her face pressed into Ashley Erin's shoulder.
- Within minutes, **Pandora falls asleep in Ashley Erin's arms.** It is the first time she has fallen asleep on anyone but Julia or Mike since the kidnapping. Ashley Erin holds her and does not move. She will sit on that floor for two hours, her legs going numb, her back aching, and she will not move, because Pandora is sleeping, and that is the most precious thing this townhouse has right now.
- Then Ashley Erin feels it — warmth, spreading across her lap. **Pandora has wet herself in her sleep.** The urine soaks through Pandora's clothes and into Ashley Erin's jeans, warm and then cooling. It is not a little. Pandora's small body, exhausted beyond its limits, has simply let go completely.
- Ashley Erin freezes. She does not move. She does not make a sound. She looks down at Pandora's sleeping face — the tear-streaked cheeks, the slightly open mouth, the hands still gripping the red dress — and she makes a decision in less than a second: **she does not wake her.** Pandora has not slept more than an hour at a time since the kidnapping. Her night terrors have left her ragged. If Ashley Erin wakes her now, the screaming will start again, and the townhouse cannot take any more screaming.
- So Ashley Erin sits there. She sits in the wet, in the warmth turning cold, in the smell that no one in the room has noticed yet because they are all lost in their own exhaustion. She holds Pandora tighter and does not move a muscle.
- Mike notices first. He sees the slight shift in Ashley Erin's posture — the way she has stiffened, the way her jaw is set — and he understands immediately. He starts to rise from the armchair, but Ashley Erin catches his eye and shakes her head. A tiny, fierce shake. *Don't. Let her sleep.*
- Mike sits back down. His eyes are wet. He watches his fifteen-year-old daughter sit in a puddle of urine because waking a grieving six-year-old is worse than being uncomfortable. He has never been more proud of her. He has never been more heartbroken.
- After twenty more minutes, Pandora stirs. She blinks awake, confused, and then she feels it — the wet, the cold, the realization. Her face crumples with shame. *"I'm sorry,"* she whispers. *"I'm sorry, I'm sorry, I'm sorry—"*
- Ashley Erin holds her tighter. She does not flinch. She does not look disgusted. She strokes Pandora's hair and says, her voice steady and warm: *"You don't have to be sorry. Not ever. You're upset. Your body is upset. That's all. You did nothing wrong."* The same words Kiley and Julia said. The same truth. Ashley Erin is fifteen, and she is already the kind of woman who sits in the wet so a child can sleep.
- Andrea Jennifer appears with a towel and clean clothes. She does not comment. She does not make a face. She just kneels down, helps Ashley Erin gently lift Pandora, and wraps the towel around her. Ashley Erin stands up — her jeans dark and soaking — and she does not look down. She does not look embarrassed. She takes Pandora toward the bathroom, the red dress still clutched in Pandora's fist.
- Kiley Rose meets them in the hallway. She takes one look at Ashley Erin's soaked jeans and Pandora's ashamed face, and she does not flinch. She reaches out and takes Pandora from Ashley Erin's arms, holding her close. *"Let's get you cleaned up, baby."*
- But the moment Pandora realizes Ashley Erin is walking away, she panics. Her hands reach out from Kiley's arms, grasping at Ashley Erin's shirt, her voice breaking: *"Don't go. Don't go, Ashley. Please don't go."*
- Ashley Erin stops. She looks at Kiley Rose. Kiley looks at Pandora. And they both understand: Pandora cannot be separated from Ashley Erin right now. Not after what just happened. Not after the shame of wetting herself on her. Ashley Erin is the only person Pandora has fallen asleep on who is not Julia or Mike. She is safe. And Pandora will not let her out of her sight.
### Beat 9: The Bathroom — Bath, Shower, and the Steam Between Them
- **So they go into the bathroom together.** Kiley Rose draws a warm bath for Pandora, and Ashley Erin steps into the shower — **across the bathroom from the tub**, not beside it. There is no glass partition, no shared wall of steam. They are on opposite sides of the room, separated by tile and distance, but still in the same space, still visible to each other through the open air and the steam rising between them.
- Pandora sits in the warm bath, Kiley kneeling beside her, washing her gently with a soft cloth. Kiley does not comment on the accident. She does not make a face. She just washes her daughter's small body with the same slow, careful movements she has used since Pandora was a baby, and she hums softly — a tuneless, gentle sound that fills the bathroom and pushes the silence back.
- Across the bathroom, Ashley Erin stands under the hot water, her soaked jeans in a wet heap on the floor. She showers quickly, efficiently, keeping one eye on Pandora the entire time. She does not close the shower curtain — she leaves it open so Pandora can see her clearly through the steam, so Pandora knows she is still there, so the distance across the bathroom does not feel like abandonment.
- And Pandora watches. She sits in the bath, Kiley's hands moving over her back, and she watches Ashley Erin across the bathroom — the water falling, the steam rising, the outline of her aunt standing on the other side of the room, not leaving, not disappearing, not becoming another empty space in her life.
- **Pandora notices that Ashley Erin is different.** She has noticed before, the way children notice things — without judgment, without categories, just with open, curious eyes. She has seen the way Ashley Erin moves, the way her voice sometimes catches, the way the older sisters look at her with a specific kind of fierce protectiveness. And now, across the steam-filled bathroom, she sees Ashley Erin's body — the body that the world has opinions about, the body that Haden will one day mock, the body that Ashley Erin herself has fought to make her own — and six-year-old Pandora Paige does not see anything wrong.
- She sees her aunt. She sees the person who sat in the wet so she could sleep. She sees the person who held the other corner of the red dress. She sees someone who is different from her mommies, different from Julia, different from Sabrina — but not different in a way that matters. Different in the way that people are different. Different the way Brandi is different from Pandora, even though they are identical. Different the way the world is full of people who do not look the same and love the same anyway.
- Pandora does not say anything about it. She does not ask questions. She is six, and the concepts are beyond her, and the grief is too heavy for curiosity right now. But she files it away — the way a child files away the most important truths without knowing they are truths: *Ashley Erin is different, and Ashley Erin is the one who stayed, and that is all that matters.*
- Ashley Erin, across the bathroom, does not know what Pandora is thinking. She only knows that a six-year-old is watching her through the steam, and she does not cover herself, does not turn away, does not hide. She stands in the hot water and lets Pandora see her, because hiding would mean something is wrong, and nothing about Ashley Erin is wrong, and she will not teach a grieving child that her body is something to be ashamed of.
- Kiley washes Pandora's hair, her fingers gentle against her scalp, and Pandora closes her eyes. For a moment — just a moment — the warm water, her mother's hands, and Ashley Erin across the bathroom make the world feel almost safe. Almost whole. The missing half still aches — there is a Brandi-shaped hole in this bath, in this bathroom, in this house — but Ashley Erin is there, and Kiley is there, and the water is warm, and for a few minutes, that is enough.

### Beat 10: Getting Dressed — Ashley Erin Immediately, Pandora Refuses
- Ashley Erin steps out of the shower and **gets dressed immediately.** She does not linger. She does not stand around in a towel. She grabs her clean jeans from the counter, pulls them on, pulls on her shirt, and she is dressed within seconds — fast, efficient, almost instinctive. It is the habit of someone who has learned that the body is a public space, that people have opinions, that getting dressed quickly is a form of armor. She does not think about it. She just does it.
- But she does not leave. She sits down on the closed toilet lid, her hair wet, her clothes slightly damp from the steam, and she waits for Pandora. Because Pandora is still in the bath, still being washed by Kiley, still watching Ashley Erin with those open, trusting eyes.
- When the bath is done, Kiley wraps Pandora in a warm towel and lifts her out of the tub. She sets her on the bath mat and reaches for the clean clothes Andrea Jennifer left on the counter — underwear, a soft shirt, warm pants.
- **Pandora refuses to get dressed fully.** She lets Kiley put her underwear on, and then she stops. She will not put on the shirt. She will not put on the pants. She wraps the towel tighter around herself and shakes her head, her jaw set in a stubborn line that looks exactly like Brandi's.
- Kiley kneels in front of her. *"Baby, you need to get dressed. You'll be cold."*
- Pandora shakes her head again. *"No."*
- *"Why not?"*
- Pandora does not answer. But Kiley understands — the way mothers understand things their children cannot say. Pandora has been stripped of everything. Her sister, her bed, her clothes, her dignity. The wet, the shame, the accident. She has been washed and undressed and dressed by other people all day. **Her body is the only thing she has left, and she is refusing to cover it up on someone else's terms.** It is not about the clothes. It is about control. It is about a six-year-old saying: *You cannot take one more thing from me. I decide what goes on my body.*
- Kiley does not push. She sets the shirt and pants aside and wraps the towel around Pandora's shoulders like a cape. *"Okay. The towel stays. But you have to promise me you'll tell me when you're cold."*
- Pandora nods. She reaches for Ashley Erin's hand, and Ashley Erin takes it. They walk out of the bathroom together — a six-year-old in underwear and a towel, and a fifteen-year-old with wet hair, holding hands, the red dress still clutched in Pandora's other fist.

### Beat 11: Closing Image — The Townhouse Holds
- Late afternoon. The light through the windows is grey and thin. Snow is falling.
- Mike is in the armchair, his arms empty for the first time all day, his phone in his hand, a text thread with the lead detective open. No new messages.
- Sabrina is on the couch, her hands still, her eyes closed. She has not let go of the cushion where Pandora was sitting, as if keeping the warmth.
- Andrea Jennifer is in the kitchen, washing the applesauce bowl, the water running over her hands. She is staring out the window at the snow. Her phone sits on the counter with Haden's unread messages.
- Ashley Erin is sitting on the couch beside Sabrina, her hair still damp from the shower, **Pandora Paige curled up against her side in underwear and a towel**, holding the red dress. She will not let go until Pandora reaches for it again.
- Kiley Rose is in the study, on the phone with Rachel Darcy, her voice low: *"They're holding her. My sisters are holding her. I don't know what I'd do without them."*
- The townhouse holds. It holds because Mike's arms are strong, because Sabrina's hands are steady, because Andrea Jennifer makes soup, because Ashley Erin holds the dress, because the youngest sisters do the work that no one else thinks to do. It is not dramatic. It is not heroic. It is love in its most ordinary, most essential form — showing up, sitting down, and not leaving.
- Outside, the snow keeps falling. Somewhere beyond the city limits, Brandi Jessica is in a hotel room with a man who laughs at her. But here, in this townhouse, she is not forgotten. Every person in this room is holding a piece of her, and they will not let go until she comes home.
