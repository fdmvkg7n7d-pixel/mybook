# SCENE OUTLINE: THE DEATH OF MIKE KEY

*This outline bridges the gap between Mike's life as a patriarch and his sudden absence. It resolves the Tier 1 questions about the location and details of his death, the immediate notification chain, and the empty days between his death and his funeral.*

---

## CANON BACKDROP & HEART HISTORY

* **The Nitroglycerine Vial:** For twenty years, Mike lived with stable angina and coronary artery disease. He carried a small, amber glass vial of sublingual nitroglycerine pills in his right trousers pocket at all times. Tapping a tiny pill out under his tongue after climbing stairs or lifting heavy firewood was a quiet, familiar ritual. 
* **The Normalcy of Illness:** Because he managed his condition for two decades without complaining or slowing down, the family had grown to treat his heart condition as a manageable chronic inconvenience rather than a mortal threat. "Dad's nitro" was just part of who he was—like his OPP mug or his grey cardigans.
* **The Sudden Failure:** The heart attack that kills him is a massive, catastrophic myocardial infarction (a "widowmaker" blockage). It is an event that no amount of sublingual nitroglycerine could reverse.
* **The World's Greatest Fortune:** Mike is the wealthiest man in the world, having built an unfathomable global empire. Yet, he lived a remarkably grounded and private life, prioritizing his family, learning ASL to connect with Sabrina, and working as a detective/consultant with the OPP because he believed in justice and personal duty. This wealth is why a $25 million gift from Terri Anne on the night of his funeral is a drop in the ocean of his estate, though it represents the entire future for Gamaliel and Klára.

---

## BEAT 1 — THE LAST MORNING: ROUTINE AND THE TINTED GLASS

The scene begins early in the morning at the Mike and Terri Anne mansion. It is a Tuesday. 

* **The Detail:** Mike is in his kitchen, making coffee. He experiences a mild, familiar twinge of chest tightness as he reaches for the high cabinet. He reaches into his pocket, pulls out the amber vial, and lets one tiny pill drop sublingually. He waits. The tightness recedes. 
* **The Witness:** Terri Anne comes down in her pale-green dressing gown. She sees him put the vial back in his pocket. It’s an unspoken exchange they’ve had thousands of times over twenty years: *Are you alright?* *I'm fine, just the usual.* She kisses his cheek, pours her coffee, and they sit at the maple island. It is a perfectly ordinary morning.

---

## BEAT 2 — THE MOMENT ITSELF: THE STUDY

It is mid-afternoon. The house is quiet. Klára and Gamaliel are at the Victoria Ballet Academy; the daughters are scattered at work and with their own families. 

* **The Setting:** Mike is in his study, sitting in the reading chair by the east window—the same chair where Terri Anne will sit on the night of the funeral. He is reviewing some old cold-case files an OPP contact sent over.
* **The Attack:** The pain hit him not as a familiar squeeze, but as a crushing, heavy weight, like a concrete block dropped onto his sternum. It is hot, sharp, and radiates instantly down his left arm and up into his jaw. 
* **The Struggle for the Pills:** Mike realizes immediately that this is different. He reaches for his right pocket. His fingers are clumsy, numb, and shaking. He manages to pull the small amber vial out, but as he tries to pry the cap off with his thumb, his hand spasms. 
* **The Spilled Medicine:** The vial slips. It hits the wooden floorboards, the plastic cap popping off. Dozens of tiny white nitroglycerine pills roll across the hardwood, scattering into the dust beneath the bookcase. 
* **How He is Found:** Terri Anne, coming down the hall to ask him if he wants tea, hears the heavy, wet thud of him collapsing to the floor. She runs in to find him on his knees, leaning against the chair, clutching his chest.

---

## BEAT 3 — THE WINDOW OF RESUSCITATION: THE HARDWOOD FLOOR

* **The Desperate Attempt:** Terri Anne drops to her knees beside him. She sees the scattered white pills on the dark wood. She grabs one from the floor, trying to place it under his tongue, but his jaw is already tight, his breathing shallow and agonal.
* **The 911 Call:** Terri Anne dial 911 on speakerphone, placing it on the floorboards. She begins chest compressions. She is a strong woman, and she performs them rhythmically, counting out loud, her knees pressing into the floor. She knows the CPR is not working, but she does not stop.
* **The OPP Arrival:** The sirens approach. Because the dispatch officer recognizes the name "Mike Key," the local OPP detachment sends two cruisers ahead of the ambulance. The officers—men who knew Mike, who had worked under him—arrive with their sirens silenced as they enter the driveway, a final quiet respect for the patriarch.
* **The Declaration:** The paramedics take over, administering shocks and epinephrine, but the monitor remains a flat, green line. Mike is pronounced dead on the floor of his study, surrounded by the scattered, useless white pills he had carried for twenty years to keep himself alive.

---

## BEAT 4 — THE NOTIFICATION CHAIN: BIRTH ORDER AND ASL

The OPP officers offer to make the calls, but Terri Anne refuses. *"This is my family,"* she says. *"We tell our own."* She calls her eldest daughter first.

### 1. Patti Anne (The Anchor)
* **The Call:** Patti Anne is in her kitchen, cutting fruit for the twins. When Terri Anne's name appears on the screen, she answers with her usual busy tone. Terri Anne’s voice is steady but hollow: *"Patti Anne. Your father is gone."*
* **The Transition:** Patti Anne drops the knife. Her world tilts, but her eldest-daughter instinct takes over. She tells Terri Anne she is coming. She instructs Aleksander to watch the twins, grabs her keys, and prepares to deliver the news to her sisters.

### 2. Jessica Isabella and Rachel Darcy (The Twins)
* **The Notification:** Patti Anne calls Rachel first because Jessica is in a meeting. Rachel Darcy immediately senses the change in Patti Anne’s voice. Patti Anne tells her: *"Dad had a heart attack. He didn't make it."*
* **The Connection:** Rachel immediately hangs up and drives to Jessica's office. They are twins; they must receive this together. When Rachel walks into Jessica's workspace without warning, pale and silent, Jessica looks up and knows. They hold each other in the lobby, the twin body-memory taking over before either speaks a word.

### 3. Kiley Rose (The Middle Anchor)
* **The Notification:** Patti Anne calls Kiley Rose. Kiley is at SickKids visiting a client. When she hears the news, her first instinct is a wave of old, irrational guilt—*did I do something? did I stress him?*—before Patti Anne's firm voice anchors her: *"Kiley, his heart just gave out. It was twenty years of wear. It is nobody's fault."*

### 4. Sabrina Bryna (The Deaf Daughter - The In-Person Rule)
* **The Family Rule:** Patti Anne refuses to tell Sabrina over the phone through a relay service or via text. That would violate Mike's ironclad rule: *no child is excluded from the truth just because telling it requires more care.*
* **The Drive:** Patti Anne drives to Sabrina's Vaughan home. When Sabrina opens the door, she sees her sister’s face and the red rims of her eyes. 
* **The ASL:** Patti Anne stands in the hallway. Her ASL is functional, though not as fluent as Mike's was. With shaking hands, she signs: **FATHER — HEART — STOP — DEAD.**
* **The Grief:** Sabrina lets out a high, thin sound—the raw sound of someone whose primary linguistic link to her family has just been severed. She collapses into Patti Anne’s arms. Little Carlota Delicia (a toddler) watches from the stairs, memorizing the shape of her mother's hands in grief.

### 5. The Younger Sisters (Fallon Glenda, Andrea Jennifer, Ashley Erin)
* **The Ripple:** Patti Anne, now with Sabrina and Aleksander, calls the three youngest sisters in order. 
* **Ashley Erin:** The youngest, receives the call while at her design studio. She hears her dead father’s voice in her head, the only father who never hesitated to call her *Ashley*. She sits in her car in the parking lot for an hour, clutching the steering wheel, mourning the man who gave her a name without demanding a defense.

---

## BEAT 5 — THE INTERVAL BETWEEN DEATH AND FUNERAL

Catholic funerals usually take place 3–5 days after death. During these days, the Mike and Terri Anne mansion becomes an emotional fortress.

* **The Gathering of the Eight:** One by one, the eight daughters and their families move into the mansion. The rooms are filled with sleeping bags, suitcases, and the soft hum of children being kept quiet.
* **The Language of Grief (Shared ASL):** In these heavy days before the funeral, the most striking aspect of the mansion is the quiet, expressive movement of hands. Because Mike and Terri Anne insisted from the very beginning that all of their daughters learn American Sign Language so that Sabrina Bryna would never be left out of a single family moment, every sister is fluent. Rather than falling back into spoken English and leaving Sabrina on the periphery, the family surrounds her. The daughters communicate largely in ASL, letting the spoken words fade where silence is gentler. They take turns sitting with Sabrina, signing stories of their father, ensuring she is at the absolute center of their shared grief. The family's default language becomes Mike’s language—the ultimate proof that the rules he and Terri Anne built outlast his physical presence.
* **The Clean-Up:** On the second night, Terri Anne and Patti Anne go into the study to clean. Patti Anne finds three of the tiny white nitroglycerine pills that had rolled under the bookcase. She picks them up, her hands trembling. 
* **The Choice:** Terri Anne takes the three pills from her daughter's hand. She doesn't throw them away. She puts them into a small porcelain dish on the desk. *"Twenty years he carried them,"* she whispers. *"They did their job for twenty years, Patti. They gave us twenty more years with him. Don't think of them as a failure. Think of them as a loan that finally came due."*

---

## WHAT THIS OUTLINE RESOLVES

1. **Location & Circumstances:** Mike dies in his study at the mansion, in his reading chair, in the mid-afternoon.
2. **Who is with him:** He is alone when the attack begins, but Terri Anne finds him immediately after he collapses and performs CPR.
3. **The Role of the Nitroglycerine:** The amber vial is dropped; the pills scatter across the hardwood. This provides a poignant, physical symbol of the transition from a managed chronic illness to a sudden death.
4. **The Notification Order:** Terri Anne calls Patti Anne first. Patti Anne manages the chain, delivering the news in birth order.
5. **The ASL Rule:** Patti Anne goes in person to tell Sabrina Bryna in ASL, upholding the family rule that no child is excluded from the truth because of their language.
6. **The Blank Days:** Establishes the beautiful, silent, and visually expressive atmosphere of the mansion in the days before the funeral, highlighting how the family unites in ASL to grieve together and keep Sabrina Bryna at the heart of the family.
