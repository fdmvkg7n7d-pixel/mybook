# Terri Anne Calls Patti Anne — Scene Outline

- **Characters:** Patti Anne Key (twenty-seven years old), Gamaliel Galvin Key (six years old), Klára Key (six years old), Terri Anne Key (offstage / on phone)
- **Offstage / referenced characters:** Brandi Jessica Key (six years old), Pandora Paige Key (six years old), Kiley Rose Key (twenty-one years old), Mike Key, Julia, the Key sisters, the Kids' Classmates, Klára’s biological mother (Catherine Fritz, who is dying of breast cancer)

- **When:** December 23, 2051, afternoon (A few hours after Brandi Jessica’s 9:00 AM pediatric diagnosis of Bilateral Retinoblastoma in New York, and right before the holiday gathering)
- **Status:** Updated scene outline
- **Tone:** Tender, emotional, supportive, reflecting on maternal duty, sibling solidarity, childhood empathy, and hidden grief

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene outlines the emotional telephone call when **Terri Anne Key calls her twenty-seven-year-old eldest daughter Patti Anne to tell her about Brandi Jessica’s retinoblastoma diagnosis**. 

The call occurs at **Patti Anne's mansion**, where she is raising her **six-year-old son, Gamaliel Galvin**. Her foster daughter, **six-year-old Klára Key** (who is currently a Grade One classmate with Gamaliel at Victoria Ballet Academy), is visiting their home. 

*(Note: In June, Klára joined Patti Anne’s household as a foster daughter. However, **Klára is not her foster sister when her mother is dying.** During this period in December 2051, Klára’s biological mother, Catherine Fritz, is actively dying of Breast Cancer. To ensure absolute emotional and historical accuracy, **Aleksander is completely removed / not present in this scene**, as Patti Anne navigates this tender and heavy season as a single foster parent. Crucially, **Patti Anne does not know about Klára's mother's condition yet**, making Klára's silent carrying of this massive grief all the more poignant and heartbreaking).*

Patti Anne is preparing to **take her six-year-old son, Gamaliel Galvin, to Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, to see where his father comes from.** The call introduces a heartbreaking conflict: Patti Anne’s fierce desire to cancel their trip and fly immediately to New York to stand with Kiley during this medical crisis, contrasted with her deep, protective love for Gamaliel, who is completely excited and has been **telling his Victoria Ballet Academy classmates—especially Klára—all about his upcoming trip.** 

Crucially, **Klára is trying her best to be happy for Gamaliel, but she is carrying a crushing, silent burden: she knows that her mother is currently dying from Breast Cancer.** This establishes a deeply poignant, parallel theme of maternal fragility, childhood grief, and the unyielding protective circle of the family.

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: The Kitchen at the Mansion — Birthday and Rio Excitement
- It is the afternoon of December 23, 2051, inside Patti Anne's sprawling mansion in Vaughan, Ontario. 
- Six-year-old **Gamaliel Galvin Key** has just come home from his Grade One class at **Victoria Ballet Academy**. He is vibrating with absolute, pure childhood excitement.
- Patti Anne (27) is preparing to **take Gamaliel to Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, for the holidays to see where his father comes from**—a journey of discovery they have been planning so he can connect with his heritage.
- Gamaliel is running around the kitchen, packed and ready, happily chatter-boxing about seeing the beaches and learning capoeira in Rio.

### Beat 2: Gamaliel Tells Klára About the Trip
- Six-year-old Grade One classmate **Klára Key** sits quietly at the kitchen table, watching Gamaliel pack his small travel journal.
- Earlier today at Victoria Ballet Academy, **Gamaliel Galvin had enthusiastically told all his classmates, especially Klára, about his upcoming trip.** He describes the giant ocean, the sandy beaches, and how he is going to find where his father lived.
- **Klára’s Silent Burden:** Klára is trying her absolute best to smile and be happy for her classmate. But behind her quiet, brave face, **she is carrying a crushing weight: she knows that her biological mother (Catherine Fritz) is currently dying from Breast Cancer.** 
- **Patti Anne Doesn't Know:** Klára is not Patti Anne's foster sister at this point; she is her classmate, visiting her home. Patti Anne has no idea about Klára's mother's terminal cancer yet. 
- The contrast is heartbreaking: Gamaliel is going to find his father's roots in a bright, sun-soaked city, while Klára's mother is fading away in a clinical room. She holds her mother's delicate necklace tightly in her small hand, seeking silent comfort, but she doesn't dim Gamaliel's joy.

### Beat 3: The Ringing Phone from New York
- The phone rings. Patti Anne answers, expecting a routine Christmas check-in from New York.
- But Terri Anne's voice on the other end is heavy, low, and laced with absolute, raw maternal pain.
- Terri Anne delivers the crushing news: **six-year-old Brandi Jessica has just been diagnosed with advanced Bilateral Retinoblastoma** during her nine o'clock check-up in New York today. Her enucleation (eye surgery) is scheduled for **January**.

### Beat 4: The Heartbreaking Conflict
- Patti Anne (27) goes completely pale, her hand clutching her phone. She looks across the kitchen at her six-year-old son, who is currently showing Klára his toy airplane, and Klára, who is gently nodding with a brave, sad smile.
- Patti Anne’s first instinct is to drop everything, cancel the flight, and take her son straight to New York:

> “Mom, we’re coming to New York. We’ll change our tickets right now. I will pack the bags and drive down tonight. Kiley (twenty-one) needs us.”

- But Terri Anne, speaking with soft, unyielding maternal wisdom, stops her:

> “No, Patti Anne. You are not coming to New York. You are taking Gamaliel to Rio de Janeiro. He has been telling his whole class about seeing his father's home. If you cancel this now, it will break his heart. Brandi's surgery isn't until January. We have Mike, Sabrina, Andrea, and Ashley here at the townhouse, and Rachel is on her way. You take your son to Brazil and give him his roots. You stand by your child first.”

- Terri Anne adds gently, noting Klára's presence in the background: *"And Patti Anne... hold Klára close. I know her heart is heavy right now. We are going to protect all of our children, every single one."*

### Beat 5: Sibling Solidarity and Asymmetry
- Patti Anne hangs up, tears streaming down her face. She pulls both Klára and Gamaliel into a tight, warm hug right there on the kitchen floor. 
- She holds Klára extra close, kissing her forehead. Patti Anne connects deeply with Klára's silent, visible sadness, though **she doesn't know about her mother's cancer yet**:

> “Klára, sweetheart, I know. I know you're carrying a lot today, and I am so proud of how brave you are. We are going to make sure you have a beautiful Christmas, and no one is going to separate us.”

- **Aleksander is Not Present:** Since Aleksander is completely removed and not present in this scene, Patti Anne acts as the single parent/guardian figure, carrying these heavy emotional burdens with absolute, single-maternal strength. She coordinates Klára's safe caretaker arrangements for the holiday.

### Beat 6: Closing Image — Heading to the Airport
- Gamaliel runs to get his winter coat, holding his passport with pure excitement: *"Mom! It's time! We're going to Rio!"*
- Klára looks at Gamaliel, her hand still holding her mother's delicate necklace. She smiles at him—a real, genuine classmate smile this time: *"Have fun, Gamaliel. Show me pictures of the ocean."*
- Patti Anne wipes her tears, her maternal shield completely intact. She kisses Klára, ensuring she is tucked in safely with a trusted caretaker/relative for the holidays, and grabs Gamaliel's hand.
- As they walk out of the Vaughan mansion to head to the airport, Patti Anne looks back at Klára standing on the porch. Her heart is heavy with the medical shadows over Manhattan and Klára's quiet sadness, completely unaware of her mother's dying battle, but surrounded by the quiet, unyielding courage of her family, she steps forward to give her son his journey of discovery, completely united.
