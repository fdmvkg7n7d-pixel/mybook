# SCENE OUTLINE: TERRI ANNE TAKES PATTI ANNE TO THE GREENHOUSES (THE FIRST HOMESCHOOLING ACTIVITY)

**Characters:** 
- **Terri Anne Williams-Key**
- **Patti Anne Key** (≈4 years old)
- **Aldoma** (the nanny)

**Setting:** The Carlisle mansion property — three distinct climate-controlled greenhouses: one for vegetables, one for herbs, and one for fresh flowers. Morning light filtering through glass.

**Timeframe:** Shortly after the kitchen-table transparency conversation (~2026). This is the first deliberate homeschooling activity Mike and Terri Anne implement.

---

## SYNOPSIS

This scene shows the philosophy in action. After deciding to homeschool and promising transparency, Terri Anne begins the girls’ education with something real and alive: the three greenhouses on their own property. She takes Patti Anne (with Aldoma’s quiet support) to explore plants, growth, and care. The scene incorporates Aldoma’s multilingual influence and includes the intimate detail of Terri Anne’s expressed milk being used for the twins.

---

## BACKGROUND ON ALDOMA (for reference)

Aldoma was hired when Patti Anne was fourteen months old. She has over twenty-five years of experience as a nanny. Originally from Switzerland, her parents were Polish immigrants. From the very beginning, she has spoken naturally to the children in French, German, and Polish during daily routines — without making it feel like formal lessons.

---

## THE SCENE

### Beat 1 — Early morning in the kitchen

- It is a bright morning a few days after the kitchen-table conversation. Patti Anne is still in her underwear from breakfast. Terri Anne, already dressed in comfortable clothes suitable for the greenhouses, kneels in front of her daughter.
- *“Would you like to come with me this morning? I want to show you something special.”*
- Patti Anne nods, curious. Terri Anne helps her into a light sweater and soft shoes.

### Beat 2 — Aldoma prepares the twins’ bottles

- Aldoma is at the refrigerator. She takes out **two glass bottles** filled with **Terri Anne’s expressed milk**.
- She moves calmly and efficiently, warming the bottles while speaking softly to the twins in their high chairs.
- As she works, she smiles at Patti Anne and says in a warm, natural voice:
  > *“Bonne journée, ma petite. Amuse-toi bien dans les serres.”*  
  > *(“Have a good day, my little one. Have fun in the greenhouses.”)*

- Patti Anne, already accustomed to Aldoma’s languages, replies simply: *“Merci, Aldoma.”*
- Aldoma adds in German: *“Viel Spaß, Liebes.”* Patti Anne beams.

### Beat 3 — Walking to the greenhouses

- Terri Anne and Patti Anne walk hand-in-hand across the manicured grounds. In the distance, three large glass structures stand in a row.
- Terri Anne speaks conversationally: *“This is part of our school now. Not just books. Real things. Things that grow.”*
- Patti Anne looks up. *“Like the plants?”*
- *“Yes. Like the plants. And like you.”*

### Beat 4 — The three greenhouses

- They reach the greenhouses. Terri Anne explains each one as they pass:
  - **Vegetable Greenhouse**: Rows of tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers, and leafy greens.
  - **Herb Greenhouse**: Basil, rosemary, thyme, lavender, mint, and more.
  - **Fresh Flower Greenhouse**: Orchids, roses, lilies, and seasonal blooms.

- Terri Anne says: *“We have three different ones. One for vegetables we eat, one for the herbs we cook with, and one for the flowers that make the house beautiful.”*

### Beat 5 — Entering the Herb Greenhouse first

- They step inside the herb greenhouse. The air is warm, fragrant, and humid.
- Terri Anne lifts Patti Anne so she can see the higher shelves. *“These are the herbs. Basil, rosemary, thyme.”*
- She lets Patti Anne touch the leaves gently. *“Feel how soft this one is. Smell this one.”*

### Beat 6 — Teaching through observation

- Terri Anne guides Patti Anne’s attention without lecturing.
- *“See how this little plant is reaching toward the light? It knows where it wants to go. That’s like you. You’re growing too.”*
- She points to a tray of seedlings. *“These were just seeds two weeks ago. Aldoma helped me plant them. Now look how tall they are.”*
- Patti Anne watches, fascinated. *“They’re babies, like Jessica and Rachel.”*
- Terri Anne smiles. *“Exactly. We have to take care of them every day, just like we take care of your sisters.”*

### Beat 7 — Multilingual elements

- As they move through the greenhouse, Terri Anne incorporates Aldoma’s influence naturally.
- She points to a row of lavender and says in French: *“Lavande.”* Then in English: *“Lavender.”*
- Patti Anne repeats: *“Lavande.”*
- Terri Anne nods approvingly. *“Aldoma will teach you more words like that. French, German, Polish. You already know some, don’t you?”*
- Patti Anne nods proudly. *“Aldoma says ‘Guten Morgen’ in the morning.”*

### Beat 8 — Moving through the other greenhouses

- They visit the **Vegetable Greenhouse** next. Terri Anne shows Patti Anne the tomatoes and explains they will pick some later for lunch.
- Then they enter the **Fresh Flower Greenhouse**. The colors and scents are striking. Patti Anne is quiet with wonder.
- Terri Anne kneels beside her. *“This is our classroom today. Not a building with bells. Just us, the plants, and whatever we notice.”*

### Beat 9 — Returning home with purpose

- Before they leave, Terri Anne lets Patti Anne choose one small herb plant to carry back. Patti Anne selects a basil plant.
- As they walk back, Terri Anne says: *“We’ll put this on the windowsill. Every morning you can check on it. That’s part of school too — taking care of living things.”*
- Patti Anne walks taller, holding the plant carefully with both hands.

### Beat 10 — Closing the loop

- Back in the kitchen, Aldoma is feeding the twins with the bottles of Terri Anne’s milk. Mike is now at the table.
- Patti Anne proudly shows everyone her basil plant. *“This is from our school.”*
- Terri Anne meets Mike’s eyes over the children’s heads. The look says: *This is how we begin.*

---

## THEMES EXPRESSED

- **Learning through life**: Education is experiential and tied to the family’s land.
- **Three distinct greenhouses**: Vegetables, Herbs, and Fresh Flowers create a rich, varied classroom.
- **Aldoma’s integration**: Her languages and daily care (including preparing Terri Anne’s milk) are woven naturally into the morning.
- **Parental presence**: Terri Anne is fully present with her daughter.
- **Continuity with transparency**: The morning begins with the promise made at the kitchen table.
