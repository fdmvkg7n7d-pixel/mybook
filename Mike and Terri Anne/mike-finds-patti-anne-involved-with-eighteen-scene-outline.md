## SCENE PURPOSE & THEMATIC CORE

In a family that radically removes societal body shame and fosters absolute respect for consensual adult agency, Mike walking in on his seventeen-year-old eldest daughter involved with an eighteen-year-old in Cap d'Agde is a load-bearing authorial test.

By the end of the scene:
1. Mike walks into their beachfront Cap d'Agde quarters late at night to find seventeen-year-old Patti Anne involved with an eighteen-year-old boy.
2. Mike does not roar, execute an unhinged "overprotective dad" trope, or threaten the boy.
3. Mike demonstrates quiet, heavy paternal disappointment, simply asking the boy to stand up and offering him a polite, unmistakable cue to leave immediately.
4. **When the eighteen-year-old leaves, Mike and Patti Anne have a lengthy conversation about boundaries.**
5. Mike explicitly names his core worry: he is just worried about the younger girls walking into an open room if she would be involved, installing the core lesson of genuine relational Fiduciary responsibility.
6. **The Behavior Promise & Voices Disclosure:** Seventeen-year-old Patti Anne promises that she will try her behavior better, but intimately confesses that she sometimes hears voices in her head, asking Mike if this is normal.
7. **Loving Paternal Care & Family Doctor Promise:** Mike behaves with profound love and care toward how Patti Anne feels, receiving her disclosure with complete steadiness and promising that when they return home to Carlisle, he personally will take her to their family doctor to secure the exact room and tools she deserves.

**Core Saga Rule Established:** *A Fiduciary patriarch does not guard an adult daughter by locking her sexuality in a tower; he protects her and the household by sitting with her to ensure she understands that true adult autonomy means holding an absolute physical shield for the innocence of the younger ones.*

## SCENE OUTLINE — BEATS & PROSE CUES

### Beat 1 — The Steamy Mediterranean Night
It is well past midnight in Cap d'Agde. The private beachfront luxury compound is completely still, accompanied only by the distant, rhythmic shushing of Mediterranean waves breaking on Plage Naturiste.

In the back rooms of the villa, Jessica (15), Rachel (15), Kiley (12), Sabrina (11 *resting after her non-wheelchair stage triumph*), Fallon (8), Andrea (7), and little Ashley Erin (5 *sleeping with her third-place ribbon and single flower*) are deeply asleep under their lightweight summer sheets. 

Out on the expansive teak pool decking (or in the large sliding-door living room), seventeen-year-old family anchor Patti Anne is quietly and intimately involved with this eighteen-year-old boy. Here in Cap d'Agde, where residual societal armor and heavy coats are set aside, their closeness feels completely unblemished, honest, and natural.

Suddenly, the wooden security gate of the compound clicks open.

---

### Beat 2 — Mike Key Walks In
Mike Key walks onto the limestone coping, returning from his quiet evening by the sea. He walks toward the lit living area.

He stops dead in the open glass doorway.

Keep the prose remarkably clean, practical, and unadorned:
* The visual: Seventeen-year-old Patti Anne and the eighteen-year-old boy on the velvet indoor-outdoor daybed, their motion suddenly freezing in the warm terrace light;
* The incredible physical scale of Mike Key filling the threshold, his immense shoulders and heavy OPP-detective eyes reading the entire posture of the room in a fraction of a second;
* The boy's throat swallowing hard, naturally intimidated upon realizing he is eye to eye with Mike Key.

---

### Beat 3 — The Unshielded Stand (Patti Anne's Composure)
This beat aligns Patti Anne's true coming-of-age capability with the core philosophy of Cap d'Agde.

Patti Anne does not gasp, scramble for a blanket, or stammer apologies. She calmly rises from the daybed, stands her absolute full height in her unadorned physical truth or light wrapper, and faces her father directly.

Her bipolar mind is remarkably steady. She proves that radical adult agency in Cap d'Agde is just as disciplined, honorable, and unblemished as ordinary swimming in the sun.

> *"Dad. This is [Name TBD]. He's eighteen."*

She names his age out loud to establish absolute consensual validity.

---

### Beat 4 — Mike's Paternal Disappointment & The Boy's Departure
Because Mike Key is a master detective who reads bodies and motives flawlessly, he does not roar or execute an unhinged "overprotective dad" trope. Instead, his heavy OPP-detective eyes carry profound, quiet **disappointment**. He steps across the threshold and looks entirely at the eighteen-year-old boy.

Mike speaks in his remarkably deep, low, resonant voice:

> *"Stand up, son."*

The eighteen-year-old boy stands up. His legs might shake under the immense physical scale of Mike Key filling the doorway, but to his credit, he meets Mike's eyes. 

Mike does not threaten him with a weapon. He gives him a firm, unmistakable, spacious cue to leave the compound immediately:

> *"Bid Patti Anne goodnight. It's time for you to go."*

The boy quickly shakes Mike's massive hand, whispers a respectful goodnight to Patti Anne, and walks out. The wooden security gate clicks shut behind him, leaving Mike and his seventeen-year-old eldest daughter alone in the warm Mediterranean night.

---

### Beat 5 — The Lengthy Boundary Conversation
**When the eighteen-year-old leaves, Mike and Patti Anne have a lengthy, emotionally profound conversation about boundaries.**

Mike reaches into his pocket, pulls out his tiny amber glass vial of sublingual nitroglycerine pills, and sets it calmly on the teak side table. He sits down on the daybed across from seventeen-year-old Patti Anne. 

Mike explains the exact source of his disappointment. He is not upset that she is seventeen, that she possesses adult autonomy, or that she is involved with a boy. He is disappointed by her complete failure to hold the overarching Fiduciary shield for their shared household.

He names his load-bearing core worry:
> *"I'm not here to guard your perimeter or lock your body in a tower, Patti Anne. You're seventeen, practically a legal adult, and your choices are entirely yours. But you're also the eldest sister in a family compound where seven younger girls sleep just down the hall."*

A deep, solemn pause.
> *"Children get up in the middle of the night. They wake up thirsty, or after a bad dream. If little five-year-old Ashley Erin or eight-year-old Fallon walked into this open living room looking for comfort, and walked into you involved with a boy, it would completely shatter their unblemished sanctuary."*

This reframes parental rules into genuine relational Fiduciary responsibility: *True coming-of-age autonomy means recognizing that adult intimacy absolutely belongs behind a closed, locked door so the innocence of the younger ones is flawlessly protected.*

---

### Beat 6 — Patti Anne's Promise & The Disclosure of the Voices
Seventeen-year-old Patti Anne listens flawlessly. She realizes that being the overarching family anchor requires practicing radical relational restraint. She fully accepts the boundary, giving Mike her solemn word that she will try her behavior better and promising that her private life will always stay behind a closed, locked door.

But then, the conversation pivots into something exceptionally raw and intimate.

Patti Anne sits forward, looking down at the warm teak decking. She realizes she cannot hold her strong facade entirely. She confesses something she has been carrying quietly:

> *"I promise I'll try my behavior better, Dad. I swear. But... sometimes I hear voices in my head."*

A heavy, incredibly fragile silence settles over the Mediterranean night.
She looks up at Mike, her bipolar mind suddenly unshielded and frightened. She asks him:

> *"Is this normal? Dad... is it normal to hear voices in my head?"*

---

### Beat 7 — Mike's Loving Care & The Family Doctor Promise
Mike Key does not flinch. He listens with immense love and care to exactly how Patti Anne feels, receiving her frightening disclosure with complete, reality-grounded warmth. He is the deeply steady patriarch who built a household on the absolute rule of unblemished family truth.

Mike reaches out and rests his immense, heavy, warm palm on Patti Anne's bare shoulder, grounding her shaking frame with absolute physical counter-pressure.

Mike says honestly:
> *"It's information, Patti Anne. It's just your mind processing the heavy things you carry. You don't ever have to be frightened of how you feel, and you never have to hide anything from me or Terri Anne."*

He validates her underlying emotions completely, and then delivers an absolutely empowering, concrete medical promise. True to his lifelong Fiduciary character, he does not leave her to navigate the healthcare system alone.

> *"When we are back home in Carlisle, I will personally take you to our family doctor. We'll sit down together, and we'll get you the exact room, care, and tools you deserve."*

**Closing Image:**  
Mike picks up his amber vial and migrates down the dim hallway toward his master suite where Terri Anne is awaiting him. Seventeen-year-old family anchor Patti Anne Key stands on the warm Cap d'Agde decking, looking out at the glittering Mediterranean Sea. The domestic sanctuary holds secure. Her private life is canonically bounded behind a locked door. And the deepest, most terrifying secret of her bipolar mind has finally been unshielded, named, and met with Mike's deeply loving promise to personally take her to their family doctor upon their return to Carlisle.
