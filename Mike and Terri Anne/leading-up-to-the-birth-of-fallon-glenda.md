# Leading Up to the Birth of Fallon Glenda — Outline

## 1. Setting the Family Stage (Autumn–Winter 2032)
- **Mike and Terri Anne Key** are expecting their sixth daughter.
- **Patti Anne** is now ~10, the family’s eldest anchor, already fluent in responsibility and household management.
- **Jessica Isabella and Rachel Darcy** are ~8, conjoined twins, navigating the world as a pair with increasing coordination and shared language.
- **Kiley Rose** is ~4, small for her age because of Turner Syndrome, walking with determination and attending regular endocrinology checkups. She is the household’s bright, watchful presence.
- **Sabrina Bryna** is ~2.5, deaf, with emerging signs of Cerebral Palsy. She is already a full member of the family’s ASL household, signing *milk, more, love, mommy, daddy* with her small hands. She moves with the early spasticity that the family has already begun to accommodate.
- The household is still living under the shadow of the 2030 kidnapping. Security measures are embedded in daily life: cameras, a gated drive, a staff member who stays near the house. Terri Anne’s left foot bears a permanent scar and a limp. The family does not speak of the kidnapping every day, but it is present in the way doors are locked and in the way Mike still checks his phone when Terri Anne is late.
- The family is based in **Carlisle/Burlington, Ontario**, in the sprawling estate that allows for homeschooling, horseback riding, and the pool that is already central to the girls’ lives.
- Terri Anne is approximately thirty weeks pregnant. The baby is due in early **December 2032**.

## 2. The Pregnancy So Far
- This pregnancy is medically uncomplicated. After the trauma and infection of the Sabrina Bryna pregnancy, Terri Anne’s body has recovered steadily.
- The family has already lived through conjoined twin pregnancy, Turner Syndrome, a kidnapping, and a deaf child with CP. A sixth child is not a crisis; it is the next expected beat in the household’s rhythm.
- Terri Anne is tired but not fragile. She manages the homeschool routine, the medical appointments for Kiley Rose and Sabrina Bryna, and the physical demands of the estate. Mike continues to take over more cooking and evening duties to ensure she rests.
- Prenatal testing confirms a single fetus, no chromosomal abnormalities, and no complications. The obstetrician is almost apologetic in how routine the appointments are. Terri Anne and Mike find this a relief they do not speak aloud.
- The baby is named **Fallon Glenda** before birth. **Fallon** chosen by Mike for its grounded, unyielding sound. **Glenda** chosen by Terri Anne for her own grandmother, a woman who raised seven children through the Great Depression and never once complained.
- Patti Anne writes the name in her notebook. Jessica and Rachel practice saying it in their twin cadence. Kiley Rose signs *baby* and *Fallon* to Sabrina Bryna, who watches with her usual intense visual focus.

## 3. Telling the Children
- The older girls notice the pregnancy before it is announced. Patti Anne sees the prenatal vitamins on the counter. Jessica and Rachel feel the difference in Terri Anne’s body when they hug her. Kiley Rose, ever observant, asks why Mommy is bigger.
- Mike and Terri Anne gather the five daughters in the living room and tell them another baby is coming.
- Patti Anne’s response is managerial: she asks about due dates, sleeping arrangements, and how the homeschool schedule will adjust. She also asks, quietly, whether Mommy will be safe this time. Mike answers directly: *"Yes. We have made sure of it."*
- Jessica and Rachel are delighted. They touch Terri Anne’s belly and sign *hello* to the baby. They settle on the name immediately: they call her *Fallon* before Terri Anne has finished explaining.
- Kiley Rose, practical and four years old, asks if the baby will need doctor visits like she does. Terri Anne tells her probably not as many, but that Kiley Rose will be an excellent helper because she already knows how to be brave at the doctor.
- Sabrina Bryna, too young to understand abstractly, crawls onto Terri Anne’s lap and pats her belly. She does not startle to the spoken words around her, but she watches the signing hands with her characteristic sharp focus. When Terri Anne signs *baby sister, Fallon*, Sabrina Bryna’s hands move in an approximation of the sign. The older girls interpret this as Sabrina’s approval.
- The family promise is repeated: *"In our family, we take care of each other."*

## 4. The Household Rhythm in Late Pregnancy
- The fall of 2032 is ordinary in the way the family most values: homeschool lessons, horseback riding in the paddock, swimming in the estate pool, and long dinners at the kitchen table.
- Terri Anne slows as the third trimester advances. Mike takes over more of the active lessons, particularly math and science. Patti Anne steps into her role as assistant teacher, reading aloud to Kiley Rose and Sabrina Bryna while Jessica and Rachel work on their handwriting.
- The household is bilingual in spoken English and ASL. Even the hearing children sign regularly, not just for Sabrina Bryna but because the family has found that signing cuts through the noise of a busy house. Kiley Rose signs as naturally as she speaks.
- Mike installs a new crib and changing station in the nursery wing. The room is painted soft green — a departure from the pink and yellow of the previous nurseries. Patti Anne helps arrange the bookshelves. Jessica and Rachel place a handmade mobile above the crib, two small paper horses they folded together.
- Security remains visible but not intrusive. The staff member near the gate is now a familiar presence. Terri Anne no longer drives alone. The children have absorbed these rules without question; they are simply part of how the house works now.

## 5. The Birth (December 2032)
- Terri Anne’s water breaks in the early morning, before dawn. Mike is awake instantly. Patti Anne hears the movement and appears in the hallway, already dressed, asking what she can do.
- The hospital plan is rehearsed. The security detail follows at a distance. The drive to the hospital is quiet, Terri Anne breathing through contractions, Mike holding her hand.
- Because of the family’s history, the hospital team is briefed: a pediatric team is present, but the birth is expected to be routine. Terri Anne is alert and calm.
- **Fallon Glenda Key** is born at **6:14 a.m. on December 2, 2032**.
- She is robust: **7 pounds, 3 ounces**. Her cry is loud and immediate. Apgar scores are excellent.
- The first cry is a relief that fills the room. Terri Anne weeps. Mike holds her hand and does not let go. The pediatrician smiles — this is a baby who needs no intervention.
- Patti Anne, Jessica, and Rachel wait in the family room with the grandparents who have arrived. Kiley Rose plays quietly on the floor, drawing in a notebook. Sabrina Bryna sits on Patti Anne’s lap, her small body tense with the vibration of the room’s anxiety.
- When the girls are brought in to meet her, Patti Anne is the first to ask, *"Is Mommy okay? Is the baby okay?"* She needs confirmation before she can celebrate.
- Terri Anne, holding Fallon Glenda, looks down at her daughter. She looks at her own scarred left foot, then at the baby’s strong, kicking legs. The contrast is immediate and unspoken: this body was born without trauma, without infection, without rescue. She is whole.

## 6. First Days and First Impressions
- The newborn hearing screening is performed before discharge. Fallon Glenda startles consistently to sound. The results are normal.
- The pediatric exam finds no signs of CP, no chromosomal concerns, no cardiac irregularities. She is a medically uncomplicated baby in a family that has learned to expect complexity.
- Mike and Terri Anne note, privately, how different it is to hold a baby who responds to voices, who turns her head toward sound. They do not say this aloud, but the ease is palpable.
- Fallon Glenda is easily soothed by being held, by the vibration of Terri Anne’s chest, and by the low hum of the household around her. She is a baby who wants to be in the middle of things.
- The family does not yet know that she will become the swimmer, the rider, the grounded and direct girl who will one day push her own daughter on a swing in Carlisle Park. They only know that she is strong, that she cries with her whole body, and that she watches the room with dark, alert eyes.
- There is no immediate diagnosis. There is no crisis. The medical team treats her as ordinary, and the family finds this both strange and welcome.

## 7. The Sisters Meet Her
- Patti Anne, Jessica, and Rachel come into the hospital room carefully, washed and dressed in matching clothes, their faces serious.
- Patti Anne is quiet at first, then practical: *"Does she need a diaper? Can I help?"*
- Jessica and Rachel lean in together, their faces close to the baby. They touch her fingers, her toes, her dark hair. They whisper that they will protect her. They sign *safe* to each other, then to the baby.
- Kiley Rose is brought in, walking with her small, determined stride. She looks at the baby with serious curiosity. She reaches out and touches Fallon Glenda’s hand. Fallon Glenda’s fingers close around Kiley Rose’s thumb. Kiley Rose smiles and says, *"She’s strong."*
- Sabrina Bryna is brought in last. She is carried by Mike, her body supported by his arm because her tone makes walking unsteady. She looks at the baby with the same visual intensity she brings to everything. She reaches out and touches Fallon Glenda’s cheek. Fallon Glenda turns her head toward the touch. Sabrina Bryna signs *sister* with her free hand. The moment is quiet and profound: the family’s first deaf child, signing to its newest hearing member, establishing a bridge that will hold for the rest of their lives.
- The photograph is taken: the five older sisters gathered around the sixth, Kiley Rose’s small hand linked with Fallon Glenda’s, Sabrina Bryna’s hand resting on the blanket. The image carries the weight of the household’s history — the scarred foot, the conjoined twins, the small girl with Turner Syndrome, the deaf baby with CP — and the presence of a new, uncomplicated body who will grow up inside all of it.

## 8. Bringing Her Home / The First Month
- The house adjusts to a sixth child under the rhythm of its existing routines. Patti Anne takes charge of fetching diapers and organizing the nursery. The twins take turns sitting beside the bassinet. Kiley Rose learns to be gentle, guided by Patti Anne’s careful instruction. Sabrina Bryna watches the baby from her ergonomic chair, signing *baby* whenever Fallon Glenda cries.
- The security measures remain. Cameras, the gated drive, the staff member. Mike is still reluctant but unyielding. Terri Anne’s left foot is still healing, still limping, still a daily reminder of what the family survived.
- Terri Anne nurses Fallon Glenda while reading aloud to the older girls, keeping the homeschool routine from collapsing. Her foot is elevated, still a permanent part of her body’s architecture.
- Mike wakes at night to change diapers and bring Fallon Glenda to Terri Anne, functioning on minimal sleep while still managing Key Corporation meetings and the ongoing emotional weight of protecting his family.
- ASL continues to be a household language. Even with a hearing baby, the family signs to her: *milk, more, love, baby, mommy, daddy, safe, home, sister*. Fallon Glenda watches the hands with unusual focus, her eyes tracking movement before she can track sound. She will grow up bilingual not by necessity but by culture.
- The household is loud, busy, and physically complex. The twins move through rooms in synchronized choreography. Kiley Rose talks and signs at once, her small body vibrating with energy. Sabrina Bryna communicates in a mix of ASL, vocalization, and physical gesture. Into this, Fallon Glenda arrives as a hearing child who will learn to navigate a deaf sister, a twin pair, and a household where physical difference is the norm.

## 9. Thematic Resonance
- **The ordinary baby:** Fallon Glenda is born without diagnosis, without trauma, without chromosomal abnormality. In a family defined by medical complexity, her ordinariness is not a relief from burden but a different kind of gift: she is the child who arrives whole, and the family’s love for her is no less fierce for its lack of crisis.
- **The sixth beat:** Fallon Glenda is the sixth of eight. She is not the eldest, not the twins, not the medically fragile baby, not the deaf child. She is the middle child in a house that will eventually hold eight daughters. Her identity will form in the space between extremes: grounded, direct, athletic, resilient.
- **The mother’s foot and the daughter’s body:** Terri Anne’s scarred left foot is visible as she nurses Fallon Glenda. The baby’s strong legs kick against the blanket. The parallel is unspoken: this body did not survive violence. It is simply alive. The mother’s scar makes the daughter’s wholeness more precious, not more superior.
- **ASL as family culture:** The family signs to Fallon Glenda from birth not because she needs it but because it is how the household speaks. She will grow up with ASL as a second language, able to communicate with Sabrina Bryna in her native tongue. This early exposure lays the groundwork for her later fluency and for the deep bond she will share with her deaf sister.
- **The swimmer and the rider:** Even as an infant, Fallon Glenda is alert to movement, to the vibration of footsteps, to the physical energy of the house. These early traits are seeds of the girl who will spend her childhood in the estate pool and the Carlisle stables, developing the athletic independence that defines her.
- **Security and sanctuary:** The kidnapping is three years past, but its presence is in the walls. Fallon Glenda is born into a house that has already learned to protect itself. She will never know a world without cameras, without gates, without her father’s vigilance. This is her normal.
- **The sister bond:** The photograph of Sabrina Bryna signing *sister* to newborn Fallon Glenda foreshadows their relationship: one deaf, one hearing; one with CP, one athletic; both grounded in the same household promise. They will be different in every way the world measures, and they will be unbreakable.
- **Patti Anne’s vigilance:** The kidnapping has hardened Patti Anne’s protective instincts. At ten, she is already the household’s second adult. She will spend the next years trying to keep Fallon Glenda safe, teaching her the rules of the estate, showing her how to be a Key sister.
- **The next two:** Fallon Glenda is born thirty months after Sabrina Bryna. Andrea Jennifer will follow eighteen months later. Ashley Erin will follow two years after that. The family is not yet complete. Fallon Glenda is the calm before the final expansion, the sixth child in a house that will eventually hold eight.
