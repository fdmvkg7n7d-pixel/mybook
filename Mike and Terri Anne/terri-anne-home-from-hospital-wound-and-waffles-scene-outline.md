# Terri Anne Home From the Hospital — The Wound and the Waffles — Scene Outline

## Scene 1: Welcome Home, Kisses on the Wound

### Setting
- **Time:** Evening, the day Terri Anne is released from the hospital after the kidnapping. Her wound-check appointment is scheduled for the following Monday.
- **Place:** Mike and Terri Anne's bedroom at the Key house. The room has been rearranged to accommodate her recovery: pillows for elevation, a bedside tray for water and medication, soft lighting, a wheelchair parked near the bed, and a small stack of pregnancy books Terri Anne had been reading before the kidnapping.
- **Characters:** Terri Anne, Mike, Patti Anne (~8), Jessica Isabella and Rachel Darcy (~6), Kiley Rose (~13 months).
- **Mood:** Tender, cautious, exhausted. The family is learning how to be together again after violence.

### Opening
- Mike helps Terri Anne into bed. Her left foot is wrapped in fresh bandages, elevated on a pillow. She moves slowly, wincing only when she thinks no one is looking.
- Sabrina Bryna is still inside Terri Anne, growing at twenty weeks, moving occasionally as if checking in.
- The house is quiet but not empty. **Aldoma** and trusted house staff have helped during the day, but Mike has sent them home so the family can have this first night alone.

### The Girls Enter
- Patti Anne leads the procession. She has washed her hands without being told, wearing her most serious face. She carries a small pile of drawings she made at the hospital waiting over the past days.
- Jessica and Rachel follow together, moving carefully. They have discussed this in their twin language and decided they will kiss Mommy's foot to make it better.
- Kiley Rose toddles in behind them, walking with the uneven gait of a small child with Turner Syndrome. She is clutching her favorite blanket.
- The girls stop at the bedside. Terri Anne smiles at them, though she is pale and tired. She opens her arms.

### Patti Anne's Approach
- Patti Anne does not rush to the bed. She first looks at the bandaged foot, then at her mother's face, then at Terri Anne's belly where the baby is growing.
- She asks, *"Does it hurt a lot? And does the baby hurt too?"* She is not asking for reassurance; she is gathering information.
- Terri Anne tells her the truth: *"My foot hurts, but the baby is safe inside me. She doesn't feel the hurt the way I do."*
- Patti Anne nods and places her drawings on the bedside table. The drawings are of the family: Mommy, Daddy, the baby inside Mommy's belly, the foot with a big red X over it and a heart beside it.
- She kisses Terri Anne's cheek, then her forehead, then her hand. She avoids the foot because she is afraid to hurt it. Terri Anne pulls her close and holds her.

### Jessica and Rachel's Approach
- Jessica and Rachel step forward together. They have decided the foot needs love, so they will give it love.
- They kneel carefully on the floor beside the bed, their shared body folding in the practiced way they have learned.
- They look at the bandage. Rachel touches it gently with one finger. Jessica whispers, *"We kiss it."*
- Together, they lean in and press soft, careful kisses on the bandage — not on the wound itself, but on the white cloth that protects it.
- They say, in their twin rhythm, *"Better, Mommy. Better."*
- Then they turn to Terri Anne's belly and each kiss the place where Sabrina Bryna is growing. *"We love you too, baby."*
- Terri Anne's eyes fill with tears. She did not cry when she was kidnapped. She cries now, because her daughters are kissing the place where she was hurt and the place where the baby lives.

### Kiley Rose's Approach
- Kiley Rose, slower to reach the bed, stands at the edge and looks up. She is too small to understand what has happened, but she understands that Mommy is in bed and that everyone is being gentle.
- She holds out her favorite blanket to Terri Anne. It is her greatest treasure, the thing she sleeps with every night. She wants to give it to her mother and to the baby inside her.
- Mike lifts her onto the bed. She crawls carefully to Terri Anne and places the blanket on her mother's chest, then gently pulls it down to cover Terri Anne's stomach — the place where Sabrina Bryna is growing.
- She does not say anything. She simply arranges the blanket with the solemn care of a child who knows she is doing something important, then pats the blanket over Terri Anne's belly.
- Then she touches Terri Anne's face with both hands, pats her cheek twice, and lays her head on her mother's shoulder.
- Terri Anne wraps her arms around Kiley Rose and holds her there, breathing in the smell of her child's hair, feeling Sabrina Bryna shift beneath the blanket.

### Terri Anne's Tears
- The feel of Kiley Rose's blanket on her belly — the blanket that has comforted her daughter through fevers and fears and every night of her small life — undoes something in Terri Anne.
- She starts to cry. Not loudly. Not dramatically. Soft, silent tears that slide down her face and into Kiley Rose's hair.
- The girls look up, alarmed. Patti Anne asks, *"Mommy, are you hurting? Did we hurt you?"*
- Terri Anne shakes her head and pulls all of them closer. She manages to say, *"No, my loves. You didn't hurt me. I'm just so lucky."*
- She thinks, but does not say aloud: she almost lost this. She almost lost the big bed, the girls' kisses, the baby inside her, the husband in the doorway, the noisy, messy, beautiful life they have built.
- She thinks of the kidnappers, and of the stupid business deal Mike made that brought them to her door. She does not blame him — she knows Mike carries enough blame for both of them — but she understands, in a new way, that their wealth and power have a cost, and that cost was almost her family.
- She kisses Kiley Rose's head, then Jessica's, then Rachel's, then Patti Anne's. She lets them wipe her tears with their small hands.
- Mike comes to the bedside and sits beside her, putting his hand on the blanket over her belly. The family does not speak. They simply breathe together.

### Mike Watches
- Mike stands in the doorway, watching his daughters love his wife and his unborn child back to life. He does not interrupt.
- For a moment, the kidnapping is not in the room. The foot is still bandaged. The fear is still recent. But the girls have made the room safe again, simply by being in it.
- When the girls are done, Mike helps them climb onto the bed one by one, and they all curl around Terri Anne like a small, breathing fortress.

### Closing Image of Scene 1
- The family falls asleep in the master bed: Terri Anne in the center, Mike on one side, the four girls scattered around them. Sabrina Bryna is still in utero, but the whole family is wrapped around her.
- The wound is kissed. The house is full. The night is quiet.

## Scene 2: Blueberry Waffles in the Morning

### Setting
- **Time:** The next morning, early. The sun is just beginning to come through the curtains. Terri Anne's wound-check appointment is on Monday — four or five days away.
- **Place:** Mike and Terri Anne's bedroom. The girls have not left; they slept in the big bed or on pallets on the floor. **Aldoma** and the house staff are present in the background: a nurse checks supplies, a housekeeper moves quietly in the hallway, a chef prepares ingredients in the kitchen. The bedroom itself remains family space, but the house is clearly operating as a managed care environment.
- **Characters:** Mike, Terri Anne, Patti Anne, Jessica and Rachel, Kiley Rose, **Aldoma** (nanny/household manager), and house staff (nurse, chef, housekeeper) moving in the background.
- **Mood:** Domestic, hopeful, fragile. The family is learning that ordinary things can still happen, but the household now revolves around Terri Anne's bedrest and wheelchair-bound mobility.

### Opening
- Mike wakes first. He has not slept deeply since the kidnapping, but he woke with a specific need: to do something normal and excellent for his family.
- He quietly slips out of bed, careful not to disturb Terri Anne or the girls. He goes downstairs in pajamas and a robe.
- In the kitchen, **Aldoma is already present**, directing the staff and preparing the kitchen. She nods to Mike and steps aside so he can make the waffles himself — she knows he needs to do this with his own hands.
- Mike makes blueberry waffles from scratch. He uses the batter recipe his mother taught him. He heats the iron, mashes fresh blueberries, melts butter, warms syrup. Aldoma quietly ensures the coffee and tea are ready and that the tray is clean.
- The act of cooking is a form of control. He cannot undo the kidnapping. He cannot heal the foot faster. But he can make waffles that taste like home.
- In the background, the nurse sets out Terri Anne's morning medications and checks that the wheelchair is accessible. The house staff move with the unobtrusive efficiency of people who have been told, without words, that the family needs calm.

### Preparing the Tray
- Mike sets a large breakfast tray with:
  - A stack of warm, golden-brown waffles studded with blueberries
  - A small pitcher of warmed maple syrup
  - A bowl of extra blueberries for the girls
  - A small dish of butter
  - Glasses of orange juice and milk
  - A mug of tea for Terri Anne (decaffeinated, because she is pregnant and recovering)
  - Paper napkins because he knows the girls will be messy
- He also brings a small plate of cut-up waffle pieces for Kiley Rose, whose small hands cannot manage a full waffle yet.
- Aldoma holds the door open as he carries the tray upstairs one-handed. She does not follow him into the bedroom. She understands the boundary.

### The Reveal
- The girls wake as Mike enters the room. The smell of waffles fills the bedroom.
- Patti Anne sits up, confused at first, then delighted. *"Waffles in bed?"*
- Jessica and Rachel wake together, as always. They sniff the air and grin at each other. Rachel says, *"Daddy made breakfast."* Jessica echoes, *"Breakfast in bed."*
- Kiley Rose is still half-asleep, but she reaches for Mike when she sees him. He sets the tray down and lifts her into his lap.
- Terri Anne wakes slowly. She sees the tray, the girls, the sunlight, and her husband's face. She smiles despite the pain in her foot. *"You made waffles."*
- She does not sit up fully. She is on bedrest and cannot move without help. Mike adjusts the pillows behind her, and the girls arrange themselves carefully around her legs and belly.

### Breakfast in Bed
- Mike arranges the tray across the bed, making sure Terri Anne's foot is still elevated and not disturbed.
- He hands out plates. Patti Anne helps pour syrup for the twins. The twins argue — gently, in their twin way — over who gets more blueberries.
- Mike cuts a small piece of waffle for Kiley Rose. She holds it in both hands and takes a careful bite. Blueberry juice stains her chin. Mike wipes it with a napkin.
- Terri Anne eats slowly. She is not hungry, but she eats because Mike made this for her and because the girls are watching. She wants them to see that Mommy is okay.
- Sabrina Bryna kicks gently inside Terri Anne. The movement is strong enough for Terri Anne to feel, but too subtle for the girls to notice through the blanket and her belly.
- Terri Anne places one hand on her stomach and smiles. She does not call attention to it. She simply holds the secret knowledge that the baby is alive and moving, and that is enough for now.
- Patti Anne notices her mother's hand on her belly and asks, *"Is the baby kicking?"*
- Terri Anne answers honestly: *"She is. But she's still too small for you to feel. Soon, though. In a few more weeks, you might be able to feel her."*
- The girls lean in, hoping. They press their hands against Terri Anne's belly, but feel nothing. They are not disappointed. They are patient. They trust their mother's word.
- The scene is messy, sweet, ordinary, and extraordinary. It is the first morning after the worst thing, and it is not perfect, but it is theirs.

### Conversation and Comfort
- Patti Anne asks practical questions: *"Will Mommy's foot always hurt?"* *"Will the baby be okay?"* *"Can we have waffles every morning after something bad happens?"*
- Mike answers honestly: *"Mommy's foot will get better but it might always be a little different. The doctor will check it on Monday. The baby is being watched by doctors. And yes, we can have waffles whenever we need them."*
- Jessica and Rachel decide to save one blueberry each for Sabrina Bryna. They place the blueberries on Terri Anne's belly, over the place where the baby is growing. Terri Anne thanks them with a kiss.
- Kiley Rose, full of waffle, climbs off Mike's lap and toddles over to Terri Anne. She pats the belly gently, then pats Terri Anne's hand. She has learned the new routine: be gentle with Mommy and the baby inside her.
- Aldoma appears briefly in the doorway to check if anything is needed. Terri Anne waves her in. Aldoma brings a fresh pitcher of water and takes the empty juice glasses, then leaves again without intruding on the moment.

### The Wheelchair Reminder
- At some point during the breakfast, Terri Anne needs to use the bathroom or simply shift position. The wheelchair is brought beside the bed by the nurse or Aldoma.
- The girls watch as Mike and Aldoma help Terri Anne into the wheelchair. The process is slow and careful: the bandaged foot must not touch the ground, her pregnant belly must be supported, and she must not strain.
- Patti Anne's face tightens with worry. Jessica and Rachel hold hands. Kiley Rose stands very still, watching.
- Terri Anne notices their fear and forces a smile. *"It's just a wheelchair, loves. It helps me get around without hurting my foot. See?"* She rolls herself a few inches to demonstrate, then stops, breathless, because even that small effort is tiring.
- Mike takes over, pushing her gently to the bathroom and then back. The girls see that their mother can move, but that moving is now a serious event. They do not complain. They adjust.
- The wheelchair is parked back beside the bed. Terri Anne returns to the pillows, exhausted. The waffle tray is waiting for her. She picks up a small piece and eats it, as if to say: *I am still here. I am still eating. This is not the end.*

### Closing Image of Scene 2
- The tray is half-empty. The bed is covered in crumbs and syrup. The girls are sticky and happy. Terri Anne is holding her pregnant belly with one hand and a half-eaten waffle with the other, her foot still elevated, her face softer than it has been in days.
- Mike sits on the edge of the bed, watching them. Aldoma and the staff are visible in the hallway, moving quietly. The wheelchair stands ready by the bed.
- He has not fixed everything. He has not healed the foot or erased the kidnapping. But he has given his family one good morning, and that is enough for today.
- The camera, if this were film, would pull back slowly: Terri Anne's pregnant belly, the bandaged foot, the wheelchair, the waffle crumbs, the girls' laughter, Aldoma's steady presence in the background, the father's exhausted joy. A family still alive, still loving, still feeding itself.

## Scene 3: Swimming While Terri Anne Watches on Her iPhone

### Setting
- **Time:** Later that same day, or possibly the following afternoon. The sun is high and warm, a rare winter bright spell.
- **Place:** The Key family's indoor pool or an enclosed, heated outdoor pool at the house. Terri Anne remains in the master bedroom, propped against pillows with her wheelchair beside the bed.
- **Characters:** Mike, Patti Anne, Jessica and Rachel, Kiley Rose, and Terri Anne (watching remotely). Aldoma may be present at the pool to help with the younger girls.
- **Mood:** Bittersweet, adaptive, loving. Terri Anne is present but physically absent. The family finds a way to include her.

### Opening
- After breakfast, the girls are restless. The house is too quiet, too careful. Patti Anne asks if they can go swimming — it is something they do as a family, something normal.
- Mike looks at Terri Anne. She cannot go. The doctor has been clear: bedrest, no strain, foot elevated, no water near the wound. But the girls need to move. They need to laugh. They need to remember that their life is still theirs.
- Terri Anne tells them to go. *"I'll watch,"* she says. *"Mike, take your phone. Call me. I want to see everything."*

### Mike Sets Up the iPhone
- Mike props his iPhone on a poolside chair or hands it to Aldoma, angled so Terri Anne can see the pool, the girls, and the water.
- He makes sure the call is stable, the lighting is good, and the phone is waterproofed or placed safely away from splashes.
- He shows Terri Anne the view: the pool, the tiles, the girls in their swimsuits. He waves at the camera. *"Can you see us?"*
- Terri Anne, in bed, nods at her own phone. She is wearing headphones or has the volume up. She can hear the echo of the pool, the girls' voices, the splash of water. *"I see you. I see all of you."*

### The Girls Swim
- Patti Anne jumps in first, showing off her strokes, glancing at the phone to make sure her mother is watching.
- Jessica and Rachel enter together, holding hands, their conjoined bodies making their usual synchronized movements in the water. Swimming is one of the places they feel most free.
- Kiley Rose is held by Mike in the shallow end. She kicks her legs and splashes, laughing when water gets in her face. She is small, but she loves the water.
- Mike swims with each of them in turn, holding Kiley Rose, racing Patti Anne, helping the twins practice floating. He keeps one eye on the phone, making sure Terri Anne is still there.
- Aldoma stands by the edge with a towel, ready to help, but she lets Mike do the parenting. She is the safety net, not the lead.

### Terri Anne Watches from Bed
- Terri Anne holds the phone with both hands, her foot still elevated, her belly round under the blanket.
- She laughs when the girls splash. She calls out encouragement — *"Good kick, Patti Anne!"* *"Jessica, you're floating so well!"* *"Kiley Rose, kick, baby, kick!"* — even though they can barely hear her through the phone.
- She feels Sabrina Bryna move inside her, as if the baby is also watching, or responding to the sound of her sisters' voices.
- She is sad. She wants to be in the water with them. She wants to hold Kiley Rose, to feel the weightlessness of the pool, to be the mother who swims with her children. But she is also grateful. She is watching them live. She is still part of it.
- She thinks about the kidnapping, the foot, the wheelchair, the bedrest, and the baby inside her. She thinks about how much she has lost in mobility and how much she has not lost in love.

### Mike's Attention Divided
- Mike is both in the pool and aware of the phone. He swims, plays, coaches, and periodically holds the phone up to his face to talk to Terri Anne.
- He asks her: *"Are you okay? Do you need anything?"* She tells him no, just keep the camera on the girls.
- He turns the phone so she can see the underwater view — the girls' legs kicking, the bubbles, the light through the water.
- He tells the girls, *"Mommy is watching. Wave to Mommy!"* They all turn to the phone and wave, wet and grinning, shouting, *"Hi, Mommy!"*
- Terri Anne waves back at the screen, alone in the bedroom, crying a little and smiling a lot.

### Closing Image of Scene 3
- The pool is bright. The girls are laughing. Mike is in the water, strong and present, turning the phone so Terri Anne can see every face.
- In the bedroom, Terri Anne is small in the big bed, the phone propped against a pillow, her bandaged foot elevated, her hand on her belly.
- The two spaces — the bright pool and the quiet bedroom — are connected by the screen, by love, by the stubborn determination of a family to stay together even when they cannot be in the same room.
- The last image: Mike holds Kiley Rose up to the camera. Kiley Rose blows a kiss — or tries to, her small hand pressing wet fingers against the phone lens. Terri Anne kisses her own phone screen in return.

## Thematic Resonance
- **Physical love as healing:** The girls' kisses on the bandage and on Terri Anne's belly are not medical care, but they are emotional repair. The family heals through touch.
- **Domestic ritual as defiance:** Making and eating waffles is an act of ordinary life after extraordinary violence. It says: we are still here, we still eat, we still love.
- **Mike's fatherhood:** He cannot undo the crime, so he does what he can do: cook, protect, provide comfort. This is the origin of his later engineering of safety and accommodations.
- **Terri Anne's vulnerability:** She allows herself to be cared for in front of her children and staff. This is not weakness; it is honesty.
- **Aldoma's role:** She is present but not intrusive. She supports the parents without replacing them. She is the steady adult in the background who makes the morning possible.
- **The girls as caregivers:** Patti Anne watches, Jessica and Rachel kiss and bless the baby, Kiley Rose gives her blanket. Each child responds to trauma in her own language.
- **Disability and love:** Sabrina Bryna is still in utero, but the family is already practicing the kind of love that will surround her disabilities later: attentive, physical, patient, and unembarrassed.
- **The foot and the future:** The bandaged foot is a visible wound in the room. The wheelchair is a visible adaptation. The waffles are a visible joy. All three exist together, refusing to let one cancel the others.
- **The unborn child as witness:** Sabrina Bryna feels her mother's stress, her mother's recovery, and her family's love before she is ever born. The womb is not separate from the household.
- **Bedrest as liminal space:** Terri Anne cannot move freely, so the world comes to her. The bedroom becomes the whole house, and the whole family learns to orbit around it.
