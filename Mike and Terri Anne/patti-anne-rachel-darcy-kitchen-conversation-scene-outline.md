# SCENE OUTLINE: PATTI ANNE & RACHEL DARCY — THE FACETIME CONVERSATION

*Several weeks after Mike's funeral. **Before Patti Anne's cruise.** Rachel Darcy tells Patti Anne what she saw in Gamaliel and Klára's pas de deux. They have the conversation over **FaceTime** — Patti Anne in her own kitchen in Vaughan, Rachel in her studio at her house, both of them on their phones, propped up against things.*

---

## TIMING WITHIN THE SAGA

- **A few weeks after Mike's funeral.** The acute shock has worn off; the steady grief has not.
- **Before the cruise** — Patti Anne is taking her household away soon (Gamaliel, Klára, the twins, Aleksander, with Willow staying behind under Terri Anne's care now that Mike is gone). This conversation has to happen *before* the cruise, because Patti Anne needs to know what Rachel is going to tell her before she is in the middle of an ocean with her foster daughter, her son, and a cell signal that comes and goes.
- **Gamaliel and Klára are sixteen.** They are keeping their relationship quiet from the rest of the extended family (though Patti Anne and Aleksander already knew and blessed it a year prior, when they were fifteen, before their first family cruise). They are still keeping it quiet from Klára's social worker.
- Rachel Darcy saw it during the pas de deux at the celebration of life. She has been carrying it ever since. She has decided she will not carry it through the cruise.

---

## WHY FACETIME

Neither sister can drive to the other for this conversation. It is the wrong week for that — kids' schedules, Patti Anne's cruise prep, Rachel's youngest in a school production, Aleksander not yet home from work. Rachel could wait a week. She does not want to.

She also knows, importantly, that this is a conversation that **cannot happen in person at Patti Anne's house** — Gamaliel and Klára could walk into any room at any time — and **cannot happen at Mike and Terri Anne's mansion** either, because Terri Anne is grieving and does not need to overhear her dead husband's name in this particular conversation right now.

FaceTime is, paradoxically, the most private place left. Two kitchens, two phones, two sisters alone in their own houses with a door closed.

Rachel calls. Patti Anne answers on the second ring. The Key sisters do not let each other's calls go unanswered, especially right now.

---

## SETTING (SPLIT-SCREEN)

### Patti Anne's side

- **Her own kitchen in Vaughan.** Patti Anne and Aleksander's house, the kitchen she has remodeled twice. Mid-afternoon. The twins (Palmer Peter and Salena Sarai, ~3) are down for a nap in the next room. Gamaliel and Klára are at Victoria Ballet Academy. Aleksander is at work.
- Patti Anne has just finished making tea. She is at her kitchen island, phone propped against the sugar bowl. She is wearing the soft cardigan she has been wearing for most of the last month.

### Rachel Darcy's side

- **Her home studio** — the small dance room she built off the back of her house when the seven kids stopped needing her to drive them everywhere. Mirrors on one wall, barre, sprung floor. A folded studio chair in the corner.
- Rachel is sitting on the folded chair. Phone propped on a shelf at her eye level. Antoine is somewhere in the house — *not* in the studio. The seven children are scattered through various activities. She has closed the studio door.

### What they see of each other

The slight visual lag, the slightly compressed colour, the way faces look on a phone screen — Patti Anne in warm afternoon kitchen light, Rachel in the colder daylight of the studio. They both look tired. Neither comments on it.

---

## BEAT 1 — RACHEL OPENS

Pleasantries take about ten seconds. *"How's Terri Anne?"* *"How's the cruise prep?"* *"How are the twins?"* Mike-shaped silences in between every answer.

Then Rachel says:

> *"Patti. I have to tell you something before you get on that boat."*

Patti Anne does not flinch. She knew, the moment Rachel's call came through and the screen lit up with her sister's face, that this was going to be about her son. She has known it longer than that. Maybe since the dance. Maybe before.

She says, *"Tell me."*

Rachel takes a breath. She is going to do this carefully. Rachel is a dancer; she has spent her whole life paying attention to bodies. What she saw at the celebration of life cannot be undone by being polite about it.

> *"It's about Gamaliel and Klára. They are in love with each other. I watched them dance. They are in love."*

Patti Anne, on the other end of the phone, in her own kitchen, lifts her tea. Takes a long swallow. Sets the mug back down. The kitchen does the thing kitchens do when a sentence like that has just been said in them, even when the sentence has come through a phone speaker. The hum of the fridge. The faint sound of one of the twins shifting in their crib next door.

Patti Anne says, to her own tea:

> *"I know."*

---

## BEAT 2 — WHAT PATTI ANNE ALREADY KNEW

This is the part Rachel did not expect. She thought she was bringing news. She thought she was going to have to convince her sister — gently, slowly, like setting down a glass.

But Patti Anne already knows. Has known. Maybe longer than Rachel has.

She tells Rachel: she has seen the way they look for each other in a room before they look for anyone else. She has watched Gamaliel hold Klára's coat in the front hall the way you hold the coat of someone you love, not the coat of a sister. She has noticed that Klára's bedroom door is closed at exactly the times she would close it if she did not want her mother to hear something. She has counted the number of times Gamaliel uses the word *we* in a sentence about Klára and the number of times he uses the word *she*, and the ratio has been going the wrong way for a year.

She has not said anything to either of them. She has not said anything to Aleksander. She has not said anything to Klára's social worker.

She has not been pretending not to know. She has been waiting to figure out what to do with what she knows.

She tells Rachel: *"Mike saw it before I did. I think he saw it the day they were thirteen and went outside in their boots and came back with a name for the dog. And he didn't say anything either. So I figured if Mike wasn't worried, I could keep not being worried for a little longer."*

Rachel almost laughs. Of course Mike. Of course.

On Rachel's screen, Patti Anne's face goes briefly out of focus as Patti Anne sets the phone down to refill her tea. Rachel waits. The little visual stutter of a video call. Patti Anne comes back into frame.

---

## BEAT 3 — WHAT RACHEL SAW THAT PATTI ANNE COULDN'T

But Rachel saw something Patti Anne could not have seen from where she was sitting in the front room. Rachel was at an angle. Rachel was the dancer in the audience. Rachel watched the partnering, not the partners.

She tells Patti Anne:

> *"You can fake a lot of things in a pas de deux. You can fake romance. You can fake longing. You can fake whatever the choreographer asks you to fake. You cannot fake the way Gamaliel caught her. There's a thing partners do when they trust each other completely — a thing in the wrist, a thing in the timing, a thing where his weight knows where her weight is going before she sends it. You learn it in years, not months. They have it. They have it the way Antoine and I have it, and we've been dancing together since I was twelve."*

She demonstrates — almost without realizing — by turning slightly in her chair, lifting her arm into a port de bras, holding the shape for a count of three. On Patti Anne's screen, in a Vaughan kitchen, Rachel becomes briefly a dancer again, in a studio across town, showing her sister a sentence with her body.

She tells Patti Anne about the lift toward the empty chair — the moment Klára extended her arm toward where Mike would have sat. *"She wasn't reaching for Mike, Patti. I mean, she was. But she was also showing Gamaliel where to stop. They were communicating. Out loud. Through their bodies. To Dad."*

She tells Patti Anne the thing that has been keeping her up at night: *"They danced for our father like a couple. That's what I saw. They danced for him like they were asking for his blessing."*

Patti Anne, on the other end, does not say anything for a moment. The phone screen catches her looking down. Rachel waits.

---

## BEAT 4 — A LONG PAUSE THROUGH A SCREEN

Patti Anne speaks. Slowly. She is figuring it out in real time.

> *"He gave it to them. Before he died. I don't know when. But he gave it to them. That's what they were doing at the funeral — they were thanking him. For the only blessing they have."*

Rachel nods. *"Yeah. That's what it looked like."*

A long video-call quiet. Neither sister fills it. Both of them are looking at each other through a small bright rectangle, and what is between them is not something a screen can hold but is being held anyway.

Patti Anne picks her tea back up. *"You called me on FaceTime because you wanted me to see your face when you told me."*

Rachel says, *"Yes."*

*"And you didn't want me alone in the room with Aleksander or the kids when I heard it."*

*"Yes."*

*"Thank you."*

*"You don't have to thank me, Patti."*

*"I do."*

---

## BEAT 5 — THE CRUISE

Patti Anne sets her mug down for good.

> *"I'm taking them on the cruise next week. Gamaliel. Klára. The twins. Aleksander. Tell me what to do."*

Rachel — who is the only one of the eight sisters who has watched two bodies become one body and then be separated back into two, who knows in her bones what it costs to be physically severed from someone you love — thinks for a long time before she answers. The video lag holds the pause for both of them, gently.

She says:

> *"Don't say anything to them on the boat. Not on the boat. There's nowhere to go on a boat. If you tell them you know, and they panic — and they will, Patti, they will panic — they have nowhere to walk it off. They have nowhere to be alone. Klára especially. She'll spiral and you'll lose her for the week. Wait until you're home."*

Patti Anne nods.

Rachel keeps going.

> *"And when you do say something — say it the way Mike would have said it. Not 'I disapprove.' Not 'this has to stop.' Say 'I see you. I have been seeing you. I am not going to pretend I haven't.' That's the version they can survive."*

Patti Anne says — and this is the second moment of the conversation Rachel will remember — *"Mike already said it for me. He said it to Klára in her bedroom when he caught them kissing. He told her she was not losing this family. I just have to be the second person who says it. I have to be the person who confirms it after he's gone."*

Rachel, on her end, reaches up and presses two fingers against the screen — against where her sister's face is, in the impossible video-call gesture that means *I cannot touch your hand, so this is what I have*. Patti Anne, in her kitchen, mirrors it. Two fingertips against two screens. The sisters' equivalent of holding hands.

---

## BEAT 6 — THE SOCIAL WORKER

There is one more piece. Patti Anne brings it up before Rachel has to.

> *"What about Klára's social worker?"*

This is the harder question. Klára is sixteen and still officially a foster child in Patti Anne's care. The relationship with Gamaliel — even if it is real, even if it is love, even if Mike blessed it before he died — is the kind of thing that, in the wrong hands, becomes a file. A meeting. A re-placement. A child sent somewhere else.

Rachel does not have a clean answer for this. She says: *"I don't know. But I know this: you don't go to the social worker first. You go to Klára first. You ask her what she wants. She has spent her whole life having other people decide what is best for her. You don't get to be one more."*

Patti Anne says, *"Okay."*

Rachel says, *"And whatever Klára says — Aleksander is on your side. Always has been. You're not going to be alone in this."*

Patti Anne says, *"I know."*

They are both crying a little, in the small way sisters cry when they are not trying to be dramatic about it. Both of them wipe their faces at roughly the same time, on opposite ends of a video call, and the screen lag makes it look briefly like one mirrored gesture.

---

## BEAT 7 — TWO CHILDREN, TWO HOUSES

A small interruption: one of Patti Anne's twins (let's say Salena Sarai, because she is the one who never fully naps) starts to fuss in the next room. Patti Anne lifts the phone, says, *"Hold on,"* and walks out of frame. Rachel can hear her sister murmuring to her daughter through the speaker. Rachel watches her own ceiling on a tilted angle of phone for thirty seconds. Then Patti Anne comes back, Salena Sarai on her hip, and Salena Sarai sees Auntie Rachel on the screen and immediately grins and shouts *"Rachie!"*

Rachel laughs. *"Hi, baby."*

Salena Sarai blows her a kiss through the screen.

Patti Anne says, into her daughter's hair: *"Auntie Rachie was just telling Mommy something important about your big sister and your big brother."*

Salena Sarai, who is three and does not know what *important* means but knows it makes Mommy's voice quieter: *"Klára?"*

Patti Anne says, *"Yes. Klára."*

Salena Sarai considers this. Then she says, with the absolute conviction of a small child reporting a fact: *"Klára loves Gamy."*

Patti Anne and Rachel look at each other through the screen. Neither of them is even slightly surprised.

Rachel says, quietly, *"Out of the mouths of babes."*

Patti Anne says, into her daughter's curls, *"Yes, Auntie Rachie. Yes."*

---

## BEAT 8 — HANGING UP

They wrap up. There is nothing more to plan tonight than what they have already planned. Rachel says, *"Have a good cruise. Call me when you're home."*

Patti Anne says, *"I will."*

Rachel says, *"I love you."*

Patti Anne says, *"I love you, too."*

Salena Sarai, still on Patti Anne's hip, leans forward and presses her open palm against the phone screen, then her mouth against the phone screen, then her forehead against the phone screen — the full toddler goodbye. Rachel mirrors back, palm-mouth-forehead, on her end, laughing.

The screen goes dark. Patti Anne stands in her own kitchen with her daughter on her hip. Rachel sits in her own studio in front of her own mirror. The conversation is over. The plan is made. The cruise is in seven days.

---

## WHAT THIS SCENE ESTABLISHES

- **Rachel Darcy tells Patti Anne what she saw in the pas de deux.** It is now on the record between the two of them.
- **The conversation is on FaceTime** — two kitchens, two sisters, two screens. Each in her own private space.
- **Patti Anne already knew.** Patti Anne and Aleksander already knew and gave their full blessing when Klára and Gamaliel were fifteen, before their first family cruise (triggered by Klára's fear of the social worker and encouraged by her psychologist). Patti Anne is not surprised by Rachel's call; she simply confirms she saw the dance too.
- **Mike knew too** — they confirm it together. He saw it possibly as early as the New Year's Eve when Klára named Willow. He died with the knowledge protected.
- **The plan for the cruise:** Patti Anne will *not* say anything to Gamaliel and Klára on the boat. She will wait until they are home. When she does say something, it will be in the language Mike used: *I see you. I am not going to pretend I haven't.*
- **The social worker** is the open problem. Klára must be consulted first — before any institutional disclosure.
- **Aleksander is presumed an ally** in whatever comes next.
- **Salena Sarai, age three, casually confirms the entire thing** without knowing what she has confirmed. *Klára loves Gamy.* The youngest member of the household has been seeing it the whole time. Children do not need partnering vocabulary to see partnering trust.
- The cruise is **seven days away.**
