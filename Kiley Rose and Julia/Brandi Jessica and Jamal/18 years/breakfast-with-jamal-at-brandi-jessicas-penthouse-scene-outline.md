# Breakfast with Jamal at Brandi Jessica’s Penthouse — Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Brandi Jessica Key (21), Jamal Al-Otaibi (21), Pandora Paige Key (21), Kiley Rose Key, Julia
- **Offstage / referenced:** Mohammed, Imam Kareem, Bassem
- **Onstage animal:** Burden, Brandi Jessica’s Labernese guide dog
- **Location:** Brandi Jessica’s penthouse, Las Vegas, Nevada
- **When:** UNLV years, ~2065–2066 — nine o’clock the morning after Kiley Rose and Julia first meet Jamal; departure day for everyone
- **Status:** Scene outline
- **Tone:** Domestic, observant, lightly humorous, honest, cautiously welcoming

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

Jamal returns to Brandi Jessica’s penthouse for the breakfast Kiley Rose invited him to attend.

The previous evening established that Jamal can answer difficult questions. Breakfast tests something quieter and more important: whether Brandi and Jamal can live their new relationship honestly during an ordinary morning.

Kiley Rose and Julia observe how Jamal behaves when there is no formal introduction to perform. Pandora brings twin humor and helps the room relax. A small disagreement between Brandi and Jamal gives the mothers a chance to see whether the couple can correct one another without secrecy, control, or withdrawal.

The scene demonstrates that the relationship’s freshness will not come from declarations. It must be created repeatedly through ordinary choices.

Breakfast is also the family’s final shared meal before everyone leaves Las Vegas that day: Kiley Rose and Julia fly to **Panama City**, Pandora Paige flies to **Los Angeles**, and Jamal takes Brandi Jessica to **Reno** to see his parents.

---

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: Nine O’Clock Means Nine O’Clock

- The penthouse is already awake, with four sets of luggage arranged near the entryway.
- Kiley Rose and Julia are flying to Panama City later that day.
- Pandora Paige is flying to Los Angeles.
- Brandi’s overnight bag is packed for Reno, where Jamal is taking her to see his parents.
- Kiley Rose is making coffee while Julia works at the stove.
- Pandora cuts fruit at the kitchen island.
- Brandi moves confidently between the refrigerator, counter, and table, organizing plates in her own home.
- Burden rests off harness near the edge of the kitchen, out of the walking path.
- At exactly nine o’clock, the doorbell rings.
- Pandora checks the time and says:

> “Nine exactly. He’s frightened of Mom.”

- Kiley answers:

> “Good. Fear teaches punctuality.”

- Brandi laughs and goes to the door.
- Jamal has not used his access code. He remembered Brandi’s instruction from the previous night: *Ring the bell.*
- When Brandi opens the door, Jamal says:

> “Nine o’clock.”

- Brandi answers:

> “You can tell time. This is promising.”

### Beat 2: Jamal Enters an Ordinary Morning

- Jamal has brought fresh bread and a container of fruit preserves from a halal bakery.
- He does not bring an elaborate gift. The point is contribution, not performance.
- He greets Kiley Rose, Julia, and Pandora without repeating the nervous formality of the previous evening.
- Julia takes the bread and gives Jamal a practical task: set out five glasses and fill the water pitcher.
- Jamal asks Brandi where the glasses belong rather than opening every cabinet.
- Brandi gives him directions from across the kitchen.
- Kiley notices that Brandi remains the authority in her own home.
- Jamal places each item where Brandi expects it and tells her when he has moved the pitcher closer to the table.
- No one praises him for basic courtesy. The accommodation remains ordinary.

### Beat 3: Everyone Makes Breakfast

- Breakfast is shared work rather than something Jamal performs for the family.
- Julia makes eggs.
- Kiley handles coffee and tea.
- Pandora finishes the fruit.
- Jamal slices the bread.
- Brandi prepares pancake batter using measuring tools she has arranged for tactile identification.
- Jamal starts to reach for the bowl when some flour spills, then stops himself.

> “Do you want a hand?”

- Brandi feels along the counter, locates the spill, and answers:

> “A cloth. Put it by my right hand.”

- Jamal does exactly that.
- Kiley sees the rhythm Pandora described: Jamal offers information or assistance without assuming control.
- Brandi cleans the counter herself and continues mixing.

### Beat 4: Pandora Breaks the Formality

- Pandora announces that Jamal’s first official family test is pancake quality.
- Jamal points out that Brandi made the batter.
- Pandora says:

> “Then your test is whether you know better than to criticize it.”

- Brandi throws a folded napkin toward Pandora’s voice and misses deliberately.
- Julia laughs. Kiley tries not to, then gives up.
- Jamal relaxes enough to tease Pandora about the enormous amount of fruit she has cut for five people.
- Pandora tells him that Bassem understands the correct quantity of fruit and can train him.
- The exchange places Jamal in the family’s ordinary humor without pretending he already belongs completely.

### Beat 5: Questions About Daily Life

- Once everyone sits, the conversation moves away from Mohammed and the affair.
- Kiley Rose and Julia ask Jamal about ordinary matters:
  - His UNLV classes.
  - The work he hopes to do after graduation.
  - How often he returns to Reno.
  - Which languages he uses with his parents.
  - What he cooks when no one is watching.
- Jamal admits that he survives on the same three meals when he is busy.
- Brandi says she knows because she has eaten all three repeatedly.
- Kiley asks about the Reno visit. Jamal confirms that his parents are expecting both of them that evening. This will be Brandi’s first visit to their home as Jamal’s girlfriend.
- Brandi says she and Jamal will leave for Reno after Kiley Rose, Julia, and Pandora depart for the airport.
- Julia asks Brandi what she and Jamal do when they are not hiding in penthouses or reporting themselves to religious leaders.
- Brandi lists deliberately unremarkable activities:
  - Studying together.
  - Grocery shopping.
  - Walking with Burden.
  - Listening to films with audio description.
  - Finding late-night food after class.
- Kiley is relieved by the ordinariness. A durable relationship needs more than crisis and attraction.

### Beat 6: The Mosque Fundraiser Assumption

- Jamal mentions a mosque community fundraiser scheduled for the coming Saturday.
- Speaking casually, he says:

> “We’ll be there from about four until—”

- Brandi interrupts:

> “You’ll be there. I didn’t say I was going.”

- The kitchen becomes quiet.
- Jamal is surprised. He says he thought they had discussed attending together.
- Brandi reminds him that he told her about the fundraiser and she said it sounded important. She never agreed to attend.
- Brandi already committed to an accessibility committee presentation at UNLV during the same hours.
- Jamal says:

> “I thought ‘that sounds important’ meant you wanted to come.”

- Brandi answers:

> “It meant it sounded important. You heard a yes because you wanted one.”

- Kiley and Julia do not intervene. This is the couple’s disagreement to manage.

### Beat 7: Brandi and Jamal Repair the Misunderstanding

- Jamal becomes defensive for one brief moment.

> “You could have told me you had another plan.”

- Brandi replies:

> “You could have asked whether I was free.”

- The tension is real but contained.
- Jamal stops, thinks, and acknowledges that Brandi is right. He turned her interest in his community into an agreement.
- Brandi then accepts her own part: she knew the fundraiser might become an invitation and avoided stating her schedule because she did not want to disappoint him.

> “I should have said I was busy when you first mentioned Saturday.”

- Jamal says:

> “I should have invited you instead of announcing us.”

- They decide:
  - Jamal will attend the fundraiser without Brandi.
  - Brandi will give her UNLV presentation.
  - They will meet afterward and tell each other how both events went.
- Jamal does not ask Brandi to cancel her commitment.
- Brandi does not treat his independent attendance as rejection.
- The disagreement ends with a plan, not punishment or silence.

### Beat 8: What the Mothers Observe

- Kiley resumes pouring coffee only after Brandi and Jamal finish resolving the issue.
- Julia asks one question:

> “Are both of you satisfied with that plan, or are you only finished arguing?”

- Brandi says she is satisfied.
- Jamal says he is disappointed Brandi cannot attend but satisfied with the plan.
- Julia accepts the distinction. Disappointment does not automatically mean coercion.
- Kiley tells them:

> “That was more useful than every perfect answer you gave us last night.”

- Jamal asks why.
- Kiley says:

> “Because last night you knew you were being watched. This morning you forgot.”

- Brandi smiles. Kiley has seen them disagree without either person becoming small.

### Beat 9: Faith Without Possession

- The fundraiser misunderstanding leads naturally into a larger conversation about faith.
- Jamal explains that he wants Brandi to know his Muslim community but does not expect her to attend every event.
- Brandi says she wants Jamal to understand her Catholic life without treating Mass as a performance he must attend to prove love.
- Julia asks how they plan to handle holidays and religious obligations if the relationship lasts.
- Brandi says they do not have every answer yet.
- Jamal adds:

> “We can learn each other’s calendars before we try to solve the rest of our lives.”

- Pandora says that may be the most sensible thing anyone has said during the visit.
- Kiley tells her not to sound so surprised.

### Beat 10: Preparing for Reno

- Kiley asks Jamal what Brandi should expect when they arrive at his parents’ home in Reno.
- Jamal talks about ordinary family evenings, his mother switching languages when she is tired, and his father insisting everyone eat before discussing anything serious.
- Kiley recognizes the same impulse behind her own breakfast invitation: feed someone before deciding what the day means.
- Jamal’s parents know that Brandi is his girlfriend and that she is coming with him. They do not yet know the complete truth about Mohammed or how the relationship began.
- Jamal plans to tell them in person while Brandi is present.
- Brandi asks what, precisely, he intends to disclose. They agree that his parents should hear:
  - Brandi was still dating Mohammed when she and Jamal became involved.
  - Mohammed was Jamal’s closest friend through the mosque.
  - Mohammed discovered the relationship.
  - Brandi ended her relationship with Mohammed.
  - Brandi and Jamal spoke with Imam Kareem and accepted responsibility.
- They will not share private sexual details. Accountability does not require surrendering Brandi’s privacy.
- Kiley asks whether Jamal’s parents understand that Brandi is Catholic and will remain Catholic. Jamal says yes.
- Julia tells both of them to enter the Reno visit as partners, not as Jamal presenting Brandi for approval.
- The wider-family step is no longer distant. It begins as soon as breakfast ends and the Las Vegas departures are underway.

### Beat 11: Breakfast Becomes Comfortable

- The pancakes are imperfect but good.
- Pandora steals the last piece of bread from Jamal’s side of the table.
- Julia refills his coffee without asking whether he is staying longer; the gesture quietly communicates that he is no longer expected to leave immediately after answering questions.
- Brandi talks about an upcoming UNLV assignment.
- Jamal asks whether she wants his help reviewing it.
- Brandi says she wants him to listen to one practice run but not rewrite anything.
- Jamal agrees.
- Kiley and Julia exchange a look. This is what they came to observe: ordinary negotiation without rescue.

### Beat 12: Departure Logistics Replace the Debrief

- Jamal checks the time, but he is not leaving for class. He has cleared the day for the trip to Reno.
- The five of them carry dishes into the kitchen and begin shifting from breakfast to departure.
- The travel plan is stated clearly:
  - Kiley Rose and Julia are flying to **Panama City**.
  - Pandora Paige is flying to **Los Angeles**.
  - Jamal is taking Brandi Jessica to **Reno** to see his parents.
- Jamal will drive Kiley Rose, Julia, and Pandora to the Las Vegas airport. After their departures, he and Brandi will continue to Reno.
- Brandi confirms that her overnight bag, medication, chargers, Catholic prayer book, scarf, long-sleeved blouse, ankle-length skirt, white tights, and Burden’s travel supplies are packed.
- She chose the scarf to cover her hair, the long-sleeved blouse, the skirt reaching her ankles, and the white tights while visiting Jamal’s parents as a gesture of respect.
- Jamal asks Brandi whether she wants him to check the bag or read the packing list aloud.
- Brandi asks him to read the list once. She confirms each item herself.
- Kiley sees that Jamal treats travel preparation as cooperation rather than supervision.

### Beat 13: The Four Women Get One Private Minute

- Jamal takes the first suitcases to the vehicle, giving Kiley Rose, Julia, Brandi, and Pandora a few minutes alone in the penthouse.
- Pandora asks Kiley whether Jamal passed the breakfast test.
- Kiley refuses the language of passing and failing.

> “He listened. Brandi spoke. They repaired something small before it became large.”

- Julia says the disagreement mattered more than Jamal’s careful answers the previous evening.
- Brandi asks whether they like him.
- Kiley answers:

> “I am beginning to.”

- Brandi becomes quiet because this is more acceptance than she expected.
- Kiley adds:

> “Do not make me regret saying that.”

- Pandora replies, *“There she is.”*
- The women laugh, but the approaching departures make the moment tender.
- Kiley tells Brandi to call after she and Jamal reach Reno—not for permission or tracking, only so the family knows they arrived safely.
- Brandi agrees.

### Beat 14: Four Directions from One Penthouse

- Jamal returns for the remaining luggage.
- Burden’s harness goes on, putting him into working mode for the trip. Brandi does not use her white cane at the same time.
- Kiley Rose and Julia check their passports and boarding information for Panama City.
- Pandora checks her Los Angeles flight and messages Bassem with her itinerary.
- Brandi stands in the center of her own living room, listening to suitcase wheels, phone alerts, and four overlapping travel plans.
- For several days, the penthouse held her mothers, her twin, and her new boyfriend. Now everyone is leaving in different directions.
- Kiley embraces both daughters. Julia joins the embrace and tells Jamal to take care on the road to Reno.
- Kiley corrects the wording gently:

> “Take care of each other.”

- Brandi answers, *“We will.”*
- Pandora hugs Brandi last and whispers:

> “Call me after you meet them.”

- Brandi says, *“You’ll be in Los Angeles.”*

> “Phones work there.”

- Before they leave, Kiley tells Brandi:

> “Fresh is not something Jamal gives you. It is something both of you keep choosing.”

- Brandi answers:

> “One trip at a time?”

- Kiley says:

> “One ordinary day at a time.”

- The five of them leave the penthouse together.
- At the airport, the family separates:
  - Kiley Rose and Julia continue toward their flight to Panama City.
  - Pandora Paige continues toward her flight to Los Angeles.
  - Brandi Jessica returns to Jamal, and the two of them begin the journey to Reno to see his parents.
- The scene ends not with the family settled, but with every branch moving: Panama City, Los Angeles, and Reno from the same Las Vegas morning.
