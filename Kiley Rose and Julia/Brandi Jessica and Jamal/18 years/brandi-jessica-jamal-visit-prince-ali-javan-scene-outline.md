# Brandi Jessica and Jamal Visit Prince Ali Javan

## Scene Outline

## Placement

**When:** Approximately twenty years after Prince Ali Javan helped Mike and Terri Anne locate seven-year-old Brandi Jessica in the UAE. Brandi Jessica is an adult and is roughly **twenty years older** than the child Prince Ali Javan remembers from the Shakib rescue context.

**Where:** United Arab Emirates — Prince Ali Javan's compound / receiving rooms.

**Characters:** Brandi Jessica Key, her husband **Jamal Al-Otaibi**, Iyeshia Caesarea Key, Kiley Rose Key, Julia, Prince Ali Javan, household attendants / guards as needed. Brandi Jessica uses her **white cane** during the visit.

---

## Scene Purpose

1. Pay off the long-ago Prince Ali Javan rescue thread by having **Brandi Jessica and her husband Jamal** visit Prince Ali Javan as adults.
2. Establish that **Iyeshia Caesarea, Kiley Rose, and Julia accompany them**.
3. Show that **Prince Ali Javan does not recognize Brandi Jessica at first** because she is twenty years older than the seven-year-old child connected to the rescue.
4. Add the visual / cultural reason for his confusion: **Brandi Jessica, Kiley Rose, and Julia are wearing Muslim Women's Full Face Niqab Veils**.
5. Establish that **Brandi Jessica is using her white cane** during the visit.
6. Establish Jamal's background in the scene as **Saudi and China Uyghur**.
7. Have Brandi Jessica tell Prince Ali Javan that they are wearing the veils because they want to respect his household.
8. Have Prince Ali Javan tell Brandi Jessica that he respects her, that what Shakib was doing was wrong, and that the women can get changed out of the veils.
9. Have Prince Ali Javan ask Brandi Jessica about her white cane.
10. Have Brandi Jessica tell Prince Ali Javan that she is **a choreographer for a mainstream cruise line without sight**.
11. Establish that **Iyeshia Caesarea is crawling by this time** and remains a happy baby in the room with Jamal and Prince Ali Javan while the women change out of the veils.
11. Have Prince Ali Javan order his staff to cook a dinner to celebrate **Brandi Jessica's safety and return**.
12. Have Brandi Jessica ask Jamal to call **Pandora Paige and Bassem** to tell them they will be coming over tomorrow morning.
13. Have Brandi Jessica ask Prince Ali Javan if he does not mind that she will nurse Iyeshia Caesarea at the dining room table.
14. Let the visit be respectful, emotionally careful, and rooted in the old relationship of trust between Prince Ali Javan and the Key family.

---

## Core Emotional Throughline

Prince Ali Javan once helped the Key family find Brandi Jessica when she was a seven-year-old kidnapped child. Twenty years later, Brandi returns as a grown woman, a mother, **Jamal's wife**, and a blind survivor whose life moved far beyond the moment Prince Ali helped reveal her location.

But Prince Ali Javan does not recognize her at first.

The girl he remembers was seven. The woman in front of him is twenty years older. Brandi Jessica is also using her **white cane**, a fact Prince Ali Javan does not have from the old rescue memory. And Brandi Jessica, Kiley Rose, and Julia are all wearing **Muslim Women's Full Face Niqab Veils**, so even if he tried to look for a familiar face, there is no face to read. Recognition has to come through names, voices, history, and the presence of the family he once helped.

---

## Scene Structure

### Beat 1: Arrival at Prince Ali Javan's Compound

- Brandi Jessica and her husband **Jamal** arrive with **Iyeshia Caesarea**.
- **Kiley Rose and Julia accompany them**.
- **Brandi Jessica is using her white cane** as she enters Prince Ali Javan's compound / receiving rooms.
- The women — Brandi Jessica, Kiley Rose, and Julia — are wearing **Muslim Women's Full Face Niqab Veils** as a gesture of respect for Prince Ali Javan's household and religious / cultural expectations.
- Brandi Jessica later states this plainly: they are wearing the veils because they want to respect his household.
- Jamal helps with spatial orientation as needed, but he does not speak for Brandi.
- Iyeshia Caesarea's presence makes the visit generational: the child rescued twenty years ago is now bringing her own daughter into the room.
- **Iyeshia Caesarea is crawling by this time**, old enough to move around the room as a happy, curious baby.

### Beat 2: Prince Ali Javan Receives Them

- Prince Ali Javan receives the visitors with formal hospitality.
- He recognizes the family name, the relationship to Mike and Terri Anne, and the connection to Kiley Rose, but he does not immediately recognize Brandi Jessica as the child from the old rescue thread.
- The niqab veils make visual recognition impossible.
- Brandi Jessica's age makes recognition even less likely: he last understood her as a seven-year-old child, not as a grown woman and mother.
- Her white cane also marks a life change he may not expect in the first moment; he helped locate a sighted kidnapped child, and the adult woman before him is blind.

### Beat 3: The Moment of Non-Recognition

- Prince Ali Javan addresses the group politely but uncertainly.
- He may assume Brandi is another relative, Jamal's wife / partner, or one of the Key women without realizing she is *that* Brandi Jessica.
- This is not an insult. It is the realistic result of time, veiling, and trauma memory.
- Kiley Rose and Julia notice the moment.
- Jamal notices too, but lets Brandi decide whether and how to identify herself.

### Beat 4: Brandi Jessica Identifies Herself

- Brandi Jessica says her name plainly:

> I am Brandi Jessica Key.

- Prince Ali Javan processes the name before the person.
- The name carries the old phone call, Shakib, Mike Key, Terri Anne, Kiley Rose's desperation, and the rescue.
- He realizes that the veiled adult woman before him is the child whose location he gave to Mike twenty years earlier.

### Beat 5: Why He Did Not Recognize Her

- The scene should explicitly note the reasons:
  - **Brandi Jessica is twenty years older.**
  - **Brandi Jessica, Kiley Rose, and Julia are wearing Muslim Women's Full Face Niqab Veils.**
  - **Brandi Jessica is using her white cane**, which marks a life change Prince Ali Javan does not immediately connect to the seven-year-old child from the old rescue.
- Prince Ali Javan may say something respectful about time passing or about not recognizing her behind the veil.
- Brandi should not be made to reassure him excessively.
- The point is not embarrassment; the point is the strangeness of survival across twenty years.

### Beat 6: The Veils as Respect

- Brandi Jessica tells Prince Ali Javan directly that she, Kiley Rose, and Julia are wearing the **Muslim Women's Full Face Niqab Veils** because they want to respect his household.
- This echoes Terri Anne's earlier respect for Prince Ali Javan's religion and household, but now it is the next generation of women showing that respect.
- Brandi's explanation should be calm and voluntary, not defensive.
- Prince Ali Javan receives the explanation with respect.
- He tells Brandi Jessica that he respects her.
- He also tells her that what Shakib was doing was wrong.
- He makes clear that if they would be more comfortable, Brandi Jessica, Kiley Rose, and Julia can get changed out of the veils. His offer should read as hospitality and respect, not pressure.

### Beat 7: Kiley Rose and Julia's Presence

- Kiley Rose and Julia accompany Brandi and Jamal because this visit touches the deepest wound of Brandi's childhood.
- Kiley Rose is the mother who lived the kidnapping and rescue from the parental side.
- Julia is the parent who entered the family during Brandi's cancer / blindness season and became part of her survival architecture.
- Their choice to wear the niqab veils is not spectacle; it is a family echo of respect.

### Beat 8: Iyeshia Caesarea in the Room

- Iyeshia Caesarea accompanies Brandi and Jamal.
- **Iyeshia Caesarea is crawling by this time.**
- Her presence makes the scene more than a trauma-return visit.
- Brandi is not only the child who was rescued; she is a mother bringing her daughter into a room where an old debt of gratitude lives.
- Prince Ali Javan may react gently to Iyeshia, recognizing that the child is proof that Brandi's life continued.
- Iyeshia remains a happy baby in the room: curious, safe, crawling near Jamal, and not frightened by the formal setting.

### Beat 9: Jamal's Role

- Jamal is present as **Brandi's husband** and Iyeshia's father.
- Jamal's background is **Saudi and China Uyghur**: his father is Saudi Arabian, his mother is Chinese Uyghur, and he carries both parts of that inheritance into the visit.
- He understands the cultural space and the etiquette of the visit.
- He supports Brandi without taking over her voice.
- His Muslim identity, Saudi background, China Uyghur heritage, Arabic, and Uyghur cultural knowledge make him a bridge, but the gratitude belongs to Brandi and her family.

### Beat 10: Prince Ali Javan Asks About Brandi Jessica's White Cane

- After recognizing Brandi Jessica, Prince Ali Javan notices / asks about her **white cane**.
- His question should be respectful and careful, not intrusive or pitying.
- Brandi can explain, briefly, that after the rescue she was diagnosed with bilateral retinoblastoma and lost her sight at seven.
- She does not need to give a full medical history unless she chooses.
- Brandi then tells him what her adult life became: she is **a choreographer for a mainstream cruise line without sight**.
- This matters because Prince Ali Javan helped locate a frightened child; now he is meeting an adult artist whose blindness did not stop her from building a professional career.
- Kiley Rose and Julia may become emotionally still because the white cane belongs to the second catastrophe of Brandi's childhood, after the kidnapping.
- Jamal stays beside Brandi, letting her decide how much to say.
- Prince Ali Javan should receive the information with respect, not pity.

### Beat 11: The Women Change Out of the Veils

- After Prince Ali Javan tells Brandi Jessica, Kiley Rose, and Julia that they can get changed out of the veils, the women accept if they are comfortable.
- The change happens respectfully and privately, in a room / space provided by the household staff.
- Brandi Jessica, Kiley Rose, and Julia remove the **Muslim Women's Full Face Niqab Veils** because Prince Ali Javan has made clear that their respect has been received and they do not need to remain veiled inside his hospitality.
- This should not be written as immodesty or rejection of the veils. It is hospitality: they wore the veils to respect his household, and he now gives them permission to be comfortable.
- **Iyeshia Caesarea stays in the room with Jamal and Prince Ali Javan** while the women change.
- Iyeshia remains a happy baby, crawling around / near Jamal, held or redirected as needed.
- Jamal watches Iyeshia without making a production of it.
- Prince Ali Javan sees Iyeshia's happiness as another sign that Brandi Jessica's life continued beyond Shakib.

### Beat 12: Prince Ali Javan Orders a Celebration Dinner

- Prince Ali Javan orders his staff to cook a dinner.
- The dinner is specifically to celebrate **Brandi Jessica's safety and return**.
- The wording matters: he is not celebrating the old trauma; he is celebrating that Brandi survived, returned to her family, grew up, became a mother, and came back to this house alive.
- The dinner becomes a gesture of formal hospitality and moral acknowledgment.
- It also answers what Shakib did: where Shakib's household held Brandi in fear, Prince Ali Javan's household receives her with honor.
- Staff move quickly and respectfully; the receiving room shifts from formal visit to hosted family dinner.
- Brandi, Kiley Rose, and Julia may be emotional at the phrase *safety and return* because those are the words they once needed more than anything.

### Beat 13: Calling Pandora Paige and Bassem

- Once the visit has settled into hospitality, Brandi Jessica asks Jamal to call **Pandora Paige and Bassem**.
- She asks him to tell them that they will be coming over **tomorrow morning**.
- The call matters because Pandora Paige lives in **Saudi Arabia** with Bassem; this regional visit naturally leads to Brandi seeing her twin sister next.
- Brandi asks Jamal to make the call because he can handle the practical phone logistics while she remains present with Prince Ali Javan, Iyeshia, Kiley Rose, and Julia.
- Jamal does not turn the call into a decision he controls. He simply carries Brandi's message: they are coming over tomorrow morning.
- The call should feel ordinary and intimate after the formal recognition: the rescued child is now an adult woman coordinating tomorrow's visit to her twin.

### Beat 14: Brandi Asks About Nursing at the Dining Room Table

- Before dinner, Brandi Jessica asks Prince Ali Javan respectfully if he does not mind that she will nurse **Iyeshia Caesarea** at the dining room table.
- This should be framed as Brandi's courtesy toward his household, not as shame about nursing.
- Prince Ali Javan should answer with respect and hospitality, making clear that Brandi and Iyeshia are welcome and that a mother feeding her child is not an offense in his home.
- Brandi can nurse at the dining room table in whatever manner is comfortable for her and appropriate to the household setting.
- Kiley Rose and Julia understand the weight of the moment: Brandi was once a frightened child in the UAE, and now she is a mother calmly asking to feed her daughter at a table prepared to celebrate her safety and return.
- Jamal stays attentive without making nursing something he must manage.
- Iyeshia remains a happy baby: crawling earlier, nursing when hungry, and being held inside a room that is choosing welcome.

### Beat 15: The Old Debt Named Carefully

- Brandi, Kiley Rose, and Julia may thank Prince Ali Javan for what he did twenty years earlier.
- The thanks should be careful:
  - he helped locate Brandi;
  - he used influence and information to help Mike and Terri Anne act;
  - he did not rescue her alone, and the scene should not rewrite the family rescue as one man's heroism.
- Prince Ali Javan can answer with dignity and humility, grounding his help in respect for Mike and Terri Anne and the old trust between families.

### Beat 16: Closing Image

- Possible closing image:
  - Prince Ali Javan looking at the veiled woman he finally understands is Brandi Jessica Key.
  - Brandi holding Iyeshia Caesarea close while Kiley Rose and Julia stand beside her.
  - Iyeshia crawling happily near Jamal while the household begins preparing the celebration dinner.
  - Brandi Jessica, Kiley Rose, and Julia changed out of the veils after Prince Ali Javan receives their gesture of respect.
  - Jamal near enough to support but not between Brandi and the old history, calling Pandora Paige and Bassem because Brandi asked him to tell them they are coming over tomorrow morning.
  - Brandi nursing Iyeshia at the dining room table after asking Prince Ali Javan if he minds.
  - Recognition arriving not through sight, but through name, memory, and the fact that Brandi is alive.

## Key Dialogue Possibilities

### Brandi Jessica

> I am Brandi Jessica Key.

> You knew my name when I was seven.

> I came because my family never forgot what you did.

> We wore the veils because we wanted to respect your household.

> This is my white cane. I lost my sight after I came home.

> I am a choreographer for a mainstream cruise line now. I do it without sight.

> Jamal, can you call Pandora and Bassem and tell them we will come over tomorrow morning?

> Prince Ali Javan, if you do not mind, I will nurse her at the dining room table.

### Prince Ali Javan

> Forgive me. I did not know you.

> You were a child in the story I carried. You are a woman now.

> The veil hid your face. Time hid the rest.

> I respect you, Brandi Jessica.

> What Shakib was doing was wrong.

> If you wish, you and your mothers may get changed out of the veils here.

> May I ask about your white cane?

> A choreographer without sight. That is an extraordinary answer to what was done to you.

> Prepare dinner for our guests. We will celebrate Brandi Jessica's safety and return.

> Of course you may nurse your daughter at my table.

### Kiley Rose

> You called my father when we needed one true piece of information.

> We brought her back because you told us where to look.

### Julia

> This is Iyeshia Caesarea. Brandi's daughter.

> She crawls now. She is fast when she wants something.

### Jamal

> She wanted to speak for herself.

> My father is Saudi. My mother is China Uyghur. I know what it means to enter a house through respect.

> I will call Pandora and Bassem now. I will tell them tomorrow morning.
