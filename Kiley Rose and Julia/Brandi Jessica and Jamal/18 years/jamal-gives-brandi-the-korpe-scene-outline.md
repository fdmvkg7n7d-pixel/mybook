## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene follows **`jamal-takes-brandi-jessica-to-see-his-parents-in-reno-scene-outline.md`**.

Kiley Rose and Julia have flown to Panama City. Pandora Paige has flown to Los Angeles. Brandi Jessica and Jamal have returned to Las Vegas after seeing Jamal’s parents in Reno.

He brings her a **körpe** — a hand-quilted Uyghur blanket.

His maternal grandmother worked on it before she was killed, and his mother preserved it through relocation and life in Reno. Traditionally sewn by mothers and grandmothers for weddings and newborns, every stitch is raised and tactile — made to be read by hands.

For Brandi Jessica, who has been totally blind since age seven and navigates the world entirely by touch, sound, and memory, it is the first time a boyfriend has given her something from his faith and culture that she can actually *receive* on her own terms. This is the "welcome to my world" beat that deepens Brandi and Jamal’s new interfaith partnership after they have brought the truth into both families — Catholic / Muslim, blind / sighted, Key / Al-Otaibi.

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: The Penthouse — Quiet After Three Departures and Reno
- Evening, Brandi Jessica's Las Vegas penthouse — privately purchased by Kiley Rose and Julia after Brandi completed her first year in a UNLV dormitory; it is not university housing.

### Beat 2: The Bundle
- Jamal comes in carrying the bundle his mother gave him in Reno — heavy cotton, folded square, tied with a simple cloth tie. No gift bag, no store tag. Handmade and preserved within his maternal family.
- He doesn't say "close your eyes" — he never has to with her. He says: *"Hold out your hands."*
- He places the bundle in her lap. It is warm from being carried against his chest through the Las Vegas evening air. Heavy. Soft. Quilted — she feels the raised stitching immediately under her fingertips, geometric diamonds and running vine patterns, each line stitched by hand.
- *"What is it?"* she asks.
- *"Körpe,"* he says. First in Uyghur Turkic. Then in Arabic. Then in English: *"A quilt. A blanket."*

### Beat 3: Reading by Touch — Three Languages
- Jamal sits beside her on the couch and guides her hands across the körpe — not doing it *for* her, letting her read it herself, the way Julia and Kiley taught everyone to let Brandi do things herself: there are no "no's," only "hows."

  - **Arabic** second — the language of his father, of the mosque, of Imam Kareem, of the temple brotherhood he broke with Mohammed to be with her.
  - **English** third — the language they share, the language they live in, Las Vegas, UNLV, her world.
- *"This one is protection,"* — her fingers find a tight diamond grid — *"my mother calls it köz — eyes. Watching over the sleeper."* Brandi goes still. Eyes watching over a blind girl while she sleeps. She presses her palm flat against it.
  *"This one is home,"* — a meandering vine border — *"ötken yul — the road that brings you back."*
  *"This one—"* — eight-pointed stars — *"yultuz. Stars. Light remembered."*
- Brandi, who lost her sight at seven on Christmas Day and carries a full visual memory of light — sunsets over Rio, her sister's face, her mother's smile — runs her thumbs over the raised stars again and again. She doesn't see them. She remembers what stars look like. Now she can feel them too.
- Burden noses at the edge of the körpe, then settles his big Labernese head on Brandi's knee, under the blanket's edge. Both of them warm under it.

### Beat 4: "Nobody Ever Gave Me Anything" — She Breaks / He Panics / "I'll Treasure It"

- Brandi's hands are still spread flat on the körpe in her lap, fingertips pressed into the quilted stars — *yultuz, light remembered*.

  - Burden lifts his head off Brandi's feet, ears up, whining low — reading the distress in the room.
- Brandi hears the panic in Jamal's voice — really hears it — and forces herself to breathe through the tears. She reaches blindly across the couch, finds his wrist, grips hard and pulls his hands back to hers, pressing both of their palms flat down onto the körpe together.

  - *"Because nobody ever —"* she has to stop, swallow — *"Diederik never gave me anything from his world. Mohammed never gave me anything from his — and he took me to the same mosque as you, for three years. Three years, Jamal. Nothing. Not once."*

  - *"You're the first man who ever gave me something I could actually hold from where you come from."*
- The panic drains out of Jamal all at once. He exhales hard, forehead dropping to touch the crown of her head. His hands steady over hers on the körpe. *"It's yours,"* he says, quieter now. *"It was always going to be yours."*
- Brandi wipes her face with the back of her wrist, laughing wetly through the tears — embarrassed, relieved, overwhelmed all at once. *"Say the names again. All three languages. Slow."*
- He does. Uyghur Turkic. Arabic. English. Protection. Home. Light remembered. She memorizes each one by touch and by sound, the way she memorizes everything — building a tactile map of his world to match the visual map of hers she still carries from age seven.
- She pulls the körpe up around her shoulders. It smells faintly of cedar and clean cotton — nothing like a store. Like someone's hands made it. Because someone's hands did.
- She turns her face toward where she hears Jamal breathing, both fists clenched tight in the quilted fabric: *"I'm going to treasure this. For the rest of my life, Jamal. Do you hear me? I'm never letting this go."*
- Jamal doesn't answer with words — he just pulls the other side of the körpe up over both of their shoulders, wrapping them together in it.
- Burden settles back down with a long sigh, head back on Brandi's feet, the crisis in the room dissolved.

Jamal gets her water, finds her cane and places it within arm's reach of the bed without being asked (so she can navigate to the bathroom independently if she wants — respecting her autonomy, the way Kiley/Julia raised her), wipes her face with a cool cloth, pulls the körpe back up over both of them. They talk quietly, half-asleep, hands intertwined. Brandi, drowsy, fingers still tracing the quilted stars on his chest: *"I'm going to treasure this. All of this."* He kisses her temple: *"Go to sleep, habibti."*

### Beat 6: Morning After — The Relationship in Daylight
- Morning light through the east-facing bedroom windows — Las Vegas sun warm on Brandi's face, which she feels even blind. Burden noses the bedroom door open and tags Brandi's hanging hand with a cold nose.
- Brandi and Jamal wake wrapped in the körpe. For the first time since Mohammed discovered them, there is no new confession or first introduction waiting to be made. Both families know the truth.
- Over coffee, they compare the two family responses. Kiley Rose and Julia are beginning to know Jamal; Jamal’s parents are beginning to know Brandi.
- Neither family excused the betrayal. Both families left the door open to what Brandi and Jamal might build next.
- Jamal asks whether Kiley admitting she was beginning to like him meant the Key family was beginning to trust him.
- Brandi answers: *"They’re willing to know you. Trust takes longer."*
- Brandi asks the same question about Jamal’s parents.
- Jamal gives her the same answer: *"They’re willing to know you. Trust takes longer."*
- Brandi traces the raised stars on the körpe. The repeated sentence makes the two families feel less like opposing sides and more like parallel doors.
- The scene ends in quiet daylight, with the family quilt from Reno now resting in Brandi’s Las Vegas home.
