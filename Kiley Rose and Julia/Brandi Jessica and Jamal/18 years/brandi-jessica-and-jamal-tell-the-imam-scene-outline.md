- **Offstage / referenced characters:** Mohammed, Kiley Rose Key, Julia, Pandora Paige Key, Burden
- **Location:** Imam's office, Las Vegas, Nevada
- **When:** UNLV years, ~2065-2066 — one to two days after Mohammed catches Brandi Jessica and Jamal in the penthouse shower
- **Status:** Scene outline
- **Tone:** Sober, respectful, accountable, interfaith, earnest
- **Content Note:** All characters are adults 21+.

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene follows **the Mohammed catch scene** (`mohammed-catches-brandi-and-jamal-shower-scene-outline.md`).

After the explosive confrontation in Brandi Jessica's penthouse, Brandi and Jamal go together to speak with **Imam Kareem** at the mosque/temple where Mohammed and Jamal grew up as temple brothers.

Brandi, a practicing Catholic, comes with Jamal out of respect for Jamal's faith community and for Mohammed — acknowledging that the betrayal hurt more than just a relationship, it hurt a brotherhood and a faith community. They are not there to ask for permission or a blessing, they are there to be accountable in person: to tell the Imam what happened before the community gossip does, to take responsibility for the affair and for breaking Mohammed's trust, and to ask for counsel on how to move forward without further harm.

This is the scene where Brandi and Jamal go public within the temple community — together, voluntarily, and on their own terms.

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: The Walk In — Together
- Morning, two days after the penthouse confrontation.
- Brandi Jessica and Jamal arrive at the mosque together, hand in hand. Burden is in harness guiding Brandi at her left side — her white cane is folded and stowed in her tote, not in use while Burden is working.
- Before entering, Brandi puts on a headscarf over her hair, out of respect. Jamal helps her adjust it.
- At the entrance they remove their shoes, as is customary. Brandi slips hers off by feel, placing them on the rack with Jamal's guidance.
- Brandi is Catholic — she has never been inside this mosque before. Jamal quietly orients her: describing the entryway, the quiet, the carpet underfoot. She listens to the acoustics the way she always does in a new building.
- They asked for a private meeting with Imam Kareem. He agreed.

### Beat 2: Telling the Imam
- Imam Kareem's office. Simple, quiet. Tea offered, accepted.
- Jamal speaks first. He tells the Imam plainly: he and Brandi have been seeing each other secretly while Brandi was with Mohammed. Mohammed caught them two nights ago in Brandi's penthouse. He does not minimize, does not blame Mohammed, does not make excuses. "It was my choice. My betrayal."
- Brandi speaks next. She introduces herself — Brandi Jessica Key, twenty-one, UNLV, Catholic, blind since age seven. She tells the Imam that Mohammed was her boyfriend for three years, that he was awful to her, that she fell for Jamal, that she cheated, and that she owns that. She is not asking the Imam to approve of her. She is here because Jamal's faith and his community matter to him, and therefore they matter to her.
- Imam Kareem listens. He knows all three of them — he watched Mohammed and Jamal grow up as temple brothers. He is measured, not explosive like Mohammed was. He names the harm clearly: a trust broken between brothers, a woman hurt, a community that will talk.

### Beat 3: Counsel
- Imam Kareem speaks to Jamal about brotherhood, accountability, and making amends to Mohammed when Mohammed is ready — not now, not forced, but eventually. He does not excommunicate or shame Jamal. He tells him: "You will carry what you did. Carry it honestly."
- He speaks to Brandi with respect. He acknowledges her faith, does not try to convert her, thanks her for coming in person. He tells her: "You came here with open hands. Keep them open."
- He tells them both: if they are going to be together, be together truthfully. No more secrets. No more overlapping. Build something clean from here.
- They ask for no formal blessing — just his counsel, and that he hear it from them first before anyone else brings it to him.

### Beat 4: Leaving
- Brandi and Jamal thank Imam Kareem and leave together.
- Outside the mosque, Burden guides Brandi down the steps into the Las Vegas sunlight she can feel but not see.
- Jamal: "You didn't have to do that."
- Brandi: "Yes I did. You're mine now. That means your people are mine too."
- They walk to the car together, publicly, no longer hiding.
- This locks in the public transition from the Mohammed relationship to the Jamal relationship.
