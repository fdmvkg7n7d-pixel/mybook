# Jamal Takes Brandi Jessica to See His Parents in Reno — Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Brandi Jessica Key (21), Jamal Al-Otaibi (21), Jamal’s mother, Jamal’s father, Jamal’s childhood imam in Reno
- **Offstage / referenced:** Kiley Rose, Julia, Pandora Paige, Mohammed, Imam Kareem
- **Onstage animal:** Burden, Brandi Jessica’s Labernese guide dog
- **Locations:** The road from Las Vegas to Reno; Jamal’s parents’ home; Jamal’s childhood school; the Reno mosque where Jamal worshipped as a child
- **When:** UNLV years, ~2065–2066 — immediately after the family’s Las Vegas airport goodbyes
- **Brandi Jessica’s clothing in Reno:** A scarf covering her hair, a long-sleeved blouse, an ankle-length skirt, and white tights, worn voluntarily to show respect for Jamal’s parents
- **At the doorway:** Brandi removes her shoes and places them in the entryway cubbyhole
- **Guide dog:** Burden accompanies Brandi throughout the Reno visit. Jamal’s parents dislike dogs and normally do not welcome them inside, but they understand that Burden is a working guide dog—Brandi’s eyes in an unfamiliar house—and welcome him
- **Status:** Scene outline
- **Tone:** Nervous, culturally grounded, accountable, intimate, warmly welcoming

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

After taking Kiley Rose, Julia, and Pandora Paige to the Las Vegas airport, Jamal takes Brandi Jessica to Reno to see his parents.

Kiley Rose and Julia are flying to Panama City. Pandora is flying to Los Angeles. Brandi and Jamal leave Las Vegas in the remaining direction: north toward the home where Jamal was raised.

Jamal’s parents know that Brandi is his girlfriend and that she is coming. They know basic facts about her, including her Catholic faith and blindness, but they do not know the full truth about Mohammed. Brandi and Jamal have agreed to tell them together.

The visit gives Brandi access to Jamal’s family life beyond the mosque and the betrayal. It also requires Jamal to maintain the same account in Reno that he gave Imam Kareem, Pandora, Kiley Rose, and Julia.

Brandi chooses respectful clothing for the visit: a scarf covering her hair, a long-sleeved blouse, an ankle-length skirt, and white tights. At the doorway, she removes her shoes and places them in a cubbyhole. Jamal’s parents did not demand the clothing, and it does not signal conversion. It is Brandi’s voluntary gesture of respect while entering their home. Burden, her guide dog, accompanies her.

Jamal’s parents are very impressed by the care Brandi takes to respect their household. They tell her directly that they appreciate her effort and welcome her into their home. Their disappointment in how the relationship began does not become rejection of Brandi.

Although Jamal’s parents dislike dogs and ordinarily do not permit them in the house, they welcome Burden. They understand that he is not visiting as a pet: he is Brandi’s trained guide dog and functions as her eyes in an unfamiliar environment. Respecting Brandi means respecting the working partnership that gives her safe, independent movement.

During dinner, Brandi tells Jamal’s parents about her career choice: she wants to become a professional choreographer. She explains that blindness changes her working process but does not reduce her artistic authority.

Before returning to Las Vegas, Jamal also shows Brandi the school he attended as a child and takes her to meet his childhood imam at the Reno mosque. These visits let Brandi know Jamal through the places and people that formed him, not only through the crisis with Mohammed.

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: Three Departures at the Airport

- Jamal drives the family from Brandi’s penthouse to the Las Vegas airport.
- Kiley Rose and Julia check in for their flight to Panama City.
- Pandora Paige checks in for her flight to Los Angeles.
- Brandi remains with Jamal and Burden after the airport goodbyes.
- Kiley hugs Brandi and reminds her to call after reaching Reno.
- Julia tells Jamal:

> “Do not protect your parents from the truth by making Brandi carry it alone.”

- Jamal answers, *“I won’t.”*
- Pandora hugs Brandi and says she expects a full report from Los Angeles.
- Brandi listens as the three women disappear into separate airport lines.
- Jamal asks whether she is ready to leave for Reno.

> “Ready enough.”

- They return to the vehicle and begin the journey north.

### Beat 2: The Nevada Road

- The first part of the trip is quiet after the crowded family morning.
- Burden settles securely in the back with his travel setup.
- Jamal describes the changing landscape when Brandi asks: the city thinning, the long Nevada road, the mountains in the distance.
- Brandi remembers Nevada visually only through secondhand descriptions; she lets Jamal build the route through sound and language.
- She asks practical questions about his parents:
  - Which language his mother uses when emotional.
  - Whether his father prefers directness or gradual conversation.
  - Where the shoe cubbyhole is located inside the door.
  - Whether Burden is welcome inside.
  - Where she will sleep.
- Jamal says Burden is expected and welcome. His parents dislike dogs and normally keep their home dog-free, but Jamal explained that Burden is a working guide dog, not a pet Brandi can simply leave behind. His parents understand that welcoming Brandi requires welcoming Burden.
- His parents also understand that Brandi and Jamal will sleep in separate rooms while staying in their home.
- Brandi accepts the arrangement without treating it as judgment.

> “Their house, their boundaries.”

- Brandi tells Jamal she brought a scarf to cover her hair, a long-sleeved blouse, a skirt reaching her ankles, and white tights. She wants to dress respectfully when she enters his parents’ home.
- Jamal makes clear that his parents did not demand it. Brandi answers that she knows; the choice is hers.
- Jamal tells her his mother has prepared the guest room and his father cleared the usual clutter from the hallway without turning the house into a medical space.

### Beat 3: Agreeing on the Truth Before Arrival

- During a stop, Brandi asks Jamal to repeat exactly what he plans to tell his parents about Mohammed.
- Jamal gives the same sequence they agreed on at breakfast:
  - Brandi was still dating Mohammed when she and Jamal became involved.
  - Mohammed was Jamal’s closest friend through the mosque.
  - Mohammed discovered them.
  - Brandi ended the relationship with Mohammed.
  - Brandi and Jamal went to Imam Kareem and accepted responsibility.
- Brandi adds that Mohammed treated her badly but insists they will not use that fact as an excuse.
- They agree not to describe private sexual details.
- Jamal asks Brandi whether she wants him to do the first telling.

> “Start it. But don’t answer for me when they ask why I stayed with Mohammed or why I chose you.”

- Jamal agrees.
- The conversation lowers Brandi’s anxiety because there will not be two competing versions in the room.

### Beat 4: Brandi Dresses for the Visit

- Before the final approach to Reno, Brandi changes into the outfit she packed:
  - White tights.
  - A long skirt with its hem reaching her ankles.
  - A long-sleeved blouse.
  - A scarf covering her hair while leaving her face uncovered.
- Brandi arranges, ties, and checks the scarf herself by touch. She asks Jamal only whether any hair remains visible at the back.
- Jamal answers the question directly without retieing it for her.
- Brandi puts her shoes back on for the approach to the house, knowing she will remove them at the doorway.
- Brandi keeps her Catholic prayer book in her overnight bag. Her outfit is courtesy toward Jamal’s parents, not a change of faith.

### Beat 5: Arrival at the Al-Otaibi Home

- They reach Reno in the evening.
- Jamal tells Brandi when they turn onto his childhood street and when the vehicle enters the driveway.
- Before they get out, he describes the front approach: a short path, two shallow steps, a covered porch, and the door directly ahead.
- Brandi harnesses Burden for the unfamiliar environment. She does not use her white cane at the same time.
- Jamal’s parents open the door before they reach it.
- His mother greets Jamal first with relief and affection, moving between Uyghur Turkic and English.
- She greets Brandi by name rather than speaking to Jamal about her.
- Jamal’s father welcomes Brandi more formally but warmly.
- Jamal’s mother notices Brandi’s scarf, long-sleeved blouse, ankle-length skirt, and white tights. She tells Brandi the clothing was not required.
- Brandi answers:

> “I know. I wanted to show respect when I came into your home.”

- Jamal’s mother tells Brandi she is very impressed that Brandi chose to learn and respect the household without being asked. She does not treat the clothing as evidence of conversion or obedience.
- Neither parent grabs Brandi or Burden. Jamal’s mother asks:

> “Would you like my arm, Jamal’s arm, or directions while Burden works?”

- Brandi answers:

> “Burden has me. Just tell me where the steps begin.”

- Jamal’s father gives a clear verbal count, and they enter.
- Just inside the doorway, Jamal tells Brandi there is a bench immediately to her right and a shoe cubbyhole below it.
- Brandi asks Burden to stop and wait. She sits, removes her shoes herself, locates an empty cubby by touch, and places both shoes inside side by side.
- Jamal removes his shoes and places them in the neighboring cubby.
- Jamal’s father watches Burden stop at the threshold, guide Brandi to the bench, and wait without wandering through the house. His uncertainty about allowing a dog inside begins to change.
- He tells Brandi:

> “We do not like dogs in the house. But he is not here merely as a dog. In a place you do not know, he is your eyes.”

- Jamal’s mother adds:

> “Then Burden is welcome wherever you are welcome.”

- Brandi thanks them and explains that the best welcome is to let Burden work without calling, feeding, or petting him while he is in harness.
- Both parents agree.
- Brandi remains in her white tights indoors. Burden stays harnessed beside her while she learns the unfamiliar house.
- Jamal’s father tells her:

> “You have shown respect for our household from your first step inside it. We are very impressed, Brandi Jessica. You are welcome here.”

- Jamal’s mother reinforces the welcome:

> “You do not have to become someone else to enter this home. You came with respect, and we receive you with respect.”

- Brandi is moved by the welcome because it distinguishes hospitality from approval of every choice she and Jamal made.

### Beat 6: Learning the House

- Jamal gives Brandi a concise orientation rather than leading her by the hand:
  - Living room to the left.
  - Dining room ahead.
  - Kitchen beyond it.
  - Guest room and bathroom down the right hallway.
- Brandi walks the route once with Burden, learning the floor transitions and door positions.
- Jamal’s mother has placed Brandi’s water glass in a consistent location and asks before moving anything.
- The parents have prepared without infantilizing her.
- Jamal’s parents have placed a washable mat and water bowl in the guest room. They do not pretend to enjoy dogs, but they have prepared a proper place for Burden.
- Jamal’s father shows Brandi where Burden’s bowls can go and asks what commands the family should avoid using around a working guide dog.
- Brandi explains that Burden will rest quietly, stay off the furniture, and remain under her control. She also explains when his harness can come off.
- Jamal’s parents agree not to call, feed, pet, or redirect him while he is working.
- The practical exchange gives everyone something useful to do with their nervousness and confirms that the household’s welcome extends to Brandi’s guide dog.

### Beat 7: Food Before Serious Conversation

- As Jamal predicted, his father insists everyone eat before discussing anything serious.
- Burden lies quietly beneath Brandi’s chair, out of the walking path. Jamal’s parents see that the working dog does not disrupt their household.
- Dinner reflects both sides of Jamal’s family background: Uyghur dishes from his mother and Saudi dishes associated with his father’s family.
- Jamal’s mother names each dish for Brandi and describes its position on the table.
- Brandi asks questions about ingredients, family recipes, and which dish Jamal hated as a child.
- His mother answers immediately. Jamal protests the accuracy of the story.
- The laughter is small but genuine.
- Jamal’s parents learn about Brandi beyond the crisis:
  - Her UNLV studies.
  - Growing up in New York City.
  - Kiley Rose and Julia.
  - Pandora Paige and their identical-twin bond.
  - Her Catholic education at the Convent of the Sacred Heart.
  - Burden’s training and work.
- Brandi’s blindness is present but does not consume the dinner.

### Beat 8: Brandi Tells Them Her Career Choice

- Jamal’s mother asks Brandi what she wants to do after finishing university.
- Brandi answers without hesitation:

> “I want to be a choreographer.”

- Jamal remains quiet so Brandi can describe the ambition herself.
- Brandi explains that choreography joins the physical and musical languages she has practiced since childhood: ballet, gymnastics, aerial work, rhythm, and spatial memory.
- Jamal’s father asks respectfully how she plans choreography without sight.
- Brandi explains her working method:
  - Musical counts and footfalls let her hear timing and weight.
  - Measured steps and tactile floor plans let her map the stage.
  - Raised formation diagrams let her study dancers’ positions and pathways.
  - Her visual memory from before age seven preserves an understanding of line, shape, color, and classical ballet positions.
  - Dancers describe purely visual spacing while Brandi makes the artistic decisions.
  - Any hands-on correction is made only with the dancer’s permission.
- Brandi says she wants to choreograph professional ballet, contemporary dance, stage productions, and filmed performance—not only work labeled as disability choreography.
- Jamal’s father asks where she expects to live and work after UNLV.
- Brandi answers honestly:

> “I don’t know where I’ll end up. I know what I want to do. I don’t know yet which city, company, or stage will let me do it.”

- She is open to following the right choreographic opportunity rather than promising Las Vegas, Reno, Los Angeles, or any other destination.
- Jamal does not answer the geography question for her or suggest that her career will simply follow his plans.
- Jamal’s mother is impressed that Brandi can be specific about the work while remaining honest about the uncertainty.

> “You are not speaking about a wish. You have already begun learning how to do the work.”

- Jamal’s father tells Brandi that discipline is visible even when choreography is not.
- Their interest allows Brandi to be known for her future, not only for blindness, Mohammed, or her relationship with their son.

### Beat 9: Jamal Tells the Complete Story

- After dinner, the family moves to the living room.
- Jamal’s father asks why Jamal sounded frightened when arranging the visit.
- Jamal says:

> “Because I have not told you how Brandi and I began.”

- He gives the complete account without changing the sequence.
- He names Mohammed as his closest friend and brother through the mosque.
- He admits that he knew Brandi and Mohammed were still together.
- He says Mohammed discovered the relationship and that the friendship ended.
- He explains that he and Brandi went to Imam Kareem afterward.
- Jamal’s mother becomes very still.
- His father asks:

> “Did Mohammed trust you?”

- Jamal answers, *“Yes.”*

> “Did you know what you were breaking?”

- Jamal answers, *“Yes.”*

- There is no defensive speech after either answer.

### Beat 10: Brandi Speaks for Herself

- Jamal’s mother asks Brandi why she remained with Mohammed if he treated her badly.
- Jamal does not answer for her.
- Brandi explains that bad relationships rarely become unbearable in one clear moment. She became used to being diminished and delayed leaving long after she knew she should.
- She says Jamal did not rescue her.

> “I chose him before I ended what I should have ended. That was my choice, and it hurt people.”

- Brandi says Mohammed’s behavior explains her unhappiness but does not excuse the betrayal.
- She also makes clear that her relationship with Mohammed is over and will remain over.
- Jamal’s mother hears the difference between accountability and self-erasure.

### Beat 11: His Parents’ Response

- Jamal’s father is deeply disappointed. His concern is not only romance; it is the betrayal of a friend and mosque brother.
- He tells Jamal:

> “A man who tells the truth after he is caught has begun accountability. He has not completed it.”

- Jamal accepts the statement.
- His mother asks whether Mohammed is safe and supported. Jamal says Imam Kareem and the mosque community know what happened, but Mohammed does not want contact with him.
- Jamal’s father says Jamal must respect that boundary.
- Neither parent demands that Jamal end the relationship as punishment.
- Neither treats Brandi as a corrupting outsider.
- They hold Jamal responsible for his own conduct and Brandi responsible for hers.

### Beat 12: The Interfaith Questions

- Jamal’s mother asks Brandi whether she intends to become Muslim.
- Brandi answers respectfully and clearly:

> “No. I am Catholic.”

- She explains that covering her hair, wearing a long-sleeved blouse, an ankle-length skirt, and white tights, and removing her shoes at the doorway are gestures of respect—not signs that she is converting.
- Jamal confirms that he does not expect conversion.
- Brandi explains that she accompanied Jamal to Imam Kareem because the betrayal affected his Muslim community, not because she was abandoning her faith.
- Jamal’s father asks how they expect a Catholic-Muslim relationship to work.
- Brandi says they are learning through specific choices rather than pretending every future question has already been solved.
- Jamal mentions the breakfast disagreement about the mosque fundraiser and how they resolved it.
- His mother appreciates that Brandi can decline a mosque event without rejecting Jamal’s faith.
- His father says mutual respect will have to survive more difficult questions than one calendar conflict.
- Brandi agrees.

### Beat 13: A Welcomed Place at the Table

- Jamal’s parents do not offer an immediate blessing for how the relationship began, but they make their welcome of Brandi unmistakable.
- His father tells Brandi:

> “You are not merely being tolerated as Jamal’s guest. You are welcome in our home. What comes after tonight depends on what both of you build.”

- Brandi answers:

> “That is more than I expected. Thank you.”

- Jamal’s mother says:

> “You respected our household before you knew whether we would accept you. We are very impressed with you, Brandi Jessica. We welcome you as yourself.”

- Jamal’s father adds that the welcome includes Burden. Their house will remain dog-free in ordinary circumstances, but Brandi’s guide dog will always be admitted with her.
- She asks Brandi whether she would like tea and what she takes in it, treating her preference as something worth remembering for future visits.
- Brandi says yes.
- The welcome does not erase accountability. It assures Brandi that she will not have to earn basic hospitality by changing her faith or identity.

### Beat 14: The Körpe’s Origin

- Later, while Jamal helps his father in the kitchen, Jamal’s mother takes a folded textile from a cedar chest.
- It is a hand-quilted **körpe** made within her Uyghur family.
- The raised stitching carries geometric diamonds, vines, and eight-pointed stars.
- Jamal’s maternal grandmother worked on the quilt before she was killed by the government in China. Jamal’s mother preserved it through relocation and years in Reno.
- She does not give it directly to Brandi that night. The relationship is too new, and the object belongs to a private mother-son decision first.
- She shows it to Jamal after Brandi has gone to the guest room.
- Jamal’s mother tells him:

> “If you are asking her to know where you come from, give her something true.”

- She places the folded körpe in his hands.
- Jamal understands that it is not approval of the betrayal. It is permission to build honestly from what survived it.

### Beat 15: Separate Rooms, Shared Honesty

- Jamal sleeps in his childhood bedroom. Brandi sleeps in the prepared guest room with Burden nearby.
- Before separating for the night, Jamal asks Brandi whether the evening was too much.
- Brandi says it was difficult but fair.
- She asks whether his parents hate her.
- Jamal answers:

> “They don’t know you yet. That’s different.”

- Brandi recognizes the phrase she used after his meeting with Kiley Rose and Julia.
- They say goodnight without violating his parents’ household boundary.

### Beat 16: Messages Across Three Destinations

- In the guest room, Brandi sends the promised arrival messages.
- Kiley Rose and Julia receive hers while traveling to Panama City.
- Pandora receives hers in Los Angeles.
- Brandi writes:

> “We made it to Reno. They know the truth. It was hard, but I’m okay.”

- Kiley replies:

> “Hard and honest is still honest. We love you.”

- Pandora replies:

> “Call me tomorrow. Los Angeles is awake.”

- Brandi sets her phone beside the bed, reaches down to touch Burden’s head, and listens to the unfamiliar Reno house settling around her.

### Beat 17: The Next Morning with His Parents

- The visit continues into the next day with an ordinary family breakfast and more conversation.
- Brandi wears the same respectful style for the day’s visits: scarf covering her hair, long-sleeved blouse, ankle-length skirt, and white tights.
- Jamal tells his parents that he wants to show Brandi his childhood school and introduce her to his childhood imam before they return to Las Vegas.
- Jamal’s parents approve. His mother says Brandi should know the places that formed Jamal, not only the people evaluating his present choices.
- When Brandi and Jamal prepare to leave, Jamal’s parents repeat that Brandi is welcome and ask her to return for another visit.
- Jamal’s mother remembers how Brandi takes her tea, signaling that she expects there to be a next time.
- Jamal’s father says the water bowl and washable mat will remain stored for Burden’s next visit.
- Jamal places the folded körpe privately in the vehicle without telling Brandi what it is.

### Beat 18: Jamal Shows Brandi His Childhood School

- Jamal first takes Brandi to one of the **Catholic schools** he attended as a child in Reno—the school where he spent the longest part of his education.
- The visit is arranged so they can walk the grounds and permitted public areas without disrupting students or violating school security.
- Burden works in harness, guiding Brandi along the unfamiliar paths and through the entrance.
- Jamal describes the school as it exists now and as he remembers it:
  - The front steps where he waited before the morning bell.
  - The hallway leading toward his childhood classrooms.
  - The cafeteria where he switched among English, Arabic, and Uyghur without thinking.
  - The library area where he completed separate supervised study while Catholic religious classes were taking place.
  - The gym where assemblies and school performances were held.
- Brandi asks whether attending Catholic schools meant that Jamal received Catholic religious instruction.
- Jamal tells her no. His parents arranged for him not to participate in the religious classes. He completed separate study during those periods, while his Muslim education came from his family and childhood imam.
- Brandi recognizes the unusual mirror between them: she attended Catholic school as a Catholic student; Jamal moved through Catholic-school hallways while remaining Muslim.

> **Jamal:** “The school taught me my academic subjects. My family and the mosque taught me my faith.”

- Brandi asks what kind of student he was.
- Jamal admits he talked too much, translated for other children when teachers could not understand them, and sometimes used intelligence to avoid admitting fear.
- Brandi asks him to describe the gym’s dimensions. She counts the echo and listens to the acoustic size of the room, automatically thinking like a choreographer.
- Jamal realizes that showing Brandi his past also lets her imagine what she might create in spaces like this one.
- He tells her:

> “Before Reno was where my parents lived, it was the whole size of my world.”

- Brandi answers:

> “I still don’t know the size of mine.”

- The line reinforces that she knows her career but not where she will end up.

### Beat 19: The Reno Mosque and Jamal’s Childhood Imam

- Jamal next takes Brandi to the Reno mosque where he worshipped as a child.
- His childhood imam is distinct from Imam Kareem, who counsels Jamal and Mohammed in Las Vegas.
- Brandi keeps her hair covered with her scarf and wears her long-sleeved blouse, ankle-length skirt, and white tights.
- At the mosque entrance, Jamal identifies the bench and shoe cubbyholes.
- Brandi asks Burden to wait, removes her shoes herself, and places them together in an empty cubbyhole. She remains in her white tights.
- The childhood imam already knows that Brandi is blind and that Burden is a guide dog. He welcomes the working dog despite the mosque’s usual concern about animals in prayer spaces and arranges a clean side area where Burden can remain beside Brandi without being distracted.
- Jamal introduces Brandi by name and identifies her as his girlfriend.
- The imam greets Brandi directly rather than speaking through Jamal.
- He remembers Jamal as a multilingual child who asked questions after everyone else wanted to go home.
- Brandi understands that this imam and mosque provided the Muslim religious education Jamal did not receive at his Catholic schools.
- Jamal gives a concise summary of what happened with Mohammed and confirms that he and Brandi already spoke with Imam Kareem in Las Vegas.
- The childhood imam does not replace Imam Kareem’s role or conduct a second judgment. He tells Jamal:

> “The boy I taught is part of the man standing here. He does not excuse the man, but he reminds me that you can still choose what kind of man comes next.”

- The imam asks Brandi whether she has come because she is considering conversion.
- Brandi answers:

> “No. I am Catholic. I came because this is part of Jamal’s life, and I want to know where he learned his faith.”

- The imam welcomes the answer. He tells her that respectful presence is not the same as surrendering belief.
- Brandi asks what Jamal was like during lessons.
- The imam shares one affectionate childhood story: Jamal once corrected a translation, then stayed after class to prove why he was right.
- Jamal protests that the story has become exaggerated over time.
- Brandi laughs. The visit gives her a version of Jamal that existed before Mohammed, UNLV, and the affair.

### Beat 20: Leaving Reno with More Than One Story

- Outside the mosque, Brandi retrieves her shoes from the cubbyhole and puts them on herself before Burden guides her back to the vehicle.
- Jamal asks what she thinks of the school and mosque.
- Brandi says:

> “Your parents showed me your home. Today you showed me the boy who lived in it.”

- Jamal says he wanted her to know he had a life before the worst thing she knows about him.
- Brandi answers that knowing the rest of him does not erase Mohammed; it makes Jamal responsible for carrying all of himself honestly.
- They begin the journey back toward Las Vegas.
- Brandi notices the faint cedar scent from the hidden bundle but does not press Jamal when he says he has something for her later.
- The next scene begins after they return to Brandi’s penthouse, where Jamal will place the körpe in her hands.
