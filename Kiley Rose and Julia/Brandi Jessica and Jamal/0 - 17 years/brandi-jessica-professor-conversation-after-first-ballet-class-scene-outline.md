# Brandi Jessica and Her Professor After the First Ballet Class — Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Brandi Jessica Key (17), her first-year ballet professor (name TBD)
- **Onstage animal:** Burden, Brandi Jessica’s Labernese guide dog
- **Offstage / referenced:** Kassie Myller, the hostile Dance students, Kiley Rose, Julia, Pandora Paige
- **Location:** The middle of the UNLV dance studio, seated on the floor
- **When:** Immediately after Brandi Jessica’s first ballet technique class, ~2062
- **Status:** Scene outline
- **Tone:** Private, honest, angry without becoming uncontrolled, medically personal, professionally grounded

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

After the other students leave the first ballet class, Brandi Jessica and her professor sit together on the floor in the middle of the dance studio. Burden lies beside them.

They discuss the rude and ridiculous reaction of the students who were horrified to see Brandi begin warming up at the barre and who do not want a blind dancer in the program.

The professor confirms that they served on the admissions committee that selected Brandi and remains ashamed of the students’ contempt. Brandi recognizes the professor’s voice from her university audition. Her family traveled with her to Las Vegas but, at Brandi’s request, let her and Burden enter the university and attend the audition without them.

Brandi then chooses to tell the professor about her bilateral retinoblastoma: she was sighted until age seven, lost both eyes through life-saving surgery, retained visual memory, received ocular prosthetics, and continued dancing afterward.

The disclosure is private. Brandi is not offering her medical history as proof that she deserves admission. She tells the professor so the person teaching her understands the complete dancer in front of them.

The professor tells Brandi that what they personally admire is her courage to undertake the Dance program. The professor makes clear that talent earned Brandi’s place; courage is what the professor admires about how she claims it. Brandi becomes deeply emotional when she hears this kindness after a class defined by contempt.

---

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: The Studio Empties

- The first ballet class ends.
- Students collect bags, water bottles, and warm-up clothes.
- The hostile group leaves together, still whispering angrily about Brandi’s presence.
- Kassie pauses near Brandi and asks whether Brandi wants her to stay.
- Brandi says:

> “No. I need to speak to the professor. I’ll meet you at the dorm.”

- Kassie accepts the answer and leaves without treating Brandi as someone who cannot remain alone with faculty.
- The door closes behind the final student.
- The studio becomes quiet except for ventilation, distant hallway sounds, and Burden shifting on his mat.

### Beat 2: Sitting in the Middle of the Floor

- The professor asks whether Brandi would prefer the office, a bench, or the studio.
- Brandi chooses the studio floor.

> “Here. This is where it happened.”

- Brandi crosses the mapped studio to the center without being led.
- She sits on the Marley floor with her legs folded comfortably.
- The professor sits on the floor facing her rather than standing above her.
- Burden moves from his resting mat to Brandi’s side and lies down beside them, his shoulder touching Brandi’s knee.
- His harness remains on with the handle folded down. He is calm, quiet, and available when Brandi is ready to leave.

### Beat 3: The Professor’s Shame

- The professor speaks first.

> “I am ashamed of what they said and how they looked at you.”

- Brandi answers:

> “Do not be ashamed that you admitted me.”

- The professor responds immediately:

> “I’m not. I was one of the committee members who chose you. I am ashamed that students selected for this program could watch a qualified dancer touch a barre and react as if they had witnessed something horrifying.”

- The professor explains that Brandi’s admission was based on:
  - Technical foundation.
  - Musical intelligence.
  - Discipline.
  - Audition work.
  - Ability to adapt without reducing artistic expectations.
  - Creative potential.
- Brandi tilts her head toward the professor’s voice.

> “I remember your voice. You were there when I auditioned.”

- The professor confirms that they sat on the audition panel and spoke several of the instructions Brandi followed that day.
- Brandi remembers the audition trip clearly:
  - Kiley Rose, Julia, and Pandora Paige traveled with her to Las Vegas.
  - They supported the trip but did not accompany her into the university audition.
  - At Brandi’s request, the family stopped at the agreed campus drop-off point and let her continue alone except for Burden.
  - Burden guided Brandi from the drop-off point into the university and toward the audition building.
  - Brandi checked in, found the audition studio, and entered without a family member speaking for her.
  - Her family waited away from the audition and trusted her to complete it independently.
- Brandi tells the professor that the audition room contained many unfamiliar voices, but the professor’s voice remained in her memory because it gave clear counts without becoming patronizing.

> “You said, ‘Whenever you’re ready, Brandi Jessica.’ You did not ask my mother whether I was ready.”

- The professor remembers seeing Brandi enter with Burden and realizing before the audition began that she expected to be addressed directly.
- The professor says the committee did not admit Brandi because she walked in alone; independence was not an inspirational bonus. They admitted her because of what she did once the audition began.
- Brandi says she knows why she belongs. What she needs to know is whether the program will continue standing behind its own decision after students complain.
- The professor promises that it will.

### Beat 4: “Rude Is Too Small a Word”

- Brandi calls the students’ reaction rude, then corrects herself.

> “Rude is too small a word. It was ridiculous.”

- She lists the absurdity:
  - They saw her locate a barre she had already mapped.
  - They watched her begin a warm-up she had performed for years.
  - They decided that blindness made pliés dangerous.
  - They treated verbal instruction as an unfair advantage even though the entire class could hear it.
  - They assumed Kassie must be her caretaker.
  - They judged Burden before watching him remain quieter than most of the students.
- Brandi says:

> “They were horrified by the idea of me before they saw a single thing I could do.”

- The professor agrees. The students reacted to blindness, not to Brandi’s technique.

### Beat 5: Brandi Chooses to Explain Retinoblastoma

- A silence settles between them.
- Brandi places one hand on Burden’s back and traces the edge of his harness.
- She tells the professor that there is something personal she wants them to understand, but not repeat to the class.
- The professor agrees to keep the disclosure private.
- Brandi begins:

> “I was not born blind.”

- She explains:
  - She was sighted through early childhood.
  - She began ballet and gymnastics while she could still see.
  - On December 23, 2051, two days before her seventh birthday, she was diagnosed with aggressive bilateral retinoblastoma.
  - Tumors filled both eyes and threatened to spread along the optic nerves toward her brain.
  - Doctors performed bilateral enucleation to save her life.
  - She lost both eyes and became totally blind.
  - She later received custom ocular prosthetics, but they do not restore sight.
- Brandi does not dramatize the medical facts. She has lived with them for a decade.

### Beat 6: The Visual Memory Behind Her Ballet

- Brandi explains why visual language still means something to her.
- She remembers:
  - The five ballet positions.
  - The geometry of a studio.
  - A dancer’s line at the barre.
  - Color, light, and shadow.
  - Kiley Rose’s face.
  - Pandora Paige’s face.
- Her blindness is not an abstract blank. It contains remembered images.
- Brandi tells the professor:

> “When you say fifth position, I do not hear an empty phrase. I remember what fifth looks like. Then I place it inside my body.”

- The professor understands that Brandi’s technical process combines visual memory with current tactile, auditory, and proprioceptive information.

### Beat 7: She Continued Dancing

- Brandi explains that Kiley Rose and Julia never considered removing her from ballet or gymnastics after the surgery.
- They treated blindness as an adjustment rather than an ending.
- Brandi relearned:
  - Barre orientation through touch.
  - Studio direction through sound.
  - Balance through internal alignment.
  - Traveling pathways through counted steps.
  - Performance spacing through mapped stages and rehearsal.
- She tells the professor:

> “I did not start ballet after losing my sight as a stunt. I continued being the dancer I already was.”

- The professor admits that the admissions committee understood Brandi’s training history but had not heard the story in Brandi’s own words.

### Beat 8: Pandora Was Screened and Found Healthy

- Brandi also tells the professor about Pandora Paige.
- Because the twins are identical, Brandi’s diagnosis triggered immediate screening and genetic investigation for Pandora.
- Pandora’s examination found no tumors, lesions, or retinal disease. She never had retinoblastoma and required no treatment.
- Later testing established that Brandi’s pathogenic *RB1* variant was not present in Pandora.
- Brandi says:

> “Pandora was frightened and screened because of me. But she was healthy. She never had retinoblastoma.”

- Brandi explains that Pandora was still affected emotionally by watching her twin face cancer, surgery, and blindness. Family trauma is not the same as sharing the diagnosis.
- The professor becomes emotional but does not turn Brandi into a tragedy or inspirational symbol.
- The professor says only:

> “That is a great deal for one seven-year-old to survive.”

- Brandi answers:

> “I survived it. I am not asking this program to keep surviving it for me.”

### Beat 9: What Brandi Does and Does Not Want

- The professor asks what Brandi wants to happen next.
- Brandi’s requests are specific:
  - Document the hostile comments.
  - Reject any complaint based solely on blindness.
  - Enforce professional conduct.
  - Protect Burden from interference.
  - Keep technical standards unchanged.
  - Continue giving direct corrections, including criticism.
  - Do not tell the class her retinoblastoma history.
  - Do not turn her into an inspirational lecture.
- Brandi says:

> “They do not need my cancer story before they can treat me like a classmate.”

- The professor agrees.
- The professor will defend Brandi’s admission using the program’s standards, not Brandi’s private medical history.

### Beat 10: The Professor’s Responsibility

- The professor acknowledges that serving on the admissions committee created a responsibility beyond voting yes.

> “Choosing you was the beginning of my responsibility, not the end of it.”

- The professor promises to:
  - Provide access without lowering expectations.
  - Correct faculty language when instruction relies only on sight.
  - Stop harassment when it occurs.
  - Ensure Brandi receives the same rehearsal and performance opportunities she earns.
  - Ask rather than assume what Brandi needs.
- Brandi says she does not need perfection from the professor.

> “You said ‘watch the demonstration’ today.”

- The professor winces.
- Brandi continues:

> “Then you corrected yourself and described it. That is what I need. Correct it and keep teaching.”

### Beat 11: The Kind Sentence

- The professor pauses before they stand.
- The professor tells Brandi there is something personal—not evaluative—that they want her to know.

> “What I admire about you is your courage to undertake this Dance program. You came to the audition at sixteen without your family beside you, entered a demanding course at seventeen, and put your hand on the barre even while the room was deciding you did not belong.”

- The professor immediately distinguishes courage from qualification:

> “Your talent and work earned your place. Courage is what I admire about the way you are claiming it.”

- The kindness reaches Brandi more deeply than the professor expects.
- Her breathing catches. Tears gather at the lower edges of her prosthetic eyes and run down her cheeks. Her shoulders begin to shake despite her attempt to remain composed.
- Burden lifts his head and presses his shoulder more firmly against Brandi’s leg.
- The professor does not touch Brandi without permission. They remain seated beside her and give her time.
- Brandi wipes her face and says through tears:

> “After the way they looked at me today, I did not expect the kindest thing anyone said in this studio to be about courage.”

- She explains that Kiley Rose, Julia, and Pandora have always believed in her. Hearing the same belief from someone inside the university program makes the admission feel real in a new way.
- The professor says Brandi never has to perform courage for the class. She is allowed to be frightened, angry, exhausted, or uncertain and still belong.
- Brandi becomes emotional again at the word *belong*.
- She asks for another moment on the floor before returning to work.
- The professor waits beside her while Burden remains pressed against her knee.

### Beat 12: Returning to the Barre

- Before leaving, Brandi asks the professor to walk with her to the barre—not to guide her, but to continue the conversation where the hostility began.
- Brandi stands independently. Burden rises beside her.
- They walk to the barre, with Brandi locating it through memory and touch.
- The professor asks whether Brandi wants to review the next class’s first combination.
- Brandi says yes.
- The professor gives the counts and directional language.
- Brandi marks the phrase slowly with one hand on the barre.
- Burden lies nearby again.
- The private conversation ends in work rather than consolation.

### Beat 13: Leaving the Studio

- Brandi returns to Burden, takes the harness handle, and gives the command to move toward the door.
- At the threshold, the professor says:

> “I am glad the committee chose you.”

- Brandi answers:

> “Then make them live with the fact that you were right.”

- Burden guides her into the hallway.
- The professor remains alone in the studio, looking at the barre where the class exposed its prejudice and where Brandi has already begun preparing for the next lesson.
