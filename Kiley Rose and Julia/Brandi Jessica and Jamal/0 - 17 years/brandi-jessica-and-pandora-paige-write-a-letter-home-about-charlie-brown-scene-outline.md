# Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige Write a Letter to Kiley Rose and Julia About Charlie Brown — Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Brandi Jessica Key (8), Pandora Paige Key (8)
- **Onstage animal:** None (Burden does not arrive until age 14)
- **Locations:** Birch Cabin — French Woods (Hancock, New York)
- **When:** Session 3, Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige's first summer at French Woods (July 2053). The evening of the day the cast list is posted.
- **Status:** New scene outline
- **Tone:** Warm, excited, twin collaboration — the news traveling home

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene captures the twins writing a letter together to Kiley Rose and Julia about Brandi being cast as Charlie Brown. It is the first letter they've written home in Session 3, and it carries news that neither of them expected when the summer began.

It establishes:
- That the twins write letters home together — Pandora writes, Brandi dictates. This is how they've always done it, and it is an act of collaboration, not dependence. Brandi decides what to say. Pandora puts it on the page.
- That the letter carries two pieces of news: Brandi got Charlie Brown, and Pandora chose not to be in the play. Both are important. Both are the girls' own choices.
- That writing the letter together is one of the quiet rituals of being twins — the same way they sit on the beam, the same way they do Partner Yoga, the same way they share a bunk.
- That Brandi wants her mothers to know before anyone else. Not the camp. Not the Hayashis. Her mothers.

## BEATS

### Beat 1: Getting Ready to Write

- It is after dinner. The cabin is settling into the evening routine — campers brushing teeth, counselors tidying, the sounds of Birch Cabin winding down.
- Brandi and Pandora are on Brandi's lower bunk. Pandora has her stationery kit — the one Julia packed for her before camp, with the colored paper and the set of pens. Brandi has her Braille slate and stylus, but for letters home, they do it together. Brandi talks. Pandora writes. It has always been this way.
- Pandora pulls out a piece of blue paper — blue because that's the one Brandi likes to feel. The texture is smoother than the white.
- Pandora: "Ready?"
- Brandi: "Ready."
- Pandora clicks her pen. She writes the date at the top: July 2053. Then she writes: Dear Mommy and Julia.

### Beat 2: The Letter

- Pandora writes in her slightly messy eight-year-old handwriting, fast and enthusiastic, the way she does everything:
  - *Dear Mommy and Julia,*
  - *We have news!! Brandi got the lead in the musical!! It's called You're a Good Man Charlie Brown and she is CHARLIE BROWN. The actual Charlie Brown. The director chose her because when she read the lines everyone in the room went quiet and the director said she was the truest Charlie Brown she could have cast. BRANDI. The truest Charlie Brown. Out of all the kids at camp.*
- Pandora looks up. "Should I tell them about the thank you?"
- Brandi: "Yes. Tell them I said thank you to the director."
- Pandora writes:
  - *And Brandi went back to the director after the cast list went up and said thank you for choosing her. And the director said she didn't choose Brandi out of belief, she chose her out of what she saw. And Brandi said "do you know that I'm blind" and the director said yes I know and Brandi said "do you know what it means" and told her everything — the markers and the script and the cues — and the director said she knows all of it and she still chose her. And Brandi said "I've known what I signed up for since I was seven" and I almost cried right there in the dining hall.*
- Pandora pauses. She rereads what she just wrote. Her hand is shaking slightly.
- Pandora: "I didn't almost cry. I did cry. A little."
- Brandi: "Tell them the truth."
- Pandora writes:
  - *Okay I cried a little. But I'm allowed to cry about my sister getting the lead in a musical. That's a valid reason to cry.*
- Brandi laughs. "Tell them about you."
- Pandora writes:
  - *I'm not in the play. I don't want to be. Theater isn't my thing. But I'm going to be in the front row on opening night and I'm going to whoop so loud that Brandi hears me. She said I'm the loud twin. I said I'm the early twin AND the loud twin. We're both right.*
- Pandora: "Should I tell them about the minors?"
- Brandi: "Yes. Tell them we're taking the same minors."
- Pandora writes:
  - *Also we're taking the same minors now!! Swimming Lessons, Painting and Drawing, Water Recreation, Horse Care, Community Service, and Gymnastics. I wanted to do the same ones as Brandi. And I'm trying spray art. Brandi says I'm going to get paint everywhere. I say that's the point.*
- Pandora: "What else?"
- Brandi: "Tell them about Songwriting. Tell them I'm writing a song."
- Pandora writes:
  - *Brandi is writing a song in Songwriting class. About us. About the family. About everything. She won't let me hear the whole thing yet but she sang me part of it and it was the most beautiful thing I've ever heard and I'm not just saying that because I'm her twin. I'm saying it because it's true.*
- A beat. Pandora's pen hovers.
- Pandora: "Do you want to add anything else?"
- Brandi thinks. A long time. The cabin is quiet around them. Pandora waits. She knows when Brandi is thinking and when Brandi is done thinking. This is not done thinking.
- Brandi: "Tell them thank you. Not like the thank you I said to the director. A different one."
- Pandora: "Okay. What kind?"
- Brandi: "Tell them thank you for fighting to get me back."
- Pandora's pen stops. She looks at Brandi. She does not say anything. She just waits.
- Brandi: "When Andrew took me. When I was seven. You fought to get me back. I don't remember all of it but I remember the sounds. I remember Mommy's voice on the phone and it sounded like she was made of steel. I remember Julia's hands on me when I came home and they wouldn't let go. Tell them thank you for not stopping until they found me. Tell them thank you for fighting for me when I couldn't fight for myself."
- Pandora writes. Her handwriting is slower now. Each word careful.
- Brandi: "And tell them thank you for being there when I was in the hospital. For the surgery when they took my eyes. For the chemo. For the radiotherapy. Tell them I know what it cost them. I know they were scared. I know they didn't sleep. I know they held the bowl when I was sick and held my hand when I was scared and held each other when I was asleep and they thought I couldn't hear them but I could. I could always hear them. Tell them I know what they gave up to be in that hospital room with me every single day. And tell them it mattered. Tell them that the reason I can stand on a stage and say 'do you know that I'm blind' and not be afraid is because they were in that hospital room and they never once made me feel like being blind was the end of everything. They made me feel like it was the beginning of something different. Tell them that."
- Pandora is not writing anymore. She is staring at the paper. Her eyes are full.
- Pandora: "Brandi..."
- Brandi: "I'm not done. Tell them thank you for the yoga. For getting on the mat with me when I was so scared of the radiation mask that I couldn't stop shaking. For teaching me how to breathe when the only thing I wanted to do was scream. For all four of us on the mat together. Tell them that's when I learned that my family will always show up. Not because they have to. Because they choose to. Every time. Without any single doubt."
- Pandora picks up her pen again. She writes. She writes it all. Every word.
- Then Brandi says: "And tell them thank you for letting us change our classes. Thank you for letting me sign up for the play without asking if I could do it first. Thank you for never saying no. Only how."
- A beat. Brandi reaches out and finds Pandora's hand.
- Brandi: "And tell Pandora thank you too."
- Pandora: "I'm right here. You don't have to write me a letter."
- Brandi: "I know. But I want to say it in the letter too. So it's on the paper. So it's real."
- Pandora is quiet. She holds the pen but doesn't write.
- Brandi: "Tell Pandora thank you for sleeping in the chair next to my bed. For holding the bucket when I was sick. For reading to me when I couldn't keep my eyes open. For learning yoga with me because I was scared and she didn't want me to be scared alone. For being the one who could see and staying anyway. For never once making me feel like I was a burden. For always just doing it before I even have to ask. For being my front row. For being my compass. For being my sister."
- Pandora's pen is on the paper but she is not writing. Her shoulders are shaking. She is crying — not the laughing kind, not the a-little-bit kind. The real kind. The kind that comes from being thanked for things you didn't know someone noticed.
- Pandora: "I can't write that."
- Brandi: "Why not?"
- Pandora: "Because my hands are shaking."
- Brandi: "Then I'll wait."
- They sit on the bunk. Pandora takes a breath. Then another. She wipes her eyes with the back of her hand. She picks up the pen.
- She writes:
  - *Brandi also says thank you to Pandora. For sleeping in the chair next to her bed. For holding the bucket when she was sick. For reading to her when she couldn't keep her eyes open. For learning yoga with her because she was scared and she didn't want her to be scared alone. For being the one who could see and staying anyway. For never once making her feel like she was a burden. For always just doing it before she even has to ask. For being her front row. For being her compass. For being her sister.*
  - *And I say: you're welcome. For all of it. Every single time. Without any single doubt.*
  - *And from Brandi to Mommy and Julia: Thank you for fighting to get me back when Andrew took me. Thank you for being in that hospital room every single day for the surgery and the chemo and the radiotherapy. I know what it cost you. I know you were scared and you didn't sleep and you held it together when I was awake and I heard you when I was asleep. I could always hear you. The reason I can stand on a stage and say "do you know that I'm blind" and not be afraid is because you were in that room and you never once made me feel like being blind was the end of everything. You made it the beginning of something different. Thank you for the yoga. For getting on the mat with me when I was so scared of the radiation mask I couldn't stop shaking. For teaching me to breathe. For all four of us on the mat together. That's when I learned that my family will always show up. Not because they have to. Because they choose to. Every time. Without any single doubt.*
  - *And thank you for letting us change our classes. Thank you for letting me sign up for the play without asking if I could do it first. Thank you for never saying no. Only how.*
  - *(She's talking about you guys. You know that. "There are no no's, only hows." She means you taught her that. She means that's why she could stand in front of a director and say "do you know that I'm blind" and not be afraid of the answer. And she means that's why she could survive everything she survived. Because you taught her that the answer to fear is never no. The answer is how. And then you showed her how. Every single time.)*
- Pandora's handwriting is getting shakier. She stops. She wipes her eyes with the back of her hand.
- Pandora: "I'm not crying again."
- Brandi: "You're crying again."
- Pandora: "It's the pen. It's slipping."
- Brandi reaches over and finds Pandora's face. She feels the wet on her sister's cheek.
- Brandi: "The pen isn't on your face, Pandora."
- Pandora laughs. A wet, snotty, eight-year-old laugh. "Shut up."
- Brandi laughs too. They sit on the bunk and laugh while Pandora has pen smudges on her face and Brandi has tears on her fingers from touching Pandora's cheek.

### Beat 3: Signing the Letter

- Pandora writes the closing:
  - *We love you. We miss you. See you on Visiting Day.*
  - *Love,*
  - *Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige*
- Then she passes the pen to Brandi. Brandi takes it in her hand. She cannot see where to sign, so she holds her finger on the spot Pandora points to, and she writes her name below Pandora's. The B is wobbly. The rest is small and careful. It is not neat. It is hers.
- Pandora: "Your B looks like a 3."
- Brandi: "Your S looks like a snake."
- Pandora: "That's because I'm the snake twin."
- Brandi: "You're not the snake twin. You're the loud twin."
- Pandora: "I can be both."
- They fold the letter together. Pandora licks the envelope — it tastes like glue, which she says out loud, and Brandi says "you didn't have to tell me that" — and she writes the address on the front.
- Pandora: "Should I draw something on the envelope?"
- Brandi: "Draw a round head."
- Pandora draws a circle with a squiggly line of hair on top. Charlie Brown's head. It looks more like a potato. She writes underneath it: BRANDI = CB.
- Brandi: "What does it look like?"
- Pandora: "It looks exactly like you."
- Brandi: "Does it look like a potato?"
- Pandora: "...A little bit like a potato. A beautiful potato."
- They put the letter on the stack for outgoing mail. It will go out in the morning camp mail run. In a few days, Kiley Rose and Julia will open a blue envelope with a potato drawing on the front, and they will read about their daughter who is Charlie Brown.

### Beat 4: After the Letter

- The cabin is getting quiet. Lights-out is soon. The other campers are settling into their bunks.
- Pandora and Brandi are still sitting on Brandi's bunk. The letter is gone — in the mail pile, on its way home.
- Brandi: "Do you think they'll cry?"
- Pandora: "Mommy will cry. Julia will pretend not to cry and then cry when she's alone."
- Brandi: "How do you know?"
- Pandora: "Because that's what they always do. Mommy cries in front of us. Julia cries later. They're both crying about the same thing. They just have different schedules."
- Brandi: "What are they crying about?"
- Pandora: "About you. About the fact that you stood in front of a director and said 'do you know that I'm blind' and the director said yes and you still got the lead. About the fact that their daughter is Charlie Brown. About the fact that you thanked them for never saying no, only how."
- Brandi is quiet.
- Brandi: "I wasn't trying to make them cry."
- Pandora: "I know. That's why they'll cry. Because you weren't trying. You were just telling the truth."
- Maya, the counselor, passes by and touches Brandi's shoulder lightly. "Lights out in five, girls."
- Pandora slides off Brandi's bunk and climbs to her own. She reaches down and Brandi reaches up. Their fingertips touch — the same way they touch every night, the same way they've touched since they were in the same crib.
- Pandora: "Goodnight, Charlie Brown."
- Brandi: "Goodnight, front row."
- Pandora: "I'm going to whoop so loud."
- Brandi: "I know. I'll hear you. I always hear you."
- Pandora: "I know. That's why I do it."
- The cabin goes dark. The letter is on its way. Brandi Jessica Key is Charlie Brown.

## RELATION TO CONTINUITY

- This scene takes place during **Session 3**, the evening of the day the cast list is posted (July 2053).
- The letter-writing ritual — Pandora writes, Brandi dictates — is established here for the first time. This is how the twins send letters home: together, on the bunk, one pen, one voice each.
- The blue paper is the kind Brandi likes to feel — a sensory detail consistent with her relationship to touch and texture.
- The letter references: Brandi being cast as Charlie Brown, the director choosing her because the room went quiet, Brandi's thank-you to the director, the "do you know that I'm blind" conversation, Pandora not being in the play, the twins taking the same minors, Brandi's songwriting, the family philosophy "there are no no's, only hows," and — most significantly — Brandi thanking Kiley Rose and Julia for fighting to get her back from Andrew, for being in the hospital every day, for the yoga during the radiation mask, and for never making her feel like blindness was the end of everything.
- This is the first time Brandi has thanked Kiley Rose and Julia for what they did during the kidnapping and the hospital. She references Andrew by name. She references hearing them when they thought she was asleep — *"I could always hear them."*
- Brandi's line — *"The reason I can stand on a stage and say 'do you know that I'm blind' and not be afraid is because they were in that hospital room and they never once made me feel like being blind was the end of everything. They made me feel like it was the beginning of something different."* — ties the entire medical backstory to the present moment at camp.
- Pandora's interpretation of how Kiley Rose and Julia will react — "Mommy cries in front of us. Julia cries later. They're both crying about the same thing. They just have different schedules" — is consistent with their established characters.
- Brandi signs her own name on the letter. It is wobbly. It is hers. She cannot see where to sign, so Pandora guides her finger to the spot. This is consistent with Brandi's approach to everything — she does it herself, with the minimum assistance needed.
- The potato drawing on the envelope — Pandora's attempt at Charlie Brown's head that looks like a potato — is a small, funny, twin moment that balances the emotional weight of the letter.
- The letter is the first time Brandi says thank you to Kiley Rose and Julia for the family philosophy. *"Thank you for never saying no. Only how."*
