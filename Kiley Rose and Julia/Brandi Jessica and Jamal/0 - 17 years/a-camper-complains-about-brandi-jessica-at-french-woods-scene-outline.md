# A Camper Complains About Brandi Jessica at French Woods — Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Brandi Jessica Key (8), Pandora Paige Key (8), Maya (counselor, mid-20s), Sloan (camper, age 9, Birch Cabin)
- **Onstage animal:** None (Burden does not arrive until age 14)
- **Locations:** Birch Cabin, French Woods (Hancock, New York) — during afternoon free time before dinner
- **When:** Day four of Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige's first summer at French Woods (July 2053). The day after Brandi walked to Ballet by herself.
- **Status:** New scene outline
- **Tone:** Tense, protective, empowering, the family philosophy tested by an outsider for the first time at camp

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene introduces the first direct challenge to Brandi Jessica's presence at French Woods — another camper openly complains that a blind girl should not be allowed to attend camp.

It establishes:
- That the "anything and everything" philosophy will not only be tested by Brandi's own limits, but by other people's prejudice.
- Pandora Paige's role as Brandi's fiercest defender when the threat comes from outside the family.
- Brandi Jessica's ability to handle hostility with calm, clear authority — a preview of how she will later confront prejudice at UNLV.
- Maya's professional handling of the situation as a counselor who was briefed in advance.
- That French Woods, like the rest of the world, contains people who see blindness as a reason to exclude rather than include — and that the Key family answer to that has always been the same.
- The camper, Sloan, is not a villain. She is a nine-year-old reacting out of ignorance and discomfort, not malice. But the impact on Brandi and Pandora is real.

## BEATS

### Beat 1: Afternoon Free Time

- It is late afternoon, the period between the last activity and dinner. Most of Birch Cabin's girls are hanging around the cabin — writing letters, playing cards, braiding each other's hair.
- Brandi Jessica is sitting on her lower bunk, running her fingers over the Braille labels Pandora helped her stick on her trunk. She is organizing her things for the next day, completely self-sufficient.
- Pandora is on the top bunk above Brandi, writing in a journal.
- The cabin is relaxed. Normal. Sloan, a nine-year-old returning camper who had the bottom bunk on the opposite side of the cabin last year, is sitting on her bed with two other girls her age.

### Beat 2: Sloan's Comment

- The conversation across the cabin is casual — girls talking about their activities, comparing majors, laughing about something that happened in Circus.
- Sloan's voice cuts through, directed at the girl beside her but loud enough for the whole cabin to hear:
  - "I don't think she should be here."
- A beat of silence. Everyone in the cabin hears it. Brandi's hands stop moving on her trunk labels.
- Sloan continues, not lowering her voice: "A blind girl at camp. She can't see where she's going. She's going to get hurt, or she's going to slow everyone down. My mom wouldn't let me do half the stuff here if I couldn't see."
- One of Sloan's friends nudges her — "Shh, she can hear you." Sloan shrugs: "I know she can hear me. I'm not being mean, I'm just saying. Camp is for kids who can actually do the activities. What's she even going to do in Circus? Or Swimming? She can't see the dock."

### Beat 3: The Cabin Goes Quiet

- Every girl in the cabin is frozen. All eyes go to Brandi and Pandora.
- Brandi does not flinch. She does not turn toward Sloan. She sits still on her bunk, her hands resting on the Braille label she was reading.
- Pandora's pen has stopped moving. She is staring across the cabin at Sloan. Her jaw is tight.
- The silence stretches. It is the kind of silence where everyone is waiting to see what happens next.

### Beat 4: Pandora Responds First

- Pandora does not yell. She does not cry. She swings down from the top bunk and stands on the cabin floor, facing Sloan directly.
- Her voice is steady, sharp, and absolutely clear:
  - "She can do every single thing you can do. She did Ballet yesterday and her alignment was better than half the class. She walked to the pavilion by herself this morning — forty-two steps, eighteen steps, seven steps, no help, no mistakes. She swam in the lake yesterday and today and she didn't need anyone to hold her hand. She's not slowing anyone down. She's not going to get hurt. She's here because she's supposed to be here."
- Sloan starts to say something — "But she can't —"
- Pandora cuts her off: "She can't see. That's the only thing she can't do. Everything else, she does. If you have a problem with that, that's your problem, not hers."
- The cabin is silent. Some of the younger girls are wide-eyed. They have never heard an eight-year-old speak like that.

### Beat 5: Brandi Speaks

- After Pandora finishes, there is another pause. Then Brandi speaks from the bunk, quietly but clearly — she does not need to stand or raise her voice.
  - "What's your name?"
- Sloan, startled to be addressed directly: "Sloan."
- Brandi: "Sloan, I'm Brandi Jessica. This is my sister Pandora. We're eight years old and this is our first summer here. I'm blind. I use a cane. My sister helps me with some things. I do a lot of things by myself. I chose Ballet, Pointe, and Swimming Lessons for my majors, same as you chose yours. If you want to ask me what I can and can't do, you can ask me. Not about me. To me."
- She lets that land. Then she adds:
  - "And I can do Circus, too. My sister is in that major. She'll describe the apparatus. I'll use my hands and my ears. I won't slow anyone down, but even if I did — camp is for everyone, not just the kids who do it the fastest."
- Sloan does not respond. She looks down at her hands.

### Beat 6: Maya Intervenes

- Maya, who has been in the cabin doorway for the last few moments, steps inside.
- She does not scold Sloan in front of everyone. She speaks calmly and firmly to the whole cabin:
  - "French Woods is a place where every camper gets the full experience. No one is here by accident, and no one is here who doesn't belong. Brandi Jessica is a camper like every other camper. If anyone has questions about how activities work, you can ask me or your other counselors. But no one in this cabin gets to tell another camper she shouldn't be here."
- She looks directly at Sloan. "Sloan, I'd like to talk to you outside for a minute."
- Sloan gets up and follows Maya out. The cabin door closes behind them.

### Beat 7: The Cabin After

- The moment the door closes, the tension breaks. Several of the younger girls look at Brandi with something new — not pity, but respect.
- One girl, a eight-year-old named Dot from the bunk next to Brandi's, says quietly: "You really walked all the way to Ballet by yourself?"
- Brandi nods. "This morning. Forty-two steps to the path, eighteen to the pavilion, six to the barre."
- Dot: "That's a lot of steps."
- Brandi smiles. "It's not that many once you know them."
- A couple of other girls drift closer. The conversation shifts — not to blindness, but to the routine, the activities, the normal things campers talk about. Brandi is included naturally.

### Beat 8: Pandora and Brandi Alone

- After a few minutes, Pandora climbs back up to sit beside Brandi on the lower bunk.
- Pandora is still furious — her fists are clenched — but she keeps her voice low so only Brandi can hear.
- Pandora: "I wanted to push her off her bunk."
- Brandi, very calmly: "I know. But you didn't. You said the right thing."
- Pandora: "She shouldn't have said that."
- Brandi: "No. She shouldn't have. But she's nine and she doesn't know any better. Now she does."
- Pandora: "Are you okay?"
- Brandi reaches over and finds Pandora's hand. She squeezes it.
  - "I'm okay. I've got you. I've got my cane. I've got forty-two steps. That's enough."
- Pandora squeezes back. "That's enough."

### Beat 9: Sloan Returns

- Sloan comes back into the cabin a while later. Whatever Maya said to her outside, she is quieter. She does not apologize to Brandi directly — not yet — but she does not say another word against her.
- Sloan goes to her bunk, lies down, and faces the wall.
- She is not reformed in one conversation. She is a child who was corrected and is processing. The prejudice does not vanish overnight.
- But the boundary has been drawn: the cabin now knows that Brandi Jessica belongs here, and that anyone who says otherwise will have to deal with Pandora, with Maya, and with the facts.

## RELATION TO CONTINUITY

- This scene takes place on **Day 4** of the twins' first summer at French Woods (July 2053).
- It is the day after Brandi walks to Ballet alone (Day 3). The "forty-two, eighteen, seven" detail is fresh and directly referenced by Pandora in her defense.
- Sloan is a nine-year-old returning camper in Birch Cabin. She is not a villain — she is a child speaking from ignorance. Her attitude may soften over the summer or it may not; she is not required to become Brandi's friend.
- This scene foreshadows the UNLV confrontation years later, where dance students react with hostility to Brandi's admission. The same pattern: prejudice stated openly → Brandi responds with calm authority → an authority figure (Maya here, the professor at UNLV) backs her.
- Maya's handling reflects the staff briefing from Kiley Rose and Julia's April phone call — she was prepared for this kind of situation and handles it professionally without making Brandi a spectacle.
- Brandi's line — "Not about me. To me." — becomes a defining principle: she insists on being addressed directly, not discussed in the third person as if she is not present.
- Pandora's defense is fierce but controlled. She does not get physical, but her words are sharp enough to draw blood. This matches her established personality: protective, articulate, unafraid.
- Dot, the eight-year-old who asks about the steps, can become a friendly presence in future cabin scenes if desired.
