# Brandi Jessica — The Tenth Day: Dressing for the Flight

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Brandi Jessica Key (seven years old), Andrew, Greenlee
- **Location:** Hotel room on the outskirts of New York City
- **When:** January 5, 2052 — the tenth day after the kidnapping (December 26, 2051)
- **Status:** New scene outline
- **Tone:** Transition. Preparation for departure. The end of one phase, the beginning of another.

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene takes place on the tenth day after the kidnapping, before the flight to the UAE. Andrew and Greenlee dress Brandi Jessica in regular clothes in the hotel room, preparing her for travel. Greenlee will accompany them on the flight — she is not leaving after New Year's Eve, but traveling with them to the UAE.

This marks the transition from the hotel room captivity to the journey to Shakib's compound. Brandi Jessica has been in this room for ten days. She has lived through the poker game, the bathroom with Greenlee, New Year's Eve, the ketamine and no-ketamine cycles. Now they are preparing to move her — to dress her differently, to take her out of this space and into a new one.

The regular clothes are not the black dress. The regular clothes are what you put on a child before you put her on a plane. They are ordinary. They are the disguise that makes a kidnapped child look like a child traveling with her parents.

---

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: The Morning of the Tenth Day
- It is the morning of January 5, 2052. Ten days since the kidnapping. Ten days in this hotel room.
- Brandi Jessica wakes up on the bed. She has been sleeping in the black dress — the short black dress with the slipped strap, the nylons, no shoes. She has been wearing it since the party, since before the poker game, since before the bathroom, since before New Year's Eve.
- The dress is wrinkled. It is dirty. It has been worn for days without being changed.
- Andrew is at the desk. Greenlee is in the chair by the window. They have been talking quietly — not about her, but about logistics, about travel, about arrangements. Brandi Jessica does not understand the words, but she understands the tone: something is happening. Something is changing.

### Beat 2: The Announcement
- Andrew looks at Brandi Jessica. He tells her they are leaving. They are going on a trip — a long trip. A plane trip.
- He does not say where. He does not say how long. He does not say when they are coming back.
- Greenlee adds something. She says it will be an adventure. She says it will be fun.
- Brandi Jessica does not ask questions. She has learned, in ten days, that the answers are worse than the questions. She has learned that asking makes things worse. She has learned that compliance is the only safety.

### Beat 3: The Clothes
- Andrew stands up. He goes to the closet or the luggage. He pulls out regular clothes — not the black dress, not the performance wear, but ordinary clothes.
- A shirt. Pants. Socks. Shoes. Regular clothes for a seven-year-old girl. The kind of clothes a child wears on a plane. The kind of clothes that do not draw attention. The kind of clothes that say *normal family traveling.*
- He brings them to the bed. He tells Brandi Jessica to stand up.

### Beat 4: Greenlee Helps
- Greenlee stands up from the chair. She walks to the bed. She helps Andrew dress Brandi Jessica.
- They remove the black dress. The dress that has been worn for ten days. The dress that was worn at the party, at the poker game, in the bathroom, on New Year's Eve. The dress with the slipped strap. The nylons.
- They take it off her. They put the regular clothes on her — the shirt, the pants, the socks, the shoes.
- Greenlee adjusts the collar. She smooths the fabric. She fixes Brandi Jessica's hair. She makes her look presentable. She makes her look like a normal child traveling with her parents.
- Brandi Jessica stands still while they dress her. She does not resist. She does not ask what the clothes mean. She does not ask where they are going. She stands still while two adults dress her the way you dress a doll before putting it in a box.

### Beat 5: The Transformation
- When they finish, Brandi Jessica looks different. The black dress is gone. The nylons are gone. She is wearing regular clothes — the kind of clothes a child wears to school, to the park, to the airport.
- She looks like a normal seven-year-old girl. She does not look like the child from the poker game. She does not look like the child from New Year's Eve. She looks like a child who is traveling with her family.
- Andrew looks at her. Greenlee looks at her. They are satisfied. She looks right. She looks like what they need her to look like.
- Brandi Jessica looks at herself. She does not recognize the clothes. These are not her clothes. These are not the clothes Kiley Rose bought her. These are not the clothes Pandora Paige would recognize. These are clothes Andrew and Greenlee chose for her — the disguise that will let them take her on a plane.

### Beat 6: The Departure
- Andrew picks up the luggage. Greenlee picks up her bag. Brandi Jessica stands by the door.
- Andrew opens the door. He looks at Brandi Jessica. He tells her to walk with them. To hold his hand or Greenlee's hand. To look like a family.
- Brandi Jessica does what she is told. She takes Andrew's hand or Greenlee's hand. She walks out of the hotel room. She walks down the hallway. She walks to the elevator. She walks out of the hotel.
- Greenlee is with them. Greenlee is not staying behind. Greenlee is traveling with them. Greenlee will accompany them on the flight to the UAE.
- A car is waiting outside. They get in the car. They drive to the airport.
- Brandi Jessica sits in the back seat between Andrew and Greenlee, or in front with one of them, and she watches the city through the window — the last time she will see New York for a long time, maybe forever.

### Beat 7: The Airport
- They arrive at the airport. They walk through the terminal. They go through security.
- Brandi Jessica looks like a normal child traveling with her parents. She is wearing regular clothes. She is holding an adult's hand. She does not cry. She does not scream. She does not draw attention.
- Andrew has the tickets. He has the passports — or the documents he needs. Greenlee has her bag. Brandi Jessica has nothing.
- They board the plane. They sit in their seats. The plane takes off.
- Brandi Jessica looks out the window as the city shrinks below them. She watches the buildings get smaller. She watches the streets disappear. She watches the country disappear. She watches the ocean appear.
- She is seven years old. She is on a plane. She does not know where she is going. She does not know when she is coming back. She does not know if she is ever coming back.
- Greenlee sits beside her. Greenlee is not going back to the city. Greenlee is going to the UAE. Greenlee is going to the compound. Greenlee will deliver her to Shakib.
- The plane flies east. The sun rises over the ocean. Brandi Jessica closes her eyes. She does not sleep. She thinks of Pandora. She thinks of Kiley. She thinks of Julia. She thinks of home.
- She does not know that she will never see home again.
