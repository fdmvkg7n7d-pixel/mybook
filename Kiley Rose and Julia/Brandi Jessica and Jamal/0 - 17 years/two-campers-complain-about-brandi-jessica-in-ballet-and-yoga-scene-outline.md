# Two Campers Complain About Brandi Jessica in Ballet and Yoga — First Day of Classes Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Brandi Jessica Key (8), Sloan (camper, age 9), Rilee (camper, age 10), Ballet Instructor, Yoga Instructor
- **Onstage animal:** None (Burden does not arrive until age 14)
- **Locations:** Dance pavilion (Ballet studio), Yoga studio — French Woods (Hancock, New York)
- **When:** The first day of activities at French Woods (July 2053), Session 2. The day after the twins arrived and chose their schedules. Brandi Jessica's very first day of classes. The twins are attending three back-to-back sessions — Session 2, Session 3, and Session 4 — the full summer. On the last day of each session, parents can visit their child; Kiley Rose and Julia will visit at the end of each session.
- **Status:** New scene outline
- **Tone:** Tense, isolating, empowering, Brandi alone without her twin on her very first day — confronted in the middle of class

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene captures the first direct challenge to Brandi Jessica's participation at French Woods — two campers complain that a blind girl should not be allowed in Ballet and Yoga. Both confrontations happen in the middle of class, while Brandi is actively practicing. Both happen on her very first day. Pandora is not present — she is at her own majors (Gymnastics and Circus). Brandi must face both alone. After each class, the instructor has a private conversation with Brandi.

It establishes:
- That Brandi Jessica can defend herself without Pandora from the very first moment.
- That the complaints interrupt class — they happen while Brandi is dancing, while she is holding a pose. The hostility enters the room and stops everyone.
- That Brandi took Ballet for three years before losing her sight — not seven. She is not an experienced dancer; she is a child who loved dance and lost it and came back anyway.
- That Brandi learned Yoga from her mommies last year, during chemotherapy and radiotherapy — Yoga was not a hobby, it was survival. She came to the mat because she needed to calm down from cancer treatments.
- That the instructors — who were briefed by the camp after Kiley Rose and Julia's April phone call — support Brandi in the moment and then seek her out after class for real conversations.
- That Brandi's calm, direct authority in the face of prejudice is her own, not a performance for Pandora's benefit.

## PART ONE: BALLET (MAJOR 1)

### Beat 1: Arrival at the Dance Pavilion

- Pandora walks Brandi to the dance pavilion before heading to her own first major — Gymnastics, which is in a separate building across camp.
- At the pavilion entrance, Pandora pauses. She describes the room quickly: "Big space. Sprung floor. Barres along the left and right walls. Mirror across the front. About ten girls already inside."
- Brandi nods. She takes her cane. She has already counted the steps from the cabin to here with Pandora on the walk over.
- Pandora hesitates. "Do you want me to stay for the first few minutes?"
- Brandi shakes her head. "I've got it. Go to Gymnastics. You'll be late."
- Pandora squeezes her hand once, hard, and then she is gone — footsteps receding down the path toward the gymnastics building.
- Brandi stands alone at the door of the dance pavilion for the first time. She takes a breath, taps her cane once on the threshold, and walks in.

### Beat 2: First Ballet Class Begins

- Brandi finds the barre by touch. The tactile barre guide that Kiley Rose and Julia asked French Woods to install is already in place — a thin raised line along the barre surface that tells her where her hand is. She runs her fingers along it and positions herself.
- The instructor begins the warm-up. Brandi follows every combination. She counts the music, listens to the instructor's verbal cues, and moves with the precision of a child who took Ballet for three years before losing her sight. Her lines are clean. Her turnout is correct.
- Some of the girls glance at her cane, leaning against the wall behind her. A few stare at her face — her eyes do not focus the way seeing eyes do. Brandi cannot see the stares, but she can feel the weight of attention.
- She ignores it. She dances.

### Beat 3: Sloan Interrupts — Mid-Class

- The instructor is leading an across-the-floor combination — glissade, pas de bourrée, chassé. The girls are moving in small groups across the sprung floor.
- It is Brandi's turn. She steps forward, counts the music in her head, and begins. Her glissade is clean. Her pas de bourrée is precise. Her chassé finishes right on the beat.
- As she reaches the other side of the floor, Sloan's voice cuts through the music:
  - "This isn't fair."
- The music is still playing but the instructor pauses it. Every girl in the studio stops.
- Sloan is standing near the barre, arms crossed, flushed. She speaks to the instructor but loud enough for the whole room:
  - "She can't see the mirror. She can't see where anyone else is. She's going to walk into someone during a combination, or fall off pointe because she can't see the edge of the stage. It's not safe. She shouldn't be in this class."
- The studio goes silent. Every girl is looking at Sloan, then at Brandi, then back at Sloan.
- Brandi is standing on the far side of the floor. She has just completed the combination. She is breathing hard. Her hand finds the barre behind her.

### Beat 4: Brandi Responds — Alone, Mid-Class

- For a moment, the only sound in the studio is Brandi's breathing and the faint hum of the pavilion fans.
- Then Brandi turns to face the direction of Sloan's voice. She does not have Pandora beside her. She does not have Maya. She is standing on the dance floor, in her first class, still catching her breath.
- Brandi speaks quietly but clearly:
  - "Sloan — I'm Brandi Jessica. I'm blind. I use a cane and I use tactile guides on the barre. I count the music and I listen to the instructor's voice. I took Ballet for three years before I lost my eyes. I remember every position, every line, every formation. I don't need the mirror to know where my body is."
- Sloan starts to interrupt — "But you can't —"
- Brandi continues, not raising her voice: "I just did the combination. You saw it. I didn't walk into anyone. I didn't fall. I didn't miss a step. If you want to ask me how I do something, you can ask me. Not about me. To me."
- A beat. Then:
  - "This is my first class here. I just did the combination across the floor with everyone else. That's not nothing."
- The studio is silent. Several girls are staring at Sloan, not Brandi.

### Beat 5: The Ballet Instructor Handles the Moment

- The instructor walks to the center of the floor. She speaks calmly but firmly, addressing the whole room:
  - "Brandi Jessica is in this class because she belongs here. She just executed that combination correctly. She has followed every combination this morning without a single disruption. If anyone has a safety concern, you bring it to me privately. You do not stop my class to question another camper's right to be in it."
- She looks directly at Sloan: "Sloan, do you have a safety concern you'd like to bring to me privately?"
- Sloan's face is red. She shakes her head.
- The instructor: "Then we're going back to work. Music from the top of the combination."
- The music starts again. The class resumes. Sloan goes back to the barre.
- Brandi returns to her place. Her heart is hammering but her hands are steady on the barre. She finishes the rest of class without incident.

### Beat 6: After Ballet — The Instructor's Conversation with Brandi

- Class ends. Girls gather their things and file out. A few glance at Brandi with something new — not pity, but respect.
- One girl pauses at the barre on her way out. "How do you know where the barre is without seeing it?"
- Brandi shows her the tactile strip. "I feel this with my hand. It tells me where I am on the barre."
- The girl nods. "That's cool."
- Sloan leaves without looking at Brandi.
- When the pavilion is mostly empty, the Ballet instructor approaches Brandi.
- The instructor sits down on the floor near the barre, gesturing for Brandi to sit too. The conversation is quiet and private.
- Instructor: "Brandi Jessica, I want you to know that you handled that beautifully. You have every right to be in this class. I've read your file, I know your history, and I can see with my own eyes that you can dance."
- Brandi, sitting with her hands on her knees: "Thank you."
- Instructor: "You said you took Ballet for three years before you lost your sight?"
- Brandi nods. "I started when I was four. I loved it. Then I got cancer when I was six and I had surgery and I lost my eyes. But I remember everything. The positions, the music, how it feels to move across the floor. I can't see the mirror anymore but I can feel when something is right."
- Instructor: "You absolutely can. Your technique is strong. I want to make sure you have everything you need in this class — anything I can adjust, any cue you need me to give verbally instead of visually. You tell me."
- Brandi: "I use the tactile strip on the barre — my mommies asked the camp to put that there for me. I count the music. If you call out the combination before it starts, I can follow it. And my sister Pandora helps me with alignment when she's here — but she's in Gymnastics and Circus, so she can't always be in this room."
- Instructor: "Then I'll make sure the verbal cues are clear enough that you don't need someone to translate. You're my student, not my project. I'm going to teach you the same way I teach everyone else — with whatever adjustments make that possible."
- Brandi smiles — the real, full smile that comes when someone treats her as a dancer, not a problem.
- Brandi: "Thank you. I really want to be good at this."
- Instructor: "You already are. Now go — I think you have Yoga next."
- Brandi picks up her cane, counts her steps to the door, and walks out.

## PART TWO: YOGA (MINOR 1)

### Beat 7: Yoga Studio

- It is the second period of the morning. Brandi walks to the Yoga studio alone — Pandora showed her the route earlier and Brandi counted the steps, but she has never walked it by herself before.
- She finds the studio. She steps inside. The room is warm and quiet — soft music, the smell of cedar. Mats are already laid out.
- Rilee, a ten-year-old from Birch Cabin, is already on her mat, stretching. She has not been in any of Brandi's classes before. This is the first time they share a room.
- Class begins. The instructor leads them through a sequence — mountain pose, forward fold, downward dog, warrior. Brandi follows by listening and by feeling the alignment in her own body. Her poses are steady and correct. She does not wobble. She does not fall.

### Beat 8: Rilee Interrupts — Mid-Class

- The class is in the middle of a warrior sequence. The instructor has just called for warrior two — arms extended, front knee bent, back foot grounded. The room is quiet except for breathing.
- Brandi is holding warrior two on the right side. Her arms are level, her front knee tracks over her ankle, her back foot is solid. She feels the alignment through her whole body.
- Rilee, two mats over, speaks up — not to the room, the way Sloan did. She speaks directly to the instructor, but her words are about Brandi:
  - "How can she be doing this right if she can't see the pose? She can't see what you're demonstrating. She could be doing it wrong and hurting herself and not even know it."
- The quiet of the yoga room shatters. Several girls turn to look at Brandi, still holding warrior two.
- Brandi's arms do not drop. Her knee stays bent. She holds the pose.

### Beat 9: Brandi Responds — Alone, Mid-Pose

- The Yoga instructor starts to respond, but Brandi speaks first. Still in warrior two. Still holding the pose. Her voice is calm:
  - "Rilee — I feel the poses. I don't need to see them. Yoga is about what's happening inside your body — your breath, your alignment, where your weight is. I know where my knee is. I know if my hips are level. I know if my spine is straight."
- Rilee: "But how do you know you're doing it right if you can't see it?"
- Brandi, still in the pose: "The same way you know when you close your eyes in savasana. You feel it. I just feel everything, all the time."
- A beat. Then Brandi adds, her voice steady:
  - "If you want to know how I do something, you can ask me. Not about me. To me. I'm happy to explain. But I'm holding this pose right now, and my alignment is correct, and you can ask the teacher if you don't believe me."
- She holds warrior two for one more breath, then slowly, deliberately, lowers her arms and steps back to mountain pose — on her own timing, not rushed, not shaken.

### Beat 10: The Yoga Instructor Handles the Moment

- The Yoga instructor speaks gently but firmly to Rilee:
  - "Rilee, I appreciate your concern for another camper's safety. But Brandi Jessica is practicing safely and correctly. I watch every student. I would not allow any camper to continue if I saw a risk of injury. If you have questions about how someone adapts a practice, I'm happy to talk about it after class. But telling a fellow camper she doesn't belong here while she's holding a pose in the middle of practice isn't concern — it's a judgment. And it's not one you need to make."
- Rilee's face is hot. She looks down at her mat.
- The instructor: "Let's come back to the breath. Everyone, close your eyes. Feel your body where it is right now."
- The class closes its eyes. Brandi is already there.
- The sequence resumes. Rilee does not speak again for the rest of class.

### Beat 11: After Yoga — The Instructor's Conversation with Brandi

- Class ends. Girls roll up their mats and leave. Rilee leaves quickly, without looking at Brandi.
- When the studio is empty, the Yoga instructor approaches Brandi, who is rolling up her mat by feel.
- The instructor sits down on the mat beside her. Her voice is warm and unhurried.
- Instructor: "Brandi Jessica, I want to check in with you. That was not an easy thing to happen in your first class."
- Brandi, sitting cross-legged on her mat: "I'm okay. I've had people say things before."
- Instructor: "I'm sure you have. You handled it with more grace than most adults I know. How long have you been practicing Yoga?"
- Brandi: "Since last year. My mommies taught me."
- Instructor: "Your mommies taught you? That's wonderful. What made them start teaching you?"
- Brandi is quiet for a moment. Then:
  - "Last year I had cancer. I had chemotherapy treatments and then radiotherapy treatments. It was really hard. My body hurt and I was scared all the time and I couldn't calm down. My mommies showed me how to do Yoga so I could breathe and be still when everything else was too much. It helped me get through it."
- The instructor takes this in. She does not rush.
- Instructor: "So Yoga was part of your healing."
- Brandi nods. "It still is. Every time I get on the mat, I remember that I got through the treatments. I can do hard things."
- Instructor: "And your sister — does she practice too?"
- Brandi smiles. "Pandora learned with me. My mommies taught both of us. And they do Partner Yoga with us — my mommy Kiley Rose and my mommy Julia get on the mat with us and we do the poses together. They hold me and help me feel the shapes, and I help them hold their balance. We learned it together during the treatments. It was our thing — all four of us."
- Instructor: "That's beautiful. So when you're in this class without her, you're still carrying that practice with you."
- Brandi: "Yes. But I can do it without her too. She doesn't have to be on the next mat for me to find the pose. I know how to feel it on my own."
- Instructor: "I can see that. Your alignment is beautiful, your breath is steady, and you clearly know your body. I want to make sure you have what you need in this class. Anything I can do — more verbal cues, different hands-on adjustments, anything — you tell me."
- Brandi: "I follow your words. If you describe the pose, I can find it. I don't need to see it. I just need to hear it."
- Instructor: "Then I'll make sure my words are enough. And Brandi Jessica?"
- Brandi: "Yeah?"
- Instructor: "You belong on this mat. Don't let anyone make you forget that."
- Brandi reaches out and finds the instructor's hand. She squeezes it once.
- Brandi: "I won't."
- She rolls up her mat, picks up her cane, counts the steps to the door, and walks out into the morning sun.

## PART THREE: BACK AT THE CABIN

### Beat 12: Pandora Finds Out

- That evening, after dinner, Pandora and Brandi are sitting together on Brandi's lower bunk. The cabin is busy with the usual evening routine — girls brushing teeth, writing letters, comparing notes about their first day.
- Sloan and Rilee are across the cabin, not speaking to Brandi, but not saying anything against her either.
- Pandora, bright and full of her own day: "How was Ballet? How was Yoga? Did you like the instructors? Did the other girls talk to you?"
- Brandi tells her. All of it. Sloan interrupting in the middle of the across-the-floor combination. Rilee interrupting while she was holding warrior two. Her own responses. The instructors backing her. The conversations after class.
- Pandora's face changes. The brightness disappears. Her jaw tightens.
- Pandora: "They said that to you in the middle of class? While you were dancing? While you were holding a pose?"
- Brandi: "Yes."
- Pandora: "On the first day?"
- Brandi: "On the first day."
- Pandora: "And I wasn't there for either one."
- Brandi: "No. You were at Gymnastics and Circus. Where you were supposed to be."
- Pandora: "I should have been there."
- Brandi reaches out and finds Pandora's hand. She holds it firmly.
  - "No. You shouldn't have. I handled it. Both of them. I told them the same things you would have told them. But I told them myself. I was standing on the dance floor when Sloan said it, and I was holding warrior two when Rilee said it, and I didn't drop either one. I'm not always going to have you in the room, Pandora. I need to know I can do this without you."
- Pandora is quiet for a long time. Then she squeezes Brandi's hand.
- Pandora: "You were holding warrior two when you answered her?"
- Brandi: "I held it for one more breath after. Then I came back to mountain."
- Pandora exhales. Then: "What did the teachers say to you after?"
- Brandi tells her about the conversations. The Ballet instructor asking what she needs. The Yoga instructor asking about her practice.
- Brandi: "I told the Yoga teacher that my mommies taught me Yoga last year during chemo and radiotherapy. That it helped me calm down. I told her you learned with me. That we do Partner Yoga together — all four of us, me and you and Kiley Rose and Julia."
- Pandora is quiet. She remembers last year. She was there for the treatments. She held Brandi's hand through all of it. She learned the poses on the mat right next to her sister because their mommies said it would help both of them.
- Pandora: "You told her that?"
- Brandi: "Yes. She asked why I started, and I told her the truth. Yoga isn't just something I do. It's how we survived. Both of us."
- Pandora leans her head against Brandi's shoulder. "You're braver than me."
- Brandi: "I'm not braver. I just didn't have a choice."
- Pandora: "You always have a choice. You chose to answer her. You chose to keep the pose. That's bravery."
- A beat. Then Brandi smiles. "I'm proud of me. But I'm glad I can tell you about it."
- Pandora: "You can always tell me."

### Beat 13: That Night

- Lights out. The cabin is dark. Sloan and Rilee are in their bunks, silent.
- Pandora whispers from the top bunk: "Brandi?"
- Brandi whispers back: "Yeah?"
- Pandora: "If anyone ever says anything like that to you again — anywhere, anytime — you tell me. Not so I can fight them. So I know. And so I can be proud of you again."
- Brandi whispers: "Deal."
- A pause. Then Brandi adds: "Pandora?"
- Pandora: "Yeah?"
- Brandi: "I told them 'not about me, to me.' I think that's going to be my thing."
- Pandora, in the dark, smiles. "It already is."

## RELATION TO CONTINUITY

- This scene takes place on the **first day of activities** at French Woods (July 2053), the day after the twins arrived and chose their schedules.
- **Brandi's first day schedule:** Breakfast → Ballet (Major 1) → Yoga (Minor 1) → Waterfront Recreation (Minor 2) → Lunch → Pointe (Major 2) → Yoga (Minor 3) → Swimming Lessons (Major 3) → Dinner.
- Both complaints happen in the morning: Sloan in Ballet (first period), Rilee in Yoga (second period).
- **Teacher conversations happen after class** — the Ballet instructor and Yoga instructor each seek Brandi out privately to check in, learn about her needs, and build a relationship.
- **Three years of Ballet:** Brandi started Ballet at age four and lost her sight at age eight. She took Ballet for three years as a sighted child, not seven.
- **Yoga from her mommies:** Last year (age 8), during chemotherapy and then radiotherapy treatments for retinoblastoma, Kiley Rose and Julia taught both Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige Yoga to help Brandi calm down. Pandora learned alongside her twin. Kiley Rose and Julia do Partner Yoga with the girls — all four of them get on the mat together. The mothers hold Brandi and help her feel the shapes; Brandi helps them hold their balance. Yoga was not a hobby — it was a coping mechanism for cancer treatment, and it belonged to the whole family. This is Brandi's own explanation in the after-class conversation with the Yoga instructor.
- **Chemotherapy and radiotherapy:** Brandi's retinoblastoma treatment included bilateral enucleation first (February 2052), then chemotherapy, then radiotherapy. This expands the medical history beyond what was previously established in the profile.
- **Pandora is not present** for either confrontation. She is at her own majors (Gymnastics and Circus). This is the first time Brandi faces hostility at camp without her twin — and it happens on Day One.
- The complaints are **class-specific**: Sloan in Ballet, Rilee in Yoga. Both campers challenge Brandi's right to be in activities where sight is assumed essential.
- Sloan's complaint is public — she speaks to the room during class. Rilee's is more personal — she speaks to the instructor but about Brandi. Both stop the class.
- Brandi's "Not about me. To me." principle originates here — she claims it herself on her very first day, and Pandora recognizes it as Brandi's own.
- **Tactile barre guide:** The raised tactile strip on the barre was not Brandi's request — it was requested by Kiley Rose and Julia when they called French Woods in April. They arranged the accommodation in advance so Brandi would have what she needed from the first class. Brandi references this in her after-class conversation with the Ballet instructor: "My mommies asked the camp to put that there for me."
- Brandi's explanation of how she does Yoga (feeling poses from the inside, verbal instruction, body awareness, learned from her mommies during cancer treatment) is consistent with real-world blind yoga practice and adds the crucial medical backstory.
- Brandi holding warrior two while responding to Rilee is a defining image — she does not break the pose, does not drop her arms, does not let the hostility interrupt her practice.
- This scene directly foreshadows the UNLV confrontation years later, where dance students react with hostility to Brandi's admission. The pattern is the same: prejudice stated openly → Brandi responds with calm authority → an instructor backs her. But at UNLV, the hostility is from adults, not children, and the stakes are higher.
- The Ballet instructor's line — "You're my student, not my project" — establishes the standard Brandi will expect from every future teacher.
- The Yoga instructor's line — "You belong on this mat" — becomes a quiet anchor for Brandi's yoga practice going forward.
- Brandi telling Pandora afterward — and insisting she handled it herself — reinforces that independence does not mean isolation.
- The scene happens **before** the Day Three independence walk. Brandi faces prejudice before she has even proven she can navigate the camp alone. Her first act of self-advocacy is not about mobility — it's about belonging.
