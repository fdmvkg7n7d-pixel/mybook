# Brandi Jessica’s Captivity in the Hotel Room — Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Brandi Jessica Key (six years old), **Andrew** (the kidnapper), Kiley Rose Key (twenty-one years old, offstage / referenced), Julia (eighteen years old, offstage / referenced), Mike Key (offstage / referenced)
- **Offstage / referenced characters:** Pandora Paige Key, Terri Anne Key, the Police / Investigators
- **Location:** A secluded, high-end luxury hotel room on the outskirts of New York City (outside the city limits)
- **When:** **December 27, 2051** (The morning after her kidnapping on December 26, several weeks *before* her rescheduled eye operation in February).
- **Status:** Updated scene outline
- **Tone:** High-stakes, terrifying, claustrophobic, survival-focused, intense psychological drama, protective resilience, financial vengeance, calculated execution

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene outlines **Brandi Jessica’s terrifying captivity inside a secluded hotel room after being kidnapped by Andrew on December 27, 2051.** 

The timeline is clarified: the day after she is taken from the townhouse Christmas party, she wakes up in a high-end hotel room outside the New York City limits. **Andrew is a wealthy man.** Though he is not wealthier than Mike Key, **he is driven by a desire for financial vengeance: he wants to pay Mike Key back for getting him into a very awful deal where Andrew lost five hundred million ($500,000,000) dollars.** 

Unlike a common thief, **Andrew is not frightened of the police or the Key family's influence because he has a meticulous, calculated plan** to secure his money and escape, which he executes with absolute composure. 

To keep six-year-old Brandi Jessica quiet, compliant, and asleep in this secure room, **Andrew uses liquid Ketamine**, slipping it into her drinks. Even though her sight is still physically intact (as her Bilateral Retinoblastoma operation has been pushed back to February due to her disappearance), the physical and chemical toll of Andrew's liquid Ketamine sedation and captivity forever shapes her unyielding, fierce independence in adulthood.

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: The Luxury Hotel Room — December 27
- It is the morning of **December 27, 2051**, the day after her abduction from the townhouse. 
- Six-year-old Brandi Jessica wakes up inside a spacious, high-end luxury hotel room outside the New York City limits. Because **Andrew is a wealthy man**, he has secured a high-end, discreet suite rather than a dingy motel, ensuring he remains insulated.
- Brandi's sight is still physically intact, but her eyes are heavy, and her mind is completely confused. She is terrified, her small hands gripping the unfamiliar bedsheets, her mind completely hazy as she wonders why she cannot go home to her mothers, Kiley and Julia, and her twin sister, Pandora. She has nothing from home — no comfort object, no familiar possession to anchor her.

### Beat 2: Andrew's Meticulous Calm and the Liquid Ketamine
- Andrew is in the room, pacing. Unlike a desperate, panicked amateur, **Andrew is not frightened of the police or Mike Key's massive influence because he has a meticulous, calculated plan.** He moves with cold, absolute composure, executing each step of his plan with business-like precision.
- **Brandi Jessica recognizes Andrew's voice.** She heard it at the townhouse Christmas party the night before — he was one of the guests. The familiarity of his voice makes the moment even more disorienting: this is not a stranger in the dark, but someone who was inside her family's home just hours ago. In her small, trembling voice, she tells him: *"I know you. You were at the party."*
- **Andrew laughs out loud.** The sound is sudden, sharp, and entirely without warmth — a cold, genuine amusement that fills the hotel room. He is not alarmed that she recognizes him; her recognition means nothing to his plan. The casual cruelty of his laughter, directed at a terrified six-year-old who has just named him, is devastating. He looks down at her and says: *"Because your ass is mine."* It tells Brandi Jessica everything she needs to know: he does not care that she knows who he is, because he is absolutely certain no one is coming for her, and she belongs to him now.
- To keep the six-year-old compliant, quiet, and unable to scream or run, **Andrew relies on liquid Ketamine.**
- He pours a small dose of the clear, odorless, and tasteless liquid Ketamine into a glass of apple juice. He brings it over, putting on a false, manipulative smile to coax the terrified child:

> “Drink your juice, Brandi. It's just a special holiday juice. It'll make you feel warm, and then you can go back to sleep. Your mom and Julia are waiting for you, but you have to rest first.”

- Brandi Jessica, thirsty and desperate for comfort, takes the glass with trembling hands and drinks it.

### Beat 3: Falling into the Dissociative Sleep
- Within minutes, the liquid Ketamine begins to take effect.
- Brandi Jessica feels a strange, cold heaviness spreading through her limbs. The dawning effect of the dissociative anesthetic makes her feel completely disconnected from her body—as if she is floating above the mattress.
- She tries to keep her eyes open, looking at Andrew’s calm, calculated face, but her vision blurs into a hazy, dream-like state. Her small hands fall open at her sides, and she sinks back onto the pillows into a deep, drug-induced sleep.
- Andrew watches her go completely still, satisfied that she will remain silent while he executes the next phase of his plan.

### Beat 4: Overhearing the Radios and Andrew's Vengeance
- In her semi-conscious, floating state, Brandi’s mind clings desperately to the memory of her family.
- She remembers the game of Uno on the braided rug, her matching red dress, and the exciting promise of seeing *The Nutcracker* at Lincoln Center. 
- Through her haze, she hears Andrew speaking calmly on his secure satellite phone, completely unfazed by the global alerts. He is consumed by a cold, calculating desire for vengeance against Mike Key. He whispers fiercely to himself about his motive:

> “That son of a bitch Mike... he got me into that awful deal. Five hundred million dollars. He made me lose five hundred million, and now he is going to pay. My plan is perfect. I will make him pay every single cent back, and there is nothing his security can do to stop me.”

- Brandi is young and the Ketamine makes her mind float, but the terrifying mention of her grandfather Mike and the **five hundred million dollar loss** sits heavily in her subconscious memory.

### Beat 5: Andrew’s Meticulous Plan
- Andrew sits at the desk, analyzing secure bank transfers and offshore routing on his laptop, his composure unbreakable.
- He knows that Mike Key's OP channels and the police are tracking vehicles, but **his calculated plan has accounted for every variable.** He has prepared secure, anonymous transport and offshore accounts that shield his identity, ensuring he remains steps ahead of the investigators.

### Beat 6: The Physical Toll
- A few hours later, Brandi Jessica stirs, waking up fitfully from the Ketamine sleep. 
- She is dizzy, nauseous, and has a severe headache as the drug begins to wear off. She reaches up to touch her eyes, which feel heavy and sore. 
- She looks at her hand, her vision still hazy. Even though her tumors have not blocked her vision yet, the sheer physical and chemical toll of the Ketamine and the abduction is draining her small body, making the upcoming February eye operation increasingly urgent.

### Beat 7: The Bath
- Andrew tells Brandi Jessica it is time for a bath. He has already drawn the water — warm, filled with the hotel's complimentary bath products. To him, it is the same as the meal: maintenance. He needs her clean and presentable for the hours ahead.
- **Brandi Jessica refuses.** She backs away from him, pressing herself against the headboard, her small body rigid with resistance. Because Kiley Rose and Julia have taught her well — her body is hers, no one touches her or sees her without her permission, and baths are private. Only her mommies and Pandora are allowed. She knows this rule the way she knows her own name.
- She tells him, her voice shaking but firm: *"My mommies said nobody's supposed to see me like that. Only them. You're not allowed."*
- Andrew is not moved. He does not negotiate with a six-year-old. He picks her up with the same business-like efficiency as everything else, carrying her toward the bathroom despite her kicking and squirming. She fights him with everything she has — scratching at his hands, twisting her small body — but she is six, and he is a grown man.
- The bath happens. Brandi Jessica sits in the water, rigid and silent, her eyes squeezed shut, her hands covering herself as best she can. She does not cry — she will not give him that. But the violation of what her mothers taught her, of the sacred privacy they instilled in her, is its own kind of wound, deeper than the Ketamine. She holds onto their voices in her head: *Your body is yours, baby. No one gets to take that from you.*
- But he just did. And there is nothing she could do to stop it.

### Beat 8: The Black Dress
- After the bath, Andrew lays out clothes on the bed — not her clothes. Not the red Christmas dress from the party, not anything from home. A short black dress and black nylons, sized for a child but utterly wrong. They are the kind of clothes that belong to a world she has no business being in.
- Brandi Jessica stares at them. She doesn't fully understand what they mean, but she knows — the way a child knows, through instinct and the echo of her mothers' voices — that these are not clothes for her. That something about them is bad.
- She refuses again. She tries to pull the hotel towel tighter around herself, but Andrew takes it away. She stands there, exposed and small, and he dresses her himself. His hands are efficient, impersonal, the same way he drew the bath and ordered the food. He does not look at her the way a parent does — he is dressing a prop, a piece of his plan.
- The nylons are cold and tight against her legs. The dress is short and itchy. She has never worn anything like this. Her mommies dress her in bright colors, in soft cotton, in things that let her run and play. This dress does not let her run. It does not feel like hers. It feels like a costume someone put her in, because that is exactly what it is.
- She stands there, a six-year-old in a short black dress and nylons, looking at herself in the hotel mirror, and she does not recognize the girl staring back. That is not Brandi Jessica. That is someone else entirely. And the horror of that — of not recognizing herself — sits alongside the bath and the juice and the laugh, another thing taken from her that she will spend her whole life trying to reclaim.

### Beat 9: The Poker Game
- That evening, three men arrive at the hotel room. They are associates of Andrew's — wealthy, calculating men who move with the same cold confidence he does. Andrew has invited them for a poker game. The suite's sitting area becomes a card table: chips, drinks, cigars, the low murmur of men conducting business under the guise of a game.
- **Andrew tells Brandi Jessica to serve them drinks.** He says it the way you'd instruct a piece of furniture — no please, no explanation. Just: *"Bring the glasses. Fill them. Go."*
- She stands frozen. Every instinct screams no. She is six years old. She does not know these men. She is in a short black dress and nylons that are not hers, in a room that is not hers, being told to walk among strangers who look at her with glancing, indifferent eyes. She looks at Andrew, hoping — childishly, desperately — that he will see how wrong this is.
- He does not. He gives her a look that silences her before she can speak. It is not anger. It is the same look he gave her in the bath, the same voice that said *"Because your ass is mine."* It says: *You will do this, and you will not fight me.*
- Brandi Jessica picks up the tray. Her hands shake so badly the glasses rattle. She walks barefoot across the carpet — Andrew did not give her shoes — and she serves the drinks. One man takes his glass without looking at her. Another glances down at her and says nothing. The third barely notices she exists.
- She is invisible to them. A six-year-old girl in a black dress, shaking like a leaf, and they do not see her. She is part of the room now, like the furniture, like the chips on the table.
- **When she is not serving drinks, Andrew makes her sit on their laps.** He gestures to the nearest man — a flick of his fingers, like calling a dog — and tells her: *"Sit."*
- Brandi Jessica does not move. She cannot. Every cell in her body locks. She has been taught — by Kiley Rose, by Julia, by every lesson on bodily autonomy they ever gave her — that she does not sit on strangers' laps. That no one touches her. That she is allowed to say no.
- But she cannot say no here. Andrew's look cuts through her, and she knows if she fights, she will lose the same way she lost the bath, the same way she lost the towel, the same way she lost everything since she woke up in this room.
- She climbs onto the man's lap. He does not acknowledge her. He holds his cards in one hand and adjusts her absently with the other, the way someone shifts a cushion. She sits rigid, her small body vibrating with revulsion, her hands balled into fists against her thighs. The nylons are slick against his suit pants. She can feel the heat of him through the fabric. She wants to scream. She does not scream.
- Over the course of the game, Andrew moves her from lap to lap. *"Go sit with him now."* She obeys. She climbs down from one stranger and onto another, a six-year-old passed between grown men like a card in their game. None of them hold her like a child. None of them ask her name. She is a warmth on their legs, nothing more.
- This is the worst thing yet — worse than the laugh, worse than the bath, worse than the dress — because it means she has been made so small that grown men can hold her on their laps and pretend she is not a child in trouble.
- She serves every round. She sits on every lap. She does not cry. She does not speak. She moves between the men and the bar like a ghost, and when the game is over and the men leave, she stands in the middle of the room, still holding the tray, still not crying, because if she starts she will never stop.

### Beat 10: The Meal and the Promise to Survive
- Andrew orders room service — a proper, generous meal for a child. Pancakes, scrambled eggs, fresh fruit, a glass of milk. It arrives on a polished cart with white linen. The meal is good, even beautiful, and that is the most disorienting part of all.
- He sets it in front of her with the same cold composure as everything else. No kindness in it — it is maintenance. He needs her alive and healthy enough to serve his plan. But to a six-year-old waking from a drug haze, hungry and aching, the food is a sickening contradiction: the pancakes taste like pancakes, the fruit is sweet, and a man who laughed in her face and told her *"your ass is mine"* is the one who put it there.
- Brandi Jessica eats in silence. She does not want to eat, but her small body is starving. Every bite feels like a betrayal of herself, and she cannot understand why.
- After the meal, she curls up on the bed, pulling her knees to her chest. She touches her collarbone, holding onto the memory of her mothers' unconditional love.
- Even at six years old, huddled in this dark, drug-hazed hotel room with the taste of pancakes still on her tongue, a fierce, silent spark of survival ignites inside her. She whispers into the empty room: *"They're going to find us. Mom and Julia are coming."*
- This harrowing experience of chemical sedation, financial vengeance, and captivity becomes the absolute crucible of her life. It is the reason why, once she is rescued and undergoes her February surgery, she and her mothers vow to never treat her as a victim, driving her to build an unshakeable, fierce, and "anything and everything" independence in her blind adulthood.
