# Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige — First Arrival at French Woods (Age 8)

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Brandi Jessica Key (8), Pandora Paige Key (8), Kiley Rose Key, Julia, French Woods staff (counselor and check-in staff)
- **Onstage animal:** None (Burden does not arrive until age 14)
- **Locations:** French Woods campus entrance/check-in area, cabin path, cabin interior (Hancock, New York)
- **When:** July of Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige's eighth year. The twins' first summer at French Woods (attending three back-to-back sessions — Session 2, Session 3, and Session 4, the full summer). On the last day of each session, parents can visit their child — Kiley Rose and Julia will visit at the end of Session 2, Session 3, and Session 4. Kiley Rose and Julia had called the camp in April of their eighth year to inform them about Andrew.
- **Status:** New scene outline
- **Tone:** Nervous excitement, careful independence, twin synchronization, family pride, the first real taste of “anything and everything”

## SCENE PURPOSE

This is the twins’ **first arrival** at French Woods at age eight — the first time Kiley Rose and Julia send them away for sleepaway camp “without any single doubt.”

The scene establishes:
- The family’s commitment to giving Brandi Jessica full, unshielded summer experiences immediately after her blindness.
- Pandora Paige’s role as Brandi’s constant helper and co-pilot from the very first moment.
- The physical and emotional logistics of Brandi navigating a new, large, wooded environment without a guide dog.
- The emotional moment of Kiley and Julia letting go while the twins step forward together.
- The beginning of the twins’ long, shared history at French Woods with other campers.

It mirrors the “Manhattan Drops” philosophy in a new setting: trust, preparation, and freedom.

## BEATS

### Beat 1: The Drive and Drop-Off
- Kiley Rose and Julia drive the twins from New York City to the Catskills.
- The car is full of camp trunks, labeled clothes, a white cane, and the twins’ identical plaid backpacks.
- Brandi Jessica sits with her hand on Pandora’s knee the whole ride. They have been talking about French Woods for weeks.
- Pandora has already memorized the camp map from the brochure and describes the route to Brandi as they get closer: “We’re turning onto the dirt road now… lots of trees… I see the big wooden sign.”
- Kiley and Julia are calm but quietly emotional. This is the first time they are sending the girls away since the cancer.

### Beat 2: Check-In
- They pull up to the check-in area. Other families are unloading.
- A counselor with a clipboard greets them. The counselor has been briefed in advance about Brandi Jessica’s blindness and has a simple plan ready.
- Brandi and Pandora stand side-by-side in matching shorts and camp t-shirts. Brandi holds her white cane. Pandora stands slightly ahead, already scanning the area and describing quietly: “There’s a big wooden arch. Lots of kids. Counselors in green shirts.”
- Kiley and Julia do the paperwork. The counselor asks Brandi directly (not the adults) how she would like to be guided around camp for the first few days.
- Brandi answers clearly: “Pandora will help me. And I’ll use my cane.”
- The counselor nods and hands Pandora a small map and a cabin key on a lanyard. “You two are in Birch Cabin. Your counselor’s name is Maya. She’s waiting down the path.”

### Beat 2.5: Private Staff Briefing About Andrew
- Before walking the girls to the cabin, Kiley Rose and Julia ask to speak privately with the head counselor (Maya) and a camp administrator.
- They explain, calmly and without hesitation, that just months earlier (December 2051), six-year-old Brandi Jessica was kidnapped by a man named Andrew from their family’s townhouse Christmas party. Andrew was a wealthy, calculating guest seeking financial vengeance against Mike Key.
- The briefing covers: the kidnapping was resolved, Brandi has physically recovered and is now blind from the subsequent retinoblastoma surgery; however, the trauma is recent. They request staff awareness of potential triggers (mentions of strangers, kidnapping, “being taken,” closed rooms, or unfamiliar men). Pandora is the primary emotional and practical support.
- Kiley Rose states firmly: “We are sending them without any single doubt. Blindness is an adjustment, never an excuse. There are no no’s, only hows. We want them to have the full French Woods experience.”
- The staff listens respectfully, takes notes, and confirms the camp’s protocols for supporting campers with recent family trauma. The twins are not present for this conversation.
- This ensures the camp is fully prepared while the family upholds its philosophy of independence.

### Beat 3: The First Walk to the Cabin (Pandora Leads)
- Kiley and Julia are allowed to walk them part of the way but not all the way to the cabin (standard first-day policy).
- The path is dirt and pine needles, uneven in places.
- Pandora takes Brandi’s free hand and walks slightly ahead, giving a running commentary:
  - “Root on your right… step over… now it’s flat again.”
  - “There’s a big rock on the left that looks like a turtle.”
  - “Kids are laughing up ahead. I think that’s the lake.”
- Brandi counts steps and listens to the sounds (birds, distant splashing, wind in the trees). She is nervous but determined.
- They pass other returning campers and new families. Some stare. Pandora stares right back until they look away.

### Beat 4: Goodbye at the Path
- Kiley and Julia stop at the marked drop-off point before the cabin loop.
- Kiley crouches in front of both girls.
- She reminds them of the family rule: “There are no no’s, only hows. You can do anything the other kids can do. If you need help, ask for it. If you don’t, don’t take it.”
- Julia gives them each a small, smooth stone she picked up from Central Park. “These are for when you miss home. Rub them and think of us.”
- Brandi is quiet. Pandora is the one who says, “We’ll be okay.”
- Kiley hugs them both, then Julia does. The twins hug back hard.
- As Kiley and Julia turn to go, Brandi calls after them: “Tell Burden we’re at camp when you get home!” (even though Burden doesn’t exist yet — it’s a running family joke they’ve started).
- Kiley laughs through tears.

### Beat 5: Arrival at Birch Cabin
- Pandora leads Brandi the final stretch to the cabin.
- Maya (their counselor, mid-20s, warm and straightforward) is waiting outside with two other new campers.
- Maya introduces herself directly to both girls and asks Brandi if she wants a quick tour of the cabin layout before the others arrive.
- Inside the cabin:
  - Pandora chooses the lower bunk on the left for Brandi and takes the one above it.
  - She immediately starts unpacking Brandi’s trunk while describing the room: “Two sets of bunks. Window on the right. Door straight ahead. Hooks for towels on the left wall.”
  - Brandi runs her hands along the bed frame and counts the steps to the door and the bathroom.
- Pandora claims the top bunk “so I can see everything and tell you what’s happening.”

### Beat 6: First Moments of Independence
- Other girls start arriving. Pandora helps Brandi introduce herself to the cabin mates.
- When one girl asks why Brandi has a cane, Pandora answers calmly and clearly: “She’s blind. She can still do everything. I help her see some things.”
- Maya pulls the twins aside briefly and says the camp has already assigned them as “bunk buddies” so they can stay together.
- Brandi and Pandora exchange a look. They already knew they would.

### Beat 7: The Moment Kiley and Julia Are Gone
- After the last goodbye wave from the path, the twins stand together outside the cabin.
- Brandi is quiet for a moment.
- Pandora says: “We’re really here.”
- Brandi nods. “No one’s going to tell us we can’t do something.”
- Pandora takes her hand again. “Not even once.”

## RELATION TO CONTINUITY

- This is the twins’ **first** summer at French Woods (age 8, summer 2053), approximately nineteen months after the kidnapping.
- It comes immediately after Brandi Jessica’s retinoblastoma diagnosis, surgery, and recovery.
- No guide dog yet (Burden arrives at age 14).
- Pandora is Brandi’s full-time helper and translator from day one.
- This scene sets up the pattern that will continue for many summers: the twins as a unit, Pandora helping, Brandi participating in everything (dance, circus arts, swimming, music, etc.).
- Future scenes can explore interactions with other campers and family connections at the camp.
- Tone and philosophy directly echo the “Manhattan Drops” and the family rule of “anything and everything.”
- **Prior Call:** Fifteen months after the kidnapping (when Brandi Jessica was seven), Kiley Rose and Julia made a private phone call to French Woods to inform the director and head staff about Andrew’s kidnapping. Staff were briefed on the trauma, potential triggers, and the family’s philosophy so they would be prepared without the twins overhearing at arrival. The check-in counselor’s prior briefing references this call.
