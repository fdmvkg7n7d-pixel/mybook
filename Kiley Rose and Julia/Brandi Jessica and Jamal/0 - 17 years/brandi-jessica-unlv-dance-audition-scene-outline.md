# Brandi Jessica’s UNLV Dance Audition — Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Primary character:** Brandi Jessica Key (16, turning 17 on December 25, 2061)
- **Supporting characters:** Kiley Rose Key, Julia, Pandora Paige Key, UNLV Dance admissions committee, Brandi’s future first-year ballet professor
- **Onstage animal:** Burden, Brandi Jessica’s Labernese guide dog
- **Locations:** A hotel room near UNLV; the hotel restaurant; the walking route from the hotel to the university; UNLV’s dance building and audition studio
- **When:** **November 2061**, approximately ten months before Brandi Jessica begins her first academic year at UNLV
- **Status:** Scene outline
- **Tone:** Intimate family preparation, nervous independence, serious artistic evaluation, earned confidence

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

In November 2061, Kiley Rose, Julia, Brandi Jessica, and Pandora Paige travel together to Las Vegas for Brandi’s UNLV Dance audition. Brandi is sixteen and will turn seventeen on Christmas Day. They share a hotel room within walking distance of the university, with Burden staying in the room with them.

The family eats breakfast together in the hotel restaurant. Brandi then gets ready for the audition in the hotel room while Kiley Rose, Julia, and Pandora support her without taking over.

All four women walk from the hotel toward UNLV. At the agreed point near campus, Brandi asks her family to stop. She and Burden continue into the university alone. Kiley Rose, Julia, and Pandora return to the hotel rather than following secretly.

The audition introduces the professor whose voice Brandi later recognizes after her first ballet class. This professor is one of the admissions committee members who selects Brandi for the Dance program.

---

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: Four Women in One Hotel Room

- The family stays in one hotel room close enough to walk to UNLV.
- The room has two beds:
  - Kiley Rose and Julia share one.
  - Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige share the other, preserving the twins’ lifelong habit of sleeping side by side.
- Burden sleeps on his travel mat beside Brandi’s side of the bed.
- Audition morning begins before the alarm because Brandi is already awake, listening to the hotel air conditioner and unfamiliar hallway sounds.
- Pandora realizes Brandi is awake without either twin speaking.

> “Nervous?”

> “Yes.”

> “Good nervous or bad nervous?”

> “Ask me after the audition.”

- Kiley and Julia wake shortly afterward.
- Kiley immediately wants to check every item in Brandi’s dance bag.
- Julia reminds her that Brandi already packed it the previous night.
- Brandi says she wants breakfast before she dresses for the audition so she does not risk spilling anything on her dance clothes.

### Beat 2: Breakfast in the Hotel Restaurant

- The four women go downstairs to the hotel restaurant in comfortable morning clothes.
- Burden works in harness and guides Brandi from the elevator to the restaurant.
- The host addresses Kiley first. Kiley redirects the host to Brandi when the question concerns Burden or Brandi’s seating.
- Burden settles beneath the table, out of the servers’ path.
- Breakfast is practical rather than ceremonial:
  - Eggs or another protein.
  - Toast.
  - Fruit.
  - Water.
  - Coffee for Kiley and Julia.
- Brandi chooses food that will sustain her without feeling heavy during movement.
- Pandora describes the morning light over Las Vegas only after Brandi asks.
- Kiley asks whether Brandi wants the family to walk into the university with her.
- Brandi answers:

> “Walk with me from the hotel. Stop when I tell you. I need to enter the university without you.”

- Kiley’s first instinct is to object because Brandi is only sixteen, will not turn seventeen until Christmas Day, and the campus is unfamiliar.
- Julia places a hand over Kiley’s before the objection becomes an argument.
- Kiley asks whether Burden knows the route.
- Brandi explains that she reviewed the route and arrival instructions in advance. Burden will guide; Brandi will direct.
- Pandora says:

> “Then we walk until you dismiss us.”

### Beat 3: Returning Upstairs to Get Ready

- After breakfast, the family returns to the hotel room.
- Brandi removes Burden’s harness and gives him water while she changes.
- Her audition clothes are simple and professional:
  - A solid-colour leotard.
  - Dance tights.
  - Ballet shoes packed for the studio rather than worn outside.
  - Warm-up layers for the walk and check-in.
  - Hair secured away from her face and neck.
- Brandi chooses and puts on the clothing herself.
- Pandora gives visual information only when Brandi requests it:
  - Whether the leotard fabric is lying flat.
  - Whether her hair is fully secured.
  - Whether a seam has twisted.
- Kiley starts to fix Brandi’s hair without asking, catches herself, and lowers her hands.

> “Do you want help?”

- Brandi considers, then asks Kiley to check one section at the back.
- Kiley makes the single requested adjustment and stops.

### Beat 4: The Audition Bag

- Brandi checks the audition bag by touch and VoiceOver list:
  - Ballet shoes.
  - Contemporary shoes or bare-foot supplies as required.
  - Water.
  - Towel.
  - Extra hair ties.
  - Accessible copy of the audition schedule.
  - Phone and charger.
  - Burden’s collapsible bowl and supplies.
  - Identification and university documents.
- Julia reads the printed audition instructions aloud one final time, not because Brandi cannot access them, but because hearing the sequence helps everyone settle:
  - Check-in.
  - Studio orientation.
  - Ballet barre.
  - Centre work.
  - Contemporary phrase.
  - Improvisation.
  - Short interview.
- Brandi repeats the order from memory.
- Kiley asks whether she wants them waiting outside the building.
- Brandi says no.

> “If I can only audition while knowing all three of you are outside the door, I am not ready to live here.”

### Beat 5: Leaving the Hotel Room

- Brandi puts Burden’s harness on.
- The room falls quiet for a moment.
- Kiley kisses Brandi’s forehead.
- Julia checks only that Brandi has her phone and room key.
- Pandora takes Brandi’s free hand for the elevator ride.
- The gesture is sisterly, not directional; Burden remains responsible for guiding.
- At the lobby, Pandora releases Brandi’s hand before they step outside.

### Beat 6: Walking from the Hotel to UNLV

- The hotel is within walking distance of the university.
- The four women walk together through the Las Vegas morning.
- Burden guides Brandi while Kiley, Julia, and Pandora walk alongside without giving competing commands.
- Brandi tracks the route through:
  - Intersections.
  - Changes in pavement.
  - Traffic sound.
  - Heat and shade.
  - The scent of landscaping near campus.
- Julia gives occasional environmental information only when it is not already available through Burden or Brandi’s own orientation.
- Kiley grows quieter as they approach the agreed stopping point.

### Beat 7: The Family Stops

- Near the campus boundary, Brandi stops.
- She knows the remaining route from the mobility instructions and the university’s advance orientation information.
- Kiley asks:

> “Here?”

> “Here.”

- Brandi hugs Julia first, then Kiley, then Pandora.
- Pandora holds her longest.
- Kiley tells Brandi she can still change her mind and ask them to continue.
- Brandi answers:

> “I know. I’m not changing it.”

- The family does not secretly follow her.
- Kiley, Julia, and Pandora turn back toward the hotel after Brandi and Burden begin moving toward the university.
- Kiley looks back repeatedly. Julia does not stop her from looking, only from following.

### Beat 8: Brandi and Burden Enter the University

- Brandi continues alone except for Burden.
- Burden guides her across the remaining campus route to the Dance building.
- Brandi uses the university’s auditory and tactile landmarks rather than calling her family for reassurance.
- At check-in, staff address Brandi directly.
- Brandi provides her identification and confirms that Burden will rest at the studio edge during the audition.
- An audition assistant gives Brandi a concise orientation to the building and studio:
  - Check-in desk.
  - Dressing area.
  - Studio door.
  - Barre positions.
  - Centre dimensions.
  - Exits.
  - Burden’s resting location.
- The assistant offers an arm. Brandi declines and asks for directional information instead.

### Beat 9: Entering the Audition Studio

- Brandi changes into ballet shoes and removes her warm-up layers.
- Burden guides her to the assigned resting mat at the studio edge.
- Brandi lowers the harness handle and tells him to settle.
- The admissions committee sits at a table beyond the active floor.
- One committee member—the professor who will later teach Brandi’s first-year ballet class—speaks:

> “Whenever you’re ready, Brandi Jessica.”

- Brandi stores the voice immediately:
  - Calm.
  - Direct.
  - No exaggerated softness.
  - No question addressed to a missing parent.
- She walks to the barre independently and places her hand on it.

### Beat 10: Ballet Barre

- The audition begins with ballet barre.
- Instructions are given verbally with counts and directions.
- Brandi completes:
  - Pliés.
  - Tendus.
  - Dégagés.
  - Rond de jambe.
  - Adagio.
  - Grand battement.
- The committee watches technical placement, musicality, control, and response to correction.
- Brandi uses visual memory from before age seven, internal alignment, touch through the barre, and musical counts.
- A committee member gives a correction involving Brandi’s supporting hip.
- Brandi applies it on the next side without needing the correction repeated.
- The future professor makes a note.

### Beat 11: Centre Work

- Brandi receives a brief orientation to the centre starting point.
- The combination includes:
  - Port de bras.
  - Adagio.
  - Pirouette preparation.
  - Traveling steps.
  - A small jump sequence.
- Brandi asks one precise question about the final facing.
- Once answered, she performs without further accommodation.
- The committee notices that she does not move timidly to appear safe.
- She commits fully while maintaining control.

### Beat 12: Contemporary Phrase and Improvisation

- Brandi changes for the contemporary portion as required.
- The phrase uses floor work, weight transfer, and directional changes.
- She listens to the instructor’s breath, counts, and footfalls while learning.
- During improvisation, the prompt concerns resistance and release.
- Brandi creates movement through:
  - Pressure against the floor.
  - Suspended balance.
  - Sudden directional shifts.
  - Stillness held against the music.
- Her work is not evaluated as impressive “for a blind dancer.”
- The committee considers structure, responsiveness, physical intelligence, and artistic choice.

### Beat 13: The Interview

- Brandi sits before the admissions committee with Burden beside her.
- The future professor asks why she wants to study Dance at university.
- Brandi answers:

> “Because I am already a dancer. I want the training that will show me what kind.”

- The committee asks what access she requires.
- Brandi explains:
  - Verbal combinations.
  - Counts and directional language.
  - Advance studio orientation.
  - Accessible academic materials.
  - Consent before physical correction.
  - A safe place for Burden.
- She adds:

> “I am not asking for easier choreography or a different grading standard.”

- A committee member asks whether Brandi understands the physical demands of the program.
- Brandi answers by summarizing her years of ballet, gymnastics, swimming, aerial work, and adaptive training.
- She does not disclose the complete retinoblastoma story during the interview. Her medical documentation is already on file; the personal history remains hers.

### Beat 14: Leaving the Audition

- The committee thanks Brandi.
- The future professor repeats:

> “Thank you, Brandi Jessica.”

- Brandi recognizes the same direct tone and stores the voice more firmly.
- She puts Burden’s harness handle into working position.
- Burden guides her out of the studio, through the Dance building, and back toward the campus route.
- Brandi does not call her family until she has fully exited the university.

### Beat 15: The Committee Discusses Brandi

- After Brandi leaves, the admissions committee reviews her audition.
- The future ballet professor speaks in support of admission.
- The professor cites:
  - Strong musical response.
  - Existing classical foundation.
  - Fast application of correction.
  - Spatial discipline.
  - Contemporary movement intelligence.
  - Clear understanding of access needs.
  - Creative potential.
- The committee discusses the practical work required to make instruction accessible.
- The professor argues that accessibility is a teaching responsibility, not a reason to reject a qualified dancer.
- The committee selects Brandi for the program.
- Her independent entrance is not treated as an admission credential. The audition work earns the place.

### Beat 16: Walking Back to the Hotel

- Brandi and Burden walk from UNLV back to the hotel.
- The return route feels shorter because the audition is over.
- Brandi notices how tired her legs are only after leaving campus.
- She enters the hotel, crosses the lobby, and takes the elevator to the family’s room.
- Kiley, Julia, and Pandora have kept their promise not to approach the university.

### Beat 17: Back in the Hotel Room

- Brandi knocks using the room key rather than calling from the hallway.
- The door opens immediately. All three women were listening for her.
- Kiley pulls Brandi into a hug, then releases her before the embrace becomes restraint.
- Pandora asks the first useful question:

> “How did it feel?”

- Brandi answers:

> “Hard. Real. I want it.”

- Julia asks whether the committee addressed Brandi directly.
- Brandi says yes.
- She mentions one professor’s voice:

> “One of them said, ‘Whenever you’re ready, Brandi Jessica.’ I think I’ll remember that voice.”

- Burden drinks water and settles on his travel mat.
- The four women sit together on the two hotel beds while Brandi recounts the audition in her own order.
- Brandi then showers, changes, and goes with Kiley Rose, Julia, Pandora, and Burden to Paradise Cantina for dinner. See `brandi-jessica-post-audition-paradise-cantina-scene-outline.md`.

### Beat 18: The Acceptance

- The formal admission decision arrives later through the university’s accessible portal and email.
- Brandi opens it herself using VoiceOver.
- Kiley Rose, Julia, and Pandora are with her when the acceptance is read aloud.
- Brandi does not celebrate because the university allowed a blind student to enter.
- She celebrates because the committee judged her work and selected her for Dance.
- The professor’s audition voice becomes part of the memory that Brandi carries into the first ballet class months later.
