# Brandi Jessica Introduces Herself in Community Service — Session 3 Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Brandi Jessica Key (8), Pandora Paige Key (8), Community Service teacher, other campers in Community Service (including some from Birch Cabin)
- **Onstage animal:** None (Burden does not arrive until age 14)
- **Locations:** Community Service classroom/meeting space — French Woods (Hancock, New York)
- **When:** Session 3, early in the session (before Brandi tells the Ballet and Yoga teachers about Andrew). During one of the daily-chosen Community Service minor periods.
- **Status:** New scene outline
- **Tone:** Brave, matter-of-fact, quietly powerful, the first time Brandi tells peers her story on her own terms

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene captures the moment Brandi Jessica — in Community Service class, where the teacher asks campers to introduce each other — stands up and tells the other campers that she had Retinoblastoma, and that her mommies gave back to the hospital where she was being treated.

It establishes:
- That Brandi is choosing to tell her story out loud, on her own terms, to people her own age — not just to teachers who already know from a file.
- That Community Service is the context where this happens naturally — because the class is about giving back, and Brandi's connection to giving back is deeply personal.
- That Kiley Rose and Julia gave back to the hospital where Brandi was treated — they didn't just receive care, they contributed. This is the family philosophy in action: "there are no no's, only hows."
- That Brandi's willingness to say "Retinoblastoma" and "my mommies gave back" is a form of the same courage she shows at the barre, on the mat, and in the pool. She does not hide. She does not apologize.
- That this is different from Sloan and Rilee's confrontation — they questioned whether she belonged. Here, Brandi volunteers the information herself, before anyone can ask.
- The connection between Brandi's story and the purpose of Community Service: giving back is not abstract for her. It is what her family does when something hard happens.

## BEATS

### Beat 1: Community Service Class

- The Community Service class meets in a classroom space — chairs in a circle, a whiteboard, supplies for the day's project on a table by the window.
- Brandi Jessica and Pandora sit next to each other in the circle. Pandora is close enough to describe things if Brandi needs it, but she doesn't lean in or hover. She lets Brandi have her own space.
- The Community Service teacher is warm and organized. She explains what Community Service means at French Woods: helping the camp, helping the surrounding community, understanding why giving back matters.
- She says something like: "Before we start, I want us to get to know each other. We're going to go around the circle and introduce ourselves. Tell us your name, how old you are, and why you chose Community Service."
- The campers begin introducing themselves one by one. Some are shy. Some are enthusiastic. Most give simple answers — "I like helping people," "My mom volunteers," "It sounded fun."
- Pandora introduces herself: "I'm Pandora Paige Key. I'm eight. I'm Brandi's twin sister. I chose Community Service because Brandi chose it and I wanted to do the same ones as her. Also because I want to help people the way my mommies help people." She says it simply, without fanfare. The fact that she and Brandi are twins is obvious to everyone in the room — but Pandora says it out loud, the same way Brandi will say what she says. No hiding.

### Beat 2: Brandi's Turn

- The circle gets to Brandi Jessica. She is sitting in her chair with her cane folded beside her. Her face is turned toward the sound of the voices.
- She does not hesitate. She sits up straight and speaks clearly.
- Pandora does not lean over to whisper or prompt. She sits still. This is Brandi's.
- Brandi: "I'm Brandi Jessica Key. I'm eight. I had Retinoblastoma. It's eye cancer. They removed both of my eyes when I was seven. I can't see anything. I use a cane. My sister Pandora helps me sometimes. I do a lot of things by myself."
- Pandora hears Brandi say her own name — "my sister Pandora" — and something catches in her chest. Brandi is introducing her, not hiding behind her.
- The room goes quiet. Not uncomfortable-quiet. Listening-quiet.
- Brandi continues: "I chose Community Service because my mommies gave back to the hospital where I was being treated. When I was going through chemo and radiation, they didn't just sit there. They found ways to help. They gave back to the other families. They gave back to the nurses. They gave back to the hospital that was saving my life. That's what my family does. When something happens to us, we don't just take. We give back too."

### Beat 3: The Teacher's Response

- The Community Service teacher does not look surprised — she already knows about Brandi from the pre-summer briefing. But she does not act like this is old news either. She hears it the way Brandi is saying it: for the first time, out loud, to this room.
- Teacher: "Thank you, Brandi Jessica. That's a beautiful reason to be here."
- She does not over-praise. She does not make it a big moment. She treats it the way Brandi offered it — as a fact, not a performance.
- Then she moves on to the next camper. The circle continues.

### Beat 4: After the Introductions

- The class begins their project — maybe organizing supplies for a local shelter, or making cards for a nursing home, or planning a camp clean-up day.
- Brandi works alongside the other campers. She asks someone to describe what they're doing, then participates fully. She sorts items by touch. She folds. She organizes.
- No one treats her differently. No one avoids her. The introduction did what it was supposed to do — it answered the question before it could become a whisper.

### Beat 5: After Class — They Go Their Separate Ways

- After Community Service, the twins head to their next activity. Brandi goes to Gymnastics. Pandora goes to Spray Art.
- They walk together to the path where their routes split. Pandora describes the fork — left to the gymnastics building, right to the art area.
- Pandora: "You said my name."
- Brandi: "What?"
- Pandora: "When you introduced yourself. You said 'my sister Pandora helps me sometimes.' You said my name."
- Brandi: "Because you do."
- Pandora: "I know. But you didn't have to. You could have just said you use a cane and do things by yourself."
- Brandi: "That wouldn't be the whole truth."
- A pause. They stand at the fork in the path.
- Pandora: "How did it feel? Saying all of that?"
- Brandi thinks about it. Really thinks about it.
- Brandi: "Like I was the one telling the story. Not the one it happened to."
- Pandora: "You were."
- Pandora reaches over and finds Brandi's hand. She squeezes it once.
- Pandora: "I'll see you at dinner."
- Brandi: "I'll see you at dinner."
- Pandora laughs. "You can't see me at dinner."
- Brandi laughs too. "I'll hear you chewing."
- They go their separate ways — Brandi toward the gymnastics building, Pandora toward spray art.

### Beat 6: That Night — The Dark

- That night, in the dark of Birch Cabin, Pandora whispers from the top bunk.
- Pandora: "You told them today. The whole class. You said Retinoblastoma out loud."
- Brandi: "I did."
- Pandora: "How did it feel?"
- Brandi thinks about it. Really thinks about it.
- Brandi: "Like I was the one telling the story. Not the one it happened to."
- Pandora: "You were."
- Pandora reaches down from the top bunk and finds Brandi's hand.
- Pandora: "I'm glad I was there."
- Brandi squeezes her hand back.

## RELATION TO CONTINUITY

- This scene takes place during **Session 3**, during one of the daily-chosen Community Service minor periods.
- Community Service is one of Brandi Jessica's and Pandora Paige's daily-chosen minors in Session 3.
- The **Community Service teacher already knows everything about Brandi** from the pre-summer briefing (April phone call from Kiley Rose and Julia). Brandi is choosing to say it out loud, not being prompted.
- Brandi's reference to "chemo and radiation" follows the corrected treatment timeline: enucleation first (February 2052), then chemotherapy, then radiotherapy.
- Kiley Rose and Julia **giving back to the hospital** is a new detail about the mothers' actions during Brandi's treatment. This is consistent with the family philosophy: "there are no no's, only hows" and "anything and everything."
- Brandi's statement — "I'm tired of people wondering" — connects to the Sloan/Rilee confrontation in Session 2, where campers questioned her presence without understanding her story. Here, she takes control of the narrative.
- This scene takes place during **Session 3**, early in the session. It comes before Brandi tells the Ballet and Yoga teachers about Andrew (which happens seven days into Session 3), but Andrew is not discussed between the twins in this scene. Brandi and Pandora never talk about Andrew before Brandi tells the teachers.
- Brandi's statement — "Like I was the one telling the story. Not the one it happened to" — is a direct evolution from her earlier stance in Session 2 — "Not about me. To me." She is now not just responding to being questioned but proactively choosing disclosure.
