# Brandi Jessica’s Post-Audition Dinner at Paradise Cantina — Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Kiley Rose Key, Julia, Brandi Jessica Key (16), Pandora Paige Key (16)
- **Offstage / referenced:** Andrew, Brandi Jessica’s retinoblastoma medical team
- **Onstage animal:** Burden, Brandi Jessica’s Labernese guide dog
- **Locations:** The family’s Las Vegas hotel room; Paradise Cantina, 4480 S. Paradise Road, Las Vegas; the hotel bar and outdoor swimming pool
- **When:** November 2061, immediately after Brandi’s UNLV Dance audition
- **Setting continuity:** Paradise Cantina is treated as a future continuation or revival of the historic Las Vegas Mexican restaurant and cantina at the same address
- **Status:** Scene outline
- **Tone:** Relieved, tired, celebratory, sisterly, family-centered

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

After Brandi Jessica completes her UNLV Dance audition and walks back to the hotel with Burden, she cleans up and changes in the hotel room.

Kiley Rose and Julia then take Brandi and Pandora to Paradise Cantina for a relaxed post-audition dinner. The girls eat in the restaurant’s dining area with their mothers. Burden accompanies them and rests beneath the table.

The dinner allows Brandi to process the audition before receiving any admission decision. The family celebrates her work and independence without pretending they already know the outcome.

Brandi and Pandora become deeply emotional as they tell Kiley Rose and Julia that they are stupendous mothers. A homophobic couple in the booth beside them overhears the family and expresses hatred toward Kiley and Julia as a same-sex couple. The twins defend their mothers, while restaurant staff make clear that harassment is not welcome.

After returning to the hotel, Brandi and Pandora walk away from Kiley Rose and Julia so their mothers can enjoy romantic drinks at the hotel bar. The twins take Burden out to the swimming-pool area and have virgin strawberry daiquiris beside the water.

At the pool, Brandi and Pandora discuss how far they have come from Andrew’s kidnapping and from the effect **Brandi’s** retinoblastoma had on the family. Pandora never had retinoblastoma herself. Kiley Rose and Julia have a parallel conversation at the bar about how proud they are of both daughters—not simply for surviving family trauma, but for the lives they have built afterward.

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: Brandi Returns to the Hotel Room

- Brandi and Burden return from UNLV to the hotel independently.
- Kiley Rose, Julia, and Pandora have kept their promise to remain away from the audition.
- All three women were listening for Brandi’s room key in the door.
- Kiley embraces Brandi first, then releases her before the hug becomes restraint.
- Pandora asks:

> “How did it feel?”

- Brandi answers:

> “Hard. Real. I want it.”

- Julia asks whether Brandi wants to talk immediately or clean up first.
- Brandi chooses to clean up.

### Beat 2: Burden’s Post-Audition Routine

- Brandi removes Burden’s harness and checks him after the walk and audition.
- She gives him water and measures his meal according to his established schedule.
- Burden stretches, drinks, and settles on his travel mat.
- Kiley offers to handle his supplies.
- Brandi says she has it.
- Kiley accepts the answer.

### Beat 3: Brandi Cleans Up

- Brandi’s audition clothes are damp from exertion.
- She organizes them herself before showering so nothing becomes misplaced in the shared hotel room.
- Brandi showers and washes away the sweat, studio dust, and tension of the audition.
- Kiley, Julia, and Pandora remain in the room but give her privacy.
- After the shower, Brandi changes into comfortable dinner clothes.
- She secures her ocular prosthetics and checks her appearance by touch.
- Pandora gives visual information only when Brandi requests it.
- Brandi asks whether her hair is even at the back.
- Pandora answers directly and adjusts nothing without permission.

### Beat 4: Choosing Paradise Cantina

- While Brandi gets ready, Kiley Rose and Julia discuss dinner options.
- They choose **Paradise Cantina**, the Mexican restaurant and cantina on Paradise Road.
- It is casual enough for an exhausted dancer, lively enough to feel celebratory, and close to the hotel and university area.
- Kiley tells the twins:

> “No formal dinner. No speeches. Mexican food.”

- Pandora approves immediately.
- Brandi asks whether the restaurant will be too loud for conversation.
- Julia calls ahead and requests a quieter booth in the dining area with enough space for Burden beneath the table.

### Beat 5: Leaving the Hotel

- Brandi puts Burden’s harness back on.
- Kiley checks that both girls have their phones and room keys.
- Brandi carries the same small bag she normally uses with Burden’s collapsible bowl and supplies.
- The four women leave the hotel together.
- Kiley Rose and Julia take the girls to Paradise Cantina on foot, using the short Las Vegas walking route from the hotel.
- Burden guides Brandi while the family walks alongside.
- No one gives him competing commands.

### Beat 6: Arriving at Paradise Cantina

- The restaurant is lively but not yet at its loudest evening hour.
- The family hears music, conversation, dishes, and the movement of servers.
- The host begins speaking to Kiley about where “she and the dog” can sit.
- Kiley redirects the host:

> “Ask Brandi. He is her guide dog.”

- Brandi confirms that a booth with clear floor space will work.
- Burden guides her to the table, then settles beneath it and out of the server path.
- The family sits in the dining area rather than at the bar.

### Beat 7: Chips, Salsa, and Silence First

- Chips and salsa arrive.
- Brandi asks for five minutes in which no one asks about the audition.
- Kiley, Julia, and Pandora agree.
- The first minutes are deliberately ordinary:
  - Passing chips.
  - Identifying the salsa bowls.
  - Ordering water and soft drinks for the girls.
  - Listening to the restaurant around them.
- Brandi’s shoulders gradually lower.
- The audition stops feeling as though it is still happening inside her body.

### Beat 8: Reading the Menu

- The server offers a printed menu.
- Brandi asks Julia to read the main sections aloud.
- Julia reads without selecting for her:
  - Tacos.
  - Burritos.
  - Enchiladas.
  - Nachos.
  - Combination plates.
  - Sides.
- Pandora describes any visual presentation only when Brandi asks.
- Brandi chooses her own meal.
- The family also orders shared food for the table so the dinner feels communal.

### Beat 9: Brandi Begins Talking

- After ordering, Brandi says she is ready to discuss the audition.
- Julia asks a technical question first:

> “Which section was strongest?”

- Brandi says the contemporary improvisation surprised her most.
- Kiley asks which section was hardest.
- Brandi says centre work in an unfamiliar room required the most concentration.
- Pandora asks what the committee sounded like.
- Brandi remembers one professor’s voice:

> “One of them said, ‘Whenever you’re ready, Brandi Jessica.’ I think I’ll remember that voice.”

- Brandi explains that the professor addressed her directly and never asked where her mother was.
- Kiley becomes emotional but keeps listening.

### Beat 10: The Walk into UNLV

- Kiley asks whether Brandi was frightened after the family turned back.
- Brandi says yes.
- She describes:
  - Burden finding the remaining campus route.
  - Checking in by herself.
  - Orienting to the studio.
  - Leaving Burden at the studio edge.
  - Putting her hand on the audition barre.
- Brandi says:

> “I needed to know I could enter without all of you before I asked them to let me stay for four years.”

- Kiley admits that turning around was one of the hardest parts of the trip.
- Brandi answers:

> “Following me would have been harder for me.”

- Kiley says she understands.

### Beat 11: “You Are Stupendous Mothers”

- Brandi thinks about everything Kiley Rose and Julia did during the audition trip:
  - Traveled to Las Vegas with both daughters.
  - Shared the hotel room.
  - Ate breakfast together.
  - Walked with Brandi toward the university.
  - Stopped when Brandi asked them to stop.
  - Returned to the hotel instead of secretly following her.
  - Waited without trying to control the outcome.
- Brandi’s voice begins to shake.

> “You came all this way because I needed you, and then you trusted me enough to let me go in alone.”

- Pandora becomes emotional at the same time. She reaches across the table for Brandi’s hand.
- The twins begin crying together—not from fear about the audition result, but from recognition of the way they have been mothered.
- Pandora tells Kiley and Julia:

> “You are stupendous mothers. Both of you.”

- Brandi repeats it:

> “Stupendous. You never make loving us mean controlling us.”

- Kiley Rose becomes tearful. Julia’s composure breaks more quietly.
- Kiley tells the girls they never owe gratitude for being loved.
- Brandi answers that gratitude is not a debt; she wants her mothers to hear the truth.
- Kiley and Julia hold hands on the table. Julia leans close and kisses Kiley’s temple.

### Beat 12: The Homophobic Couple in the Next Booth

- A couple is seated in the booth directly beside the family.
- They have been watching Kiley Rose and Julia, listening to the twins call both women their mothers, and reacting with visible disgust.
- The couple assumes Kiley and Julia are lesbians. Kiley is bisexual, but the distinction would not matter to people whose hostility is directed at any same-sex family.
- One person in the adjoining booth says loudly enough for the family to hear:

> “This is supposed to be a family restaurant.”

- The other replies that two women should not be raising girls and complains about having to see them show affection in public.
- Brandi hears the words immediately. Pandora sees the couple staring.
- Brandi asks Pandora quietly where the voices are coming from. Pandora identifies the booth beside them.
- Kiley tells the twins:

> “You do not have to defend us.”

- Brandi answers:

> “I know. I want to.”

- Without raising her voice, Brandi turns toward the neighbouring booth.

> “It is a family restaurant. This is my family.”

- Pandora adds:

> “And they are stupendous mothers.”

- The couple responds with another hostile remark about lesbians.
- Julia calmly asks them to stop speaking about the family.
- A server has heard the exchange and brings the manager.
- The manager tells the neighbouring couple that Paradise Cantina serves families, including same-sex families, and that harassment of other guests is not permitted.
- The couple is given a choice: stop or leave.
- They choose to leave, angry that the restaurant will not support their prejudice.
- The manager apologizes to Kiley, Julia, Brandi, and Pandora.
- Kiley says the staff did the right thing by addressing the people causing the problem rather than asking the family to move.
- Burden remains quiet beneath the table throughout the confrontation.
- The twins are still tearful, but the couple does not take ownership of the family’s emotional moment.

### Beat 13: Dinner Arrives

- The food interrupts the emotional intensity at the right moment.
- The server identifies each plate.
- Julia describes Brandi’s plate using a clock-face orientation only because Brandi asks.
- Burden remains quiet beneath the table.
- The family eats while discussing lighter audition details:
  - The studio floor.
  - Brandi’s warm-up.
  - A committee member dropping a pen.
  - Whether the hotel breakfast was enough food.
- Pandora makes Brandi laugh by imitating Kiley’s expression when the family was ordered to stop at the campus boundary.

### Beat 14: No One Predicts the Result

- Kiley wants to say that Brandi must have been accepted.
- Julia stops that certainty before it becomes pressure.

> “She did the audition. The decision belongs to the committee now.”

- Brandi appreciates the distinction.
- She does not want the family treating admission as guaranteed or rejection as proof that she failed.
- Brandi says:

> “I know what I gave them. Now they decide whether it belongs there.”

### Beat 15: A Quiet Family Toast

- Kiley raises her water glass rather than making a public speech.
- The others do the same.

> “To doing the work before knowing the answer.”

- Julia, Brandi, and Pandora repeat it.
- The toast celebrates the audition, not an imagined acceptance.
- Brandi becomes emotional again and smiles through the last of her tears. She reaches across the table and finds Pandora’s hand.

### Beat 16: Paradise Cantina Becomes Part of the Memory

- The family remains at the table after finishing the meal.
- Brandi stores the evening through sound and texture:
  - Music from the dining room.
  - The rough edge of the table.
  - Cold condensation on her glass.
  - Pandora’s fingers around hers.
  - Burden’s back beneath her resting foot.
- The empty neighbouring booth remains beside them, but the homophobic couple does not become the centre of the memory.
- Years later, Paradise Cantina will remain part of the audition memory because Brandi and Pandora called Kiley Rose and Julia stupendous mothers, the family allowed uncertainty to exist without trying to solve it, and the restaurant defended their right to share the evening in peace.

### Beat 17: Walking Back to the Hotel

- The family walks back to the hotel together.
- Burden guides Brandi through the Las Vegas night.
- Brandi’s legs are tired from the audition, but she refuses an unnecessary vehicle ride for a short route she has already walked.
- Kiley and Julia let the twins talk ahead of them.
- Pandora asks what Brandi will do if she is accepted.
- Brandi says:

> “Come back and learn the route without all of you.”

### Beat 18: Returning to the Hotel Lobby

- The family returns to the hotel together.
- As they cross the lobby, Pandora notices the hotel bar and the quieter tables arranged for couples.
- Brandi hears the change in music, glassware, and conversation and understands where they are.
- The twins exchange a quick private whisper.
- Brandi and Pandora tell Kiley Rose and Julia that they are going to the outdoor swimming-pool area for a while.
- Kiley asks whether they are certain; the family has spent the whole day together.
- Pandora answers:

> “That is why you two should have one romantic drink without us.”

- Brandi adds:

> “Stupendous mothers are still allowed to have a date.”

- Kiley and Julia become emotional again, but the twins are already smiling.

### Beat 19: Kiley Rose and Julia at the Hotel Bar

- Kiley Rose and Julia accept the invitation rather than insisting the daughters remain with them.
- They choose a quiet table in the hotel bar where they can see the route toward the pool entrance.
- The twins do not need supervision from that table; its position simply lets the mothers know where the girls went.
- Kiley and Julia order romantic drinks of their choice.
- They hold hands and process the day privately:
  - Brandi entering UNLV without them.
  - The audition uncertainty.
  - The twins calling them stupendous mothers.
  - The homophobic couple at Paradise Cantina.
- Julia tells Kiley that the cruelty in the neighbouring booth does not outweigh the daughters’ love.
- Kiley says letting Brandi walk away toward the audition was painful, but watching her return independently was worth the fear.
- The hotel setting reminds Kiley of Andrew’s kidnapping: a hotel was once a place where Brandi was trapped and taken from the family. Tonight, Brandi returned to a hotel by choice after walking independently into a university audition.
- Kiley and Julia discuss how both twins have healed:
  - Brandi survived Andrew’s captivity and the retinoblastoma crisis that took her sight.
  - Pandora survived the terror of Brandi’s disappearance and the fear of watching her twin face cancer and bilateral enucleation.
  - Brandi learned to live, dance, travel, and imagine a future after losing both eyes.
  - Pandora was screened and found healthy. She never had retinoblastoma and does not carry a medical debt for having normal vision.
- Julia says healing does not mean Andrew or **Brandi’s** retinoblastoma became unimportant. It means those events no longer control every room the twins enter.
- Kiley tells Julia:

> “I am so proud of both of them. Not only because they survived. Because of who they chose to become afterward.”

- Julia raises her glass to Brandi and Pandora at the pool, even though the twins cannot see the gesture from outside.
- Their drinks become a quiet date inside the larger family trip.

### Beat 20: The Twins Walk Out to the Swimming Pool

- Brandi puts her hand on Burden’s harness handle.
- Pandora walks beside her, not in front of her.
- The twins leave the lobby bar area and follow the hotel route to the outdoor swimming pool.
- Burden guides Brandi through the exterior doors and onto the pool deck.
- Pandora describes the pool lighting, water, empty lounge chairs, and available tables.
- They choose a table safely away from the pool edge with enough room for Burden beneath it.
- Brandi gives Burden water from his collapsible bowl.

### Beat 21: Virgin Strawberry Daiquiris by the Pool

- The twins order **virgin strawberry daiquiris** from the pool service counter.
- They state clearly that both drinks must be nonalcoholic.
- The frozen drinks arrive in plastic poolside glasses.
- Pandora places Brandi’s drink directly beside her right hand and tells her where the straw is.
- The twins touch their glasses together.

> “To the audition?” Pandora asks.

> “To stupendous mothers,” Brandi answers.

- They drink beside the illuminated pool while Burden rests under the table.
- Brandi listens to the water and says she can still hear the audition counts in her head.
- Pandora asks whether Brandi is frightened of moving to Las Vegas if accepted.
- Brandi says yes.
- Pandora tells her that fear did not stop her from walking into the university alone.
- Brandi answers that fear may be allowed to come with her; it simply does not get to choose the direction.
- The sound of the hotel pool makes Brandi think about Andrew and the hotel where he held her captive as a child.
- Brandi says:

> “A hotel used to belong to Andrew in my head. Tonight it belongs to us.”

- Pandora admits that Andrew harmed her too, even though he kidnapped Brandi:
  - She lived through Brandi’s disappearance.
  - She feared that her twin would never return.
  - She carried the helplessness of being the sister left at home.
- Brandi tells Pandora she never needed to minimize that pain simply because Andrew physically took only one twin.
- The conversation turns to Brandi’s retinoblastoma:
  - Brandi lost both eyes but kept her visual memory, her dancing, and her life.
  - Pandora was screened because her identical twin had cancer, but the examination found no tumors or retinal disease.
  - Pandora never had retinoblastoma and never received treatment for it.
  - Pandora was still frightened by Brandi’s illness and affected by the possibility of losing her twin.
  - Brandi tells Pandora that witnessing family medical trauma does not make the diagnosis Pandora’s own.
- Pandora says:

> “Andrew and what retinoblastoma did to you were enormous. They used to feel like the whole world.”

- Brandi answers:

> “They are still important. But look how far away we have come from them.”

- Pandora looks toward Brandi, then remembers that Brandi cannot see the expression. She squeezes Brandi’s hand instead.
- Both twins become emotional again and cry quietly beside the pool.
- Burden lifts his head and rests it against Brandi’s leg.
- Brandi says healing did not return her eyes or erase Andrew. Healing gave both twins lives larger than what happened to them.
- They recognize the distance they have traveled:
  - From captivity to choosing where they go.
  - From oncology rooms to a university Dance audition.
  - From fear of separation to giving their mothers private time without panic.
  - From being defined by survival to making adult plans.
- The twins discuss the homophobic couple.
- Pandora admits that she wanted to scream at them.
- Brandi says the strongest answer was the truth:

> “They are our mothers. They are stupendous. That couple does not get a vote.”

- The poolside conversation belongs only to the twins. Kiley and Julia remain at the bar and do not interrupt.

### Beat 22: Back in the Shared Hotel Room

- After finishing the virgin strawberry daiquiris, Brandi and Pandora return to the lobby with Burden.
- Kiley Rose and Julia have finished their romantic drinks.
- The four women reunite without anyone treating the short separation as abandonment.
- Kiley tells the twins that she and Julia spent part of their romantic drink discussing how proud they are of both daughters.
- Brandi and Pandora reveal that they spent part of their poolside drink discussing the same history: Andrew, Brandi’s retinoblastoma, and how far the family has come. Pandora makes clear that she never had the disease.
- The parallel conversations make all four women emotional.
- Kiley says:

> “You were extraordinary children. We are even prouder of the young women you have become.”

- Julia hugs both twins and tells them that healing belongs to them; it is not a performance they owe their mothers.
- They return to the same hotel room.
- Brandi removes Burden’s harness and prepares him for the night.
- Kiley and Julia share one bed; Brandi and Pandora share the other.
- In the dark, Pandora asks whether Brandi still wants UNLV after experiencing the audition.
- Brandi answers:

> “More than I did this morning.”

- The family goes to sleep without knowing the committee’s decision.
- The acceptance arrives later through the accessible university portal.
