# Pandora Paige's Session 2 Classes — French Woods (Age 8, First Summer 2053)

1. Breakfast
2. **Major 1:** Gymnastics
3. **Minor 1:** Gymnastics
4. **Minor 2:** Waterfront Recreation
5. Lunch
6. **Major 2:** Circus
7. **Minor 3:** Gymnastics
8. **Major 3:** Swimming Lessons
9. Dinner

**Note on minors:** Campers can choose new minors every day. Pandora chooses Gymnastics for two of her three minor slots because she loves it and minors are flexible daily — she can always swap one out to try something different on any given day. Waterfront Recreation gives her time at the lake.

---

## MAJOR 1: GYMNASTICS

**Location:** Gymnastics building — separate structure on the other side of camp from the dance pavilion

Pandora majors in Gymnastics at the camp gym. She works on tumbling, balance beam, floor routines, and vault. Gymnastics is one of the Key family's two core physical languages (mirroring her aunt Jessica Isabella's gymnastics). Pandora is sighted, naturally athletic, and fearless — she attacks each apparatus with energy and enthusiasm.

- **Floor Exercise:** Round-off, back handspring, back tuck. She is working on connecting multiple tumbling passes. Her landings are clean and powerful.
- **Balance Beam:** She walks the beam end to end confidently and is working on simple leaps and turns. She dismounts with control. She is the one who guided Brandi Jessica onto the beam on Visiting Day.
- **Vault:** She is learning the approach, the spring, and the basic vault over the table. She runs hard and commits fully.
- **Bars (Uneven Bars):** She is working on the glide kip and basic circling elements. This is her weakest apparatus but she does not avoid it.

Pandora's role as Brandi's helper extends into Gymnastics — when Brandi visits the gymnastics building, Pandora describes the apparatus, the spacing, and the layout. She positioned Brandi's hand on the beam so Brandi could feel its width and texture before her first walk.

## MINOR 1: GYMNASTICS

Pandora chooses Gymnastics as her first minor as well. This gives her a second period of gymnastics practice each morning — extra time on the apparatus she wants to develop. Because minors can change daily, she can swap this out for a different activity on any day she wants to try something new.

---

## MINOR 2: WATERFRONT RECREATION

**Location:** Camp lake and waterfront area

Waterfront Recreation takes place at the lake. This is separate from Swimming Lessons (which are in the swimming pool). Waterfront Recreation includes open swimming, dock jumping, water games, canoeing, and free time on the water.

- Pandora is a strong swimmer and loves the lake. She dives off the dock, races other campers, and spends the period in and out of the water.
- This is also where she and Brandi overlap — Brandi's Waterfront Recreation minor is at the lake too, so the twins have time together in the water even when their majors are different.
- Pandora describes the lake environment for Brandi: the position of the dock, the other swimmers, the shoreline. In the water, Brandi doesn't need as much help — she reads the water pressure and acoustic echoes — but Pandora is always nearby.

---

## MAJOR 2: CIRCUS

**Location:** Circus pavilion — high ceilings, rosin and canvas smell, silks and trapeze rigged overhead

Pandora majors in Circus. She works on aerial silks, static trapeze, and basic circus apparatus. She is drawn to the height, the spectacle, and the physical challenge of performing above the ground.

- **Aerial Silks:** She climbs hand over hand, wraps the silks around her legs and waist, and performs basic drops. She is working on climbing higher and adding rotations to her wraps. She describes the feeling as "like flying."
- **Static Trapeze:** She learns basic poses on the trapeze bar — sitting, standing, hanging by her knees. She is building upper body strength and learning to trust the rigging.
- **Ground Circus Skills:** Juggling, balancing, acro — she dabbles in the ground-based circus arts between aerial sessions.

Pandora's circus skills connect to her gymnastics — the body awareness, the spatial orientation, and the fearlessness translate directly between the two disciplines. Her aerial work also gives her a vocabulary for describing height and position to Brandi, who cannot see the silks or trapeze but wants to understand what her sister experiences.

---

## MINOR 3: GYMNASTICS

Pandora chooses Gymnastics for her third minor slot as well. This gives her an additional practice period in the late afternoon, after Circus and before Swimming Lessons. She uses this time to work on whichever apparatus she wants to improve — sometimes beam, sometimes floor, sometimes bars.

Because minors can change daily, she occasionally swaps this slot out for a different activity when she wants to explore something new — Yoga, Creative Writing, or another offering. But Gymnastics is her default.

---

## MAJOR 3: SWIMMING LESSONS

**Location:** Swimming pool (separate from the lake)

Pandora takes Swimming Lessons as a major in the camp swimming pool. She works on strokes, breathing technique, diving, and water safety with the camp instructors. This is also a shared major with Brandi Jessica — both twins have Swimming Lessons, so they are in the pool together for this period.

- **Strokes:** Freestyle, backstroke, breaststroke. Pandora is a natural swimmer with strong strokes.
- **Diving:** She is learning to dive from the pool deck and from the low diving board.
- **Racing:** She races Brandi in the pool. She usually wins, but only by a stroke — Brandi's flip turns are often cleaner.

In the pool, Pandora helps Brandi with orientation — describing the lane positions, the location of the wall, the position of other swimmers. But Brandi moves freely in the water, reading pressure and sound, and often needs less help here than anywhere else at camp.

---

## PANDORA'S ROLE HELPING BRANDI JESSICA ACROSS ALL CLASSES

Pandora's helping role extends beyond her own class schedule. Because her majors and Brandi's majors are different for most of the day, she helps Brandi in the following ways:

- **Before classes:** She walks Brandi to the dance pavilion before heading to Gymnastics. She describes the room layout so Brandi can orient herself.
- **Between classes:** When their schedules overlap (Waterfront Recreation at the lake, Swimming Lessons in the pool), Pandora is Brandi's co-pilot — describing the environment, helping with navigation, providing visual translation.
- **After classes:** In the cabin at night, Pandora narrates the entire day back to Brandi — describing every costume, every expression, every audience reaction, every visual detail Brandi could not see.
- **In Gymnastics specifically:** When Brandi visits the gymnastics building, Pandora guides her through the space, describes the apparatus, and positions Brandi's hands so she can feel the equipment before attempting it.
- **In Circus specifically:** Pandora describes the silks and trapeze in vivid detail so Brandi can imagine the height, the feeling, and the spectacle — even though Brandi does not take Circus herself.
- **In Swimming Lessons and Waterfront Recreation:** The twins are together in the water. Pandora describes the pool layout, the lane lines, the dock, and the other swimmers. Brandi often needs less help in the water than on land.
