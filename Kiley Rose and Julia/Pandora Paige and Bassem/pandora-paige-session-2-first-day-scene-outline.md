# Pandora Paige's First Day of Classes — Session 2 Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Pandora Paige Key (8), Brandi Jessica Key (8), Gymnastics Instructor, Circus Instructor, Swimming Instructor, Maya (counselor, mid-20s), other campers in Gymnastics, Circus, and Swimming
- **Onstage animal:** None (Burden does not arrive until age 14)
- **Locations:** Birch Cabin, path to dance pavilion, gymnastics building, lake/waterfront area, cafeteria, circus pavilion, swimming pool — French Woods (Hancock, New York)
- **When:** The first day of activities of Session 2, Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige's first summer at French Woods (July 2053). The day after the twins arrived and chose their schedules.
- **Status:** New scene outline
- **Tone:** Nervous excitement, bold discovery, the strangeness of being apart from her twin, Pandora finding her own footing

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene follows Pandora Paige through her first day of classes at French Woods — the same day Brandi faces Sloan and Rilee alone. While Brandi's story is about defending her right to belong, Pandora's is about being separated from her twin for the first extended period and discovering who she is when Brandi is not in the room.

It establishes:
- That Pandora's first day is full of energy, physical achievement, and joy — she is in her element in Gymnastics, Circus, and the water.
- That being away from Brandi is the hardest part of Pandora's day, even though she does not say it aloud.
- That Pandora checks on Brandi instinctively — she walks her to the dance pavilion, she watches for her at Waterfront Recreation, she looks for her between classes.
- That Pandora has her own relationships with her instructors and her own accomplishments that are separate from her role as Brandi's helper.
- That when Pandora and Brandi reunite at the end of the day, the full weight of what happened to Brandi alone in Ballet and Yoga lands on Pandora for the first time.

## BEATS

### Beat 1: Morning — Walking Brandi to Ballet

- The cabin is waking up. Pandora is dressed first, as usual. Brandi is organizing her things by touch — running her fingers over the Braille labels on her trunk.
- Pandora: "Ready?"
- Brandi: "Ready."
- They walk together from Birch Cabin toward the dance pavilion. Pandora describes the campus as they go — she has already memorized the layout from the brochure and from walking around during orientation.
- At the pavilion entrance, Pandora pauses. She gives Brandi the quick description she always does: "Big space. Sprung floor. Barres along the left and right walls. Mirror across the front. About ten girls already inside."
- She hesitates. "Do you want me to stay for the first few minutes?"
- Brandi shakes her head. "I've got it. Go to Gymnastics. You'll be late."
- Pandora squeezes Brandi's hand once, hard. Then she lets go.
- She stands at the pavilion entrance and watches Brandi walk in alone — cane tapping, steps careful, chin up. Brandi does not look back.
- Pandora turns and heads toward the gymnastics building on the other side of camp. It is the first time she has walked anywhere at French Woods without Brandi beside her.
- The path feels wider than it did before. She can hear more birds. She can see the lake through the trees. It is strange to notice these things without having to describe them out loud.

### Beat 2: Major 1 — Gymnastics (First Time)

- Pandora arrives at the gymnastics building. It is a large, separate structure with high ceilings, spring floors, mats, beams, bars, and a vault runway. It smells like chalk and rubber.
- The Gymnastics instructor is a compact, muscular woman in her thirties who introduces herself and asks about each camper's experience level.
- Pandora: "I've done some gymnastics at home. My aunt Jessica Isabella is a gymnast — she taught me basic tumbling. I can do a round-off back handspring."
- The instructor: "Let's see it."
- Pandora runs, hurdles, and throws the round-off back handspring on the spring floor. It is clean, powerful, and confident.
- The instructor nods. "Good. We'll build from there."
- The class warms up together — stretching, conditioning, running laps. Then they split into rotations. Pandora starts on floor.
- She is in her element. The spring floor is bouncy and responsive under her feet. The instructor leads them through tumbling progressions — round-off, back handspring, back tuck. Pandora lands the back tuck on her first attempt.
- Other campers in the class are impressed. One girl, a returning camper named Wren, says: "You're really good. Is this your first summer?"
- Pandora: "Yes. My sister and I just got here."
- Wren: "What's your sister taking?"
- Pandora: "Ballet. Pointe. Swimming. Yoga."
- A small knot forms in her chest. Brandi is in Ballet right now. Alone. Without Pandora to describe the room or adjust her arm at the barre.
- She pushes the thought away and goes again. Round-off, back handspring, back tuck, stuck landing.

### Beat 3: Minor 1 — Gymnastics (Second Period)

- Pandora stays in the gymnastics building for her second period — she chose Gymnastics as her first minor too. The instructor moves the group to the balance beam.
- The beam is about four feet high, four inches wide, padded leather on top. Pandora mounts and walks it end to end without hesitation.
- The instructor teaches them a simple leap on the beam — step, step, leap, step. Pandora tries it. Her leap is small but controlled.
- Instructor: "You commit to the movement. That's the most important thing on beam. Don't hesitate."
- Pandora: "I don't hesitate."
- Instructor: "I can tell."
- She practices the leap over and over. Each time it gets a little higher, a little more confident. She dismounts with a small jump and sticks the landing.
- She is breathing hard. Her hands are chalky. She is happy.
- But she is also counting the periods in her head. Brandi is in Yoga right now. Second period. Alone again.

### Beat 4: Minor 2 — Waterfront Recreation (The Lake)

- Pandora walks to the lake for Waterfront Recreation. This is the first period where she and Brandi overlap.
- She scans the waterfront, looking for her twin. The dock, the shoreline, the other campers —
- Brandi is already there. Standing at the edge of the dock with her cane folded, already in her swimsuit — she wore it under her dance clothes for Ballet and Yoga, so she only had to remove her outer layer when she got to the lake. Her face is turned toward the water. She is listening to the lake.
- Pandora's chest loosens. She jogs over.
- Pandora: "Hey. How was Ballet? How was Yoga?"
- Brandi: "Fine. I'll tell you later."
- She says it quickly, without elaboration. Pandora notices, but she does not press. They are in public. Other campers are nearby.
- They swim together in the lake. Pandora describes the dock and the other swimmers, but Brandi doesn't need much help in the water — she reads the pressure and the sound and moves freely.
- They dive off the dock together. They race to the buoy. Brandi loses, but not by much.
- For this one period, the twins are together again, and the morning feels normal.

### Beat 5: Lunch — The Cafeteria

- They walk to the cafeteria together. The dining hall is loud and crowded — campers, counselors, the smell of grilled cheese and tomato soup. Each cabin eats at its own table, and Birch Cabin's table is near the window.
- Brandi navigates the line by herself, counting steps from the door to the serving area. Pandora describes the food options. Brandi makes her own choices.
- They sit down at the Birch Cabin table. Sloan and Rilee are already there, along with the other girls from the cabin. Maya sits at the end of the table.
- Pandora eats quickly — she is hungry from two periods of gymnastics. Brandi eats more slowly, picking at her food.
- Pandora: "Are you okay?"
- Brandi: "I'm okay."
- Pandora: "You're sure?"
- Brandi: "I'll tell you tonight. After Swimming."
- Pandora wants to push. She wants to ask right now. But she sees something in Brandi's face — a quiet steadiness that she recognizes. It is the same look Brandi gets when she has done something hard and is not ready to talk about it yet.
- Pandora nods. "Okay. Tonight."
- She finishes her grilled cheese and thinks about the silks. This afternoon is Circus. She has never been on aerial silks before. She cannot wait.

### Beat 6: Major 2 — Circus (First Time)

- Pandora walks to the circus pavilion. It is a tall, open structure with high ceilings — the highest building on campus. Silks hang from the rigging in long red columns. A static trapeze is mounted at one end. The floor is covered with thick crash mats. The air smells of rosin and canvas.
- The Circus instructor is a tall man with calloused hands and a calm voice. He explains the basics of aerial work: grip, wrap, climb, control.
- Pandora looks up at the silks. They are high — higher than the balance beam, higher than the uneven bars. They swayed slightly in the draft.
- She has never been afraid of height.
- The instructor teaches them to climb — hand over hand, feet wrapping the silk in a figure-eight pattern. Most of the new campers struggle to get more than a few feet off the ground. Pandora climbs halfway up on her first attempt.
- Instructor: "Good grip. Strong legs. Now come down slowly — control the descent."
- She slides down, the silk smooth against her legs and palms. Her hands sting. She does not care.
- They practice basic wraps and a simple drop — wrap the silk around the waist, lean back, let the fabric catch. The first drop is terrifying. The second is thrilling. By the third, Pandora is grinning.
- Wren from Gymnastics is in this class too. She watches Pandora climb and says: "You're not scared of anything, are you?"
- Pandora: "I'm scared of lots of things. Just not this."
- She does not say what she is scared of. She is scared of being in this pavilion while Brandi is in a Yoga studio across camp, holding a pose, alone, without her.

### Beat 7: Minor 3 — Gymnastics (Third Period)

- Back to the gymnastics building for her third period of Gymnastics. Pandora is tired now — her hands are raw from the silks, her legs are sore from the beam, but she is not done.
- The instructor gives her time on the uneven bars, her weakest apparatus. She practices the glide kip — the swing, the pull, the turnover. She misses it three times before she catches it.
- Instructor: "You're strong. You'll get it."
- Pandora: "I know I'll get it. I just have to do it enough times."
- She tries again. And again. On the fifth attempt, she catches the kip cleanly.
- She is so focused she almost forgets to check the clock. Brandi is finishing Yoga. Her last period alone.
- One more class after this. Swimming. They will be together again.

### Beat 8: Major 3 — Swimming Lessons (The Pool)

- Pandora walks to the swimming pool. Brandi is already at the edge, her cane folded, her swimsuit on — she has been wearing it under her clothes since Ballet this morning. Her face is turned toward the sound of the water.
- The pool is different from the lake — enclosed, chlorinated, with clear lanes and painted lines. Swimming Lessons are here, in the pool, not at the lake.
- Pandora sits beside Brandi on the pool deck.
- Pandora: "Last class. We made it through the first day."
- Brandi smiles. "We made it."
- The Swimming instructor divides them into lanes by ability. The twins end up in adjacent lanes.
- They work on strokes, breathing, and diving. Pandora practices backstroke. Brandi works on freestyle and flip turns.
- At one point, the instructor has them race a 25-meter sprint. Pandora wins, but only by a stroke — Brandi's flip turn at the wall is cleaner and faster.
- Pandora, panting: "Your flip turn is annoying."
- Brandi: "Your stroke is annoying."
- They are both grinning.
- In the pool, for the last period of the day, everything is easy. No one is questioning whether they belong. No one is complaining. It is just two sisters swimming laps, and the water does not care that one of them cannot see it.

### Beat 9: After Swimming — The Walk Back to the Cabin

- They walk back to Birch Cabin together after Swimming. The sun is low. The camp paths are full of campers heading to dinner.
- Pandora: "Tell me now. What happened in Ballet? What happened in Yoga?"
- Brandi tells her. All of it. Sloan interrupting in the middle of the across-the-floor combination. Rilee interrupting while she was holding warrior two. Her own responses. The instructors backing her.
- Pandora stops walking. She is two steps ahead of Brandi before she realizes it and turns back.
- Pandora: "They said that to you? In the middle of class? While you were dancing? While you were holding a pose?"
- Brandi: "Yes."
- Pandora: "And I wasn't there."
- Brandi: "You were here. You were doing Gymnastics and Circus. Where you were supposed to be."
- Pandora's fists are clenched at her sides. She wants to go find Sloan and Rilee right now. She wants to walk into the gymnastics building and the Yoga studio and tell them exactly what she thinks.
- Brandi hears the change in Pandora's breathing. She reaches out and finds Pandora's hand.
- Brandi: "Pandora. I handled it."
- Pandora: "You shouldn't have had to."
- Brandi: "Maybe not. But I did. I told them they could ask me how I do things. Not about me, to me. The teachers backed me. I held warrior two the whole time. I didn't drop the pose."
- Pandora is quiet. She is furious and proud and guilty all at once.
- Pandora: "Tomorrow, I'm going to check on you between every class."
- Brandi: "You don't have to do that."
- Pandora: "I want to."
- Brandi: "Then I'll look forward to it."
- They walk the rest of the way to the cabin hand in hand.

### Beat 10: Dinner in the Cafeteria

- Dinner is loud and ordinary. Spaghetti and garlic bread and salad. Campers trading stories about their first day. Each cabin eats at its own table, and Birch Cabin sits together as usual — Sloan, Rilee, Brandi, Pandora, Dot, and the other girls, with Maya at the end.
- Sloan and Rilee do not speak to Brandi. They do not look at her. They talk to each other and to the other girls, but a careful silence sits around Brandi's end of the table. Brandi does not know they are avoiding her gaze — she cannot see it. But Pandora sees it. She sits slightly closer to Brandi than usual.
- Brandi eats calmly. She navigates the cafeteria tray by touch — she has already mapped this space.
- Pandora tells Brandi about her day. About Gymnastics — the back tuck, the balance beam, the glide kip she finally caught. About Circus — the silks, the height, the drop. About Swimming — the race she won by a stroke.
- Brandi: "What does it feel like? The silks?"
- Pandora thinks about it. Really thinks about it.
- Pandora: "It feels like the whole world gets small under you, and the only thing that's real is the silk in your hands and the air around you. And then you drop, and for one second you're not holding anything, and then the silk catches you and you're flying."
- Brandi: "That sounds beautiful."
- Pandora: "Next summer I'll describe it so well you'll feel like you're up there with me."
- Brandi smiles. "Promise?"
- Pandora: "Promise."

### Beat 11: That Night — Birch Cabin

- Lights out. The cabin is dark. Sloan and Rilee are in their bunks, silent.
- Pandora whispers from the top bunk: "Brandi?"
- Brandi whispers back: "Yeah?"
- Pandora: "I'm sorry I wasn't there today. When they said those things to you."
- Brandi: "You were doing your own classes. That's where you were supposed to be."
- Pandora: "I know. But I still wish I'd been there."
- A pause.
- Brandi: "Pandora?"
- Pandora: "Yeah?"
- Brandi: "I'm glad you had a good first day. Tell me again about the silks."
- Pandora tells her. In the dark, in the quiet cabin, she describes the climb, the wrap, the drop, the catch. She describes the height, the feel of the silk, the way the air moves when you swing. She describes it the way she always describes things — vividly, precisely, as if she is translating the visible world into a language only Brandi can hear.
- Brandi listens with her eyes closed — which is to say, she listens the way she always listens. Completely.
- When Pandora finishes, Brandi whispers: "It sounds like flying."
- Pandora: "It is."
- Brandi: "Then I'm flying too. Because you told me."
- In the dark, in the top bunk, Pandora smiles. She has never felt more like a twin and less like a helper. Tonight, she did not adjust Brandi's arm at the barre or guide her across a room. Tonight, she gave Brandi something to see.

- **Wren** is a returning camper who appears in both Gymnastics and Circus. She can become a friendly presence in future scenes.
- Pandora's description of the silks to Brandi in the dark is the first instance of Pandora translating visual experience into shared language at camp — the same skill she uses as Brandi's visual translator, but applied to something Brandi cannot access physically. This is distinct from her role as helper; it is her role as a twin who shares the world.
- Pandora's line — "I'm scared of lots of things. Just not this" — reveals that her fear is not physical but emotional. She is afraid of being apart from Brandi, afraid of what might happen when she is not there.
- The glide kip on the uneven bars is Pandora's weakest skill but she persists until she catches it. This establishes her work ethic and determination.
- Brandi's flip turn is cleaner than Pandora's, even though Pandora usually wins the sprint. This is consistent with Brandi's profile: in the water, she is completely free.
- Sloan and Rilee are present at the Birch Cabin table at dinner but do not interact with Brandi or Pandora. Because each cabin eats at its own table, the twins share every meal with Sloan and Rilee — adding tension to the daily routine.
