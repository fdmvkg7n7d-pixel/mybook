# Kiley Rose and Julia Tell Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige About French Woods — Bedtime Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Kiley Rose Key (twenty-three years old), Julia (twenty years old), Brandi Jessica Key (eight years old), Pandora Paige Key (eight years old)
- **Location:** The twins’ shared bedroom in the family townhouse, New York City
- **When:** The evening after Kiley Rose and Julia’s call to French Woods about Andrew. April of Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige’s eighth year. During the girls’ bedtime routine.
- **Status:** New scene outline
- **Tone:** Warm, excited, emotional, loving, anticipatory, full of hope and the family philosophy

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene shows Kiley Rose and Julia telling Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige the exciting news that they will be attending French Woods in July of their eighth year.

The conversation takes place in the girls’ bedroom while they are getting ready for bed.

The twins become very emotional about the news — overjoyed, tearful, and thrilled at the prospect of going to camp together.

It reinforces the family’s “anything and everything” philosophy and sets up the first arrival at French Woods.

The mothers have just made the private call to the camp that same day, but they do not mention the Andrew briefing to the girls.

## BEATS

### Beat 1: Bedtime Routine
- The twins are in their shared bedroom getting ready for bed.
- Brandi Jessica moves around the familiar room completely independently — she does not need any help in the house at all. She uses her white cane or memory of the space.
- Pandora Paige is doing the exact same things on her own (changing into her pajamas, brushing her teeth, etc.).
- They are talking quietly and giggling as they usually do at bedtime.
- Both girls are in their pajamas. The room is cozy and familiar, with the usual sounds of the townhouse at night.

### Beat 2: Mothers Enter
- Kiley Rose and Julia come into the bedroom together after the call.
- They sit on the edge of the bed (or the lower bunk if the girls have bunks).
- The mothers are calm but clearly excited and a little emotional themselves.
- “Girls, can we talk to you for a minute before lights out?”

### Beat 3: The Announcement
- Kiley Rose and Julia tell the girls the news:
  - They have decided that Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige will attend French Woods Festival of the Performing Arts this summer.
  - They will go in **July** of their eighth year.
  - It is a wonderful creative camp in the Catskills where campers choose their own majors and minors (ballet, circus arts, music, swimming, etc.).
  - They will be at camp with many other kids, sharing the experience of independence and creativity.
  - The girls will stay for all three sessions — Session 2, Session 3, and Session 4, back-to-back-to-back — and have an amazing full summer of independence and fun.
  - On the last day of each session, parents can visit their child, so Kiley Rose and Julia will come see them at the end of Session 2, Session 3, and Session 4.

### Beat 4: The Girls’ Emotional Reaction
- Both girls become very emotional.
- Pandora gasps and covers her mouth, then starts bouncing on the bed.
- Brandi’s face lights up, and she reaches out for her mothers’ hands.
- They both start talking at once — “Really? We get to go? Both of us? Together?”
- Tears of joy appear. Pandora hugs Brandi tightly. Brandi squeezes her mothers.
- Pandora: “We’re going to French Woods! We’re really going!”
- Brandi: “I get to go too? Even with my cane? Even though I can’t see?”
- The excitement is overwhelming and beautiful.

### Beat 5: Reassurance and Philosophy
- Kiley Rose and Julia gently reassure them.
- Kiley Rose: “Without any single doubt. You are both going.”
- Julia: “Blindness is an adjustment, never an excuse. There are no no’s, only hows.”
- They explain that Pandora will be Brandi’s co-pilot and helper the whole time at camp. (Brandi does not need help inside the house.)
- The mothers emphasize that the camp already knows how to support Brandi and that the family believes in them completely.

### Beat 6: Questions and Quiet Excitement
- The girls ask a flurry of questions: What will we do? Will we sleep in cabins? Can we do ballet and trapeze? Will the cousins be in the same cabin?
- Pandora immediately starts planning how she will describe everything to Brandi and help her with alignment in dance class.
- The girls are too excited to go straight to sleep. They keep whispering and hugging after the mothers leave the room.

### Beat 7: Goodnight
- Kiley Rose and Julia tuck them in, kiss them, and turn off the light.
- The twins lie in the dark, still whispering excitedly to each other.
- Last line of the scene: one of them (probably Pandora) says softly, “We’re really going to French Woods,” and Brandi answers with a huge smile in her voice, “We’re really going.”

## KEY DIALOGUE ANCHORS

> **Kiley Rose:** “We have some very exciting news. You’re both going to French Woods this summer.”

> **Pandora Paige (crying happily):** “We get to go? Both of us? Together?”

> **Brandi Jessica (reaching for her mothers):** “I get to go too? Even with my cane?”

> **Julia:** “Without any single doubt. Blindness is an adjustment, never an excuse. There are no no’s, only hows.”

> **Pandora Paige (to Brandi, later in the dark):** “I’ll help you with everything at camp. Just like always.”

> **Brandi Jessica:** “We’re really going to French Woods.”

## RELATION TO CONTINUITY

- This scene takes place the **evening after** Kiley Rose and Julia’s private phone call to French Woods (in April of the twins’ eighth year).
- The call informed the camp about Andrew so staff would be prepared.
- This is the first time the girls are told they will be attending French Woods.
- The twins are eight years old.
- It directly leads into the first arrival scene in July.
- Pandora’s role as Brandi’s helper at camp (navigation, dance adjustments, visual descriptions, etc.) is emphasized. Brandi Jessica does not need any help inside the house.
- The family philosophy is stated clearly.
- The girls’ emotional reaction shows how much they have wanted this kind of independence and adventure.
