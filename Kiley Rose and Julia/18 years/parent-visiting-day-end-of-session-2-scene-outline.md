# Parent Visiting Day — End of Session 2 Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Brandi Jessica Key (8), Pandora Paige Key (8), Kiley Rose Key, Julia, Maya (counselor, mid-20s), Sloan (9), Rilee (10), Mr. and Mrs. Hayashi (Sloan and Rilee's parents), French Woods camp director or head counselor
- **Onstage animal:** None (Burden does not arrive until age 14)
- **Locations:** French Woods campus — main visiting area, Birch Cabin, dance pavilion, gymnastics building, circus pavilion, Yoga studio, swimming pool, cafeteria, camp office/administrative area (Hancock, New York)
- **When:** The last day of Session 2, Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige's first summer at French Woods (July 2053). Parent Visiting Day. No classes are held on Visiting Day.
- **Status:** New scene outline
- **Tone:** Warm reunion, fierce maternal protection, proud demonstrations, the family philosophy tested by adults this time

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene captures Parent Visiting Day at the end of Session 2 — the first time Kiley Rose and Julia see the twins since drop-off. No classes are held on Visiting Day; the studios, gyms, and facilities are available for campers to show their families what they have been learning. It is also the scene where Sloan and Rilee's parents complain to the camp about Brandi Jessica, and where Kiley Rose and Julia reveal who their parents are.

It establishes:
- The joy and emotion of Kiley Rose and Julia reuniting with the twins after their first session away.
- That the twins show their mothers what they have learned in each of their classes — not in formal class sessions, but in the empty studios and facilities on a day when campers have the run of the campus with their families.
- That Kiley Rose and Julia ask Brandi Jessica if she would like to try the balance beam — they offer, they don't assume.
- That Sloan and Rilee are sisters, and that their parents share their prejudice.
- That Kiley Rose and Julia reveal their parents are Mike and Terri Anne Key.
- That the twins ask their mothers if they would mind them changing their majors for Session 3 and Session 4.
- That Swimming Lessons take place in the swimming pool; Waterfront Recreation takes place at the lake.

## BEATS

### Beat 1: Arrival — Kiley Rose and Julia Come to Camp

- It is Parent Visiting Day. No classes are held. The camp is buzzing — families arriving, campers running to greet parents, hugs and laughter everywhere.
- Kiley Rose and Julia park the car and walk onto the campus. Julia is carrying a large canvas bag — letters the twins have been writing home all session, collected to give back to them.
- They find the Birch Cabin path. Kiley Rose spots the cabin first.
- The twins are sitting on the cabin steps, waiting. Brandi's cane is beside her. Pandora is scanning the path.
- Pandora sees them first: "They're here!"
- Brandi stands up. She does not run — she has mapped this path, she knows where they are by the sound of their footsteps on the gravel. But her face lights up.
- The reunion is full of hugs, laughter, and tears. Kiley Rose crouches and holds both girls at once. Julia wraps her arms around all three of them.
- Kiley Rose: "Look at you. You're taller."
- Pandora: "I grew half an inch. Brandi grew a quarter inch."
- Brandi, laughing: "She measures us on the door frame. She makes me feel the mark."

### Beat 2: Catching Up — The Girls Tell Their Mothers Everything

- They find a spot on the grass near the cabin. Other families are scattered around the campus doing the same thing.
- Brandi and Pandora tell Kiley Rose and Julia about the summer. Everything. Their classes, their counselors, the food, the friends they've made.
- Brandi tells them about learning the routes — forty-two steps to the path, eighteen to the pavilion, seven to the barre. She tells them she walks to Ballet and Yoga by herself now.
- Pandora tells them about Gymnastics and Circus. She describes the trapeze, the silks, her first routine.
- Then, quietly, Brandi tells them about Sloan and Rilee.
- Brandi: "On the first day, a girl named Sloan said I shouldn't be in Ballet because I can't see the mirror. And her sister Rilee said I shouldn't be in Yoga because I can't see the poses."
- Kiley Rose's face changes. Julia goes very still.
- Kiley Rose: "What happened?"
- Brandi: "I told them they could ask me how I do things. Not about me, to me. The teachers backed me. Sloan walked out. Rilee walked out."
- Pandora: "She handled it without me. I was at Gymnastics and Circus. She was alone in both classes."
- Kiley Rose's eyes fill. Not with pity — with fierce, overwhelming pride.
- Kiley Rose: "You did that by yourself?"
- Brandi: "I was holding warrior two when Rilee said it. I didn't drop the pose."
- Julia reaches out and takes Brandi's hand. She squeezes it. She does not say anything. She does not need to.

### Beat 3: The Girls Show Their Mothers Their Classes

- After catching up, Brandi and Pandora ask the same question at the same time: "Do you want to see what we can do?"
- Kiley Rose: "More than anything."

---

#### Beat 3A: Ballet — Brandi Shows the Barre

- They walk to the dance pavilion. Brandi leads the way — forty-two steps, eighteen, seven — with Kiley Rose and Julia walking behind her, watching her cane tap the path.
- Inside the pavilion, it is quiet. No classes on Visiting Day. The sprung floor is empty, the mirror reflects an empty room, the barres are clear.
- Brandi finds her barre by touch. She shows Kiley Rose the tactile strip — the raised guide that Kiley Rose and Julia asked the camp to install before the summer.
- Brandi: "This is what you asked them to put here. Feel it."
- Kiley Rose runs her fingers along the tactile strip. She has never seen it before — she only requested it over the phone in April. It is thin, precise, and perfectly placed. Her eyes fill again.
- Julia feels it too. "It works?"
- Brandi: "It works. Watch."
- Brandi takes first position at the barre. She runs through a simple combination — plié, tendu, dégagé, rond de jambe — her hand tracking the tactile strip, her body perfectly aligned, her movements clean and musical. She counts under her breath.
- Kiley Rose watches her daughter dance. This is the same child who was doing these positions as a sighted four-year-old. The positions have not changed. Only the way she finds them.
- Julia stands very still. She is the one who taught Brandi to count. She recognizes her own system in Brandi's murmured numbers.
- Brandi finishes the combination and turns toward her mothers. "Well?"
- Kiley Rose, her voice thick: "Perfect."
- Julia: "Show me pointe."

#### Beat 3B: Pointe — Brandi Shows the Shoes

- Brandi pulls her pointe shoes from her bag. She puts them on by feel — she has done it every class all summer. She laces them with quick, practiced fingers.
- She rises onto pointe at the barre, one hand on the tactile strip, her feet arching over the boxes. She holds relevé for several beats, then lowers with control.
- Kiley Rose: "You're doing that without seeing where your feet are?"
- Brandi: "I feel the floor. I feel the edge of the shoe. I know where I am."
- She does a slow rise and lower three times. Each one is controlled, balanced, and sure.
- Julia puts her hand on Kiley Rose's arm. Neither of them speaks. They just watch.

#### Beat 3C: Gymnastics — Pandora Shows Her Routine, and Brandi Tries the Beam

- They leave the dance pavilion and Brandi leads them across campus toward the gymnastics building — a separate structure on the other side of the camp. Pandora walks beside Brandi, describing the route, but Brandi has already learned it.
- Inside the gymnastics building, the equipment is available but the space is empty. No classes today.
- Pandora shows them what she has been working on. She does a floor exercise passage — round-off, back handspring, back tuck — landing cleanly on the spring floor.
- Kiley Rose claps. Julia whistles.
- Pandora: "That's nothing. Watch this."
- She mounts the balance beam and walks it end to end, then does a simple dismount. Clean, steady, confident.
- Brandi, listening from the side: "Is it high?"
- Pandora: "About four feet. It's not that high."
- Brandi: "It sounds high when you land."
- Pandora laughs. "That's because I land hard."
- A beat. Then Kiley Rose looks at Brandi.
- Kiley Rose: "Brandi? Would you like to try the balance beam?"
- The room goes quiet. Julia looks at Kiley Rose. Pandora looks at Brandi.
- Brandi is still for a moment. Then she nods slowly.
- Brandi: "Yes. I would."
- Pandora guides Brandi to the beam. She places Brandi's hand on the leather surface so Brandi can feel its width and texture. Pandora walks the length of the beam, tapping it so Brandi can hear where it is.
- Pandora: "It's about four inches wide. Padded leather on top. It's at hip height. I'll walk beside you the whole time. You can hold my arm if you want."
- Brandi: "I want to try it by myself first."
- She takes her cane away and sets it against the wall. She steps onto the low mounting platform beside the beam. She places one foot on the surface, feeling its width through her shoe. She shifts her weight.
- Her hands are out to her sides. Her feet find the center of the beam by feel. She takes one step. Then another. Then another.
- She walks the beam slowly, carefully, feeling every inch through her bare awareness of her body. She does not look for the end — she listens for Pandora's voice.
- Pandora, walking alongside: "You're almost at the middle. Five more steps to the end."
- Brandi takes five more steps. She reaches the end. She steps down onto the dismount platform.
- She is breathing hard. She is smiling.
- Brandi: "It's wide enough. I can feel the edges. I could do it again."
- Kiley Rose is standing very still. Her eyes are bright. Julia has her hand over her mouth.
- Pandora: "You walked the whole beam on the first try."
- Brandi: "I counted my steps. I always count my steps."
- Kiley Rose: "You always count your steps."
- She says it like a prayer.

#### Beat 3C (continued): The Twins Sit on the Beam

- Brandi is still standing at the end of the beam. She runs her hand along the leather surface, the way she runs her fingers along the tactile strip on the barre — reading it.
- Brandi: "Pandora? Can we sit on it?"
- Pandora: "Right now?"
- Brandi: "Right now."
- Pandora mounts the beam and sits. She puts her hands out. Brandi steps up onto the mounting platform, finds Pandora's hands, and Pandora guides her onto the beam. Brandi swings her legs up and sits beside her sister.
- Their shoulders touch. Their legs dangle side by side. The beam is four inches wide, four feet off the ground — wide enough for two eight-year-olds if they sit close.
- They sit the way they sit on Brandi's lower bunk in Birch Cabin — pressed together, shoulders touching, comfortable in the small space.
- Brandi: "This is like our bunk."
- Pandora: "Except if you roll over, you fall off."
- Brandi laughs. "I won't roll over."
- Kiley Rose and Julia stand below them, watching. The gym is quiet. The sunlight comes through the high windows in long slants of gold. The twins' feet swing gently above the spring floor.
- Brandi: "On the floor I'm always counting steps. Always mapping. Always making sure I know where I am. Up here, I don't have to count. I just have to sit. The beam does the work."
- Pandora: "The beam holds you up."
- Brandi: "Yes. The beam holds me up. I don't have to hold myself up. I just have to trust it."
- A pause.
- Pandora: "You trust the beam?"
- Brandi: "I trusted you to put me on it. And then I trusted the beam to keep me here. It's four inches wide and four feet off the ground and it hasn't let me fall yet."
- Pandora: "It won't let you fall. I won't let you fall either."
- Brandi: "I know. That's why I can sit here."
- They sit on the beam for a long, quiet moment. Their feet swing in unison. Kiley Rose takes out her phone and photographs them — two eight-year-olds on a balance beam, shoulders touching, feet dangling, the afternoon light coming through the gym windows. She sends the photo to Mike and Terri Anne with no caption. She doesn't need one. They'll know what they're looking at.
- After a while, Pandora: "We should show them the silks."
- Brandi: "Okay. But I'm not jumping off."
- Pandora: "I'll help you down."
- Pandora slides off the beam first, then holds her arms up. Brandi pushes off and drops the four feet. Pandora catches her — arms around her waist, both of them stumbling slightly, both of them laughing.
- Brandi: "You caught me."
- Pandora: "I always catch you."
- Brandi finds her cane against the wall. She picks it up and taps it once on the floor.

#### Beat 3D: Circus — Pandora Shows the Silks

- They walk to the circus pavilion. It smells of rosin and canvas. Silks hang from the high ceiling, and a static trapeze is rigged at one end. No classes today — the space is quiet.
- Pandora climbs the silks — hand over hand, feet wrapping, rising high above the crash mat. She does a simple wrap and drops, catching herself a few feet from the floor. She is beaming.
- Julia: "Pandora Paige Key, you are going to give me a heart attack."
- Pandora, still hanging in the silks: "But aren't I amazing?"
- Kiley Rose: "You are absolutely amazing. Now please come down before I stop breathing."
- Pandora slides down the silks and lands softly on the mat.
- Brandi, from the edge of the pavilion, running her hand along the silk rope that hangs near the entrance: "What does it feel like up there?"
- Pandora: "Like flying. Next summer I'll describe it so well you'll feel like you're up there with me."

#### Beat 3E: Yoga — Brandi and Pandora Show Partner Yoga

- They walk to the Yoga studio. It still smells of cedar. Mats are rolled against the wall. No classes today — the room is empty and warm.
- Brandi and Pandora unroll two mats side by side. They look at Kiley Rose and Julia.
- Brandi: "We want to show you something. We've been practicing Partner Yoga — the way you taught us at home."
- Pandora: "You two should get on the mat with us."
- Kiley Rose and Julia look at each other. Then they kick off their shoes.
- The four of them do Partner Yoga together on the studio floor — Brandi and Pandora mirroring each other, Kiley Rose and Julia moving alongside them. Brandi and Pandora demonstrate the poses they learned during chemotherapy and radiotherapy: tree pose with hands clasped, warrior three with Pandora's hand on Brandi's back for balance, a seated twist where they hold each other's arms.
- Julia holds Brandi in a forward fold — her hands on Brandi's back, feeling the breath move through her daughter's body. This is the same position she held during the treatments, when Brandi's body hurt and she couldn't stop shaking.
- Kiley Rose and Pandora do partner tree — standing side by side, one arm around each other's waists, their other hands pressed together in namaste. Steady. Balanced.
- Brandi: "See? We can still do it. Even here."
- Kiley Rose, on the mat next to her daughters: "You can do it anywhere."
- Julia, her hand still on Brandi's back: "You always could."
- For a moment, the Yoga studio is the most peaceful place on earth. Four women on two mats, breathing together, holding each other up. The same practice that got them through cancer is now getting them through camp.

#### Beat 3F: Swimming Pool — Swimming Lessons

- They walk to the swimming pool. The water is still and clear. No classes today — the pool is open for families.
- Brandi walks to the edge of the pool by herself — she has counted these steps too. She does not hesitate. She dives in.
- She surfaces, swims a clean freestyle lap, reads the water pressure and the acoustic echo of the pool walls to execute a perfect flip turn.
- Pandora dives in beside her. They swim together for a moment, side by side, then race to the edge. Pandora wins, but only by a stroke.
- Brandi, pulling herself out of the pool: "I would have beaten you if I could see the finish."
- Pandora: "You would have beaten me if you didn't slow down to show off your flip turn."
- Kiley Rose and Julia sit at the edge of the pool with their feet in the water, watching their daughters. Kiley Rose leans her head against Julia's shoulder.
- Kiley Rose: "They're okay. They're really okay."
- Julia: "They're better than okay."

---

### Beat 4: The Hayashi Family Arrives

- Across the campus, Mr. and Mrs. Hayashi arrive to visit Sloan and Rilee. They are a well-dressed couple in their late thirties. Mr. Hayashi is a real estate developer; Mrs. Hayashi is a pediatric dentist. They are the kind of parents who are involved, organized, and certain they know what is best for their children.
- Sloan and Rilee run to greet them. The hugs are warm but brief — the Hayashis are not a demonstrative family.
- Sloan and Rilee start telling their parents about the summer. And then, inevitably, they tell them about Brandi Jessica.
- Sloan: "There's a blind girl in my Ballet class. She can't see anything. She has a cane. She shouldn't be in the class — it's dangerous."
- Rilee: "She's in my Yoga too. She can't see the poses. It doesn't make sense. The teachers let her stay but I don't think it's right."
- Mr. Hayashi frowns. Mrs. Hayashi's expression tightens.
- Mrs. Hayashi: "A blind child in Ballet? And Yoga? At this camp? Who allowed that?"
- Sloan: "The teachers said she belongs there. But she can't see the mirror or the demonstrations. It's not safe."
- Mr. Hayashi: "Where are the camp administrators? I'd like to have a conversation about this."

### Beat 5: The Hayashis Complain to the Camp

- Mr. and Mrs. Hayashi find the camp office. They request a meeting with the camp director or head counselor.
- They speak calmly but firmly:
  - "Our daughters are in classes with a child who cannot see. She uses a cane. She cannot follow visual demonstrations. She is a safety risk in physical activities like Ballet, Pointe, and Yoga. We believe the camp has a responsibility to all its campers, not just one."
  - "Sloan says the girl walked into Ballet class alone — with no adult supervision and no aide. What if she had fallen? What if she had walked into another camper?"
  - "We're not asking for anything unreasonable. We're asking that the camp ensure the safety of all children in these activities. If that means she can't participate in certain classes, that's not discrimination — that's common sense."
- The camp director listens respectfully. Then she responds:
  - "Mr. and Mrs. Hayashi, thank you for sharing your concerns. Brandi Jessica Key is a camper in good standing. She was admitted through the same process as every other camper. Her family informed us in advance of her blindness and any related needs, and we made appropriate accommodations — including a tactile barre guide in the dance pavilion, which was installed at her family's request before the summer began."
  - "I've received reports from her Ballet and Yoga instructors. She follows every combination and every pose correctly. She has not caused a single safety incident. She has not disrupted any class. Her instructors have told me she is a strong, capable student."
  - "French Woods does not exclude campers on the basis of disability. We make accommodations so that every child can participate fully. If your daughters have specific safety concerns, I'm happy to address them. But we will not remove a camper from her chosen classes because another family believes she doesn't belong."
- Mr. Hayashi: "So you're telling us there's nothing you'll do?"
- The director: "I'm telling you that Brandi Jessica will continue in her classes as scheduled. If your daughters are uncomfortable, I can explore schedule adjustments for Sloan and Rilee. But the adjustment will not come at the expense of another camper's right to be here."
- Mrs. Hayashi: "That's not acceptable."
- The director: "I understand you feel that way. My door is always open. But this camp's policy is clear."
- The Hayashis leave the office. They are not satisfied.

### Beat 6: The Hayashis Find Kiley Rose and Julia

- The Hayashis, having learned from their daughters which cabin Brandi Jessica is in, make their way toward Birch Cabin.
- They find Kiley Rose, Julia, Brandi Jessica, and Pandora sitting together on the grass near the cabin. The twins are still slightly damp from the pool, their hair in messy braids.
- Mrs. Hayashi approaches. She speaks directly to Kiley Rose and Julia:
  - "Are you the parents of the blind girl?"
- Kiley Rose's eyes meet Mrs. Hayashi's. She does not release Brandi from her lap.
- Kiley Rose: "Her name is Brandi Jessica. She's our daughter. Who are you?"
- Mrs. Hayashi: "I'm Mrs. Hayashi. My daughters Sloan and Rilee are in Brandi's classes. They've told us about the situation. We've spoken to the camp administration."
- Mr. Hayashi steps forward: "We think it's inappropriate for a blind child to be in physical activity classes with sighted children. It's a safety issue. It's not fair to our daughters, and frankly, it's not fair to yours."
- A beat. Julia stands up slowly. She does not raise her voice. She does not step toward them. She stands between the Hayashis and Brandi.
- Julia: "Brandi Jessica takes Ballet, Pointe, Swimming Lessons, and Yoga. She follows every combination and every pose. She has not been injured. She has not injured anyone. She has not disrupted a single class. Her instructors have confirmed this. What exactly is your concern?"
- Mr. Hayashi: "She can't see. She could fall. She could walk into someone. She could —"
- Julia: "She could. So could your daughters. Every child in that studio could fall or walk into someone. That's what happens in physical activities. The difference is that Brandi has already learned to navigate those risks without her eyes. She counted the steps from her cabin to the dance pavilion and she walks there by herself. She holds warrior two while other people question whether she belongs. Today she walked the balance beam on the first try. She does not need you to protect her from her own life."
- Mrs. Hayashi: "We're thinking of all the children —"
- Kiley Rose speaks now. She is still holding Brandi on her lap. Her voice is low and absolutely steady:
  - "No. You're thinking of your children. And that's fine — you should think of your children. But do not stand in front of my daughter and tell me what's fair to her. She lost her eyes to cancer when she was six years old. She survived surgery. She survived chemotherapy. She survived radiotherapy. She learned Yoga from her mommies so she could calm down during those treatments. And now she is at this camp, taking the same classes as every other child, without any single doubt. If your daughters have a problem with that, that is not Brandi's problem to solve. It's yours."
- Mr. Hayashi: "We have every right to advocate for our children's safety."
- Kiley Rose: "You do. And so do I. And I am telling you — my daughter is safe, she is capable, and she is staying. If you want to know who you're talking to, my name is Kiley Rose Key. This is my partner Julia. Our parents are Mike and Terri Anne Key. You may have heard of them. Our family sends our children to this camp without any single doubt, and we are not the ones who will be leaving."
- The air is very still. The name lands. Mike and Terri Anne Key. Other families on the grass have gone quiet. Pandora is standing now, slightly in front of Brandi, her jaw set.
- The Hayashis recognize the name, or they don't — but they recognize the certainty behind it. They recognize that they are standing in front of a family that will not yield.
- Mrs. Hayashi opens her mouth, then closes it. Mr. Hayashi puts his hand on his wife's arm.
- Mr. Hayashi: "We've made our position clear to the camp. We'll be following up."
- Kiley Rose: "You do that."
- The Hayashis turn and walk away.

### Beat 7: After the Hayashis Leave

- The moment the Hayashis are out of earshot, the tension breaks. Kiley Rose's arms tighten around Brandi. Julia sits back down, her hands shaking slightly.
- Brandi, from Kiley Rose's lap, is quiet. She did not speak during the confrontation. She listened to the whole thing.
- Brandi: "Mama? Are you okay?"
- Kiley Rose laughs — a short, fierce, slightly watery laugh. "Am I okay? You're asking me if I'm okay?"
- Brandi: "You told them about Grandma and Grandpa."
- Kiley Rose: "I did. They needed to know who they were talking to."
- Pandora: "What did it mean? When you said Mike and Terri Anne Key?"
- Kiley Rose looks at Julia. Julia nods slightly.
- Kiley Rose: "It meant that our family doesn't back down. It meant that your grandfather and your grandmother raised us to fight for what's right. And it meant that anyone who wants to push us around should know who we are first."
- Pandora: "That was really cool."
- Julia: "It was a little bit cool."
- Brandi: "I liked the part about Yoga."
- Julia: "Which part?"
- Brandi: "The part where you said my mommies taught me so I could calm down during my treatments. Because that's true. And because it's our thing, not just my thing."
- Julia reaches over and takes Brandi's hand. "It will always be our thing."
- Pandora: "She held warrior two while a ten-year-old told her she didn't belong, and she didn't drop the pose. She can handle two parents."
- Julia reaches over and takes Pandora's hand. "You're fierce, Pandora."
- Pandora: "I learned it from my mommies."

### Beat 8: Lunch in the Cafeteria

- They walk to the camp cafeteria for lunch. The dining hall is full of families — campers eating with their parents, the usual camp noise turned up by the visiting-day crowd. Each cabin eats at its own table, and the Birch Cabin table is where the twins usually sit. Today, Kiley Rose and Julia join them there.
- Brandi navigates the cafeteria line by herself — she has learned the layout by now. She counts the steps from the door to the serving area. Pandora describes the food options. Brandi makes her own choices.
- They sit down at the Birch Cabin table. Brandi between her mothers, Pandora across from them. Sloan and Rilee are at the other end of the table with their parents, who have joined for Visiting Day. The two families do not speak. The length of the table is enough.
- It is an ordinary camp lunch — trays, plastic cups, the smell of grilled cheese and tomato soup. But it is also the first meal Kiley Rose and Julia have shared with the twins since drop-off, and every bite tastes like home.
- During lunch, the twins look at each other. A silent twin conversation passes between them. Brandi nods slightly.
- Brandi: "Mama? Julia? We want to ask you something."
- Kiley Rose: "What is it?"
- Pandora: "We want to change our majors for Session 3 and Session 4."
- Julia: "Change your majors? You chose them yourself on the first night."
- Brandi: "We know. And we love them. But we've been here for a whole session now, and we've learned so much. We want to try different things."
- Kiley Rose: "What would you change to?"
- Pandora: "We haven't decided yet. We're still thinking about it. We just wanted to ask if you would mind."
- Kiley Rose and Julia look at each other. The family rule has always been: there are no no's, only hows.
- Kiley Rose: "Do you remember what I told you on the first day of camp?"
- Brandi and Pandora, together: "There are no no's, only hows."
- Kiley Rose: "That applies to this too. If you want to change your majors, that's your choice. It's your summer. We trust you."
- Julia: "But you have to think it through. Talk to your counselors. Make sure the new classes are right for you. And if you change your mind again, that's okay too."
- Brandi: "We will."
- Pandora: "Thank you, Mommy. Thank you, Julia."
- Kiley Rose: "You don't have to thank us for trusting you. That's what we do."

### Beat 9: The Rest of Visiting Day

- After lunch, they walk some more. They visit the lake — the waterfront recreation area where Waterfront Recreation takes place. They stand on the dock and look out at the water.
- Brandi: "This is where Waterfront Recreation is. Swimming Lessons are in the pool, but the lake is for recreation. I swim here too."
- Kiley Rose: "Which do you like better?"
- Brandi: "The pool for lessons. The lake for fun. The lake doesn't have lanes, so I have to listen harder. But it feels freer."
- They meet Maya, who greets them warmly and tells them Brandi is "one of the bravest campers I've ever had."
- Kiley Rose: "She's the bravest person I've ever known."
- Maya: "She comes by it honestly."

### Beat 10: Goodbye

- Visiting Day ends in late afternoon. Families say goodbye at the same marked drop-off points.
- Kiley Rose crouches in front of both girls, just like she did on the first day of camp.
- Kiley Rose: "Two more sessions. You're halfway there. You're doing beautifully."
- Julia gives them each a small smooth stone, just like the ones from the first drop-off. "For the next two sessions. Rub them and think of us."
- Brandi holds her stone. She runs her thumb over its surface.
- Pandora: "We'll write more letters."
- Kiley Rose: "You'd better."
- Hugs. Long, hard hugs. Kiley Rose and Julia walk back toward the parking lot. The twins stand together and watch them go — or, in Brandi's case, listen to their footsteps fade.
- Pandora: "They'll be back at the end of Session 3."
- Brandi: "I know."
- Pandora takes her hand. "Come on. Let's go back to the cabin."

### Beat 11: The Hayashi Sisters That Night

- That night, after lights out, Sloan and Rilee are quiet in their bunks. Their parents have left.
- Sloan whispers across the cabin to Rilee: "Did Mom and Dad talk to you?"
- Rilee whispers back: "They said if we're uncomfortable, they can move us to different classes next session."
- Sloan: "Are you going to move?"
- A long pause.
- Rilee: "I don't know. She really did hold warrior two the whole time. And her alignment was better than mine."
- Another pause.
- Sloan: "She did the across-the-floor combination perfectly. I saw it."
- They are quiet after that. The seeds of doubt — not about Brandi, but about their own certainty — have been planted.
