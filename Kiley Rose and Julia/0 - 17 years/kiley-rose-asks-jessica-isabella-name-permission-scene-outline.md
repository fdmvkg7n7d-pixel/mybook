# Kiley Rose Asks Jessica Isabella Permission to Use Her Name — iPhone Scene Outline

## Setting

- **Time:** Autumn **2044**, after Kiley has felt the twins move and the names have begun to feel real, but before the birth.
- **Place:** The conversation happens **over their iPhones**, most likely through FaceTime.
  - Kiley is at home or wherever Mike and Terri Anne have her settled during the pregnancy.
  - Jessica Isabella and Emma are living in **Halifax**, so Jessica cannot simply sit beside Kiley in person.
- **Characters present on the call:** Kiley Rose Key and Jessica Isabella Key.
- **Nearby/off-page presence:**
  - Mike and Terri Anne are elsewhere near Kiley, giving her privacy.
  - Emma is in Halifax with Jessica Isabella, close enough to notice if Jessica becomes emotional, but she gives Jessica privacy for the call.
- **Mood:** Intimate, grateful, tearful, sisterly, and reverent. The iPhone screen creates distance, but the emotional closeness is strong. This is a naming scene, but it is also a scar-memory scene.

---

## 1. Why Kiley Needs to Ask

- Kiley has been thinking seriously about the twins' names.
- One name has settled around the older/first twin: **Brandi Jessica**.
- Kiley wants to use **Jessica** — Jessica Isabella's first name — because Jessica Isabella saved her life when Kiley was seven.
- She does not want to simply take the name or announce it without asking.
- To Kiley, the name carries too much history:
  - the kitchen accident,
  - the boiling water,
  - Jessica pushing her out of the way,
  - Jessica's burns and scars,
  - Kiley walking away unhurt,
  - Kiley now being alive to carry twins.
- Kiley is deeply grateful not only to be alive, but to be alive **and** pregnant with two daughters she already loves.
- Because Jessica Isabella and Emma are living in Halifax, Kiley decides the asking has to happen over their iPhones rather than waiting until they can be in the same room.

---

## 2. Kiley Calls Jessica Isabella

- Kiley texts first or calls directly, depending on the emotional rhythm:
  - *"Can I FaceTime you? Just us?"*
- Jessica answers from Halifax.
- Emma may be visible for a second in the background before realizing this is private and stepping away.
- Jessica notices immediately that Kiley is nervous.
- Kiley may be physically awkward now because of the pregnancy, needing pillows or help getting comfortable before the call.
- Jessica cannot physically help her through the phone, which makes both sisters feel the distance.
- Kiley places one hand over the twins and tries to begin.
- Jessica waits, letting Kiley take her time.

---

## 3. The Distance Matters

- The iPhone screen becomes part of the emotional texture:
  - Kiley can see Jessica's face but cannot touch her hand.
  - Jessica can hear Kiley's voice shake but cannot sit beside her.
  - The sisters are separated by geography, but not by love.
- Jessica may quietly move to a private room in the Halifax home so Kiley has her full attention.
- Kiley sees a glimpse of Jessica's life with Emma in Halifax — a lamp, a doorway, Emma's voice in the distance — and feels how grown-up and far away everyone has become.
- This makes the request feel even more important: the name is a way of carrying Jessica close even when they are not in the same city.

---

## 4. Kiley Names the Debt Without Making It a Debt

- Kiley says she has been thinking about names.
- She tells Jessica that she wants one of the babies to carry Jessica's first name.
- Kiley is careful and emotional:
  - She is not trying to repay Jessica.
  - She knows a name cannot repay what happened.
  - She knows Jessica did not ask her to live in debt.
  - But she is grateful.
- Kiley says some version of:
  - *"You saved my life."*
  - *"If you hadn't pushed me, I might not be here."*
  - *"And now I get to have them. I get to have twins. I don't know how to say thank you for that."*
- This is the emotional heart of the scene: Kiley connects survival to motherhood.
- Jessica hears it over the iPhone and goes quiet, because the distance does not soften the weight of the words.

---

## 5. Asking Permission Clearly

- Kiley asks directly:
  - *"Can I use your first name?"*
  - *"Can one of my daughters be Brandi Jessica?"*
- She does not assume Jessica will say yes.
- She makes room for Jessica's feelings because the name belongs to Jessica before it belongs to the baby.
- The question itself shows respect: Kiley is not claiming Jessica's sacrifice as family property.
- Kiley may add:
  - *"I wanted to ask you myself. Not through Mom. Not through Daddy. Me to you."*

---

## 6. Jessica Isabella's Reaction

- Jessica is moved but also protective of Kiley.
- She does not want Kiley to turn the scars into a debt ledger.
- Jessica may remind her:
  - She pushed Kiley because Kiley was her sister.
  - She did not do it to be repaid.
  - Kiley's daughters do not owe Jessica anything either.
- Possible line:
  - *"I didn't save you so you would spend your life paying me back."*
- Kiley says she knows — or is trying to know.
- Jessica sees that this is gratitude, not repayment.
- Emma may quietly appear in the background, sense Jessica is crying, and withdraw again without intruding.

---

## 7. Jessica Gives Her Blessing

- Jessica gives permission.
- The permission should be simple and emotionally powerful.
- Possible lines:
  - *"Yes. Use it."*
  - *"Brandi Jessica. I like that."*
  - *"If she carries my name, let it mean she was loved before she was born."*
- Kiley cries because Jessica has not only given permission; she has removed the shame from the asking.
- Because they are on iPhones, Jessica cannot place a hand on Kiley's belly.
- Instead, Kiley places her own hand over the twins while Jessica watches through the screen.
- If one or both twins move, Kiley tells Jessica immediately:
  - *"They moved."*
- Jessica smiles through tears, receiving the movement as the twins' answer from a distance.

---

## 8. The Name Becomes Real

- Kiley says the full name aloud into the phone:
  - *"Brandi Jessica Key."*
- Saying it after Jessica's permission changes the name from an idea into a promise.
- Jessica may ask about the other twin's name.
- Kiley says **Pandora Paige** is forming too — the hope after everything opens.
- The names now carry two different emotional meanings:
  - **Brandi Jessica:** gratitude, survival, sisterly protection, and a life saved.
  - **Pandora Paige:** hope, uncertainty, and the future still opening.

---

## 9. Closing Image

- The sisters sit quietly in two different places, connected by their iPhones.
- Kiley's hand rests over the twins.
- Jessica's face is small on the screen but full of feeling.
- Jessica's permission settles between them like a blessing, despite the distance between Halifax and Kiley's room.
- Kiley is still frightened of the pregnancy, but this part now feels clear.
- One daughter will carry the first name of the sister who saved Kiley's life.
- Kiley whispers the name once more:
  - *"Brandi Jessica."*
- Jessica hears it through the phone and answers softly:
  - *"Hi, Brandi Jessica."*

---

## Thematic Resonance

- **Naming as gratitude, not repayment:** Kiley asks because she is grateful, not because Jessica's scars are a debt that can be paid.
- **The sister who saved her life:** Jessica's childhood act of protection becomes part of the next generation's identity.
- **Permission matters:** Kiley honors Jessica's bodily history and name by asking rather than assuming.
- **Distance and connection:** Jessica Isabella and Emma are living in Halifax, but the iPhone call allows the sisters to keep this moment intimate.
- **Twins as miracle:** Kiley's gratitude is intensified because she is alive to carry two daughters, something Turner Syndrome made seem nearly impossible.
- **Generational echo:** Brandi Jessica's name carries the first-generation sister-saving-sister story into the second generation.
