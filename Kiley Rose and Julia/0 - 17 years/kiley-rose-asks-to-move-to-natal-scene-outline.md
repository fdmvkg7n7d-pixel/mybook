# Kiley Rose Asks to Move to Natal — Scene Outline

> **Scope:** Shortly after Kiley Rose learns the full truth about Fallon Glenda. Mike and Terri Anne come to find her in the twins' bedroom, where she is changing Brandi Jessica. In the middle of that ordinary, intimate moment — a nappy change, a baby on the mat, her hands busy — Kiley Rose asks them, quietly and carefully, whether they would mind if she moved to Natal with Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige. She has thought about it. She is resolved. She is also nervous, because she loves them and she does not want to hurt them after everything this week has already cost. Mike and Terri Anne trust her completely. They let her go.
>
> **Time:** May 2045 — shortly after Kiley Rose learns the truth about Fallon. The twins are five months old. Kiley Rose is sixteen.
> **Place:** The twins' bedroom in the Key family's Rio de Janeiro residence. A small, warm room — a changing mat, a crib, the soft clutter of two five-month-olds' world.
> **Characters present:** Kiley Rose, Mike Key, Terri Anne Williams-Key. Brandi Jessica on the changing mat. Pandora Paige nearby in the crib or a bassinet.
> **Mood:** The intimacy of an ordinary moment → the ask → the held breath → release.

---

## 1. What Knowing About Fallon Did to Her

- **Learning the truth:** When Kiley Rose learns what happened to Fallon — the full truth, not the partial version from the gathering, but the real shape of it — something shifts in her. She does not fall apart. She is not a falling-apart person. But she goes very quiet, the way she goes quiet when something large is settling into place inside her and she needs to wait for it to land before she can speak.
- **What it does:** It makes Rio feel different. Not just Lazaro's city — it has always been Lazaro's city, the beach where she met him, the beach where she watched him kiss someone else, the place where she carried her daughters and was left. But now Rio is also the city where something was done to Fallon. Where a person in authority used his position to hurt a twelve-year-old girl. Where Fallon lay awake alone all night worrying and could not sleep. Kiley Rose cannot separate these things. They are all the same city. She does not want to raise Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige in it.
- **Why Natal:** Natal is northeast Brazil — far enough from Rio to be a different world, close enough to still be Brazil. She has read about it. Sun-drenched, coastal, unhurried. A city that does not know her history, or Fallon's, or any of this week's weight. A beach that has never seen Lazaro. A place where she can begin to build something that is not shadowed by everything Rio has been.
- **The family going to Carlisle:** She knows they are going — Fallon needs to go home, and the family will go with her. Kiley Rose is not going to Carlisle. She has been here — in the twins' bedroom, in the night feeds, in the quiet of early mornings — deciding that. Not out of distance from Fallon, not out of selfishness. Out of the knowledge that she is sixteen years old with five-month-old twin daughters and she needs to begin building something of her own. Knowing what Fallon carried alone — all those weeks, all that silence — has made Kiley Rose more certain, not less. She will not carry things alone. She will go somewhere new and build something.
- **The ask:** She decides to ask. Not to tell — to ask. Out of love and respect for her parents, she will bring it to them as a question even though, in the quiet of her own mind, it is already a decision. That is who Kiley Rose is: she knows what she wants and she approaches it with courtesy.

## 2. The Twins' Bedroom

- **Mike and Terri Anne come to her:** They find her in the twins' bedroom — not looking for her, just moving through the house the way parents move through a house where something large has recently happened, checking on each child in turn. The door is open. They hear her before they see her.
- **The singing:** Kiley Rose is singing to the girls. Not performing — not projecting, not self-conscious. The quiet, private singing of a mother alone with her babies, the kind that happens when no one is supposed to be listening. Her voice is low and unhurried, moving through something soft — a lullaby, a song she has sung to them before, something that belongs to this room and these two girls and nobody else. Pandora Paige is in the crib, awake, watching her mother with the grave absorption of an infant who finds everything worth attending to. Brandi Jessica is on the changing mat, legs in the air, making the small sounds of a baby who has opinions about the proceedings but is being somewhat mollified by the music.
- **Mike and Terri Anne in the hallway:** They stop before they reach the doorway. They do not mean to — they simply stop, the way you stop when you hear something that asks you to be still for a moment. They stand in the hallway and listen. Mike's hand finds Terri Anne's without either of them deciding it. They do not look at each other. They listen to their daughter sing to her daughters, in a bedroom in Rio de Janeiro, five months into a motherhood that arrived too early and has never once been small.
- **The song ending:** It does not end dramatically — it simply finishes, the way songs do when a mother reaches the end of a verse and the baby has settled and the next thing needs doing. Kiley Rose bends back to the change. Her hands resume their work.
- **The room:** Small and warm and entirely the twins'. A changing mat with a soft cover. The shared crib — Kiley Rose chose one crib, wanting them close to each other from the beginning. A small window with light coming through. The smell of baby powder and clean laundry. It is the most ordinary room in the house and it is also the most entirely Kiley Rose's — the space she has built around her daughters since December, the room that is already theirs.
- **Mike and Terri Anne appear in the doorway:** They move forward when the song ends — giving it its moment before they enter. There is something about watching Kiley Rose with Brandi Jessica — the practiced ease of a sixteen-year-old who has been doing this for five months, who knows exactly how to hold the baby's legs, who has her own particular voice she uses with the girls, lower and softer than her regular voice — that keeps them at the threshold for a moment longer.
- **Kiley Rose, without turning:** She has heard them — she always hears when someone enters the twins' orbit.

  *"Come in,"* she says. Still busy with Brandi Jessica. *"I just need a second."*

  She finishes the change — efficient, careful, the way she does everything with the twins — and lifts Brandi Jessica up against her shoulder. Then she turns. She sees both of them in the doorway and something in her face settles into its resolute expression. She has been waiting for a moment like this one. A moment when all three of them are in the same space and the house is quiet enough.

  *"Can I talk to you both about something?"*

---

## 3. The Ask

- **How she starts:** Not with Natal. With the reason. She is still holding Brandi Jessica against her shoulder, one hand patting her back in the automatic rhythm of a mother who does not have to think about it anymore. She looks at Mike and Terri Anne and begins.

  *"I've been thinking about staying in Brazil. Not in Rio. Somewhere else. Natal."*

  She watches their faces. She continues before they can respond — not because she is afraid of their response but because she wants to give them the whole thought before they answer.

  *"Rio is — it's Lazaro's city. And now it's — after what happened to Fallon, it feels like something else too. Something I don't want to stay inside."*

  She does not elaborate on Fallon. She does not need to. She looks at her parents and they understand.

  *"I don't want Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige to grow up in a city where their mother is always looking over her shoulder. Or carrying what this city carries now."*

  A pause. She holds Pandora Paige a little more deliberately.

  *"Natal is far enough. It's still Brazil. I like Brazil. I want to raise them somewhere warm, somewhere close to the ocean. I just — I don't want it to be here."*

- **The question:** She looks at them both — Mike first, then Terri Anne.

  *"Would you mind? If we went to Natal instead of Carlisle?"*

  She says *we* — herself and the twins. She is already thinking as a unit of three.

  *"I'm not trying to be far from you. I just need somewhere that's mine."*

- **The held breath:** She waits. She is resolved and she is nervous and these two things sit in her chest at the same time. She knows what she wants. She is still afraid of their answer. Not because she thinks they will say no — but because saying this out loud, asking to go somewhere of her own, feels like the beginning of something large and she is not entirely sure she is ready for how large it is.

---

## 4. Mike and Terri Anne

- **The look between them:** Mike and Terri Anne exchange a glance — the brief, complete communication of two parents who have been together long enough to have entire conversations in a single look. What the look says: *we see her. We hear her. She has thought this through.*
- **Mike speaks first:** He steps into the room properly — he and Terri Anne have been in the doorway until now. He sits on the edge of the crib, or leans against the wall, bringing himself to a lower level the way he always does. He does not answer the question immediately. He asks one of his own — not to interrogate, but because he wants to understand completely before he responds:

  *"Have you looked into it? Natal — do you know where you'd live, what it would look like for the girls?"*

  Kiley Rose — still holding Brandi Jessica against her shoulder, still patting her back:

  *"Not the specifics yet. I wanted to ask you first."*

  Mike nods. He considers this — the fact that she came to them before she had the plan fully built. She asked first. That matters to him. She is sixteen years old and she asked first.

- **Terri Anne:** She is looking at Kiley Rose the way she looks at things she wants to understand completely — the nurse's attention, the mother's eyes.

  *"Is this about Lazaro? Or is it about something bigger than Lazaro?"*

  Kiley Rose thinks about this honestly.

  *"Both,"* she says. *"Lazaro is part of it. But it's also — I'm sixteen and I have two daughters and I've been living in my parents' house my whole life. I need to find out what it feels like to be somewhere that I chose."*

  Terri Anne receives this. She does not flinch from it. She does not hear it as rejection. She hears it as exactly what it is: a sixteen-year-old mother telling the truth about what she needs.

- **Mike's answer:** He leans forward. His elbows on his knees — the posture he takes when he is saying something that matters.

  *"Kiley Rose. You are sixteen years old with five-month-old twins. You carried them through a pregnancy that nearly killed you, alone, after the person who should have stayed didn't. You have been holding this family together for the last week while we dealt with your sister. You have earned the right to tell us where you want to go."*

  He holds her gaze.

  *"If you want Natal, you go to Natal. We will make sure you have everything you need to do it right."*

- **Terri Anne:** She reaches across and puts her hand over Kiley Rose's free hand — the one not holding Pandora Paige.

  *"We are not losing you to Natal. You understand that? You are not going somewhere we can't reach you. You are going somewhere you choose."*

  A beat.

  *"That is not the same thing as leaving."*

- **Kiley Rose's exhale:** She does not cry. She has cried enough this week. She simply breathes — a long, slow exhale that releases something she has been holding since before she sat down. Her shoulders come down slightly. Pandora Paige stirs against her chest and she puts her chin down toward the baby's head for a moment.

  *"Thank you,"* she says. Quietly. The two words carrying everything.

---

## 5. The Practical Conversation

- **What Mike does next:** He does not let the emotion sit too long — not because he wants to rush past it, but because Kiley Rose is a practical person and the most loving thing he can do now is treat her plan seriously. He asks questions:
  - What does she know about Natal — the neighbourhood, the cost of living, the medical access for her and the twins?
  - Has she thought about the twins' care while she settles in?
  - Does she want help finding a place, or does she want to find it herself?
- **Kiley Rose:** She has thought about some of it and not all of it. She answers what she knows and says plainly when she doesn't know yet. She is not embarrassed by the gaps — she came to them before the plan was complete on purpose. She trusts them to help fill the gaps without taking the plan away from her.
- **What they agree on:**
  - Mike will ensure Kiley Rose has the financial foundation she needs — an apartment found and secured, a support structure in place before she arrives.
  - Terri Anne will research medical providers in Natal — paediatricians for the twins, a GP for Kiley Rose, specialists accessible if needed.
  - They will visit. Regularly. Natal is not the other side of the world. It is a flight. They will come.
  - Kiley Rose will call. Every day if she wants, or as often as she needs. She will not be unreachable and they will not be unreachable.
  - The door — the Key family's standing promise — is always open. If Natal is not right, if she needs to come back, she comes back. No questions. No *I told you so.* Just the door open.
- **The one condition that is not a condition:** Mike does not frame it as a condition. He frames it as a truth:

  *"You are sixteen. There will be things in Natal that are harder than you expect. When that happens — when, not if — you call us. Not after you've managed it alone. When it's happening."*

  Kiley Rose: *"I know."*

  Mike: *"I mean it."*

  Kiley Rose: *"I know, Dad. I will."*

## 6. After — The Patio

- **The conversation ending:** Not abruptly — it loosens gradually, the way good conversations do. The plan is not yet a plan — it is an intention and a permission and the beginning of logistics. But the important thing has been said and received.
- **Kiley Rose stands:** She shifts Pandora Paige to her shoulder. She looks at Brandi Jessica in the bassinet — still sleeping, fists curled, entirely unaware of any of this.
- **What she feels:** Not entirely unafraid. Natal is somewhere she has never been. She is sixteen. She has two babies. She will be far from the family that has held her through everything. She knows all of this. She is going anyway. That is who she is. She has been going anyway her whole life.
- **Mike, standing:** He puts his hand on the top of her head — briefly, the same gesture he gave Fallon at the police station, the gesture that carries everything he cannot say — and then he goes inside. Terri Anne stays for a moment.
- **Terri Anne and Kiley Rose, alone:** Mike goes. Terri Anne stays for a moment longer in the twins' bedroom. She looks at Pandora Paige in the crib. Then at Brandi Jessica on Kiley Rose's hip. Then at Kiley Rose.

  *"You're going to be a wonderful mother in Natal,"* she says. *"You already are one here."*

  Kiley Rose does not answer. She looks at Brandi Jessica's face — the small perfect face of a five-month-old who trusts her completely, who has no idea that her mother has just decided where they are going to live. She nods once.

- **Closing image:** Kiley Rose in the twins' bedroom after Mike and Terri Anne have gone. Brandi Jessica on her hip now, the nappy change long finished, the baby settled. Pandora Paige in the crib, watching her sister and her mother with the grave attention of a five-month-old who finds everything worth watching. The small warm room. The shared crib Kiley Rose chose because she wanted them close. The window with its Rio light — the city outside, already beginning to be behind her. She looks at her daughters. She does not know yet what Natal looks like, what the light is like in the morning, what the ocean sounds like from a window she will choose for herself and for them. She will find out. They will find out together.

## 7. Character Work

### Kiley Rose (16)
- **The resolve underneath the nerves:** She came to them with a decision dressed as a question. She did that out of love — she did not want to simply announce it and walk away. She asked. That is who she is: she knows what she wants and she moves toward it with courtesy.
- **"I need somewhere that's mine."** The most honest sentence in the scene. She is sixteen and she has never chosen her own city. Natal is the beginning of that.
- **The exhale:** She does not cry when they say yes. She exhales. The body releasing what the mind has been holding. That is Kiley Rose — not a crier, not a performer. Just present, and then released.
- **Pandora Paige as the reason:** She holds Pandora Paige through the entire ask. The baby is not a prop — she is the point. Everything Kiley Rose is doing, she is doing for the two of them.

### Mike Key
- **The question before the answer:** He asks her what she knows about Natal before he responds. He takes her plan seriously enough to ask about it — which is the most respectful thing he could do.
- **"You have earned the right to tell us where you want to go."** He does not frame it as permission. He frames it as something she has already earned. There is a difference, and it matters enormously to a sixteen-year-old who has been waiting to be seen as capable.
- **The hand on her head:** The same gesture he gave Fallon at the police station. Everything in one touch.

### Terri Anne Williams-Key
- **"Is this about Lazaro? Or something bigger?"** She asks the real question. She does not let Kiley Rose stay in the safe, surface answer. She asks for the full truth and Kiley Rose gives it.
- **"You are not going somewhere we can't reach you. You are going somewhere you choose. That is not the same thing as leaving."** The most important thing Terri Anne says. She reframes the move completely — not departure, not distance, not loss. Choice. Kiley Rose choosing. That is what it is.
- **The final words on the patio:** *"You're going to be a wonderful mother in Natal. You already are one here."* She says it and leaves. She does not wait for a response. Some things don't need one.

## 8. Thematic Motifs

- **Rio as Lazaro's city:** The beach where Kiley Rose first loved him. The beach where she saw him kiss someone else. The city where she carried her daughters and was left. She does not want to raise them in a city full of someone who chose to leave.
- **The singing:** She is singing to them before anyone arrives. Private, unhurried, entirely for the girls. Mike and Terri Anne hear it from the hallway and stop — Mike's hand finding Terri Anne's — and listen before they enter. The singing is the scene's opening image of who Kiley Rose already is as a mother: someone who fills the room with warmth before anyone asks her to. Someone who is already building the world her daughters live in, note by note, in an ordinary bedroom in a city she is about to leave.
- **"Somewhere that's mine:"** Kiley Rose has lived her whole life in her parents' spaces — the Carlisle mansion, the Rio house, the family's orbit. Natal is the first place she will choose entirely for herself and her daughters. The beginning of a life that is theirs.
- **The ask before the plan:** She came to them before she had the details worked out. She asked first. This is both her love for them and her wisdom — she knows she needs their help to do this right, and she is not too proud to say so.
- **The exhale:** The body's response to being trusted. She did not know, until that moment, how much she needed them to say yes. The exhale tells her.
- **The hand on her head:** Mike's gesture — the same one he gave Fallon at the police station two days ago. It means the same thing both times: *I cannot say all of what I feel. This touch says it.*
- **The city she hasn't seen yet:** Natal exists in this scene only as a direction — northeast, far enough, new. She does not know what it looks like yet. That not-knowing is the whole point. She is going toward something she hasn't chosen before.
