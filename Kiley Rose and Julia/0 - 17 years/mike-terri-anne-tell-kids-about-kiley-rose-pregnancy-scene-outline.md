# Telling the Kids About the Pregnancy — Scene Outline

## Setting
- **Time:** **Mid-summer 2028**, during **Terri Anne's sixteenth week of pregnancy**. The family has known privately for some time but waited until the second trimester to tell the children, wanting the news to feel settled and safe.
- **Place:** The Key family kitchen and back den in Halifax. Dinner is finished. The evening light is still bright, the sun lingering high and warm over the longest days of the year.
- **Characters present:** Mike, Terri Anne, Patti Anne (~6), Jessica Isabella and Rachel Darcy (~4, conjoined twins).
- **Mood:** Warm, deliberate, quietly hopeful. The pregnancy is far enough along to feel real; Terri Anne's belly is just beginning to show beneath her summer dress. The announcement carries the weight of a secret kept and now entrusted to the children.

## 1. The Opening Moment — Gathering the Girls
- Mike clears the table while Terri Anne pours milk for the girls.
- Patti Anne senses something is coming; she has noticed her mother's body changing, her dresses fitting differently, and the quiet conversations between her parents after bedtime.
- Mike invites the girls to the couch: "We have something to tell you."
- Jessica and Rachel maneuver together onto the cushions, their shared hip settling automatically. Patti Anne sits on the floor in front of them, facing her parents, already alert and responsible.

## 2. The Announcement
- Terri Anne says it directly: *"Mommy is going to have another baby. You're going to have another sister."*
- Patti Anne's reaction is immediate and practical: she launches into a long list of questions about the baby's care.
  - *"Where will the baby sleep?"*
  - *"Who will feed her?"*
  - *"Will she cry at night?"*
  - *"How do we change her diaper?"*
  - *"Can I hold her?"*
  - *"What if she gets sick?"*
  - *"Will she go to the hospital with Mommy?"*
  - *"Do babies need a special kind of bath?"*
  - *"Can I read to her even if she doesn't understand words yet?"*
- Mike and Terri Anne answer each question with patience and calm, framing Patti Anne's worry as competence rather than anxiety.
- Jessica and Rachel react in their near-unison twin rhythm: first silence, then a shared, radiant grin. They do not ask questions. They simply love the baby already.
- Rachel asks if the baby can sleep in their room. Jessica adds, *"We can share our bed."* They do not see any obstacle to this plan.

## 3. Patti Anne's Care Plan
- Patti Anne declares she will be the baby's helper. She lists her qualifications with growing confidence:
  - She knows how to fold diapers (she has practiced on her dolls).
  - She can read stories, even if the baby only understands the sound of her voice.
  - She can make sure the twins do not accidentally sit on the baby or roll over on her.
  - She can help keep the baby's room quiet when the baby is sleeping.
  - She can help Mommy remember when it is time for the baby to eat.
- Mike tells her she is going to be an excellent big sister, but that the baby will also need time with Mommy and Daddy, especially at first.
- Patti Anne absorbs this with a solemn nod. She is already building a mental schedule for the baby.
- Terri Anne tells her the most important thing she can do is love the baby and be gentle. Patti Anne writes this down mentally as Rule Number One.

## 4. The Twins' Unconditional Love
- Jessica and Rachel want to touch Terri Anne's belly. They have seen pregnant women before but never in their own house.
- Terri Anne lifts her shirt slightly. Her belly is just beginning to round, a soft rise that the twins have not noticed until now.
- The twins reach out with their free hands, one after the other, and press their palms against her.
- They feel nothing yet—the baby is still small—but Terri Anne tells them, *"Right now she's smaller than your thumb. But she will grow."*
- Rachel asks if the baby can hear them. Jessica says, *"Hi, baby. We love you."* Rachel echoes, *"We love you."*
- They decide to call her "Baby Kiley" until she is born, and they already begin talking to her every morning and night.
- Patti Anne, watching them, is both exasperated and moved. *"She can't hear you yet,"* she says. The twins ignore this. They know what they feel.

## 5. The First Hints of Complexity
- Mike and Terri Anne do not mention Turner Syndrome yet. They only say that the baby might be very small when she is born and might need a few extra doctor visits.
- Patti Anne picks up on the word *might*. She asks, *"Is the baby sick?"*
- Terri Anne answers carefully: *"The baby is not sick. But some babies need a little more help to grow strong. This baby might be one of those babies."*
- Patti Anne accepts this, though she files it away and begins planning how to help with doctor visits too.
- Jessica and Rachel do not seem worried. They are fascinated by the idea of a baby needing help, because they themselves need help with stairs and shoes and chairs. To them, needing help is normal and loving.

## 6. The Family Promise
- Mike kneels in front of the girls so they can see his face at their level.
- He tells them: *"No matter how small the baby is, no matter how many doctors she needs, this baby is part of our family. And in our family, we take care of each other."*
- Patti Anne repeats the sentence, almost to herself: *"In our family, we take care of each other."*
- Jessica and Rachel put their free hands together and say, in unison, *"We take care of Kiley."*
- They have already named her. They have already claimed her.
- Terri Anne, watching them, feels the first quiet certainty that this child will be held by many hands.

## 7. Closing Image
- The girls go to bed with questions still forming. Patti Anne's are practical: What kind of crib will they need? How many blankets? Will the baby have their brown eyes or Mommy's blue ones?
- The twins' questions are simpler and deeper: Will Kiley be warm? Will she know them? Can they hold her first?
- Patti Anne, from her bedroom, calls out one last question: *"Can we pray for the baby?"*
- Mike and Terri Anne go to her doorway. The twins are already in their shared bed, their bodies fitted together like puzzle pieces in the moonlight.
- Mike leads a simple prayer: *"God, thank you for the new baby. Keep her safe. Help us love her well. Amen."*
- Patti Anne adds her own line: *"And help us be good big sisters. Especially me."*
- Jessica and Rachel, half-asleep, murmur together: *"We love you, Kiley."*
- The parents laugh quietly, kiss the girls, and close the door.

## 8. Thematic Resonance
- **Patti Anne's competence:** She meets the news with a long list of care questions, revealing her lifelong role as the responsible eldest sister who plans before she emotes.
- **The twins' love:** Jessica and Rachel do not ask how to manage the baby; they simply love her. Their immediate, uncomplicated affection becomes the emotional foundation of Kiley Rose's childhood.
- **Family inclusion:** Even at four, the conjoined twins are not sheltered from the news; they are enlisted as participants and already name their sister.
- **Honest framing:** Mike and Terri Anne do not hide the possibility of extra medical care, but they refuse to let complexity steal the joy.
- **Kiley Rose's inheritance:** Before she is born, she is already prayed for, planned for, named, and loved by three sisters who will each protect her in their own way.
