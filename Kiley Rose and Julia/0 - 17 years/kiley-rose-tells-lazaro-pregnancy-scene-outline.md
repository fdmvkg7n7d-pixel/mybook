# Kiley Rose Tells Lazaro About the Pregnancy — Scene Outline

> **Content boundary:** Kiley Rose is fifteen and Lazaro is sixteen. The conception remains off-page. This scene focuses on the emotional consequence of the pregnancy news, Mike and Terri Anne's protective presence, and the beginning of the relationship fracture.

---

## Setting

- **Time:** Early summer **2044**, shortly after Kiley's positive test and early medical confirmation.
- **Place:** Rio de Janeiro, somewhere public enough to feel safe but private enough for a serious, supervised conversation:
  - a quiet hotel courtyard,
  - a shaded promenade table,
  - a café patio away from the busiest foot traffic,
  - or a quiet edge of the beach with Mike and Terri Anne close beside her.
- **Characters present:** Kiley Rose Key, Lazaro da Silva, Mike Key, Terri Anne Williams-Key.
- **Parent positioning:** Mike and Terri Anne are there because Kiley is fifteen, medically high-risk, and does not want to carry the conversation alone. They do **not** speak for her at first. Kiley leads. They protect the space.
- **Mood:** Tenderness under supervision, first love becoming adult consequence. The scene begins with Kiley trying to tell the boy she loves and ends with her seeing that Lazaro's fear may be stronger than his loyalty.

---

## 1. Kiley Before the Meeting

- Kiley prepares what she is going to say but cannot make the words feel natural.
- She has already heard medical terms from doctors:
  - high-risk,
  - Turner Syndrome,
  - cardiology,
  - aorta,
  - maternal-fetal medicine,
  - biweekly doctor appointments — every two weeks at minimum,
  - Mike or Terri Anne taking her to every appointment,
  - twin pregnancy if this scene happens after the first ultrasound.
- She carries the news physically: one hand drifting toward her abdomen, then pulling away because she is not ready for the gesture to mean something in front of Lazaro.
- Mike offers to handle the conversation if she wants him to.
- Terri Anne offers a softer version: they can sit beside Kiley, and she can speak when ready.
- Kiley chooses the middle path:
  - She wants Mike and Terri Anne present.
  - She wants to say the words herself.
  - She does not want Lazaro to feel ambushed by adults, but she also does not want to be isolated.
- This is an agency beat: Kiley accepts protection without surrendering her voice.

---

## 2. Mike and Terri Anne Set the Ground Rules

- Before Lazaro arrives, Mike quietly reminds Kiley:
  - She can stop the conversation at any time.
  - She does not have to answer invasive questions.
  - She does not have to debate her body.
- Terri Anne reminds her:
  - Facts can be shared calmly.
  - Medical risk belongs in medical rooms with qualified doctors.
  - Fear is not the same thing as authority.
- Kiley asks them not to take over unless she looks to them or Lazaro starts pressuring her.
- Mike agrees, though it costs him effort.
- Terri Anne tells Kiley she will be watching her breathing and her face, not just listening to the words.

---

## 3. Lazaro Arrives as the Boy She Loves

- Lazaro arrives still inside the old version of their relationship.
- He starts to smile, then sees Mike and Terri Anne with Kiley.
- The smile falters. He understands immediately that this is serious.
- He may greet Mike and Terri Anne awkwardly, trying to be polite while looking at Kiley for explanation.
- Kiley notices everything she is about to lose:
  - his familiar voice,
  - the way Rio light falls around him,
  - the ease they had before adults had to sit near them.
- Lazaro asks if something happened.
- Kiley hears the fear already entering his voice.

---

## 4. Kiley Says It Directly

- Kiley does not let Mike or Terri Anne speak first.
- She says:
  - *"I'm pregnant."*
- Lazaro initially does not understand or refuses to understand.
- He looks from Kiley to her parents, as if one of them will correct her.
- Mike does not correct anything.
- Terri Anne stays still and lets Kiley own the sentence.
- Lazaro asks if she is sure.
- Kiley says there was a test, then a doctor.
- If the twin ultrasound has already happened, she adds:
  - *"There are two."*
- If the twin reveal is being saved for another scene, she only says she is pregnant and medically high-risk.
- Lazaro's expression changes from confusion to panic.

---

## 5. Lazaro's First Reaction

- Lazaro asks practical questions too fast:
  - How far along?
  - Are they sure?
  - Can Turner Syndrome make the test wrong?
  - What did the doctor say?
  - Does her whole family know?
  - What are they supposed to do?
- His questions are not cruel yet; they are fear trying to organize itself.
- Kiley answers as calmly as she can.
- She explains that Turner Syndrome makes the pregnancy more dangerous, not less real.
- She tells him that doctors are taking it seriously and that she will have **biweekly appointments** with her doctor/high-risk team — **every two weeks at minimum**.
- She adds that Mike or Terri Anne will take her every time, and that she is comfortable with Mike staying through full appointments because he is her father, protector, and advocate.
- Terri Anne only adds medical clarification if Lazaro starts misunderstanding the facts.
- Mike remains quiet, but his presence makes it clear Kiley is not alone.

---

## 6. The Word “Impossible”

- Lazaro says some version of:
  - *"But I thought you couldn't."*
- Kiley feels the sting of hearing her own medical history turned into disbelief.
- She repeats what Terri Anne told her:
  - *"Rare isn't the same as never."*
- This line connects mother and daughter across scenes.
- Terri Anne's face shows that Kiley has remembered the lesson exactly.
- Lazaro looks younger after hearing it, as if the future has arrived too fast.
- Mike's silence becomes heavier because he hears the first sign that Lazaro is not ready.

---

## 7. Kiley Hopes for Partnership

- Kiley tells Lazaro she is scared.
- She does not ask him for solutions; she asks him to stand with her in the truth.
- She may say:
  - *"I don't need you to know everything right now. I just need you not to run."*
- Lazaro wants to comfort her, but he is acutely aware of Mike and Terri Anne watching.
- His fear is already bigger than his tenderness.
- He says they are too young.
- Kiley says she knows.
- He says this could hurt her.
- Kiley says she knows that too.
- Terri Anne gently reinforces that Kiley's health is being taken seriously.
- Mike adds, if needed, that no one in this conversation is pretending the situation is simple.

---

## 8. The First Sign of Pressure

- Lazaro does not say *abortion* immediately if you want the scene to fracture slowly.
- Instead, he says:
  - *"There are options."*
  - *"My parents will know what to do."*
  - *"Maybe the doctors can fix this."*
- Kiley hears what is underneath.
- Mike shifts physically — not interrupting yet, but ready.
- Terri Anne watches Kiley, letting Kiley decide whether to answer.
- Kiley asks Lazaro what he means by *options*.
- He avoids answering directly.
- Kiley becomes still.
- The stillness echoes Mike's stillness from the test scene, but hers is protective.

---

## 9. Mike and Terri Anne Hold the Boundary

- If Lazaro keeps circling around pressure, Mike speaks for the first time.
- Mike does not threaten him. He clarifies the rules:
  - Kiley is here to tell him the truth.
  - She is not here to be cornered.
  - Medical decisions will not be debated by frightened teenagers or pressured families.
- Terri Anne adds the medical boundary:
  - Kiley's risks are real.
  - The doctors are involved.
  - Risk is not a license for anyone to take over Kiley's body.
- This shows Mike and Terri Anne as protective without stealing the scene from Kiley.

---

## 10. Kiley States Her Boundary

- Kiley says she has not figured out every detail yet, but no one gets to speak about her body as if she is not there.
- She makes clear that the pregnancy is medically serious and she will listen to doctors, but pressure is different from care.
- If her Catholic faith is included in this scene, she says plainly that termination is not something she can choose.
- She does not argue theology at length; she simply states the ground she stands on.
- Possible line:
  - *"I am scared enough without you trying to make my fear into your decision."*
- Mike and Terri Anne let her sentence stand. They do not soften it for Lazaro.

---

## 11. Lazaro Retreats Emotionally

- Lazaro becomes overwhelmed.
- He may say he needs time.
- He may say he cannot talk until he speaks to his parents.
- He may insist that his parents will know what is best.
- Mike notices the shift: Lazaro is moving the decision away from Kiley and toward adults who are not her parents.
- Kiley realizes the same thing more painfully:
  - She came to tell her boyfriend.
  - Now she is about to face a family negotiation.
- Lazaro is still physically present, but emotionally he has stepped back.

---

## 12. Kiley Leaves With the News Still Hers

- Kiley does not beg him to stay.
- She may cry, but not in a way that surrenders her boundary.
- She tells him any future conversation with his parents will include Mike and Terri Anne.
- She walks away first if possible.
- This is important: she is not abandoned in the scene's final motion; she chooses to leave a conversation that is no longer safe for her.
- Mike and Terri Anne walk with her, not ahead of her.
- They do not demand immediate emotional processing.
- Kiley says only:
  - *"He wants his parents to decide."*
- Mike understands the danger in that sentence.
- Terri Anne understands the grief.

---

## 13. Closing Image

- Kiley looks back once and sees Lazaro still standing there, smaller than he looked before.
- Rio remains beautiful, which makes the heartbreak sharper.
- The place that once held first love now holds the first fracture.
- Kiley places one hand over her abdomen, this time not pulling it away.
- Mike stays close on one side; Terri Anne stays close on the other.
- She is frightened, but the news is no longer waiting to be spoken, and she did not have to speak it alone.

---

## Thematic Resonance

- **Protected agency:** Mike and Terri Anne are present, but Kiley remains the one who tells the truth.
- **First love versus first consequence:** Lazaro can participate in romance, but adulthood frightens him.
- **Medical disbelief:** Kiley's Turner Syndrome becomes a source of others doubting what her body is doing.
- **Support versus control:** The Key parents protect without replacing Kiley's voice.
- **The beginning of abandonment:** Lazaro does not disappear all at once; he retreats one step at a time toward his parents' authority.
- **Kiley's strength:** She is scared, young, and heartbroken, but she remains the speaker of her own truth.
