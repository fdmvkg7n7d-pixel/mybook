# Kiley Rose Tells Mike and Terri Anne — Lazaro Sighting and Bisexual Disclosure — Scene Outline

> **Scope:** The same evening as the beach sighting. Kiley Rose comes home, sits on the patio with Mike and Terri Anne, and tells them what she saw. The pain of Lazaro on the beach unlocks a truth she has been carrying longer than today — she is bisexual. She asks them, in the most frightened voice she has used since the delivery room, whether they still love her and her daughters. She is sixteen years old. She is nursing Pandora Paige. She is not entirely sure she will survive this conversation.
>
> **Time:** ~May 2045. The twins are five months old. Kiley Rose is sixteen.
> **Place:** The family patio — open air, early evening light, the particular quiet of a household that has put its children to bed or settled them. Safe ground.
> **Characters present:** Kiley Rose, Mike Key, Terri Anne Williams-Key. Brandi Jessica is asleep nearby or in a bassinet on the patio. Pandora Paige is nursing at Kiley Rose's breast throughout much of the scene.
> **Mood:** Raw grief → confession → terror → absolute love.

---

## 1. Coming Home

- **The state she is in:** She has walked home from the beach with the twins in the stroller, holding herself together for the length of the walk the way she held herself together through bed rest, through the delivery room, through every appointment where someone named a risk and she chose to carry it anyway. By the time she reaches the house, the holding is almost gone.
- **What she does first:** She does not go straight to Mike and Terri Anne. She takes the twins inside. She changes them. She settles Brandi Jessica into the bassinet on the patio — asleep, or nearly. She picks up Pandora Paige, who is hungry, and sits down in the patio chair with her and begins to nurse. She does this first because it is what Pandora Paige needs, and doing something concrete for her daughters is how Kiley Rose has always steadied herself.
- **Mike and Terri Anne:** They are already on the patio — the particular easy quiet of two parents in the evening, with the day mostly done. They see Kiley Rose's face when she sits down. Terri Anne sees it first, the way she always sees things first. She does not say anything immediately. She waits. She has learned that Kiley Rose comes to things at her own pace, and the worst thing you can do is rush her.
- **The silence before:** Kiley Rose nurses Pandora Paige and looks at nothing in particular. Pandora Paige nurses with the complete absorption of a five-month-old who does not know what her mother is carrying. Brandi Jessica sleeps in the bassinet beside them, her small chest rising and falling. The evening light is soft. None of this should feel like the setting for what is about to happen, and yet it is.

---

## 2. Lazaro — The Beach

- **How it starts:** Kiley Rose does not plan an opening. She simply begins, the way you begin when you have been holding something since morning and your body is tired of the weight:

  *"I saw Lazaro today. At the beach."*

- **The pause:** Mike sets down whatever he is holding. Terri Anne goes still. Neither of them speaks. They give her the space to continue.

- **What she tells them:** She describes it plainly at first — the stroller, the walk, the familiar figure across the sand. She describes the children: three of them, playing around him. She describes the woman. And then she describes the kiss — not because she wants to, but because it is the thing she cannot not say. Deep. Passionate. The kind of kiss that announces a whole life.

  Her voice stays mostly even until she gets to the kiss. Then it doesn't.

  *"He looked — he looked like he was exactly where he wanted to be. Like it was easy. Like the whole thing was easy for him."*

- **What she does not say yet:** She does not say *and he left me alone with two daughters*. She does not say *and I nearly died carrying them*. She does not say *and I was so in love with him*. She does not have to. Mike and Terri Anne know every one of those sentences already.

- **Pandora Paige, nursing:** Throughout this, Pandora Paige nurses. Kiley Rose's hand rests on her daughter's back — the automatic, continuous contact of a nursing mother. Pandora Paige does not know that her mother is falling apart above her. She only knows the warmth and the milk and the heartbeat. Kiley Rose's hand on her back tightens slightly when her voice breaks, then loosens again deliberately.

- **Mike's response:** He does not reach for Kiley Rose immediately. He knows her — she does not always want to be touched when she is raw. He sits forward, elbows on his knees, and looks at her with his full attention. When she finishes, he says:

  *"I'm sorry you had to see that."*

  Not: *he's not worth it.* Not: *you're better off.* Just: *I'm sorry you had to see that.* Because she did. And she shouldn't have had to. And that is the truth.

- **Terri Anne's response:** She moves her chair closer to Kiley Rose. Not touching — just closer. A narrowing of the distance, an offer of proximity that Kiley Rose can take or leave.

  *"Do you want to talk about it more, or did you just need to say it?"*

  Kiley Rose thinks about this.

  *"Both,"* she says. *"I think both."*

---

## 3. The Door Opens

- **The transition:** They sit for a moment in the aftermath of Lazaro — in the particular quiet that follows grief that has been named out loud. Pandora Paige has finished nursing. Kiley Rose adjusts her, holds her against her shoulder, begins to pat her back softly. The motion is rhythmic and automatic. It gives her hands something to do.
- **What comes next:** She does not plan it. She has not planned it. But something about saying *he kissed her like he meant it, like it was easy* has opened a door she has been keeping shut, and now it is open, and what is behind it is not about Lazaro at all.
- **The confession:** She does not look at either of them when she says it. She looks at Pandora Paige's head, at the soft dark hair, at the small perfect weight of her in her arms.

  *"I need to tell you something else."*

  Mike and Terri Anne wait.

  *"When I watched him kiss her — I wasn't just upset because he left. I was upset because —"*

  She stops. Starts again.

  *"I've been — I think I've known for a while that I'm — that I like — that it's not just boys. It hasn't been just boys for a long time."*

  Another stop. She makes herself finish it.

  *"I'm bisexual."*

  The word lands in the evening air. Kiley Rose watches Pandora Paige's back rise and fall. She does not look up.

---

## 4. The Question

- **The silence:** It is not a long silence. But it feels long to Kiley Rose, the way silences feel long when everything you are depends on what comes next.
- **She fills it before they can:** Because she is sixteen and she is terrified and the silence is unbearable:

  *"Do you still love us?"*

  Her voice breaks completely on the last word. *Us* — not just her. Her daughters. Brandi Jessica asleep in the bassinet. Pandora Paige warm against her chest. She is not only asking for herself. She has never been only asking for herself.

  *"Do you still love them? And me? Now that you know?"*

  She is crying now — not the controlled, deliberate tears of someone who has decided to cry, but the kind that arrive without permission and cannot be called back. Pandora Paige, sensing the change in her mother's body, makes a small uncertain sound. Kiley Rose's hand moves in circles on her back. *I'm here. I'm here. I'm here.*

---

## 5. Mike and Terri Anne Answer

- **Mike moves first:** He gets out of his chair and crouches in front of Kiley Rose — the same crouch, the same eye-level, the same absolute presence he has offered her at every door she has been afraid to walk through. He puts one hand on her knee. He waits until she looks at him.

  When she does, he says:

  *"Kiley Rose. Look at me."*

  She looks.

  *"Nothing you are changes what you are to us. Do you understand me? Nothing."*

  He does not rush past it. He holds her gaze and lets it land.

  *"You are our daughter. Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige are our granddaughters. That is not a conditional sentence. There is no 'unless' in it. There never was."*

- **Terri Anne:** She reaches across and takes Kiley Rose's free hand — the one not holding Pandora Paige — and holds it in both of hers. She does not say many words. She does not need to. But what she says, she says clearly:

  *"We have loved you since before you were born. We will love you when you are old and grey and Pandora Paige's grandchildren are running around your feet. Nothing in between changes that. Not Lazaro. Not who you love. Nothing."*

  A beat.

  *"And those girls —"* she looks at Pandora Paige, at Brandi Jessica in the bassinet, *"— those girls have been ours since December. That doesn't change either."*

- **Kiley Rose crying:** She cries the way people cry when the thing they were most afraid of does not happen — not with relief exactly, but with the particular exhaustion of someone who has been braced for a blow that never comes. She does not try to stop. She holds Pandora Paige and cries, and Mike stays crouched in front of her, and Terri Anne holds her hand, and the evening goes on around them, indifferent and gentle.

- **Pandora Paige:** She has settled again — soothed by the circles Kiley Rose keeps drawing on her back, by the heartbeat she knows, by the body she trusts completely. She does not know what this conversation has cost her mother. She only knows that her mother is here. She closes her eyes.

---

## 6. After — The Patio, Quieter Now

- **The shift:** The worst is past. The three of them sit in the aftermath — not fixed, not resolved, but through the hardest part. Kiley Rose wipes her face with her free hand. She looks at Pandora Paige, then at Brandi Jessica in the bassinet, still asleep.
- **Mike sits back in his chair:** He does not go far. He stays close in the way he stays close — present without pressing.
- **Terri Anne keeps Kiley Rose's hand:** For a little while longer. Then Kiley Rose squeezes it once, and Terri Anne lets go, and the gesture is complete.
- **What Kiley Rose says, eventually:** Not about Lazaro. Not about being bisexual. About the twins:

  *"I just want them to be okay. Whatever else happens. I just want them to be okay."*

  Terri Anne:

  *"They are okay. Look at them."*

  Kiley Rose looks. Pandora Paige asleep against her chest. Brandi Jessica asleep in the bassinet, her small fists curled beside her face.

  She nods. Something settles.

- **Mike, quietly:** *"You're going to be okay too, you know."*

  Kiley Rose considers this for a moment.

  *"Yeah,"* she says. *"I think I know that."*

  She does not entirely believe it yet. But she is starting to.

---

## 7. Mike Lights the Barbecue

- **The turn:** Mike stands. Not abruptly — he lets the quiet finish itself first. Then he stands, puts a hand briefly on Kiley Rose's shoulder as he passes, and walks to the barbecue. He does not announce what he is doing. He simply does it. This is how he has always brought the family back from hard places — not with words, but with charcoal and tongs and the smell of something real being made.
- **The barbecue:** The Key family's Brazilian tradition. Churrasco — meat on long skewers over charcoal, the way it is done in Brazil, where the family has spent time and where so much of their shared history lives. Tonight he is making a full spread:
  - **Churrasco** — picanha (top sirloin cap), chicken thighs, and linguiça (Brazilian pork sausage) on long skewers over the charcoal, rotated slowly, basted with nothing but coarse salt and time. The fat renders. The outside chars. The smell reaches every corner of the house.
  - **Arroz e feijão preto** — white rice and black beans, the backbone of every Brazilian meal. Terri Anne starts the beans inside — they have been soaking since morning, because this is a tradition and traditions are planned for — and brings them out to the patio table when they are ready. Bay leaf, garlic, and a curl of bacon fat.
  - **Farofa** — toasted cassava flour with butter, onion, and a little parsley. Mike makes it in a pan on the side burner, stirring it until it is golden and fragrant. Kiley Rose has loved farofa since she was small. He knows this.
  - **Vinagrete** — a simple Brazilian salsa of tomato, onion, white vinegar, olive oil, and fresh parsley. Terri Anne makes it at the outdoor table with a knife and board, chopping while the meat cooks.
  - **Pão de queijo** — Brazilian cheese bread, small and round and impossible to resist. Terri Anne keeps a batch in the freezer for nights like this. She puts them in the oven and they come out golden and hot and smelling like home.
- **The charcoal smell:** It reaches inside the house within minutes. This is not accidental. Mike knows what charcoal on a Brazilian evening does to a Key family — it pulls people toward the patio the way music pulls people toward a dance floor. He has done this before. He knows exactly what he is doing.

---

## 8. The Family Gathers

- **The sisters drift out:** One by one, drawn by the smell and the particular quality of light on the patio that means something good is happening. Patti Anne is in Vaughan with one-year-old Gamaliel Galvin — her own house, her own baby, her own evening. Jessica Isabella (20) and Emma (20) are in Halifax, attending Dalhousie University, raising four-year-old identical twins Samira Chaka and Sahara Hannan. Rachel Darcy (20) is in Charlottesville, Virginia, attending the University of Virginia and living with Antoine. But the four younger sisters are home, and the charcoal reaches them the way it always does.
- **Sabrina Bryna (14):** The first out. She reads the patio the way she reads every room — by face and posture rather than sound. Mike signs to her as he turns the skewers — *food soon, sit down, you look hungry* — and she signs back something that makes him laugh. She settles into the chair closest to Kiley Rose and watches the charcoal with the focused appreciation of someone who understands that fire and patience produce something worth waiting for.
- **Fallon Glenda (12):** She comes out next, still in whatever she was wearing for the afternoon, and gravitates immediately to the grill to watch Mike work. She does not hover — she just stands near him with her arms folded, asking occasional questions about the picanha that Mike answers without taking his eyes off the fire. It is their particular mode of being together: side by side, mostly quiet, comfortable.
- **Andrea Jennifer (10) and Ashley Erin (8):** The youngest two appear together, Ashley Erin slightly ahead, already reaching for the pão de queijo the moment Terri Anne sets the basket on the table.

  Terri Anne: *"Those are hot."*

  Ashley Erin: *"I know."*

  She takes one anyway. Terri Anne does not stop her. Andrea Jennifer sits beside Kiley Rose — not too close, not too far — and does not ask anything. She just sits. She is already the observer, already the one who understands that sometimes presence is the whole offering.

- **The patio, gathered:** Within twenty minutes of Mike lighting the barbecue, Kiley Rose (16), Sabrina Bryna (14), Fallon Glenda (12), Andrea Jennifer (10), Ashley Erin (8), Mike, and Terri Anne are all on the patio in various configurations — some at the table, some near the grill, some on the steps. The twins are the calm centre of it: Brandi Jessica still asleep in the bassinet, Pandora Paige passed gently to whoever Kiley Rose trusts to hold her while she moves to the table.

## 9. The Meal

- **Mike at the grill:** He is entirely himself here — unhurried, precise, rotating the skewers at the right moment, testing the picanha with a finger, keeping the sausage from the hottest part of the fire. He has a glass of something cold beside the grill. He talks to whoever drifts close without ever losing track of what is on the fire.
- **The table filling:** Terri Anne moves between the kitchen and the patio — beans, rice, vinagrete, farofa, bread — setting the table without ceremony, the way the family has always done it. No one is assigned a seat. People sit where they end up.
- **The meat coming off the grill:** Mike brings the skewers to the table on a long wooden board and carves directly at the table — the traditional Brazilian service, sliced thin and served immediately while it is still resting and hot. The picanha goes first, pink in the centre, charred at the edge, the fat cap crisped and salty. Then the linguiça, split and smoky. Then the chicken, falling off the skewer.
- **The eating:** Loud, overlapping, the way Key family meals are loud. Someone reaches across someone else. Farofa gets spilled. Ashley Erin takes a second piece of pão de queijo and Terri Anne pretends not to notice. Fallon Glenda and Andrea Jennifer argue briefly and cheerfully about who gets the last of the linguiça — Fallon wins on the grounds that she stood by the grill longest. Sabrina Bryna signs something to Mike and he signs back and they both look satisfied.
- **Kiley Rose at the table:** She eats. Slowly at first, then properly — the food reaching her in the way food reaches people after grief, as something the body was waiting for without knowing it. She does not talk much. She does not need to. The family is around her. The table is full. Pandora Paige is warm in whoever's arms she is currently in. Brandi Jessica has woken and is being held by Sabrina Bryna, who holds her with the careful, sure-handed steadiness of someone who has been waiting all evening for a turn.
- **The farofa:** When Kiley Rose reaches for the farofa, Mike notices across the table. He says nothing. But something in his face settles — the small satisfaction of a father who made the right thing.

## 10. Closing Image — The Patio at Night

- **After the meal:** The dishes are not rushed. The table stays full of people long after the food is gone — the particular Key family habit of lingering, of treating the end of a meal as an invitation to stay rather than a signal to leave.
- **The twins:** Brandi Jessica is passed back to Kiley Rose, who nurses her now — the evening feeding, the one that usually means the longest sleep. Pandora Paige is drowsy in Terri Anne's arms across the table.
- **The charcoal:** The grill has gone low — orange embers, no more flame. Mike sits beside it with his arms on his knees, watching it the way men watch fires at the end of an evening. Easy. Not thinking about anything in particular. Or thinking about everything and choosing to let it rest.
- **The sisters:** Fallon Glenda has drifted inside. Andrea Jennifer is still at the table, turning her glass slowly in her hands. Ashley Erin is cross-legged on the patio steps, picking at the last of the farofa with a spoon. Sabrina Bryna sits close to the bassinet, one hand resting lightly on its edge, watching Brandi Jessica sleep. The patio has that end-of-evening quality — warm and a little drowsy, nobody quite ready to say goodnight.
- **Kiley Rose:** She is nursing Brandi Jessica. The table is still around her — the empty dishes, the farofa bowl scraped almost clean, the vinagrete with its little pool of olive oil and parsley. She looks at it all and feels, not happiness exactly, but something adjacent to it. Something solid. The word for it is *home.*

## 11. Character Work

### Kiley Rose
- **What she carries into this scene:** The shock of the beach. The old grief of Lazaro's abandonment. A truth about herself she has known *for a long time* and has not said out loud to anyone.
- **Why Lazaro unlocks it:** Watching him kiss that woman — being confronted with the full picture of what he chose instead of staying — forces Kiley Rose to reckon with what she actually wants, not just what she lost. And what she actually wants is more complicated than a boy on a beach in Rio. It always was.
- **The *us* in her question:** She does not ask *do you still love me.* She asks *do you still love us.* She has been a mother for five months and she is already incapable of asking for herself without asking for them too.
- **Pandora Paige as anchor:** The nursing is not incidental. It is what holds Kiley Rose in her body throughout this scene — the physical demand of a baby who needs her, the warmth, the weight, the motion of her hand on that small back. It keeps her from disappearing into the grief.

### Mike Key
- **The crouch:** He does it again. He always comes down to her level first. At the Capoeira studio door, on the beach with the strangers, and now here — on the patio, in the dark, in front of his sixteen-year-old daughter who is asking whether she is still loved.
- **His words:** *"Nothing you are changes what you are to us."* He does not say *I don't care that you're bisexual* — because that would make it something to be dismissed rather than something to be received. He receives it. He holds it. He tells her it changes nothing.
- **What he does not do:** He does not make it about himself. He does not say *I had a feeling* or *I always knew* or *that explains things.* He does not make her bisexuality a subject for him. He makes it a fact about her that he loves along with every other fact about her.

### Terri Anne Williams-Key
- **The chair:** She moves closer before she speaks. Distance narrowed, not eliminated — letting Kiley Rose choose.
- **Her words:** *"We have loved you since before you were born."* Backwards and forwards. Not conditional on anything that has happened since.
- **The granddaughters:** She names Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige explicitly — answers the full question Kiley Rose asked. She does not make Kiley Rose ask it twice.

### Pandora Paige
- **Function:** She is the most important person in this scene who says nothing. Her presence — nursing, sleeping, breathing against her mother's chest — is the reason Kiley Rose holds herself together enough to speak. She is also the reason the *us* in Kiley Rose's question is so devastating: she is five months old and she is already someone her mother would step in front of anything for.

### Brandi Jessica
- **Function:** Asleep in the bassinet. Present without knowing it. She and her sister form the two poles of Kiley Rose's world in this scene — the one she is holding and the one she is watching over.

## 12. Thematic Motifs

- **The word *us*:** Kiley Rose does not ask for herself alone. She never does. From the moment the twins were born, her love and her fear have been plural. The question *do you still love us* is the most Kiley Rose sentence in the scene.
- **The hand on the back:** Throughout the scene, Kiley Rose's hand moves in circles on Pandora Paige's back — automatic, continuous, the motion of a mother keeping her daughter calm while she herself is falling apart. It is one of the most quietly devastating images in the scene.
- **The crouch:** Mike comes down to her level. He has always come down to her level. This is who he is.
- **The beach, again:** The beach is never a neutral space for Kiley Rose. It is where she first loved. Where she first saw the beach sighting. And now where something was cracked open that led her home to this patio and to this truth. The beach gives and takes and gives again.
- **The nursing:** Pandora Paige nurses through the hardest part of the conversation. The body keeps going. The baby keeps feeding. Life keeps its own rhythm even when the person living it is broken open.
- **Nothing in between:** Terri Anne's phrase — *we will love you when you are old and grey... nothing in between changes that* — collapses past and future into a single unconditional present. It is the most complete answer to Kiley Rose's question.
