# Jessica Isabella and Emma Discuss Fallon Glenda — Scene Outline

> **Scope:** The evening of May 3, 2045. Hours after Mike Key called Halifax to deliver the devastating news about twelve-year-old Fallon Glenda. The four-year-old twins, Samira Chaka and Sahara Hannan, are finally asleep. In the quiet of their Halifax apartment, Jessica Isabella and Emma sit together to process the weight of what they have learned. The conversation explores the specific resonance the news has for them—two mothers raising children born from a situation that "rhymes" with Fallon's—and Jessica Isabella's deep understanding of the guilt Fallon is carrying.
>
> **Time:** May 3, 2045. Evening.
> **Place:** Jessica Isabella and Emma's apartment in Halifax, Nova Scotia.
> **Characters present:** Jessica Isabella Key (20), Emma Fritz (20). (Samira Chaka and Sahara Hannan are asleep in the next room).
> **Mood:** Intimate, heavy, reflective, and fiercely protective.

---

## 1. The Quiet Apartment

- **The evening routine:** The day has been a blur of classes and parenting, all executed while carrying the heavy weight of Mike's morning phone call. Now, the apartment is finally quiet. The four-year-old twins are asleep. 
- **The physical space:** Jessica Isabella and Emma are sitting on the sofa in their small living room. The TV is off. Only a single lamp is on. They have not really talked about it yet—they needed the silence of the evening to let the truth land fully.
- **The exhaustion of carrying it:** Emma leans her head against the back of the sofa, letting out a long, slow breath. Jessica Isabella is sitting forward, her elbows on her knees, staring at the coffee table.

## 2. The Resonance of the Story

- **Emma speaks first:** Emma breaks the silence. She brings up the unfinished sentence from the morning phone call—her offer that the twins are there if Fallon ever needs to talk to someone who understands. 
- **The parallel:** Emma names it gently. Samira Chaka and Sahara Hannan were born to Emma when she was sixteen, as a result of assault. While Fallon’s situation (twelve years old, a trusted teacher) is its own specific horror, the resulting reality—a child born of trauma—rhymes deeply with their own lives. 
- **The difference:** Emma points out that when she was pregnant, she was terrified and hiding it until Mike Key found her on the stairs in Carlisle. She had Jessica Isabella beside her. Fallon is four years younger and had to carry it completely alone in a foreign country. 

## 3. Jessica Isabella and the Guilt

- **Jessica Isabella’s perspective:** Jessica Isabella finally speaks. She isn't focused on the logistics of the pregnancy right now; she is focused on the emotional terror Fallon must be feeling.
- **The bedroom door:** Jessica Isabella brings up the image that struck her most on the phone call: Fallon knocking on their parents' bedroom door. 
- **Understanding the shame:** Jessica Isabella knows, intimately, what it feels like to carry guilt that doesn't belong to you. When she was burned as a child, and when she struggled with her own complex medical realities, she often felt like a burden to the family. She knows that twelve-year-old Fallon—who hid in oversized clothes and apologized to her parents for ruining everything—is currently drowning in the belief that this is her fault.
- **The decree:** Jessica Isabella looks at Emma, her voice hard and absolute. *"We are not going to let her apologize. When we see her, if she says she's sorry, we stop her immediately. She doesn't get to carry the blame for what an adult did to her."*

## 4. Preparing for Carlisle

- **Looking forward:** Emma nods, agreeing completely. She reaches out and takes Jessica Isabella’s hand. 
- **The logistics of love:** They begin to plan their trip to Carlisle. They know Mike and Terri Anne will bring the family back to Ontario soon. They discuss how they will balance their university classes with a trip home to see Fallon.
- **What they will bring:** They won't bring pity. Emma notes that Fallon will have enough doctors and adults managing her life. What Jessica Isabella and Emma can bring is normalcy—the loud, chaotic, ordinary love of older sisters and four-year-old nieces who will just want to play with their young aunt. 

## 5. The Closing Affirmation

- **The shared history:** Jessica Isabella squeezes Emma's hand. She looks toward the closed door of the girls' bedroom, where Samira Chaka and Sahara Hannan are sleeping safely.
- **The promise:** *"My dad saved you on the stairs,"* Jessica Isabella says quietly. *"And he's going to save her now. We just have to be the people she can sit with when she's too tired to be brave."*
- **The end of the night:** The scene ends with the two of them sitting in the quiet apartment, anchored by their shared history, ready to be exactly what Fallon needs them to be when the time comes.

## 6. Character Work

### Jessica Isabella (20)
- **The Empath:** Her immediate focus isn't on the medical reality, but the psychological one. Because of her own history with trauma and feelings of burden, she perfectly diagnoses Fallon's crushing guilt. Her absolute refusal to let Fallon apologize shows her fierce, protective growth.

### Emma Fritz (20)
- **The Anchor:** She provides the lived experience. She knows exactly what it means to be a teenage girl pregnant from trauma. She is already thinking about how to integrate her daughters—who are living proof that a beautiful life can grow from a dark beginning—into Fallon's healing process.

## 7. Thematic Motifs

- **The Rhyming History:** The Key family saga frequently uses parallel experiences to bind the sisters together. Emma’s trauma at sixteen and Fallon’s trauma at twelve are distinct, but they create a profound, unspoken bridge of understanding between Halifax and Carlisle.
- **The Bedroom Door/The Stairs:** Jessica Isabella comparing Fallon knocking on the bedroom door in Rio to Mike finding Emma on the stairs in Carlisle reinforces the idea of the Key family adults as the ultimate safety net—the people you go to when you can't carry the secret anymore.
- **Intercepting the Guilt:** Just as Kiley Rose will stop Fallon from apologizing in Rio, Jessica Isabella determines from hundreds of miles away that she will do the exact same thing. The sisters instinctively know how to protect each other's minds.
