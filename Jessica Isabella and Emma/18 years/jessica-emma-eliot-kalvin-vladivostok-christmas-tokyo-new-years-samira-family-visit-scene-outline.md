# Scene Outline: Vladivostok Christmas with Samira Chaka and Andrei, then Tokyo New Year's Stopover

## Placement

**When:** Christmas season / New Year, when **Eliot David and Kalvin LaMarcus are sixteen years old**. **Christmas happens first** with **Samira Chaka, Andrei, and Caylee Brea** in Vladivostok. After that Christmas visit, Jessica Isabella, Emma, Eliot David, and Kalvin LaMarcus stop in **Tokyo for New Year's Eve**. On **New Year's Day**, the boys' father, **Dr. Rémi Arsenault**, flies to Tokyo. **Eliot David and Kalvin LaMarcus stay in Tokyo with Rémi for a week**, while **Jessica Isabella and Emma fly back to Vancouver**. On the seventh day, Rémi flies back to Vancouver with the twins; the next day, Rémi leaves the twins with Jessica Isabella and Emma.

**Where:** **Vladivostok** first — Samira Chaka and Andrei's home, Samira's dance studio, and Christmas spaces with Caylee Brea. Then **Tokyo** — the New Year's Eve / New Year's Day stopover and the boys' one-week stay with Rémi. Jessica Isabella and Emma then fly back to **Vancouver**. Rémi and the twins return to **Vancouver** together on the seventh day, and Rémi leaves the twins the next day.

**Characters:** Jessica Isabella, Emma, Eliot David, Kalvin LaMarcus, Samira Chaka, Andrei, Caylee Brea, Dr. Rémi Arsenault. Sahara Hannan, Vladislav, and Shahira Hala may be referenced or appear by iPhone, but they are not the destination of this trip.

---

## Scene Purpose

1. Establish that **Christmas with Samira Chaka, Andrei, and Caylee Brea comes first**.
2. Show **Jessica Isabella, Emma, Eliot David, and Kalvin LaMarcus visiting Samira Chaka and Andrei** in Vladivostok.
3. Show Samira's adult life directly: wife, mother, dance-studio owner, and still Jessica Isabella and Emma's daughter.
4. Let Eliot David and Kalvin LaMarcus spend Christmas with their niece **Caylee Brea** and see their older sister's world.
5. Establish that after Christmas, they travel through / stop in **Tokyo for New Year's Eve**.
6. Establish that **Eliot David and Kalvin LaMarcus's father, Dr. Rémi Arsenault, flies to Tokyo to be with his sons for New Year's Day**.
7. Establish that **Eliot David and Kalvin LaMarcus stay in Tokyo with Rémi for a week**.
8. Establish that **Jessica Isabella and Emma fly back to Vancouver** after handing the boys over to Rémi in Tokyo.
9. Establish that the twins attend **Saint George School** in Vancouver.
10. Establish that on the seventh day, **Rémi flies back to Vancouver with Eliot David and Kalvin LaMarcus** on the same-day time-zone flight.
11. Establish that **the next day, Rémi leaves the twins** with Jessica Isabella and Emma.
12. Keep the corrected Sahara boundary intact: Sahara Hannan has Sugar Diabetes, works for the Russian embassy as an intelligence officer alongside Vladislav, and Samira does not know or suspect that embassy / intelligence work.

---

## Core Emotional Throughline

This trip begins with visible adulthood.

Samira Chaka's life can be touched: her home, her husband Andrei, her daughter Caylee Brea, her dance studio, the city she chose, and the routines she built around motherhood, marriage, and art. Jessica Isabella and Emma can stand inside it and see that their daughter is not merely surviving far away — she is rooted.

That visibility brings pride. It also clarifies the current twin contrast. **Samira Chaka owns a dance studio.** **Sahara Hannan works for the Russian embassy as an intelligence officer alongside Vladislav.** Samira knows about Sahara's husband, daughter, and Sugar Diabetes, but she does not know or suspect Sahara's embassy / intelligence work.

After Christmas, Tokyo becomes the hinge: not home, not the first destination, but a bright pause between one family gathering and the new year. They spend New Year's Eve there, suspended between countries, time zones, and family truths.

Then, on New Year's Day, Rémi flies to Tokyo to be with Eliot David and Kalvin LaMarcus. He is their father. He is not replacing Jessica Isabella or Emma; he is showing up in the exact way the family has made room for him to show up. The boys do not have to choose which version of family is real. All of it is real.

After Rémi arrives, the shape of the trip changes again: **Eliot David and Kalvin LaMarcus stay in Tokyo with Rémi for a week**, while **Jessica Isabella and Emma fly back to Vancouver**. The handoff is not abandonment. It is trust: the boys are old enough to spend a week in Tokyo with their father, and Jessica and Emma can leave because Rémi has shown up. They are also old enough to return to their real Vancouver school rhythm afterward: the twins attend **Saint George School**.

# Part One — Christmas in Vladivostok with Samira Chaka's Family

## Beat 1: Arrival in Vladivostok for Christmas

- Jessica Isabella, Emma, Eliot David, and Kalvin LaMarcus arrive in Vladivostok for Christmas with **Samira Chaka's family**.
- Samira is waiting with **Andrei** and **Caylee Brea**.
- Caylee Brea is four years old.
- Eliot tracks geography and logistics: Vancouver, Washington, DC, Tokyo later, time zones, luggage, and how the whole plan fits.
- Kalvin tracks faces: how tired his mothers are, how excited Samira is, how Caylee Brea looks at the visitors.
- Christmas with Samira's family is the first emotional destination of the trip, not an afterthought after Tokyo.

## Beat 2: Caylee Brea Meets the Canada Family

- Caylee Brea knows she is meeting family from Canada, but the exact relationships may be big for her to hold at once:
  - Jessica Isabella and Emma are her grandmothers / grandmother figures through Samira;
  - Eliot and Kalvin are her young uncles;
  - they are older than children but not fully adult in her eyes.
- Caylee may ask a childlike question:

> Are you the Canada uncles?

- Eliot answers verbally and precisely.
- Kalvin answers physically / warmly, kneeling down or offering a hand.
- Samira watches her daughter meet her brothers and becomes emotional in a quiet way.

## Beat 3: Christmas in Samira's Home

- The family celebrates Christmas in Samira and Andrei's home.
- Vladivostok gives the holiday a specific adult texture:
  - winter streets;
  - embassy neighborhoods / formal city energy in the background;
  - warm home light;
  - food on the table;
  - decorations chosen by Samira, Andrei, and Caylee;
  - a household that blends Samira's Key-Fritz roots with Andrei's Russian life.
- Samira blends what she inherited from Jessica and Emma with the life she built with Andrei:
  - Key-family truth-telling;
  - Catholic / interfaith family memory;
  - dancer's discipline;
  - the practical rhythms of owning a dance studio;
  - motherhood.
- Caylee Brea is the center of the child-energy in the room.

## Beat 4: Jessica and Emma See Samira's Adult Life

- Jessica Isabella sees that Samira has built a house where the practical parts of adulthood are organized and real.
- Emma sees that Samira is not alone. Andrei stays in quiet, practical ways.
- They are so proud of Samira.
- The pride should be specific:
  - she left young;
  - she survived state pressure earlier in life;
  - she built a marriage;
  - she became a mother;
  - she owns a dance studio.
- This is not a scene where Jessica and Emma know every hidden thing in the family. It is a scene where they can see Samira's visible adult work and love it.

## Beat 5: The Dance Studio Visit

- Samira takes them to her **dance studio** during the Christmas visit.
- The studio may be quiet for the holiday or running a small winter class / rehearsal, depending on the calendar.
- Eliot and Kalvin see the physical proof of Samira's adult authority.
- This is not Bolshoi anymore. This is Samira's space.
- Caylee Brea may know the studio like a second home: where to sit, where not to touch, where her mother stands when class begins.
- Jessica and Emma see the long arc from the cruise spotting / Bolshoi training to this studio.

## Beat 6: Andrei's Role

- Andrei hosts gently.
- He is not performative; he is steady, practical, quietly attentive.
- He helps with food, coats, translating / local logistics if needed, and Caylee's small needs.
- His care echoes the traits that drew Samira to him in adolescence: protective restraint, calm presence, no showy ego.
- Jessica and Emma can see why Samira chose him.

## Beat 7: Eliot and Kalvin in the Older-Sister World

- Eliot David and Kalvin LaMarcus are sixteen.
- They are old enough to remember the shadow of Samira's earlier disappearance / secrecy and young enough to still be amazed by her adult life.
- Eliot asks questions about the studio, the city, the schedule, and how Samira runs a business.
- Kalvin watches Caylee Brea and notices how Samira changes when her daughter calls for her.
- The brothers experience Samira not as the almost-mythic older sister in Russia, but as a wife, mother, and studio owner.

## Beat 8: The Sahara Boundary in the Background

- Sahara Hannan may be referenced, but this is not a Sahara visit.
- Correct knowledge boundary:
  - Sahara has Sugar Diabetes.
  - Samira knows Sahara has Sugar Diabetes.
  - Samira knows Sahara is married to Vladislav.
  - Samira knows Sahara has Shahira Hala.
  - Samira does **not** know or suspect that Sahara and Vladislav work for the Russian embassy as intelligence officers.
- If Samira and Sahara call or text during the visit, no one reveals Sahara's embassy / intelligence work.

## Beat 9: Christmas Dinner / Family Table

- The family gathers for Christmas dinner.
- Table image:
  - Samira and Andrei hosting;
  - Caylee Brea at the child-center;
  - Jessica and Emma proud and slightly overwhelmed;
  - Eliot and Kalvin half guests, half uncles;
  - the invisible shape of Sahara's hidden work held outside the spoken room.
- The meal should feel earned and warm.

## Beat 10: Leaving Vladivostok After Christmas

- After the Christmas visit, the family prepares to travel again.
- The route takes them through **Tokyo**.
- This time, Tokyo is not only a connection: they will stop there for **New Year's Eve**.
- The boys carry Samira's world with them as they leave: Caylee's voice, Andrei's steadiness, the studio mirrors, their mothers' pride.

# Part Two — Tokyo New Year's Stopover

## Beat 11: Flying Through Tokyo

- Jessica Isabella, Emma, Eliot David, and Kalvin LaMarcus fly through Tokyo after the Vladivostok Christmas visit.
- The travel day should carry the texture of long-haul family movement:
  - passports;
  - luggage;
  - airport soundscapes;
  - time-zone confusion;
  - Emma counting heads and documents;
  - Jessica keeping the medical / practical details moving;
  - Eliot asking route questions;
  - Kalvin watching everyone's fatigue.
- Tokyo is now the planned New Year's stopover after Christmas with Samira's family.

## Beat 12: Tokyo on New Year's Eve

- They arrive / settle in Tokyo for New Year's Eve.
- The city gives the family a different kind of New Year's than the Key family's usual Canadian winter or Vladivostok family visit:
  - city lights;
  - train stations;
  - hotel windows;
  - food, crowds, quiet rituals, or a carefully chosen place to count down the year.
- Jessica and Emma make the evening safe and structured, but they also let the boys feel the adventure.
- Eliot David wants to understand the plan minute by minute.
- Kalvin LaMarcus feels the emotional weather: his mothers' tiredness, the boys' anticipation, and the afterglow of seeing Samira's world.

## Beat 13: New Year's Eve Without Rémi Yet

- Rémi is not with them on New Year's Eve.
- His arrival is planned for the next day.
- The boys know he is coming to Tokyo for New Year's Day.
- They also know they will **stay in Tokyo with Rémi for a week** after Jessica Isabella and Emma fly back to Vancouver.
- That knowledge changes the evening: New Year's Eve is with their mothers; New Year's Day begins the week with their father.
- Jessica and Emma do not treat Rémi's arrival as an intrusion. It is part of the plan.

## Beat 14: Rémi Flies to Tokyo

- On **New Year's Day**, **Dr. Rémi Arsenault flies to Tokyo** to be with Eliot David and Kalvin LaMarcus.
- He is their father, and the scene should allow that word without taking anything away from Jessica Isabella and Emma.
- The visit is not only for one meal or one greeting: **the boys will stay in Tokyo with Rémi for a week**.
- His arrival can be at:
  - the hotel lobby;
  - Tokyo Haneda / Narita arrivals;
  - a train station / arranged meeting point;
  - breakfast / lunch on New Year's Day.
- The key is that he made the long trip specifically to be with his sons for New Year's Day and the week that follows.

## Beat 15: The Boys' Reaction

- Eliot David reacts with words first: questions, route details, how long the flight was, whether Rémi slept, whether he brought anything from Halifax.
- Kalvin LaMarcus reacts physically first: a hug, leaning into Rémi, staying close.
- The boys are sixteen, not small children, but the arrival still matters.
- Rémi's presence confirms that fatherhood in this family is not only biology and not only daily custody. It is showing up when it counts and staying for the week he promised.

## Beat 16: Jessica and Emma's Response

- Jessica Isabella and Emma make room for Rémi without insecurity.
- They may be emotional, but the emotion is not jealousy here; it is the complicated tenderness of seeing their sons loved by another adult who matters.
- Emma, the stayer, understands what it means when someone gets on a plane and comes.
- Jessica, the reacher, understands the relief of not having to be the only adult holding every thread.
- They are also preparing to leave: **Jessica Isabella and Emma will fly back to Vancouver**, while Eliot and Kalvin remain in Tokyo with Rémi for the week.
- The goodbye is planned, not abrupt. The boys know the structure; Rémi knows the structure; Jessica and Emma trust it.

## Beat 17: New Year's Day in Tokyo Together

- The family spends New Year's Day in Tokyo with Rémi:
  - breakfast / lunch;
  - a walk;
  - a shrine / city visit / quiet hotel day, depending on tone;
  - a message to Samira saying they made it to Tokyo / Rémi arrived;
  - a moment where the boys tell Rémi what they saw at Samira's dance studio.
- The point is not tourism detail unless desired later. The point is that Rémi is with his sons on New Year's Day.

## Beat 18: Jessica and Emma Fly Back to Vancouver

- After Rémi arrives and the New Year's Day handoff is secure, **Jessica Isabella and Emma fly back to Vancouver**.
- This is not written as abandoning the boys. It is a planned parental handoff to Rémi.
- Eliot David and Kalvin LaMarcus are sixteen and understand the plan.
- The goodbye has layers:
  - Jessica and Emma have just watched Samira's rooted adult life in Vladivostok;
  - they know Sahara's life has hidden layers that are not theirs to expose;
  - they are leaving the boys with Rémi for a week in Tokyo;
  - they are flying back to Vancouver with the knowledge that family can stretch without snapping.
- Rémi takes over the Tokyo-week logistics from there.

## Beat 19: Eliot and Kalvin Stay in Tokyo with Rémi for a Week

- **Eliot David and Kalvin LaMarcus stay in Tokyo with Rémi for a week.**
- This week is father-and-sons time.
- The week should feel steady, not extravagant. Rémi is there to be with his sons, not to buy their affection.
- The boys are sixteen, so the week can give them real independence inside clear structure:
  - phones charged;
  - hotel address saved;
  - agreed meet-up times;
  - transit cards / tickets handled practically;
  - Rémi knowing when to let them lead and when to step in.

### Day 1 — New Year's Day Handoff

- Jessica Isabella and Emma fly back to Vancouver after the planned handoff.
- Rémi and the boys keep New Year's Day simple after the emotional goodbye:
  - hotel settling;
  - food close by;
  - a short walk to learn the neighborhood around the hotel;
  - a call / message confirming to Jessica and Emma that everyone is settled.
- Eliot wants the week structured immediately.
- Kalvin wants reassurance through presence: Rémi physically there, not only promising to be there.

### Day 2 — Asakusa / Old Tokyo Texture

- Rémi takes Eliot and Kalvin to an older Tokyo neighborhood such as **Asakusa**.
- Possible beats:
  - temple gates / street food / souvenir stalls;
  - the boys comparing Tokyo's public order to Vladivostok's coastal order;
  - Rémi letting them choose food or small gifts for Jessica and Emma;
  - Eliot asking history / route questions;
  - Kalvin watching how Rémi navigates being a father outside his normal Halifax context.

### Day 3 — Museums / Books / Quiet Learning

- Rémi gives them a quieter day built around museums, bookstores, or a science / history visit.
- This fits Rémi's temperament: he does not need spectacle; he likes learning beside them.
- Eliot enjoys information density.
- Kalvin notices that Rémi is good at silence — not empty silence, but companionable silence.
- The day can include buying notebooks, small books, or gifts connected to Japan.

### Day 4 — Modern Tokyo / Trains / City Systems

- The three of them explore a more modern part of the city: train lines, major crossings, observation views, or a technology / design district.
- Eliot becomes fascinated by the systems: how trains connect, how the city routes people, how nobody seems lost despite the scale.
- Rémi lets him map part of the route.
- Kalvin is more interested in how the crowds feel and how Rémi keeps them together without gripping too tightly.

### Day 5 — Water / Bay / Open Air

- After several dense city days, Rémi chooses a day with more open air: Tokyo Bay, a riverside walk, a park, or a quieter waterfront area.
- The day gives the boys room to talk about Samira's Vladivostok life and Sahara's hidden life without being trapped at a table.
- They tell Rémi more about:
  - Caylee Brea;
  - Samira's dance studio;
  - how proud Jessica and Emma looked;
  - how carefully everyone avoids saying things they do not know about Sahara's work.
- Rémi listens without claiming answers he does not have.

### Day 6 — The Boys Lead the Day

- Rémi lets Eliot and Kalvin plan much of the sixth day.
- Eliot handles the route.
- Kalvin chooses at least one place based on feeling rather than efficiency: somewhere calm, beautiful, or emotionally easy.
- This is the clearest sign that Rémi trusts them as sixteen-year-olds.
- The day ends with packing beginning, gifts gathered, and the reality of the flight home settling in.

### Day 7 — Same-Day Flight Back to Vancouver

- On the seventh day, **Rémi flies back to Vancouver with Eliot David and Kalvin LaMarcus**.
- The flight is on the same calendar day because of the time-zone / International Date Line crossing.
- **Flight detail:** the flight leaves Tokyo at **10:00 p.m.** and arrives in Vancouver at **2:45 p.m. the same day**.
- Rémi handles the final fatherly checks before departure:
  - passports;
  - boarding passes;
  - phones charged;
  - gifts / luggage;
  - message sent to Jessica and Emma.
- Eliot understands the time math and may explain it more than once.
- Kalvin feels the strange emotional reality of leaving Tokyo at night and arriving in Vancouver in the afternoon of the same date.
- Jessica Isabella and Emma are in Vancouver on the receiving side of the plan.
- The boys are returning not only to their mothers, but to their Vancouver school life at **Saint George School**.
- The arrival is a reunion, not the end of Rémi's presence yet: he comes through the airport with the twins and hands them back into the Vancouver side of the family in person.

## Beat 20: Vancouver Arrival and the Next-Day Goodbye

- Rémi, Eliot David, and Kalvin LaMarcus arrive in Vancouver at **2:45 p.m. the same day** their flight left Tokyo at 10:00 p.m.
- Jessica Isabella and Emma are already back in Vancouver and waiting for them.
- The boys come home carrying the full arc of the trip: Vladivostok Christmas, Tokyo New Year's, a week with Rémi, and the strange same-day flight.
- Rémi does not leave them immediately at the airport.
- **The next day, Rémi leaves the twins.**
- The next-day goodbye matters because it lets the week end gently instead of collapsing at baggage claim.
- Rémi leaves Eliot and Kalvin with Jessica Isabella and Emma, making the handoff complete.
- The boys' ordinary Vancouver structure is waiting for them: they attend **Saint George School**.
- The goodbye is sad but not unstable: he came, stayed, flew back with them, and left them safely with their mothers.

## Beat 21: Closing Image

- Possible closing image:
  - Rémi leaving the twins the next day in Vancouver after the Tokyo week.
  - Eliot holding the itinerary, still pleased that Tokyo 10:00 p.m. became Vancouver 2:45 p.m. the same day.
  - Kalvin carrying the goodbye in his body but not as abandonment.
  - Jessica and Emma with the boys again, proud of all four children, even with the ache of hidden work in the family.
  - Vancouver around them, Tokyo behind them, Vladivostok Christmas still fresh, and the new year fully begun.
