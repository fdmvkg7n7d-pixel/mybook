# Boxing Day at the Whistler Mansion — Christmas Presents After Catherine's Grave — Scene Outline

> **Scope:** After Jessica Isabella, Emma, Samira Chaka, and Sahara Hannan visit Catherine Fritz's grave in Vancouver on Boxing Day, they drive to Mike and Terri Anne's Whistler mansion to open their Christmas presents. This scene acts as the emotional second half of Boxing Day: grief at the graveyard, then warmth, family safety, and Christmas abundance in Whistler.
>
> **Time:** Boxing Day afternoon/evening, immediately after the Vancouver graveyard visit.
> **Place:** Mike and Terri Anne's Whistler mansion, Whistler, British Columbia.
> **Characters present:** Jessica Isabella Key, Emma Fritz, Samira Chaka Key-Fritz, Sahara Hannan Key-Fritz, Mike Key, and Terri Anne Williams-Key. Patti Anne and Gamaliel Galvin are not at Whistler; they visit Mike and Terri Anne's Carlisle mansion for Christmas in their separate file. Kiley Rose and her girls' Natal Christmas is handled in their separate file.
> **Mood:** Worn-out grief → mountain refuge → childlike Christmas excitement → Emma allowing herself to receive safety without guilt.

## 1. The Drive to Whistler

- **The drive to Whistler:** Just after visiting the graveyard, Jessica, Emma, Samira Chaka, and Sahara Hannan leave Vancouver and drive north to **Whistler**, to **Mike and Terri Anne's Whistler mansion**. The Sea-to-Sky Highway becomes the emotional bridge between grief and family warmth: ocean, mountains, winter light, and the girls slowly coming back to themselves in the back seat.
- **Emma after the cemetery:** Emma is wrung out. She does not have much to say at first. Jessica drives or sits beside her, depending on logistics, keeping one hand available whenever Emma reaches for it.
- **The girls' shift:** Samira and Sahara understand that the graveyard was serious, but they are also children. As the mountains rise around them, they begin whispering about presents, snow, and whether Grandma Catherine would have liked Whistler. Their return to ordinary excitement is not disrespectful; it is life doing what life does.

## 2. Arrival at the Whistler Mansion

- **Arrival at the Whistler mansion:** The mansion is warm, prepared, and waiting — Mike and Terri Anne's west-coast mountain refuge. The Christmas tree is already decorated, fires are lit, and wrapped gifts are arranged for the girls. The contrast with the graveyard is deliberate: from stone and winter silence to warmth, lights, and family abundance.
- **Who is already there:** Mike and Terri Anne are at the Whistler mansion, ready to receive Jessica, Emma, Samira, and Sahara after the graveyard visit.
- **Who is elsewhere:** **Patti Anne and Gamaliel Galvin visit Mike and Terri Anne's Carlisle mansion** for Christmas and stay over for three nights; their Carlisle visit is developed separately in `../../Patti Anne and Aleksander/18 years/patti-anne-gamaliel-carlisle-christmas-three-night-visit-scene-outline.md`. **Kiley Rose and her girls stay in Natal** for Christmas; their Natal Christmas is developed separately in `../../Kiley Rose and Julia/0 - 17 years/kiley-rose-and-girls-stay-in-natal-christmas-scene-outline.md`.
- **Opening Christmas presents:** Because Christmas Day in Vancouver was kept quiet and structured around the hotel, Samira and Sahara open their main Christmas presents here on Boxing Day afternoon/evening. They rush toward the tree, then remember Emma's tears and check themselves. Jessica gently tells them it is okay to be excited.
- **Emma's emotional response:** Watching her daughters open presents in Mike and Terri Anne's Whistler mansion after standing at Catherine's grave overwhelms Emma in a different way. Catherine could not give Emma this kind of safety, not because she lacked love, but because life did not give her the resources or years. The Key family can give it now, and Emma lets herself receive it without guilt.
- **Jessica names it:** Quietly, while the girls are opening gifts, Jessica says:

  *"This doesn't replace her."*

- **Emma answers:**

  *"I know."*

  A pause.

  *"But I think she would be glad they have this."*

- **The girls bring Catherine with them:** One of the twins — likely Sahara — places one small gift ribbon or a piece of wrapping paper aside and says it is for Grandma Catherine, or Samira says they should tell Grandma Catherine what they got next time. This keeps Catherine present in the joy rather than leaving her behind at the grave.
- **Closing image:** The scene closes with Emma sitting near the Christmas tree at the Whistler mansion, Jessica beside her, Samira and Sahara surrounded by opened gifts. Emma is tired, tearful, and no longer alone. Catherine has been visited. The girls have opened their presents. Grief and Christmas have occupied the same day, and the family has survived both.
