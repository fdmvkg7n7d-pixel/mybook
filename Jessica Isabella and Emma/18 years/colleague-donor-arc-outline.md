# SCENE OUTLINE: ASKING RÉMI
## Jessica Isabella and Emma Ask Their Colleague

**Part of the ~2054 second child arc.**
**Feeds into:** full arc notes in `another-child-2055-outline.md`
**Characters:** Jessica Isabella Key (30), Emma Fritz (30), Dr. Rémi Arsenault (36)
**Tone:** Quiet, serious, intimate. Three people in a room having an honest conversation that will change all of them.

---

## CONTEXT

- Jessica and Emma have visited the sperm bank and reviewed the profiles.
- They rejected all options — not because the donors were inadequate, but because the anonymity did not fit this family. They need someone they know. Someone the sons can find.
- Jessica suggested Rémi. Emma agreed. They decided to ask him together.
- Rémi is a neurosurgeon at QEII Health Sciences Centre and a trusted senior colleague from their Dalhousie medical program. Both women know him well — professionally and personally.

---

## SETTING

- Rémi's **private office** at QEII Health Sciences Centre, Halifax.
- Not a meeting room — his own space. A desk, a few chairs, medical texts on shelves, a window. Personal but professional. The door closes.
- Jessica and Emma have asked for time with him. He has said yes without asking why.
- Mid-morning, mid-week. Private. No one else present.

---

## SCENE STRUCTURE

### Arrival
- All three seated. Rémi behind his desk or pulled around to sit across from them — close enough that this is a conversation, not a consultation.
- Jessica and Emma sit together. Shoulders close. Emma is in the room. She chose to be here.

### The Ask — Jessica
- Jessica opens. She is the reacher; she begins.
- She lays it out plainly:
  - They want another child.
  - They went to the sperm bank. The profiles were not right — not wrong, but not enough for this family.
  - They need someone they know. Someone the sons can find without a file number and a clinic intermediary.
  - Someone who will be honest with the boys about what this was and what it wasn't.
- She says: *We thought of you.*
- She stops. She lets it land.

### The Ask — Emma
- Rémi looks at Jessica. Then at Emma.
- Emma speaks. She is here; she is asking; this is both of them.
- She makes clear:
  - This is not only Jessica's ask. It is hers too.
  - She knows exactly what they are asking and what it means for all three of them.
  - Whatever he decides — yes or no — nothing between them changes professionally or personally.
  - He owes them nothing.
  - They are asking because they trust him. Because they believe the boys would be lucky to come from someone like him.
- She stops. She does not fill the silence.

### Rémi's Questions
- Rémi does not answer immediately. He came prepared. He has a list of questions — written, on his desk — because he is that kind of person: he knew, when they asked for the meeting, that this might be coming, or something like it, and he wanted to be ready to think clearly.
- He works through the list. The questions are serious and practical. He does not ask them to make the women uncomfortable; he asks them because the answers matter and because he respects them enough not to pretend otherwise.

**His questions include:**

1. What role, if any, do they want him to play in the sons' lives — now, and later?
2. Will the boys know who he is and how they came to be?
3. What happens if conception takes multiple attempts — are they prepared for that, practically and emotionally?
4. What does Emma need from this arrangement to feel secure in it?
5. If the sons come looking for him when they are older, what does he say to them?
6. What do they need from him if something goes wrong — medically, emotionally, between them?
7. Does anyone else know, or will anyone else know, about this arrangement?
8. Is there anything they need from him beyond the biological — and is there anything he should not do or say that would make this harder for them?

- Jessica and Emma answer each question. Some answers are immediate; some they work out together as they speak.
- The key answers, as settled in this scene:
  - He is not a co-parent. He is not in the household. But his name is not hidden.
  - The boys will be told the truth when they are old enough — plainly, kindly, fully.
  - Multiple attempts are expected and accepted. They understand this going in.
  - Emma's security comes from the arrangement being honest and boundaried — nothing beyond what is agreed.
  - If the sons come looking, Rémi will be honest with them. He will not make it complicated.
  - No one else will know. Not colleagues, not friends. This stays between the three of them until Jessica and Emma choose to tell their family.
  - He does not push for updates. He does not ask to be kept informed of the pregnancy beyond what they choose to share.

- When the questions are done, he sets the list face-down on the desk.

### Rémi's First Response
- He says: *Give me a few days.*
- He says: *This is not a no. I want you to know that before you leave this office.*
- He does not explain further. He needs to think — alone, and in private.

### Leaving
- Jessica and Emma thank him. They leave together.
- They walk out of the building side by side without speaking.
- Outside — Emma exhales. Jessica takes her hand.
- Two words between them: *Okay. / Okay.*
- No more is needed right now.

---

## THREE DAYS LATER — RÉMI'S ANSWER

### Setting
- His private office again. Same chairs. He asked them to come back.

### What he says
- He says yes.
- He does not explain at length.
- He says: *I trust you both. And I think a child raised by you will be all right.*
- He looks at both of them when he says it — not just Jessica. He understands the answer belongs equally to both women.

### What he does not say
- He does not say it was an easy decision.
- He does not say he has no reservations.
- He says yes, and he means it, and that is enough.

### His privacy
- During the three days, he tells no one.
- Not a colleague, not a friend, not a family member.
- He thinks it through entirely alone. This is a decision that belongs to the three people in that office and no one else, and he treats it that way from the beginning.

### Emma's response
- She thanks him. Her voice is steady.
- Her hands, under the table, are not.
- She is already beginning to feel that the thing she agreed to is going to cost her something she did not fully anticipate.
- She agrees anyway.

---

## THE ARRANGEMENT — WHAT IS AGREED

- Jessica will sleep with Rémi as many times as necessary until conception is confirmed.
  - It takes **five attempts** across five separate fertile windows before conception is confirmed — approximately five months.
  - Getting pregnant is not guaranteed on the first attempt. They all know this going in. Rémi, as a physician, knows it more precisely than most. A healthy woman at 30 has roughly a 20% chance of conception per cycle; five attempts is well within the normal range.
  - Each attempt is timed to Jessica's fertile window — the six-day period each cycle during which conception is possible.
  - Each of the five attempts is its own beat — clinical, agreed, without romance or attachment.
  - Emma knows each time, in advance and after. She is not kept in the dark. She agreed to the arrangement and she continues to agree, each time, with full knowledge.
- This is not a relationship. It is not an affair. It is an agreement made by two women and one man with full honesty and full consent.
- Rémi will be known to the sons. His name will not be a secret.
- When the boys are old enough, they will be told the truth — as clearly and as kindly as the truth allows.
- Rémi's role in the sons' lives beyond that is determined by the boys, not by the adults.
- No romantic attachment on any side. This is the agreement.
- No one outside the three of them knows. This remains private until Jessica and Emma choose otherwise.

---

## WHAT MULTIPLE ATTEMPTS MEAN FOR EMMA

- The arrangement spans **five months** — one attempt per fertile window, five windows total.
- Each of the five attempts renews Emma's jealousy. She agreed the first time. She agrees the second time. The third. The fourth. The fifth.
- The cost does not stay the same — it accumulates. Each month she agrees again, and each month the thing she is carrying gets heavier.
- It is not one night she has to survive. It is five months of knowing, each time, that it is happening or has happened, and choosing to hold the agreement anyway.
- She does not break the arrangement. She does not ask Jessica to stop. But the weight builds — in small things, in silences, in the way she holds herself when she passes Rémi in the neurosurgery corridor.
- By the fifth attempt, the jealousy is no longer possible to keep folded. This is what forces the kitchen table scene.
- See Emma's jealousy arc beats below.

---

## THE KITCHEN TABLE SCENE — JESSICA'S PROMISE

- After the fifth attempt, Emma finally speaks the cost out loud. Not to ask Jessica to stop. Just to say: *It hurts.*
- Jessica hears it. And she makes a promise: **I won't do it again. Not after tonight.**
- Not a question. Not a negotiation. A promise. The arrangement with Rémi ends.
- The fifth attempt worked. Conception is confirmed. Eliot David and Kalvin LaMarcus are on the way. Jessica will not sleep with Rémi again — not because the arrangement failed, but because it succeeded, and because the promise is about love, not logistics. There is no need to find another donor or return to the sperm bank. The promise stands: never again.
- This is the emotional centre of the arc. Emma's silence breaks for the first time in five months. Jessica's response is not to defend or explain — it is to stop the thing that is hurting the person she loves.
- Emma never asked Jessica to stop. She said only: *It hurts.* Jessica decided: *then no more.*

## KEY CHARACTER NOTES FOR THIS SCENE

### Jessica
- Opens the conversation because reaching is what she does.
- Answers Rémi's questions directly. Does not soften or evade.
- Watches Emma as much as she watches Rémi throughout.
- Takes Emma's hand outside. She already knows Emma is carrying something. She does not push yet.

### Emma
- Her being in the room is the scene's quiet statement. She chose to be there.
- Speaking second is not passivity — she wanted him to hear it from her directly.
- Answering the question about what she needs: this is hard for Emma, who has spent her life needing less. She answers honestly anyway. The practice of saying what she needs is still new.
- Her *thank you* at the end is the first moment she has thanked someone for agreeing to something that is already hurting her.
- The unsteady hands: she knows what she has set in motion.

### Rémi
- The written list of questions is the most revealing thing about him. He anticipated this. He prepared. He did not come to the meeting unready.
- His three-day pause is integrity, not reluctance.
- He tells no one during those three days. The privacy is instinctive and complete.
- His yes is plain, warm, and final: *I trust you both. I think a child raised by you will be all right.*
- He looks at both of them. He understands this marriage.
