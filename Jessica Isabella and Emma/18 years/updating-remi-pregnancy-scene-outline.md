# SCENE OUTLINE: TELLING REMI AND THE BIRTH INVITATION

**Part of the ~2054 second child arc (colleague-donor arc).**
**Follows:** the kitchen table scene (Jessica's promise — not after tonight); the fifth attempt succeeded; conception confirmed.
**Feeds into:** the Russia vacation, telling the twins, the birth (2055).

**Characters:** Jessica Isabella Key (30), Emma Fritz (30), Dr. Remi Arsenault (36)

**Setting:** Remi's private office at QEII Health Sciences Centre, Halifax. The same office where they asked him. The same chairs. The same desk with the medical texts on the shelves. But this time, the news is different.

**Tone:** Quiet, warm, professional but personal. Three people who made an honest agreement and are now sharing the result of it. No drama, no tension. Just truth, shared the way this family shares all hard and good things.

---

## BEFORE THE SCENE

- The pregnancy test is positive. Conception confirmed on the fifth attempt.
- The kitchen table scene has already happened. Jessica made her promise. Emma accepted it. The arrangement is over.
- Jessica and Emma decide to tell Remi in person — the same way they asked him. Not by text. Not by phone. In person, face to face, the way you tell someone when the news matters.
- They call ahead. He makes time.

---

## THE SCENE

### Arrival

- Jessica and Emma walk into Remi's office together. Same as before. Shoulders close.
- Remi stands when they enter. He knows something is coming — he can tell by the way they are looking at him.
- He sits. They sit. The chairs are the same. The room is the same. But the news is different.

### The News

- Jessica speaks first. She is the reacher; she always begins.
- She says it plainly: *It worked.*
- She says: *We are pregnant.*
- She says: *Twins.* (The ultrasound already showed two. It always runs in the Key family — twins, pairs, doubles.)
- She does not smile at first. She lets the news land. She lets him absorb it.

### Remi's Response

- Remi is quiet for a moment. Not because he does not care — because he does. Because he is a physician who knows what pregnancy means, and a man who knows what this agreement meant to all three of them.
- He says: *That is wonderful news.*
- He means it. He says: *Two boys?* (He does not know yet, but he asks — the same way he asked his list of questions before. He wants to know. He wants to understand.)
- Jessica says: *Two boys. Identical.*
- Remi nods. He processes this the way he processes everything: carefully, completely, without rushing.
- He says: *Congratulations.* And he looks at both of them. Not just Jessica. Both. Because he understood the agreement from the start — it belonged to both women equally.

### The Invitation

- Jessica does not stop at the news. She goes further. But this time, she does not speak alone.
- Both women ask him. Together. The way they asked him the first time.
- Jessica opens: *We want you to be there when they are born.*
- Emma adds, before he can answer: *In the room. When it happens. If you want to be.*
- This is not Jessica reaching while Emma stays quiet. This is both of them, side by side, making the same ask they made the first time — together, as equals, as the two mothers who share everything.
- Remi does not answer immediately. He thinks. He always thinks.
- He says: *Yes.*
- One word. Plain. Final. The same way he said yes to the original arrangement.
- He says: *I would like that.*

### The Agreement

- They settle the practicalities:
  - Remi will be contacted when labor begins.
  - He will come to the hospital. He will be let into the labor room.
  - He will be present for the birth. Not as a co-parent. Not as a partner. As the man whose gift made these children possible — the man they chose, the man they trust, the man they want to witness what his choice created.
  - His role in the room is defined: quiet, present, not interfering. Emma will be at Jessica's side. Remi will be there, witnessing, supporting in the way they agree upon.
  - After the birth, he will meet the boys. He will hold them if he wants to. He will know their names. He will know their faces. And he will know that the agreement he made was worth it.

### Leaving

- Jessica and Emma thank him. They leave together.
- Outside — Emma exhales. Jessica takes her hand.
- Emma says: *You sure about this?* (Not doubting. Just checking. Because Emma always checks — she always makes sure the people she loves are doing what they actually want to do, not what they think they should do.)
- Jessica says: *Yes. He deserves to see what he helped make.*
- Emma says: *Okay.* And she means it.
- Two words between them: *Okay. / Okay.*
- The same as the day they left his office after asking him. But this time, the weight is different. This time, it is lighter.

---

## WHY THIS SCENE MATTERS

- **Remi's dignity:** He agreed to the arrangement with integrity — a written list of questions, three days of private thought, a plain yes. He deserves to know when it worked. He deserves to be told in person, the way he was asked.
- **The invitation to the birth:** This is not about making Remi a co-parent. It is about honesty, dignity, and the Key family rule: *truth is told.* He was part of the story. He gets to see how the chapter ends.
- **Emma's consent:** Emma does not flinch. She does not object. She says *okay.* This is the same woman who carried the weight of the arrangement for five months in silence — and now, when it is over, she is the one who says *yes* to letting him witness the result. That is growth. That is strength.
- **Both women ask together:** This is not Jessica reaching while Emma stays quiet. Both women make the invitation — the way they made the original ask. Together, as equals, as the two mothers who share everything. Jessica opens; Emma speaks too. The symmetry with the asking scene is deliberate: they asked together, they are inviting together.
- **The same office:** The symmetry matters. They asked him in this room. They tell him in this room. The circle is complete. The agreement was honest from start to finish.
