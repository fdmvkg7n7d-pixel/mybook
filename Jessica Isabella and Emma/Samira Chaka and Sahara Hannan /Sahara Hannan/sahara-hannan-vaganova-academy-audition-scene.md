# Sahara Hannan’s Vaganova Academy Audition — Beat Sheet

## Purpose of This File
This is a **beat sheet / planning version**, not a full prose chapter. It gives Sahara Hannan’s Vaganova audition the same planning treatment as Samira Chaka’s Bolshoi entrance test.

## Characters Present
- **Sahara Hannan Key** — age eleven; auditioning/testing for the Vaganova Academy in St. Petersburg.
- **Samira Chaka Key** — Sahara’s identical twin; present for support but quietly afraid of being left behind.
- **Jessica Isabella Key** — biological mother; proud, protective, anxious, especially because of Sahara’s Type 1 diabetes.
- **Emma Fritz** — co-mother; calm, practical, attentive to emotional needs and diabetes management.
- **Vaganova Academy examiners** — formal, traditional, precise, focused on line, placement, musicality, discipline, and teachability.
- **Translator / academy liaison** — helps the family understand instructions, paperwork, medical details, and next steps.

## Setting
- **Time:** Around 2051, when Sahara is eleven.
- **Place:** St. Petersburg, Russia — Vaganova Academy.
- **Context:** A Vaganova teacher noticed the twins’ talent during the Northern Europe cruise. Sahara’s audition comes before Samira’s Bolshoi test and begins the twins’ first real separation arc.

## Core Emotional Question
Can Sahara accept being chosen first without feeling she is abandoning Samira?

---

## Beat Sheet

### 1. St. Petersburg Feels Elegant and Intimidating
- The family arrives in St. Petersburg after the cruise-related recommendation.
- The city feels different from Halifax and different from what Moscow will later feel like.
- St. Petersburg has canals, pale buildings, old grandeur, and a sense of artistic history.
- Sahara feels the beauty of the place but also the pressure of what it represents.
- The Vaganova Academy feels like a temple of classical ballet.

### 2. Sahara Is Excited but Guilty
- Sahara wants this opportunity badly.
- She tries not to show too much excitement because Samira has not yet been chosen for her own path.
- Sahara worries that being first means leaving Samira behind.
- Samira notices Sahara holding back.
- This creates a quiet emotional tension between the twins before the audition even begins.

### 3. Morning Preparations at the Hotel
- The family lays out everything carefully:
  - ballet clothes,
  - hair supplies,
  - shoes,
  - documents,
  - recommendation materials,
  - medical forms,
  - diabetes supplies.
- Jessica checks the paperwork and medical notes repeatedly.
- Emma manages the diabetes checklist calmly.
- Sahara is embarrassed by the extra attention but knows it matters.

### 4. Diabetes Safety Check
- Emma and Sahara check Sahara’s glucose/CGM status before leaving.
- Supplies are confirmed:
  - fast sugar,
  - snack,
  - water,
  - backup testing supplies,
  - written medical instructions,
  - emergency contacts.
- Sahara says she knows what to do, slightly sharply.
- She apologizes after realizing she snapped.
- Emma does not take offense and reminds her that knowing is exactly why they check.
- Jessica tells Sahara that diabetes is not a weakness in her dancing; it is part of keeping her safe enough to dance.

### 5. Outside the Vaganova Academy
- The family reaches the academy.
- Sahara stares at the building longer than she means to.
- Samira makes a small joke that the building looks like it could correct someone’s posture from across the street.
- Sahara laughs, easing the tension.
- The twins enter side by side.

### 6. Waiting Area
- The waiting area has other young dancers, parents, teachers, and academy staff.
- Sahara hears Russian and familiar ballet terms.
- She feels both very young and strangely ready.
- Samira sits close beside her; this time Sahara does not step away.
- Sahara needs the closeness before entering the audition room.
- The liaison confirms paperwork and medical notes.

### 7. Medical Disclosure to the Academy
- The academy staff reviews Sahara’s Type 1 diabetes information.
- A quiet planned glucose check happens before the test.
- Sahara handles it competently and without drama.
- If asked whether she can manage training safely, Sahara answers clearly:
  - she has managed school, ballet, gymnastics, travel, and performance with diabetes;
  - she knows her body;
  - she knows when to ask for help;
  - she will not hide a medical problem to seem stronger.
- This moment shows maturity, not weakness.

### 8. Saying Goodbye at the Door
- Sahara’s name is called.
- Samira grips her hand once.
- Sahara whispers that if she gets scared, she will pretend Samira is doing it with her.
- Samira admits, with humor and honesty, that if Sahara gets in, she will pretend she is not jealous.
- Sahara understands that Samira is proud but also hurting.
- Sahara enters the audition room carrying both hope and guilt.

### 9. First Impression of the Test Room
- The Vaganova studio feels refined, strict, and traditional.
- Mirrors, barres, piano, examiners, and quiet formality.
- The examiners are not cruel, but they are exacting.
- Sahara understands that Vaganova is looking for a different quality than raw athletic power.
- This room wants line, purity, musicality, and discipline.

### 10. Barre Work — Classical Placement
- The audition begins with basic barre exercises.
- The examiners assess:
  - posture,
  - turnout,
  - feet,
  - line,
  - control,
  - coordination of arms and head,
  - musical timing,
  - ability to follow instruction.
- Sahara is nervous at first but technically careful.
- Her natural lyricism begins to show.
- She receives a correction about épaulement, shoulders, or arm coordination.
- She repeats the exercise with more clarity.
- The examiners notice she can refine quickly.

### 11. Centre Work — Sahara Starts Too Careful
- Sahara moves into the centre.
- She begins too cautiously because she is afraid to make a mistake.
- A teacher asks her to let the phrase continue through the arms, eyes, and breath.
- Sahara repeats the combination.
- Her movement becomes more continuous and lyrical.
- The Vaganova quality appears: elegance, line, restraint, musical phrasing.
- Unlike Samira’s later Bolshoi test, Sahara does not need to get bigger; she needs to become clearer.

### 12. Planned Diabetes Check / Short Pause
- A planned pause allows Sahara to confirm her glucose is safe.
- The pause is brief and matter-of-fact.
- Sahara handles the check herself, with adult oversight as needed.
- The academy sees that her diabetes is managed responsibly.
- The moment does not derail the audition.
- It reinforces that Sahara’s body is not an obstacle; it is something she has learned to listen to.

### 13. Outside the Room — Samira’s Fear
- Jessica and Emma wait with Samira.
- Jessica is anxious about both the audition and the diabetes management.
- Emma stays calm and practical.
- Samira is quieter than usual.
- She asks what happens if the academy only needs one of them.
- Jessica tells her Sahara being seen does not make Samira invisible.
- Emma adds that schools choose paths, but family does not choose one child over another.
- Samira wants to believe them, but the fear remains.

### 14. Final Combination — Sahara’s Musicality
- Sahara is asked to perform or repeat a final phrase.
- She thinks of water, canals, Halifax rain, and the sound of Samira breathing beside her at night.
- She lets the music stretch through the steps rather than stopping at counts.
- Her finish is clear, sustained, and emotionally honest.
- The examiners see her gift as lyrical and disciplined rather than explosive.
- This confirms why Vaganova fits Sahara.

### 15. Conditional Acceptance / Recommendation
- The examiners confer.
- Through the translator, they say Sahara shows strong potential for Vaganova training.
- They may note requirements:
  - medical safety plan,
  - language preparation,
  - placement decision,
  - guardianship/living arrangements,
  - continued diabetes management protocol.
- Sahara is recommended for **conditional acceptance** or entry into the academy path.
- The result is joyful but also immediately practical.

### 16. Reunion in the Hall
- Sahara exits trying to stay composed.
- Jessica asks what happened.
- Sahara says they think she can train there.
- Jessica is relieved and emotional.
- Emma is proud but already listening carefully to the practical conditions.
- Samira is proud immediately, then feels the separation hit.
- Sahara sees both reactions on her face.

### 17. Emotional Landing Between the Twins
- Sahara steps close to Samira.
- Sahara tells her that going first does not mean going alone.
- Samira says that in one way, it does.
- Sahara answers that it does not mean that inside.
- The twins hold hands while Jessica and Emma talk to the liaison about the next steps.
- The scene ends with Sahara chosen, Samira proud, and both girls beginning to understand that their shared life is changing.

---

## Canon Result
- **Sahara receives conditional acceptance / recommendation for Vaganova training.**
- Her diabetes is treated as a serious but manageable part of the plan.
- This audition begins the twins’ separation arc before Samira later tests for Bolshoi in Moscow.

## Style Notes for Future Expansion
- Keep this as an outline unless specifically asked for prose.
- Do not turn Sahara’s diabetes into melodrama.
- Show Sahara as responsible, capable, and honest about her body.
- Keep the Vaganova tone refined, exacting, and traditional.
- Contrast Sahara’s style with Samira’s:
  - Sahara: line, lyricism, restraint, musical clarity.
  - Samira: power, attack, amplitude, dramatic boldness.
- The emotional conflict should be quiet but deep: Sahara wants this, but she does not want to hurt Samira.
