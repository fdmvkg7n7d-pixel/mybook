## Core Emotional Throughline

This scene has three movements.

First, in Vladivostok, **Samira Chaka and Andrei have a very emotional conversation**. Samira owns a dance studio, has a husband who loves her, and is holding the life she built. But the holidays make distance visible: Jessica Isabella and Emma, Eliot and Kalvin, Sahara and Shahira, all the people she loves are scattered across the world. Samira feels the ache of being far away from her twin, even while living a full adult life.

Second, **Sahara Hannan calls Samira Chaka**. The call is not about spy work, suspicion, or confession. The call is about **holidays, their daughters, and the younger twins' travel**. It is a twin call and a mother call: Samira holding Caylee Brea, Sahara holding Shahira Hala, both sisters using their iPhones to keep family closeness alive across distance.

Third, after the call, **Sahara Hannan and Vladislav talk about the call**. Vladislav knows what Sahara does because he also works at the Russian embassy as an intelligence officer. He understands why she could talk about holidays, daughters, husbands, Sugar Diabetes, and family — but not the embassy. The after-call conversation is where Sahara lets the weight show. Vladislav also reminds her of the hard consequence: if she tells Samira the embassy / intelligence truth, the state would send **Samira Chaka and Andrei to prison in Solikamsk**.

## Scene Structure

### Beat 1: New Year's Day in Vladivostok

- Samira Chaka is at home in **Vladivostok** with Andrei and Caylee Brea.
- The holiday atmosphere is present but quieter after Christmas / New Year's movement.
- Samira's dance studio is part of the emotional background:
  - schedules waiting after the holiday;
  - students returning soon;
  - the studio as proof of what she built;
  - the studio as a responsibility she loves.
- Caylee Brea is four years old and close to Samira — being held, climbing into her lap, or wanting attention while the adults talk.
- Andrei notices Samira's emotional state.

### Beat 2: Samira Chaka and Andrei's Very Emotional Conversation

- Samira and Andrei have a private, very emotional conversation before the call.
- The conversation can happen while Caylee is nearby or after Caylee has been settled briefly.
- Samira may be emotional because:
  - the holidays make distance hurt;
  - she misses Sahara;
  - she misses being able to share everything instantly with her twin;
  - she knows Sahara has Sugar Diabetes and still worries about her body from far away;
  - she is proud of her dance studio but feels the cost of building a life so far from the rest of the family;
  - she knows Jessica Isabella and Emma worry about things they do not say.
- Andrei does not try to fix it quickly.
- He lets Samira be emotional without making her defend the feeling.
- He may remind her that calling Sahara is not small; it is how the sisters keep the bridge alive.

### Beat 3: Andrei Grounds Samira

- Andrei speaks with the same steady, gentle quality that first drew Samira to him.
- He can remind her:
  - Sahara knows she loves her;
  - Caylee and Shahira knowing each other matters;
  - the holidays do not have to be perfect to be real;
  - the dance studio can wait for a few minutes while she answers her sister.
- Samira may become more emotional because Andrei is not asking her to be less attached to Sahara.
- This beat should show their marriage as quiet and strong.

### Beat 4: Sahara Calls Samira

- **Sahara Hannan calls Samira Chaka** on her iPhone.
- It is important that Sahara calls Samira, not the other way around.
- Samira answers while holding **Caylee Brea**.
- Sahara is on the screen holding **Shahira Hala**.
- The image is canon: **Samira Chaka is holding Caylee Brea and Sahara Hannan is holding Shahira Hala while they are on their iPhones.**
- Samira may wipe her face or steady her voice before answering, but Sahara can still hear that she has been emotional.

### Beat 5: The Call Is About Holidays and Their Daughters

- The call is about ordinary family things:
  - Happy New Year;
  - how the holidays went;
  - what Caylee Brea did for Christmas / New Year;
  - what Shahira Hala did for Christmas / New Year;
  - how Eliot David and Kalvin LaMarcus were during the Christmas visit;
  - whether the younger twins are settled with Rémi in Tokyo / safely on their travel plan;
  - whether the girls liked their gifts;
  - whether either child stayed up too late;
  - small funny daughter stories;
  - family travel updates if appropriate.
- Samira can ask about Shahira.
- Sahara can ask about Caylee.
- Sahara can ask about Eliot David and Kalvin LaMarcus, because they just visited Samira's family in Vladivostok with Jessica Isabella and Emma before continuing to Tokyo.
- Samira can tell Sahara that the younger twins were excited, observant, and very sixteen, and that they are now with Rémi for the Tokyo week.
- Samira can ask how Vladislav is.
- Sahara can ask how Andrei is.
- Samira can ask about Sahara's Sugar Diabetes in a normal sisterly way if it fits the scene.
- Sahara can ask about Samira's dance studio in a normal sisterly way if it fits the scene.
- The call should feel warm, intimate, and domestic.

### Beat 6: What the Call Is Not About

- The call is **not** about Russian embassy work.
- The call is **not** about intelligence operations.
- The call is **not** about Samira suspecting anything.
- The younger twins are a safe family topic; they do not lead into embassy work or hidden state-work details.
- Samira does **not** know that Sahara and Vladislav work at the Russian embassy as intelligence officers.
- Samira does **not** suspect that Sahara and Vladislav work at the Russian embassy as intelligence officers.
- Sahara does not hint at it.
- Vladislav does not hint at it.
- The secrecy stays intact.

### Beat 7: The Daughters in the Frame

- **Caylee Brea** is four years old.
- **Shahira Hala** is five years old.
- The daughters interrupt in childlike ways:
  - Caylee wants to see Shahira.
  - Shahira waves at Caylee.
  - One girl shows a toy, ribbon, book, or holiday gift.
  - One child misunderstands how the phone works and asks whether the cousin is "inside" the phone.
- The cousins' presence keeps the call from becoming too heavy.
- The daughters are proof that the twins' lives have continued into another generation.

### Beat 8: Samira and Sahara's Twin Language

- Under the ordinary holiday conversation, the twin language is active.
- Sahara can hear that Samira was emotional before the call.
- Samira can hear that Sahara is being careful, but she does not interpret that as suspicion about embassy work.
- Their closeness is real even though not everything is said.
- The sisters do not need to confess everything for the call to matter.

### Beat 9: Ending the Call

- The call ends with love intact.
- Samira may tell Sahara she loves her.
- Sahara may tell Samira to kiss Caylee for her.
- Samira may tell Sahara to kiss Shahira for her.
- The girls may wave goodbye.
- The ending should feel tender, not resolved.

### Beat 10: Samira and Andrei After the Call

- After the call ends, Andrei checks on Samira.
- Samira may say the call helped and hurt at the same time.
- Andrei understands that this is true.
- Caylee may still be in Samira's arms or nearby, grounding the moment in motherhood.
- Samira returns to the holiday day with the call still in her body.

### Beat 11: Sahara and Vladislav After the Call

- After the call ends, Sahara stays quiet for a moment with Shahira Hala in her arms.
- Vladislav notices the cost of the call.
- **Sahara Hannan and Vladislav have a conversation about the call.**
- Vladislav knows what Sahara does because **he also works at the Russian embassy as an intelligence officer**.
- He knows exactly what she did not say.
- Sahara may say:
  - Samira sounded happy;
  - Samira sounded tired;
  - Caylee is getting big;
  - it was good to hear her;
  - it hurt not to say the embassy truth;
  - Samira does not suspect anything, and that makes the secrecy easier and harder at the same time.
- Vladislav does not tell Sahara she should have said more.
- He does not tell her it should not hurt.
- He says the professional truth and the marital truth can both be real: she kept the line, and it still cost her.
- Vladislav also reminds Sahara why the line cannot be crossed: **the state would send Samira Chaka and Andrei to prison in Solikamsk if Sahara tells**.
- This warning should land as frightening but practical, not melodramatic. Vladislav is not threatening Sahara; he is naming the state's threat and the danger to Samira and Andrei.

### Beat 12: Vladislav's Support

- Vladislav reminds Sahara that secrecy has weight even when it is necessary.
- Because he is also an intelligence officer at the embassy, he can say this from inside the same structure.
- He may say:

> I know what you did not say. I know why. That does not make it painless.

> If you tell Samira, they will not only punish you. They will send Samira and Andrei to Solikamsk.

- He can also remind her that Samira's ignorance is not proof of distance; it is proof Sahara has kept the boundary clean.
- He reminds her again, plainly, that keeping Samira ignorant is part of keeping Samira and Andrei out of Solikamsk.
- Sahara may become emotional again, but less alone.

### Beat 13: Closing Image

- Closing image options:
  - Samira in Vladivostok holding Caylee after the call, Andrei beside her.
  - Sahara in Washington, DC holding Shahira after the call, Vladislav beside her.
  - Two iPhones dark now, two daughters still warm in their mothers' arms.
  - The sisters separated by distance and secrecy, but still connected by motherhood, holidays, and the twin bond.
