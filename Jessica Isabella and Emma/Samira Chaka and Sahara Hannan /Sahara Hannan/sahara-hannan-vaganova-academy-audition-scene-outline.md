# Sahara Hannan’s Vaganova Academy Audition — Scene Outline

## Characters
- **Sahara Hannan Key**: Eleven years old. Identical twin of Samira Chaka. Gifted in ballet and gymnastics. Has Type 1 diabetes, diagnosed at age four, so the audition includes an added layer of medical vigilance and self-management. She is drawn to the refined, lyrical, disciplined Vaganova style in St. Petersburg.
- **Samira Chaka Key**: Sahara’s twin. Present as emotional support. At this point, Samira has not yet gone to Bolshoi. Sahara’s audition comes first and begins the twins’ separation arc.
- **Jessica Isabella Key**: Biological mother. Protective and anxious, especially because of Sahara’s diabetes and the intensity of Russian ballet training.
- **Emma Fritz**: Co-mother. Steady, practical, and attentive to both Sahara’s emotional state and diabetes care.
- **Vaganova Teacher / Talent Spotter**: The teacher connected to the earlier Northern Europe cruise spotting, or a teacher who knows of the recommendation. Sees Sahara’s rare musicality and line.
- **Vaganova Academy Examiners**: Formal, precise, and traditional. They assess classical proportions, turnout, musicality, coordination, flexibility, artistry, discipline, and teachability.
- **Translator / Academy Liaison**: Helps the family understand formal instructions and process details.

## Setting
- **Time**: Around **2051**, when Sahara is eleven.
- **Location**: St. Petersburg, Russia — the Vaganova Academy.
- **Context**: During the Northern Europe cruise, a Vaganova teacher noticed rare talent in both twins. Sahara’s audition is the first major Russian ballet test either twin takes. This is the first door that opens away from Halifax and away from the twins’ shared everyday life.

## Core Emotional Question
Can Sahara accept being chosen first without feeling she is abandoning Samira?

## Key Plot Points
- The family arrives in St. Petersburg after the cruise-related recommendation leads to an official audition/test.
- The city feels elegant, old, and artistic, matching the Vaganova tradition.
- Sahara is excited but tries to hide it because she does not want Samira to feel left behind.
- Samira is proud but quietly unsettled; she has always done everything beside Sahara.
- Jessica and Emma review Sahara’s diabetes supplies before the audition: glucose meter/CGM status, fast sugar, water, snacks, and any academy medical requirements.
- Sahara feels embarrassed by the extra checks but knows they are necessary.
- The audition assesses her classical line, turnout, coordination, musicality, flexibility, and ability to absorb correction.
- Sahara’s strength is different from Samira’s: less explosive, more lyrical, precise, and musical.
- The examiners notice her ability to sustain line and phrase movement with restraint.
- A diabetes-related moment occurs: either a quick glucose check before entering, a low/high concern during waiting, or a planned pause that demonstrates Sahara’s maturity rather than weakness.
- Sahara makes a small technical mistake, receives a precise correction, and improves with quiet focus.
- Outside, Samira confronts the first feeling of being the twin not currently chosen.
- Sahara is accepted/recommended for Vaganova training, possibly conditional on medical planning and guardianship/living arrangements.
- The emotional ending centers on Sahara telling Samira that going first does not mean going alone.

## Scene Outline

### 1. St. Petersburg Arrival
- The family arrives in St. Petersburg, still carrying the emotional afterglow of the cruise and the shock of the Vaganova teacher’s interest.
- The city feels different from Moscow: canals, pale buildings, historic grandeur, and a quieter elegance.
- Jessica tries to frame the audition as an opportunity, not a demand.
- Emma watches Sahara closely, noticing both excitement and guilt.
- Samira keeps saying practical things — where the bags are, what time they need to leave — because she does not know how to say she is scared of being left behind.

### 2. Morning Preparations
- In the hotel room, the family prepares for the audition.
- Ballet clothes, hair, documents, shoes, and medical supplies are laid out carefully.
- Emma checks Sahara’s diabetes supplies with calm routine:
  - CGM/glucose check.
  - Fast sugar.
  - Water.
  - Snack.
  - Backup supplies.
  - Written medical notes for the academy.
- Sahara says, “I know,” a little sharply, then apologizes.
- Emma does not take offense. She says knowing is why they check.
- Jessica tells Sahara that diabetes is not a flaw in her dancing; it is just part of how they keep her safe enough to dance.

### 3. Outside the Vaganova Academy
- The academy building feels beautiful but intimidating.
- Sahara stares at it longer than she means to.
- Samira whispers that it looks like the kind of place that would correct your posture from across the street.
- Sahara laughs, tension breaking.
- The family enters together.

### 4. Waiting Area
- Other students wait with parents, teachers, or guardians.
- Sahara hears Russian and ballet vocabulary around her.
- She feels both very young and strangely ready.
- Samira sits close beside her.
- This time, Sahara does not step away; she needs the closeness.
- The liaison confirms paperwork and medical notes.
- A quiet diabetes check happens before the test. Sahara handles it competently.
- One examiner or staff member sees the check and asks through the translator if she can manage training safely.
- Sahara answers clearly that she has managed ballet, gymnastics, school, travel, and performance with diabetes, and she knows how to ask for help before it becomes an emergency.

### 5. Saying Goodbye at the Door
- Sahara’s name is called.
- Samira grips her hand once.
- Sahara whispers, “If I get scared, I’ll pretend you’re doing it with me.”
- Samira whispers back, “If you get in, I’ll pretend I’m not jealous.”
- Sahara looks startled.
- Samira gives a small smile to show it is honest but not cruel.
- Sahara goes into the room carrying both pride and guilt.

### 6. Barre Work — Classical Discipline
- The examiners begin with basic barre work.
- Vaganova emphasis: placement, purity of line, coordination of arms and head, turnout, musical timing, and restraint.
- Sahara is not as explosive as Samira, but her control and musical sensitivity stand out.
- She receives a correction about épaulement or arm coordination.
- She repeats the exercise with more clarity.
- The examiners notice her ability to refine rather than merely repeat.

### 7. Centre Work — Line and Musicality
- Sahara moves into the centre.
- She is nervous and initially dances too carefully.
- A teacher asks her to let the phrase continue through the arms and eyes, not stop at the counts.
- Sahara repeats the combination.
- Her movement becomes more lyrical, elegant, and continuous.
- The Vaganova style begins to make sense on her body.
- She is not trying to be bigger; she is trying to be truer.

### 8. Medical Awareness Moment
Possible versions:

#### Version A — Pre-Planned Check
- The academy allows a brief planned check between sections.
- Sahara handles it quickly and returns without drama.
- This shows maturity and reassures the academy that she can train responsibly.

#### Version B — Slight Low During Waiting
- Before entering, Sahara’s glucose trends low.
- Emma catches it, Sahara treats it, and they wait until she is safe.
- The audition starts a little later, showing the family will not sacrifice health for opportunity.

#### Version C — Question From Examiner
- The examiners ask about her diabetes after the physical assessment.
- Sahara answers directly, refusing shame.
- She says she does not dance despite her body; she dances by listening to it.

**Recommended canon choice:** Version A, with a brief examiner question. It keeps the audition flowing while integrating Sahara’s diabetes respectfully.

### 9. Outside the Room — Samira’s First Separation Fear
- Jessica and Emma wait anxiously.
- Samira tries to be supportive but is quieter than usual.
- Jessica asks if she is okay.
- Samira says, “What if they only need one of us?”
- Emma answers that schools may choose one path at a time, but family does not.
- Jessica adds that Sahara being seen does not make Samira invisible.
- Samira wants to believe it.

### 10. Final Combination
- Inside, Sahara is asked to repeat a final phrase.
- She thinks of water, canals, Halifax rain, and the quiet rhythm of breathing beside Samira at night.
- She lets the phrase stretch beyond the steps.
- Her finish is not dramatic; it is clear, sustained, and emotionally honest.
- The examiners confer.

### 11. Decision / Recommendation
Possible result options:

#### Version A — Conditional Acceptance
- Sahara is recommended for Vaganova training, conditional on medical plan, language preparation, placement, and living arrangements.
- This mirrors Samira’s later Bolshoi conditional acceptance.

#### Version B — Invitation to Summer Intensive First
- Sahara is invited to a Vaganova summer intensive as a trial step before full admission.
- This creates a slower transition.

#### Version C — Strong Recommendation for Future Entry
- The academy says she is gifted but young and recommends continued preparation before entering full-time.
- This delays the separation.

**Recommended canon choice:** Version A or B. If Sahara begins at Vaganova at age eleven, Version A fits best, with practical conditions.

### 12. Reunion With Samira
- Sahara exits trying to be calm.
- Jessica asks what they said.
- Sahara says they think she can train there.
- Samira is proud immediately, then the separation hits.
- Sahara sees it.
- She steps close and says, “I went first. That does not mean I go alone.”
- Samira says, “It kind of does.”
- Sahara answers, “Not inside.”
- The twins hold hands as the mothers talk through logistics with the liaison.

## Tone & Style Notes
- The tone should be elegant, tense, and tender.
- Vaganova should feel refined, exacting, and steeped in tradition — different from Samira’s later Bolshoi audition.
- Do not make diabetes melodramatic. Show it as real, serious, managed, and part of Sahara’s life.
- The examiners should be strict but not cruel.
- The emotional focus is not only whether Sahara gets in, but how being chosen first affects both twins.
- Sahara’s style should contrast Samira’s:
  - Sahara: lyricism, line, restraint, musical clarity.
  - Samira: power, attack, amplitude, dramatic boldness.
