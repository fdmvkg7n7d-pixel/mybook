# Samira Chaka’s Bolshoi Academy Entrance Test — Scene Outline

## Characters
- **Samira Chaka Key**: Twelve years old. Identical twin of Sahara Hannan. A gifted gymnast and ballet student from Halifax. This is her entrance test/audition for the **Bolshoi Ballet Academy in Moscow**. She is trying to prove she belongs in Russia’s Moscow tradition of ballet — not as Sahara’s echo, but as herself.
- **Sahara Hannan Key**: Samira’s identical twin. Already connected to the Vaganova path in St. Petersburg. Her presence is emotionally complicated: she is Samira’s closest person, but also the person Samira is trying to step away from just enough to become separate.
- **Jessica Isabella Key**: Biological mother. Young, protective, proud, and anxious. She knows what it means to have a body judged, trained, and tested, but she also believes deeply in Samira’s choice.
- **Emma Fritz**: Co-mother. Calm, steady, and observant. She gives Samira quiet reassurance and helps Jessica not panic outwardly.
- **Bolshoi Academy Examiners / Teachers**: A small panel of Russian ballet teachers and academy staff. They are formal, exacting, and not unkind. They test technique, musicality, strength, flexibility, coordination, artistry, and whether Samira can take corrections.
- **Translator / Academy Liaison**: Helps Jessica and Emma understand the process when needed. Samira has learned some Russian ballet terms but still relies on translation for formal instructions.

## Setting
- **Time**: Around **2052**, when Samira Chaka is twelve.
- **Location**: Moscow, Russia — the Bolshoi Ballet Academy / Moscow State Academy of Choreography.
- **Context**: Samira and Sahara were both noticed for ballet talent after the Northern Europe cruise/Vaganova spotting. Sahara’s path has already bent toward Vaganova in St. Petersburg. Samira now takes her own test in Moscow, choosing the Bolshoi Academy — the bolder, more athletic, dramatic pillar of Russian ballet.

## Core Emotional Question
Can Samira stand in a room without Sahara beside her and still know who she is?

## Key Plot Points
- Samira arrives at the academy with Jessica, Emma, and Sahara.
- The building feels formal and historic, different from Halifax and different from any local studio she has known.
- Sahara wants to support her, but Samira feels the pressure of being compared to her twin.
- Jessica and Emma try to reassure her without making the moment heavier.
- Samira goes into the audition/test room without her mothers and without Sahara.
- The examiners assess her basic ballet technique, musicality, physical readiness, flexibility, coordination, jumps, turns, and response to correction.
- Samira’s gymnastics background shows in her strength, fearlessness, and explosive power.
- The examiners notice that her dancing leans naturally toward the Bolshoi style: big, dramatic, athletic, brave.
- Samira makes at least one mistake, receives a correction, and improves immediately — this becomes one of the strongest points in her favor.
- Outside the room, Jessica struggles with waiting; Emma steadies her.
- Sahara has a private moment of realizing Samira may truly leave for Moscow and not follow her to St. Petersburg.
- Samira exits exhausted but upright, trying not to show how much she wants this.
- The result may be given immediately or after a short wait: Samira is accepted / invited to the next stage / strongly recommended for admission.
- The scene ends with Samira understanding that choosing Bolshoi is not choosing against Sahara — it is choosing herself.

## Scene Outline

### 1. Arrival in Moscow
- The family arrives at the academy early.
- Moscow feels large, cold, and imposing compared with Halifax.
- Jessica checks paperwork too many times: passport copies, medical forms, training history, recommendation letters, audition schedule.
- Emma notices and gently puts a hand over the folder to stop her from flipping through it again.
- Sahara stands close to Samira at first, shoulder almost touching shoulder, as if they are still one unit.
- Samira slowly steps half a pace away — not rejecting her, just practicing distance.

**Emotional beat:** Samira is scared, but she does not want fear to make her smaller.

### 2. Waiting Hall
- Other young dancers wait with parents or teachers.
- Some speak Russian fluently; some are international applicants.
- Samira hears familiar ballet words — *plié, tendu, adagio, allegro* — mixed with Russian instructions she only partly understands.
- She feels the gap between talent and belonging.
- Sahara whispers something only Samira would understand, a private twin-language joke from childhood.
- Samira smiles, then says quietly that she has to listen in Russian now.
- Sahara understands the deeper meaning: Samira is preparing to enter a world where Sahara cannot translate her.

### 3. Jessica and Emma’s Reassurance
- Jessica crouches or kneels to Samira’s level before the test.
- She tells Samira that no school gets to decide her worth.
- Emma adds that the test only tells them whether this is the right door at the right time.
- Samira says she knows — but she wants this door.
- Jessica almost says too much, then stops herself.
- Emma gives Samira the cleaner, steadier sentence: “Then go show them how you move.”

### 4. Entering the Test Room Alone
- Samira is called in.
- Sahara instinctively starts to move with her, then stops.
- Samira looks back once.
- The door closes between the twins.
- Inside: mirrors, barres, piano, high ceiling, polished floor, examiners at a table.
- Samira feels the absence of Sahara beside her like missing music.
- Then the pianist plays a few notes, and her body remembers what to do.

### 5. Barre and Basics
- The examiners begin with simple exercises.
- They are not looking for tricks first; they are looking for placement, discipline, turnout, line, musicality, control, and attention.
- Samira’s gymnastics strength makes some movements powerful, but one examiner corrects her for using force where ballet needs length.
- She flushes, nods, tries again.
- The second attempt is visibly better.
- The examiners exchange a small look: she can take correction quickly.

### 6. Centre Work
- Samira moves into centre combinations.
- The choreography is short but revealing: balance, port de bras, coordination, timing, épaulement, and musical phrasing.
- She starts too cautiously.
- A teacher asks her to repeat it with more breath, more theatre, more courage.
- This instruction unlocks her.
- She dances bigger the second time — not messy, not wild, but more alive.
- Her natural dramatic quality appears.

**Bolshoi-style beat:** The panel sees that Samira’s strength is not only technical. She has attack, amplitude, and presence.

### 7. Allegro / Jumps
- The test shifts to jumps.
- Samira’s gymnastics background becomes an advantage.
- She jumps high and lands with control, though she has to be reminded not to over-muscle the preparation.
- One examiner asks for the combination again, smaller and cleaner.
- Samira wants to show power, but she obeys.
- The cleaner version proves she has discipline as well as force.

### 8. Musicality and Expression
- The pianist plays a short phrase and Samira is asked to move across the floor.
- No twin. No mother. No familiar studio.
- She thinks of Halifax, the apartment, late nights, Jessica studying, Emma staying, Sahara breathing beside her in the dark.
- The movement becomes personal.
- She does not perform sadness; she performs resolve.
- The examiners see a child with a story inside her, but not a child crushed by that story.

### 9. Outside the Room
- Jessica hears muffled piano through the door and tries to read every sound.
- Emma reminds her they cannot parent Samira through the wall.
- Sahara sits quietly, unusually withdrawn.
- Jessica notices and asks if she is okay.
- Sahara says, “If she gets in, she’ll be in Moscow.”
- Emma gently answers, “And you’ll still be her sister.”
- Sahara says, “I know. But not every day.”
- This is the first time the separation feels real.

### 10. Final Correction
- Inside, a teacher gives Samira one final correction — something precise about line, shoulders, or preparation.
- Samira repeats the phrase.
- This time, she combines both parts of herself:
  - the gymnast’s power,
  - the ballerina’s discipline,
  - the Key family’s stubbornness,
  - and her own wish to be seen separately from her twin.
- She finishes breathing hard but controlled.
- She waits respectfully.

### 11. The Decision / Recommendation
Possible versions:

#### Version A — Immediate Acceptance
- The panel confers briefly.
- Through the translator, they say Samira is recommended for admission.
- They note that she will need hard work, language support, and refinement, but she has the qualities they want.
- Samira nods formally, then nearly breaks into tears only after she leaves the room.

#### Version B — Invitation to Final Stage
- The panel says she has passed this stage and is invited to the next/final evaluation.
- This keeps the arc open and allows a second scene.
- Samira exits with hope instead of certainty.

#### Version C — Conditional Acceptance
- The panel accepts her conditionally, pending language preparation, placement level, and medical/fitness clearance.
- This creates a practical transition arc for moving from Halifax to Moscow.

**Recommended canon choice:** Version C. It feels realistic and gives Jessica and Emma emotional/practical decisions to make after the test.

### 12. Reunion in the Hall
- Samira comes out and tries to stay composed.
- Jessica reads her face immediately and asks, “What happened?”
- Samira says, “They think I can learn here.”
- Emma closes her eyes in relief.
- Jessica hugs Samira carefully, not wanting to crush her after the audition.
- Sahara waits half a second, then steps in and hugs her too.
- Samira whispers, “I’m not leaving you.”
- Sahara whispers back, “Yes, you are. But not in the bad way.”
- That becomes the emotional landing of the scene.

## Tone & Style Notes
- Keep the scene proud, tense, and intimate.
- The test should feel rigorous but not cruel.
- Avoid making the examiners villains. They are exacting because the school is serious.
- Emphasize Samira’s ability to respond to correction; this is often more important than perfection.
- Let Jessica and Emma’s parenting styles contrast:
  - Jessica is emotionally protective and visibly anxious.
  - Emma is quiet, steady, and practical.
- Let Sahara’s reaction be layered: proud, scared, slightly left behind, but loving.
- The main victory is not only admission. It is Samira choosing an identity that belongs to her.
