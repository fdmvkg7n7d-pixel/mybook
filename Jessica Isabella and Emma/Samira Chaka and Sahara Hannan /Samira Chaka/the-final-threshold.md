# The Final Threshold — Saying Goodbye to Andrei

**Purpose:** To capture the heartbreaking and visceral transition from Samira’s civilian life to her existence as a state asset. This scene emphasizes the desperation of a final encounter—a primal attempt to anchor one another before the "Siberian Silence" begins.

**Characters:**
- **Samira Chaka Key (20):** She has just come from the KGB offices. She is terrified, but her resolve is a shield. She chooses to exclude her family from her truth to protect them.
- **Andrei:** Her steady, observant partner. The news of her disappearance triggers a visceral need to reclaim her from the state before she is gone.

**Setting:** 
- **Samira’s Moscow Apartment:** A space of transition. The suitcase is packed. The red coat hangs by the door.

---

## Beat Sheet

### 1. The Declaration of Departure
- Samira tells Andrei she is leaving now.
- She delivers the blow: **"I don't know how long I'll be gone."**
- She reveals the decision of silence: **"I am not telling my family. Not Jessica, not Emma... and not Sahara."**

### 2. The Breaking Point (The Visceral Response)
- Andrei's rational exterior collapses. He acts on a primal, desperate impulse.
- Driven by the fear of the coming blackout, Andrei **hungrily and hard** takes her behind.
- This is a desperate attempt to memorize her body and claim her one last time before the state transforms her into "Candidate Four."

### 3. The Aftermath of Intensity
- A heavy, weighted silence follows.
- Samira begins to settle back into her "mask" of Candidate Four to survive the departure.
- Andrei wraps the heavy wool scarf around her neck—a final, tender gesture of protection.

### 4. The Final Departure
- Andrei lets her go.
- Samira picks up her suitcase, leaves her red coat on the hook, and locks the door.
- The click of the lock is the final punctuation mark on her civilian life.
