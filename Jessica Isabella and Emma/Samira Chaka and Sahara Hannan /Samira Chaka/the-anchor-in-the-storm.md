# The Anchor in the Storm — Remi and the Boys

**Purpose:** To provide a grounding contrast to the high-tension search for Samira. This scene focuses on the "safe harbor" of the boys' daily lives and Remi’s role as a stabilizing force.

**Characters:**
- **Remi:** Patient, affectionate, and dependable. He keeps the boys' world stable so the mothers can fight.
- **Eliot David & Kalvin LaMarcus Key-Fritz (5):** Energetic twins, unaware of the crisis.

**Setting:** 
- **The Halifax Home, Local School, Community Pool, and Gymnastics Center.**

---

## Beat Sheet

### 1. The Morning Whirlwind (School Run)
- Chaos of breakfast and socks.
- Remi manages the routine with playful ease.
- The school gate: Remi ensures the boys enter their day with a sense of normalcy.

### 2. The Blue Space (Swimming Lessons)
- The sensory experience of the pool.
- The boys practice kicks and bubbles; Remi cheers them on.
- Reinforcement of the family principle: bodies as tools for exploration.

### 3. The Legacy of Movement (Gymnastics Lessons)
- The gym: springy floors and high bars.
- The boys attempt rolls and balance, shouting, *"Look, Remi! I'm doing it like Samira!"*
- Remi hides his pain and supports their aspiration.

### 4. The Wind-Down (The Return Home)
- The post-activity crash. The boys are exhausted and cling to Remi.
- Remi tidies the house and prepares a space of peace for the mothers' return.
- Final image: Remi on the sofa with the two boys asleep against him.
