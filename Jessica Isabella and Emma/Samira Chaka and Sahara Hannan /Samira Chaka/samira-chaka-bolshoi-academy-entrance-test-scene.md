# Samira Chaka’s Bolshoi Academy Entrance Test — Beat Sheet

## Purpose of This File
This is a **beat sheet / planning version**, not a full prose chapter. It preserves the important story beats from the expanded scene while keeping the material in outline form.

## Characters Present
- **Samira Chaka Key** — age twelve; auditioning/testing for the Bolshoi Ballet Academy in Moscow.
- **Sahara Hannan Key** — Samira’s identical twin; emotionally supportive but quietly afraid of separation.
- **Jessica Isabella Key** — biological mother; proud, protective, anxious.
- **Emma Fritz** — co-mother; calm, steady, practical.
- **Bolshoi Academy examiners** — formal, exacting, focused on technique, musicality, physical readiness, and teachability.
- **Translator / academy liaison** — helps the family understand the process.

## Setting
- **Time:** Around 2052, when Samira is twelve.
- **Place:** Moscow, Russia — Bolshoi Ballet Academy / Moscow State Academy of Choreography.
- **Context:** Sahara’s path is already connected to Vaganova in St. Petersburg. Samira is now testing for Bolshoi in Moscow, marking her first major independent step away from her twin.

## Core Emotional Question
Can Samira stand in a room without Sahara beside her and still know who she is?

---

## Beat Sheet

### 1. Moscow Feels Different From Halifax
- Samira notices that Moscow feels larger, colder, more formal, and more intimidating than Halifax.
- The Bolshoi Academy building feels historic and serious.
- The setting makes the audition feel real.
- Samira understands that this is not just another ballet class — this is a possible doorway into a completely different life.

### 2. Jessica’s Anxiety Shows
- Jessica checks the paperwork repeatedly: passports, medical forms, recommendation letters, audition schedule.
- Samira notices and gently points out that Jessica has already checked everything several times.
- Emma calmly stops Jessica from opening the folder again.
- Emma reminds Jessica not to make Samira carry her fear.
- Jessica apologizes to Samira and recenters herself.

### 3. Sahara Stands Beside Samira — Then Samira Steps Away
- Sahara stands very close to Samira, the way the twins always do in unfamiliar places.
- Samira shifts half a step away.
- It is not rejection; it is practice for separation.
- Sahara notices but does not move closer again.
- This small physical distance foreshadows the larger emotional distance created by Bolshoi and Vaganova.

### 4. Waiting Hall at the Academy
- The family waits with other young dancers and parents.
- Samira hears Russian mixed with familiar ballet terms.
- She understands some words but not everything.
- The familiar ballet vocabulary helps her feel less lost.
- Sahara whispers a private twin joke to help Samira relax.
- Samira smiles, then says she has to listen now — meaning she must step into this world on her own.

### 5. Jessica and Emma Reassure Samira
- Jessica kneels or crouches to Samira’s level.
- Jessica tells her that no school gets to decide her worth.
- Emma frames the truth more simply: Samira wants this door.
- Samira admits she does.
- Emma tells her to go show them how she moves.
- This gives Samira a clear emotional push into the audition.

### 6. Samira Enters Alone
- The liaison calls Samira’s name.
- Sahara instinctively starts to move with her, then stops.
- Samira looks back once.
- The door closes between the twins.
- This is the first major visual symbol of their separation.

### 7. First Impression of the Test Room
- Large studio, mirrors, barres, piano, examiners at a table.
- The examiners are serious but not cruel.
- The translator explains instructions when needed.
- Samira feels the absence of Sahara physically — no matching body beside her, no shared rhythm.
- The piano begins, and her training takes over.

### 8. Barre Work — Strength Needs Refinement
- The examiners begin with basic barre exercises.
- They assess placement, discipline, turnout, line, control, and musicality.
- Samira’s gymnastics background shows in her strength and power.
- A teacher corrects her for using too much force in a tendu or similar basic movement.
- Samira repeats it with more length and less attack.
- The panel notices that she can take correction quickly.

### 9. Centre Work — Samira Starts Too Small
- Samira moves into the centre.
- Without Sahara nearby, she initially dances too cautiously.
- A teacher asks her to repeat the phrase with more breath, theatre, and courage.
- The correction unlocks her.
- On the second attempt, Samira dances bigger and with more presence.
- The examiners begin to see her natural Bolshoi qualities: attack, amplitude, athleticism, and dramatic energy.

### 10. Allegro / Jumps — Her Gymnastics Becomes an Asset
- The test shifts to jumps.
- Samira’s power and fearlessness stand out.
- She jumps high and lands well, but the examiners ask her to repeat with a smaller preparation and cleaner discipline.
- Samira obeys instead of showing off.
- The second version proves she is not only powerful; she can be trained.

### 11. Outside the Room — The Family Waits
- Jessica hears music and silence through the door and struggles not to panic.
- Emma reminds her that she cannot parent Samira through the wall.
- Sahara sits quietly, realizing that if Samira gets in, she will truly be in Moscow.
- Sahara says she knows Samira will still be her sister, but not every day.
- Jessica and Emma understand that the separation is becoming real for both twins.

### 12. Musicality / Expression Test
- Samira is asked to move across the floor to a musical phrase.
- She thinks about Halifax, her mothers, Sahara, and the family that made her feel she belongs.
- The movement becomes personal without becoming melodramatic.
- She dances with resolve rather than sadness.
- The examiners see that she carries emotional depth as well as physical ability.

### 13. Final Correction
- A teacher gives one last precise correction — shoulders, line, or not closing herself off at the end.
- Samira repeats the phrase.
- This time she combines:
  - the gymnast’s power,
  - the ballerina’s discipline,
  - the Key family’s stubbornness,
  - and her own need to be seen separately from Sahara.
- She finishes tired but controlled.

### 14. Conditional Admission
- The examiners confer.
- Through the translator, they say Samira has strong potential.
- They note areas to refine:
  - gymnastics habits,
  - language preparation,
  - placement level,
  - medical/fitness review,
  - transition logistics.
- They recommend **conditional admission**.
- This keeps the result realistic and creates future planning for Jessica and Emma.

### 15. Reunion in the Hall
- Samira exits and tells her family that the academy thinks she can learn there.
- Jessica is emotional and hugs her.
- Emma is relieved and proud, emphasizing that the examiners saw Samira learn.
- Sahara initially stands slightly apart.
- Samira fears the distance has already begun.
- Sahara steps forward and hugs her.

### 16. Emotional Landing Between the Twins
- Samira tells Sahara she is not leaving her.
- Sahara answers honestly that yes, she is — but not in the bad way.
- This becomes the emotional meaning of the scene.
- Bolshoi is not a rejection of Sahara.
- It is Samira’s first step into being herself.

---

## Canon Result
- **Samira receives conditional admission / recommendation for admission to the Bolshoi Ballet Academy.**
- The acceptance is not presented as simple or finalized; Jessica and Emma still need to handle practical details.
- The emotional result is more important than the administrative one: Samira proves she can stand alone.

## Style Notes for Future Expansion
- Keep this as an outline unless specifically asked for prose.
- If expanded later, keep the tone tense, proud, intimate, and restrained.
- Do not make the examiners villains.
- Emphasize teachability over perfection.
- Keep Sahara’s reaction loving but complicated.
- Keep Jessica anxious and protective, Emma calm and grounding.
