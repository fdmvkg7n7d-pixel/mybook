# SCENE OUTLINE: SPERM BANK VISIT – FINDING THE DONOR

**Part of the ~2054 second child arc (Jessica & Emma age 30).**

**User direction:** "They go to a bank and find donor."

**Context:**
- Jessica and Emma have decided they want another child.
- Path: Jessica carries the child herself (her own eggs + donor sperm). This is done "separated" — Rachel Darcy is not involved.
- They have been searching via a "donation drive" (previous outline) and now go to the sperm bank to review options and select a donor in person.
- This is an active, intentional step – not passive anonymous selection.
- Telling the existing twins (Samira and Sahara, ~14) will happen before Jessica gets pregnant.

**Characters:**
- Jessica Isabella Key (30)
- Emma Fritz (30)
- Sperm bank staff/counselor (supportive, professional; perhaps a specific counselor they meet with)
- The twins (Samira and Sahara, ~14) are not present but will be told before the pregnancy starts.

**Setting:** A high-end, private sperm bank/fertility clinic. Discreet location. Private consultation room with tablets or binders of donor profiles. Comfortable, clinical but warm – soft lighting, tissues, water, informational materials on the process. Emphasis on privacy and confidentiality (critical for the Key family).

**Time:** After the donation drive event or as part of it. They have narrowed options or are using the bank's resources following the drive.

**Tone:** Intimate, serious, hopeful, a little clinical and overwhelming. Mix of practical decision-making and emotional weight. Saga restraint: no graphic details. Focus on their partnership, values, the future child, and how this fits their complex family (Jessica carrying the pregnancy herself + Emma as co-parent).

## BEFORE THE SCENE

- They have had the big conversations with each other.
- Medical plans are in motion or scheduled.

- They have prepared a list of criteria: health/genetics, values/ethics (openness about donation, views on contact with child), personality/education/background that feels compatible with their interfaith, loving, truth-telling family.

## THE SCENE – AT THE SPERM BANK

**Arrival and setup:**
- They arrive together. Staff greets them discreetly. They are taken to a private room.
- Staff explains the process: profiles (detailed medical, family history, photos if available, donor statements/essays, personality inventories), options for anonymity vs. identity-release, legal aspects.
- They may meet with a counselor first to discuss emotional/ethical aspects.

**Key beats:**
1. **Reviewing profiles:** Sitting side by side with tablets or printed binders. Reading, discussing quietly.
   - Jessica: Looks for traits that complement or balance – perhaps someone with a creative or resilient story, or health history that aligns with their medical awareness.
   - Emma: Focuses on character, kindness, sense of responsibility. She may be moved by donors who talk about wanting to help families like theirs.
   - They read donor letters or watch short videos if available. Comments like "He sounds like he values family the way we do" or "This one talks about wanting the child to know their story someday."

2. **Staff interaction:** A counselor or coordinator joins for questions.
   - Questions about the donation process (kept high-level).
   - Clarifying identity-release options (important for their "tell the truth" family rule).
   - Any red flags or standout details in medical/genetic info.
   - Discussion of how many vials/embryos, costs (though wealth makes this less of an issue, it might still come up practically).

3. **The emotional weight:**
   - One profile stands out. They linger on it.
   - Conversation: "This feels right." "I can see this person in our child's story without it taking over."
   - They talk about the twins: how new brothers from a donor will fit, how they'll explain it later. They decide to tell Samira and Sahara **before** Jessica gets pregnant (during the three-week Russia vacation visiting Moscow, St. Petersburg, Sochi Winter Olympics). New canon: Samira will like the idea of a sibling from the start (shares during activities and talks with Sahara about their bond); Sahara will initially not like it but comes to like the idea by the *end of the vacation* after family activities across cities, twin talk, reflection on diabetes/family history of truth and "your body is yours", quiet conversations with mothers, Olympics inspiration. Includes swimming where the twins are undressed (per family bodily autonomy values). Jessica and Emma are so glad to hear this with specific glad response.
   - Faith/ethics moment: Brief reflection on creating life this way, how it aligns with their beliefs (love and family above all).

4. **Decision or next step:**
   - They don't necessarily choose on the spot, but they find "the one" or narrow to 1-2.
   - They request more information, or decide to move forward with one.
   - Staff helps with next steps (purchase, paperwork, coordination with their fertility team for IVF with Jessica's eggs).

5. **After the review (in the room or on the way out):**
   - Quiet moment between them. Holding hands, a deep breath.
   - Jessica: "We're really doing this."
   - Emma: "Together. Like always."

**End image:** Jessica and Emma leaving the bank, folder or digital confirmation in hand. A mix of excitement, relief, and the gravity of having taken a concrete step toward their new child. They take a quiet moment together on the way home (Rachel informed later, per family plans).

## QUESTIONS FOR THE DONOR (Prepared List They Use at the Bank)

They come prepared with (or ask the counselor to cover) these questions while reviewing profiles or in discussion. Questions are asked through staff or via donor statements where direct contact isn't possible.

**Medical & Genetic History**
- What is your complete personal and family medical history (including parents, siblings, grandparents)?
- Have you or any close family members been diagnosed with genetic conditions, hereditary diseases, cancer, heart disease, diabetes, mental health disorders, or birth defects?
- Have you undergone carrier screening, genetic testing, or any other health screenings? What were the results?
- Any history of infertility, developmental issues, or chronic illnesses in your family?
- Current health: medications, allergies, exercise habits, diet, substance use (smoking, alcohol, drugs)?
- Blood type and any other relevant physical/genetic details?

**Personal Background**
- Age, ethnicity/background, physical description (height, weight, eye/hair color, etc.).
- Education level, field of study, current occupation and career path.
- Family background: parents' and siblings' ages, occupations, health, and relationship to you.
- Any children of your own? Their ages and health?
- Marital/relationship status and history.

**Donation Motivations & Philosophy**
- Why did you decide to donate sperm?
- How many donations have you made? Any known successful pregnancies or children from your donations?
- What do you hope the recipient family and the child will know or understand about you?
- What are your views on donor-conceived children and their right to know their origins?

**Anonymity, Contact & Future**
- Are you open to identity-release (the child contacting you at 18 or later)?
- Would you be willing to meet the child, exchange letters, or provide medical updates if requested?
- Any preferences or concerns about the type of family using your donation (e.g., two mothers, surrogacy, interfaith households)?
- How do you feel about the child knowing the full story of their conception?

**Personality, Values & Lifestyle**
- How would you describe your personality, strengths, weaknesses, and core values?
- Hobbies, interests, talents, passions, or notable life experiences?
- Religious, spiritual, cultural, or philosophical beliefs? How important are they, and how do you practice them?
- Views on family, parenting, education, diversity, and different family structures?
- Any particular qualities or messages you'd want to pass on to a child conceived with your donation?

**Saga-Specific Considerations (Discussed Between Jessica & Emma or with Counselor)**
- Compatibility with an interfaith household (Catholic roots with Muslim heritage honored in names and traditions)?
- Alignment with our family's core values: unconditional love, truth-telling (no secrets about origins), chosen family, and bodily autonomy?
- Any red flags around openness, responsibility, or views on the child's future autonomy?
- How might this donor's background complement or enrich a family that includes a genetic mother (Jessica) and two primary parents (Jessica and Emma)?

They use these to evaluate profiles, request more details from staff. The list helps them stay aligned with the saga's emphasis on honesty, care, and intentional family-building. (Any sharing with Rachel happens later, per open items.)
