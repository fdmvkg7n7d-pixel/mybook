**User clarification:** Jessica Isabella and Emma are doing this separated. Rachel Darcy is not involved.

**Context from existing canon:**

- At ~2054, the twins are approximately 14 years old.
- Jessica carried the first pregnancy (biological mother).
- Emma is the steadfast co-parent (not biological).

- The twins (teens) are consulted and involved in the decision per family rule.
- This emphasizes the couple building their family independently on their own terms this time.

## THE CONVERSATION — JESSICA AND EMMA DECIDE

**Setting:** Their home in Halifax (or wherever they are at 30; possibly still tied to Dalhousie or new careers). Evening, after the twins are in bed or out. Quiet, intimate space.

**Key beats:**
- They have been happy with the twins but feel the pull for another child. The twins are now independent teens (ballet training in Russia at Vaganova/Bolshoi — their paths are underway; the parents visit them there).
- Jessica brings it up first (the reacher): "I keep thinking... what if we had one more?"
- Emma (the stayer) listens, then reveals she has been thinking the same but didn't want to push — "I didn't want to ask for more than we already have."
- They talk about the practicals: Jessica's body/history (burns, conjoined separation — medical consult needed), Emma's desire to parent another, the twins' ages and how a new sibling would fit.
- Faith discussion: Catholic and Muslim perspectives on family expansion, assisted reproduction, carrying another child. They find resonance in both traditions' emphasis on love and care.
- They decide to do this independently, without involving Rachel this time.

**Emotional core:**
- Jessica and Emma are choosing to build this chapter separated from previous family arrangements.
- Both are scared but excited — the "your body is yours" principle means full consent and autonomy for Jessica carrying this time.
- They are committed to telling the truth to the twins and any future child.

## THE PROCESS (HIGH-LEVEL ARC BEATS)

1. **Consults and decisions:** Fertility specialists, family meetings (Terri Anne, other sisters if appropriate). Donor chosen via sperm bank/donation drive.
2. **Telling the twins (before pregnancy):** Honest conversation with Samira and Sahara (~14) during a three-week family vacation in Russia visiting three cities: Moscow (Samira's Bolshoi), St. Petersburg (Sahara's Vaganova, where the initial telling happens), and Sochi (Winter Olympics games city). During the vacation, the twins swim (hotel pools or beaches) and are comfortable being undressed, aligning with the family's bodily autonomy ("your body is yours") values. They are told the plan, involved in the decision, and given space to process before anything medical happens. New canon: Samira likes the idea of having a sibling from the start (enthusiastic); Sahara initially does not like it (thoughtful resistance, possibly tied to diabetes or change) but comes to like the idea by the *end of the vacation* (influenced by the multi-city trip and Olympics atmosphere). Jessica and Emma are so glad to hear this. The foreign multi-city setting (twins' training + Olympics) highlights the twins' independence while keeping family truth central; their initial differing reactions and positive resolution underscore individuation and the value of space for feelings to evolve.
3. **Egg retrieval and IVF:** Jessica goes through the egg retrieval process. Emma supports. Embryos created with the selected donor sperm.
4. **Transfer and pregnancy:** Embryos transferred to Jessica. She carries the pregnancy. Jessica and Emma are deeply involved together (appointments, support). The twins are kept informed but the pregnancy starts after they were told.
5. **Birth:** Jessica and Emma have identical twin sons: Eliot David and Kalvin LaMarcus. The whole family present (per Key family style). Naming honors family traditions.
6. **Parenting the new children:** Jessica and Emma as the two mothers. Samira and Sahara become big sisters to their new brothers.
7. **Ongoing family structure:** The new sons have two mothers. Rachel may be told later as family, but is not part of the conception or carrying.
