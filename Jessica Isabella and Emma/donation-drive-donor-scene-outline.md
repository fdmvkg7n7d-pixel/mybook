# SCENE OUTLINE: THE DONATION DRIVE (FINDING THE SPERM DONOR)

**Part of the ~2054 second child arc.**

**Characters:** Jessica Isabella Key (age 30), Emma Fritz (age 30), fertility clinic staff or drive organizers. Brief cameos from other attendees or potential donors (if they meet any). The twins (Samira and Sahara, ~14) are not involved at the drive itself for privacy/sensitivity (they will be told later, before any pregnancy, during a family vacation in Russia).

**Setting:** A sperm bank or fertility clinic's "donor drive" event (could be a public-ish open house, a vetted private drive among trusted networks, or a combination). Modern, clinical but welcoming space – profiles on screens or in binders, informational sessions, perhaps a small reception area. Emphasis on privacy and discretion (the Keys' wealth and fame make this tricky; they likely use a high-end, confidential service or a private arrangement).

**Time in saga:** ~2054, after Jessica and Emma decide they want another child and commit to the path of Jessica carrying (her own eggs + donor sperm). This is the active search phase for the donor, before the sperm bank visit for final selection and before telling the twins. The drive happens in Halifax (or wherever they are based); the Russia vacation and telling come later in the arc.

**Tone:** Hopeful but weighty, intimate, a bit awkward and clinical mixed with emotional depth. The saga's signature restraint: no graphic details about donation process. Focus on the emotional search – what qualities they value in a donor, the ethics, the future child's right to know origins, faith intersections, and how this decision ripples through their unique family structure (Jessica as genetic and carrying mother, Emma as co-parent).

**User directive tie-in:** Donor choice via donation drive then sperm bank.

## BEFORE THE SCENE — SETUP

- Jessica and Emma have had the big conversation and decided on another child (Jessica carrying, "separated" with no Rachel involvement).
- Medical consults are underway or scheduled (Jessica's history reviewed: burns, conjoined separation, previous pregnancy at 16).
- The missing piece: the sperm donor.
- They have researched options: anonymous bank donors, known donors from their circles, or participating in a "donation drive" – an event or campaign where donors come forward, profiles are shared, and selection happens with more transparency or personal element.
- Why a drive? It feels more active and intentional than scrolling profiles alone. It allows them to ask questions (ethically, through facilitators), get a sense of the person behind the donation, and perhaps feel more connected to the choice. Given the family's emphasis on truth and not hiding origins, a drive might align better with eventual openness.
- They have prepared: lists of desired traits (health, values, perhaps cultural/religious resonance with their interfaith household, intelligence, kindness, etc.), questions about the donor's views on contact with the child later, genetic testing, etc.
- Privacy is paramount. As the Key family, they likely attend under discretion, perhaps with a trusted intermediary or in a private session arranged by the clinic. Or it's a drive within a network of families with similar assisted reproduction stories.
- They know the full plan includes telling the existing twins before any medical steps or pregnancy begin (the telling will happen later during a vacation in Russia).

**Emotional undercurrent:**
- Jessica: As the genetic contributor and the one who will carry, she wants the donor to complement or balance in a way that feels right for the child.
- Emma: The stayer who wants stability and love above all; she may be particularly attuned to how the donor's story could affect the child's sense of belonging.
- Both: This is not just "getting sperm" – it's choosing a piece of the child's origin story. It echoes the heavy origins of the twins (the beach incident) but in a chosen, positive, controlled way. They are determined to do this with full agency and family involvement (including telling the twins first).

## THE SCENE — AT THE DONATION DRIVE

**Arrival and atmosphere:**
- They arrive together. The space is professional yet warm – informational posters about the donation process (kept high-level), donor profiles available (anonymized or with photos/stories depending on the drive type), staff explaining options.
- If it's a group drive: Other couples or individuals there, but the Keys keep a low profile or have a private room.
- If more private: A curated selection of 5-10 donor profiles presented specifically for them, with the opportunity to request more info or even (in some programs) meet donors in a mediated way.

**Key beats:**

1. **Reviewing profiles together:** They sit with binders or tablets. Reading about health history, education, hobbies, reasons for donating, views on anonymity vs. openness. Jessica might focus on genetic compatibility or traits that "feel like us." Emma might look for emotional intelligence or stories of kindness/resilience.

2. **The drive element:** There is an event aspect – perhaps a short presentation by donors (anonymous or not), Q&A with staff about the process, or stories from other families who used the drive. They hear about the "donation drive" philosophy: encouraging men to donate as an act of generosity, with emphasis on the child's future.

3. **Ethical and faith discussion on-site or immediately after:** In the car/home afterward, or even with a counselor at the clinic. They talk about:
   - Should the child know the donor? (Aligns with Key family "tell the truth" rule.)
   - Interfaith angle: How does a donor's background fit with raising the child Catholic with Emma's Muslim heritage honored?
   - The twins: How will Samira and Sahara (14) feel about a half-sibling from a donor? (They plan to tell them before pregnancy, during the upcoming three-week Russia trip visiting three cities: Moscow, St. Petersburg, Sochi Winter Olympics. New canon: Samira will like the idea from the start (and help Sahara process); Sahara initially will not like it but comes to like the idea by the *end of the vacation* after family activities across cities, twin talk about bond, reflection on diabetes/family truth/"your body is yours", quiet conversations with mothers, Olympics inspiration. Includes swimming where the twins are undressed (per family bodily autonomy). Jessica and Emma are so glad with specific response. Reactions to be explored in the telling scene.)
   - No involvement from Rachel (this is their independent chapter).

4. **A specific donor stands out:** During the drive/review, one profile or interaction resonates. (Leave room for TBD – e.g., a donor who is a teacher, or has a story of chosen family, or has a cultural background that bridges, or simply feels "right" without over-romanticizing.)

5. **The decision process:** They don't choose on the spot. They take profiles home, discuss privately, consult doctors, pray or reflect according to their faiths. This narrows to options for the follow-up sperm bank visit.

6. **Emotional moment:** One of them (perhaps Emma) has a quiet realization or tear: "This is how we build our family now – intentionally, together, with eyes open."

**End image:** Jessica and Emma leaving the drive/event, holding hands or leaning on each other, profiles in a folder. Hope mixed with the gravity of it. They are choosing the next piece of their child's story with the same care they have shown everything else. They head home to plan the bank visit and the Russia trip where they'll tell the twins.
