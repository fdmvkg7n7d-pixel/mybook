# SCENE OUTLINE: 2 TIMOTHY 1:7 — JESSICA ISABELLA AT THE GYM

**Characters:**
- **Jessica Isabella Key** (~7 years old)
- **Mike Key** — her father

**Setting:** The gymnastics competition venue in Ottawa. A quiet corner of the gym, away from the other competitors. The apparatus floor is visible in the distance — beams, mats, vault runway. The air smells like chalk and floor wax. Other gymnasts are warming up. Jessica Isabella and Mike have found a small alcove near the parent seating area, where it is slightly quieter.

**Timeframe:** Competition morning, just before warm-ups begin. This fits between Beat 3 ("Competition Morning") and Beat 4 ("First Events") of the existing Ottawa scene outline.

---

## THE SCENE

### Beat 1 — The gym is loud

- Jessica Isabella is standing near the edge of the gym floor, holding her gym bag strap with both hands. The building is huge — high ceilings, bright lights, echoes of feet pounding the spring floor, coaches calling out corrections.
- She has been quiet since they arrived. Not withdrawn — watchful. Her eyes move from the balance beam to the uneven bars to the other gymnasts stretching in their leotards.
- Mike is beside her. He has not rushed her. He let her take off her jacket, hang it over a chair, and stand there to absorb the space.

### Beat 2 — The quiet corner

- Mike touches her shoulder. *"Let's find a place to sit for a minute before you warm up."*
- He leads her to a small alcove near the parent seating — a bench against the wall, slightly out of the main traffic. They sit down together.
- The gym noise is muffled here, not gone, but softer.
- Jessica Isabella sets her gym bag beside her. She unzips it — not to check her grips or her water bottle. She reaches past them and pulls out her small Bible.

### Beat 3 — The verse she brought

- Mike watches her hold it. He does not say anything. She brought it from the hotel room; he saw her pack it that morning.
- She opens it where the ribbon marker is — 2 Timothy 1. She has been reading this letter in the evenings at home, picked it because of the homeschooling lesson about courage.
- She looks at the page. Her lips move silently, reading the words to herself first.
- Then she reads aloud, her voice steady in the gym noise:
  > *"For God hath not given us the spirit of fear; but of power, and of love, and of a sound mind."*

### Beat 4 — The understanding

- She closes the Bible but keeps her hands on it. She looks at Mike.
- *"I'm still scared, Daddy. But I think that's okay. Because the verse doesn't say I won't be scared. It says God didn't *give* me fear. So fear is not from Him. It's just... something I feel. But power and love and a sound mind — those are from Him. So I already have them."*
- Mike does not look away. He hears what she is saying — a seven-year-old who has already learned to distinguish between the fear she feels and the truth she holds.
- He says, quietly: *"When did you get so wise?"*
- Jessica Isabella shrugs, a small seven-year-old shrug. *"I've been reading it every night since we learned about Timothy."*

### Beat 5 — The prayer

- A coach calls for the youngest age group to begin warm-ups.
- Jessica Isabella stands. She puts her Bible back in her bag and zips it carefully.
- Before she walks toward the floor, she turns to Mike. *"Daddy, can we pray? Just for a second?*"
- Mike nods. He puts his hand on her shoulder. She puts her hand on top of his.
- She prays — not a long prayer, not a formal one. Just a seven-year-old talking to God in the middle of a loud gym:
  > *"God, thank you that You didn't give me fear. Thank you that You gave me power and love and a sound mind. Help me do my best. And help me remember that You're with me even when I'm scared. Amen."*
- Mike says *"Amen"* with her.

### Beat 6 — She walks toward the floor

- She walks toward the warm-up area. She does not look back — but Mike watches her go.
- She has her grips in one hand. Her hair is in a ponytail. She is seven years old and she is walking into her first competition with a verse in her chest.
- Mike sits back down on the bench. He does not follow her to the parent viewing area yet. He sits for a moment, alone, in the quiet corner of the loud gym.
- He thinks: *That Bible was a good gift. That verse was a good choice. And that girl is going to be just fine.*

---

## THEMATIC RESONANCE

- **The building as threshold:** The gym is not just a venue — it is the place where fear meets faith. Jessica Isabella brings her Bible into the loud, bright, overwhelming space and draws strength from it there.
- **Her own theology:** She interprets the verse herself — fear is not from God, so it does not define her. A remarkable distinction for a seven-year-old.
- **She prays aloud:** This is the first time we see Jessica Isabella pray on her own, without prompting, in a public place.
- **Mike on the bench:** He lets her go. He watches. He trusts the verse she has hidden in her heart.
