# SCENE OUTLINE: PITANGUEIRAS BEACH INCIDENT (JESSICA AGE 15)

**Characters:** Jessica Isabella Key (15); Mike Key (father, present for business trip); two men (perpetrators — keep off-page/non-graphic); hospital staff; Emma (off-page awareness, in spirit).

**Setting:** Pitangueiras Beach, Guarujá (near São Paulo, Brazil). Beach bathroom. Then hospital.

**Time in saga:** Jessica is 15 (one year before the pregnancy at 16 and the secret relationship with Emma at 16). Mike has taken her on a business trip to São Paulo. This is the origin event that leads to the twins (Samira Chaka and Sahara Hannan).

**Tone:** Heavy, careful, restrained. The saga's standard for trauma: **never depict the harm itself**. Focus on Jessica's agency, Mike's protection, the hospital negotiation, the faith decision, and the quiet knowing that Emma carries even from a distance. No graphic details. The horror is in the fact and the aftermath, not the description.

**Content note:** This is the event that results in the pregnancy. It is handled exactly like the Emerald Ginger arc and Carlota's history — off-page, non-graphic, survivor-centered. The story's focus is belief, protection, family response, and "your body is yours."

---

## BEFORE THE SCENE — CONTEXT

- Mike has brought Jessica (15) with him on a business trip to São Paulo. He is in meetings. Jessica is exploring, being a normal 15-year-old on a rare trip.
- She goes to Pitangueiras Beach in Guarujá — a popular, busy beach. She swims. It is ordinary.
- Emma is back in Canada (age ~15, still just the gymnastics friend who waited at the door). Jessica has not told her yet, but Emma will know when Jessica returns — the way Emma always knows.
- This is before the kitchen accident scars have fully settled in family memory for everyone, but Jessica already carries her own history of bodily autonomy lessons from Mike and Terri Anne.

---

## THE INCIDENT (OFF-PAGE / REPORTED)

Jessica goes into the beach bathroom.

Two men follow her in.

**What happens is kept entirely off-page.** It is never narrated, never detailed, never shown. The reader learns only that something terrible occurred — enough to result in pregnancy, enough to send her to hospital, enough for Mike to be furious but present.

The focus is never the act. It is:

- Jessica's survival and agency afterward.
- Mike finding out and choosing to stay.
- The hospital.
- Her refusal of the morning-after pill on religious grounds.
- Mike backing her completely.

---

## MIKE FINDS OUT AND STAYS

Mike is in a meeting when it happens. He is contacted. He drops everything.

He is **furious** — protective rage — but he does not let the anger take him out of the room where Jessica needs him.

He stays with her in the hospital.

He helps her walk to the bathroom (she trusts him completely and without any doubt — this detail is canon and powerful).

He is the father who shows up in the worst moment.

---

## THE HOSPITAL — THE MORNING-AFTER PILL AND THE NEGOTIATION

The hospital offers Jessica the morning-after pill.

She refuses.

She is a **practicing Catholic**. Her faith matters to her. This is her decision, from the deepest part of who she is. (This mirrors the skin graft negotiation at age 11 where she insisted on ice cream and terms before allowing doctors to touch her body.)

**Mike backs her up completely.** Without hesitation. Without argument. He tells the hospital that his daughter has made her decision and it will be respected.

He stands beside her faith the way he stands beside everything else about her.

This is the spiritual counterpart to the earlier "your body is yours" lesson. Mike taught her no one touches her body without permission. Now, in a hospital in Brazil, the same principle applies to her faith, her beliefs, and her choice.

He has always backed her.

---

## EMMA'S AWARENESS (OFF-PAGE BUT PRESENT)

Emma does not witness the incident. She is in Canada.

But when Jessica returns, Emma knows something is wrong. She is the one who stayed at the door after the burns. She will be the one who stays through this.

Jessica tells Emma what happened. The harm itself remains off-page and non-graphic, and Jessica does not have to narrate every detail. But Emma is not left guessing; Jessica chooses to trust her with the truth.

When Jessica later takes the pregnancy test, Emma is in the bathroom with her. Emma is present for the result from the first moment.

At the first doctor appointment, Mike does not come. Terri Anne and Emma accompany Jessica. Emma holds Jessica's right hand; Terri Anne holds Jessica's left hand. The doctor confirms pregnancy and two babies, but they do not yet know that Samira Chaka and Sahara Hannan are identical twins.

The pregnancy will become visible. Emma will be there in the margins — hospital stairwells, notes, quiet presence — even before anyone knows she is more than a friend.

The names later (Samira and Sahara) will be Jessica's way of writing Emma into the story from the beginning.

## AFTER THE SCENE — WHERE IT LEADS (CANON)

- Pregnancy with Samira Chaka and Sahara Hannan (father unknown). They are later known to be identical twins, but Jessica, Emma, Terri Anne, and the doctors do not know that at the first appointment; they only know there are two babies.
- The secret relationship with Emma deepens at 16.
- The bathroom discovery four months after birth (Rachel Darcy sees them kissing; secret ends).
- Family response: full support. Mike fires Randy when he disowns Emma.
- The twins are loved without condition. Their origin is heavy. Their lives are not.
- This event is the reason the twins exist, but it does not define them. The saga focuses on the family that rallied, the mother who carried, the partner who stayed, and the children who were wanted.

**Intergenerational mirror:** This is one of the hard things the Key family has faced. Later events (Emerald Ginger's arc, Carlota's) will echo the question: does the family repeat the pattern or break the chain on purpose?
