# Eliot David Key-Fritz and Kalvin LaMarcus Key-Fritz — Character Profile

*Jessica Isabella & Emma's branch. Identical twin sons, born in 2055. The younger siblings of Samira Chaka and Sahara Hannan, and the second set of twins in the household.*

## Vital Statistics
- **Birth Year:** ~2055
- **Parents:** Jessica Isabella Key (biological mother) and Emma Fritz (co-mother)
- **Siblings:** Samira Chaka Key (older sister, twin to Sahara) and Sahara Hannan Key (older sister, twin to Samira)
- **Biological father / donor:** Dr. Rémi Arsenault (Acadian-French Canadian neurosurgeon, chosen after a five-month arrangement that ended beautifully and firmly). In the boys' later childhood/adolescent continuity, Rémi is referred to as their father and shows up for them as such.
- **Location:** Halifax, Nova Scotia (South End, Key-Fritz mansion)
- **School:** Early childhood / Junior Primary at **Armbrae Academy** in Halifax (located on Oxford Street, adjacent to Dalhousie University, highly convenient for their mothers' surgical schedules; Mrs. Patterson is their beloved Junior Primary/Kindergarten teacher). By the sixteen-year-old Vancouver/Tokyo continuity, the twins attend **Saint George School** in Vancouver.

## The Twin Dynamic
Eliot and Kalvin represent the "Twin Mirror." They grow up in a house where the most powerful bond they witness—before their mothers' marriage—is the bond between their older sisters, Samira and Sahara. Eliot and Kalvin absorb this twinship as an inheritance and a physical grammar.

- **Eliot David:** The older twin by three minutes. He is the verbal architect, the loud, logical, demanding one. He tracks adult behavior, counts lies, and refuses to let the family's "cheerful wall of normalcy" stand when something is wrong. He is the twin who demands the truth.
- **Kalvin LaMarcus:** The younger twin. Quieter, physically expressive, the twin who monitors rooms and reads emotional weather. He holds the physical toll of the family's stress (e.g., bedwetting when Samira disappears). His primary currency is comfort, touch, and proximity.

## Core Arcs and Canon Integration

### Age 0-4 (~2055 - ~2059)
- **The Setup:** They are born into a massive, loving, intergenerational family network. Their older sisters (Samira and Sahara) adore them. They swim on vacations, travel, and are raised with the Key family's absolute baseline rule: *"your body is yours."* 
- **The Donor's Presence:** They know Rémi. He is not a parent, but he is an "uncle" figure, safe and steady, part of the architecture of their lives without bearing the daily weight of raising them.

### Age 5 (~2060) — The Missing Sister
- **The Disappearance:** When Samira disappears into Facility 12 in Siberia, the boys are 5 years old and in Junior Primary at Armbrae Academy.
- **The Mirror:** They immediately feel the rupture in the older twins' bond. Eliot tracks his mothers' lies about Samira being "busy"; Kalvin begins hiding wet pajamas in the hamper and stops eating at school.
- **The Comfort Scene:** In a critical turning point for the family, Jessica and Emma decide *not* to lie to them. They tell the boys that bad people have taken Samira, giving them agency to grieve and help. Kalvin offers his transitional object (a stuffed rabbit named *Mira*), and Eliot draws a map to guide Samira home, showing the family's reliance on truth, no matter how young the child. *(See `18 years/eliot-kalvin-samira-disappearance-comfort-scene.md`)*.

### Future Hooks (To Be Developed)
- **The Armbrae Morning Routine:** In the wake of Samira's disappearance, when Jessica and Emma realize they cannot hold everything, Rémi steps into a daily rhythm: he arrives at the Key-Fritz mansion in the mornings, manages breakfast, and takes Eliot and Kalvin to school at Armbrae Academy. It solidifies his role as an active father figure—reliable, consistent, and protecting the boys' routine while their mothers manage the geopolitical crisis.
- **The Return:** How Samira's eventual rescue and return shapes their understanding of safety, heroism, and the twin bond.
- **Adolescence:** How they distinguish themselves from the overwhelming shadow of their famous, internationally-trained older ballet sisters.
