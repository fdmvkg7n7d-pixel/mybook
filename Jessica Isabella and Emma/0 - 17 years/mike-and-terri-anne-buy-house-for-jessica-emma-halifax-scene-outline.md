# Mike and Terri Anne Buy a House for Jessica Isabella and Emma — Halifax — Scene Outline

> **Scope:** Mike and Terri Anne buy a seven-room house in Halifax for Jessica Isabella, Emma, Samira Chaka, and Sahara Hannan. The family moves from their smaller place into the house together. Mike stays for four days to help settle them in. Terri Anne goes home after the move. The house becomes a home.
>
> **Time:** TBD — sometime after Samira and Sahara are born, before or during Rachel's University of Virginia years.
> **Place:** Halifax, Nova Scotia — the new house.
> **Characters present:** Mike Key, Terri Anne Williams-Key, Jessica Isabella Key (17), Emma Fritz (17), Samira Chaka (18 months), Sahara Hannan (18 months).
> **Mood:** Warm, deliberate, unhurried. A family that makes room for each other.

---

## 1. The Decision

- **Mike and Terri Anne talk about it** before they do anything else. They talk the way they always talk — quietly, in the evening, after the house has settled and the younger ones are asleep.
- **Mike:** *"They need more space."*
- **Terri Anne:** *"I know."*
- **Mike:** *"Two children in that apartment. No garden. No yard."*
- **Terri Anne:** *"Emma is nineteen. Jessica is nineteen. They're raising two children in an apartment."*
- **Mike:** *"We can help."*
- **Terri Anne:** *"We should help."*
- **They do not talk about it as a gift.** They talk about it as what it is — a family making room for its own. Jessica has given her eggs to Rachel. Emma has built a life with her. Samira and Sahara are their grandchildren. The house is not charity. It is what this family does.
- **Mike calls Jessica.** He does not preamble.
- **Mike:** *"We're buying you a house."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"What?"*
- **Mike:** *"A house. In Halifax. Seven rooms. We'll go look at it together."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"Mike, you don't have to —"*
- **Mike:** *"We're not doing anything we don't have to. We're doing what we want to."*
- **A long pause.**
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"Emma is going to cry."*
- **Mike:** *"Let her cry."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"I'm going to cry too."*
- **Mike:** *"Let that too."*
- **Terri Anne takes the phone.** She does not say much — she rarely does on the phone. She says: *"It's a good house. We looked at it this week. There's a garden."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"There's a garden."*
- **Terri Anne:** *"Yes. For Samira and Sahara."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"Okay."*
- **Terri Anne:** *"Okay."*
- **They hang up.** Jessica calls Emma. Emma cries.

---

## 2. The House

- **Mike and Terri Anne fly to Halifax.** They come from London, England — Mike had business there for two days before flying to Canada. He has been traveling for a week. He does not mention it. He has always been the one who carries the travel quietly, who arrives without making the journey into a story. They have already seen the house — Mike scouted it on a previous trip, walked through every room, measured the garden, counted the windows. He does not buy a house without walking through it first. He has always been this way.
- **The house is on a quiet street** near the water. It is a low, wide house — brick, older, well-maintained. Seven rooms: a kitchen, a living room, a dining room, and four bedrooms. A garden in the back. A small yard. Enough space.
- **Jessica and Emma meet them at the house.** They come with Samira and Sahara — the children in their stroller, the parents tired in the specific way that comes from raising two toddlers in a two-bedroom apartment.
- **Mike opens the door.** He stands aside and lets Jessica go in first.
- **Jessica walks through the house.** She does not say anything immediately. She walks through the kitchen, the living room, the dining room. She goes upstairs. She comes back down. Emma is standing in the doorway with the children, waiting.
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"There are four bedrooms."*
- **Mike:** *"Yes."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"We only need two."*
- **Terri Anne:** *"You might need more later."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"We might."*
- **They go to the garden.** It is small but real — a rectangle of grass, a fence, enough room for two children to run. Samira points at the grass. Sahara points at the fence.
- **Emma is quiet.** She is standing beside Jessica, holding Sahara's hand, looking at the garden.
- **Mike:** *"Emma?"*
- **Emma:** *"I'm okay."*
- **Mike:** *"You don't look okay."*
- **Emma:** *"I look overwhelmed. There's a difference."*
- **Mike:** *"What's the difference?"*
- **Emma:** *"Okay means I'm fine with it. Overwhelmed means I'm not fine with it but I'm not going to pretend I don't feel it."*
- **Mike:** *"That's a good answer."*
- **Emma:** *"I'm learning."*
- **Jessica puts her hand on Emma's back.** She does not say anything. She just does that — the gesture of someone who knows when to touch and when not to. Emma leans into it slightly. They stand together and look at the garden.
- **Mike:** *"We'll have the papers signed by the end of the month."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"The end of the month."*
- **Mike:** *"You'll have time to plan the move."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"Okay."*
- **They leave the house.** They go to dinner at a restaurant near the water. They sit together — Mike, Terri Anne, Jessica, Emma, Samira in a high chair, Sahara in a booster seat. The table is full in the way that tables with this family are always full.
- **Mike orders for everyone.** He always orders for everyone. He has always known what they eat.

## 3. The Move

- **The move happens on a Saturday.** Mike drives from Carlisle with a truck. Terri Anne comes on a plane. They arrive the day before and spend the night in a hotel, and in the morning they begin.
- **Jessica and Emma have already packed.** Most of what they own fits in the truck — they are young, they have not accumulated much. The apartment was small. The new house is bigger than they need, at least for now.
- **Mike carries boxes.** He carries all of them. He has always been the one who carries — to the car, to the truck, to the house. He does not ask for help. He does not need it.
- **Terri Anne organizes.** She knows where everything goes — she scouted the house, she knows the layout. She directs: the kitchen things here, the bedroom things there, the children's things in the room at the back.
- **Samira and Sahara are in the garden.** Jessica let them out while the adults worked. They are in the grass, doing what eighteen-month-olds do — picking at things, falling over, getting up, falling over again.
- **Emma works quietly.** She does not talk much — she never does. She moves boxes, arranges furniture, holds Sahara when she comes in from the garden with grass in her hands.
- **By afternoon,** the house is settled. Not fully — not completely — but settled enough. The beds are made. The kitchen has what it needs. The children's room has toys on the floor and a crib in the corner.
- **Mike stands in the living room.** He looks at the house — the high ceilings, the wide windows, the garden visible through the back door. He has done this before — stood in a house he bought for one of his daughters, looked at what he made room for.
- **Terri Anne comes to stand beside him.**
- **Terri Anne:** *"It's a good house."*
- **Mike:** *"It's a good house."*
- **Terri Anne:** *"She'll be okay here."*
- **Mike:** *"They'll all be okay here."*
- **They do not say anything else.** They just stand. The afternoon light is coming in through the windows. The garden is visible through the back door. Samira is in the grass. Sahara is standing at the fence, looking at something on the other side.
- **Terri Anne goes home.** She leaves on Sunday — two days after arriving, the move done, the house settled enough. She hugs Jessica at the door. She hugs Emma upstairs, where Emma is with the children. She tells Mike to stay as long as he needs.
- **Mike stays.**

---

## 4. Day One — Mike Stays

- **Terri Anne has been gone for a day.** Mike is still in the house with Jessica, Emma, Samira, and Sahara. He is sleeping in the guest room — the fourth bedroom, the one they do not need yet.
- **He wakes up early** and makes coffee. He stands at the kitchen window and looks at the garden. Samira is already awake, in the grass, doing what eighteen-month-olds do.
- **Jessica comes down in her pajamas.** She sees him at the window.
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"You're up early."*
- **Mike:** *"I always am."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"I know."*
- **They stand together.** They do not need to fill the space with words. They have always done this — the quiet standing, the early morning, the way a father and daughter can be together without talking.
- **Emma comes down with Sahara.** Emma makes her own coffee. She does not say much in the morning — she never does. She sits at the table and holds Sahara on her lap.
- **Mike:** *"I can take them to the park today. Give you two a break."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"You don't have to."*
- **Mike:** *"I want to."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"Okay."*
- **He takes Samira and Sahara to the park.** He pushes them in the stroller, walks them to a park near the house, lets them out on the grass. He sits on a bench and watches them. Other parents are there — young, some of them, none of them with a father who is sixty and has done this seven times before.
- **Samira falls.** He picks her up. She cries for a moment, then stops. She goes back to playing. He picks up a stone from the ground and puts it in his pocket — the way he has always done, the stone from every place his grandchildren have played.
- **Sahara points at a dog.** He shows her the dog. She says something that is not yet a word. He says something back. She smiles.
- **He stays at the park for two hours.** He brings them home. Jessica and Emma are in the kitchen, talking quietly, finishing something. They look rested. They look like people who have had a morning that was not entirely about the children.
- **Mike puts Samira down.** He puts Sahara in Emma's arms. He sits at the table.
- **Mike:** *"Good park."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"Thank you."*
- **Mike:** *"It was a good park."*
- **They have lunch.** Mike makes sandwiches — he is good at sandwiches, the way he has always been good at things that are simple and useful. They eat. The children eat. The afternoon is quiet.

---

## 5. Day Two — Mike Stays

- **The second day is quieter.** Mike goes to the grocery store. He stocks the fridge — not because they cannot do it, but because he wants to. He buys what they need and some things they do not need but will like: good bread, good cheese, fruit the children will eat.
- **Jessica and Emma go out** — just the two of them, for an hour, to a café near the water. They have not been alone together in months. The children are with Mike.
- **Mike sits on the floor with Samira and Sahara.** He builds something with blocks that Sahara keeps knocking down. He lets her knock it down. Samira watches and laughs. He builds it again. She knocks it down again. He does this for forty-five minutes without getting tired of it.
- **He thinks about the house.** Not the house as a purchase — the house as a home. The way Jessica looks in the kitchen. The way Emma sits at the table. The way Samira points at the grass and Sahara points at the fence. He bought this for them. He stayed for them. He is here.
- **Jessica and Emma come back.** They come in quietly, not wanting to wake anyone, and find Mike on the floor with the children, building blocks.
- **Emma:** *"You're good at that."*
- **Mike:** *"I've had practice."*
- **They have dinner.** Mike cooks — pasta, bread, a salad. He cooks the way he always cooks when he visits: simply, with attention, the meal of someone who knows what a family needs at the end of a day.

---

## 6. Day Three — Mike Stays

- **The third day is ordinary.** Mike wakes up early. He makes coffee. He stands at the window. The garden is wet from rain the night before. Samira is awake early, watching from the back door.
- **Mike:** *"You want to go out?"*
- **Samira makes a sound.** She does not say yes yet — she is eighteen months, words are still mostly sounds — but her body says yes. She is already pulling toward the door.
- **He puts her boots on.** They go outside. The grass is wet. He does not care. She walks in it, delighted, the way children are when they discover that the world is wet and cold and still okay.
- **Sahara wakes up.** Jessica brings her out. The four of them — Mike, Jessica, Emma, Sahara — stand in the garden in the early morning. Nobody says much. Nobody needs to.
- **Mike fixes a loose board** on the fence. He finds a hammer in the garage and a nail and he fixes it. He does not ask permission. He does not need to. He is the father. The house is his. The fence is his to fix.
- **Jessica watches him.** She does not say anything. She just watches. This is what he does — the fixing, the making things right, the quiet work of a man who has spent his life building things for his children.
- **That afternoon, Mike calls Terri Anne.** He tells her about the park, the grocery store, the fence. She tells him about the five who are still there in Rio de Janeiro — what they are doing, how Kiley Rose asked about him.
- **Mike:** *"Tell her I'm almost done."*
- **Terri Anne:** *"She'll want to hear it from you."*
- **Mike:** *"I know."*
- **He hangs up.** He sits on the couch with the children for a while — Samira on one side, Sahara on the other. They are watching something on the television, something simple, the kind of thing that holds their attention for twenty minutes before they are done with it.

---

## 7. Day Four — Mike Goes Home

- **The fourth morning.** Mike wakes up and knows it is the last morning. He has been here four days. That is enough. Jessica and Emma are settled. The house is home. He can go.
- **He makes breakfast.** Eggs, toast, the kind of breakfast he makes when he is leaving — deliberate, unhurried, the meal of someone who is taking his time because the time is running out.
- **Jessica comes down.** She sees the breakfast and knows.
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"You're going today."*
- **Mike:** *"I should get back."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"I know."*
- **They sit together.** They eat. Emma comes down with the children. They eat together — all of them, the five of them, the last breakfast before Mike goes home.
- **Samira is in her high chair.** She is eating eggs, dropping most of them on the floor. Mike picks up a piece of egg from the floor and puts it back on her tray. She laughs. He laughs too.
- **Sahara is in her booster seat.** She is not eating — she is playing with a spoon, banging it on the table. Emma takes the spoon away gently and gives her a piece of toast instead. She takes a bite. She looks at Mike. She smiles.
- **Mike:** *"I'm going to miss you both."*
- **Emma:** *"You can come back."*
- **Mike:** *"I know."*
- **After breakfast, Mike loads his bag.** He does not have much — a bag, a book he did not finish, a stone from the park in his pocket.
- **Jessica walks him to the door.**
- **Mike puts his hands on her shoulders.** He does not say anything immediately. He just looks at her — his daughter, nineteen years old, in a house he bought for her, with two children and a partner and a life that is fully hers.
- **Mike:** *"I love you."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"I love you too."*
- **Mike:** *"Call me if you need anything."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"I will."*
- **He holds her.** Long, careful, the hug of a father to a daughter who has a house now, a garden, a life. He does not say anything else. He does not need to.
- **Emma is at the top of the stairs** with Samira and Sahara. She does not come down — the children are awake, she needs to stay. She waves. Mike waves back.
- **He picks up his bag.** He opens the door. He looks at the house one more time — the kitchen, the living room, the garden through the back door.
- **Mike:** *"Lock it behind me."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"I will."*
- **He goes.** The door closes. Jessica locks it. She stands in the hallway and listens to the house — the quiet of a house that is new, the particular silence of a room that is just beginning to be lived in. She goes back to the kitchen. Emma is there. The children are there. The morning is theirs.
- **Mike drives to the airport.** He is tired — not from the four days, from the watching. He is always tired after he leaves. But he is not sorry. He bought a house. He stayed four days. He fixed the fence. He is going home.
- **He calls Terri Anne from the airport.** He tells her he is on his way. She tells him the five are fine. She tells him Kiley Rose is asking about him.
- **Mike:** *"Tell her I'm almost there."*
- **Terri Anne:** *"She's going to want to hear it from you."*
- **Mike:** *"I know."*
- **He boards the plane — Halifax to London, England.** He takes the window seat — he has always taken the window seat, even when he is not the one going somewhere new. He looks out the window as the plane takes off. Below him, the city is getting smaller. The water is visible. The garden is not visible, but he knows where it is.
- **He sleeps on the flight to London.** He wakes up over the Atlantic. The clouds are thick below him. He drinks water and reads nothing and waits.
- **In London,** he has four hours between flights. He walks through the terminal. He does not buy anything. He does not call anyone. He finds a seat near his gate and sits. He is in transit — between Halifax and London, between the house he bought and the next leg of the journey home.
- **He boards the second flight — London to Rio de Janeiro.** He takes the window seat again. He looks out the window as London falls away below him. He has been coming and going from London for years. He knows the shape of it from the air — the city, the river, the particular grey of the English sky.
- **The flight to Rio is long.** He sleeps in stretches. He thinks about the four days — the park, the blocks, the fence, the stone in his pocket from the playground. He thinks about the five who are waiting in Rio de Janeiro. He thinks about the house in Halifax, the garden, Jessica standing in the doorway with Samira in her arms.
- **He closes his eyes.** He flies to Rio.

---

## 8. Closing Image — Rio de Janeiro

- **Mike arrives in Rio de Janeiro late.** The city is warm and familiar — he has been coming here for years, long before the disclosure, long before everything became what it is now. The air is thick and close and smells like the coast. He has always known this smell.
- **He collects his bag.** He walks through the terminal. He does not rush. He is going home — the family lives here, the house is here, Terri Anne is here. Everything he comes back to is here.
- **He takes a car to the house.** The drive is familiar — he has driven this route a hundred times. The city moves past the windows: the buildings, the lights, the particular density of a place that is always alive. He knows every turn.
- **He arrives at the house.** The porch light is on, and the kitchen light, and the light in the hall. Terri Anne left them on — she always leaves them on. She has been home for two days already, since she left Halifax. She knows he is coming tonight.
- **He parks.** He sits for a moment. He is not tired anymore — he is just home.
- **He gets out.** He opens the front door. The house is warm. The smell of something — whatever Terri Anne made before she went to bed — is still in the air.
- **Terri Anne is in the kitchen.** She did not go to sleep. She was waiting. She stands when he comes in.
- **Mike:** *"I'm home."*
- **Terri Anne:** *"I know."*
- **They stand together.** They do not need to fill the space with words. They have done this before — the coming home after traveling, the quiet recognition that someone is back. He puts his hand on her face, the way he does, and holds it there.
- **He tells her about Halifax.** Not everything — not the blocks, not the fence, not every detail. Just what she needs to know: the house is good, they are settled, the children are well, Jessica and Emma are in a good place.
- **Terri Anne:** *"And London?"*
- **Mike:** *"London was fine."*
- **Terri Anne:** *"You look tired."*
- **Mike:** *"I am tired."*
- **They go to bed.** The house is quiet. The night is warm. The bed is theirs.

## 9. The Phone Call — After

- **A week later.** Rachel calls from University of Virginia. She hears about the house. She asks what it looks like. Jessica describes it — the kitchen, the garden, the room she and Emma share that they do not need yet.
- **Rachel:** *"Mike bought you a house."*
- **Jessica:** *"Mike and Terri Anne."*
- **Rachel:** *"They bought you a house."*
- **Jessica:** *"They bought us a house. There's a difference."*
- **Rachel:** *"What's the difference?"*
- **Jessica:** *"They bought it for all of us. Not just for me."*
- **Rachel is quiet for a moment.**
- **Rachel:** *"I should come visit."*
- **Jessica:** *"Yes. You should."*
- **Rachel:** *"I'll come at Christmas."*
- **Jessica:** *"We'll be here."*
- **They hang up.** Jessica goes to the garden. Samira is in the grass. Sahara is at the fence, looking at something on the other side.
- **She stands in the doorway.** She watches them. She thinks about the apartment — the small rooms, the narrow hall, the way the sound bounced off the walls. She thinks about the house — the high ceilings, the wide windows, the garden.
- **She thinks about what it means** that her parents bought this. Not charity — family. The thing this family does, which is make room. Always make room.
- **Samira makes a sound.** Jessica goes into the garden. She picks up her daughter. She holds her. The grass is warm under her feet. The afternoon is quiet.
- **The house is hers.**

## Character Work

### Mike Key
- **The one who provides:** He does not ask whether Jessica needs help. He knows she does. He buys the house the way he has always done things — directly, without preamble, with the quiet certainty that this is what a father does.
- **The one who stays four days:** He leaves Terri Anne after the move and stays alone. He takes the children to the park, fixes the fence, stocks the fridge, makes breakfast. He is not doing too much — he is doing what a father does when his daughter needs time to settle.
- **The one who collects stones:** He picks up a stone from the park and puts it in his pocket. The way he has always done — the stone from every place his grandchildren have played.
- **The one who goes home:** He knows when to leave. Four days is enough. He drives to the airport, takes the window seat, flies home.

### Terri Anne Williams-Key
- **The one who organizes:** She directs the move — the kitchen things here, the bedroom things there. She makes the house a home before anyone else moves in.
- **The one who goes first:** She leaves after two days. She hugs Jessica at the door, hugs Emma upstairs, tells Mike to stay as long as he needs. She goes home to the five who are still there.
- **The one who says it plainly:** *"It's a good house. You should have a good house."* And: *"We know we didn't have to. That's not why we did."*

### Jessica Isabella Key (19)
- **The one who receives:** She does not argue when Mike tells her. She says *okay* and means it. She cries, but she does not argue. She knows what this family does.
- **The one who explains:** When Rachel asks, she says: *"They bought us a house. For all of us."* She makes sure her sister knows that Emma is included.
- **The one who stands in the doorway:** She stands in the garden doorway and watches her children. She thinks about what it means. She does not say it out loud. She does not need to.

### Emma Fritz (19)
- **The one who is overwhelmed:** She is not from a large family. She is learning the shape of this family from the inside — the way they make room, the way they provide, the way a father buys a house without asking whether you need it.
- **The one who learns:** She tells Mike the difference between *okay* and *overwhelmed*. She is learning to name what she feels. She is learning that this family can hold it.
- **The one who stays:** She is at home in Halifax with Jessica and the children. She is learning to be part of this family from the inside, and she is not lost in it — she is found by it.

### Samira Chaka and Sahara Hannan (18 months)
- **The ones who play in the grass:** They do not know that the house was bought for them. They just know that there is a garden, and the grass is soft, and someone is always there.
- **The ones who know Mike:** In four days, they learn that he will pick them up when they fall, that he will build blocks and let them knock them down, that he will put stones in his pocket from the places they play.

## Thematic Motifs

- **Seven rooms:** Not five, not eight — seven. Enough for now and for later. Enough to grow into. The number is deliberate, the way all numbers in this family are deliberate.
- **The garden:** Visible through the back door, large enough for two children to run, warm in the afternoon. The garden is what Jessica tells Rachel about first.
- **The stone in his pocket:** Mike picks up a stone from the park. He has always done this — the stone from every place his grandchildren have played. The collection grows. The family grows. Both things are true.
- **Four days:** Not too long. Not too short. Enough for the house to become a home, for the children to learn his face, for Jessica and Emma to have a morning that was not entirely about the children.
- **The fence:** He fixes the loose board without asking permission. He does not need to. He is the father. The house is his. The fence is his to fix.
- **The locked door:** *"Lock it behind me."* Jessica does. The house is theirs. The door is locked. Mike is gone and the house is still home.
- **Make room:** The thing this family does — always make room. For the children who are here and the children who are coming and the children who are already grown and far away.
