# Sahara Hannan at Vaganova — Bedtime in the Top Bunk Dormitory Scene Outline

## Purpose of This File

This is a **planning / scene-outline file** for **Sahara Hannan Key** at the **Vaganova Ballet Academy** on the same narrative day as the Halifax swimming-pool conversation where **Samira Chaka** tells **Jessica Isabella** and **Emma** that she misses Sahara.

This scene shows Sahara’s side of the first-week separation. While the Halifax scene happens later around the mansion pool, this scene is set in St. Petersburg as Sahara and her three dormitory friends are **getting ready for bedtime** in their shared Vaganova dorm room.

---

## Timeline Placement

- **Time:** **August ~2051**, one week after Sahara begins living at Vaganova.
- **Ages:** Samira Chaka and Sahara Hannan are about **eleven**. Jessica Isabella and Emma are both **twenty-seven**.
- **Time of day:** Evening in St. Petersburg, during the dormitory bedtime routine.
- **Parallel continuity:** This is the same narrative day as Samira’s pool conversation in Halifax, but not necessarily the exact same clock moment. By the time Samira is at the Halifax pool around eight o’clock, Sahara’s bedtime note may already be tucked near her pillow.
- **Location:** **Vaganova Ballet Academy dormitory**, St. Petersburg, Russia.
- **Immediate continuity:**
  - Jessica Isabella, Emma, and Samira personally took Sahara to Russia.
  - Sahara has now been living at Vaganova for one week.
  - Jessica, Emma, and Samira have returned home to Halifax.
  - Sahara shares a dormitory room with **three other friends**.
  - The room has **two bunk beds**.
  - Sahara sleeps on **one of the top bunk beds**.

## Characters Present

### Sahara Hannan Key

- Eleven years old.
- First year at Vaganova.
- Identical twin of Samira Chaka.
- Has Type 1 diabetes, managed with a diabetes patch and a carefully prepared safety plan.
- Lyrical, precise, disciplined, and more outwardly controlled than she feels.
- Sleeps on the **top bunk**, near the window or wall.

### Three Dormitory Friends / Roommates

These names can be changed later, but the outline uses them as working names:

1. **Anya Morozova** — bottom bunk under Sahara.
   - From St. Petersburg or nearby.
   - Practical, rule-aware, quietly kind.
   - The first one Sahara trusts with small dormitory details.

2. **Lidia Volkova** — top bunk across from Sahara.
   - Serious, blunt, observant.
   - Notices when someone is trying too hard to be fine.
   - Does not over-comfort; she tells the truth plainly.

3. **Katya Sokolova** — bottom bunk under Lidia.
   - Warmer, funnier, more socially open.
   - Tries to make the room feel less frightening by joking softly.
   - The first one who makes Sahara almost laugh in the dormitory.

## Dormitory Room Layout

- Two bunk beds, four girls total.
- **Sahara:** top bunk on one side.
- **Anya:** bottom bunk directly below Sahara.
- **Lidia:** top bunk opposite Sahara.
- **Katya:** bottom bunk below Lidia.
- Each girl has a small shelf, trunk, or wardrobe space.
- The room is narrow and ordered:
  - folded practice clothes,
  - ballet slippers / soft shoes,
  - hairpins and ribbons,
  - towels,
  - schoolbooks / Russian language notes,
  - a shared desk or small table,
  - one window with St. Petersburg evening beyond it.
- Sahara’s diabetes supplies are arranged with Emma’s precision:
  - Sahara’s own iPhone, with the patch app open, within reach of the top bunk,
  - glucose tablets or fast sugar in a small pouch attached near the bunk rail,
  - water bottle,
  - spare patch / adhesive supplies placed where she can find them,
  - emergency card in Russian and English,
  - backup supplies stored where the dormitory adult knows to find them.

## Core Emotional Question

How does Sahara admit, during an ordinary bedtime routine, that she misses Samira while still choosing to stay at Vaganova?

---

## Emotional Core

Sahara is exactly where she chose to be. The studios are real. The teachers are real. The corrections are real. The dream is real.

But bedtime is harder than class.

During the day, Vaganova gives her structure: barre, centre, meals, language, rules, schedules, corrections. At night, the room becomes small and intimate, full of girls who are kind but unfamiliar. Everyone is changing, brushing teeth, folding clothes, climbing bunks, settling into the sounds of sleep.

At home, bedtime always included Samira somewhere nearby. Even when they were not talking, Sahara knew where Samira was. She knew the sound of her breathing, the shape of her movement, the exact kind of silence that meant Samira was still awake.

In the Vaganova dormitory, Sahara has a top bunk, three new friends, and no Samira.

## Scene Setup

### 1. The Dormitory Bedtime Routine

- The girls return to the dormitory after the evening routine: supper, washroom, final instructions, or quiet study period.
- The room is busy in a soft, tired way:
  - hairpins being removed,
  - tights folded,
  - leotards hung or placed for laundry,
  - slippers lined up,
  - toothbrushes and washcloths,
  - whispered reminders about morning class,
  - someone searching for a missing ribbon.
- The day has been physically hard, and everyone is tired.
- Sahara participates correctly. She knows the rules. She is careful, neat, and quiet.
- Her neatness is partly discipline and partly homesickness disguised as competence.

### 2. Sahara’s Bedtime Diabetes Patch Check

- Before climbing to the top bunk, Sahara checks her diabetes patch using the patch app on her own iPhone.
- The reading is stable or manageable — no crisis.
- She places glucose tablets / fast sugar where she can reach them from the top bunk.
- She checks her water bottle.
- She confirms the emergency card is still in its place.
- Emma’s planning is present in the room even though Emma is in Halifax.

Possible internal beat:

> Emma thought of everything except how to make the bed not feel like the wrong bed.

### 3. The Top Bunk

- Sahara climbs to the top bunk.
- The height feels strange:
  - she is above the room,
  - close to the ceiling,
  - looking down at Anya’s bed below,
  - looking across at Lidia’s top bunk,
  - hearing Katya moving on the lower bunk opposite.
- The top bunk gives her privacy, but it also makes her feel suspended.
- At home, she and Samira knew each other’s positions without thinking. Here, Sahara has to learn a new map of bodies and sounds.

## Beat Sheet

### Beat 1 — The Room Gets Ready for Sleep

- Anya folds her practice clothes precisely and checks the room rules.
- Lidia complains softly about a correction from class while retying or sorting her hair ribbon.
- Katya tries to make someone laugh by imitating a teacher’s stern expression, but quietly enough not to get in trouble.
- Sahara smiles politely but does not fully enter the joking.
- She is present, but part of her attention is still in Halifax.

### Beat 2 — The Empty Habit

- Sahara reaches for something or turns to say something she would normally say to Samira.
- She stops before speaking.
- It may be tiny:
  - a comment about the water being too cold,
  - a joke about a correction,
  - a question about whether her braid is straight,
  - a complaint that only Samira would understand.
- The other girls notice the stop.
- Sahara covers it by checking her supplies or adjusting her blanket.

### Beat 3 — Anya Notices the Practical Details

- Anya watches Sahara attach or place the glucose pouch near the top bunk.
- She asks carefully, not rudely:

> “You keep that there every night?”

- Sahara explains briefly:

> “If I go low, I need it close.”

- Anya nods and treats it as useful information, not drama.
- She may say:

> “Then if you ask for it, I hand it up?”

- Sahara is touched by the practicality.
- This is the first small way Anya becomes safe.

### Beat 4 — Lidia Names the Silence

- Lidia, from the opposite top bunk, looks over at Sahara.
- She says bluntly:

> “You are quieter tonight.”

- Sahara says:

> “I am tired.”

- Lidia answers:

> “We are all tired. That is not the same thing.”

- The room pauses.
- Lidia’s bluntness is not cruel; it makes pretending harder.

### Beat 5 — Katya Asks About Samira

- Katya climbs into her lower bunk or sits cross-legged with her blanket.
- She asks softly:

> “Is it your sister?”

- Sahara freezes a little.
- The room already knows fragments:
  - Sahara has an identical twin,
  - Samira is still in Halifax,
  - they danced and swam and grew up together,
  - Samira did not stay at Vaganova.
- Sahara nods.

### Beat 6 — Sahara Says the First Truth

Sahara says the simple truth without making it dramatic.

Possible dialogue:

> “I miss Samira.”

Then quickly:

> “I want to be here. I do. I am not asking to go home.”

Lidia says:

> “Those are different things.”

Anya adds:

> “You can want here and miss home.”

Katya says:

> “That sounds like a lot for bedtime.”

Sahara, honest:

> “It is.”

### Beat 7 — Sahara Explains the Twin Absence

- Sahara tries to explain what is hard, but the words are clumsy because she and Samira never needed many words for this.

Possible Sahara lines:

> “At home, I know where she is without looking.”

> “When I turn over, I know if she is awake.”

> “When we swim, I know where the splash came from.”

> “Here, everyone is kind, but nobody is her.”

- She may add quickly:

> “That is not your fault.”

- This protects the friends from feeling accused.
- It also makes clear that friendship is not replacement.

### Beat 8 — The Friends Respond Without Replacing Samira

Each friend responds differently:

- **Anya** offers practical kindness:

> “Tomorrow I can show you the quiet stair. If you need to write her.”

- **Lidia** offers blunt truth:

> “Missing someone does not mean you chose wrong.”

- **Katya** offers humor:

> “If your twin is like you, she is probably also being serious somewhere.”

- Sahara almost laughs.
- The almost-laugh matters because the room becomes survivable.

### Beat 9 — Sahara Thinks of Halifax Evening

- Sahara works out the time difference or already knows it.
- If it is bedtime in St. Petersburg, it is earlier in Halifax.
- She imagines the Halifax mansion:
  - the pool still open later in the day,
  - August light lasting into evening,
  - Jessica and Emma nearby,
  - Samira by the water.
- She says quietly:

> “She might go to the pool tonight.”

- The specificity makes the missing real.
- Katya asks what pool.
- Sahara describes it only briefly: home, water, summer, Samira.

### Beat 10 — The Top Bunk Becomes Her New Place

- Sahara climbs fully into the top bunk or settles against the wall.
- She arranges her blanket, water, glucose, and note paper.
- Across the room, Lidia is also in a top bunk; they can see each other in the dim light.
- Sahara realizes the top bunk is now part of her life.
- It is not home yet, but it is hers for tonight.

Possible internal beat:

> The top bunk was not beside Samira. But it was a place Sahara could learn from. A place she could return to after class. A place high enough to feel private and low enough to hear the room breathing.

### Beat 11 — Sahara Worries About Being Disloyal

- Sahara admits she feels guilty for making friends.
- Not because Samira would forbid it, but because having new people close feels like proof that life continues without her twin.

Possible Sahara line:

> “If I like it here, it feels like I am leaving her twice.”

Anya:

> “No. You already left once. Liking it just means you are not suffering for nothing.”

Lidia:

> “Also, if you hate it, she will worry more.”

Katya:

> “So you must like at least some of it. For medical reasons.”

- Sahara laughs quietly.
- The laugh is small, but real.

### Beat 12 — Sahara Writes a Private Note Before Lights-Out

- Before the final lights-out, Sahara writes a private note to Samira.
- She writes from the top bunk or while sitting on the edge before fully lying down.
- The note is not polished. It is a twin note.

Possible note:

> Samira,
>
> I am on the top bunk. It is too high and not high enough to see Halifax. The girls in my room are kind. They are not you. I miss you. I want to stay. Both things are true.
>
> If you go to the pool tonight, splash once for me.

- This note can later become part of the early weekly-letter pattern.
- It is not a formal letter to Jessica and Emma.
- It is the first private bridge from Sahara’s new bed to Samira’s old world.

### Beat 13 — Final Bedtime Settling

- The girls finish settling into the bunks.
- Anya reminds Sahara where the water is, because she is practical.
- Lidia says top-bunk crying is dangerous because it falls on people.
- Katya says if anyone cries, they have to do it quietly because morning class will not care.
- Sahara smiles in the dark.
- The room becomes quiet.

### Beat 14 — Closing Image

- Sahara lies on the top bunk with the note near her pillow / under the sheet / tucked into a notebook.
- Her own iPhone screen glows briefly with the patch app, then darkens.
- The glucose pouch is within reach.
- Anya breathes below her.
- Lidia shifts across from her on the other top bunk.
- Katya settles under Lidia.
- The room is still Vaganova, still far from Halifax.
- But now Sahara has three friends in the room.
- She whispers, too quietly for anyone but herself:

> “Goodnight, Samira.”

- Later, across the ocean, Samira will be by the Halifax pool telling Jessica and Emma that she misses Sahara.

## Thematic Work

- **Bedtime as the rupture:** Class keeps Sahara busy; bedtime makes the missing audible.
- **Parallel twin absence:** Samira misses Sahara by the Halifax pool; Sahara misses Samira from the Vaganova top bunk.
- **The top bunk:** Physical height becomes emotional distance — Sahara is above the room, learning a new map of bodies and sounds.
- **New friends are not replacements:** The three roommates help Sahara survive the room without becoming substitutes for Samira.
- **Diabetes autonomy:** Emma’s planning is present through the patch supplies, fast sugar, routines, and emergency cards, but Sahara is the one managing her body at bedtime.
- **First-week reality:** The dream is real, but so is homesickness.
- **Two truths:** Sahara wants Vaganova and misses Samira. Both are true.

## Final Image

The Vaganova dormitory settles into bedtime quiet. Two bunk beds. Four girls. Three new friends breathing in the dark.

Sahara lies on the top bunk with her note tucked close:

> If you go to the pool tonight, splash once for me.

The little light from her own iPhone fades. The room is not Halifax. The bed is not beside Samira’s. The ceiling is close, and the ocean is far.

But below and across from her, Anya, Katya, and Lidia are there.

Sahara closes her eyes.

> “Goodnight, Samira.”
