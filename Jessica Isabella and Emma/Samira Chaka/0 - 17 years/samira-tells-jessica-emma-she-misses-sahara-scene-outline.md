# Samira Chaka Tells Jessica Isabella and Emma She Misses Sahara — Scene Outline

## Purpose of This File

This is a **planning / scene-outline file** for the first clear moment when **Samira Chaka Key** tells **Jessica Isabella** and **Emma** that she misses her twin sister, **Sahara Hannan**, after the family has taken Sahara over to Russia and Sahara has begun living at the **Vaganova Ballet Academy** in St. Petersburg.

This is not a months-later homesickness scene. It is the first-week rupture. Jessica, Emma, and Samira have already brought Sahara to Russia, completed the Vaganova handoff, and returned to Halifax. Sahara has only been living at Vaganova since last week, but the separation is already real inside the house.

---

## Timeline Placement

- **Time:** **August ~2051**, at the end of summer, **around eight o’clock in the evening**.
- **Ages:** Samira Chaka and Sahara Hannan are about **eleven**. Jessica Isabella and Emma are both **twenty-seven**.
- **Location:** The **Key-Fritz mansion in Halifax**, around the mansion’s private swimming pool.
- **Immediate continuity:**
  - Jessica Isabella, Emma, and Samira personally **take Sahara Hannan over to Russia**.
  - Sahara begins living at **Vaganova Academy** in St. Petersburg.
  - One week later, Jessica, Emma, and Samira are back home in **Halifax**.
  - The scene takes place around the mansion’s **swimming pool**.
  - Samira Chaka is **undressed** near the pool after swimming / being by the water. The scene should keep its focus on Samira’s emotional exposure, not on physical description.

---

## Characters Present

- **Samira Chaka Key** — eleven; Sahara’s identical twin; bolder, more athletic and dramatic; physically comfortable around the pool but emotionally exposed by Sahara’s absence.
- **Jessica Isabella Key** — biological mother; former conjoined twin to Rachel Darcy; understands twin absence as something physical, not merely emotional.
- **Emma Fritz** — co-mother; steady, practical, deeply attentive to both daughters; still carrying the aftershock of leaving Sahara at Vaganova with her diabetes plan, supplies, and dorm routine.
- **Sahara Hannan Key** — absent from the pool but present in every empty space. She is at Vaganova, where she has been living for one week.

---

## Core Emotional Question

How does Samira tell her mothers she misses Sahara when they are back home in Halifax, in the mansion that still feels built for two sisters moving through it together?

---

## Emotional Core

Samira thought the hard part would be the flight, the suitcases, the formal arrival at Vaganova, or the goodbye at Sahara’s dorm. But the hardest moment comes later, back home in Halifax, at the mansion swimming pool.

They brought Sahara safely to Russia. Sahara is where she chose to be. Vaganova has accepted her. Emma has checked the diabetes plan. Jessica has spoken with the adults. Samira has hugged Sahara goodbye and pretended to be proud in the uncomplicated way everyone needed her to be.

Then she is by the pool with Jessica and Emma, undressed and unarmored, and the water is wrong because Sahara is not in it.

The truth comes out:

> “I know she’s only been there since last week. I know we took her there. I know she wanted this. But I miss her.”

Jessica and Emma do not minimize it. They do not say, “It has only been a week.” They understand that Samira is not measuring distance by the calendar. She is measuring it in the missing body beside hers.

---

## Setting Details — The Halifax Mansion Pool

- The mansion is home, not a temporary residence. That makes the absence sharper.
- The pool is private and familiar — part of the girls’ ordinary summer life in Halifax.
- Around **eight o’clock**, the end-of-summer light is low but not gone, coming through the pool-room windows or across the terrace.
- The pool area carries traces of the family’s attempt to keep routine intact:
  - towels folded on chairs,
  - water glasses or tea nearby,
  - Emma’s bag with glucose supplies / emergency snacks and spare patch supplies still close out of habit, even though Sahara is not home,
  - Jessica’s own iPhone nearby in case Vaganova calls,
  - perhaps travel documents, a Russia folder, or Vaganova notes left on a nearby table.
- Samira is undressed in the private pool area, comfortable in the family’s normal body-autonomy culture. The dramatic meaning is that she has no uniform, costume, or twin-performance role to hide behind.

## Scene Setup

### 1. One Week After Leaving Sahara at Vaganova

- The family has completed the Russia trip and returned to Halifax.
- Sahara has now spent one full week living at Vaganova.
- There may have been a short first iPhone message or FaceTime call:

> “I am here. The room is small. The studios are beautiful. My numbers were okay today. Tell Samira the hallways are too quiet.”

- Sahara is no longer coming back to the mansion at night.
- Samira keeps expecting that she will.

Possible background beats:

- Emma has memorized the Vaganova contact numbers.
- Jessica has kept her own iPhone close, even during ordinary family time.
- Samira has pretended to be interested in normal Halifax routines.
- Everyone is trying to make the transition feel successful.

### 2. The Pool as Trigger

- The twins have always shared water easily: pools, beaches, swimming on vacations, and the Key family’s “your body is yours” philosophy.
- At the mansion pool, Samira gets into the water or sits near it.
- She instinctively leaves room for Sahara:
  - one side of the pool feels like Sahara’s side,
  - a splash echoes wrong,
  - Samira turns to say something and no one is there,
  - the empty second towel stays dry.
- The pool becomes the first place where the separation feels undeniable back home.

## Beat Sheet

### Beat 1 — Jessica and Emma Around the Pool

- It is around **eight o’clock in the evening**.
- Jessica and Emma are sitting near the pool, trying to rest after the stress of the Russia trip and return.
- Emma may be reviewing practical notes even while pretending not to:
  - Vaganova schedule,
  - diabetes contacts,
  - emergency plan,
  - supply list,
  - time-zone calculations.
- Jessica may have a book open but not be reading it.
- Their bodies are home in Halifax; their minds are still partly at Vaganova.
- Samira is by the pool, undressed and quiet.

### Beat 2 — Samira Is Too Still

- Normally Samira is physical around water — movement, splashing, practicing turns, inventing games.
- Here, she is too still.
- She may sit at the pool edge with her feet in the water.
- Or she may swim one length and stop halfway.
- Or she may stand near the second towel and stare at it.
- Jessica notices first because Samira’s movement pattern is incomplete.
- Emma notices because Samira has not made a joke in too long.

### Beat 3 — The Empty Second Towel

Use one concrete object to focus the ache.

Recommended object: **Sahara’s unused towel**.

- Two towels were laid out automatically, because Jessica or Emma is used to preparing for both twins.
- Samira sees Sahara’s towel, dry and folded.
- She says, not quite accusingly:

> “Why did we put out two?”

- Emma looks at the towel and realizes she did it without thinking.
- Jessica’s face changes because she understands the body-memory of preparing for two.

### Beat 4 — Samira Begins With Logistics

Samira does not start with feelings. She starts with practical questions about Sahara.

Possible lines:

> “What time is it there?”

> “Is she asleep now?”

> “Is she allowed to swim there?”

> “Do they know what to do if her patch alert goes off?”

> “Does anyone there know how she looks right before she goes low?”

> “If she misses us, does she have to ask permission to call?”

- Emma answers carefully.
- Jessica does not interrupt.
- Both mothers understand that Samira is asking: *Who is watching my sister now that I am not beside her?*

### Beat 5 — The Line That Breaks Her

Samira says something small that opens the scene.

Possible line:

> “She should be in the pool.”

Or:

> “This water is too quiet.”

Or:

> “I keep thinking she is going to come through that door.”

- She may look toward the entrance from the mansion as if Sahara could still appear with wet hair and a towel.
- She knows Sahara is at Vaganova.
- Knowing does not stop the expectation.

### Beat 6 — Samira Says She Misses Sahara

The confession should be plain and immediate.

Possible central dialogue:

> “I miss her.”

Then, quickly:

> “I know she’s only been there since last week. I know we took her there. I know she wanted this. I know she’s not gone forever.”

She struggles.

> “But I miss her like she is farther away than Russia.”

- Jessica and Emma do not correct the timeline.
- They do not say it is “only” one week.
- They do not use reason as comfort too quickly.
- Samira is telling them that the emotional distance has already opened.

### Beat 7 — Jessica Names the Twin-Body Absence

Jessica responds with the kind of recognition only she can offer.

Possible Jessica dialogue:

> “Your body keeps expecting her.”

Samira looks up.

> “That starts right away. It does not wait until the calendar says it is serious.”

Jessica may add, gently:

> “I know something about listening for a sister who is not where your body thinks she should be.”

- She does not turn the scene into a speech about Rachel Darcy.
- She gives Samira legitimacy: this is real, not dramatic.

### Beat 8 — Emma Makes the Pool Safe for the Truth

Emma’s response should be practical and tender.

Possible Emma dialogue:

> “Missing does not have a waiting period.”

> “You are allowed to miss her while she is doing the right thing for herself.”

> “Proud and lonely can happen beside the same swimming pool.”

- Emma may move closer but ask before touching, respecting Samira’s body autonomy.
- If Samira wants touch, Emma wraps a towel around her shoulders or sits beside her.
- If Samira does not want touch, Emma simply stays.
- The undressed detail remains about trust and emotional vulnerability, not exposure.

### Beat 9 — Samira’s Fear Under the Missing

Samira admits the deeper fear.

Possible lines:

> “What if she gets used to sleeping there before I get used to sleeping without her?”

> “What if Vaganova knows how to take care of her but does not know how to know her?”

> “What if she becomes Sahara-at-Vaganova and I am still Samira-without-Sahara?”

> “What if she comes home at Christmas and this pool feels like a place she visits?”

- This is not resentment of Sahara’s talent.
- It is fear that Sahara’s new life will become real faster than Samira can adapt.

### Beat 10 — Jessica Protects Both Girls’ Truths

Jessica clarifies that Samira’s grief will not be used against Sahara.

Possible Jessica dialogue:

> “Sahara can belong at Vaganova.”

> “And you can miss her here.”

> “One truth does not erase the other.”

She may add:

> “We are not going to make your missing into a chain around her ankle.”

> “And we are not going to make her courage into a rule that says you have to be silent.”

- This is crucial: Sahara’s autonomy remains intact.
- Samira’s emotional honesty is also protected.

### Beat 11 — Samira Says the Thing She Is Ashamed Of

Samira says she thought she should be easier.

Possible line:

> “She is the one at the Academy. I am the one at home in the mansion with both of you. I thought I was supposed to be fine.”

Emma answers:

> “You are not just at the mansion.”

Jessica completes it:

> “You are at the mansion without her.”

- This reframes Samira’s situation as real loss, not comfort she has no right to question.

### Beat 12 — The Mothers Offer Connection, Not a Fix

Jessica and Emma offer concrete ways to help Samira carry the first separation without pretending to erase it.

Possible options:

- A planned call with Sahara, if Vaganova rules and time zones allow.
- A private note Samira can send to Sahara.
- A promise that they will not hide Samira’s missing from themselves, but also will not burden Sahara with guilt.
- A family ritual for the remaining August nights: every evening they say one true thing about Sahara, one true thing about Samira, and one thing they are proud of.
- A later visit plan, without making it sound like rescue.

Possible Emma line:

> “A call will not put her in the pool.”

Possible Jessica line:

> “But it can put her voice beside you for a little while.”

### Beat 13 — Samira Chooses What To Tell Sahara

Samira worries that telling Sahara “I miss you” will make Sahara feel guilty.

Jessica and Emma help her make the truth loving instead of heavy.

Possible note Samira writes or dictates later:

> Sahara,
>
> I miss you. I am proud of you. Both things are true. Do not come back unless you want to. But the pool is wrong without you, and your towel stayed dry today, which was rude.

Or:

> I keep leaving room for you in the water. I think that means you are still here and also not here, which is annoying, so congratulations.

- The note should sound like Samira: physical, honest, funny at the edge, and fiercely loving.

### Beat 14 — Closing Physical Image

Recommended closing:

- Samira stays by the pool with Jessica and Emma.
- The second towel remains visible.
- Emma gently moves it closer to Samira rather than putting it away — not erasing Sahara, not pretending she is present.
- Jessica sits on Samira’s other side.
- The three of them look at the water.
- The pool is still too quiet.
- But Samira is no longer holding the quiet alone.

Possible final image:

> The water settles. Sahara’s towel is still dry. Samira sits between her mothers, undressed and unhidden, with the eight-o’clock August light moving across the pool tiles. Vaganova is across the ocean, and it is also inside the room with them. Jessica does not tell her to be brave. Emma does not tell her to be grateful. They sit with her until the silence becomes something they can share.

## Thematic Work

- **The handoff trip:** Jessica, Emma, and Samira personally bring Sahara to Russia; the separation becomes real after they return to Halifax.
- **The pool as twin-memory:** Water exposes the missing twin through habit, sound, space, and the unused second towel.
- **Undressed as unarmored:** Samira’s undressed state reflects emotional vulnerability and ordinary family bodily comfort, not visual detail.
- **Autonomy without silence:** Sahara’s choice remains respected, while Samira’s grief is allowed to be spoken.
- **Twin-body expectation:** Samira’s body expects Sahara before her mind can reason with the facts.
- **Jessica/Rachel echo:** Jessica recognizes twin absence from her own history without taking over Samira’s scene.
- **Emma’s practical tenderness:** Emma makes space for immediate grief and offers structure without pretending structure is a cure.

## Final Image

The eight-o’clock August light lies across the private pool at the Halifax mansion. The house is quiet behind them. Somewhere across the ocean, Sahara is inside Vaganova, learning the first shapes of her separate life.

Beside the water, Samira says:

> “I miss her like she is farther away than Russia.”

Jessica sits on one side of her. Emma sits on the other. Sahara’s towel stays dry, folded near Samira’s knee.

No one moves it away.

The pool is still wrong without Sahara. But now Jessica and Emma know. Now the missing is not only inside Samira’s body. It is in the open air, beside the water, where her mothers can help her hold it.
