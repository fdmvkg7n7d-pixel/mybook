## Scene Outline

### Morning Arrival at the New Building
- The four of them (Patti Anne, Aleksander, Gamaliel, and Klára) head out on Monday morning to the new academy building.
- It is a short walk, tram ride, or drive from Gamaliel and Klára's home.
- Patti Anne and Aleksander see the exterior for the first time — a proper building rather than the small original studio.
- First impressions: the signage (possibly "Mike Key Studio" or the full academy name), the scale of the place, and signs of the summer camp already in full swing (banners, groups of campers arriving, etc.).

### Full Tour of the New Building — They Show Everything
- Gamaliel and Klára lead a comprehensive tour, showing **every part** of the new building.
- **Studios**: Multiple large, bright dance studios with proper barres, mirrors, and sprung floors. They open every studio door so Patti Anne and Aleksander can see the full scale. Some are in use by summer camp groups.
- **Offices and Administrative Space**: Gamaliel shows the business side — where enrollment happens, finances are managed, and the academy is run as a growing institution. They go into every office and administrative area.
- **Changing Rooms and Facilities**: They show the practical spaces for students, including showers, lockers, and support areas.
- **Additional Features**: Community room, small performance space, storage, teacher areas, and any other rooms in the building. Gamaliel and Klára are thorough, wanting their visitors to see the complete facility.
- They point out how the space allows for expansion (more students, more teachers, full academy operations rather than just a studio).
- Gamaliel explains the business growth and how Terri Anne's gift (the twenty-five million) helped seed this, along with ongoing revenue and planning.
- Klára shares the artistic vision: the dream of a proper graded ballet academy in Prague, recruiting teachers (references to Rio or other efforts), and building something lasting.

### The Intensive Summer Camp (Approximately 200 Campers)
- As part of the tour, Gamaliel and Klárá take Patti Anne and Aleksander to see the intensive summer camp in action within the new building.
- There are about **two hundred campers** participating.
- The camp is vibrant and busy — groups of young dancers moving between studios, warm-ups in the hallways, rehearsals, technique classes, and the general hum of activity that fills the entire facility.
- They show specific camp activities:
  - Technique classes in the larger studios.
  - Rehearsals for end-of-camp performances.
  - Perhaps a masterclass or special session happening that day.
- **They watch some classes**: Patti Anne and Aleksander watch portions of several technique or rehearsal classes in action. This gives them a direct sense of the training level and the energy of the program.
- **Meeting the campers and teachers**: During the tour of the camp areas, they meet some of the campers (young dancers from various backgrounds) and several teachers/staff. Conversations are warm and brief — questions about where the visitors are from, excitement about the new building, or light comments on the classes. Gamaliel and Klára introduce their parents/foster parents proudly.
- Gamaliel explains the logistics: how the new building makes it possible to host such a large group (200 campers) with proper space, multiple simultaneous classes, and support facilities.
- Klára highlights the artistic side — the level of training, the diversity of the campers, and how the camp serves as both a recruitment tool and a community outreach program.
- The camp ties directly into the academy's growth and the need for more teachers/staff as the program expands.

- Brief moments of pride from Gamaliel and Klára as they show their "parents" what they've accomplished and the lively camp. The meetings with campers and teachers add personal warmth.
