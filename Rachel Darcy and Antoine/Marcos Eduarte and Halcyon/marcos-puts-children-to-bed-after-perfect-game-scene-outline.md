# Scene Outline: Marcos Eduarte Puts His Children to Bed After the Perfect Game

## Overview

- **Characters:** Marcos Eduarte Key-d'Aboville, Alessandrine Ariana, Kaisa Kalyca, Felipe Hephaestus, Spyridon Jacenty, Rachel Darcy Key, Melissa Lizette, Annabella Nicki, Larissa Leola, Kella Nakeisha, Armella Beatrice.
- **Location:** Marcos Eduarte’s Toronto penthouse near the Blue Jays ballpark.
- **Timing:** The night of Marcos Eduarte’s perfect game against the Milwaukee Brewers, after the post-game news conference.
- **Status:** Revised scene outline.

---

## Placement

- This scene follows **marcos-eduarte-perfect-game-first-start-after-halcyon-jacian-loss-scene-outline.md**.
- Marcos has just pitched a perfect game in his first start after Halcyon and Jacián Damaskinos’s deaths.
- The perfect game happened in the **third game of the season**.
- Marcos had been in Florida and flew back to Toronto with the team.
- The final out was a strikeout looking against the Milwaukee Brewers.
- Marcos immediately got his children after the last out.
- Spyridon Jacenty hugged Marcos so tightly that Rachel Darcy could not get him off Marcos for the news conference.
- Marcos took the children to the news conference, and Rachel Darcy joined them.
- The children are still living in Toronto.
- The triplets are still attending school in Toronto.
- The later Spring Mountain Vineyard move has not happened yet.

---

## Sleeping Arrangement

- Alessandrine Ariana and Kaisa Kalyca, the identical twins, sleep together in their own bedroom.
- Felipe Hephaestus sleeps in another bedroom.
- Spyridon Jacenty sleeps in another bedroom.
- The children have separate bedrooms, but the bedtime routine still begins together because the family is too raw to separate immediately after the game.

---

## Core Situation

- Marcos brings the children home to the Toronto penthouse after the perfect game and news conference.
- The public night is over, but the private night is still happening.
- The children need bath, pyjamas, food, story, and sleep, not more celebration.
- Marcos gives his children a bath together.
- Rachel Darcy helps Marcos get the triplets dressed into their nighties and pyjamas.
- Marcos gets Spyridon Jacenty dressed into his pyjamas.
- In the middle of getting Spyridon dressed, Spyridon starts closing his deep blue eyes.
- Rachel brings the triplets to the kitchen for a bedtime snack.
- Their favorite bedtime snack is a baked apple with cinnamon.
- The baked apple with cinnamon was Halcyon’s favorite dessert.
- After the snack, Felipe Hephaestus goes into his bedroom.
- The identical twins go into their bedroom.
- The book **Peter Pan for Children** is in Felipe Hephaestus’s bedroom.
- Felipe Hephaestus wants Marcos Eduarte to read **Peter Pan for Children**.
- The identical twins want to hear the story about their parents.
- Marcos reads **Peter Pan for Children** to Felipe.
- Marcos tells Alessandrine Ariana and Kaisa Kalyca the story of how Halcyon met him.
- The children are asleep when Marcos’s sisters call him about the perfect game.
- After the calls, Rachel Darcy and Marcos Eduarte have a very emotional conversation about his life as a single father to four children and a baseball pitcher.

## Scene Beats

### Beat 1: Returning From the Ballpark

- Marcos, Rachel Darcy, and the children return to the Toronto penthouse after the perfect game and news conference.
- The city outside is still alive with the news.
- The penthouse is quieter than the stadium, but the energy of the night has followed them home.
- The children are exhausted from the game, the crowd, the news conference, and the emotions of seeing their father pitch again.
- Marcos is physically drained but too alert to sleep.
- Rachel helps settle the house, but Marcos insists on doing bedtime himself.
- He needs to end the night as their father, not as the pitcher who made history.

### Beat 2: Bathing the Children Together

- Marcos gives the children a bath together.
- The bath is practical, warm, and ordinary after a night that has been too public.
- The children are tired enough that the usual bath energy is muted.
- Alessandrine Ariana and Kaisa Kalyca stay close to each other.
- Felipe Hephaestus is quieter than usual.
- Spyridon Jacenty keeps looking for Marcos whenever Marcos shifts position.
- Rachel stays nearby to help with towels, clothes, and the transition out of the bath.
- The bath is not about celebration.
- It is about bringing the children back into their bodies and back into the ordinary safety of home.

### Beat 3: Getting the Triplets Dressed

- Rachel Darcy helps Marcos get the triplets dressed.
- Alessandrine Ariana and Kaisa Kalyca are helped into their nighties.
- Felipe Hephaestus is helped into his pyjamas.
- The triplets are seven and can do much of it themselves, but tonight they accept help more easily.
- Rachel’s hands are calm and practiced.
- Marcos keeps glancing from one child to another, still counting them silently.
- The triplets are old enough to know that their father pitched something historic.
- They are also old enough to know that their mother is not there to send them to bed.

### Beat 4: Marcos Dresses Spyridon

- Marcos gets Spyridon Jacenty dressed into his pyjamas.
- Spyridon is three years old and utterly worn out.
- In the middle of getting him dressed, Spyridon starts closing his deep blue eyes.
- Marcos slows down instead of rushing him.
- Spyridon leans into his father’s hands and voice.
- The pyjama sleeve, the soft fabric, and Marcos’s nearness become enough to pull him toward sleep.
- Marcos feels the weight of Spyridon’s exhaustion and innocence.
- Spyridon does not understand the perfect game.
- He understands that Daddy is home and holding him.

### Beat 5: Rachel Takes the Triplets to the Kitchen

- While Marcos finishes settling Spyridon, Rachel brings the triplets to the kitchen for a bedtime snack.
- The kitchen is quiet and warm.
- Rachel prepares or serves baked apples with cinnamon.
- Baked apple with cinnamon is the triplets’ favorite bedtime snack.
- It was also Halcyon’s favorite dessert.
- The smell of warm apple and cinnamon changes the room immediately.
- The snack brings Halcyon into the night without making the children speak her death again.
- Alessandrine Ariana and Kaisa Kalyca recognize the dessert as their mother’s favorite.
- Felipe Hephaestus eats slowly, listening more than talking.
- Rachel lets the snack be both comfort and memory.

### Beat 6: Spyridon’s Bedroom

- Marcos carries or guides Spyridon to his bedroom.
- Spyridon sleeps in his own bedroom.
- His deep blue eyes are mostly closed by the time Marcos reaches the room.
- Marcos tucks him in gently.
- Spyridon may still keep a hand on Marcos’s sleeve for a moment.
- Marcos stays until Spyridon’s grip loosens.
- He does not force Spyridon into full wakefulness for a story.
- Spyridon has had too much night already.

### Beat 7: The Triplets’ Snack Becomes Memory

- In the kitchen, the triplets eat the baked apples with cinnamon.
- Rachel sits with them rather than standing over them.
- The snack is simple, but it carries Halcyon’s presence.
- One of the twins may mention that Mommy loved this dessert.
- Rachel confirms it without making the moment too heavy.
- The triplets become quiet around the taste.
- The cinnamon, apple, and warmth give them a way to remember their mother through something ordinary and sweet.
- When Marcos returns from Spyridon’s room, he sees the triplets at the kitchen table with Rachel.
- The sight nearly undoes him more than the stadium did.

### Beat 8: The Children Go to Their Bedrooms

- After the snack, Marcos begins moving the triplets toward bed.
- Felipe Hephaestus goes into his bedroom.
- Alessandrine Ariana and Kaisa Kalyca go into the identical twins’ bedroom.
- The separation is gentle and practical.
- Marcos does not keep everyone in one room for the whole bedtime.
- The separate rooms matter because each child needs a different kind of quiet.
- Rachel helps the transition without making it feel like the children are being split apart.

### Beat 9: Felipe Wants Peter Pan for Children

- Marcos enters Felipe Hephaestus’s bedroom.
- The book **Peter Pan for Children** is already in Felipe’s bedroom.
- Felipe Hephaestus asks Marcos Eduarte to read **Peter Pan for Children**.
- Felipe wants the comfort of pages, pictures, and his father’s voice.
- Marcos reads **Peter Pan for Children** to Felipe in Felipe’s bedroom.
- He keeps his voice low and steady.
- Felipe listens from under the covers, tired but comforted by the fact that his father came to his room and read from the book that belongs there.
- The story gives Felipe something familiar and child-sized after a night that has been too large.

### Beat 10: The Twins Want the Story About Their Parents

- Marcos then goes to Alessandrine Ariana and Kaisa Kalyca in the identical twins’ bedroom.
- The twins are close together in their beds or tucked near each other in the room they share.
- The twins do not want **Peter Pan for Children** tonight.
- Alessandrine Ariana and Kaisa Kalyca want to hear the story about their parents.
- They want to hear how Halcyon met Marcos Eduarte.
- Marcos understands that the twins are asking for origin, not entertainment.
- They are asking for their mother in the only way bedtime can safely give her back for a few minutes.
- Marcos sits where both girls can see him.
- He lets the room get quiet before he begins.

### Beat 11: Marcos Tells the Twins How Halcyon Met Him

- Marcos tells the twins about Clarence Park near the Blue Jays stadium.
- He tells them that he met Halcyon on the day he signed with the Toronto Blue Jays.
- He explains that he was young, overwhelmed, and trying to breathe after the noise of the contract.
- He tells them their mother was sitting with her belongings carefully arranged.
- He makes clear that Halcyon was not a helpless girl waiting to be saved.
- She was watchful, proud, sharp, and careful.
- She noticed everything.
- She asked practical questions.
- She did not trust easily.
- Marcos tells the twins that the first thing he learned about their mother was that she belonged to herself.

### Beat 12: The Carlisle Part

- Marcos tells the twins that he asked Halcyon if she wanted to come to Carlisle.
- He tells them she had choices.
- He tells them she asked whether she could leave if she wanted.
- He tells them she kept her bag.
- He tells them she wanted to know whether the gates opened from the inside.
- He tells them that he should have called Rachel Darcy first and did not.
- Rachel, listening from nearby or passing in the hall, hears that part and lets it stand.
- Marcos does not make himself the hero of the story.
- He makes Halcyon the center of it.

### Beat 13: The Twins Hear Their Mother

- Alessandrine Ariana and Kaisa Kalyca listen intensely.
- The identical twins are looking for their mother’s shape in the story.
- Marcos answers their questions without sanding the story smooth.
- He tells them that their mother was brave, careful, and difficult to fool.
- He tells them that she loved them with the same sharp, watchful intensity.
- He keeps the focus on Halcyon being real, brave, and theirs.

### Beat 14: The Perfect Game Enters the Bedtime Quiet

- One of the twins asks a small question about the perfect game before fully settling.
- The question is quieter than the stadium and smaller than the reporters’ questions.
- Marcos says the perfect game does not make the sadness go away.
- He says he was glad they were there.
- He tells them that reaching for them after the last out was the most important thing he did all night.
- He does not make a speech.
- He lets the words be small enough for bedtime.

### Beat 15: The Children Fall Asleep

- Marcos checks Felipe Hephaestus again in Felipe’s bedroom.
- Felipe is asleep with **Peter Pan for Children** still near him.
- Marcos checks Alessandrine Ariana and Kaisa Kalyca in the twins’ bedroom.
- The twins are asleep after hearing the story of how their parents met.
- Spyridon Jacenty is already asleep in his own room, his deep blue eyes closed after fighting sleep through pyjamas.
- Marcos moves between the rooms instead of staying in only one doorway.
- He checks each child because separate bedrooms feel too separate tonight.

### Beat 16: The First Sister Call — Melissa Lizette

- Marcos’s phone begins buzzing only after the children are asleep.
- Melissa Lizette is the first sister to call, or the first call he answers.
- Marcos steps away from the bedroom doors before answering.
- He answers softly because the children are sleeping.
- Melissa is emotional, proud, and trying not to overwhelm him.
- She tells him he was unbelievable.
- She also tells him that Halcyon should have been there.
- Marcos does not have to answer that fully.
- Melissa understands the silence.

### Beat 17: Annabella Nicki Calls

- Annabella Nicki calls next or joins through a family call.
- Her response is quieter and more visual.
- She talks about the final pitch, the way he stood after the umpire called strike three, and the way he reached for the children.
- She tells him that the perfect game was historic, but the first thing she will remember is Spyridon holding on to him.
- Marcos is undone by the precision of that observation.
- Annabella sees what other people miss.

### Beat 18: The Older Sisters Call

- Larissa Leola calls from far away.
- Kella Nakeisha calls from her household.
- Armella Beatrice calls with the emotional clarity of someone who thinks in images and memory.
- Each sister reacts differently, but all of them are proud.
- None of them treats the perfect game as if it fixes the loss.
- They call because Marcos is their brother.
- They call because he stood on the mound after death entered his house.
- They call because they saw him reach for his children first.
- The calls create a circle around the penthouse after the children are asleep.

### Beat 19: Rachel and Marcos After the Calls

- After the calls, Rachel remains with Marcos in the quiet.
- The sisters’ calls have ended or slowed into text messages.
- Rachel tells Marcos that the children needed the bath, the snack, and the stories more than they needed any more public celebration.
- Marcos admits that he did too.
- Rachel does not try to make the perfect game larger than the children’s bedtime.
- She understands that the bath, the pyjamas, the baked apples with cinnamon, **Peter Pan for Children**, and the twins’ parent story were the real work of the night.
- Marcos has pitched a perfect game, faced the press, put the children to bed, and answered his sisters.
- Now he is simply a widowed father standing between separate bedroom doors.

### Beat 20: The Single Father Conversation

- Rachel and Marcos move to the kitchen, hallway, or living room after checking the children one more time.
- The penthouse is finally quiet enough for Marcos to stop holding himself upright for everyone else.
- Marcos becomes very emotional with Rachel.
- He talks about being a single father to four children.
- He talks about Alessandrine Ariana and Kaisa Kalyca needing the story of their parents.
- He talks about Felipe Hephaestus wanting **Peter Pan for Children** because he needed something solid and familiar.
- He talks about Spyridon Jacenty gripping him so tightly that nobody could take him away.
- He admits that he does not know how to be enough for all four children without Halcyon.
- He admits that pitching is the one part of his life where the task is clear.
- Fatherhood is not like pitching.
- There is no catcher calling signs for grief.
- There is no inning limit on bedtime, nightmares, school mornings, missing Mommy, or explaining why Jacián Damaskinos is not coming home.

### Beat 21: Baseball and Fatherhood

- Marcos tells Rachel that baseball now feels both necessary and impossible.
- Baseball gives him structure, income, discipline, and a place where his body knows what to do.
- Baseball also takes him away from the children.
- Every road trip now feels like a question about whether he is abandoning them.
- Every start asks him to be focused while part of him is counting the children at home.
- He wonders how a starting pitcher can also be the daily parent the children need.
- He wonders whether the perfect game will make people think he is fine.
- He tells Rachel that he is not fine.
- Rachel does not contradict him.
- She lets him say it plainly.

### Beat 22: Rachel’s Answer

- Rachel answers as his mother, not as a manager of logistics.
- She tells Marcos that he does not have to become Halcyon and himself at the same time.
- He has to be their father.
- He has to tell the truth.
- He has to come back when he says he will come back.
- He has to let the family help.
- Rachel reminds him that no one in this family does anything alone.
- She does not minimize the difficulty of being a single father and a baseball pitcher.
- She tells him it will require schedules, help, travel plans, school coordination, and brutal honesty with the children.
- She also tells him that the children already know the most important thing: after the last out, he reached for them first.

### Beat 23: Marcos Lets Himself Break

- Marcos finally lets himself cry in front of Rachel.
- The tears are not the public tears people expected after the perfect game.
- They are quieter and more exhausted.
- Rachel holds him or stays close enough for him to lean into her presence.
- He is her grown son, a widower, a father of four, and a professional athlete all at once.
- Rachel lets all of those identities exist without asking him to choose one in that moment.
- The conversation does not solve the future.
- It gives Marcos permission to face it without pretending the perfect game made him whole.

### Beat 24: Final Image

- The final image is Marcos standing in the hallway between the children’s bedrooms.
- Alessandrine Ariana and Kaisa Kalyca are asleep in the identical twins’ bedroom.
- Felipe Hephaestus is asleep in his own bedroom, with **Peter Pan for Children** nearby.
- Spyridon Jacenty is asleep in his own bedroom, finally released from the grip he kept on Marcos all night.
- The kitchen still smells faintly of baked apple and cinnamon.
- Marcos’s phone rests nearby with messages and family love still arriving.
- The perfect game is already becoming history outside the penthouse.
- Inside the penthouse, Halcyon’s favorite dessert, Felipe’s **Peter Pan for Children**, and the twins’ story about their parents are what remain.
