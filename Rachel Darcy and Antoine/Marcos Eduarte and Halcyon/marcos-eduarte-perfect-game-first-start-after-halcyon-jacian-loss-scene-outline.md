# Scene Outline: Marcos Eduarte’s Perfect Game — First Start After Halcyon and Jacián Damaskinos’s Deaths

## Overview

- **Characters:** Marcos Eduarte Key-d'Aboville, Rachel Darcy Key, Alessandrine Ariana, Kaisa Kalyca, Felipe Hephaestus, Spyridon Jacenty, Toronto Blue Jays manager, Blue Jays catcher, Blue Jays teammates, Milwaukee Brewers players.
- **Location:** Toronto Blue Jays ballpark.
- **Opponent:** Milwaukee Brewers.
- **Timing:** The third game of the season; Marcos Eduarte’s first Major League start after the deaths of Halcyon and newborn Jacián Damaskinos.
- **Status:** Revised scene outline.

---

## Placement

- Halcyon and Jacián Damaskinos died in February, just before spring training.
- Marcos was placed on compassionate bereavement leave by the Toronto Blue Jays.
- At the time of this first start back, the children are still living in Toronto.
- The triplets, Alessandrine Ariana, Kaisa Kalyca, and Felipe Hephaestus, are also still attending school in Toronto.
- The later Spring Mountain Vineyard move has not happened yet.
- Rachel Darcy and Marcos’s children are in the audience at the Blue Jays ballpark.
- This game is the third game of the season.
- Marcos had been in Florida with the Blue Jays and flew back to Toronto with the team.
- This start is Marcos’s first start after the deaths.
- In this start, Marcos pitches a perfect game against the Milwaukee Brewers.

---

## Core Situation

- Marcos returns to the mound carrying private grief and public expectation.
- The Blue Jays organization has protected him from media intrusion, but everyone knows this is his first start back.
- The audience knows he is pitching just after the deaths of his very gorgeous wife Halcyon and his newborn son Jacián Damaskinos.
- The audience admires Marcos Eduarte’s strength to stand on the mound so soon after losing them.
- The family is not watching from a distance.
- Rachel Darcy and the children are physically present in the audience.
- The game becomes historic because Marcos allows no baserunners.
- It is a perfect game: no hits, no walks, no hit batters, no errors that allow a runner, and no Milwaukee Brewers player reaches base.
- The perfect game does not erase the deaths.
- It becomes the first public proof that Marcos can stand on the mound after losing Halcyon and Jacián Damaskinos.

---

## Scene Beats

### Beat 1: Before the Game

- Marcos arrives at the ballpark earlier than usual.
- He has recently flown back to Toronto with the team after being in Florida.
- The clubhouse is quieter around him than it used to be.
- His teammates know not to crowd him with speeches.
- The manager checks in with him privately.
- The check-in is human before it is professional.
- Marcos says he is ready to start.
- He does not say he is healed.
- No one asks him to pretend that he is.

### Beat 2: The Family in the Audience

- Rachel Darcy brings Alessandrine Ariana, Kaisa Kalyca, Felipe Hephaestus, and Spyridon Jacenty to the ballpark.
- The children are still living in Toronto.
- The triplets are still attending school in Toronto.
- The family sits in protected family seating arranged by the Blue Jays organization.
- The triplets understand enough to know the game matters.
- Spyridon understands only that Daddy is pitching and everyone is watching him.
- Rachel does not make the children perform excitement.
- She lets them sit, fidget, whisper, worry, or cheer in whatever way they can manage.
- Marcos knows where they are sitting.
- He does not look at them for long before the game starts because if he looks too long, he may not be able to pitch.

### Beat 3: The First Inning Against Milwaukee

- Marcos walks to the mound with his grief contained inside his body.
- The crowd gives him a long ovation.
- The ovation carries admiration for his strength as much as support for his return.
- Many people in the audience are thinking about Halcyon and Jacián Damaskinos, even though Marcos does not want the grief turned into spectacle.
- The Milwaukee Brewers’ leadoff hitter steps in.
- Marcos does not look up for too long.
- He takes the ball.
- He begins.
- His mechanics are clean, almost severe.
- The first three Brewers batters go down in order.
- It is too early for anyone to name what is beginning.

### Beat 4: The Game Tightens

- In the middle innings, Marcos’s command sharpens.
- His catcher realizes first that every pitch has unusual clarity.
- The defense behind him begins moving with extra care.
- No one says perfect game out loud.
- The dugout begins to leave him alone between innings.
- Marcos sits by himself, breathing, not looking at the scoreboard more than necessary.
- The silence around him becomes part of the game.

### Beat 5: Grief Becomes Precision

- Marcos is not pitching from rage.
- He is not pitching to prove that the deaths meant something.
- He is pitching because the mound gives him one narrow task at a time.
- One pitch.
- One batter.
- One inning.
- The structure holds him when emotion cannot.
- The game becomes less like performance and more like survival through precision.

### Beat 6: Rachel Understands What Is Happening

- In the audience, Rachel realizes before the children do that no Milwaukee Brewers player has reached base.
- She does not say it out loud.
- She watches Marcos’s shoulders between pitches.
- She recognizes the same discipline she once learned in dance: repetition, balance, breath, and the body doing what grief cannot explain.
- The triplets sense the adults’ tension and become quieter.
- Spyridon asks a small child’s question that cuts through the row.
- Rachel answers gently without breaking the spell of the game.

### Beat 7: The Ninth Inning

- By the ninth inning, the ballpark knows.
- The broadcast knows.
- The Blue Jays dugout knows.
- The family in the audience knows.
- Marcos still has to throw the pitches.
- The first out comes.
- The second out comes.
- The final Milwaukee batter steps in.
- Marcos does not think about perfection.
- He thinks about the catcher’s glove.
- He thinks about the next pitch.

### Beat 8: The Last Out — Strikeout Looking

- The count reaches the final pitch.
- Marcos throws with the same contained precision he has carried all night.
- The pitch catches the zone.
- The Milwaukee batter does not swing.
- The umpire calls strike three.
- The last out is a strikeout looking.
- For one fraction of a second, the stadium holds still.
- Then the ballpark erupts.
- Marcos has pitched a perfect game against the Milwaukee Brewers in his first start after Halcyon and Jacián Damaskinos’s deaths.

### Beat 9: Marcos Immediately Gets His Children

- His teammates rush toward the mound.
- The catcher reaches him first.
- The celebration begins, but Marcos’s first instinct is not the cameras or the mound pile.
- Just after the last out, Marcos immediately looks for his children.
- The Blue Jays staff already know where the family is seated.
- Security and team staff help bring the children down or help Marcos reach them safely.
- Marcos gets Alessandrine Ariana, Kaisa Kalyca, Felipe Hephaestus, and Spyridon Jacenty as soon as he can.
- The children come to him in the middle of the perfect-game chaos.
- He gathers them before the public story can swallow the private one.
- Alessandrine Ariana, Kaisa Kalyca, and Felipe Hephaestus become very emotional in Marcos Eduarte’s arms.
- The triplets cry into him because they understand enough to know that Daddy has done something huge and Mommy is not there to see it.
- Marcos holds all three triplets as tightly as he can without letting go of Spyridon Jacenty.
- Spyridon Jacenty hugs Marcos Eduarte so tight that he locks himself around his father.
- Spyridon does not understand the perfect game, but he understands Daddy, noise, grief, and not wanting to be put down.
- The perfect game belongs to baseball, but the first embrace belongs to his children.

### Beat 10: Rachel Watches the Embrace

- Rachel watches Marcos reach for the children immediately after the last out.
- She is proud of the perfect game.
- She is more moved by the fact that Marcos’s first movement after history is toward his children.
- She understands that this is the real return.
- Not the strikeout.
- Not the scoreboard.
- Not the record.
- The real return is Marcos choosing fatherhood in the first second after baseball gives him a miracle.

### Beat 11: Rachel Cannot Get Spyridon Off Marcos

- After the on-field celebration, staff begin preparing Marcos for the post-game news conference.
- Rachel tries gently to take Spyridon Jacenty so Marcos can go speak to the media.
- Spyridon hugs Marcos even tighter.
- Rachel cannot get him off Marcos Eduarte without upsetting him further.
- Spyridon’s grip makes the situation clear before anyone has to say it.
- Marcos is not going to separate from his children for the news conference.
- Marcos looks at Rachel and understands the decision immediately.
- He decides he should take his children to the news conference with him.
- Rachel joins them too.

### Beat 12: The News Conference With the Children

- Reporters want the perfect game story.
- Reporters want the grief story.
- The Blue Jays organization protects Marcos from being consumed by either one.
- Marcos enters the news conference with Alessandrine Ariana, Kaisa Kalyca, Felipe Hephaestus, and Spyridon Jacenty with him.
- Rachel Darcy joins them, close enough to help the children if they become overwhelmed.
- The triplets are still very emotional.
- Spyridon stays pressed tightly against Marcos.
- Marcos does not apologize for bringing the children.
- The manager and Blue Jays staff make room for the family at the table.
- Marcos speaks briefly if he can.
- He does not turn Halcyon and Jacián Damaskinos into a headline.
- He says only what he can bear to say.
- The manager keeps the boundary firm.
- The game belongs to baseball, but the grief belongs to the family.
- The children being present makes that boundary visible to everyone in the room.

### Beat 13: Final Image

- The final image is Marcos at the news conference with his children after the perfect game.
- The perfect-game ball is nearby, but it is not the center of the image.
- Alessandrine Ariana, Kaisa Kalyca, Felipe Hephaestus, and Spyridon Jacenty are with their father.
- Spyridon is still holding Marcos tightly.
- Rachel is beside them.
- The game is perfect.
- The family is not.
- But Marcos has stood on the mound again, struck out the final Brewers batter looking, reached for his children first, and refused to leave them behind for the public story.

## Emotional Notes

- The perfect game should not be written as a magical cure for grief.
- It is historic, but it is also emotionally complicated.
- The audience may admire Marcos Eduarte’s strength, but the scene should not make his grief feel like a performance for them.
- The audience’s admiration should sit beside, not replace, the private reality that Halcyon and Jacián Damaskinos are gone.
- Marcos’s precision should feel like containment, not triumphalism.
- The family should be physically present in the audience, not watching from Spring Mountain Vineyard.
- The children are still living in Toronto at this point.
- The triplets are still attending school in Toronto at this point.
- Marcos immediately getting his children after the last out is the emotional center of the scene.
- The triplets becoming very emotional in Marcos’s arms should show that they understand the grief underneath the historic game.
- Spyridon hugging Marcos so tightly that Rachel cannot get him off should drive the news-conference decision.
- Marcos taking the children to the news conference should show that fatherhood comes before media expectations.
- The final out being a strikeout looking should feel clean, stunned, and almost suspended before the stadium erupts.
- The Blue Jays organization should continue protecting Marcos from intrusive media.
