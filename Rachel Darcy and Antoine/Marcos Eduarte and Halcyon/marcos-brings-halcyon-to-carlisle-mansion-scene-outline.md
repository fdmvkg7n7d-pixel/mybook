# SCENE OUTLINE: MARCOS BRINGS HALCYON TO THE CARLISLE MANSION

## Placement

**When:** The same day Marcos Eduarte signs his professional contract with the Toronto Blue Jays, after he meets Halcyon in Clarence Park.  
**Where:** Toronto / the drive to Ontario / the Carlisle mansion.  
**Characters:** Marcos Eduarte Key-d'Aboville, Halcyon, Rachel Darcy Key. Optional: Jessica Isabella, Melissa Lizette, Annabella Nicki, household staff/security depending on who is at Carlisle.  
**Marcos's age:** Approximately eighteen.  
**Halcyon's age:** Seventeen.  
**Context:** Halcyon has been living in the park around the Blue Jays stadium. Marcos has just signed with Toronto and meets her in the park the same day. Instead of leaving her there, he brings Halcyon to the Carlisle mansion with Halcyon's consent — but he does **not** call Rachel Darcy first.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene establishes:

- Halcyon entering the Key family world for the first time.
- Carlisle as a sanctuary again — not glamorous wealth, but controlled safety.
- Marcos's first major adult decision after signing with the Blue Jays.
- Rachel's protective response when Marcos arrives with Halcyon without calling first.
- Halcyon's agency: she agrees to come, keeps control of her belongings, and is not forced to tell her whole story immediately.
- The emotional complexity of Marcos beginning to care for Halcyon while the adults make sure safety comes before romance.

---

## Emotional Core

**Safety without ownership.**

Marcos wants Halcyon safe, but he has to learn immediately that wanting to protect someone does not give him the right to control her. Carlisle can offer shelter, food, privacy, and adults who know how to move carefully — but Halcyon still belongs to herself.

---

## Tone

Quiet, careful, protective, and tense around the edges. The scene should feel like a young woman stepping into safety without yet trusting it, and a newly signed baseball player realizing adulthood is not only contracts and jerseys.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — No Call to Rachel

Before Marcos brings Halcyon anywhere, he **does not** call Rachel.

This is the mistake and the emotional complication of the scene. Marcos is seventeen, newly signed, overwhelmed by the contract, and frightened by the idea of leaving Halcyon in Clarence Park. He acts from urgency instead of procedure.

He is not trying to hide her forever, and he is not acting with malice. But he is still bringing a frightened seventeen-year-old girl to Carlisle without warning his mother first.

This means Rachel's first response will happen at the door, not over the phone.

### Beat 2 — Halcyon's Consent

Marcos returns to Halcyon and offers Carlisle carefully.

He explains that Carlisle is his family's home in Ontario. He says there is space, food, a private room, and adults there who will help — but he is honest that he has not called Rachel first. He makes it clear Halcyon can say no.

Halcyon does not trust this easily.

She asks practical questions. She wants to know whether she can keep her belongings with her, whether the door locks, whether anyone will call police without telling her, whether she can leave if she wants.

Marcos answers plainly.

The most important line:

> "You get to choose."

### Beat 3 — Gathering Her Things

Halcyon gathers her belongings from the park.

This should be written with dignity. Her things are not trash. They are her life.

Possible items:

- a backpack;
- a rolled blanket;
- a notebook;
- a water bottle;
- a small pouch with documents or keepsakes;
- shoes or clothing arranged carefully.

Marcos offers to carry something. Halcyon allows only one item, or refuses at first. Either choice is respected.

### Beat 4 — The Drive

The drive from Toronto toward Carlisle is quiet.

Marcos is aware of the absurd contrast: that morning he signed with the Toronto Blue Jays; now he is driving away from the stadium with a seventeen-year-old girl who has been sleeping near it.

Halcyon watches the roads, exits, signs, and distance. She may memorize the route because not knowing where she is going frightens her.

Marcos does not fill the silence. He lets her watch.

### Beat 5 — First Sight of Carlisle

The Carlisle mansion appears through gates, acreage, trees, and distance from the road.

For Marcos, Carlisle means family safety.
For Halcyon, it may look like another world entirely.

She may tense when she sees the gates.

Marcos notices and says:

> "They open from the inside too."

This line matters. Carlisle is safety, not a cage.

### Beat 6 — Quiet Arrival at Carlisle

Rachel is not waiting with advance knowledge. Marcos brings Halcyon into Carlisle quietly, without calling first and without immediately waking or alerting Rachel.

This is part of the mistake. Marcos is acting from urgency and protectiveness, but he is also avoiding the adult conversation he should have had before bringing Halcyon home.

Halcyon is frightened, exhausted, and still unsure whether she has made a safe choice. Marcos focuses on the practical: getting her indoors, keeping her belongings with her, and giving her somewhere quiet to sit or sleep.

### Beat 7 — Food, Room, Shower, No Interrogation

The first care is practical:

- food;
- water;
- a bathroom;
- clean clothes if Halcyon wants them;
- a private bedroom;
- a door that closes;
- permission to keep her belongings in sight.

No one demands the full story yet.

Marcos knows Rachel would say something like:

> "Tonight you eat and sleep. Tomorrow we can talk about what you want to do next."

But Rachel has not been told yet. That knowledge hangs over the scene.

### Beat 8 — Marcos Has to Step Back

This is difficult for Marcos.

He brought Halcyon here. He feels responsible. But Rachel quietly makes clear that Halcyon's safety cannot depend only on his intense attention.

Marcos has to step back enough for Halcyon to breathe.

He may leave her at the bedroom door rather than entering. He may say goodnight from the hallway.

> "I'll be here in the morning if you want."

Halcyon does not have to answer warmly.

### Beat 9 — The Secret Until Morning

After Halcyon is settled, Marcos does not wake Rachel.

This keeps the situation quiet overnight, but it also means the truth will land harder in the morning. Melissa Lizette and Annabella Nicki will find out first, and then Rachel.

### Beat 10 — Final Image

Halcyon is in a Carlisle guest bedroom with her backpack and belongings close by. Before sleeping, she takes a long hot shower to wash the dirt off, and Marcos gives her one of Terri Anne's old nighties to sleep in. For the first time in months, she is clean, warm, and behind a closed door. Her backpack remains in the guest bedroom the next morning when she goes downstairs.

The door is closed, but not locked against her.

Outside, Marcos stands in the hallway for one second too long before walking away.

Carlisle has taken in another wounded person, but this time Rachel does not know yet. This time, Marcos is not the child being protected. He is the one learning what protection costs — and what secrecy costs.

## Key Dialogue Possibilities

**Marcos Eduarte**
> "You get to choose."  
> "My family's house is called Carlisle."  
> "They open from the inside too."  
> "No one touches your bag unless you say so."  
> "I'll be here in the morning if you want."

**Halcyon**
> "Why would your mother say yes?"  
> "Can I leave if I want?"  
> "I'm keeping my bag."  
> "That house has gates."  
> "I don't want everyone looking at me."

**Rachel Darcy**
> "You should have called me."  
> "She is a guest. Not a secret."  
> "I'm Rachel. I'm Marcos's mother."  
> "Tonight you eat and sleep. Tomorrow we can talk about what you want to do next."  
> "Love is not control, Marcos. Not even when you're scared."

## What This Scene Establishes

1. Marcos brings Halcyon to the Carlisle mansion after meeting her near Rogers Centre on the day he signs with the Blue Jays.
2. Halcyon is seventeen and living in the park, so the family prioritizes safety, consent, and practical care.
3. Marcos does **not** call Rachel before bringing Halcyon to Carlisle, which makes the decision impulsive and complicated.
4. Rachel does not find out until the next morning; Halcyon comes downstairs to the family kitchen while Melissa Lizette and Annabella Nicki are eating breakfast.
5. Carlisle continues its saga role as the Key family fortress of safety.
6. Halcyon keeps agency over her belongings, her story, and her choices.
7. Marcos begins learning the difference between love, rescue, responsibility, and control.
8. Halcyon takes a long hot shower, sleeps in one of Terri Anne's old nighties, and sleeps well for the first time in months.
9. Halcyon's backpack remains upstairs in the guest bedroom when she goes down to the family kitchen the next morning.
10. The relationship can grow from here, but the first priority is Halcyon's safety and dignity.
