# SCENE OUTLINE: MARCOS SIGNING DAY WITH RACHEL — MELISSA IN EDINBURGH AND ANNABELLA AT TEMPLE

## Placement

**When:** The autumn day Marcos Eduarte signs his professional contract with the Toronto Blue Jays.  
**Where:** Toronto / Blue Jays signing location TBD.  
**Characters:** Marcos Eduarte Key-d'Aboville and Rachel Darcy Key are physically present. Melissa Lizette Key-d'Aboville is present by phone/video from The University of Edinburgh. Annabella Nicki Key-d'Aboville is present by phone/video from Temple University.  
**Context:** Marcos has been drafted out of high school by the Toronto Blue Jays as a starting pitcher. He does not attend university. The triplets are seventeen, so Rachel Darcy has to sign / co-sign the contract as Marcos's mother/legal guardian. On the day of the signing, Rachel Darcy is physically with him. Melissa Lizette is attending The University of Edinburgh for Linguistics and English Language MA (Hons) that autumn and staying in Baird House with another woman, and Annabella Nicki is attending Temple University, staying in Morgan Hall with three other women, following Rachel Darcy’s footsteps through a serious university dance path.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene establishes:

- Marcos crossing the threshold from high-school baseball into professional baseball while still seventeen.
- Rachel Darcy physically present because she has to sign / co-sign the contract for Marcos as his mother/legal guardian.
- Melissa Lizette and Annabella Nicki not physically present because they are each beginning separate university paths that autumn.
- Melissa receiving the news from The University of Edinburgh.
- Annabella receiving the news from Temple University.
- The emotional reality that Marcos is the one signing, but the triplet unit is still present through distance, phone calls, and the shared knowledge that something has changed.
- The beginning of Rachel’s wider family calls about Marcos’s contract.
- This signing day leads directly into Marcos finding **Halcyon** in Clarence Park later the same day.

---

## Emotional Core

**The triplet unit at the edge of separation.**

Marcos is entering a professional contract, but because he is seventeen, Rachel has to sign / co-sign it. The future is his. The mound is his. But the legal signature also belongs to his mother.

Melissa and Annabella are not in the room. That absence matters. Melissa is in Edinburgh studying language. Annabella is at Temple following Rachel Darcy’s dance footsteps. Marcos is in Toronto becoming a professional baseball player.

The three who grew up as one unit are now stretched across different futures.

---

## Tone

Quietly monumental. Emotional but controlled. The scene should feel like a family holding its breath in a professional room where everyone else is focused on paperwork, parental consent, signatures, schedules, and development plans.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — The Room Before the Signature

Marcos sits with the contract in front of him, but Rachel is the one who has to legally sign / co-sign because he is seventeen.

The Blue Jays representatives are present or nearby, but the emotional focus is on the family side of the room.

Rachel is close enough to see the pen and the signature lines that require her name.

Melissa and Annabella are not physically there. Their absence is not abandonment. It is the first visible shape of the triplets’ adult separation.

### Beat 2 — Rachel Watches Her Son

Rachel understands the magnitude of what is happening.

Marcos is not going to university. He is entering directly into the Blue Jays system, but because he is still seventeen, Rachel's signature is required. That makes the moment heavier: she is not only witnessing his choice; she is legally helping open the door.

The organization will test his arm, manage his innings, send him through Dunedin, Vancouver, back to Dunedin, New Hampshire, Buffalo, and eventually — if everything holds — Toronto.

Rachel is proud, but she is also frightened. Baseball is no longer a dream she can protect from a distance. It has become a contract.

### Beat 3 — Melissa Lizette in Edinburgh

Melissa is not physically in the signing room. That autumn, she is attending **The University of Edinburgh**, studying **Linguistics and English Language MA (Hons)**.

Rachel will call her from Toronto with the news, and that call becomes its own scene.

Melissa’s response is warm, emotional, and shaped by distance. She is proud of Marcos, but she feels the ache of not being beside her triplet brother when the contract is signed.

Possible line over phone/video later:

> “Toronto better print the whole name correctly.”

The line matters because Melissa studies language, and Marcos’s full **Key-d'Aboville** name is part of what the contract means.

### Beat 4 — Annabella Nicki at Temple

Annabella is not physically in the signing room either. That autumn, she is attending **Temple University**, following Rachel Darcy’s footsteps through a serious university dance path.

Rachel will call her from Toronto with the news, and that call becomes its own scene.

Annabella’s response is quieter, precise, and shaped by the fact that she is also in motion away from the triplet unit. She is proud of Marcos, but she understands the cost of becoming separate.

Possible line over phone/video later:

> “He kept the whole name, right?”

The line matters because Annabella sees identity in posture, shape, and the refusal to make oneself smaller.

### Beat 5 — Rachel Signs / Co-Signs

Because Marcos is seventeen, Rachel Darcy has to sign / co-sign the contract.

Marcos may sign where he is allowed to sign, but Rachel's signature is the legal threshold. The moment should not be overblown. It is a hand moving across paper. But everyone in the room understands that Rachel's hand is helping move her son's whole life.

His name still matters:

> **Marcos Eduarte Key-d'Aboville**

The full name goes onto the contract before it ever goes onto a jersey. Rachel signs beside or beneath that name, knowing she is authorizing a future that will take him away from ordinary childhood.

### Beat 6 — The Immediate Aftermath

There may be congratulations from the Blue Jays representatives. There may be handshakes, folders, schedules, first instructions, talk of medical evaluation, arm testing, development plans, and reporting dates.

Marcos hears some of it. Rachel hears all of it, because her signature has made her responsible in a different way.

The parts that mean leaving will later be heard by Melissa in Edinburgh and Annabella at Temple.

### Beat 7 — Marcos and Rachel

After the official moment, the smaller private beat is between Marcos and Rachel.

Melissa and Annabella cannot hug him from where they are. Rachel can.

Rachel does not make the moment smaller by pretending the distance from his sisters does not hurt.

She tells him that she will call them.

Marcos nods because he cannot yet say how much he needs that.

### Beat 8 — Rachel Begins Calling the Triplets

Rachel steps into the next role: telling the wider family, beginning with the triplet sisters who are not in the room.

She calls Melissa in Edinburgh.

She calls Annabella at Temple.

The calls ripple outward from the room where the signing just happened.

Marcos remains nearby or just outside the room, overloaded by the signatures, the Blue Jays representatives, the development plans, and the knowledge that his life has changed before he has even had time to feel it.

### Beat 9 — The Pull Toward the Park

After the contract and the first family calls, Marcos becomes overloaded by the room.

He needs air.

This beat should point toward the next major same-day event: Marcos leaving the signing-day pressure and going to Clarence Park, where he will find **Halcyon**.

The scene does not need to include the full Halcyon meeting, but it should establish the emotional reason he ends up in the park: he is not celebrating loudly; he is trying to breathe.

### Beat 10 — Final Image

The final image is simple:

Marcos’s contract on the table with Rachel Darcy’s signature on it. Rachel’s phone in her hand. Melissa in Edinburgh waiting for or receiving the call. Annabella at Temple waiting for or receiving the call.

The Blue Jays future has begun, but the original triplet shape is no longer contained in one room. It now stretches across Toronto, Edinburgh, and Philadelphia.
