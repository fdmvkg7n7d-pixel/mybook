# Scene Outline: Halcyon Finds the Lump — Marcos Eduarte’s Stage I Testicular Cancer

## Overview

- **Characters:** Marcos Eduarte Key-d'Aboville, Halcyon, Rachel Darcy Key, Blue Jays team physician, urologic oncologist, Blue Jays player development / coaching staff.
- **Location:** Toronto / Blue Jays medical facilities / hospital or urologic oncology clinic.
- **Timing:** The second year of Marcos Eduarte and Halcyon’s relationship.
- **Relationship Placement:** Two years before Halcyon becomes pregnant with the triplets Alessandrine Ariana, Kaisa Kalyca, and Felipe Hephaestus.
- **Medical Canon:** Stage I testicular cancer, left testicle, germ cell tumor.
- **Treatment Canon:** Surgery followed by chemotherapy.
- **Baseball Impact:** Marcos is away from baseball for six months.
- **Public Canon:** A couple of weeks after the diagnosis, once Marcos Eduarte and Halcyon have moved through the first shock, the Toronto Blue Jays tell the fans the truth about his condition.
- **Legacy Canon:** After Marcos Eduarte is all better, he and Halcyon start the **Key-d'Aboville Foundation** for testicular cancer.
- **Future Foundation Canon:** After Halcyon and Jacián Damaskinos die, Marcos decides the foundation should split into two halves: testicular cancer and deaths of pregnant mothers.
- **Status:** Revised scene outline.

---

## Core Situation

- In the second year of their relationship, Halcyon finds a tiny lump in Marcos Eduarte’s left testicle.
- The moment is private, intimate, and frightening, but it should not be written graphically.
- Halcyon notices because she is attentive to Marcos’s body and because she knows when something feels wrong.
- Marcos wants to dismiss it at first because it is tiny and because he is a professional athlete whose body is always being monitored for other things.
- Halcyon does not let him dismiss it.
- The diagnosis is **Stage I testicular cancer**.
- The cancer is a germ cell tumor.
- Marcos receives chemotherapy.
- Marcos is away from baseball for six months.
- A couple of weeks after the diagnosis, the Toronto Blue Jays tell the fans the truth about his condition.
- The cancer is treatable, but the diagnosis changes Marcos and Halcyon’s understanding of the body, privacy, masculinity, and future family.
- Marcos and Halcyon do not start the foundation while they are still in shock.
- After Marcos Eduarte is all better, he and Halcyon start the **Key-d'Aboville Foundation** for testicular cancer awareness and support.
- After Halcyon and Jacián Damaskinos die, Marcos decides the foundation should split into two halves: testicular cancer and deaths of pregnant mothers.

## Scene Beats

### Beat 1: The Second Year of the Relationship

- Marcos and Halcyon are in the second year of their relationship.
- They are no longer only the boy who brought her from Clarence Park and the girl who came to Carlisle.
- They have built trust through ordinary routines, Blue Jays schedules, quiet rooms, travel, arguments, repair, and the slow learning of each other’s bodies and fears.
- They are still young.
- They are not yet parents.
- Halcyon is not yet pregnant with the triplets.
- The triplets are still two years away.

### Beat 2: Halcyon Finds the Lump

- During a private moment of closeness, Halcyon notices a tiny lump in Marcos’s left testicle.
- The scene should remain non-graphic and focused on emotional reaction rather than physical description.
- Halcyon pauses because the difference is small but real.
- Marcos notices her pause before she says anything.
- Halcyon asks him whether he has felt it before.
- Marcos tries to minimize it at first.
- He says it is probably nothing.
- Halcyon does not accept that answer.
- Her steadiness changes the room.

### Beat 3: Marcos’s First Reaction

- Marcos becomes embarrassed before he becomes afraid.
- The location of the lump makes the fear feel private and humiliating in a way a shoulder injury or elbow strain would not.
- He is used to trainers examining his arm, back, shoulder, legs, and mechanics.
- This is different.
- He wants to put his clothes back on, change the subject, and pretend it can wait.
- Halcyon stays calm.
- She does not tease him.
- She does not make the moment sexual or dramatic.
- She treats the lump as a medical fact that needs attention.

### Beat 4: Halcyon Insists He Gets Checked

- Halcyon tells Marcos he has to call the doctor.
- Marcos says he will do it later.
- Halcyon knows what later means when fear is involved.
- She tells him that later is not good enough.
- The insistence is not controlling.
- It is care.
- She reminds him that his body is not only a baseball body.
- It is the body he lives in.
- It is the body she loves.
- It is the body that has to survive beyond the season.

### Beat 5: Calling the Blue Jays Medical Staff

- Marcos contacts the Blue Jays team physician discreetly.
- He does not tell the clubhouse.
- He does not tell reporters.
- He barely wants to tell the doctor.
- The team physician treats the report seriously and arranges urgent evaluation.
- Marcos becomes more frightened because of how quickly the medical system moves.
- Halcyon stays with him through the first step.
- She does not let the speed make him feel alone.

### Beat 6: Ultrasound and Blood Work

- Marcos undergoes a testicular ultrasound.
- Blood work is ordered, including tumor markers.
- The medical staff explain the process plainly.
- Marcos watches the professionals become careful.
- Halcyon sees the carefulness too.
- She keeps her face controlled because Marcos is watching her for clues.
- He is still trying to be the athlete who can take information without flinching.
- The fear is already under his skin.

### Beat 7: Diagnosis — Stage I Testicular Cancer

- The urologic oncologist tells Marcos and Halcyon that the findings are consistent with testicular cancer.
- It is in the left testicle.
- It appears to be Stage I.
- The doctor explains that Stage I means it appears localized and has been found early.
- The doctor explains that this is a germ cell tumor, the common origin category for testicular cancer.
- The doctor explains that testicular cancer is often highly treatable, especially when caught early.
- Marcos hears the word cancer and goes still.
- Halcyon hears Stage I and holds onto it because it is the first piece of hope.
- The diagnosis is serious, but it is not hopeless.

### Beat 8: The Treatment Plan

- The doctor explains that the first major step is surgery to remove the affected testicle.
- The doctor explains the procedure in simple, dignified language.
- The doctor explains that pathology will confirm the exact type of tumor.
- Imaging and tumor markers will help confirm staging.
- The final treatment plan includes chemotherapy.
- The doctor explains that chemotherapy is medicine used to attack cancer cells throughout the body.
- Marcos asks what this means for baseball.
- The answer is that he will be away from baseball for six months.
- The six-month absence hits him hard.
- Halcyon understands that he is hearing two losses at once: cancer and the season.

### Beat 9: Fertility and Future Family

- The medical team discusses fertility preservation before treatment.
- Marcos and Halcyon are not yet parents.
- The conversation lands heavily because the triplets are still in the future, unknown to them.
- Marcos does not know how to think about future children while still absorbing the word cancer.
- Halcyon listens carefully.
- She does not turn the fertility conversation into pressure.
- She treats it as another piece of care for a future they have not yet fully named.

### Beat 10: Telling Rachel Darcy

- Marcos tells Rachel Darcy after he and Halcyon understand the first facts.
- Rachel listens with the disciplined stillness of a mother who knows panic will not help him.
- She does not make him feel ashamed.
- She does not let him pretend the diagnosis is smaller than it is.
- Rachel asks practical questions about staging, surgery, treatment, time away from baseball, and what support he and Halcyon need.
- Halcyon is present if Marcos wants her there.
- Rachel sees that Halcyon has already stepped into the role of the person who noticed, insisted, and stayed.
- Rachel’s respect for Halcyon deepens.

### Beat 11: Six Months Away From Baseball

- Marcos has to tell the Blue Jays he will be away from baseball for six months.
- At first, the organization protects his dignity and privacy while he and Halcyon absorb the diagnosis.
- A couple of weeks after the diagnosis, once Marcos and Halcyon are ready for the truth to be public, the Toronto Blue Jays tell the fans the truth about his condition.
- The Blue Jays do not hide the cancer behind a fake injury.
- The public explanation is direct, respectful, and medically limited.
- Fans are told that Marcos Eduarte has been diagnosed with Stage I testicular cancer and will be away from baseball for treatment.
- Inside the team, the necessary people know he has a germ cell tumor and will receive chemotherapy.
- Marcos struggles with not being able to pitch.
- Baseball has been the place where his body makes sense.
- Now his body has become a medical problem he cannot train through.
- Halcyon reminds him that survival is not the same thing as weakness.

### Beat 12: Surgery

- Marcos undergoes surgery to remove the affected left testicle.
- The scene should remain medically dignified and non-graphic.
- Halcyon is there before and after.
- Rachel is also part of the support system.
- Marcos wakes sore, frightened, and changed.
- The first step is over, but the emotional meaning is only beginning.
- Halcyon does not treat him as less masculine.
- She treats him as alive.
- That matters more than he can say.

### Beat 13: Recovery With Halcyon

- During recovery, Halcyon helps him through the small humiliations and practical discomforts of healing.
- She does not smother him.
- She also does not let him isolate inside pride.
- She learns the appointment schedule, medication timing, and follow-up plan.
- Marcos slowly accepts that being cared for does not make him less himself.
- The relationship deepens because fear has entered it and did not destroy it.

### Beat 14: Chemotherapy

- Marcos receives chemotherapy after surgery.
- The chemotherapy is part of making sure the germ cell cancer is treated aggressively even at Stage I.
- The treatment makes the diagnosis feel real in a different way than surgery did.
- Marcos feels the effects in his energy, appetite, body, and sense of control.
- Halcyon stays with him through the treatment rhythm.
- She learns which days are worst and which routines help.
- Marcos hates needing care, but chemotherapy teaches him that toughness is not the same as refusing help.
- The Blue Jays keep supporting him while fans know the truth about why he is gone.

### Beat 15: The Six-Month Baseball Absence

- Marcos spends six months away from baseball.
- He misses the rhythm of the field, the bullpen, the mound, teammates, and competition.
- He also learns that Halcyon is not only present for the beautiful parts of his life.
- She is present for fear, medical waiting, body change, and the boredom of recovery.
- The absence from baseball becomes a test of identity.
- Marcos has to discover who he is when he cannot pitch.
- Halcyon helps him stay connected to life without letting baseball be the only proof that he is alive.

### Beat 16: The Foundation Begins as an Idea

- The foundation does not begin immediately.
- For the first weeks after the diagnosis, Marcos and Halcyon are in shock.
- They can only move through surgery, chemotherapy planning, baseball absence, fear, and the first facts.
- A couple of weeks after the diagnosis, the Blue Jays tell the public the truth about his condition.
- Even after the public statement, Marcos and Halcyon are not ready to build a foundation yet.
- The foundation idea begins later, after treatment and recovery have given them enough distance to think about other young men who might ignore a lump because of embarrassment.
- Halcyon says the tiny lump could have been missed if she had not said something.
- Marcos realizes how easily he might have waited.
- They talk about athletes, pride, privacy, and the danger of silence.
- The idea for a foundation begins here.
- It is not a publicity move.
- It grows from the private knowledge that embarrassment could have cost him his life.

### Beat 17: Marcos and Halcyon Start the Key-d'Aboville Foundation

- After Marcos Eduarte is all better, he and Halcyon start the **Key-d'Aboville Foundation** for testicular cancer awareness and support.
- The foundation uses Marcos Eduarte’s last name because the Key-d'Aboville name is already public through baseball.
- The foundation focuses on early detection, medical follow-through, fertility preservation, chemotherapy and treatment support, and reducing shame around talking about testicular health.
- The foundation especially speaks to young men, athletes, and families who do not know what symptoms to take seriously.
- Halcyon’s role is central.
- She is the one who found the tiny lump and insisted he get checked.
- Marcos is the public baseball name.
- Halcyon is the reason the story started early enough to save him.
- The foundation begins as a testicular cancer foundation only.
- It will not split into two halves until after Halcyon and Jacián Damaskinos die.

### Future Foundation Split After Halcyon and Jacián Damaskinos Die

- After Halcyon and Jacián Damaskinos die, Marcos revisits the foundation’s purpose.
- He decides the Key-d'Aboville Foundation should split into two halves.
- One half remains focused on testicular cancer.
- The other half focuses on deaths of pregnant mothers and childbirth-related maternal loss.
- The change keeps Halcyon inside the foundation’s future without erasing the original reason it began.
- The foundation becomes a record of both survivals and losses: Marcos’s Stage I germ cell testicular cancer, and Halcyon’s death as a pregnant mother during childbirth.

### Beat 18: Final Image

- The final image is Marcos and Halcyon after diagnosis, surgery, chemotherapy, recovery, and the first Key-d'Aboville Foundation discussions once he is all better.
- They are not yet parents.
- They do not know that in two years Halcyon will be pregnant with the triplets.
- They do not know how much joy and grief still waits ahead of them.
- What they know is that a tiny lump in the left testicle changed their lives.
- What they choose is to make sure other people do not ignore the same warning.
