# Scene Outline: Marcos Eduarte’s Sisters Call After the Testicular Cancer News

## Overview

- **Characters:** Marcos Eduarte Key-d'Aboville, Halcyon, Rachel Darcy Key, Larissa Leola, Kella Nakeisha, Armella Beatrice, Melissa Lizette, Annabella Nicki, Armando Cortez Junior referenced.
- **Location:** Marcos and Halcyon’s Toronto penthouse / the sisters’ separate locations / Armando’s separate life.
- **Timing:** A couple of weeks after Marcos Eduarte’s Stage I germ cell testicular cancer diagnosis, after the Toronto Blue Jays tell fans the truth about his condition.
- **Status:** New scene outline.

---

## Placement

- This scene follows **marcos-eduarte-testicular-cancer-diagnosis-scene-outline.md**.
- Marcos has already been diagnosed with Stage I germ cell testicular cancer in the left testicle.
- Halcyon found the tiny lump and insisted he get checked.
- Marcos has had or is preparing for surgery and chemotherapy.
- Marcos will be away from baseball for six months.
- The Toronto Blue Jays have now told the fans the truth about his condition in a respectful, medically limited way.
- The public statement causes Marcos’s sisters to call him.
- Rachel Darcy also reaches for him as his mother.
- Armando Cortez Junior does not meaningfully respond because he is in his own world and does not care about the family.

---

## Core Situation

- Once the Blue Jays statement goes public, Marcos’s private diagnosis becomes known beyond the tightest family circle.
- His five sisters call him because they love him and because the word cancer lands hard.
- Rachel Darcy calls or comes to him too, even though she already knows, because public news makes the fear feel new again.
- Halcyon stays close while the calls come in.
- Marcos is grateful, overwhelmed, embarrassed, frightened, and exhausted.
- Armando Cortez Junior’s lack of care becomes another quiet wound in the family pattern.

---

## Scene Beats

### Beat 1: The Blue Jays Statement Goes Public

- The Toronto Blue Jays release a respectful statement about Marcos Eduarte’s condition.
- The statement tells fans the truth: Marcos has been diagnosed with Stage I testicular cancer and will be away from baseball for treatment.
- The statement does not give unnecessary private details.
- It does not turn his body into spectacle.
- It explains the six-month absence without hiding the cancer behind a fake injury.
- Marcos reads the statement after it is released.
- Halcyon reads it with him.
- Seeing the truth in public language makes the diagnosis feel newly real.

### Beat 2: Marcos and Halcyon in the Penthouse

- Marcos and Halcyon are in the Toronto penthouse when the calls begin.
- They are still in the first weeks after diagnosis, still shocked, and not yet ready to build the foundation.
- The penthouse is close to the stadium, but baseball feels far away.
- Marcos’s phone starts lighting up.
- Halcyon sees his reaction before he says anything.
- He is not sure he can answer everyone.
- Halcyon tells him he does not have to perform strength for his sisters.
- He can answer as himself.

### Beat 3: Rachel Darcy Reaches Him

- Rachel Darcy reaches Marcos as his mother, not as a manager of crisis.
- She already knows the diagnosis, but the public statement has shifted the emotional landscape.
- She checks on how he feels now that fans know.
- She asks whether the Blue Jays handled the statement correctly.
- She asks whether he wants her to come over or whether he wants space.
- Marcos does not know what he wants.
- Rachel accepts that answer.
- She tells him that cancer becoming public does not make his body public property.
- That sentence helps him breathe.

### Beat 4: Melissa Lizette Calls

- Melissa Lizette calls with visible emotion.
- She tries to sound bright at first and fails.
- She tells Marcos she loves him.
- She tells him she is angry that this is happening to him.
- She asks what he needs and then immediately admits she does not know what to ask.
- Marcos is relieved by her honesty.
- Melissa does not make jokes about the body part.
- She treats the diagnosis as cancer, fear, and family.
- Marcos hears the triplet bond underneath every pause.

### Beat 5: Annabella Nicki Calls

- Annabella Nicki calls with quieter precision.
- She has read the Blue Jays statement carefully.
- She noticed what the team said and what they protected.
- She tells Marcos the statement was respectful.
- She asks if he is in pain.
- She asks what the next day looks like, not the whole future.
- Marcos appreciates the smaller question.
- Annabella does not ask him to explain everything at once.
- She gives him one manageable frame.

### Beat 6: Larissa Leola Calls

- Larissa Leola calls from far away.
- The distance makes her upset because she cannot simply appear at the penthouse.
- She tells Marcos she hates being far from him.
- She asks whether Rachel is with him.
- She asks whether Halcyon is okay too.
- Marcos realizes that Larissa understands Halcyon is carrying the diagnosis with him.
- Larissa tells him that six months away from baseball is not the same as losing himself.
- Marcos wants to believe her.

### Beat 7: Kella Nakeisha Calls

- Kella Nakeisha calls and goes straight to the emotional truth.
- She says the word cancer plainly.
- She does not whisper around it.
- She asks if he is scared.
- Marcos almost says no, then stops.
- He tells her yes.
- Kella accepts the answer without flinching.
- She reminds him that fear is not a betrayal of strength.
- That lands because Marcos has been trying to keep strength and fear in separate rooms.

### Beat 8: Armella Beatrice Calls

- Armella Beatrice calls with the visual memory of the public statement already forming in her mind.
- She says the Blue Jays statement made his absence visible but did not make it ugly.
- She asks whether he wants any family photographs kept away from media circulation for now.
- She is thinking like a photographer and a sister.
- Marcos is moved that she understands image can protect or expose.
- Armella tells him that if he ever wants to use his image for awareness, it should be his choice.
- This quietly foreshadows the later Key-d'Aboville Foundation.

### Beat 9: Halcyon Watches the Calls

- Halcyon watches Marcos move through the calls.
- She sees how each sister knows a different part of him.
- Melissa reaches the triplet nerve.
- Annabella gives shape.
- Larissa names distance.
- Kella names fear.
- Armella names image and protection.
- Halcyon understands more fully that loving Marcos means entering a family network that will not stay politely distant during crisis.
- She is relieved by it and overwhelmed by it.

### Beat 10: Armando Cortez Junior Does Not Call

- Armando Cortez Junior does not call.
- He is in his own world.
- He does not care about the family in the way the others do.
- If he sees the news, he treats it as background noise.
- If someone messages him, he ignores it, delays, or responds with something shallow and useless.
- His absence is not logistical.
- His absence is emotional.
- Marcos may not say much about it, but the silence is noticed.
- Rachel notices it too.
- It confirms something painful about Armando without requiring a confrontation in this scene.

### Beat 11: Marcos’s Exhaustion After the Calls

- After the calls, Marcos is exhausted.
- He has heard love in five different voices.
- He has also heard the cancer become real again each time someone says it or avoids saying it.
- Halcyon sits with him in the quiet afterward.
- He admits he did not know being loved could be this tiring.
- Halcyon understands.
- They are still a couple in shock.
- They are not yet ready to turn the diagnosis into a foundation.
- For now, surviving the calls is enough.

### Beat 12: Rachel’s Second Check-In

- Rachel checks in again after the sister calls or later that night.
- She asks whether he is done answering the phone for the day.
- Marcos says yes.
- Rachel tells him he is allowed to stop.
- She reminds him that public truth does not mean unlimited access.
- Marcos takes that boundary seriously.
- Halcyon is grateful because she can see he needed permission to stop being available.

### Beat 13: Final Image

- The final image is Marcos and Halcyon in the penthouse after the calls.
- The phone is face down or silenced.
- The Blue Jays statement is public now.
- His sisters have reached him.
- Rachel has anchored him.
- Armando has not come through.
- Marcos is still sick, still frightened, and still loved.
- The foundation will come later, after he is all better.
- Tonight, the only task is to keep breathing through the truth being known.

## Emotional Notes

- The calls should feel loving but exhausting.
- Each sister should reach a different aspect of Marcos.
- Rachel’s role is to help him hold boundaries after the diagnosis becomes public.
- Halcyon should remain present and central, because she found the lump and is living the shock with him.
- Armando’s lack of response should feel cold, not comic.
- The foundation should not begin here.
- This scene happens after the public statement, but before recovery and before the Key-d'Aboville Foundation is created.
