# SCENE OUTLINE: RACHEL CALLS ARMANDO ABOUT MARCOS'S CONTRACT

## Placement

**When:** The same day Marcos Eduarte signs his professional contract with the Toronto Blue Jays.  
**Where:** Rachel's location TBD / Armando Cortez Junior in Bangkok.  
**Characters:** Rachel Darcy Key, Armando Cortez Junior Key-d'Aboville, Amporn. Marcos Eduarte is present emotionally but not necessarily in the call.  
**Context:** Marcos has signed with the Toronto Blue Jays out of high school. Rachel begins telling the family. When she calls Armando, he is in Bangkok. Rachel Darcy does **not** know about Amporn. Armando and Amporn are in bed when the call comes. Amporn walks into the bathroom while Armando steps outside onto the balcony to take Rachel's call. When the call ends, Armando follows Amporn into the bathroom. This should remain non-graphic and unsettling rather than eroticized.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene establishes:

- Rachel sharing Marcos's contract news with one of the older siblings.
- Armando Cortez Junior's adult life in Bangkok.
- Amporn as part of Armando's immediate world.
- The reality that the seven siblings are now geographically scattered.
- Armando's emotional indifference toward Marcos despite the importance of the milestone.
- Early emotional setup for Armando being unable to attend Marcos's first baseball game / first Blue Jays start.

---

## Emotional Core

**Family news crossing distance.**

This is a scene about Armando not caring about Marcos Eduarte. Rachel calls with enormous news, but Armando is not down the hall, not in Los Angeles, not even in North America. He is in Bangkok, in the middle of life with Amporn, and Marcos's milestone lands as an interruption rather than a joy.

The call has Rachel's love in it, but Armando does not return that feeling toward Marcos. There is delay, background noise, time-zone dislocation, and the ache of Rachel realizing one of her children does not care about another the way she wishes he did.

---

## Predatory Framing Note

Armando Cortez Junior is a predator, but Rachel Darcy does **not** know that during this call. She also does **not** know about Amporn. Armando is secretive: when Rachel calls, he is in bed with Amporn. Amporn walks into the bathroom, and Armando steps outside onto the balcony before answering or continuing the call. The scene can let the reader feel that something is wrong through his secrecy, dismissiveness, evasiveness, and the way Amporn is hidden from Rachel — but Rachel should not identify him as predatory yet. The writing should make the danger clear to the long arc without graphic detail or eroticizing it.

---

## Tone

Cool, interrupted, emotionally uncomfortable. To Rachel, Armando registers as distant, evasive, and unpleasant rather than openly predatory. To the reader/outline, he is a creep and a predator, but his secrecy keeps Rachel from knowing the full truth. The scene should show that Armando is not simply busy — he is concealing Amporn, stepping out onto the balcony so Rachel cannot know who is inside. He is detached from Marcos, and Marcos's milestone does not reach him the way Rachel hopes it will.

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — Rachel After the Signing

Rachel has just come through the emotional intensity of Marcos signing with the Toronto Blue Jays.

Her son is officially a professional baseball player. He is not going to university. He is entering the Blue Jays system as a starting pitcher.

Rachel begins calling the family.

### Beat 2 — The Call to Bangkok

Rachel calls Armando Cortez Junior.

The distance matters. The phone is not simply a device; it is the only bridge between Rachel and her adult son in Bangkok.

Depending on the time difference, the call may come at an inconvenient hour or during the middle of Armando's day.

### Beat 3 — Armando Is In Bed With Amporn

Armando is in bed with Amporn when Rachel calls. This should be stated non-graphically and without erotic detail. Rachel does not know Amporn exists.

The phone interrupts him. Armando looks at the caller ID and sees Rachel. Amporn gets up and walks into the bathroom. Armando steps outside onto the balcony before taking or continuing the call. The balcony becomes part of his secrecy: Bangkok air, city noise, glass door behind him, Amporn hidden inside and out of Rachel's awareness.

The scene should let him feel secretive and emotionally off. The reader may understand or later learn that the secrecy is predatory, but Rachel does not know that yet.

Amporn's hidden presence should make Armando's adult life feel real while also emphasizing that he prioritizes secrecy and his own gratification over emotional investment in Marcos. There should be an uncomfortable edge to Armando here, but Rachel should interpret it as distance, evasiveness, or distraction — not yet as proof that he is a predator.

### Beat 4 — Armando Answers or Calls Back

Option A: Armando answers immediately, but the call is interrupted by the reality around him.

Option B: Armando misses the first call and calls Rachel back quickly.

Either version should preserve the emotional truth: he is not emotionally available, and his detachment becomes impossible for Rachel to miss.

### Beat 5 — Rachel Tells Him

Rachel tells him plainly:

> "Marcos signed. Toronto. The Blue Jays."

Armando goes quiet for a beat.

He knows what this means. Marcos is not just talented anymore. Marcos is gone into the machine of professional baseball.

### Beat 6 — Armando's Response

Armando is not truly proud in the way Rachel needs him to be. Even his polite words feel slightly wrong. The reader/outline knows there is a predatory secrecy beneath him, but Rachel only hears detachment, impatience, and something withheld. If he says the correct words, they come through distance and detachment.

Possible response:

> "He actually did it."

Rachel does not hear Amporn clearly and does not know Amporn is there. If there is any sound from inside, Armando controls it by closing the balcony door or lowering his voice.

Amporn remains hidden from Rachel in this scene. The reader may know Amporn is inside; Rachel does not.

### Beat 7 — The First Game Problem Is Foreshadowed

Rachel may mention that there will be games, travel, milestones.

Armando already knows Bangkok will make everything difficult.

He does not promise what he cannot guarantee.

> "Tell him congratulations. I can't really talk right now."

This is more dismissive than affectionate, even if he makes it sound polite.

### Beat 8 — Ending the Call

Rachel ends the call with mixed feelings.

She has told her son. He knows. But Rachel has to face that knowing is not the same as caring.

Armando ends the call, goes back inside, and follows Amporn into the bathroom. Rachel returns to Marcos and the Blue Jays signing day, unaware of what Armando stepped away from and what he returns to.

The scene ends with Rachel understanding that the family is not only separated by geography. Some bonds are weaker than she wanted to believe.
