## ACTIVITIES

### River Way Ranch Camp — Armando Cortez Junior's Schedule

Armando Cortez Junior attends **River Way Ranch Camp** (Sanger, California), the premier co-ed overnight summer camp in the foothills of Sequoia/Kings Canyon National Parks, alongside **Larissa Leola**, **Kella Nakeisha**, and **Armella Beatrice**. Armando and Kella Nakeisha are paired as a camp pair — the second-born singleton and one of the twins, crossing birth-order lines. At camp, he is known as Kella's big brother — not the boy with the dark arc, not the middle singleton.

**Armando's Majors (3): wakeboarding, rock climbing, mini-bikes**

- **Wakeboarding (Major)** — Speed on the water. Armando, the second-born, the boy, the kid who wants edges and velocity, takes the board and the tow rope and the lake. Where Larissa cuts the wake on skis, Armando catches air. It is the closest thing to flight, and he is good at it.
- **Rock climbing (Major)** — Vertical. Alone on the wall. The harness, the chalk, the problem-solving of finding the next hold. In a family full of dancers and gymnasts who move in groups and pairs and trios, rock climbing is Armando's solo act — the sport where no one else can climb for you. It builds the same stillness martial arts will later build, at a different age, with a different kind of risk.
- **Mini-bikes (Major)** — Speed on dirt. The internal combustion version of everything Armando loves: throttle, control, the line through the turn. He is the only one of the four on mini-bikes. It is his signature at River Way — the thing the counselors know him for.

**Armando's Minors (3): swimming, horseback riding, archery**

- **Swimming (Minor)** — The family constant. Shared with Larissa, Kella, and Armella. The lake, the dock, the four eldest in the water.
- **Horseback riding (Minor)** — He rides, but it's not his passion. Connects him to his aunt Fallon Glenda, and to Larissa and Kella (both of whom major in different riding styles). For Armando, the horse is not the point; the trail is.
- **Archery (Minor)** — Solitary. Precise. The bow, the breath, the release. The sport where you compete against yourself. Armando is the only one of the four in archery — a quiet, deliberate skill that balances the speed of wakeboarding and mini-bikes.

**Not taking:** waterskiing, ropes course, photography, theater, gymnastics, arts & crafts (among others). Waterskiing and ropes course belong to Larissa — the eldest's territory. Theater belongs to the twins. He leaves them to it.

### River Way — The Eldest Four's Territory

River Way is where the four eldest become their own unit — paired across birth-order lines (Larissa + Armella, Armando + Kella), remixed from the groupings home imposes. At the same time, the triplets are across the country at French Woods. The split is deliberate: each group gets a summer place that is theirs alone.

For Armando, River Way is complicated. He is the boy with a dark arc coming — the open thread in his profile, the undetermined trouble around age ~14 — but at camp, he is simply Kella's big brother, the kid on the mini-bike, the one who can climb anything. Camp does not know what's coming. Neither does he.

**The four's shared activity:** Swimming (Minor for all).

Like everyone in this family, Armando Cortez Junior is proof of the same conviction: that family is not defined by biology alone, that love is what makes a parent, and that *"no one in this family does anything alone."*

Whether the loss shapes the trouble or the trouble is its own thing, the story will decide.
