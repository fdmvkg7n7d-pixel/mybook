# MARCOS EDUARTE BASEBALL FUTURE ARC

## Placement

**Character:** Marcos Eduarte Key-d'Aboville  
**Parents:** Rachel Darcy Key and Antoine d'Aboville  
**Sibling Position:** Sixth-born of seven; one of the triplets with Melissa Lizette and Annabella Nicki  
**Born:** ~2061  
**Core Dream:** To become a professional baseball player  
**Primary Sport Identity:** Baseball player in a family dominated by dance, gymnastics, swimming, medicine, and performance  
**Professional Organization:** Toronto Blue Jays

## Core Concept

Marcos Eduarte is the Key-d'Aboville child whose dream points away from the family’s expected languages.

His sisters move through dance, gymnastics, swimming, and the arts. Rachel Darcy carries the body-memory of dance. Jessica Isabella carries the family’s gymnastics inheritance. Sabrina Bryna’s line carries discipline and stillness. Fallon Glenda’s world touches horses and endurance. But Marcos looks at a baseball diamond and sees the place where his body makes the most sense.

He is not rejecting the family by choosing baseball. He is translating them.

Ballet gives him balance. Gymnastics gives him explosive body control. Swimming gives him breath and endurance. Martial arts gives him stillness before movement. The Key family’s physical inheritance does not disappear in Marcos — it becomes baseball.

The name on the jersey matters:

> **KEY-D'ABOVILLE**

It is long. It is unusual. It does not fit neatly into the ordinary space across a player’s shoulders. That is exactly why Marcos keeps it.

He is Rachel’s son. He is Antoine’s son. He is Jessica’s biological child by the family’s gift between sisters. He is the boy who carries all of that into the most public thing he owns: his name.

## Proposed Baseball Identity

### Primary Position

**Pitcher.**

This is the strongest fit for Marcos because it uses everything his body has learned:

- ballet balance and repeatable mechanics
- gymnastics body control and spatial awareness
- swimming endurance and breath discipline
- martial arts stillness before explosive movement
- horseback riding balance, patience, and feel
- the emotional independence of being the triplet who can stand alone on the mound

Pitching gives Marcos a place where precision, rhythm, and pressure all meet. He is not simply throwing hard; he is arranging motion. Every pitch begins in stillness, moves through a practiced sequence, and ends with consequences he has to accept.

The mound suits him because it is solitary without being disconnected. His sisters, his mother, and his family are still part of him — but when he pitches, the responsibility is his alone.

### Secondary Role

**Starting Pitcher.**

Marcos is a starter. Starting gives him the rhythm, structure, and long-game strategy that suit him best. He is built for preparation, pacing, pitch sequencing, and returning to the mound inning after inning after something goes wrong.

During development, the Blue Jays may still protect his arm with pitch counts and carefully managed innings, but his role and identity are clear from the beginning: he is being developed as a starting pitcher, not converted into a reliever.

### Pitching / Throwing

**Throws:** Right [TBD — recommended]  
**Bats:** [TBD; secondary because he is a pitcher]

Suggested direction: Marcos becomes a **right-handed pitcher**, giving him a clean, traditional pitching profile while letting his distinctiveness come from movement quality, command, and the Key-d'Aboville name rather than artificial perfection.

### Playing Style

Marcos is not the biggest player on the field. He is not the loudest. He is not the one who performs swagger easily.

He wins through:

- command
- repeatable mechanics
- body discipline
- pitch sequencing
- stamina across multiple innings
- emotional control under pressure
- relentless repetition
- the ability to study hitters and patterns over several at-bats

He is a starting pitcher coaches trust because he can carry pressure across an entire game without panicking.

## Childhood Foundation

### Ages 4–6 — Before and After Antoine’s Death

Marcos’s earliest relationship with baseball begins as something physical before it becomes ambition.

He likes:

- the sound of a ball hitting leather
- the smell of grass and glove oil
- the shape of the diamond
- the clean order of bases
- the fact that everyone has a place to stand

Antoine dies when Marcos is about five. This becomes a foundational fracture in Marcos’s life.

At five, he does not grieve like an adult. He grieves through fragments:

- a hand closing around his small hand
- a voice behind him at a practice field
- a photograph he stares at too long
- older siblings going quiet when Antoine is mentioned
- Rachel’s face changing when someone says his father’s name

Baseball becomes one of the places where Marcos can carry a father he barely remembers. Not because Antoine must have been a baseball man, but because the field gives Marcos somewhere to put feelings that do not have language yet.

### Ages 7–11 — French Woods and the Triplet Body

At French Woods, Marcos is still one of the three.

He takes ballet with Melissa and Annabella because that is their shared language. He swims with them because that is the family constant. But baseball becomes the place where he steps away from the triplet unit without breaking it.

Important emotional contradiction:

- He loves being one of the triplets.
- He also needs something that belongs only to him.

Baseball gives him that.

### Ages 12–17 — Sports & Arts Center Shift

At twelve, Marcos begins spending more time at the French Woods Sports & Arts Center side.

This is a major turning point. It is not a rejection of Melissa and Annabella. It is the first time he lets his own ambition change the physical map of his summer.

He still crosses back for shared time with his sisters. He still takes ballet when possible. He still swims. But his days become more baseball-centered:

- batting practice
- pitching mechanics
- strength and conditioning
- arm care and conditioning
- games
- pressure situations
- failure in front of other athletes

He begins to understand that loving baseball is not the same as being chosen by baseball.

## Teen Competitive Arc

### The Problem of the Key Name

As Marcos gets older, coaches, parents, and scouts know who his family is.

The Key family wealth creates suspicion around him:

- Did he get this private coach because of money?
- Did he get the roster spot because of his name?
- Is he serious, or is this another rich-family hobby?

Marcos hates this.

He cannot make the money disappear. He cannot make his name ordinary. So he becomes obsessive about earning visible proof.

He wants the stat line to answer for him.

### High School Years

Marcos attends **IMG Academy** in Bradenton, Florida from grades 6–12, boarding there with Melissa Lizette and Annabella Nicki. IMG becomes his serious baseball environment while the girls pursue private gymnastics training off-campus.

His high-school arc should include:

1. **Freshman Year:** Physically gifted but not yet dominant. Coaches notice his movement quality, repeatable mechanics, and unusual calm on the mound.
2. **Sophomore Year:** He begins to separate himself as a pitcher. His command is promising but inconsistent.
3. **Junior Year:** Major growth year. He becomes a legitimate pitching prospect.
4. **Senior Year:** Scouts are present. The family has to confront whether baseball is still a dream or now a life path.

### The Triplet Tension

Melissa and Annabella remain central.

They do not lose Marcos to baseball, but they do have to share him with it.

Possible tension beat:

- Marcos misses a triplet tradition because of a tournament.
- Melissa is hurt but hides it under performance energy.
- Annabella notices before anyone else and says something precise but quiet.
- Marcos realizes ambition has costs even before he becomes professional.

This is important because Marcos should not become a generic sports hero. He is still a triplet. His ambition affects the people who share his original identity.

## Draft Decision — No University

### Central Question

Does Marcos attend university first, or does he go directly into professional baseball?

### Confirmed Arc

**Marcos does not attend any university.**

He is drafted out of high school and signs. There is no college bridge, no UCLA, no Stanford, no campus chapter where he gets to grow slowly under the protection of a university program. His baseball dream becomes real immediately, and that choice defines the shape of his adulthood.

This makes Marcos’s arc sharper and more dangerous:

- he enters professional baseball very young;
- he leaves the ordinary rhythm of family life earlier than Rachel expected;
- he has to mature inside buses, clubhouses, training facilities, and minor-league pressure;
- he loses the soft landing that university might have given him;
- he has to prove that skipping university was not recklessness, but commitment.

Rachel may struggle with this choice. Education matters in the Key family, and she wants him protected. But Marcos knows that baseball timing is unforgiving. If a professional organization offers him the path, he takes it.

His decision is not framed as anti-education. It is framed as single-minded vocation.

> “If I wait, I’m not being safer. I’m just waiting.”

## Early Professional Arc

### Signing Out of High School

Marcos signs after being drafted out of high school. Because he is seventeen, Rachel Darcy has to sign / co-sign the contract as his mother/legal guardian.

Suggested draft range: not first overall, not impossibly perfect. A more grounded and satisfying arc is:

- late first round, competitive balance round, or second round;
- high enough to validate the dream;
- low enough that he still has to fight for everything.

The signing moment is both triumph and rupture. He is still seventeen, still one of Rachel’s children, still one of the triplets — and Rachel’s signature is required before his life can belong to the Toronto Blue Jays’ professional baseball calendar.

### Rookie Ball / Development Complex

Instead of a freshman year, Marcos enters the Blue Jays’ rookie/development system. His first professional year is not designed to prove how fast he can climb; it is designed to test and understand his arm.

This is his real education.

He learns:

- how lonely professional ambition can be;
- how little family money matters once he is alone on the mound;
- how to live away from Melissa and Annabella;
- how to fail in front of coaches who are paid to evaluate him;
- how to be treated as an investment rather than a child;
- how to protect the parts of himself that are not baseball.
- how to think like a starter: not one pitch, not one inning, but the whole game.


### Blue Jays Farm Progression — One, Two, One, Three, Four, Five

Marcos's professional path through the Blue Jays system follows a deliberate **1-2-1-3-4-5** structure:

1. **Dunedin Blue Jays** — first-year arm testing and controlled professional introduction at the Blue Jays’ Single-A affiliate in Dunedin, Florida.
2. **Vancouver Canadians** — first High-A challenge once the organization believes his arm can handle more competitive innings.
1. **Return to the Dunedin Blue Jays** — not a failure, but a controlled reset/refinement period with the Single-A team where the Blue Jays protect his arm, adjust mechanics, monitor workload, and make sure his delivery remains healthy.
3. **New Hampshire Fisher Cats** — Double-A test, where hitters are advanced enough to expose whether he can truly start.
4. **Buffalo Bisons** — Triple-A finishing stage, major-league-adjacent pressure, travel, scouting reports, and rotation discipline.
5. **Toronto Blue Jays** — major-league debut as a starting pitcher.

The important point is that the Blue Jays do **not** rush him. Because Marcos is drafted out of high school and does not attend university, the organization treats his first year as an arm-evaluation year. They want to learn what his body can safely handle before they ask him to carry a full professional starter's workload.

This first year includes:

- pitch counts;
- innings limits;
- biomechanical review;
- bullpen monitoring;
- recovery tracking;
- shoulder and elbow strength testing;
- delivery consistency work;
- careful observation of how his ballet-trained balance helps or complicates his pitching mechanics.

For Marcos, this is frustrating because he wants to compete immediately. For the Blue Jays, it is necessary. They are not only developing a pitcher; they are protecting a possible future starter.

Note: Dunedin is not just a training location here — Marcos is with the **Dunedin Blue Jays**, Toronto’s Single-A affiliate, while the organization also uses its Dunedin facilities to monitor and protect his arm.

### First Full Minor-League Season

He is not handed anything.

He has to sit sometimes. He has to throw limited innings. He has to accept pitch counts, bullpen sessions, mechanical corrections, and days when his command disappears. He has to learn that being drafted does not mean being chosen forever.

This humbles him in a way wealth never could.

### Breakthrough Season

His command and mound presence become his signatures. Coaches and commentators begin to talk about his mechanics, sequencing, and calm under pressure. His velocity improves, but it is not effortless.

A key mentor could be a minor-league coach who sees the ballet in his delivery and does not mock it.

### The Jersey Name Becomes Public

As Marcos rises through the system, reporters ask whether he would shorten his name.

Marcos says no.

> “That’s my name.”

## Professional Arc

### Draft

Marcos is drafted out of high school by the **Toronto Blue Jays** and signs rather than attending university. The Blue Jays draft him as a **starting pitcher**, with the intention of developing him through their system as part of a future rotation.

### Minor Leagues

The minor leagues are essential.

This is where Marcos learns that professional baseball is not romantic.

He faces:

- long bus rides
- slumps
- injuries
- loneliness
- pressure to change his delivery
- resentment from players who assume he has never struggled
- the first real distance from Melissa and Annabella
- the grief of Antoine returning in unexpected ways during milestones

He also discovers the parts of himself that are not glamorous but durable.

### Major League Debut — Toronto Blue Jays

Marcos eventually reaches the major leagues with the **Toronto Blue Jays**.

The debut should be emotionally huge but written quietly.

Rachel is there. Jessica is there if possible. Melissa and Annabella are there. Some siblings are there. **Larissa Leola and Armando Cortez Junior cannot make it**: Larissa is living in **Queensland**, and Armando is living in **Bangkok**. Larissa’s absence is distance and logistics. Armando’s absence is colder: he does not care about Marcos Eduarte, and he is a creep and a predator. Terri Anne is there if the timeline allows. Antoine is not there, and that absence is the emotional center.

The camera catches the back of his Blue Jays jersey:

> **KEY-D'ABOVILLE**

For the family, it is not just a sports moment. It is Rachel’s motherhood made visible, Antoine’s name carried forward, Jessica’s gift made flesh, and Marcos’s own work finally undeniable.

## Halcyon — The Park Meeting After the Signing

### Placement

This relationship begins on the same day Marcos signs his professional contract with the **Toronto Blue Jays** out of high school.

He is entering professional baseball, suddenly carrying money, attention, expectation, and the pressure of being a young drafted starting pitcher. At the same time, he meets **Halcyon**, who is **seventeen**, in Clarence Park near the Blue Jays stadium in Toronto.

Halcyon is living in Clarence Park when Marcos first meets her.

### Important Framing

Halcyon is seventeen and should not be written as a prize, a rescue project, or proof that Marcos is good. She is a young person with her own history, pride, survival skills, boundaries, and reasons for being where she is.

Marcos does not "save" her by becoming a baseball player with a contract. The relationship should begin with recognition, not rescue.

He sees her because he is observant. She notices him because he keeps returning to the same place, at the same time, with the same quiet habits. Their connection grows slowly.

### Why This Matters for Marcos

This begins at the exact moment when Marcos could become consumed by baseball, money, and public identity.

Halcyon meets him before he is a major leaguer, before the Blue Jays jersey payoff, before the public knows the name **KEY-D'ABOVILLE**. She knows him at the strange threshold between childhood and professional life.

She also complicates his life immediately:

- He has just signed a contract.
- He is still very young.
- His family is protective.
- Rachel will worry about both of them.
- Marcos has to learn the difference between helping someone and controlling their choices.
- Halcyon has to decide whether Marcos is safe enough to trust.

### Relationship Dynamic

Marcos is disciplined, quiet, and physically contained.

Halcyon should have a contrasting but compatible energy: watchful, sharp, self-protective, perhaps poetic or dryly funny. Her name already carries a strange beauty — **Halcyon** suggests calm, birds, sea, myth, and a peace that may not match the instability of her life when Marcos meets her.

Possible dynamic:

- Marcos offers consistency.
- Halcyon offers honesty.
- Marcos is used to structured systems: family, training, teams, schedules.
- Halcyon has survived outside systems that failed her.
- Marcos has to learn not every problem can be solved by money.
- Halcyon has to learn that accepting care does not mean surrendering independence.


### Bringing Halcyon to Carlisle

After meeting Halcyon in Clarence Park, Marcos does not leave the encounter as a passing moment. He brings her to the **Carlisle mansion** in Ontario — the Key family’s old fortress of safety — but he does **not** call Rachel Darcy first.

This choice matters because Carlisle is not a hotel, not a transaction, and not Marcos trying to hide her. It is the place the Key family has always used when someone needs protection, quiet, privacy, and adults who know how to move carefully around trauma.

Because Halcyon is seventeen, the scene must be handled with care:

- Marcos asks; he does not pressure.
- Halcyon chooses whether to come.
- Rachel is **not** called in advance; Marcos brings Halcyon to Carlisle impulsively, and Rachel learns when they arrive.
- The family treats Halcyon as a guest and a young person in need of safety, not as Marcos’s possession.
- No one demands her full story at the door.
- A private room, food, shower, clean clothes, and rest come before questions.
- The romance remains emotionally cautious and age-appropriate while her safety is stabilized.

For Marcos, bringing Halcyon to Carlisle without calling Rachel first is the first major test of his new adulthood. He has signed a professional contract, but money and status are not enough. He has to learn how to ask for help from his family, how to protect Halcyon’s dignity, and how not to confuse love with ownership.

For Halcyon, Carlisle is overwhelming: gates, acreage, rooms, security, family wealth, and the strange terror of being offered safety after surviving without it. She may not trust it at first. She may sleep with her belongings close. She may need to know where every exit is. The family lets her.

### Family Reaction

Rachel is likely alarmed at first — not because Halcyon is unhoused, but because Marcos is young, newly signed, emotionally intense, and vulnerable to making enormous promises before he understands their weight.

Jessica may be more analytical and careful, wanting to know whether Halcyon is safe and whether Marcos is confusing love with rescue.

Melissa and Annabella will read Marcos quickly. As his triplet sisters, they can tell whether he is performing heroism or genuinely altered by this girl.

The Key family has the resources to help, but the emotional rule should be clear:

> Help cannot erase Halcyon’s agency.

### Possible First Scene Image

Marcos goes to Clarence Park after signing, needing quiet after the noise of the contract, congratulations, and family pride. He brings a baseball or glove. He is not there to celebrate. He is there because the open space helps him breathe.

Halcyon is there with her belongings arranged carefully nearby — not scattered, not careless, but organized in the way of someone who has to know where every object is.

Marcos notices the order before he notices the hardship.

That is the beginning.

## Core Theme

Marcos Eduarte’s baseball arc is not about escaping the Key family.

It is about proving that the Key family inheritance can become something new.

He does not dance professionally, but ballet lives in his delivery.  
He does not become a gymnast, but gymnastics lives in his body control.  
He does not follow medicine, modeling, film, or dance, but he carries the same discipline.  
He does not remember Antoine fully, but he carries his name.  
He is biologically Jessica’s child, but Rachel is his mother in every way that counts.  
He is one of three, but his dream is his own.

The diamond is where all of that becomes visible.
