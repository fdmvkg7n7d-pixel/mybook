- **Characters:** Rachel Darcy Key, Armella Beatrice Key-d'Aboville, Terri Anne Williams-Key, Kella Nakeisha Key-d'Aboville referenced.
- **Location:** Rachel in Los Angeles / Armella at Terri Anne’s home after school at Gulliver Preparatory School.
- **Timing:** After Armella leaves Skyland Trail, moves in with Terri Anne, and begins attending Gulliver Preparatory School.
- **Status:** New scene outline.

- Kella remains in Los Angeles and has more space before daily reintegration with Armella.

## Core Situation

- Rachel Darcy talks to Armella by phone or video call after Armella has begun settling into Terri Anne’s home and Gulliver Preparatory School.
- Rachel hears Armella’s voice and is deeply relieved.
- Armella does not sound “fixed.”
- She sounds present.
- She sounds more grounded.
- She sounds like a girl who is beginning to grow stronger.
- Rachel is so glad to hear Armella Beatrice’s voice.

## Scene Beats

### Beat 1: Rachel Waits for the Call

- Rachel is in Los Angeles when the call is scheduled.
- The house is quieter without Armella in it.
- Kella’s need for space is still being honored.
- Rachel has spent the day managing school, triplets, appointments, and the emotional math of two daughters living in different places.
- She keeps checking the time.
- She tells herself not to expect too much from one call.
- She expects to hear strain, fear, or loneliness.
- She is prepared to be steady if Armella sounds fragile.

### Beat 2: Armella Calls From Terri Anne’s House

- Armella calls from Terri Anne’s home after school.
- The background is different from Los Angeles and different from Skyland Trail.
- There may be the sound of Terri Anne moving in the kitchen, a door closing, dishes, homework, or ordinary house rhythm.
- Armella is no longer in a treatment-center room.
- She is also not back in the house where everything happened.
- That middle place matters.
- Rachel answers carefully, ready to listen before she asks too many questions.

### Beat 3: Rachel Hears Armella’s Voice

- Armella speaks.
- Rachel hears her voice and feels the relief physically.
- Armella’s voice is still cautious, but it is clearer than Rachel expected.
- She sounds tired from school, but not lost.
- She sounds nervous, but not collapsed.
- She sounds like someone who has had a full day and survived it.
- Rachel is so glad to hear her.
- Rachel has to pause before answering because the gladness rises too quickly.

### Beat 4: Armella Tells Rachel How School Goes

- Armella tells Rachel Darcy how school is going at Gulliver Preparatory School.
- She starts with ordinary details because ordinary details are easier than feelings.
- She tells Rachel about her classes.
- She tells Rachel about the teachers.
- She tells Rachel about the hallways, the lunch routine, the homework, and the feeling of being the new girl.
- She admits she got lost once or twice.
- She says a teacher helped her without making it a big production.
- She says the school day is tiring, but not impossible.
- She says no one is treating her like a problem to be watched every second.
- She says she can breathe there.
- Rachel listens carefully because the details are not small to her.
- Every ordinary detail means Armella is beginning to live in the present again.

### Beat 5: Gulliver Preparatory School as a New Structure

- Armella explains that Gulliver feels different from Los Angeles and different from Skyland Trail.
- Gulliver is not a treatment home.
- Gulliver is not the house where everything happened.
- It is school.
- It has bells, classes, assignments, teachers, classmates, and expectations.
- Armella may mention that no one there knows the whole story unless the adults decide they need to know.
- Gulliver gives her a day that belongs to the present.
- Rachel hears that and understands why Terri Anne’s plan is working.

### Beat 6: Terri Anne’s Structure

- Armella talks about Terri Anne’s household rhythm.
- Terri Anne is direct.
- Terri Anne expects her to get up, eat, go to school, do homework, attend therapy, and tell the truth.
- Terri Anne does not hover.
- Terri Anne also does not let Armella drift.
- Armella admits that the structure helps.
- Rachel hears that Armella is not interpreting structure as rejection anymore.
- That is a sign of growth.

### Beat 7: Rachel’s Relief

- Rachel tells Armella that she is glad to hear her voice.
- She means more than the words can hold.
- She is glad Armella is alive and speaking.
- She is glad Armella is not disappearing into shame.
- She is glad Armella is strong enough to describe a school day.
- She is glad Terri Anne’s house is holding her.
- She is glad she did not force the immediate Los Angeles return.
- Rachel’s relief is maternal, practical, and almost painful.

### Beat 8: Armella Asks About Kella

- Armella asks about Kella.
- She asks carefully, not demanding access or reassurance.
- Rachel tells her the truth in a measured way.
- Kella is going to school.
- Kella is functioning.
- Kella still needs space.
- Kella’s need for space is not hatred.
- Rachel tells Armella that Kella is so happy too to hear that Armella is doing better at Terri Anne’s and Gulliver.
- Kella’s happiness does not erase the hurt, but it proves that love is still alive and active.
- Armella absorbs that.
- Rachel hears Armella trying not to push.
- That is another sign of growth.

### Beat 9: Rachel Does Not Overpromise

- Rachel does not promise that everything will be fine soon.
- She does not promise Kella will be ready quickly.
- She does not promise that Los Angeles will feel easy when Armella returns.
- She tells Armella that this stage matters.
- Terri Anne, Gulliver, therapy, schoolwork, calls, and ordinary days are all part of coming home slowly.
- Armella listens.
- She does not argue with the slowness.

### Beat 10: Terri Anne Appears Briefly

- Terri Anne may appear briefly in the background or take the phone for a moment.
- She tells Rachel that Armella got through the day.
- She does not exaggerate.
- She does not make Armella sound perfect.
- She simply reports steadiness.
- Rachel trusts Terri Anne’s plainness.
- Terri Anne’s calm makes Rachel feel less alone.

### Beat 11: The Call Ends

- The call ends gently.
- Armella has homework, dinner, therapy notes, or a school task waiting.
- Rachel does not keep her on the phone too long.
- She lets Armella return to the structure that is helping her.
- When the call ends, Rachel sits still for a moment.
- The house in Los Angeles is still complicated.
- Kella is still hurt.
- Armella is still away.
- But Rachel has heard Armella’s voice, and Armella sounded stronger.

### Beat 12: Terri Anne Makes Mike’s Favorite Dessert

- After the call ends, Terri Anne sees what the call has cost Armella emotionally.
- Armella is stronger, but she is still carrying a lot.
- Terri Anne does not make a speech.
- She goes into the kitchen and makes Mike’s favorite dessert for Armella Beatrice.
- She makes a banana split with chocolate mint ice cream.
- The dessert carries Mike’s old rules: chocolate mint in the middle, vanilla and strawberry on the sides, cold chocolate sauce, whipped cream piled too tall, two cherries, and no nuts.
- Terri Anne does not present it as a reward for being good.
- She presents it as family comfort.
- Armella understands that this dessert belonged to Mike and that Terri Anne is sharing something from the deep family kitchen with her.
- The banana split gives Armella a sweet, ordinary place to put the feeling after hearing Rachel’s relief and Kella’s happiness.
- Terri Anne sits with her while she eats.
- The moment says that Armella is accountable, but she is also still loved and still fed.

### Beat 13: Terri Anne Tells Armella About Her Grandfather

- While Armella eats, Terri Anne tells her about Mike Key, her grandfather.
- Terri Anne does not turn the story into a lecture.
- She tells Armella that the banana split was Mike’s favorite because he liked pleasure to be specific and generous.
- She tells Armella about his chocolate mint rule and the two cherries.
- She tells Armella that Mike believed children deserved the truth in words they could carry.
- She tells Armella that Mike did not throw children away when they were complicated.
- He expected truth.
- He expected responsibility.
- He also made room at the table.
- Terri Anne says this carefully because she does not want Armella to mistake comfort for permission to avoid consequences.
- Armella listens while eating the dessert that belonged to a grandfather whose family rules are still holding her.
- She understands that Terri Anne is telling her about Mike because Mike’s way of loving the family still matters.
- The story gives Armella another inheritance: not money, not reputation, but the expectation that love and accountability can sit at the same kitchen table.
- Armella will remember this dessert.
- Years later, she will make Mike’s banana split for her own future children.
- When she makes it for them, she will follow the same rules Terri Anne taught her: chocolate mint in the middle, vanilla and strawberry on the sides, cold chocolate sauce, whipped cream piled too tall, two cherries, and no nuts.
- The dessert becomes a way Armella carries Mike and Terri Anne’s family table forward into her own motherhood.

### Beat 14: The Triplets Have Many Questions

- After the call, the triplets have many questions.
- Melissa Lizette, Marcos Eduarte, and Annabella Nicki have been waiting in the emotional weather of the house even if they were not part of the call.
- They want to know whether Armella sounded happy.
- They want to know whether Armella likes Gulliver Preparatory School.
- They want to know whether Terri Anne is being nice to her.
- They want to know whether Armella still has the Bluey plush doll.
- They want to know whether Armella is coming home soon.
- They want to know whether Kella is happy too.
- They want to know whether they are allowed to call Armella.
- They want to know whether Armella is still in trouble.
- They want to know when they will see Armella again.
- Rachel answers carefully because the triplets are old enough to feel the fracture but not old enough to carry the whole adult explanation.
- Rachel tells them Armella sounded stronger.
- Rachel tells them Gulliver is helping her have ordinary school days again.
- Rachel tells them Terri Anne is taking good care of her.
- Rachel tells them Kella is happy to hear Armella is growing stronger, but Kella still needs space.
- Rachel tells them that loving someone and needing space from them can both be true.
- Rachel also tells them that before they go to French Woods, they will visit Armella Beatrice and their grandmother Terri Anne.
- The visit gives the triplets something concrete to hold onto.
- They do not have to imagine Armella only through phone calls.
- They will see her with Grandmother.
- The triplets do not fully understand, but they listen.
- Their questions show that Armella’s absence is not only Kella’s wound or Rachel’s burden.
- The whole sibling group is trying to understand the new shape of the family.

### Beat 15: Final Image

- The final image is Rachel holding the phone after the call ends and sitting with the triplets’ questions around her.
- She is tired, but relieved.
- She is so glad to have heard Armella Beatrice’s voice.
- She is also aware that Melissa Lizette, Marcos Eduarte, and Annabella Nicki need answers too.
- In another state, Armella is living with Terri Anne and attending Gulliver Preparatory School.
- The distance still hurts.
- For the first time, the distance also feels like it is doing its work.
