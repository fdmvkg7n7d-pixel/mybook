# RACHEL ASKS FALLEN GLENDA AND CHARLIE

## Scene Outline — Beats, Dialogue Cues, and Thematic Notes

**POV:** Primarily Rachel Darcy Key (with Fallon Glenda and Charlie present)
**Tone:** A mother asking for help. Not collapsing. Not demanding. Asking. The way Key women ask — directly, honestly, with the understanding that the answer will be yes because the Key family does nothing alone.
**Location:** Rachel calls Fallon Glenda and Charlie — by phone or iPad. Fallon Glenda and Charlie are in **San Francisco** (about six hours from Los Angeles).
**Ages:** Rachel Darcy is **~48.** Fallon Glenda is **~39** (born ~2033). Charlie is **~39** (same school year as Fallon Glenda; Charlie's birthday is in February, Fallon Glenda's is in December).
**Time in saga:** ~2072 — after the emotional conversation, after the kitchen scene, after Jessica and Emma's conversation in Halifax. Before the family meeting.

## CONTENT NOTE

This scene is the **ask** — Rachel asking her sister and her sister's partner to take care of four of her seven children while she takes Armella to residential treatment. The drama is in the asking: Rachel is a widowed mother of seven who has held everything together for six years, and now she is asking for help. Not because she is weak. Because the Key family does nothing alone. The saga's handling standard applies: **off-page, non-graphic, but emotionally true.**

## BEFORE THE SCENE — WHAT RACHEL CARRIES INTO THE CALL

*Establish the weight Rachel brings into this conversation.*

- Rachel has held Kella through the emotional conversation. She has confronted Armella. She has called Jessica and Emma. She has walked into the kitchen and held the whole family — the triplets, the Bluey dolls, the chocolate mint ice cream, the silence between the twins.
- Now she needs to make the practical arrangements. Armella is going to residential treatment outside LA. Rachel needs to go with her — or at least be involved in the treatment, be present for the family therapy sessions, be the mother who holds her daughter through this.
- But Rachel cannot take all seven children with her. And she cannot leave the other children alone. And she cannot ask Kella to be in the same house as Armella right now — the twins are not speaking, and Kella needs space.
- **The solution:** Fallon Glenda and Charlie. They are in San Francisco — close enough to be practical, far enough to give Kella the space she needs. Fallon Glenda is a physician. Charlie is the steady, chosen father figure. They have four daughters of their own (Lÿsa Mandy, Rebecca Brazilia, Charlie Janya, Calida Dawn). They know how to manage a household of children. They are family.
- **Rachel's interior:** She is about to ask her sister to take in four of her children. She is not collapsing. She is not demanding. She is asking. And she knows the answer will be yes, because the Key family does nothing alone.

## BEAT 2 — THE ASK

*Rachel asks Fallon Glenda and Charlie to take care of Kella, Melissa Lizette, Marcos Eduarte, and Annabella Nicki.*

- Rachel names what she needs. She is direct. She is honest. She does not soften it.
- **Dialogue cue (Rachel):** "Armella is going to residential treatment. Outside LA. I need to go with her — or at least be involved, be present, be there for the family therapy sessions. And I need someone to take care of Kella, Melissa, Marcos, and Annabella. I need someone to take them in while I'm gone."
- **Dialogue cue (Rachel, continuing):** "I'm asking you. You and Charlie. I'm asking you to take care of four of my children while I take the fifth to treatment."
- **Fallon Glenda's response:** Fallon Glenda does not hesitate. She does not ask how long. She does not ask about logistics. She does not ask about school, or schedules, or bedrooms. She hears her sister asking for help, and she says yes.
- **Dialogue cue (Fallon Glenda):** "Yes. Of course. Send them to us. We have the room. We have the food. We have the love. Send them to us."
- **Charlie's response:** Charlie speaks. He is quiet, steady, sure.
- **Dialogue cue (Charlie):** "They're family. They're always welcome here. You don't have to ask twice."

**Thematic note:** The ask is direct. The answer is immediate. This is the Key family's deepest instinct: *no one does anything alone.* Rachel asks. Fallon Glenda says yes. Charlie says yes. There is no negotiation, no hesitation, no condition. The children will come to San Francisco, and they will be held, and Rachel will take Armella to treatment, and the family will hold everyone at the same time.

## BEAT 3 — THE DETAILS

*They talk about the practical arrangements.*

- Rachel and Fallon Glenda talk about the details. Not the emotional details — those have been handled. The practical details: when, how, what the children need.
- **Dialogue cue (Rachel):** "Kella needs space. She and Armella are not speaking. She needs to be somewhere she doesn't have to see Armella, doesn't have to be in the same house, doesn't have to carry any of this. Can you give her that?"
- **Dialogue cue (Fallon Glenda):** "Yes. She can have her own room, or she can share with Rebecca Brazilia and Charlie Janya — they're close in age. Whatever she needs. We'll figure it out."
- **Dialogue cue (Rachel):** "The triplets — Melissa, Marcos, Annabella — they're nine. They don't fully understand what happened. They know something is wrong. They asked me. I told them something true, at the level they could hold. They're going to need stability, routine, love. They're going to need someone who can hold them while I'm not there."
- **Dialogue cue (Charlie):** "We can do that. Lÿsa Mandy is older — she can help with the younger ones. Calida Dawn is younger, but she's good with kids. We'll make it work."
- **Dialogue cue (Rachel):** "Thank you."

**Thematic note:** The practical details are handled quickly, efficiently, with love. Fallon Glenda and Charlie do not need to be told how to manage a household of children — they have four of their own. They do not need to be told how to hold children through a crisis — they have done it before. They just need to know who is coming, and when, and what they need. And Rachel tells them.

## BEAT 4 — KELLA

*Rachel talks specifically about Kella.*

- Rachel talks about Kella — the twin who was betrayed, the fifteen-year-old who walked into a room and saw her boyfriend with her face, the daughter who is in pain and who needs space and who needs to be held.
- **Dialogue cue (Rachel):** "Kella is hurting. She's not speaking to Armella. She told me what it felt like — she said Armella used the face they share. She said it was like everything she thought she knew about who she is just stopped. I need you to hold her, Fallon. I need you to be the person who holds her while I'm not there."
- **Dialogue cue (Fallon Glenda, steady):** "I will. I promise you. I will hold her. She can talk to me, or she can not talk to me. She can cry, or she can not cry. She can be angry, or she can be quiet. Whatever she needs. I will hold her."
- **Rachel's interior:** She hears her sister's promise and she feels the weight of it lift from her shoulders — not all of it, not the whole weight, but some of it. Kella will be held. Fallon Glenda will hold her. And Rachel can take Armella to treatment and know that the daughter who was betrayed is not alone.

**Thematic note:** Fallon Glenda's promise — *"I will hold her"* — is the promise of a physician, a sister, a mother, a woman who knows what it means to hold a child through something hard. Fallon Glenda became a mother at thirteen. She knows what it means to be young and hurting and in need of someone who will not flinch. She will hold Kella. And Kella will be held.

Fallon Glenda and Charlie are holding four of them. Jessica and Emma are on iPad. The wider family network is mobilizing. Rachel is not alone.
