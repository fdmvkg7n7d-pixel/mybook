# KELLA CATCHES ARMELLA AND TODD

## Scene Outline — Beats, Dialogue Cues, and Thematic Notes

**POV:** Primarily Kella Nakeisha (with brief shifts to Todd's and Armella's perception)
**Tone:** Devastation. The clean, sharp crack of a world breaking in a single moment.
**Location:** The family home in **Los Angeles, California** — the house Rachel and Antoine built, where the seven children grew up. The specific room where the discovery happens is for the author to decide. The family attends **Viewpoint School** in Calabasas (the private college-preparatory day school where Kella, Armella, and Todd are all students).
**Ages:** Kella Nakeisha, Armella Beatrice, and Todd Benstin are all **~15.**
**Time in saga:** ~2072 (Armella and Kella are 15; Antoine has been dead for ~7 years; Rachel is widowed, managing seven children.)

**The sexual content is never depicted.** The scene begins at the moment of discovery — Kella walking in — and what she sees is rendered with restraint. The saga's standard applies: **off-page, non-graphic.** The drama is entirely in Kella's perception, her reaction, and the immediate aftermath. What happened before she walked in is known to the author and to the characters. It is not shown.

## BEFORE THE SCENE — WHAT KELLA CARRIES INTO THE ROOM

*Establish, briefly, the ordinary world that is about to end.*

- Kella is fifteen. She has a boyfriend — **Todd Benstin**, a classmate at **Viewpoint School** in Calabasas, the private college-preparatory day school where Kella, Armella, and Todd are all students. She is one of identical twins, and she has spent her whole life learning to be seen as separate from Armella Beatrice. At River Way Ranch Camp, she majored in theater (being seen, being recognized). At Viewpoint, she pursues theater and performing arts — the stage is where she is not half of a pair, where she is the one in the spotlight, alone, speaking words that are hers. She chose the spotlight.
- She trusts Todd. She trusts Armella. She trusts the face she shares with her twin — the face that has been both gift and burden since birth.
- She does not know what is about to happen. She is walking into a room expecting nothing. Maybe she's looking for Todd. Maybe she's looking for Armella. Maybe she's looking for her camera, her phone, her sweater. She is just walking into a room.
- **Thematic note:** Kella is the twin who performs, who stands in the light, who wants to be recognized. In a few moments, she will be forced to watch the thing she never wanted to see. The twin who sees (Armella, the photographer) will be the one who failed to see. The irony is devastating, and Kella will feel it as betrayal, not as poetry.

## BEAT 1 — THE DOOR

*The ordinary act that changes everything.*

- Kella opens a door. Or turns a corner. Or looks up from her phone. The specific geography is for the author to decide — what matters is that she was not expecting this, and now she is seeing it.
- **Rendered with restraint:** We stay in Kella's POV. We do not describe what she sees in detail. We describe what happens to her *face, her body, her breath* as the information lands. A held breath. A stopped heart. The world reorganizing.
- **Kella's interior:** The first fraction of a second — *is that…?* — and then the certainty. Yes. That is Armella. That is Todd. And Todd thinks that is me.

**Thematic note:** The face she shares with her twin — the face she has spent her life learning to be seen through — is now the instrument of her betrayal. She is looking at her own face, and it is not her.

---

## BEAT 2 — THE RECOGNITION (TODD'S POV SHIFT)

*Todd realizes — or is forced to realize — what has happened.*

- Brief shift to Todd's perception. He sees Kella standing in the doorway. He sees her face — the same face he thought he was with — and he sees that something is terribly wrong.
- **The moment of understanding:** Todd looks from Kella (in the doorway) to Armella (beside him) and back to Kella. The two faces are identical. And one of them is looking at him with horror.
- **Todd's interior:** The confusion arrives like a wave. *Wait. Which one… I was with… she said… but that's…* He doesn't finish the thought. He doesn't need to. Kella's face tells him everything.
- **Dialogue cue (Todd, barely audible):** "Kella?"

**Thematic note:** Todd trusted the face. The face lied — or didn't. He doesn't know yet. All he knows is that the person he thought he was with is standing in the doorway, and she is not the person beside him.

---

## BEAT 3 — ARMELLA'S FREEZE

*Armella is caught by her twin — the person she most resembles, the person she betrayed.*

- Armella sees Kella. She freezes. There is no denying it. There is no explaining it away. Kella saw. Kella knows.
- **Armella's interior:** The impulsive, careless, reckless moment collides with its consequence. She wasn't thinking — and now she has to think, all at once, about everything she didn't think about. Kella's face. Todd's confusion. The family. The betrayal. The harm.
- **Dialogue cue (Armella, barely):** "Kella—"
- Kella does not let her finish. She doesn't need to hear it. She has already seen enough.

**Thematic note:** Armella is the photographer — the one who sees, the one behind the camera. In this moment, she is the one being seen. And what is seen is not good.

---

## BEAT 4 — KELLA'S REACTION

*Very emotional. Very betrayed. Very done.*

- Kella's reaction is immediate, visceral, and decisive. She is very emotional — not calm, not measured, not processing quietly. She is in an acute state of betrayal, and she reacts accordingly.
- **What Kella does:** The author will decide the specifics — does she scream? Does she freeze? Does she run? Does she say something? What matters is that her reaction is *very emotional* and *very betrayed.*
- **Dialogue cue (Kella, to Todd — the breakup, swift and final):** "We're done."
  - Or: "Don't. Don't say anything. We're done."
  - Or: *[silence — she just looks at him, and he understands.]*
- **Todd's response:** He tries to speak. He tries to explain. He doesn't even know what to explain. *I thought… I didn't know… Kella, I—*
- Kella does not want to hear it. She is done. She turns and walks away — or she tells them both to get out — or she stands there, shaking, unable to move. The author will decide.

**Thematic note:** Kella has clarity. Unlike Todd (confused) and unlike Armella (impulsive), Kella knows exactly what happened. She was betrayed. She does not need to understand why. She needs to be believed, protected, and supported — and she needs to get out of this room.

## BEAT 5 — THE AFTERMATH BEGINS

*The room empties. The family is about to learn.*

- The three of them are no longer in the same space. Kella has left, or Todd has left, or Armella has left — the room is no longer holding all three of them.
- **Kella, alone (or finding someone):** She needs to tell someone. She needs her mother. She needs to be believed. She is very emotional, very betrayed, and she needs Rachel.
- **Todd, alone:** He is confused, ashamed, and cut off from the person he thought he was in a relationship with. He doesn't know what to do. He doesn't know who to call. He is fifteen, and he has just been caught in something he doesn't understand.
- **Armella, alone:** She is caught. She is seen. She has to face what she did — and she has to face her mother, and her twin, and the family, and herself. She wasn't thinking. Now she has to think.

**Forward hooks:**
- Kella tells Rachel → Rachel's anger → Rachel calls Jessica Isabella → the family mobilizes.
- Todd goes home → his family is contacted → his own confusion and shame.
- Armella waits → for Rachel, for consequences, for the weight of what she did to land.
- **Viewpoint School** — the social fallout at school. Classmates may learn. Teachers and counselors may need to be involved. Kella, Armella, and Todd all attend Viewpoint — the school is the ecosystem where the incident has consequences beyond the family.
