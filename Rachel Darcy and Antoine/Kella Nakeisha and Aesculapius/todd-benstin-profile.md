## ROLE IN THE SAGA

Todd Benstin is a **minor character** who enters the saga through his relationship with Kella Nakeisha and exits (or transforms) through the incident with Armella Beatrice. He is not a villain. He is not a predator. He is a **fifteen-year-old boy who was very confused** — caught in a situation the saga's twin motif was practically designed to produce.

He is the person Kella trusted romantically. He is the person who could not tell the twins apart. He is the person who was with Armella believing she was Kella. He is the person Kella caught, and the person Kella quickly broke up with.

His confusion deserves gentleness — from the family, from the narrative, from the author.

---

## THE INCIDENT

Todd was with Armella Beatrice, believing she was Kella Nakeisha. The identical twins share a face; Todd could not tell them apart. Armella did not correct him. The moment escalated. Todd did not realize — or did not realize until Kella walked in and her reaction told him everything.

**Todd's state:** Very confused. Not angry, not certain, not accusatory. Lost.

**His confusion operates on multiple levels:**

- **Perceptual:** He literally could not tell them apart. Two people share a face, and he trusted the face.
- **Emotional:** Was what he felt for *"Kella"* actually what he felt? If he couldn't tell them apart, does he even know who he's attracted to? Is attraction to a face the same as attraction to a person?
- **Moral:** Did he do something wrong? He was with his girlfriend — or he thought he was. If he was with the wrong twin, is that cheating? Was he betrayed, or was he complicit in something he didn't understand?
- **Relational:** Can he look at Kella the same way? Can he look at Armella? Can he be in the same room as either of them?

---

## THE DISCOVERY — KELLA CAUGHT THEM

Kella walked in. She saw it with her own eyes. In that moment, Todd realized — or was forced to realize — that he was with Armella, not Kella. Kella's reaction (horror, betrayal, recognition) told him everything.

Kella is very emotional. She quickly breaks up with Todd. There is no deliberation. There is no *"let me think about this."* Todd does not have a chance to explain. Kella does not want to hear it. She is done.

Todd is now: single, confused, ashamed, and cut off from the person he thought he was in a relationship with.

## AFTER THE INCIDENT

- Todd's family is contacted — they need to know what happened, and that their son was caught by Kella.
- Todd may benefit from his own counseling — not PSB-specific, but adolescent therapy focused on identity, relationships, boundaries, and processing an experience that doesn't have a clean category.
- His relationship with Kella is over — at least in the immediate aftermath. Whether it might be revisited later (after treatment, after time, after healing) is for the author to decide.
- His relationship with Armella is undefined — they are not friends, not enemies, not anything. They are two people who share a terrible moment and now must figure out how to exist in the same world.

---

## NARRATIVE FUNCTION

Todd serves several functions in the Armella Beatrice treatment arc:

1. **The catalyst** — his confusion is what makes the incident possible. Without the twins' shared face and Todd's inability to distinguish them, the incident does not happen.
2. **The mirror** — Todd's confusion reflects Armella's impulsivity. He didn't think (he trusted the face). She didn't think (she didn't correct him). Both of them failed to see clearly, and both of them caused harm.
3. **The collateral** — Todd is not the primary victim (Kella is) and not the primary person who caused harm (Armella is), but he is caught in the middle. He is damaged by what happened, even though he didn't plan it and didn't understand it.
4. **The question** — Todd raises the saga's question about identity and recognition: *Can you love someone if you can't tell them apart from their twin? Is attraction to a face the same as attraction to a person?*
