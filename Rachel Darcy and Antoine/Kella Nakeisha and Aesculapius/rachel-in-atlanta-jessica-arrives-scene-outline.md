# RACHEL IN ATLANTA — A COUPLE OF DAYS, THEN JESSICA ARRIVES

## Scene Outline — Beats, Dialogue Cues, and Thematic Notes

**POV:** Primarily Rachel Darcy Key (with shifts to Jessica Isabella Key)
**Tone:** Part one: quiet, exhausted, the stillness of a mother alone for a brief window. Part two: relief, the reunion of two women who began as one, steel meeting steel. Then: Rachel returns to her other children.
**Location:** A **two-bedroom suite** at a hotel near **Skyland Trail**, Atlanta, Georgia. (The suite has two bedrooms because Armella is staying overnight. Rachel stays in the lobby/living area. Jessica stays in Rachel's room when she arrives.) Then **Skyland Trail**. Then the airport. Then back to Los Angeles.
**Ages:** Rachel Darcy is **~48.** Jessica Isabella is **~48** (Rachel's identical twin).
**Time in saga:** ~2072 — the first couple of days after Armella's admission to Skyland Trail.

---

## CONTENT NOTE

This scene covers **the couple of days** Rachel spends in Atlanta after admitting Armella to Skyland Trail, and **Jessica's arrival** to be with her during that brief window. Rachel cannot stay in Atlanta for the full 12 weeks — her other children are required to attend school in Los Angeles, and she needs to be there for them. She stays in Atlanta for a couple of days, long enough to get Armella settled, meet the treatment team, attend the first family therapy session, and be held by her twin. Then she flies back to Los Angeles. The saga's handling standard applies: **off-page, non-graphic, but emotionally true.**

## PART ONE — RACHEL ALONE (THE FIRST NIGHT)

### Before the Scene — What Rachel Carries into the Room

*Establish the weight Rachel brings into this hotel room.*

- Rachel has driven five children from Los Angeles to San Francisco. She has left four of them with Fallon Glenda and Charlie. She has watched her daughters hug each other and cry. She has watched Kella help Armella into a limousine. She has talked to Armella in the limousine. She has flown across the country with her daughter. She has walked Armella through the door of Skyland Trail. She has met the treatment team. She has said goodbye — for now.
- She has checked into the hotel. She has closed the door. She is alone.
- **Rachel knows she cannot stay.** She has a couple of days — enough to get Armella settled, to meet the treatment team, to attend the first family therapy session, to be held by Jessica when she arrives. But she cannot stay for the full 12 weeks. Her other children are in Los Angeles. They are required to attend school — Kella at Viewpoint School in Calabasas, the triplets at their school. Fallon Glenda and Charlie are holding them in San Francisco for now, but the children need to be back in LA for school. Rachel needs to be there.
- **Rachel's interior:** She is exhausted. She is a widowed mother of seven who has just done the hardest thing she has ever done. And she knows she has only a couple of days here before she has to go back. She is going to use every minute.

### Beat 1 — The Door Closes

*Rachel closes the door. She is alone. For a couple of days.*

- Rachel closes the door of the **two-bedroom suite**. She puts her bag down. The suite has two bedrooms — one for Armella (who is staying overnight before being fully admitted to Skyland Trail, or who has a pass to stay with Rachel), and one that will be Rachel's (and later Jessica's, when she arrives). Rachel is in the **lobby/living area** of the suite. She is alone for the first time since the discovery.
- **The silence:** The silence is not the silence of the twins (that was loud, that was heavy, that was the sound of two people who share a face not speaking to each other). This silence is different. This is the silence of a mother who has been holding everything together and who has no one to hold her. And she knows this silence is brief — she has only a couple of days here before she flies back to her other children.
- **Rachel sits down.** She sits on the couch in the lobby/living area of the suite. She does not move for a moment. She is just sitting, in the lobby of a two-bedroom hotel suite, in Atlanta, alone, for the first of the couple of days she has here.

### Beat 2 — The Phone Calls

*Rachel calls the people who are holding her family.*

- Rachel picks up her phone. She calls — or she texts, or she sends a message — the people who are holding her family while she is here.
- **She calls Fallon Glenda.** She checks in. She asks about Kella. She asks about the triplets. She asks about the Giants game. She asks about the women's conversation. She asks about everything. She tells Fallon Glenda she will be back in a couple of days — that the children need to be back in LA for school, and she needs to be there.
  - **Dialogue cue (Fallon Glenda, on the phone):** "They're okay. Kella is okay. She's sleeping. The triplets had a great time at the game. Charlie brought them home exhausted and happy. We'll hold them until you're back. Don't worry about anything here."
  - **Dialogue cue (Rachel):** "Thank you. I'll be back in a couple of days. The kids need to be back in LA for school. I need to be there."
  - **Dialogue cue (Fallon Glenda):** "I know. Go be with Armella. We've got this."
- **She calls Jessica and Emma.** She checks in on the iPad. She tells them Armella is inside. She tells them she is in the hotel. She tells them she is only here for a couple of days. She tells them she needs Jessica to come — not for weeks, just for these couple of days, just to be here, just to hold her.
  - **Dialogue cue (Rachel, to Jessica, on iPad):** "Can you come? Just for a couple of days. I just need you here. I can't do this alone, even for a couple of days."
  - **Dialogue cue (Jessica, immediately):** "I'm on the next flight."
- **She checks on the triplets.** She calls — or she texts Fallon Glenda — to ask if the triplets are asleep. She asks if Marcos is okay. She asks if Melissa and Annabella are sharing a bed the way they share a bedroom at home. She asks everything a mother asks when she is two thousand miles away from her youngest children and she knows she will be back in a couple of days.
- **She calls her mother — Terri Anne Williams-Key.** Rachel calls her mother. Terri Anne is the matriarch, the grandmother, the woman who has held this family through everything. Rachel needs to hear her mother's voice. She needs to be held by the woman who held her when she was a child, the woman who is now holding the family from a distance.
  - **Dialogue cue (Terri Anne, on the phone):** "Rachel, honey. How are you?"
  - **Dialogue cue (Rachel, her voice breaking):** "I'm okay, Mom. I'm in Atlanta. Armella is here. She's staying overnight. I'm in the hotel. I just — I needed to hear your voice."
  - **Dialogue cue (Terri Anne):** "I'm here, baby. I'm right here. You're doing the right thing. You're holding everyone. I'm so proud of you."
  - **Rachel's interior:** She hears her mother's voice and she feels the weight of it — the weight of being held by the woman who has held her since she was born, the woman who is now holding the family from a distance, the woman who is the matriarch of everything. Rachel is not alone. Her mother is on the phone. Her mother is here.
- **She looks at photographs on her iPad.** Rachel opens her iPad and looks at photographs. She looks at photographs of her children — all seven of them. She looks at photographs of Antoine — her husband, the father of her children, the man who is gone, the man who would be here if he were alive. She looks at photographs of the family — the whole family, together, before everything happened, before the discovery, before the betrayal, before the treatment. She looks at the photographs and she holds them, and she holds the memories, and she holds the love, and she holds the grief.
  - **Rachel's interior:** She is looking at Antoine's face on the screen. She is looking at her children's faces. She is looking at the life they had, the life they have, the life they are trying to build. She is looking at the photographs and she is holding everything — the love, the grief, the hope, the fear — and she is sitting in the lobby of a two-bedroom hotel suite in Atlanta, alone, and she is not alone, because the photographs are here, and her mother's voice is here, and Jessica is coming tomorrow, and the family is holding her from every direction.

### Beat 3 — Rachel Cries

*Rachel cries. Alone. In the hotel room. For the first time today.*

- The phone calls are done. The phone is down. The room is quiet. And Rachel cries.
- **She cries for Kella** — the daughter who was betrayed, who said *"I love you but I couldn't forgive you right now."* Rachel cries for Kella.
- **She cries for Armella** — the daughter who caused the harm, who walked through the door of Skyland Trail holding a Bluey plush doll. Rachel cries for Armella.
- **She cries for the triplets** — the nine-year-olds who are in San Francisco, who are happy, who do not understand, who will be back in LA for school in a couple of days. Rachel cries for the triplets.
- **She cries for Antoine** — the husband who is gone, who would be here if he were alive. Rachel cries for Antoine.
- **She cries for herself** — the widowed mother of seven, who is alone in a hotel room in Atlanta for a couple of days, who is doing the hardest thing she has ever done, and who has to go back to her other children and hold them too. Rachel cries for herself.
- **She cries until she is done.** And then she stops. And she washes her face. And she gets into bed. And she is still alone, but she has cried, and the crying has let some of the weight out, and she can breathe now. And she knows Jessica is coming tomorrow.

## PART TWO — JESSICA ARRIVES (THE NEXT DAY)

### Beat 4 — The Airport

*Rachel meets Jessica at the airport. The twins see each other.*

- The next day. Rachel is at **Hartsfield-Jackson Atlanta International Airport**, waiting at the arrivals gate. She is waiting for her twin. She is waiting for Jessica. She has been alone for one night. She will not be alone anymore — at least not for the couple of days she has left here.
- **Jessica comes through the gate.** She is pulling a small suitcase — she is only here for a couple of days too. She has left Emma in Halifax with Eliot David and Kalvin LaMarcus. She has come to hold her twin for the brief window that Rachel has here.
- **They see each other.** They walk toward each other. They hug. They hold on. They hold on the way only twins can — the way only two people who were literally joined at birth can hold each other.
- **Dialogue cue (Jessica, holding Rachel):** "I'm here."
- **Dialogue cue (Rachel, holding Jessica, her voice breaking):** "You're here."

### Beat 5 — Skyland Trail Together

*Rachel and Jessica go to Skyland Trail together. They meet Armella. They meet the treatment team.*

- Rachel and Jessica go to Skyland Trail together. They walk through the door together — the twin who carried the children and the twin who gave the eggs, the two women who made Armella possible, walking into the treatment center side by side.
- **They meet the treatment team.** They sit down with the clinicians. They talk about Armella — about what happened, about the PSB, about the impulsivity, about the face the twins share, about the family's history, about the 12-week program. Jessica brings her medical expertise — she is a neurosurgeon, she understands adolescent brain development, she understands the neurological underpinnings of *"not thinking."* Rachel brings her mother's knowledge — she knows Armella, she knows Kella, she knows the family, she knows the weight of what has happened.
- **They meet Armella.** They see Armella inside Skyland Trail — the fifteen-year-old girl who came from Jessica's eggs and who is Rachel's daughter, who caused the harm, who is learning to think.
- **Dialogue cue (Jessica, to Armella, gently):** "Hey, Armella. I'm here. I came to be with your mom, and I wanted to see you too. I love you, and I'm not going anywhere — even when I go back to Halifax, I'm not going anywhere. I'm on iPad. Day or night. You call me."
- **Dialogue cue (Armella, quietly):** "Hi, Aunt Jessica."
- **Dialogue cue (Rachel, to Armella):** "I have to go back in a couple of days. Your brothers and sisters need me. They're going back to school in LA. But I will be here for every session, every call, every moment. I will fly back for family therapy. I will be on iPad. I am not leaving you. Do you hear me?"
- **Dialogue cue (Armella):** "I hear you."

### Beat 6 — The Sisters Together

*Jessica and Rachel are together. For a couple of days. They hold each other.*

- Jessica and Rachel spend the couple of days together. They go to Skyland Trail for family therapy sessions. They sit in the hotel room at night and talk. They eat together. They walk together. They hold each other the way they have always held each other — from the inside, from the place where they were joined, from the place where they are still connected.
- **Dialogue cue (Rachel, to Jessica, late at night, in the hotel room):** "I don't know what I would do without you."
- **Dialogue cue (Jessica, to Rachel):** "You don't have to find out. I'm here. I'm always here. We began as one. We're still one. We're always one."
- **Jessica speaks with Kella on iPad.** While she is in Atlanta, Jessica calls Kella on iPad. She checks in on her niece — the daughter who was betrayed, the daughter who said *"I love you but I couldn't forgive you right now,"* the daughter who is in San Francisco with Fallon Glenda, who is trying to be a normal fifteen-year-old and is not succeeding. Jessica holds Kella from a distance, the way she holds everyone in this family.
  - **Dialogue cue (Jessica, to Kella, on iPad):** "Hey, Kella. How are you doing?"
  - **Dialogue cue (Kella, quietly):** "I'm okay."
  - **Dialogue cue (Jessica):** "You don't have to be okay. You know that, right? You can be whatever you need to be."
  - **Dialogue cue (Kella, after a pause):** "I know. Thank you, Aunt Jessica."
  - **Jessica's interior:** She is looking at Kella on the screen — the fifteen-year-old girl who came from her eggs, who is Rachel's daughter, who was betrayed, who is trying to hold it together in San Francisco while her twin is in Atlanta and her mother is in a hotel room. Jessica holds her from a distance. She holds her the way she holds everyone in this family — from the inside, from the place where she is connected, from the place where she is always present.
- **Dialogue cue (Rachel):** "I have to go back. The kids need to be in school in LA. Kella needs to be at Viewpoint. The triplets need to be at their school. I have to be there for them."
- **Dialogue cue (Jessica):** "I know. You're their mother. You have to be there. And I'll be on iPad. Day or night. You call me. And I'll come back if you need me. I'll always come back."

### Beat 7 — Rachel Flies Back

*Rachel flies back to Los Angeles. Jessica flies back to Halifax. The family is split again.*

- The couple of days are over. Rachel flies back to Los Angeles — to her other children, to Kella and the triplets, to the house where everything happened, to the life that continues. Jessica flies back to Halifax — to Emma, to Eliot David and Kalvin LaMarcus, to her work, to her life.
- **Meanwhile, Charlie drives the children to Los Angeles.** Charlie — Fallon Glenda's partner, the steady, chosen father figure — drives Kella and the triplets from San Francisco to Los Angeles. They are going home. They are going back to school. Kella is going back to Viewpoint in Calabasas. The triplets are going back to their school in LA. Rachel will meet them there when she lands.
- **At the airport:** Rachel and Jessica hug again. They hold on. They let go.
- **Dialogue cue (Jessica, at the gate):** "Call me. Anytime. I'm here."
- **Dialogue cue (Rachel):** "I will. Thank you for coming."
- **Dialogue cue (Jessica):** "I would always come."
- **Rachel boards her flight to Los Angeles.** She sits in her seat. She looks out the window. She thinks about Armella in Atlanta, in Skyland Trail, holding a Bluey plush doll, learning to think. She thinks about Kella and the triplets in San Francisco, waiting for her to come back, waiting to go home to LA, waiting to go back to school. She thinks about the face her daughters share, and the two thousand miles between them, and the iPad that will connect them, and the family that is holding everyone from every direction.
- **She is going home.** She is going back to her other children. She is going back to the life that continues. She is going back to be the mother who holds everyone, who is not alone, who has Jessica on iPad and Fallon Glenda down the street and the wider family mobilizing from every direction.

**End image:** Two planes and a car. One plane carrying Rachel to Los Angeles, to her other children, to the house where Charlie is driving Kella and the triplets, to school and life and the ordinary. One plane carrying Jessica to Halifax, to Emma and the twins, to work and home. One car carrying Charlie and the children from San Francisco to Los Angeles, from Fallon Glenda's house to their own home, from the extraordinary back to the ordinary. The twins are separated again — but they are not alone. They are connected by the iPad, by the phone, by the bond that began at birth and that will never break. And Armella is in Atlanta, in Skyland Trail, holding a Bluey plush doll, learning to think, with her mother on a plane and her aunt on a plane and her uncle driving her siblings home and the whole family holding her from every direction. Rachel will fly back to Atlanta **biweekly** — every two weeks — for family therapy sessions at Skyland Trail. The family is split across the country, and they are holding each other from every direction.
