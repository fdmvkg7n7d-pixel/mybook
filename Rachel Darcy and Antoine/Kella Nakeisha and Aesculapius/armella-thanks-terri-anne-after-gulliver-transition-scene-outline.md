### Beat 1: After School at Gulliver

- Armella comes home from Gulliver Preparatory School after an ordinary school day.
- She has homework, books, and the tiredness of someone who has spent the day trying to function normally.
- The day was not perfect.
- She may have gotten overwhelmed, missed a social cue, or felt the old shame rise unexpectedly.
- She still got through the day.
- Terri Anne notices that without making it a ceremony.
- The house receives Armella as expected, not as a fragile guest.

### Beat 2: The Ordinary Routine

- Terri Anne has the afternoon or evening routine ready.
- There is food, homework time, therapy follow-through, and a clear expectation that Armella will participate in the household.
- Terri Anne does not hover.
- Terri Anne also does not let Armella disappear into her room and avoid the day.
- The routine has become familiar enough that Armella can feel its shape before anyone explains it.
- That familiarity is part of why she is growing stronger.

### Beat 3: Armella Notices What Terri Anne Has Done

- Armella realizes that Terri Anne has been holding many things at once.
- Terri Anne has given her a bedroom.
- Terri Anne has coordinated Gulliver Preparatory School.
- Terri Anne has made sure therapy continues.
- Terri Anne has kept Rachel informed.
- Terri Anne has given Kella more space without making Armella feel abandoned.
- Terri Anne has treated Armella as accountable but not ruined.
- Armella understands that this is care.

### Beat 4: The Thank-You Begins Awkwardly

- Armella tries to thank Terri Anne and does not know how to begin.
- She may start with something small, like thanking her for dinner, the school pickup, or helping with a teacher email.
- Terri Anne hears the larger thank-you underneath the small one.
- Terri Anne does not rush her.
- Armella has to practice saying the real thing.
- The pause is part of the work.

### Beat 5: Armella Says It Clearly

- Armella finally thanks Terri Anne for taking her in.
- She thanks Terri Anne for letting her stay instead of making her go straight back to Los Angeles.
- She thanks Terri Anne for Gulliver.
- She thanks Terri Anne for not treating her like the worst thing she did.
- She thanks Terri Anne for not pretending the worst thing did not happen.
- She thanks Terri Anne for giving her rules without making the house feel like another treatment center.
- The thank-you is simple, but it costs Armella something to say it honestly.

### Beat 6: Terri Anne’s Answer

- Terri Anne accepts the thanks plainly.
- She does not make Armella’s gratitude into a performance.
- She does not say that everything is fine now.
- She tells Armella that family does not mean avoiding consequences.
- It also does not mean throwing someone away.
- She tells Armella that she is expected to keep doing the work.
- She also tells Armella that she is allowed to become stronger.
- Terri Anne’s answer is direct, steady, and unsentimental.

### Beat 7: Kella Is Named Carefully

- Armella brings up Kella.
- She may say that she knows Kella still needs space.
- She may say that she is grateful Terri Anne gave Kella that space too.
- Terri Anne does not speak for Kella.
- Terri Anne says Kella’s hurt is real.
- Terri Anne also says Armella’s work is real.
- Both truths can exist in the same room.
- Armella accepts that more calmly than she could have before treatment.

### Beat 8: Rachel Is Named Too

- Armella says she knows Rachel made the decision because she was trying to protect both daughters.
- She understands that more now than she did when Rachel first told her she was not returning straight to Los Angeles.
- Terri Anne confirms that Rachel’s decision was hard.
- Terri Anne does not let Armella turn Rachel’s difficulty into guilt.
- Rachel did what mothers do when one child needs space and another child needs care.
- Armella hears that and lets it settle.

### Beat 9: Gulliver as Proof of Strength

- Armella talks briefly about Gulliver Preparatory School.
- She says school is still hard.
- She also says she can do it.
- Terri Anne treats that as important.
- Gulliver is not magic.
- Gulliver is structure.
- Armella is growing stronger because she keeps getting up, going to school, doing homework, attending therapy, and telling the truth.
- Terri Anne makes sure Armella understands that strength is being built, not bestowed.

### Beat 10: A Small Domestic Gesture

- Terri Anne may hand Armella a plate, a cup of tea, a folded towel, or a school paper that needs signing.
- The ordinary gesture keeps the scene grounded.
- Armella is not being placed on a pedestal for saying thank you.
- She is being folded back into daily life.
- That is exactly what she needs.

### Beat 11: Final Image

- The final image is Armella sitting in Terri Anne’s house after thanking her.
- Her Gulliver school things are nearby.
- The Bluey plush doll may be visible in the room or upstairs on her bed.
- Terri Anne continues the ordinary routine around her.
- Armella is not fixed.
- Armella is stronger.
