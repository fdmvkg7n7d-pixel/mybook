# KELLA NAKEISHA AND CHARLIE JANYA — THE DORMITORY, RETHINKING THE OFFER

## Scene Outline — Beats, Dialogue Cues, and Thematic Notes

**POV:** Kella Nakeisha and Charlie Janya (alternating; mostly in the dark, mostly in their own heads)
**Tone:** Quiet. Private. The kind of thinking that happens when the lights are off and the day is done and the thing you said no to is still sitting in the room with you.
**Location:** Kella and Charlie Janya's dormitory room at UNLV, Las Vegas, Nevada. Night.
**Ages:** Kella Nakeisha and Charlie Janya are both ~18–20.
**Time in saga:** ~2075–2077 — the same night as the Reno shopping trip.

---

## CONTENT NOTE

This scene is about **rethinking** — not about deciding, not about acting. The cousins are in their beds, in the dark, and the recruiter's offer is still in the room with them. The drama is entirely internal and between the two of them: the slow, honest, private conversation that happens when two young women who trust each other admit that the thing they refused is not entirely gone from their minds. The saga's handling standard applies: **no graphic content.** The focus is on the cousins' interiority, their bond, and the question of what they want.

---

## BEFORE THE SCENE — WHAT THE COUSINS CARRY INTO THE DARK

*Establish, briefly, the state they are in when the lights go out.*

- Kella and Charlie Janya are back in their dormitory room at UNLV. They have showered, brushed their teeth, put on pajamas, done the ordinary rituals of the end of a day. They are in their beds. The lights are off.
- They have not talked about the recruiter on the drive home — or they have talked around it, or they have joked about it. But they have not said the thing they are now thinking.
- **The unspoken question:** The recruiter offered them real money. He was not wrong about the money. And they are university students, and university students think about money, and the thing they said no to is not the same thing as the thing they have forgotten.

---

## BEAT 1 — THE DARK

*The room is quiet. The day is over. The offer is still there.*

- The cousins are in their beds. The room is dark. They can hear each other breathing. They are not asleep.
- **Kella's interior:** She is thinking about the recruiter's words. *Real money. The kind of money that changes what your life looks like after university.* She is thinking about what her life looks like now — university, loans, the ordinary struggle of a young woman trying to build something. She is thinking about what it would mean to have that money. She is thinking about what it would cost.
- **Charlie Janya's interior:** She is thinking about the same thing. She is also thinking about her mother — Fallon Glenda, who became a mother at thirteen, who chose Charlie (her father), who built a life that was not the life anyone expected. Charlie Janya is thinking about what her mother would say. She is thinking about what her mother would *not* say.

**Thematic note:** The scene needs to sit in the dark for a moment before anyone speaks. The reader needs to feel the weight of the silence — the two cousins, each in her own bed, each thinking the same thing, each waiting for the other to say it first.

---

## BEAT 2 — THE FIRST WORDS

*One of them says it. The other one was already thinking it.*

- One of them speaks first. It could be either one — the author will decide. What matters is that the first words are honest, and that the other cousin was already thinking the same thing.
- **Dialogue cue (one of them, in the dark):** "I'm still thinking about it."
- **Dialogue cue (the other, after a beat):** "Me too."
- **The relief:** The thing that was unsaid is now said. The cousins are not alone in their rethinking. They are together in it, the way they are together in everything.

**Thematic note:** This is the saga's recurring theme of the bond between women who trust each other — the bond that allows them to say the thing that is hard to say, and to be met not with judgment but with *I was thinking it too.* Kella and Charlie Janya are cousins, not twins of each other, but they share the same year, the same university, the same room, and now the same unspoken thought. The scene should honor that bond.

---

## BEAT 3 — THE HONEST CONVERSATION

*They talk about it. Not the fantasy. The real thing.*

- The cousins talk about the offer. They talk about the money. They talk about what it would mean — not just the money, but the work, the Ranch, the town, the life. They talk about what they would lose and what they would gain.
- **Dialogue cue (Kella):** "It's not like we'd be the first women to do it."
- **Dialogue cue (Charlie Janya):** "It's not like we'd be the last."
- **Dialogue cue (Kella):** "Mom would lose her mind."
- **Dialogue cue (Charlie Janya):** "My mom wouldn't care."
- **The moment of recognition:** Kella hears Charlie Janya say that her mother wouldn't care, and Kella thinks about her own mother — Rachel Darcy, who is already angry about Armella, who is already holding this family together, who would be pretty angry about this. And Charlie Janya thinks about her mother — Fallon Glenda, who became a mother at thirteen, who knows what it is to make a choice that the world does not understand, who would not be angry, who would not be surprised, who would simply ask: *Is this what you want?*

**Thematic note:** The two mothers' responses are already present in this scene, even though the mothers are not. Rachel Darcy's anger and Fallon Glenda's acceptance are the two poles of the conversation — the two ways a mother can respond to her daughter's choice. The cousins know this. They are already living inside the difference.

---

## BEAT 4 — THE QUESTION

*They do not decide. They ask each other the question.*

- The cousins do not decide in this scene. They are not ready. What they do is ask each other the question — the real question, the one underneath the money and the Ranch and the mothers.
- **Dialogue cue (Charlie Janya, in the dark):** "Do you want to?"
- **Dialogue cue (Kella, after a long silence):** "I don't not want to."
- **Dialogue cue (Charlie Janya):** "That's not the same thing."
- **Dialogue cue (Kella):** "I know."
- **Silence.** The question is in the room. It is not answered. It does not need to be answered tonight.

**Thematic note:** The scene ends on the question, not the answer. The cousins are honest with each other — *I don't not want to* is an honest answer, and it is not the same as *yes,* and they both know it. The saga's recurring theme of female agency — the right to choose, the right to take time, the right to not know yet — is fully present here.

---

## BEAT 5 — THE DECISION

*They decide to call him.*

- The cousins talk. They think. They lie in the dark with the question in the room. And then, at some point — maybe late, maybe early, maybe one of them says it and the other one agrees — they decide.
- **They decide to call Mitch MacLead.** The card is in Charlie Janya's wallet. The phone number is on the card. They are going to call him.
- **Dialogue cue (one of them, in the dark):** "Let's call him."
- **Dialogue cue (the other, after a beat):** "Okay."
- **End image:** Two young women in their beds, in the dark, in a dormitory room in Las Vegas, who have made a decision. The city outside is bright. The room is dark. The card is in Charlie Janya's wallet. They are going to call him.

**Forward hooks:**
- The decision — **they decide to call him.** The card is in Charlie Janya's wallet. They are going to call Mitch MacLead.
- The phone call — what happens when they call.
- Telling their families — Rachel Darcy's anger, Fallon Glenda's acceptance.
- Mound House, Nevada — Kella's adult life, with Aesculapius and Melissa Amanda, in the town where the Bunny Ranch is located.
