# Scene Outline: Annabella Nicki at Temple University in the Autumn Marcos Signs With the Blue Jays

## Overview

- **Characters:** Annabella Nicki Key-d'Aboville, Rachel Darcy Key by phone/video, Marcos Eduarte Key-d'Aboville referenced, Melissa Lizette referenced.
- **Location:** Temple University, Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.
- **Timing:** The autumn of the year Marcos Eduarte signs his professional contract with the Toronto Blue Jays.
- **Status:** New scene outline.

---

## Placement

- Marcos Eduarte signs with the Toronto Blue Jays out of high school in the autumn.
- Rachel Darcy is physically present for the signing because Marcos is young enough that her legal presence/signature matters.
- Annabella Nicki is not in Toronto for the signing.
- Annabella is attending **Temple University**.
- She is staying in **Morgan Hall** with three other women.
- She is following Rachel Darcy’s footsteps by pursuing a serious university dance path.
- Melissa Lizette is also away at university, attending The University of Edinburgh.
- This scene shows how Marcos’s signing reaches Annabella from Toronto to Philadelphia.

---

## Core Situation

- Annabella Nicki is at Temple University when Marcos signs his Blue Jays contract.
- Annabella is staying in **Morgan Hall** with three other women.
- She is building a separate life in Philadelphia while Marcos begins his professional baseball life in Toronto.
- She is proud of him.
- She is also hit by the ache of triplet separation.
- Temple matters because Annabella is following Rachel Darcy’s footsteps through dance: not by attending the same university, but by taking dance seriously as an adult academic and artistic path.
- The signing makes Marcos’s future public and professional.
- The call makes Annabella feel how far away she is from the room where it happened.

---

## Scene Beats

### Beat 1: Annabella at Temple

- Annabella is on or near the Temple University campus when Rachel’s call comes.
- She may be in or near **Morgan Hall**, the dormitory she shares with three other women and that is becoming her Philadelphia home base.
- Philadelphia feels different from Toronto, Los Angeles, French Woods, and every family home.
- She is surrounded by university buildings, studio schedules, students moving between classes, and the ordinary noise of a campus day.
- Her life at Temple is beginning to become her own.
- She is following Rachel Darcy’s footsteps by choosing a serious dance path.
- That choice connects her to Rachel while still separating her from her siblings.

### Beat 2: Rachel Calls

- Rachel Darcy calls Annabella from Toronto after the signing or during the first wave of family calls.
- Annabella sees her mother’s name and knows the call matters.
- Rachel tells her that Marcos signed with the Toronto Blue Jays.
- Rachel says the full name is on the contract: **Marcos Eduarte Key-d'Aboville**.
- Annabella becomes very still.
- She is not surprised that Marcos signed.
- She is surprised by how much it hurts not to be in the room.

### Beat 3: The Triplet Distance

- Annabella is proud, but she is far away.
- Marcos is in Toronto.
- Melissa is in Edinburgh.
- Annabella is in Philadelphia.
- The original triplet shape is stretched across countries and cities.
- She thinks of French Woods, ballet, swimming, and the summers when the three of them were always findable by looking for the other two.
- Now Marcos belongs to the Blue Jays system.
- Melissa belongs to Edinburgh and language.
- Annabella belongs to Temple and dance.

### Beat 4: Following Rachel Darcy’s Footsteps

- Annabella thinks about Rachel Darcy’s history as a dancer and university dance student.
- Rachel’s path taught the family that dance could be more than childhood training.
- It could be study.
- It could be discipline.
- It could be a serious adult way of understanding a body.
- Annabella is following that path at Temple, but in her own quiet way.
- She is not trying to become Rachel.
- She is receiving one of Rachel’s inheritances and making it her own.

### Beat 5: Annabella’s Reaction

- Annabella’s reaction is quieter than Melissa’s would be.
- She notices details even through the phone: Rachel’s breath, the way her mother pauses before saying “signed,” and the way pride and fear sit together in the call.
- Annabella asks whether Marcos looked scared.
- Rachel answers honestly.
- Annabella asks whether he kept the full name.
- Rachel says yes.
- Annabella is relieved because the full name means Marcos has not made himself smaller to fit baseball.

### Beat 6: Message for Marcos

- Annabella asks Rachel to give Marcos a message.
- She wants him to know she is proud of him.
- She wants him to know he looked like himself even from far away.
- She may tell Rachel to say that he should not shorten the name, not ever.
- Rachel promises to tell him.
- Annabella trusts that Rachel will deliver the message exactly enough.

### Beat 7: After the Call

- The call ends.
- Annabella remains at Temple with the news sitting around her.
- Morgan Hall, shared with three other women, makes the distance from Toronto practical and real.
- Her studio clothes, books, or sketchbook are still nearby.
- The campus keeps moving as if nothing has happened.
- For Annabella, the family map has changed.
- She writes or sketches something small: a mound, a jersey back, the long name, or three marks standing apart.
- She does not need the sketch to be good.
- She needs it to hold the moment.

### Beat 8: Final Image

- The final image is Annabella Nicki at Temple University, holding the news of Marcos’s signing from Philadelphia.
- Morgan Hall is her dormitory now, shared with three other women.
- She is following Rachel Darcy’s footsteps through dance.
- Melissa is in Edinburgh.
- Marcos is in Toronto.
- The triplets are separated by geography for the first time in a way that feels permanent.
- Annabella is still his triplet sister.

## Emotional Notes

- The scene should hold pride and distance at the same time.
- Annabella should not be written as absent from love because she is absent from Toronto.
- Temple University should represent her adult dance path and her connection to Rachel Darcy.
- “Following Rachel Darcy’s footsteps” should mean taking dance seriously as an adult discipline, not becoming a copy of Rachel.
- The triplet bond should remain central even across geography.
