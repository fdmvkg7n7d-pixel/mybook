# SCENE OUTLINE: RACHEL CALLS ARMELLA AND CHLOË ABOUT MARCOS'S CONTRACT

## Placement

**When:** The same day Marcos Eduarte signs his professional contract with the Toronto Blue Jays.  
**Where:** Rachel's location TBD / Armella Beatrice in **Tampa Bay, Florida**.  
**Characters:** Rachel Darcy Key, Armella Beatrice Key-d'Aboville, Chloë Paris. Marcos Eduarte is present emotionally but not necessarily in the call.  
**Chloë Paris's age:** Twenty months.  
**Armella's status:** Pregnant with another child, later established as Wyatt Seth.  
**Context:** Marcos has signed with the Toronto Blue Jays out of high school. Rachel begins telling the family. When she calls Armella Beatrice, Armella is living in Tampa Bay, Florida with twenty-month-old Chloë Paris. Armella is pregnant with another child. Chloë Paris is Armella Beatrice's child and part of Armella and Sebastian's family. Armella is very excited for Marcos, and Chloë Paris responds to the excitement in age-appropriate toddler ways.

## Scene Purpose

This scene establishes:

- Rachel calling Armella Beatrice about Marcos's Toronto Blue Jays contract.
- Twenty-month-old Chloë Paris as part of Armella's immediate household in Tampa Bay.
- Armella being genuinely excited for Marcos Eduarte, with Chloë Paris reflecting that excitement in toddler ways.
- Armella's specific way of receiving the news through image, memory, and the visual symbolism of Marcos in a Blue Jays jersey.
- Armella being pregnant with another child during this family milestone; the later child is **Wyatt Seth**.
- Another warm family response that contrasts with Armando Cortez Junior's cold, secretive, indifferent call from Bangkok.

---

## Emotional Core

**The image of the moment.**

Armella has always been the sibling with the lens — the one who notices framing, light, distance, and what a moment will look like when people remember it later. When Rachel tells her Marcos has signed with the Blue Jays, Armella immediately understands the image: Marcos on a mound, in Toronto colors, with **KEY-D'ABOVILLE** across his back.

Chloë Paris is only twenty months old, so she does not understand the contract. But she understands Armella's excitement — the lifted voice, the sudden brightness, the energy around the phone. This is not a private reaction trapped inside Armella; the joy widens outward through the household.

---

## Tone

Warm, bright, visual, and excited, with a gentle toddler presence. The call should carry Armella's photographic imagination, Chloë Paris's twenty-month-old reactions, and the added tenderness of Armella being pregnant with another child.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — Rachel Calls Armella

Rachel has just come through the emotional intensity of Marcos signing with the Toronto Blue Jays.

Her son is officially a professional baseball player. He is not going to university. He is entering the Blue Jays system as a starting pitcher.

Rachel calls Armella Beatrice in Tampa Bay, Florida.

### Beat 2 — Armella Answers With Chloë Paris Nearby

Armella answers. Chloë Paris is nearby — twenty months old, close enough to react to Armella's voice and the phone. Armella is pregnant with another child, which gives the scene an additional layer: she is receiving news about her brother's future while carrying another future inside her own body.

Rachel says it plainly:

> "Marcos signed. Toronto. The Blue Jays."

Armella goes still for a beat, not because she is unmoved, but because she is seeing it.

### Beat 3 — Armella Sees the Jersey

Armella's first emotional response is visual.

She imagines Marcos in a Blue Jays jersey. She imagines the mound. She imagines the long name across his back.

Possible line:

> "Mom, his name is going to be on the jersey. The whole name."

Rachel knows exactly what Armella means.

### Beat 4 — Chloë Paris Responds Like a Toddler

Armella repeats the news aloud with Chloë Paris nearby.

Chloë Paris is twenty months old, so she does not ask baseball questions. She reacts to Armella: clapping, babbling, reaching for the phone, repeating a word like "ball," or leaning against Armella's pregnant belly while the adults talk.

Chloë Paris should feel present and loved, but not overdefined until her relationship/context is confirmed.

### Beat 5 — Armella's Sisterly Pride

Armella's pride is specific to who she is.

She understands that Marcos has stepped into a future that can be seen. A contract is paper, but the jersey, the mound, the first photograph — those are how the family will remember it.

Possible line:

> "Someone has to take the first picture of him in that jersey."

### Beat 6 — Rachel Receives the Warmth

Rachel lets herself receive Armella and Chloë Paris's excitement.

After the uncertainty of calling scattered adult children, another warm response steadies her.

Armella may ask Rachel to tell Marcos that she wants the first official photo, or that she is proud of him. The pregnancy can subtly shape the beat: Armella is imagining Marcos's future while feeling the movement or weight of the child she is carrying.

### Beat 7 — Message for Marcos

Armella gives Rachel a message for Marcos.

Chloë Paris may babble, clap, or say one small toddler word into the phone.

The call ends with Rachel carrying another pocket of joy back into Marcos's signing day.
