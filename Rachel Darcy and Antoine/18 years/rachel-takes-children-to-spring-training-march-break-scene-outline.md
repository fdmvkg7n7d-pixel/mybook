# Scene Outline: Rachel Darcy Takes the Children to Spring Training for March Break

## Overview

- **Characters:** Rachel Darcy Key, Marcos Eduarte Key-d'Aboville, Alessandrine Ariana, Kaisa Kalyca, Felipe Hephaestus, Spyridon Jacenty, Armella Beatrice Key-d'Aboville, Sebastian, Chloë Paris, Wyatt Seth, Toronto Blue Jays players and families.
- **Location:** Florida spring training, with Armella Beatrice and Sebastian’s home as the family base.
- **Timing:** March Break, shortly after Halcyon and Jacián Damaskinos’s deaths and before Marcos’s third-game-of-the-season perfect game in Toronto.
- **Status:** New scene outline.

---

## Placement

- Halcyon and newborn Jacián Damaskinos died in February, just before spring training.
- Marcos Eduarte was placed on compassionate bereavement leave by the Toronto Blue Jays.
- Previous spring training, Halcyon drove the children down to Florida to see Marcos Eduarte.
- Halcyon always took the children to spring training too.
- During March Break, Rachel Darcy takes Marcos’s four surviving children to Florida for spring training, continuing the tradition Halcyon used to carry.
- The children are still living in Toronto at this point.
- The triplets are still attending school in Toronto.
- The later Spring Mountain Vineyard move has not happened yet.
- While the players are at spring training, they can stay anywhere they want.
- Marcos is not required to stay in a team hotel or team housing.
- Marcos chooses to stay with Armella Beatrice and Sebastian during this Florida period.
- Rachel and the children can also stay with Armella Beatrice and Sebastian’s family.

---

## Armella and Sebastian’s Household

- Armella Beatrice and Sebastian’s home becomes the family base during March Break.
- Armella Beatrice and Sebastian have two children by two different men.
- Their children are **Chloë Paris** and **Wyatt Seth**.
- Chloë Paris and Wyatt Seth are part of the household rhythm when Rachel arrives with Marcos’s children.
- The household is full, busy, and emotionally warm.
- It gives Marcos’s children a family environment instead of a hotel.

---

## Core Situation

- Rachel Darcy brings the children to Florida so they can see Marcos during spring training.
- This is something Halcyon always did with the children too, so her absence is felt immediately.
- The children become very emotional when they see Marcos Eduarte.
- Marcos is also deeply emotional because he has been away from them while trying to return to baseball.
- The children are allowed to be on the field and watch him.
- Other players’ older children hug Marcos’s children.
- The spring training environment becomes a temporary baseball-family village around the children’s grief.

---

## Scene Beats

### Beat 1: Rachel Plans the March Break Trip

- Rachel Darcy decides the children need to see Marcos during March Break.
- Previous spring training, Halcyon drove the children down to Florida to see Marcos Eduarte.
- Halcyon always took the children to spring training too, so Rachel understands that not going would make the loss feel even sharper.
- The triplets are still attending school in Toronto, so March Break creates the first practical opening.
- Rachel does not frame the trip as a vacation.
- She frames it as family contact, stability, and reassurance.
- The children have seen Marcos in grief and in baseball, but they need to see where he is when he is away.
- They need proof that Florida does not mean disappearance.
- They also need proof that the spring training tradition still belongs to them, even without Halcyon.
- The memory of Halcyon driving them down the previous spring training is still fresh enough to shape the whole trip.

### Beat 2: The Previous Spring Training Memory

- The previous spring training trip is vivid for the children.
- Halcyon drove them down to Florida herself to see Marcos Eduarte.
- The drive had snacks, bags, rest stops, small arguments, music, and Halcyon’s practical attention to every child.
- For the children, Florida spring training is connected to their mother’s hands on the steering wheel and her voice organizing the trip.
- Rachel knows she cannot recreate that exactly.
- She can only continue the route so the children do not lose the tradition on top of losing Halcyon.

### Beat 3: Staying With Armella Beatrice and Sebastian

- Rachel brings the children to Armella Beatrice and Sebastian’s home in Florida.
- Marcos is already staying there rather than isolating himself in a hotel.
- Spring training rules allow players to choose where they stay, so this is a permitted family arrangement rather than a special exception.
- Armella’s household is active and lived-in.
- Chloë Paris and Wyatt Seth are present as part of Armella and Sebastian’s family.
- The children enter a house with cousins, food, noise, bags, shoes, and ordinary family movement.
- Rachel is grateful that the children can sleep under family care instead of in an anonymous room.
- Armella understands without being told that the children need softness and structure.

### Beat 4: Chloë Paris and Wyatt Seth

- Chloë Paris and Wyatt Seth help establish that Armella and Sebastian’s house is a family home, not just a place to sleep.
- Chloë Paris responds to the visiting children with curiosity, affection, or age-appropriate excitement.
- Wyatt Seth responds in his own age-appropriate way.
- The fact that Armella Beatrice and Sebastian have two children by two different men is simply part of the household’s structure.
- No one treats that structure as shameful or strange.
- The Key family’s ordinary rule applies: family is the people who show up and care.

### Beat 5: The Children See Marcos

- The emotional center of the arrival is the moment the children see Marcos Eduarte.
- The children are carrying the memory that Halcyon drove them here the previous spring training.
- Alessandrine Ariana, Kaisa Kalyca, and Felipe Hephaestus become very emotional.
- Spyridon Jacenty reacts with immediate physical need, wanting Marcos’s arms.
- Marcos tries to stay steady, but the children’s reaction breaks through his composure.
- He knows they are not only reacting to seeing him.
- They are also reacting to being at spring training without Halcyon.
- He gets down to their level or pulls them close.
- The reunion is not dramatic for show.
- It is the children confirming that Daddy is still reachable.

### Beat 6: Marcos at Armella and Sebastian’s House

- Marcos staying with Armella Beatrice and Sebastian matters.
- Because players can stay anywhere they want during spring training, Marcos chooses a family house over a hotel.
- It shows that he is not trying to survive alone between practices.
- It gives him a place to return after the field where he is brother, father, and grieving husband rather than only pitcher.
- Armella watches him carefully because she sees images other people miss.
- Sebastian helps make the house practical: meals, rides, schedules, doors opening and closing, children moving through rooms.
- Rachel sees that Marcos is safer here than he would be alone.

### Beat 7: Going to the Spring Training Field

- Rachel takes the children to the spring training field to watch Marcos.
- The Blue Jays staff remember that Halcyon drove the children down the previous spring training and regularly brought them to see Marcos.
- The Blue Jays staff know who the children are and why this matters.
- The children are allowed to be on the field in appropriate areas.
- They are allowed to watch Marcos warm up, throw, stretch, and move through drills.
- The field is bright, open, and very different from the Toronto penthouse.
- For the children, it helps connect Daddy’s absence to a real place.
- He is not vanishing when he goes to baseball.
- He is here, on this field, doing work they can see.
- The field is also a place where Halcyon stood with them after driving them down the previous spring training, so the children feel both closeness and loss at once.

### Beat 8: Watching Marcos Practice

- The triplets watch Marcos with intense concentration.
- They are old enough to connect his body on the mound with the perfect game they will later see in Toronto.
- Spyridon understands less, but he knows which player is Daddy.
- Marcos is aware of them watching.
- His mechanics are not as sealed off as usual because he keeps feeling their presence.
- The coaches and players give him room.
- No one makes the children feel like an inconvenience.

### Beat 9: Other Players’ Older Children

- Other Blue Jays players’ older children notice Marcos’s children.
- Some of those children are old enough to know that their mother died and that the baby died too.
- They approach carefully, guided by the emotional rules children learn around clubhouses and family areas.
- The older children hug Marcos’s children.
- The hugs are awkward, sincere, and deeply moving.
- Alessandrine Ariana and Kaisa Kalyca receive the hugs differently but do not pull away.
- Felipe Hephaestus is quiet but allows himself to be included.
- Spyridon may not understand why everyone is hugging, but he recognizes kindness.

### Beat 10: Baseball Family

- The other players’ children create a circle around Marcos’s children without making speeches.
- This is not the same as losing Halcyon.
- It does not fix anything.
- It does show the children that baseball is not only the thing that takes Marcos away.
- Baseball also contains people who know them, see them, and care what happened.
- Rachel notices this and stores it away.
- Marcos notices it too and is almost undone by it.
- The hugs acknowledge what everyone at spring training can feel: Halcyon should have been the one bringing the children here.

### Beat 11: Marcos and the Children on the Field

- Marcos brings the children close after practice or during an approved break.
- The children are allowed to stand with him on the field.
- He shows them small, safe details: the mound from a distance, the dugout, the grass, the warm-up area, or where he stands before throwing.
- He does not turn the field into a performance.
- He makes it understandable.
- The children need to know what Daddy’s baseball world looks like.
- Marcos needs to see them inside it without feeling that he has betrayed Halcyon.

### Beat 12: Rachel Watches Marcos Balance Both Lives

- Rachel watches Marcos moving between player and father.
- She sees the tension in his shoulders when coaches need him and children need him at the same time.
- She sees him try not to fail either world.
- Rachel understands that this is the problem he will carry all season.
- Spring training makes the conflict visible: baseball has a schedule, and grief does not.
- Rachel begins to understand what kind of support Marcos will need if he is going to remain both a pitcher and a father.

### Beat 13: Returning to Armella and Sebastian’s House

- After the field, the family returns to Armella and Sebastian’s house.
- The children are tired in a different way than they were when they arrived.
- They have seen Marcos’s baseball world.
- They have been hugged by other players’ children.
- They have watched him work.
- Chloë Paris and Wyatt Seth fold back into the household’s ordinary noise.
- Armella and Sebastian’s home becomes the place where the field experience can soften into dinner, showers, pajamas, and sleep.

### Beat 14: Marcos’s Quiet Relief

- That night, Marcos is quieter than usual.
- He is relieved the children saw him.
- He is also afraid because seeing him may make future separations harder.
- Rachel tells him that the children needed the truth of where he goes.
- She tells him that seeing the field was better than imagining it.
- She also tells him that continuing Halcyon’s spring training tradition mattered.
- Marcos admits that watching the other players’ children hug his children nearly broke him.
- Rachel understands.
- The hugs were not a cure.
- They were proof that the children are not invisible.

### Beat 15: Final Image

- The final image is the children asleep or settling in Armella Beatrice and Sebastian’s house after seeing Marcos at spring training.
- Chloë Paris and Wyatt Seth are part of the household’s night sounds.
- Marcos is under the same roof as his children for the moment.
- Rachel is awake, thinking ahead to Toronto, school, baseball, and the impossible calendar of grief.
- Outside, Florida spring training continues.
- Inside, the children have seen Daddy’s field and know he is still theirs.

## Emotional Notes

- The March Break trip should feel necessary, not recreational.
- Halcyon always taking the children to spring training should make Rachel’s trip feel like a continuation of a mother’s tradition, not a replacement for it.
- The children becoming very emotional when they see Marcos should be the emotional center of the arrival.
- Armella and Sebastian’s home should feel like family infrastructure around grief.
- Chloë Paris and Wyatt Seth should establish Armella and Sebastian’s household without turning the scene into their full backstory.
- Marcos staying with Armella and Sebastian should show that he is accepting family support.
- The children being allowed on the field should feel generous and protective, not chaotic.
- The other players’ older children hugging Marcos’s children should be gentle, awkward, and sincere.
- Rachel should observe how baseball can both separate Marcos from his children and create a community around them.
