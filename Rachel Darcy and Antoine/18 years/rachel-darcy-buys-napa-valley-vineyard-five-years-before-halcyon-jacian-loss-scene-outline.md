# Scene Outline: Rachel Darcy Buys Spring Mountain Vineyard

## Overview

- **Characters:** Rachel Darcy Key, Marcos Eduarte Key-d'Aboville, Halcyon, Alessandrine Ariana, Kaisa Kalyca, Felipe Hephaestus, vineyard staff / realtor / estate lawyer as needed.
- **Location:** Spring Mountain Vineyard, Spring Mountain District, Napa Valley, California.
- **Timing:** Five years before Halcyon and newborn Jacián Damaskinos die during childbirth, during a Toronto Blue Jays road trip to play the San Francisco Giants.
- **Status:** New scene outline.

---

## Placement

- This scene happens **five years before** the February childbirth loss of Halcyon and baby Jacián Damaskinos.
- Halcyon is alive.
- The Toronto Blue Jays are in San Francisco playing the San Francisco Giants.
- Marcos Eduarte is in the Bay Area with the team, and the family uses the trip to see the Spring Mountain property.
- Marcos Eduarte and Halcyon are already building their adult life together.
- Their first children, the triplets **Alessandrine Ariana**, **Kaisa Kalyca**, and **Felipe Hephaestus**, are very young.
- **Spyridon Jacenty** has not been born yet.
- **Jacián Damaskinos** has not been conceived yet.
- Rachel Darcy does not know that this vineyard will later become part of the family’s grief geography.

---

## Core Situation

- Rachel Darcy buys **Spring Mountain Vineyard** in Napa Valley.
- At first, it is an investment property.
- On the surface, it is a beautiful California property, a Cabernet Sauvignon estate, and a long-term family investment.
- Until Halcyon and Jacián Damaskinos die, Spring Mountain Vineyard functions primarily as an investment property with professional staff and limited public tasting.
- Underneath, it is Rachel doing what the Key family always does: building a place before the family knows it will need one.
- Spring Mountain Vineyard becomes another sanctuary property, different from Carlisle but emotionally connected to it.
- Carlisle is the family fortress.
- Spring Mountain Vineyard becomes a place of sunlight, rootedness, recovery, and long views.

---

## Scene Beats

### Beat 1: The Blue Jays Road Trip to San Francisco

- The Toronto Blue Jays are in San Francisco to play the San Francisco Giants.
- Marcos Eduarte travels with the team for the road series.
- Rachel Darcy, Halcyon, and the young triplets are also in California during the series.
- The road trip creates the practical opening for the family to see Spring Mountain Vineyard.
- The vineyard visit is fitted around Marcos’s baseball schedule, team obligations, and game days.
- San Francisco becomes the bridge between Marcos’s Blue Jays life and the Napa Valley property Rachel is considering.

### Beat 2: Rachel Arrives at Spring Mountain Vineyard

- Rachel Darcy arrives at Spring Mountain Vineyard on a clear California morning.
- The property sits in the Spring Mountain District of Napa Valley.
- The vineyard is spread across mountain slopes and terraces, with neat rows of vines, a weathered estate house, and a small working production area.
- The air smells like dry grass, warm soil, grapes, oak barrels, and sun.
- Rachel notices the land physically before she thinks about the purchase legally.
- As a dancer, she reads the slope of the ground almost like choreography.
- The property feels old, quiet, and patient.

### Beat 3: Why Napa Matters

- California is not random for Rachel.
- Rachel and Antoine built much of their adult family life in Los Angeles.
- The seven children were born into a West Coast chapter of the family before later arcs pulled everyone across the continent.
- Spring Mountain Vineyard gives Rachel a California anchor that is not Los Angeles, not a performance city, and not a hospital or professional obligation.
- It is land.
- It is agriculture.
- It is a place where time moves by seasons rather than schedules.

### Beat 4: The Property Tour

- A realtor, estate manager, or vineyard steward walks Rachel through the property.
- They show her the main house, guest cottages, barrel room, tasting room, old stone walls, and the vines.
- Rachel asks practical questions about water rights, staff housing, harvest cycles, Cabernet Sauvignon production, soil, fire risk, road access, privacy, limited public tasting, and long-term management.
- She is not buying a fantasy.
- She is buying a real place with real maintenance and real responsibility.
- The staff quickly understand that Rachel is not a celebrity buyer looking for a postcard.
- She is a mother and grandmother evaluating whether a place can hold a family.

### Beat 5: Marcos and Halcyon Visit

- Marcos Eduarte and Halcyon come to see the vineyard with the young triplets while the Blue Jays are in San Francisco playing the Giants.
- Marcos is still learning how strange it feels to be both a professional athlete and a young father.
- Halcyon walks the property with careful attention, reading exits, shade, sightlines, and quiet places the way someone with her history always does.
- Alessandrine Ariana, Kaisa Kalyca, and Felipe Hephaestus are small enough to turn the rows of vines into a maze.
- Rachel watches Halcyon watching the children.
- Rachel sees that Halcyon relaxes more here than she expected.
- That matters.

### Beat 6: Rachel’s Private Reason

- Rachel does not tell everyone the full private reason at first.
- She is not buying the vineyard only for wine.
- She is buying it because families need places that are not built around crisis.
- She wants a place where Marcos, Halcyon, and the children can go when baseball, media, Toronto, and Carlisle all become too loud.
- She wants a place where her grandchildren can run between vines and still be protected.
- She wants a place where Halcyon can feel safe without feeling watched.
- She wants a place where Rachel herself can stand in the sun and feel something growing that does not ask her to perform.

### Beat 7: The Business Decision

- Rachel reviews the purchase details with precision.
- Spring Mountain Vineyard includes the land, residence, working vines, equipment, existing staff contracts, and brand rights.
- Rachel decides to keep the experienced vineyard staff rather than sweeping in and replacing everyone.
- Continuity matters to her.
- People who know the land are part of the land’s safety.
- She buys the vineyard as herself, not as a decorative extension of the Key empire.
- For the first five years, it remains an investment property rather than a permanent family residence.
- Limited public tasting is allowed, carefully managed, and kept small enough that privacy remains intact.
- It is Rachel Darcy’s property.
- It is Rachel Darcy’s decision.

### Beat 8: Halcyon’s Reaction

- Halcyon does not gush.
- Her response is quieter than that.
- She asks if the children would really be allowed to come here.
- Rachel makes clear that the vineyard is being bought for family use, not only investment.
- Halcyon looks across the rows of vines and understands that Rachel is offering another version of Carlisle.
- Not a rescue.
- Not a cage.
- A place with doors, food, beds, land, and adults who mean what they say.
- Halcyon’s shoulders loosen.

### Beat 9: Marcos Understands Later Than Halcyon

- Marcos initially sees the vineyard as beautiful and generous, especially because he is mentally split between the property visit and the Blue Jays road series against the Giants.
- He understands the practical side: privacy, space, off-season refuge, a place away from cameras.
- Halcyon understands the emotional architecture sooner.
- Rachel is building a place where a family can breathe before anyone is drowning.
- Marcos eventually sees it when he watches his children run to Rachel from between the vines.
- He realizes his mother is not only buying land.
- She is buying a future gathering place.

### Beat 10: The Signing

- Rachel signs the purchase papers for Spring Mountain Vineyard.
- The legal act is quiet compared to what it will mean later.
- There is no grand toast required.
- Rachel may taste the vineyard’s wine, but the emotional center is not the wine.
- The emotional center is the deed, the keys, the land, and the knowledge that another family place now exists.
- Rachel stands in the house after the signing and listens to the quiet.
- She can already imagine the rooms full.

### Beat 11: Five Years Before the Loss

- The scene should carry dramatic irony without making Rachel prophetic.
- No one knows Halcyon will die five years later.
- No one knows Jacián Damaskinos will be born and lost on the same day.
- No one knows Rachel will later need every sanctuary she has ever built.
- Spring Mountain Vineyard is not bought because tragedy is coming.
- The vineyard is bought because Rachel believes families deserve beauty before tragedy comes.

### Beat 12: Final Image

- The final image is Rachel Darcy standing at the edge of the vines as late afternoon light turns the rows gold.
- Halcyon is nearby with the young triplets.
- Marcos is watching his children run in the safe open space.
- Rachel holds the keys or the signed folder in one hand.
- Spring Mountain Vineyard is hers now.
- Five years later, it will mean something no one in this scene can yet bear to know.

### Future Canon: After Halcyon and Jacián Damaskinos Die

- For the five years between the purchase and the tragedy, Spring Mountain Vineyard remains primarily an investment property.
- The estate produces Cabernet Sauvignon.
- Limited public tasting is allowed, but it is controlled, quiet, and secondary to privacy.
- After the initial Toronto period following Halcyon and Jacián Damaskinos’s deaths, Marcos Eduarte asks Rachel Darcy to take care of his children at Spring Mountain Vineyard.
- Rachel’s role changes from owner-investor to daily grandmother and household anchor on the property.
- This happens after Marcos’s first start back, when the children are still living in Toronto and the triplets are still attending school in Toronto.
- In the middle of the summer of Halcyon and Jacián Damaskinos’s deaths, Marcos decides that his surviving children should live at Spring Mountain Vineyard permanently.
- The decision is not made in the first shock of February.
- It comes after months of seeing the children breathe more easily among the vines, sun, space, and Rachel’s steady care.
- The children can attend schools in San Francisco.
- The family arranges the necessary transportation, schedules, and support so the children can live at the vineyard while being educated in San Francisco.
- Spring Mountain Vineyard stops being only an investment property and becomes the children’s permanent home base.

## Emotional Notes

- Rachel should be calm, practical, and quietly visionary.
- Spring Mountain Vineyard should not feel like a luxury purchase only.
- It should feel like a Key-family sanctuary being established before the family knows it will need it.
- Halcyon’s reaction should be subtle and grounded in her history of needing safe places.
- Marcos should recognize the emotional meaning more slowly than Halcyon.
- The five-year timing should create gentle dramatic irony, not melodramatic foreshadowing.
