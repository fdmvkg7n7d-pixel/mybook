## Core Situation

- Melissa and Annabella are beginning school in different cities.
- The first-day energy is exciting, frightening, and lonely.
- Marcos and the sisters call to wish them good luck.
- The calls do not erase the distance.
- The calls make the distance survivable.
- The family stretches around Melissa and Annabella instead of letting them step into the school year alone.

## Scene Beats

### Beat 1: Annabella’s First School Morning at Temple

- Annabella Nicki wakes in **Morgan Hall** in Philadelphia at the beginning of her school year, sharing space with three other women.
- Temple University is no longer a place she is visiting.
- It is where she is beginning.
- She thinks about dance, Rachel Darcy, studio space, class schedules, and the fact that she is not sharing the morning with Melissa and Marcos.
- Her phone is nearby before she leaves for the day.
- She expects calls, but still feels lonely before they come.

### Beat 2: Melissa’s First School Morning in Edinburgh

- Melissa Lizette wakes in **Baird House** in Edinburgh at the beginning of her school year, sharing space with another woman.
- The city feels old, wet, beautiful, and far from everyone.
- Her Linguistics and English Language MA (Hons) notes, schedule, and books are already beginning to claim her desk.
- She thinks about language, names, and how strange it is that her brother’s full name is now part of a professional baseball contract.
- The time difference makes everything slightly off.
- She is starting the same family season as Annabella, but in a completely different clock.

### Beat 3: Marcos Calls Annabella

- Marcos calls Annabella before her day fully begins or during a quiet gap.
- He wishes her good luck at Temple.
- He is already inside the Blue Jays path, so his good-luck call carries the strange weight of someone whose own year will not look like school.
- He tells her that Rachel’s footsteps do not mean she has to become Rachel.
- Annabella knows he means it.
- She asks how he is doing with the Blue Jays schedule.
- He gives a short answer because the day is about her.
- The call is brief, but it steadies her.

### Beat 4: Marcos Calls Melissa

- Marcos calls Melissa in Edinburgh.
- The time difference makes the call feel slightly unreal.
- He wishes her good luck with Linguistics and English Language.
- Melissa jokes that he had better learn how to pronounce his own name in every accent if he expects broadcasters to manage it.
- Marcos laughs because only Melissa would turn his baseball life into a language problem.
- The laugh helps both of them.
- He tells her he misses her.
- She says she misses him too, then quickly says something lighter because otherwise she will cry.

### Beat 5: Larissa Calls

- Larissa Leola calls both sisters in her own warm, expansive way.
- She tells Annabella that Philadelphia is lucky to have her.
- She tells Melissa that Edinburgh will not know what hit it.
- Larissa’s good-luck wishes are full of older-sister confidence.
- She makes both girls feel as though leaving home is not abandonment.
- It is a family-approved act of becoming.

### Beat 6: Kella Calls

- Kella Nakeisha calls with theatrical sincerity.
- She knows what it means to step into a campus and try to become yourself there.
- She tells Annabella to dance like she is not asking permission.
- She tells Melissa to let language belong to her.
- Kella’s support is vivid and emotional.
- She does not let distance make her casual.
- Both Melissa and Annabella feel held by her intensity.

### Beat 7: Armella Calls

- Armella Beatrice calls more quietly.
- She is attentive, observant, and careful with both sisters.
- She asks Annabella what the dance studios feel like.
- She asks Melissa what Edinburgh sounds like.
- Her questions are specific because Armella notices things that other people skip.
- Her good-luck wishes feel gentle and precise.
- She tells them that starting somewhere new is brave even when everyone expects you to act excited.
- That lands with both of them.

### Beat 8: Rachel Watches the Calls Land

- Rachel Darcy is aware of the calls even if she is not present for every one.
- She may receive texts afterward, see missed-call screenshots, or hear from the girls how everyone reached out.
- Rachel understands that this is how the family protects distance.
- Not by refusing separation.
- By sending voices across it.
- She is proud of all of them.
- She is also sad because the triplets are not in one room anymore.

### Beat 9: The Sisters Compare Notes

- Melissa and Annabella text each other after some of the calls.
- They compare who called first, who cried, who tried to joke, and what Marcos said.
- The messages become a small bridge between Edinburgh and Philadelphia.
- Melissa may say that Marcos sounds older already.
- Annabella may say that he sounds scared but determined.
- They both understand that they are all beginning different years.
- The triplets are still connected, but now connection has to be chosen and maintained.

### Beat 10: First Steps Into the School Year

- Annabella leaves for her Temple day with the calls still inside her.
- Melissa leaves for her Edinburgh day with the calls still inside her.
- Neither feels completely ready.
- Both feel less alone.
- Marcos’s good-luck call and the sisters’ good-luck calls become part of the emotional threshold into the school year.

### Beat 11: Final Image

- The final image is split between Philadelphia and Edinburgh.
- Annabella steps out of Morgan Hall and into Temple carrying dance, Rachel’s footsteps, and the voices of her family.
- Melissa steps out of Baird House and into Edinburgh carrying linguistics, language, and the voices of her family.
- Marcos is not with them.
- The sisters are not with them.
- But the calls have made the distance audible.
- The school year begins.
