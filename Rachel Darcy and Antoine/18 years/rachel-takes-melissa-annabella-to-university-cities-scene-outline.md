# Scene Outline: Rachel Takes Melissa Lizette and Annabella Nicki to Their University Cities

## Overview

- **Characters:** Rachel Darcy Key, Melissa Lizette Key-d'Aboville, Annabella Nicki Key-d'Aboville, Marcos Eduarte Key-d'Aboville referenced.
- **Locations:** Philadelphia, Pennsylvania; later Edinburgh, Scotland.
- **Universities:** Temple University for Annabella Nicki; The University of Edinburgh for Melissa Lizette.
- **Timing:** Autumn of the year Marcos Eduarte signs with the Toronto Blue Jays.
- **Status:** New scene outline.

---

## Placement

- Marcos Eduarte has signed with the Toronto Blue Jays in the autumn.
- Melissa Lizette is beginning **Linguistics and English Language MA (Hons)** at **The University of Edinburgh**.
- Annabella Nicki is beginning her path at **Temple University**, following Rachel Darcy’s footsteps through dance.
- Annabella Nicki will stay in **Morgan Hall** at Temple with three other women.
- Melissa Lizette will stay in **Baird House** at The University of Edinburgh with another woman.
- Rachel Darcy takes both girls to their university cities.
- Melissa accompanies Rachel and Annabella to Philadelphia first.
- After Philadelphia, Rachel continues with Melissa toward Edinburgh.
- This is one of the major separation moments for the triplets.

---

## Core Situation

- Rachel Darcy is taking her two daughters into separate adult lives.
- Annabella is going to Philadelphia and will live in **Morgan Hall** with three other women.
- Melissa is going to Edinburgh and will live in **Baird House** with another woman.
- Marcos is entering professional baseball with the Toronto Blue Jays.
- The triplets are no longer moving as one unit.
- In Philadelphia, Rachel, Melissa, and Annabella get one hotel room.
- Melissa and Annabella sleep in the same bed for their last time.
- The moment is quiet, intimate, and sibling-centered.
- It marks the end of one form of childhood closeness and the beginning of adulthood across different cities.

---

## Scene Beats

### Beat 1: Leaving Together

- Rachel leaves with Melissa Lizette and Annabella Nicki for the university trip.
- The bags are different from camp bags.
- These are not French Woods trunks for a summer session.
- These are university bags.
- The difference is felt before anyone says it.
- Melissa has Edinburgh ahead of her.
- Annabella has Philadelphia ahead of her.
- Marcos has the Blue Jays ahead of him.
- Rachel feels the triplet unit stretching in three different directions.

### Beat 2: Melissa Comes to Philadelphia First

- Melissa accompanies Rachel and Annabella to Philadelphia.
- She does not have to come, but she wants to.
- She wants to see where Annabella will live.
- She wants to stand beside her sister before the ocean comes between them.
- Annabella is quietly grateful.
- Rachel notices that Melissa’s presence steadies Annabella.
- Melissa is not performing cheerfulness.
- She is choosing to be present for the first goodbye.

### Beat 3: First Sight of Philadelphia

- Philadelphia feels different from Los Angeles, Toronto, French Woods, and every family house.
- The city is old, dense, busy, and full of history.
- Rachel watches Annabella watch it.
- Annabella takes in streets, buildings, campus edges, studio possibilities, and the feeling of being small in a real city.
- Melissa takes it in differently, already thinking about the next city, the next flight, and the distance to Edinburgh.
- Rachel sees both reactions at once.

### Beat 4: Temple University and Morgan Hall

- Rachel and Melissa accompany Annabella to Temple University.
- Annabella sees the campus not as a visitor now, but as a student.
- Annabella will stay in **Morgan Hall** with three other women.
- Morgan Hall makes the Temple transition concrete: a room shared with three other women, a building, a key or access card, elevators, hallways, and the first physical shape of her Philadelphia life.
- The dance spaces matter most to her.
- Rachel understands that immediately.
- Rachel sees her own younger self flicker in Annabella’s posture.
- Annabella is following Rachel Darcy’s footsteps, but not copying her.
- She is entering dance as herself.
- Melissa watches Annabella become real in the new place.

### Beat 5: The Hotel Room

- That night, Rachel, Melissa, and Annabella get one hotel room.
- The hotel room is practical, temporary, and emotionally loaded.
- It has enough space for bags, travel clothes, university paperwork, and the strange silence before separation.
- Rachel takes one bed or gives the girls the bed that makes sense for them.
- Melissa and Annabella share the same bed.
- They know without saying it that this is the last time.
- Not because they will never be close again.
- Because childhood closeness is changing shape.

### Beat 6: The Girls in the Same Bed

- Melissa and Annabella lie beside each other in the hotel bed.
- They are not children at French Woods anymore.
- They are not little triplets after a swimming class or ballet day.
- They are almost adult women on the edge of separate cities.
- Melissa is going across the Atlantic.
- Annabella is staying in Philadelphia.
- Marcos is entering professional baseball.
- The bed holds the last physical shape of the sisters before their adult geography takes over.

### Beat 7: What They Talk About

- The sisters talk quietly so Rachel can sleep or pretend to sleep.
- They talk about Temple.
- They talk about Edinburgh.
- They talk about Marcos signing with the Blue Jays.
- They talk about whether the triplets will still feel like triplets when they are not in the same place.
- Melissa may joke lightly because otherwise she will cry.
- Annabella may answer with one precise sentence that makes Melissa cry anyway.
- They do not solve the distance.
- They name it.

### Beat 8: Rachel Listening

- Rachel is awake even if her eyes are closed.
- She hears enough of the conversation to understand what the girls are doing.
- She does not interrupt.
- She lets them have the bed and the dark and the last sister-night.
- Rachel remembers being separated from Jessica Isabella in ways her daughters cannot fully understand.
- She knows that separation is sometimes chosen, sometimes surgical, sometimes geographic, and always bodily.
- She lets Melissa and Annabella hold each other through this chosen separation.

### Beat 9: Morning in Philadelphia

- Morning comes too quickly.
- The girls wake in the same bed for the last time.
- No one says the phrase immediately.
- The hotel room becomes a place of small movements: showers, folded pajamas, repacked bags, coffee, hair, shoes, and checking the time.
- Melissa and Annabella move around each other with the old triplet ease.
- Rachel watches and knows this ease will become rarer now.

### Beat 10: Annabella’s First Goodbye

- Rachel and Melissa take Annabella to Temple, Morgan Hall, or the place where the goodbye has to happen.
- The goodbye is not theatrical.
- It is too real for that.
- Melissa hugs Annabella tightly.
- Annabella holds on longer than she planned.
- Rachel hugs Annabella after or before Melissa.
- Rachel tells Annabella that following her footsteps does not mean walking in her shadow.
- Annabella receives that and carries it into Temple.

### Beat 11: Philadelphia Without Annabella

- After leaving Annabella, Rachel and Melissa return to the car, train station, airport, or hotel corridor without her.
- The absence is immediate.
- Melissa feels it sharply because she came to Philadelphia to help deliver Annabella into this new life.
- Now Annabella is where she is supposed to be.
- That does not make leaving her easy.
- Rachel gives Melissa space to feel the first half of the separation before the second half begins.

### Beat 12: Toward Edinburgh

- Rachel and Melissa continue toward the next city.
- Edinburgh is farther than Philadelphia in every way.
- It is across an ocean.
- Melissa’s university path is built around language, structure, sound, and meaning.
- The trip now turns from Annabella’s dance inheritance to Melissa’s linguistic future.
- Rachel understands that she is not only delivering daughters to school.
- She is delivering the triplets into adulthood.

### Beat 13: Final Image

- The final image is Rachel and Melissa leaving Philadelphia after Annabella’s goodbye, carrying the memory of the hotel room with them.
- Annabella is at Temple University, with **Morgan Hall** now becoming the building she returns to at night, where she lives with three other women.
- Melissa is on her way to The University of Edinburgh, where **Baird House** is waiting for her and she will live with another woman.
- Marcos is signed with the Toronto Blue Jays.
- The triplets still belong to one another.
- They no longer belong to one room.

## Emotional Notes

- The hotel room should feel ordinary and sacred at the same time.
- Melissa and Annabella sleeping in the same bed for the last time should be written as sibling closeness, not melodrama.
- Rachel’s silence while listening should carry her own history with Jessica Isabella and bodily separation.
- Melissa accompanying Annabella to Philadelphia should show love in action.
- Annabella following Rachel Darcy’s footsteps should mean dance as serious adult study, not imitation.
- The scene should hold pride, grief, distance, and the beginning of adult independence together.
- The beginning-of-school-year good-luck calls are handled in a separate scene outline.
