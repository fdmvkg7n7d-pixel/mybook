## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene outlines the emotional moments when **Rachel Darcy Key tells her partner Antoine about Brandi Jessica’s retinoblastoma diagnosis**. 

As Kiley Rose's older sister (twenty-five) who has just received the crushing news from New York, Rachel is preparing to travel to Manhattan to join the family at the townhouse. This private conversation with her equal-aged partner, Antoine, serves as a major emotional milestone. 

They are **now living in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania**. The scene explores the theme of reproductive and bodily asymmetry: Rachel, who cannot biologically carry children due to her conjoined separation, reflects on the absolute fragility and preciousness of Kiley's children (sired from a rare spontaneous Turner Syndrome pregnancy) now facing this aggressive pediatric cancer as she and Antoine prepare for **the births of their upcoming twins, Larissa Leola and Armando Cortez Junior.**

Crucially, **they are supposed to visit Antoine's family in France for the holidays.** However, **Antoine understands that Rachel Darcy needs to be with her family in New York, so he cancels their joint travel and flies to France alone** to visit his parents, demonstrating his deep relational understanding, love, and support for Rachel’s place as a sister during this family crisis.

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 1: The Urgent Call from New York to Philadelphia
- It is the afternoon of December 23, 2051. Rachel Darcy and Antoine d'Aboville are **now living in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania**, where Rachel continues her dance work and they prepare for their upcoming surrogacy plans.
- Rachel is in her studio room at their Philadelphia residence, her mind still catching its breath after receiving the agonizing call from Kiley Rose in New York City.
- She is packing her bags frantically, her hands trembling. Her niece Brandi Jessica (six years old) has just been diagnosed with advanced Bilateral Retinoblastoma at nine o'clock this morning.
- Antoine enters the room, immediately noticing her frantic, tear-streaked face. He drops what he is carrying, steps in close, and gently catches her wrists to steady her.

### Beat 2: Telling Antoine
- Rachel Darcy collapses against his chest for a brief moment, then looks up and **tells Antoine about Brandi Jessica's devastating condition**:

> “Kiley called... they took Brandi Jessica and Pandora Paige to the pediatrician for their annual check-up at nine o'clock this morning. The doctor found it, Antoine. Brandi has Bilateral Retinoblastoma. The tumors are highly aggressive. To save her life, they have to remove both of her eyes. Her operation was supposed to be in January, but because of the kidnapping, they had to push it back.”

- Antoine holds her tightly, absorbing the crushing shock of the news. He asks quietly about Pandora Paige, and Rachel explains that doctors screened her as a precaution because she is Brandi’s identical twin. Pandora’s examination was clear: no tumors or retinal disease. Pandora never had retinoblastoma and required no treatment.

### Beat 3: Reflecting on Bodily and Reproductive Asymmetry
- Sitting together on the edge of the sofa in their Philadelphia living room, Rachel Darcy becomes incredibly reflective, sharing her deepest vulnerabilities with Antoine. 
- She reflects on the profound asymmetry of their bodies: Kiley, who has Turner Syndrome and was supposed to be infertile, was blessed with this rare, miraculous spontaneous twin pregnancy, only for her children to face this brutal pediatric cancer.
- Rachel, who began her life conjoined to Jessica Isabella and received none of the shared reproductive organs, looks at Antoine with wet eyes, reflecting on **their upcoming identical twins, Larissa Leola and Armando Cortez Junior, who will be carried by their surrogate Alexis**:

> “It is so unfair, Antoine. Kiley wasn't supposed to have children, and she was blessed with these beautiful twins, only for the world to try and take Brandi's sight. And here we are... living here in Philadelphia, unable to carry biologically, preparing for Alexis to carry our twins, **Larissa Leola and Armando Cortez Junior,** next year. It makes me realize how fragile and precious every single child is.”

- Antoine pulls her close, kissing her forehead with absolute, unyielding devotion. He reassures her that their path to parenthood—though different—is built on pure, deliberate love, and that their family is ready to wrap both Kiley and her daughters in a protective shield.

### Beat 4: The Decision — Antoine Flies to France
- As Rachel finishes packing her bags for New York, she suddenly stops, looking at their international flight itinerary on the desk. **They are supposed to visit Antoine's family in France for the holidays.** 
- She turns to Antoine, her eyes full of conflict: *"Antoine, your parents... our trip to France. I can't go. I can't leave Kiley right now."*
- Antoine doesn't hesitate for a single second. He cups her face, demonstrating his deep relational understanding, maturity, and absolute support:
  
> “Rachel, I understand. Your sister needs you. Brandi and Pandora need you. You belong with your family in New York right now. I will go to France myself to visit my parents. They will understand completely, and I will explain everything to them. You go to Carlisle, and go to Manhattan. Be there for Kiley.”

- For Rachel, hearing her partner release her from her holiday obligations to his family without a shred of guilt is a profound, grounding relief. It confirms how deeply he respects the unyielding bonds of her family.

### Beat 5: Closing Image — The Promise of Family
- Antoine helps her finish packing, ensures she has her tickets, and drives her straight to the Philadelphia train station so she can join Mike, Terri Anne, Sabrina, Andrea, and Ashley at Kiley and Julia's townhouse in Manhattan tonight.
- **Antoine flies to France alone**, while Rachel boards the train to New York. At the door, Rachel Darcy looks at Antoine. Even in the shadow of the devastating February operation, his steady, loving support gives her the quiet strength to be the strong older sister Kiley needs right now.
- She touches her bare collarbone on the train, feeling the unshakeable weight of their shared future. She knows that when she arrives in New York, she will stand with her family as an unbreakable wall of love.
