# Rachel Darcy and Antoine Discuss Fallon Glenda — Scene Outline

> **Scope:** The evening of May 3, 2045. Hours after Mike Key called Charlottesville to tell twenty-year-old Rachel Darcy the devastating news about her twelve-year-old sister, Fallon. The day has passed in a heavy, distracted blur. Now, in the quiet of their apartment, Rachel Darcy and her partner, Antoine, sit down to process the reality of the trauma. The scene focuses on Rachel Darcy’s visceral, bodily understanding of Fallon's vulnerability, and Antoine’s steadfast, grounding presence.
>
> **Time:** May 3, 2045. Evening.
> **Place:** Rachel Darcy and Antoine’s apartment in Charlottesville, Virginia.
> **Characters present:** Rachel Darcy Key (20), Antoine.
> **Mood:** Heavy, visceral grief grounded by deep, steady partnership.

---

## 1. The Heavy Evening

- **The atmosphere:** It is dark outside. Rachel Darcy is sitting on the floor of their Charlottesville apartment, her back against the sofa, her knees pulled up to her chest. She is a dancer; she rarely sits completely still, but tonight, she is frozen. 
- **The day’s toll:** She went to her classes today because it was easier than sitting at home, but she remembers nothing from them. The fact of the phone call has been ringing in her ears all day: *She’s twelve. She's pregnant. A swimming teacher in a position of trust.*
- **Antoine’s presence:** Antoine comes into the living room holding two mugs of tea. He doesn't ask how she is—he knows how she is. He sets the tea down, sits on the floor right beside her, and simply presses his shoulder against hers. 

## 2. The Bodily Empathy

- **Rachel Darcy speaks:** She breaks the silence, her voice tight. She doesn’t talk about the logistics or what Mike is doing. She talks about Fallon’s body.
- **The dancer’s perspective:** As a dancer, Rachel Darcy’s entire life is about bodily autonomy, control, and physical safety. She tells Antoine what it means to be twelve years old—how small Fallon still is, how her body was just starting to change, and how a grown man chose to make that body a site of trauma. 
- **The physical horror:** *"She was alone in that body for weeks,"* Rachel Darcy says, her voice breaking. *"Every day waking up and feeling it change and thinking it was her fault."* The visceral horror of Fallon carrying that physical reality in silence is what wrecks Rachel Darcy the most.

## 3. Antoine’s Grounding Response

- **The Anchor:** Antoine does not offer empty platitudes. He does not say *everything happens for a reason* or *she’ll be fine.* He listens to the darkest, sharpest edges of Rachel Darcy’s grief and holds them with her.
- **The redirect:** When Rachel Darcy spirals into anger at the swimming teacher, her breathing getting shallow, Antoine gently takes her hand. He redirects her focus from the man who hurt Fallon to the family who is saving her.
- **The reminder:** *"Your dad is there,"* Antoine says quietly, his voice a steady, low rumble. *"Your dad, and your mom. They have her. The door opened, and she isn't alone in it anymore."*

## 4. The Distance and the Urgency

- **The geography:** Rachel Darcy expresses the deep frustration of being in Virginia while her little sister is in Brazil (and soon to be in Ontario). She is the athletic, physical sister; she wants to physically stand in front of Fallon and shield her.
- **The instruction remembered:** She recalls what she told her dad on the phone that morning: *"Tell her I love her. Tell her right now, today."* She asks Antoine if he thinks Mike actually said it.
- **Antoine's certainty:** Antoine smiles, a small, sad, absolute smile. *"It's Mike Key. He probably said it before he hung up the phone."*

## 5. Preparing to Be the Shield

- **Looking ahead:** Rachel Darcy takes a deep breath, uncurling her legs. She leans her head on Antoine's shoulder. 
- **The promise:** She tells Antoine that when they go to Carlisle to see Fallon, they will not treat her like glass. Fallon is an athlete, a horseback rider, a girl who used to run around the Carlisle estate. Rachel Darcy vows that she will help Fallon reclaim her physical confidence. *"We'll take her outside. We won't let her hide."*
- **The closing image:** Antoine kisses the top of her head. The two of them sit on the floor of the Charlottesville apartment, looking forward, already preparing to help Fallon rebuild her life when the time comes.

## 6. Character Work

### Rachel Darcy (20)
- **The Visceral Sister:** Because her life is rooted in physical expression (dance, movement), her empathy for Fallon is profoundly bodily. She understands the sheer physical terror of losing autonomy over a twelve-year-old body, which makes her grief sharp and specific.

### Antoine
- **The Grounding Force:** Antoine operates as a steady, unflappable anchor. He doesn't try to fix the unfixable; he just sits on the floor with her and reminds her of the one truth that matters: Mike and Terri Anne have Fallon now.

## 7. Thematic Motifs

- **Bodily Autonomy:** A major theme of Rachel Darcy's arc is physical agency. Framing Fallon's trauma through Rachel Darcy's eyes emphasizes the violation of a child's bodily autonomy and sets up Rachel Darcy's later role in helping Fallon reclaim her physical confidence (like riding horses again).
- **The Distance:** The geographic spread of the Key sisters (Virginia, Halifax, Vaughan, Brazil) highlights the emotional weight of the phone calls. They cannot physically shield her yet, so they have to trust the parents to do it until they can gather in Carlisle.
- **The Steadfast Partners:** Antoine’s quiet support mirrors Emma’s support for Jessica Isabella. The partners of the Key sisters understand that loving them means holding space for the massive, intense gravity of the Key family’s love for one another.
