## Core Situation

- Armella has to pick an extracurricular activity at Gulliver.
- The choice matters because it is one more step into ordinary school life.
- Terri Anne does not choose for her.
- Rachel does not choose for her.
- Armella chooses the cheerleader team.
- The choice surprises her because cheerleading means being visible, coordinated with others, and responsible to a group.
- It gives Armella a structured team activity where she has to show up, listen, practice, and move with other girls rather than disappear behind the camera.
- It also lets her build strength, confidence, rhythm, and school belonging.

## Scene Beats

### Beat 1: The Extracurricular Form

- Armella receives an extracurricular form at Gulliver Preparatory School.
- The form lists clubs, sports, arts activities, service groups, academic teams, and school-spirit activities.
- The ordinary nature of the form surprises her.
- It assumes she is a student who will choose something.
- It does not treat her as a patient.
- It does not treat her as a problem.
- It asks what she wants to do.
- That question feels heavier than it should.

### Beat 2: Terri Anne Lets Her Choose

- Armella brings the form home to Terri Anne.
- Terri Anne reads it without making a production of it.
- Armella asks what she should pick.
- Terri Anne does not answer for her.
- Terri Anne says the point is for Armella to choose something she can show up for.
- The activity should not be about punishment, image repair, or proving she is fixed.
- It should be something she can attend regularly and honestly.
- Armella understands that Terri Anne is giving her responsibility, not pressure.

### Beat 3: Armella Notices the Cheerleader Team

- Armella studies the list.
- Some activities feel too quiet.
- Some feel too much like hiding.
- Some feel too much like performing goodness.
- Then she sees the cheerleader team.
- The option makes her pause.
- Cheerleading is visible.
- Cheerleading is physical.
- Cheerleading requires trust, timing, practice, and teamwork.
- It is not something she can do entirely from behind a lens.

### Beat 4: Why Cheerleading

- Armella thinks about why the cheerleader team catches her attention.
- She has spent so much of her life watching people.
- She has spent so much of her life behind the camera.
- Cheerleading would ask her to be seen as herself.
- It would ask her to move with other girls and not use identical-twin confusion, distance, or observation as protection.
- It would ask her to learn timing, placement, responsibility, and trust.
- That frightens her.
- It also feels like exactly the kind of structured risk that could help her grow stronger.

### Beat 5: The Cheer Coach

- Armella meets the Gulliver cheer coach.
- The coach explains expectations plainly.
- Cheerleaders attend practices.
- Cheerleaders learn routines.
- Cheerleaders show up for games and school events.
- Cheerleaders represent the school.
- Cheerleaders are responsible to each other.
- The coach makes clear that it is not only smiling and uniforms.
- It is discipline, conditioning, timing, safety, and team trust.
- Armella listens carefully because rules help her feel safe.

### Beat 6: Armella Chooses

- Armella chooses the cheerleader team as her extracurricular activity.
- She signs up for it herself.
- The act is small, but it matters.
- It is a choice made after treatment, in a new school, inside a structured environment.
- She is not being assigned to it by adults.
- She is not choosing it to escape accountability.
- She is choosing it because it gives her a way to practice visibility, teamwork, and follow-through.

### Beat 7: Telling Rachel

- Armella tells Rachel Darcy during a phone call.
- Rachel is glad to hear that Armella chose something.
- Rachel asks why the cheerleader team.
- Armella explains that photography is still hers, but she does not want to only watch people anymore.
- She says cheerleading will make her show up with other people and be responsible to them.
- Rachel hears the growth in that answer.
- Rachel is so proud of Armella for choosing the cheerleader team.
- Rachel tells Armella that cheerleading is like ballet in important ways.
- It has counts, timing, formations, balance, extensions, strength, discipline, and trust in the people moving beside you.
- Rachel understands why a structured physical team could help Armella become stronger.
- Armella becomes very emotional when Rachel tells her she is proud of her.
- The pride lands harder than Armella expected because it is not vague praise.
- Rachel is proud of a specific choice Armella made for herself.
- Armella starts crying during or just after the call.
- Terri Anne sees Armella crying and starts hugging her.
- Terri Anne does not interrupt Rachel’s support.
- Terri Anne’s hug gives Armella somewhere physical to put the emotion.
- Rachel supports the choice without any single doubt.
- Rachel does not overpraise her.
- She tells Armella that showing up regularly will matter more than saying the right thing about it.

### Beat 8: Kella’s Reaction

- Rachel later tells Kella, or Armella tells Kella directly, that Armella chose the cheerleader team.
- Kella is happy to hear it.
- Kella understands that this is not Armella hiding behind photography.
- Kella also understands that Armella being visible at school is a brave step.
- Kella supports Armella without any single doubt.
- Kella’s happiness does not erase the past.
- It shows that she can want Armella to grow stronger even while still needing space.

### Beat 9: First Practice

- Armella attends her first cheerleader team practice.
- The gym or field space feels loud, bright, and exposed.
- She learns names.
- She listens to counts.
- She watches the coach demonstrate steps and formations.
- She feels awkward because she cannot disappear into observation.
- She has to move.
- She has to count.
- She has to stand in formation.
- She has to trust that mistakes can be corrected without becoming disasters.

### Beat 10: The Team

- The other girls on the team are not instantly best friends.
- They are teammates first.
- That is enough.
- They help Armella learn where to stand.
- They correct her counts.
- They include her in the practice rhythm.
- The ordinary teamwork matters more than emotional speeches.
- Armella begins to understand that belonging can be built through repetition.

### Beat 11: Reporting Back to Terri Anne

- Armella comes home after the first practice.
- Terri Anne asks how it went.
- Armella gives a practical answer first.
- Then she admits it was hard to be visible.
- She also admits it felt good to be part of a team.
- Terri Anne treats that as important.
- A team means trust.
- A team means responsibility.
- A team means returning next practice and doing it again.
- Armella is tired, but steadier.

### Beat 12: Photography Remains Personal

- Armella does not give up photography.
- Her camera remains part of her personal practice.
- She may photograph small details after practice: shoes by a gym bag, sunlight on bleachers, hands tying ribbons, or the empty field.
- Photography still helps her notice the world.
- Cheerleading helps her enter the world.
- Both can exist.
- She does not need to choose only one way of being.

### Beat 13: Final Image

- The final image is Armella at Terri Anne’s table with her cheer practice schedule beside her homework.
- Her camera is nearby, but the cheerleader team form is signed.
- Gulliver is no longer only a place she attends.
- It is a place where she has a team.
- Armella is still healing.
- Armella is growing stronger.
