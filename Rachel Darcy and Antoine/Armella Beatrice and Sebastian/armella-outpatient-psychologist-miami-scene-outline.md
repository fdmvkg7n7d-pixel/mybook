## Overview

- **Characters:** Armella Beatrice Key-d'Aboville, Terri Anne Williams-Key, outpatient psychologist in Miami, Rachel Darcy Key referenced, Kella Nakeisha Key-d'Aboville referenced.
- **Location:** Miami, Florida — outpatient psychology office; Terri Anne’s car / home afterward.
- **Timing:** During Armella’s post-treatment transition, after Skyland Trail, while she is living with Terri Anne and attending Gulliver Preparatory School.
- **Status:** New scene outline.

## Core Situation

- Armella begins seeing a psychologist as an outpatient in Miami.
- This is not a punishment.
- This is continuation of care.
- Skyland Trail gave her intensive treatment and tools.
- The Miami outpatient psychologist helps her practice those tools in ordinary life: school, home, family calls, accountability, and future reintegration with Kella.
- Terri Anne helps make the appointment structure real by driving her, waiting for her, and bringing her home afterward.

---

## Scene Beats

### Beat 1: The Appointment on the Calendar

- Terri Anne writes the outpatient psychology appointments into the household schedule.
- The appointments are biweekly unless the psychologist is on vacation.
- They sit beside Gulliver classes, homework, meals, therapy follow-up, and calls with Rachel.
- Armella sees the appointment on the calendar and feels two things at once.
- She is relieved that there is a plan.
- She is also tired of needing plans around her.
- Terri Anne does not let the appointment become shameful.
- She treats it like medical care, school, or any other necessary responsibility.

### Beat 2: Driving to Miami

- Terri Anne drives Armella to the psychologist’s office in Miami.
- The car ride is quiet at first.
- Armella watches the city through the window.
- Miami feels different from Atlanta, different from Los Angeles, and different from Terri Anne’s house.
- This is not the controlled world of Skyland Trail.
- This is ordinary life with therapy folded into it.
- Terri Anne does not fill the silence just to make it easier.
- She lets Armella breathe.

### Beat 3: The Waiting Room

- The psychologist’s office is calm but not institutional.
- Armella notices chairs, plants, magazines, intake forms, a water cooler, and the quiet way people avoid staring at each other.
- She remembers the first night at Skyland Trail and how strange it felt to enter treatment.
- This is different.
- She is not moving in.
- She is coming for an hour and then going back to school, homework, Terri Anne, and Gulliver.
- That difference matters.

### Beat 4: Meeting the Psychologist

- The psychologist greets Armella directly.
- They do not speak only to Terri Anne.
- The psychologist explains confidentiality and its limits in age-appropriate, clear language.
- The psychologist explains that outpatient therapy is a place to keep practicing the skills Armella learned at Skyland Trail.
- Armella listens carefully.
- She is wary of being treated like a case file.
- The psychologist’s calm directness helps.

### Beat 5: What Armella Carries In

- Armella does not tell the whole story all at once.
- She begins with the facts she can say.
- She went to Skyland Trail.
- She hurt Kella.
- She is living with Terri Anne now.
- She is going to Gulliver Preparatory School.
- She is trying to do better without pretending better means finished.
- The psychologist does not rush her.
- The psychologist lets Armella set down one piece at a time.

### Beat 6: Treatment Goals

- The psychologist asks what Armella wants help with now.
- Armella says she wants to keep thinking before acting.
- She wants to stop confusing shame with accountability.
- She wants to respect Kella’s space without collapsing into despair.
- She wants to manage school without feeling like everyone can see her history on her face.
- She wants to become stronger without pretending she is fixed.
- The psychologist writes these goals down.
- Seeing the goals written makes them feel possible and frightening.

### Beat 7: Kella Is Present Without Being There

- Kella is not in the room, but Kella is present in everything Armella says.
- Armella talks about not pushing for forgiveness.
- She talks about wanting Kella to know she is working without demanding that Kella reward her for working.
- The psychologist names that as an important distinction.
- Repair is not a performance.
- Repair is repeated behavior over time.
- Armella writes that down or repeats it quietly.

### Beat 8: Gulliver and Ordinary Days

- The psychologist asks about Gulliver Preparatory School.
- Armella talks about classes, hallways, homework, cheerleader team interest, and being new.
- She says school is tiring but not impossible.
- She says the routine helps.
- She says Terri Anne’s house helps because the expectations are clear.
- The psychologist points out that ordinary days are part of recovery.
- Armella had not thought of ordinary days as work before.
- Now she does.

### Beat 9: Terri Anne’s Role

- The psychologist asks what living with Terri Anne is like.
- Armella says Terri Anne is direct.
- Terri Anne does not hover.
- Terri Anne does not let her disappear.
- Terri Anne gives structure without making the house feel like Skyland Trail.
- Armella admits that she needs that.
- Saying she needs it is hard.
- It is also true.

### Beat 10: Ending the First Session

- The first session ends before everything is solved.
- That is part of the point.
- The psychologist schedules the next appointment.
- The regular plan is biweekly appointments.
- If the psychologist is on vacation, the appointment schedule adjusts around that absence.
- Armella leaves with a small assignment, reflection, or practice skill.
- It is something manageable: notice one moment when she wants to hide, write down what happened, and bring it next time.
- Armella can do that.
- She is not sure she wants to.
- She can still do it.

### Beat 11: Terri Anne Waiting

- Terri Anne is waiting when Armella comes out.
- She does not ask for details Armella does not want to give.
- She asks whether Armella is ready to go home.
- Armella says yes.
- Terri Anne accepts the answer.
- The restraint matters.
- Armella is allowed to have a private therapy space.
- Terri Anne is still responsible for getting her there and home.

### Beat 12: The Drive Back

- On the drive back, Armella is quieter but not shut down.
- She tells Terri Anne one small thing: the psychologist said ordinary days are part of recovery.
- Terri Anne says that sounds right.
- She does not turn it into a lesson.
- Armella watches the road and lets the sentence settle.
- Therapy is not only what happens in the office.
- Therapy is also going back to the house and doing the next ordinary thing.

### Beat 13: Reporting to Rachel

- Later, Rachel asks how the appointment went.
- Armella gives a short answer at first.
- Then she says she thinks the psychologist might help.
- Rachel is relieved, but she keeps her response steady.
- She does not make Armella responsible for Rachel’s relief.
- Rachel says she is proud of Armella for going.
- Armella hears it and lets it land a little.

### Beat 14: Final Image

- The final image is Armella at Terri Anne’s home after the first outpatient appointment.
- Her Gulliver schoolwork is nearby.
- The therapy assignment is tucked into a notebook.
- The day is not dramatic anymore.
- That is the point.
- Armella is living the bridge between treatment and ordinary life.
- She is not fixed.
- She is practicing.

## Emotional Notes

- The outpatient psychologist should feel like continuation of care, not punishment.
- Terri Anne’s role is logistical, steady, and respectful of Armella’s privacy.
- Armella should not be expected to tell the whole story immediately.
- Kella’s space should remain central without requiring Kella to appear in the scene.
- Gulliver Preparatory School should be part of recovery because ordinary days are practice.
- The scene should show Armella growing stronger through repetition, accountability, and support.
