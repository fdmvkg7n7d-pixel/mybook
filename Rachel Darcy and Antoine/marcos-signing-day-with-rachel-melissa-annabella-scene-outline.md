# SCENE OUTLINE: MARCOS SIGNING DAY WITH RACHEL, MELISSA, AND ANNABELLA

## Placement

**When:** The day Marcos Eduarte signs his professional contract with the Toronto Blue Jays.  
**Where:** Toronto / Blue Jays signing location TBD.  
**Characters:** Marcos Eduarte Key-d'Aboville, Rachel Darcy Key, Melissa Lizette Key-d'Aboville, Annabella Nicki Key-d'Aboville.  
**Context:** Marcos has been drafted out of high school by the Toronto Blue Jays as a starting pitcher. He does not attend university. The triplets are seventeen, so Rachel Darcy has to sign / co-sign the contract as Marcos's mother/legal guardian. On the day of the signing, Rachel Darcy and Marcos's triplet sisters Melissa Lizette and Annabella Nicki are with him.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene establishes:

- Marcos crossing the threshold from high-school baseball into professional baseball while still seventeen.
- Rachel Darcy physically present because she has to sign / co-sign the contract for Marcos as his mother/legal guardian.
- Melissa Lizette and Annabella Nicki present as his triplet sisters, anchoring him at the exact moment he begins a life path that will separate him from them.
- The emotional reality that Marcos is the one signing, but the triplet unit is still there.
- The beginning of Rachel’s wider family calls about Marcos’s contract.
- This signing day leads directly into Marcos finding **Halcyon** in Clarence Park later the same day.

---

## Emotional Core

**The triplet unit at the edge of separation.**

Marcos is entering a professional contract, but because he is seventeen, Rachel has to sign / co-sign it. The future is his. The mound is his. But the legal signature also belongs to his mother, and Melissa and Annabella are with him because they were his first world.

This is not a scene where they hold him back. They are there to witness him becoming separate.

Rachel sees all of it: her son stepping into professional baseball, her daughters standing beside him, and the shape of the triplets changing without breaking.

---

## Tone

Quietly monumental. Emotional but controlled. The scene should feel like a family holding its breath in a professional room where everyone else is focused on paperwork, parental consent, signatures, schedules, and development plans.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — The Room Before the Signature

Marcos sits with the contract in front of him, but Rachel is the one who has to legally sign / co-sign because he is seventeen.

The Blue Jays representatives are present or nearby, but the emotional focus is on the family side of the room.

Rachel is close enough to see the pen and the signature lines that require her name. Melissa Lizette and Annabella Nicki are with him, not hovering, but present in the way only triplets can be — close even when they are silent.

### Beat 2 — Rachel Watches Her Son

Rachel understands the magnitude of what is happening.

Marcos is not going to university. He is entering directly into the Blue Jays system, but because he is still seventeen, Rachel's signature is required. That makes the moment heavier: she is not only witnessing his choice; she is legally helping open the door. The organization will test his arm, manage his innings, send him through Dunedin, Vancouver, back to Dunedin, New Hampshire, Buffalo, and eventually — if everything holds — Toronto.

Rachel is proud, but she is also frightened. Baseball is no longer a dream she can protect from a distance. It has become a contract.

### Beat 3 — Melissa Lizette’s Reaction

Melissa is visibly emotional in her own way.

She may be the triplet who reacts with warmth, movement, or a sudden attempt at lightness. She understands that Marcos’s dream is real now, but she also understands that real means away.

Possible line:

> “You’re actually doing it.”

It can sound excited and a little wounded at the same time.

### Beat 4 — Annabella Nicki’s Reaction

Annabella is quieter.

She notices details:

- the way Marcos holds the pen;
- the angle of his shoulders;
- the Blue Jays logo on the paperwork;
- Rachel’s hand tightening around the edge of her chair;
- Melissa trying not to cry or trying not to joke too much.

Annabella may not say much, but when she does, it lands.

Possible line:

> “You look like yourself.”

### Beat 5 — Rachel Signs / Co-Signs

Because Marcos is seventeen, Rachel Darcy has to sign / co-sign the contract.

Marcos may sign where he is allowed to sign, but Rachel's signature is the legal threshold. The moment should not be overblown. It is a hand moving across paper. But everyone in the room understands that Rachel's hand is helping move her son's whole life.

His name still matters:

> **Marcos Eduarte Key-d'Aboville**

The full name goes onto the contract before it ever goes onto a jersey. Rachel signs beside or beneath that name, knowing she is authorizing a future that will take him away from ordinary childhood.

### Beat 6 — The Immediate Aftermath

There may be congratulations from the Blue Jays representatives. There may be handshakes, folders, schedules, first instructions, talk of medical evaluation, arm testing, development plans, and reporting dates.

Marcos hears some of it. Rachel hears all of it, because her signature has made her responsible in a different way.

Melissa and Annabella hear the parts that mean he will be leaving.

### Beat 7 — The Triplets Together

After the official moment, the triplets have a smaller private beat.

Melissa may hug him first. Annabella may touch his sleeve or stand close enough that Marcos feels her there. Marcos may not have words yet.

This should not become melodramatic. The point is that the three of them know something has shifted.

Possible exchange:

> Melissa: “You’re still ours.”  
> Marcos: “I know.”  
> Annabella: “No. You’re still you.”

### Beat 8 — Rachel Begins Calling the Family

Rachel steps into the next role: telling the wider family.

She begins making calls — Larissa in Queensland, Kella in Mound House, Armella in Tampa Bay, Armando in Bangkok, and others as needed.

Marcos, Melissa, and Annabella remain nearby. The calls ripple outward from the room where the signing just happened.

### Beat 9 — The Pull Toward the Park

After the contract and the first family calls, Marcos becomes overloaded by the room: the signatures, the Blue Jays representatives, the development plans, the fact that Rachel had to sign because he is still seventeen, and the knowledge that his life has changed before he has even had time to feel it.

He needs air.

This beat should point toward the next major same-day event: Marcos leaving the signing-day pressure and going to Clarence Park, where he will find **Halcyon**.

The scene does not need to include the full Halcyon meeting, but it should establish the emotional reason he ends up in the park: he is not celebrating loudly; he is trying to breathe.

### Beat 10 — Final Image

The final image is simple:

Marcos’s contract on the table with Rachel Darcy’s signature on it. Rachel’s phone in her hand. Melissa and Annabella beside their brother.

The Blue Jays future has begun, but for one last moment before the system takes him — and before he walks toward the park where he will find Halcyon — Marcos is still standing inside the original shape of his life: his mother and his two triplet sisters with him.
