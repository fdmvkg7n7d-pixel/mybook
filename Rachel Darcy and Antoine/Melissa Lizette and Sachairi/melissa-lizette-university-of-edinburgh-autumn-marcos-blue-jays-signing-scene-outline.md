- **Program:** **Linguistics and English Language MA (Hons)**.

## Placement

- Marcos Eduarte signs with the Toronto Blue Jays out of high school in the autumn.
- Rachel Darcy is involved in the signing because Marcos is still young enough that her legal presence/signature matters.
- Melissa Lizette is not in Toronto for the signing.
- Melissa is attending **The University of Edinburgh**.
- She is studying **Linguistics and English Language MA (Hons)**.
- She is staying in **Baird House** with another woman.
- The news reaches her in Scotland through Rachel Darcy.
- This scene shows how Marcos’s signing lands across the ocean for one of his triplet sisters.

## Core Situation

- Melissa Lizette is at the University of Edinburgh in the autumn when Marcos signs his Blue Jays contract.
- Melissa is staying in **Baird House** with another woman.
- Annabella Nicki is also away at university, attending Temple University and staying in **Morgan Hall** with three other women.
- She is building a separate life in Scotland while Marcos is beginning his professional baseball life in Toronto.
- She is proud of him.
- She is also hit by the ache of triplet separation.
- The signing makes Marcos’s future public and professional.
- The call makes Melissa feel how far away she is from the room where it happened.

---

## Scene Beats

### Beat 1: Melissa at Edinburgh

- Melissa is on or near the University of Edinburgh campus when Rachel’s call comes.
- She may be in or near **Baird House**, the dormitory she shares with another woman and that is becoming her Edinburgh home base.
- The city around her is stone, rain, old buildings, narrow streets, and student movement.
- Her books and notes are for **Linguistics and English Language MA (Hons)**.
- She has been thinking about language, sound, structure, meaning, and how people carry identity through words.
- That makes the call about Marcos’s name feel especially sharp.
- His full name is about to become a professional baseball name.
- Her own work is about language.

### Beat 2: Rachel Calls

- Rachel Darcy calls Melissa from Toronto after the autumn signing or during the first wave of family calls.
- Melissa sees her mother’s name and knows immediately that the call matters.
- Rachel tells her that Marcos signed with the Toronto Blue Jays.
- Rachel says the whole name is on the contract: **Marcos Eduarte Key-d'Aboville**.
- Melissa goes still.
- She is not surprised that Marcos signed.
- She is surprised by how much it hurts not to be in the room.

### Beat 3: The Triplet Distance

- Melissa is proud, but she is also far away.
- Marcos and Annabella are not beside her.
- The original triplet shape is stretched across continents.
- Melissa feels the distance physically.
- She thinks of French Woods, ballet, swimming, and all the summers when the three of them moved as a unit.
- Now Marcos belongs to the Blue Jays system.
- Annabella is somewhere else in the family map.
- Melissa is in Edinburgh with linguistics notes open in front of her.

### Beat 4: Linguistics and the Name

- Melissa thinks about the name **Key-d'Aboville** as language.
- It carries Rachel.
- It carries Antoine.
- It carries the family’s unusual biology and chosen motherhood.
- It carries a hyphen that refuses to simplify itself.
- Melissa understands why Marcos will not shorten it.
- A name is not only a label.
- A name is history made pronounceable.
- Her degree suddenly feels connected to the jersey that does not exist yet.

### Beat 5: Melissa’s Reaction

- Melissa becomes emotional.
- She may laugh first because that is easier than crying.
- She tells Rachel that Toronto better print the whole name correctly.
- She says Marcos is actually doing it.
- She says she wishes she were there.
- Rachel lets both truths stand: Melissa is proud, and Melissa is missing the moment.
- Rachel does not tell her not to be sad.
- Rachel knows that joy and distance can occupy the same call.

### Beat 6: Message for Marcos

- Melissa asks Rachel to give Marcos a message.
- She wants him to know she is proud of him.
- She wants him to know he is still one of the three.
- She may say that signing with Toronto does not mean he has escaped the triplets.
- It means he has given the triplets one more place to find him.
- Rachel promises to tell him.
- Melissa knows Rachel will.

### Beat 7: After the Call

- The call ends.
- Melissa remains in Edinburgh with the news sitting in the room around her.
- Baird House is around her now, shared with another woman, making the distance from Toronto practical and real.
- Her books are still open.
- The city is still moving.
- Nothing around her has changed, but everything in the family has shifted.
- She writes Marcos’s full name in the margin of her notes or sees it in her head as if it is already across a jersey.
- The moment becomes part of her own university memory.

### Beat 8: Final Image

- The final image is Melissa Lizette at the University of Edinburgh, holding the news of Marcos’s signing from across the ocean.
- Baird House is her dormitory now, shared with another woman.
- Her Linguistics and English Language notes are in front of her.
- Her brother’s full name is in her mind.
- She is far from Toronto.
- She is still his triplet sister.
