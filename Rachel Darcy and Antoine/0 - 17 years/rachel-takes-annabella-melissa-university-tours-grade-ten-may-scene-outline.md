# Scene Outline: Rachel Takes Annabella and Melissa on Grade Ten University Tours

## Overview

- **Characters:** Rachel Darcy Key, Annabella Nicki Key-d'Aboville, Melissa Lizette Key-d'Aboville, Marcos Eduarte Key-d'Aboville, Fallon Glenda Key, Charlie, Calida Dawn Key referenced, Temple University tour staff, University of Edinburgh tour staff.
- **Locations:** Rachel Darcy’s Los Angeles mansion; Philadelphia, Pennsylvania; Edinburgh, Scotland.
- **Timing:** May of Melissa Lizette, Marcos Eduarte, and Annabella Nicki’s Grade Ten year.
- **Status:** New scene outline.

---

## Placement

- This scene happens in **May of Grade Ten** for the triplets: Melissa Lizette, Marcos Eduarte, and Annabella Nicki.
- It happens years before Marcos signs with the Toronto Blue Jays.
- It happens years before Melissa attends The University of Edinburgh.
- It happens years before Annabella attends Temple University.
- Rachel Darcy is beginning to help the girls imagine adult academic futures before the final rush of Grade Eleven and Grade Twelve.
- In the same month and year, Rachel takes Annabella Nicki to Philadelphia for a tour of **Temple University**.
- In the same month and year, Rachel takes Melissa Lizette to Edinburgh for a tour of **The University of Edinburgh**.
- Fallon Glenda watches the remaining triplets at Rachel’s Los Angeles mansion during both trips.
- Charlie takes care of Calida Dawn while Fallon is helping Rachel.
- Calida Dawn is Fallon Glenda and Charlie’s fourth daughter, born when they are thirty-one years old.

---

## Core Situation

- Rachel Darcy takes each daughter separately to see the university that may become hers.
- Annabella gets Philadelphia and Temple with Rachel.
- Melissa gets Edinburgh and The University of Edinburgh with Rachel.
- Marcos remains in Los Angeles during both trips.
- When Rachel takes Annabella to Philadelphia, Fallon Glenda watches Marcos Eduarte and Melissa Lizette at the Los Angeles mansion.
- When Rachel takes Melissa to Edinburgh, Fallon Glenda watches Marcos Eduarte and Annabella Nicki at the Los Angeles mansion.
- The tours mark an early, gentle beginning of the triplets’ later adult geographic separation.
- Rachel is not forcing decisions yet.
- She is helping each girl see a possible future with her own eyes.

---

## Scene Beats

### Beat 1: May Planning at the Los Angeles Mansion

- The scene opens in May at Rachel Darcy’s Los Angeles mansion.
- The triplets are in Grade Ten.
- Marcos Eduarte is deepening into baseball.
- Melissa Lizette is drawn toward language, performance, sound, and meaning.
- Annabella Nicki is drawn toward dance, drawing, quiet movement, and the inward version of Rachel Darcy’s artistic inheritance.
- Rachel has been thinking about university visits for months.
- She does not want the girls’ first encounter with possible adult futures to be through brochures or websites only.
- She wants them to stand in the cities and feel the spaces.

### Beat 2: Asking Fallon Glenda for Help

- Rachel asks Fallon Glenda to help with the logistics.
- Fallon agrees to watch the triplets who remain in Los Angeles during each trip.
- Charlie takes care of Calida Dawn so Fallon can help Rachel with the triplets.
- Calida Dawn is Fallon and Charlie’s fourth daughter, born when they are thirty-one.
- Fallon understands that these tours are not vacations.
- They are threshold experiences.
- She also understands that the triplets are beginning to separate in ways that will become permanent later.
- Fallon’s job is to keep the Los Angeles mansion steady while Rachel travels.
- This lets each daughter have Rachel’s full attention on her own tour.

### Beat 3: First Trip — Annabella to Philadelphia

- Rachel takes Annabella Nicki to Philadelphia first.
- Melissa Lizette and Marcos Eduarte remain at the Los Angeles mansion with Fallon Glenda.
- Annabella travels quietly.
- She notices light, lines, buildings, and the geometry of a new city.
- Rachel notices Annabella noticing.
- Philadelphia feels older and denser than Los Angeles.
- It feels like a city with history pressed into the sidewalks.
- Annabella does not say immediately whether she likes it.
- Rachel gives her time.

### Beat 4: Temple University Tour

- Rachel and Annabella tour Temple University.
- The tour includes campus buildings, student spaces, residence life, academic areas, and the parts of campus that matter to a future dance student.
- Annabella pays special attention to dance spaces, studios, rehearsal rooms, performance venues, and the rhythm of students moving through campus.
- Rachel watches Annabella’s posture change when they enter or discuss dance spaces.
- Rachel understands that Annabella is not only looking at a school.
- She is testing whether her body can imagine living there.
- Rachel quietly feels the echo of her own university dance years.

### Beat 5: Rachel and Annabella in Philadelphia

- After the official tour, Rachel and Annabella walk or sit somewhere quiet in Philadelphia.
- Rachel asks what Annabella noticed.
- Annabella answers with details rather than declarations.
- She talks about the students, the buildings, the city, and the possibility of being known there as herself.
- Rachel does not ask whether this is definitely the place.
- She tells Annabella that a university tour is not a contract.
- It is a question asked in person.
- Annabella receives that and relaxes.

### Beat 6: Back in Los Angeles — Fallon With Marcos and Melissa

- While Rachel and Annabella are in Philadelphia, Fallon Glenda watches Marcos Eduarte and Melissa Lizette at the Los Angeles mansion, while Charlie takes care of Calida Dawn.
- Marcos keeps to baseball rhythms, training, schoolwork, and food.
- Melissa is restless because Annabella is away with Rachel.
- Fallon keeps the house easy and grounded.
- She does not over-parent them.
- She makes sure they eat, sleep, complete school responsibilities, and do not turn Annabella’s absence into drama.
- Melissa asks questions about Philadelphia even before Annabella comes home.
- Marcos pretends not to care too much but listens anyway.

### Beat 7: Annabella Comes Home

- Rachel and Annabella return to Los Angeles.
- Melissa wants details immediately.
- Marcos asks fewer questions but watches Annabella’s face.
- Annabella does not perform certainty.
- She tells them what she saw.
- She talks about Temple as a possible place, not a final decision yet.
- The triplets feel the first small shift: one of them has seen a future place without the other two.
- It is not a rupture.
- It is a rehearsal for distance.

### Beat 8: Second Trip — Melissa to Edinburgh

- Later in the same month, Rachel takes Melissa Lizette to Edinburgh.
- Marcos Eduarte and Annabella Nicki remain at the Los Angeles mansion with Fallon Glenda.
- The trip is larger than Philadelphia because it crosses the Atlantic.
- Melissa feels the distance before the plane even leaves.
- Edinburgh is not just a city.
- It is another country, another weather, another academic world, another future clock.
- Rachel knows this trip will ask more of Melissa emotionally.

### Beat 9: Arrival in Edinburgh

- Rachel and Melissa arrive in Edinburgh.
- The city feels old, wet, stone-built, and alive with language.
- Melissa responds quickly to sound: accents, street names, overheard student conversations, and the shape of English in a different place.
- Rachel sees Melissa become alert in a way that is different from performance.
- This is not the stage or the circus rig.
- This is language as a living environment.
- Melissa begins to understand why Edinburgh might be hers.

### Beat 10: University of Edinburgh Tour

- Rachel and Melissa tour The University of Edinburgh.
- The tour includes academic buildings, libraries, student areas, residence possibilities, and the spaces connected to linguistics and English language.
- Melissa pays attention to lecture halls, seminar rooms, libraries, and places where students read, argue, write, and listen.
- She asks questions about Linguistics and English Language.
- She wants to know how language is studied seriously.
- Rachel realizes that Melissa’s future may be built from words the way Annabella’s may be built from movement.
- This is another form of art.

### Beat 11: Rachel and Melissa in Edinburgh

- After the tour, Rachel and Melissa spend time walking through Edinburgh.
- Melissa talks more here than Annabella did in Philadelphia.
- She is excited and frightened by the scale of leaving.
- She says Edinburgh feels impossibly far from the triplets.
- Rachel does not deny that.
- Rachel tells her that distance can be real without being abandonment.
- Melissa needs to hear that before she can imagine choosing the place.

### Beat 12: Back in Los Angeles — Fallon With Marcos and Annabella

- While Rachel and Melissa are in Edinburgh, Fallon watches Marcos Eduarte and Annabella Nicki at the Los Angeles mansion, while Charlie takes care of Calida Dawn.
- Annabella is quieter with Melissa gone.
- Marcos notices and stays near her more than usual without making a production of it.
- Fallon sees the triplet bond from the outside and understands that Melissa’s absence changes the room.
- She keeps the mansion steady again.
- She lets the two remaining triplets miss Melissa without making the missing dramatic.
- The Los Angeles house becomes a practice space for future absence.

### Beat 13: Melissa Comes Home

- Rachel and Melissa return from Edinburgh.
- Annabella wants details this time.
- Marcos listens for what Edinburgh did to Melissa’s voice.
- Melissa talks about stone buildings, language, rain, libraries, and the feeling of hearing English become strange and new again.
- Annabella recognizes that Melissa has seen a possible future.
- Marcos recognizes that both sisters now have possible places that are not home.
- The triplets are still together, but the future has acquired geography.

### Beat 14: Rachel Reflects on the Two Tours

- Rachel thinks about the two trips together.
- Philadelphia gave Annabella a possible dance future.
- Edinburgh gave Melissa a possible language future.
- Marcos’s future is already bending toward baseball fields and scouts.
- Rachel sees the three paths beginning to separate.
- She is proud.
- She is also sad.
- She understands that successful parenting means helping children leave with enough structure to return when they need to.

### Beat 15: Final Image

- The final image is the triplets back together in the Los Angeles mansion after both tours.
- They are still in Grade Ten.
- Nothing is final yet.
- But Philadelphia has entered Annabella’s imagination.
- Edinburgh has entered Melissa’s imagination.
- Baseball has already claimed Marcos’s imagination.
- Fallon’s watching has helped make the trips possible.
- Rachel sees the future beginning before any of them are ready to call it that.

## Emotional Notes

- The scene should feel like early foreshadowing, not final commitment.
- Rachel should give each daughter individual attention without making the other triplets feel abandoned.
- Fallon Glenda’s role should be practical, loving, and stabilizing.
- Charlie taking care of Calida Dawn should show how Fallon is able to help Rachel without leaving her own fourth daughter unsupported.
- Annabella’s Philadelphia response should be quieter and more embodied.
- Melissa’s Edinburgh response should be more verbal, auditory, and language-focused.
- Marcos should remain present in the Los Angeles beats as the sibling whose path is separating through baseball.
- The tours should begin the emotional geography that later becomes Temple, Edinburgh, and the Blue Jays.
