# SCENE OUTLINE: PSALM 30:11 — RACHEL DARCY AT MOHAWK COLLEGE

**Characters:**
- **Rachel Darcy Key** (~7 years old)
- **Mike Key** — her father

**Setting:** Mohawk College, backstage or a quiet corridor near the recital hall. The building is busy with young dancers, costumes, parents, and instructors. Somewhere nearby, a piano is being tuned. Stage lights warm the hallway from the open auditorium doors. Rachel Darcy has already arrived, checked in, and seen the stage once. Now she and Mike have found a quieter spot — a bench in a corridor where the traffic is lighter, near a window that looks out at the college grounds.

**Timeframe:** The morning of the recital, after arrival at Mohawk College and before backstage waiting. This fits between Beat 4 ("Arrival at the Venue") and Beat 5 ("Backstage Waiting") of the existing recital scene outline.

---

## THE SCENE

### Beat 1 — Arrival at Mohawk College

- The building feels important to Rachel Darcy. The hallways, the stage doors, the other young dancers in costumes — everything says *this is real, this is happening.*
- She has been quiet since they parked. Not nervous in the same way Jessica Isabella was before gymnastics — Rachel's nerves are quieter, deeper. She is thinking about her body and what it will do on that stage.
- Mike walks beside her, carrying her dance bag. He does not fill the silence with chatter. He lets the building speak to her.

### Beat 2 — Finding a quiet place

- After they check in and see the stage briefly, Mike notices a side corridor with a bench near a window. The light is soft there, away from the backstage bustle.
- *"Let's sit for a minute before you get into costume."*
- Rachel nods. They sit on the bench. Outside the window, the college grounds are green and quiet. Inside, the muffled sound of a piano being tuned drifts through the wall.

### Beat 3 — The verse in her bag

- Rachel reaches into her dance bag. Beneath her ballet shoes and tights is her small Bible — the one Terri Anne gave her, the one whose leather is still stiff because she hasn't worn it in yet.
- She pulls it out and holds it in her lap. She doesn't open it right away.
- Mike notices. He says, gently: *"Is that the verse you've been reading?"*
- Rachel nods. *"I found it during homeschool. The one about dancing."*
- She opens the Bible. The ribbon marker is at Psalm 30. She turns the pages carefully — they are still new enough to stick together at the corners.
- She finds the verse. She runs her finger along the words before she says them aloud.

### Beat 4 — She reads

- Her voice is soft, almost lost in the corridor noise, but she reads clearly:
  > *"Thou hast turned for me my mourning into dancing: thou hast put off my sackcloth, and girded me with gladness."*
- She stops. She looks at the page for a long moment.

### Beat 5 — What she says next

- Mike waits. He has learned with his daughters to let them speak first.
- Rachel looks up at him. Her eyes are wet, but she is not crying — not yet.
- *"Daddy, I think this verse is about me."*
- Mike's voice is gentle. *"Tell me why."*
- Rachel looks back down at the page. She traces the words again.
- *"Because... before the surgery, I couldn't dance. I could barely walk. Jessica and I had to learn how to move in our own bodies. That was the mourning part. But now I can dance. I can go on a stage and move my arms and my legs and nobody is attached to me. That's the dancing part."*
- She looks up at him. *"God turned it. He turned my mourning into dancing."*

### Beat 6 — Mike's reply

- Mike is quiet. When he speaks, his voice is rougher than he meant it to be.
- *"Rachel Darcy. Do you know how many people spend their whole lives trying to understand what that verse means? And you figured it out when you were seven, because you lived it."*
- A tear escapes down Rachel's cheek. She wipes it with the back of her hand quickly, embarrassed, though there is no reason to be.
- Mike reaches over and gently takes her hand. *"When you go on that stage today, you are not just dancing a recital. You are dancing the verse. You are showing everyone what it looks like when God turns mourning into dancing."*
- Rachel looks at her ballet shoes. At the stage door. At the Bible in her lap.
- She says, very quietly: *"I want to dance it for Him."*

### Beat 7 — The call

- A teacher calls for the youngest group to begin gathering backstage. It is almost time.
- Rachel closes her Bible and puts it carefully back in her dance bag. She zips it and stands.
- She picks up her ballet shoes.
- Before she walks toward the backstage door, she turns to Mike. She does not say anything. She just looks at him.
- Mike says: *"Dance it for Him, sweetheart. I'll be right here."*
- She nods. She walks toward the stage door.

### Beat 8 — Mike stays on the bench

- Mike does not follow immediately. He stays on the bench by the window.
- He looks out at the green college grounds and thinks about his daughter — the one who was born attached to her twin, who learned to walk in a separate body, who found a verse about dancing and made it her own.
- He thinks: *She's going to be okay. More than okay.*

## THEMATIC RESONANCE

- **The building as threshold:** Mohawk College is where Rachel Darcy first performs alone. The corridor, the window, the bench — this is where she connects her story to scripture before she steps on stage.
- **Her own interpretation:** Rachel interprets Psalm 30:11 through her own body — the surgical separation, the loss, the freedom, the dance. This is not her parents teaching her. This is her discovering.
- **"Dance it for Him":** The moment she names her performance as an offering — not for a score, not for applause, but for God.
- **The stiff Bible:** Her Bible is still new, still being broken in. This is the beginning of her own faith journey, separate from her twin's.
