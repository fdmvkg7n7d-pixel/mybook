# Mike, Jessica Isabella, Emma, Samira Chaka, and Sahara Hannan — Halifax — Scene Outline

> **Scope:** Mike drives with Jessica, Emma, Samira, and Sahara to Halifax. He says goodbye to his daughter and son-in-law, holds his grandchildren, and then drives back to Carlisle alone. The family splits at Halifax — Jessica and Emma stay, Mike goes home to Terri Anne and the five who are still there.
>
> **Time:** TBD — late August 2043, the afternoon and evening after the goodbye at UVA.
> **Place:** Halifax, Nova Scotia — Jessica and Emma's home.
> **Characters present:** Mike Key, Jessica Isabella Key (17), Emma Fritz (17), Samira Chaka (18 months), Sahara Hannan (18 months). And later, alone on the drive home: Mike Key.
> **Mood:** Quiet, warm, deliberate. The particular tiredness of a father who has just watched two daughters begin.

---

## 1. The Drive from Charlottesville

- **Mike leaves University of Virginia and drives to the hotel.** Jessica and Emma are waiting with the children. They load the cars. They are ready.
- **They drive north.** Two cars — Mike in one, Jessica and Emma in the other with Samira and Sahara. The drive from Charlottesville to Halifax is long — hours on the road, the kind of distance that used to be nothing for this family and now feels like something.
- **Mike leads.** He drives steadily, the way he always drives — unhurried, attentive. Jessica follows behind him at the same pace.
- **They stop once** along the highway — a rest stop, a coffee, a change for Samira. They do not rush it. The children need the break. The adults need the walk.
- **Samira is fussy.** Jessica holds her. Emma changes her. Mike stands nearby and watches, the way he always does — present, not intervening unless he is needed.
- **They drive on.** The afternoon becomes evening. The highway becomes older, quieter roads. The landscape changes — Virginia to West Virginia to somewhere in between to something that starts to look like Nova Scotia.
- **They arrive in Halifax in the evening.** The city is warm and coastal. The house is lit — someone left lights on for them, or Jessica programmed them before they left. Home.

---

## 2. The House

- **They go inside.** The house is warm and quiet — the particular warmth of a home that has been lived in, that has children in it, that smells like the life it holds.
- **Mike sets his bag down.** He looks around. He has been here before — visits, stops, the kind of traveling father who comes and goes and always comes back. He knows the house. He knows the layout. He knows where everything is.
- **Samira and Sahara are tired.** They have been in the car for hours. Jessica takes Samira upstairs to change her and put her to bed. Emma puts Sahara in front of a glass of water and sits with her on the kitchen floor.
- **Mike sits at the kitchen table.** He is tired — not from the driving, from the watching. He is always tired after a goodbye, and this one was two: Rachel in Charlottesville, Jessica in Halifax. Two daughters beginning.
- **Jessica comes back downstairs.** She sits across from him. Emma is still with Sahara in the kitchen.
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"You okay?"*
- **Mike:** *"I'm okay."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"You look tired."*
- **Mike:** *"I am tired."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"You just drove eight hours."*
- **Mike:** *"Not from the driving."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"From the watching."*
- **Mike:** *"Yes."*
- **A beat.** They sit. The kitchen is quiet. The children are settling upstairs.
- **Mike:** *"Rachel called. Before. She left a voicemail."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"I know. I called her back. She was in the practice room."*
- **Mike:** *"That's where she goes."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"I know where she goes."*
- **Mike:** *"She's okay."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"I know she's okay. I just — I want to be there."*
- **Mike:** *"I know."*
- **They sit.** The evening settles. Emma comes in from the kitchen, Sahara asleep in her arms. Emma takes her upstairs. Jessica follows. Mike is alone in the kitchen.

---

## 3. The Evening — Dinner

- **They eat late.** Something simple — pasta, bread, the kind of meal that does not require much. Mike made it. He always makes dinner when he visits. He does not cook often at home — Terri Anne does that — but when he visits his daughters, he cooks.
- **They sit at the table:** Mike at one end, Jessica on one side, Emma on the other. The children are asleep upstairs. The house is quiet in a way that is different from the car, different from the road. Home quiet.
- **Mike:** *"I should head out in the morning."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"I know."*
- **Emma:** *"You're sure? You could stay another day."*
- **Mike:** *"I should get back. Terri has the others."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"How are they?"*
- **Mike:** *"Good. Andrea made dinner again. Kiley Rose asked when I'm coming home."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"She's always asking that."*
- **Mike:** *"She always wants to know."*
- **A pause.** Jessica is quiet. She is thinking.
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"Rachel's the third one to go somewhere else."*
- **Mike:** *"No. She's the third one to go somewhere else. She won't be the third one to leave."*
- **Jessica Isabella looks at him.**
- **Mike:** *"They're never gone, Jess. They're just somewhere else."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"I know. I just mean — she was the last one who was here every weekend."*
- **Mike:** *"I know."*
- **They eat.** The conversation moves to other things — the house, the children, the school year ahead. They do not talk about the distance more than they have to. They have always known how to talk around the things that matter without avoiding them.

---

## 4. The Night

- **Mike sleeps in the guest room.** He has slept here before. The room is familiar. The house is quiet. He lies awake for a while, listening.
- **The house sounds different** when it is not full. When Samira and Sahara are asleep, when Jessica and Emma are in their room, when the only sound is the particular quiet of a house that is resting. He has heard this sound before — in Vaughan, in Carlisle, in the houses of his daughters who have gone somewhere else and made their own rooms quiet.
- **He thinks about Rachel.** In Charlottesville. In her room. Locked in. Beginning. He thinks about Jessica, in the next room, holding the life she built. He thinks about Terri Anne, in Carlisle, holding the five who are still there.
- **He closes his eyes.** He sleeps.

---

## 5. The Morning — The Goodbye

- **Mike wakes up early.** He always wakes up early. He goes downstairs. He makes coffee. He stands at the kitchen window and looks at the city — the harbor, the morning light, the water.
- **Jessica comes down.** She is not fully awake yet. She comes into the kitchen and stands beside him.
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"You're up early."*
- **Mike:** *"I always am."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"I know."*
- **They stand together.** They do not need to fill the space with words. They have always done this — the quiet standing, the early morning, the way a father and daughter can be together without talking.
- **Emma comes down.** She makes coffee too. She sits at the table. She does not say much — she is learning the shape of this family, and she knows when to be quiet and when to speak.
- **Samira wakes up.** Jessica goes upstairs to get her. She comes back with Samira in her arms, the child's hair messy from sleep, her face soft and new.
- **Mike takes Samira.** He holds her the way he holds all of them — the weight of a grandchild who does not know yet that this is just one of many visits. She leans her head against his shoulder. She is warm and small and completely present.
- **Mike:** *"I'll miss you, little one."*
- **Samira makes a sound.** Not words — just a sound. She does not know what he means. She will know later.
- **Sahara comes down.** She is more awake than Samira. She comes to Mike and holds his hand. He bends down and holds her. He holds them both — one in his arms, one holding his hand — and he knows this is what he is going home to: the others, the five, the house that is still full.

---

## 6. The Goodbye

- **Mike loads his car.** He does not have much — a bag, a coffee cup, the book he did not finish. He loads it carefully, the way he always does.
- **Jessica and Emma stand at the door.** Samira is in Jessica's arms. Sahara is beside Emma, holding her hand.
- **Mike stands in front of his daughter.** He puts his hands on her shoulders — the way he does, the way he has always done. Steady, direct, certain.
- **Mike:** *"I love you."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"I love you too."*
- **Mike:** *"I'll call when I get home."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"I know."*
- **Mike:** *"And I'll call when I get to Carlisle."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"I know you will."*
- **He holds her.** Long, careful, the hug of a father to a daughter who is already a mother, who gave her eggs to her sister, who carries what she carries and knows what she knows. He does not say anything else. He does not need to.
- **He hugs Emma.** Brief, real. He says: *Take care of her.*
- **Emma:** *Always.*
- **He bends down and picks up Sahara.** He holds her for a moment. He looks at her face — the same face as Samira, and different. He puts her down.
- **He looks at Samira in Jessica's arms.** He waves. She waves back. She does not know what this wave means. She will know later.
- **He gets in the car.** He starts the engine. He rolls down the window.
- **Mike:** *"Merry Christmas."*
- **Jessica Isabella:** *"Merry Christmas, Dad."*
- **He drives away.** The house gets smaller in the mirror. Jessica stands at the door with Samira. Emma stands beside her. Sahara is holding Emma's hand. They watch him go. He watches them in the mirror until the road turns and he cannot see them anymore.

---

## 7. The Drive Home

- **Mike drives from Halifax to Carlisle.** It is a long drive — the same distance in reverse, the kind he has done a hundred times, the kind he will do a hundred more. The road is familiar. The car is quiet.
- **He calls Terri Anne.** He tells her he's on his way. She tells him the others are fine. She tells him Andrea made dinner. She tells him Kiley Rose asked when he's coming home.
- **Mike:** *"Tell her I'm almost there."*
- **Terri Anne:** *"She's going to want to hear it from you."*
- **Mike:** *"I know."*
- **He drives through the morning.** The highway is long. The sun is out. He is tired in the way that is just tiredness — the good kind, the kind that comes from doing something that needed to be done.
- **He thinks about Rachel.** In Charlottesville. In her room. Locked in. Beginning. He thinks about Jessica, standing at the door with Samira in her arms, watching him drive away. He thinks about Terri Anne, at home with the five who are still there, holding the house together the way she always has.
- **He drives.**
- **The highway is long.** The day is clear. The car holds him and keeps going.

---

## 8. Carlisle — Home

- **Mike arrives at the Carlisle mansion late afternoon.** The house is lit up — Terri Anne left the porch light on, and the kitchen light, and the light in the hall. He has come home at night before. He has always known which lights meant: we are still here, come in.
- **He parks.** He sits for a moment. He is not tired anymore — he is just home.
- **He gets out.** He opens the front door. The house is warm. The smell of dinner is still there — whatever Andrea made, it is still in the air.
- **Kiley Rose comes first.** She comes down the stairs before anyone else, the way she always does, moving fast, not quite eight years old but already someone who runs toward things instead of away from them.
- **Kiley Rose:** *"You're home!"*
- **Mike:** *"I'm home."*
- **He picks her up.** He holds her — the weight of her, the realness of her, the fact that she is here and she is his and the house is full of her.
- **Terri Anne comes next.** She stands at the kitchen door. She is not surprised, not dramatic, not needing anything. She just stands there, and that is enough.
- **Mike sets Kiley Rose down.** He walks to Terri Anne. He puts his hand on her face, the way he does, and he holds it there for a moment.
- **Mike:** *"I'm home."*
- **Terri Anne:** *"I know."*
- **Then the others come.** Sabrina Bryna comes down the stairs in her bare feet, Ashley Erin follows with her guitar, Andrea Jennifer is in the kitchen with a dish towel. Fallon Glenda is already asleep — she is young enough that late nights are not for her yet.
- **They do not ask about Rachel or Jessica.** They already know. Terri Anne told them. They know Rachel is in Charlottesville, Jessica is in Halifax, and Mike is home.
- **Mike sits at the kitchen table.** Terri Anne sits beside him. The others move around them — Sabrina signing something to Andrea, Ashley putting her guitar away, Kiley Rose asking for a glass of water.
- **Mike does not tell them everything.** He tells them what they need to know: Rachel is settled, Jessica is home, the drive was long, the house is quiet now.
- **Kiley Rose:** *"Is Rachel coming back?"*
- **Mike:** *"At Christmas."*
- **Kiley Rose:** *"That's a long time."*
- **Mike:** *"Yes. It is."*
- **Kiley Rose:** *"But she's coming back."*
- **Mike:** *"She's always coming back."*
- **Kiley Rose nods.** She accepts this. She goes back to her water. The kitchen settles into its normal rhythm — the movement, the noise, the way eight people in a large house sounds when they have always lived together.
- **Mike looks at Terri Anne.** She looks back. Neither of them says anything. They do not need to. They have done this before — the coming home after a leaving, the sitting in the kitchen with the others, the quiet acknowledgment that someone is gone and someone else is here and both of those things are true at the same time.
- **Mike:** *"I'll go check on Fallon."*
- **Terri Anne:** *"She's asleep."*
- **Mike:** *"I know. I'll check anyway."*
- **He goes upstairs.** He opens the door to Fallon's room. She is asleep — her face relaxed, her breathing even. He stands in the doorway for a moment. He does not go in. He just looks.
- **He closes the door.** He goes to his room. Terri Anne is already there. The house is quiet. The night is over.
- **Mike lies down.** He does not sleep immediately — he lies still and listens to the house. The sounds of his family settling, the particular quiet of eight people in a large house at the end of a day.
- **He thinks about Rachel.** In Charlottesville. In her room. Locked in. Beginning.
- **He thinks about the others.** Here. Asleep, or almost asleep, or awake in their rooms doing whatever they do at night.
- **He thinks about Terri Anne beside him,** and the fact that she is there and she is his and the house is full of people who belong to each other, even when they are not all in the same room.
- **He closes his eyes.**
- **He sleeps.**

---

## 9. Closing Image

- **The next morning.** Mike wakes up early. He always wakes up early. He goes downstairs. He makes coffee. He stands at the kitchen window and looks at the grounds — the stable, the garden, the trees.
- **The house is quiet.** The others are still asleep. The sun is coming up over the east side of the property. The light is soft and early and full of possibility.
- **He thinks about the drive — Halifax, Carlisle. He thinks about Jessica, in her house by the water, holding Samira, beginning another day. He thinks about Rachel, in Charlottesville, in her room, locked in, working. He thinks about Terri Anne, beside him, and the five who are still here: Kiley Rose, Sabrina Bryna, Fallon Glenda, Andrea Jennifer, Ashley Erin.**
- **He thinks about the five who are still here:** Kiley Rose, Sabrina Bryna, Fallon Glenda, Andrea Jennifer, Ashley Erin. Five daughters in a house that has always been full of daughters. Five more years before the next one leaves, maybe. Maybe sooner. Maybe later. He does not know. He just knows he will be here when they go, and here when they come back, and here in the mornings when the house is quiet and the coffee is hot and the day is beginning.
- **He pours a second cup.** He sits at the kitchen table. He does not turn on his phone yet. He just sits.
- **The house begins to wake.** Kiley Rose comes down first, the way she always does. She sits across from him. She does not say anything — she just sits. She knows how to do this. They have always done this together: the early morning, the quiet, the coffee, the waiting for the others to wake up.
- **Mike looks at his daughter.** She is not Rachel. She is not Jessica. She is Kiley Rose — seven years old, with Turner Syndrome, with gymnastics and swimming, with a life that is hers and no one else's.
- **He does not say anything.** He just looks at her. She looks back.
- **The morning begins.** The house fills. The day happens.
- **He is home.**

## Character Work

### Mike Key
- **The traveler:** He drove from Charlottesville to Halifax to Carlisle. He said goodbye at each stop. He did not rush it. He did it the way he has always done it — carefully, completely, with the knowledge that the goodbye is part of the love.
- **The one who knows:** He knows the difference between letting go and watching someone walk. He knows that Rachel is not gone — she is in Charlottesville. He knows that Jessica is not gone — she is in Halifax. He knows that the five at home are here, and that is enough.
- **The one who comes home:** He comes home to Terri Anne, to the five who are still there, to the kitchen table and the early morning and the quiet that is not loneliness. He comes home and he stays.
- **The one who watches:** He watches Kiley Rose sit across from him in the early morning. He does not say anything. He just looks. That is enough.

### Jessica Isabella Key (17)
- **The one who stays:** She has Halifax. She has Emma. She has Samira and Sahara. She has a house by the water and a life that is fully hers. She is not lonely. She is just far from her twin.
- **The one who gives:** She gave her eggs to Rachel. She is still learning what that means — what it means to carry something for someone else and then live with the distance between you.

### Emma Fritz (17)
- **The one who stays:** She will stay in Halifax with Jessica. She will be there when they come back from Virginia. She will hold the family together on that side of the distance. She is learning the shape of this family from the inside, and she is not lost in it — she is found by it.
- **The one who takes care:** Mike says *Take care of her* before he leaves. She says *Always.* She means it.

### Kiley Rose Key (the youngest of those still at home)
- **The one who asks:** *"Is Rachel coming back?"* She needs to know. Mike tells her. She accepts it. She sits across from him in the early morning and does not need to ask anything else. She just sits.

### Terri Anne Williams-Key
- **The one who waits:** She leaves the lights on. She knows which lights mean: come in. She does not ask for details. She just waits, and when he comes home she stands at the kitchen door, and that is enough.

### Samira Chaka and Sahara Hannan (18 months)
- **The ones who do not know yet:** At 18 months, they are too young to understand that their aunt is about to leave or that their grandfather is going home. They just know that today there was a wave at the door, and then the car got smaller, and then it was gone.

## Thematic Motifs

- **The lights:** Terri Anne leaves the porch light on, the kitchen light on, the hall light on. Coming home to lights is a kind of language.
- **The early morning:** Mike wakes up early. He makes coffee. He watches the grounds. He waits. This is not loneliness — it is the shape of his days.
- **Kiley Rose in the morning:** She comes down first. She sits across from him. She does not need to speak. They sit together, and that is the whole conversation.
- **The locked door:** Rachel locked the door in Charlottesville. Mike asked her to. She did it because she wanted to. The locked door is the beginning of everything — the first act of independence, the first boundary, the first moment of being alone and not afraid.
- **Coming home:** The house is full of people who belong to each other. Even when someone is gone, someone else is here. That is the whole point.
- **The wave at the door:** Mike waves to Samira. She waves back. She does not know what it means. She will know later. The wave is a promise and a goodbye and a promise to come back, all in one motion.
