# Mike and Rachel Darcy — Good-byes at the University — Scene Outline

> **Scope:** The morning of the goodbye. Mike drives Rachel to her dorm. They carry her things upstairs. They stand in her room. They say what needs to be said. Then Mike leaves, and Rachel stands in her room alone for the first time, in the place that will be hers for the next four years.
>
> **Time:** TBD — late August 2043, the morning after the dinner and campus walk.
> **Place:** University of Virginia, Charlottesville — Rachel's dorm room, third floor.
> **Characters present:** Mike Key, Rachel Darcy Key (17).
> **Mood:** Deliberate, unhurried, clear-eyed. A goodbye that is not rushed and a standing-alone that is not frightening.

---

## 1. The Morning

- **They check out of the hotel.** Mike has already packed. He has been awake since early — the way he always is before he leaves somewhere. He does not sleep well the night before a goodbye. He never has.
- **Jessica and Emma are already up.** They came back from Halifax last night — or rather, they never went to Halifax yet. The plan is: Mike drives Rachel to campus in the morning, and later in the day Mike, Jessica, Emma, Samira, and Sahara drive to Halifax together. But first, the goodbye.
- **Rachel Darcy is calm.** She slept well. She always sleeps well before something begins.
- **They get in the car.** Mike drives to campus. The drive is short — five minutes from the hotel. They have done this drive before, during orientation, when Rachel chose her room.
- **Mike parks near the dorm.** They get out. They go inside.

---

## 2. The Room

- **The room is on the third floor.** It is small — two beds, two desks, two closets. A window that looks out over the lawn. Rachel chose it because of the window. Mike helped her carry the boxes when they arrived for orientation. He remembers standing in this room, looking out, knowing what she was choosing.
- **They carry her things upstairs.** Not much — the boxes arrived weeks ago. Just the bag she brought on the plane, her practice clothes, her notebook. The room already feels like hers. The desk has her name on it. The bed has her sheets.
- **Mike sets the bag down.** He stands in the room and looks around. He has done this before — stood in hotel rooms, dorm rooms, dressing rooms — with his daughters before something began. He has always known what to do.
- **He knows what to do now.**
- **Rachel Darcy is arranging her things.** She does not need to — they are already arranged. She is just touching them, claiming them, making the room real by moving through it.
- **Mike sits on the edge of her bed.** Not the desk chair — the bed. He chose the bed because it is where she will sleep, and he wants to sit somewhere that matters.
- **Rachel Darcy stops.**
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"What?"*
- **Mike:** *"Nothing. Just sitting."*

---

## 3. The Room

- **The room is quiet.** Outside, the campus is moving — students, families, the sound of the first week beginning. Inside, it is just the two of them.
- **Mike:** *"This is a good room."*
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"It's small."*
- **Mike:** *"You don't need big. You need yours."*
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"I've never had just mine before."*
- **Mike:** *"No. You haven't."*
- **A beat.** She is right — she has never had a room that was only hers. The Key house was always shared. Even after the twins were separated, they shared a bedroom and bathroom. Now she has a door and a window and a bed that no one else will sleep in.
- **Mike:** *"Is that hard?"*
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"No. It's just — different."*
- **Mike:** *"Different isn't hard. It's just new."*
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"I know."*
- **She sits on her desk chair.** She faces him. They are across the room from each other now — not side by side, not close. This is the setup for the conversation they need to have.

---

## 4. The Conversation

- **Mike:** *"I need to tell you some things before I go."*
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"Okay."*
- **Mike:** *"First — call us. Not every day. Not because you have to. But because we want to hear from you."*
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"I will."*
- **Mike:** *"Second — if something is wrong, tell us. Not just us. Tell Jessica. Tell Emma. Tell someone in this family. You are not alone there."*
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"I know."*
- **Mike:** *"Third — don't wait too long to come back. Not because we need you. Because you need to know that you can."*
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"Mike."*
- **Mike:** *"I know."*
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"I'll come back."*
- **Mike:** *"I know you will."*
- **A long pause.** The room is very quiet. The campus noise is still there, but it is outside, and it is not theirs.
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"I want to tell you something."*
- **Mike:** *"Okay."*
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"I am going to be okay here. I am going to learn the building and the practice rooms and the people. I am going to dance and I am going to cheer and I am going to do the assignment about understanding being human."*
- **Mike:** *"I know."*
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"And I want you to know that I know what you did."*
- **Mike:** *"What did I do?"*
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"You brought me here. You flew from Rio with me. You sat in this room and you didn't say anything that would make me feel bad about leaving. You just — let me go."*
- **Mike:** *"I didn't let you go. I watched you walk."*
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"That's the same thing."*
- **Mike:** *"No. It's not."*
- **A beat.** She does not argue with him. She just hears it.
- **Mike:** *"I have to go."*
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"I know."*
- **Mike stands.** He does not rush. He picks up his bag — the small bag he brought on the plane, nothing more. He looks at the room one more time: the desk, the window, the bed with her sheets, the practice bag in the corner.
- **He looks at his daughter.** She is standing now, facing him, seventeen years old, about to begin her first year at a university she chose because it was far enough to matter and close enough to come back from.

---

## 5. The Goodbye

- **Mike crosses the room.** He puts his hands on her shoulders — not her arms, her shoulders. He has always held her this way: steady, direct, certain.
- **Mike:** *"I love you."*
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"I love you too."*
- **Mike:** *"I'll see you at Christmas."*
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"I'll be here."*
- **He holds her.** Not a long hug — just a real one. The kind that says what needs to be said. Then he lets go.
- **He picks up his bag.** He walks to the door.
- **He stops.** He does not turn around.
- **Mike:** *"Lock the door."*
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"I will."*
- **Mike:** *"And text me when I get to Halifax."*
- **Rachel Darcy:** *"I will."*
- **Mike:** *"Okay."*
- **He leaves.** The door closes. The room is quiet.

---

## 6. The Alone

- **Rachel Darcy stands in the room.** The door is closed. The hallway is quiet — students going to class, families leaving, the ordinary machinery of a morning beginning.
- **She locks the door.** She does it because he asked her to. She does it because she wants to.
- **She walks to the window.** She stands there and looks out over the lawn. The sun is coming in sideways, the way it does in the morning. The trees are old and wide. The students are walking to where they need to go.
- **She does not cry.** She is not sad. She is just — here. In the room that is hers. In the building that will be hers for the next four years. In the city that is not Carlisle and not Rio and not anywhere she has been before.
- **She texts Mike:** *Made it. Locked the door. Have a good drive.*
- **She puts her phone down.** She sits on her bed. She looks at the room again — the desk, the window, the practice bag, the sheets she chose because they were the right color and she liked them.
- **She is alone.** For the first time in her life, she is completely, entirely, hers.
- **She does not feel lonely.** She feels the space. She knows what to do with it.
- **She picks up her notebook.** She goes to her desk. She opens the notebook to a fresh page.
- **She begins.**

---

## 7. Closing Image

- **Later that afternoon.** The campus is still. The first-day-of-class energy has settled into something quieter. Rachel is in the practice room on the second floor of the dance building.
- **She is working.** Not choreography — something simpler. The basics. Foot position. Alignment. The ground beneath her.
- **She is remembering what Mike said:** *You don't need big. You need yours.*
- **The practice room is small.** It has a mirror and a barre and a window that faces the side of the music building. She can hear, faintly, someone playing flute somewhere above her. She cannot tell who it is. She does not look to find out.
- **She works.** The afternoon passes. The light changes.
- **She goes back to her room.** She locks the door. She sits on her bed. She looks out the window.
- **The campus is quiet now.** Evening is coming. The students who left in the morning are coming back. The lawn is still. The trees are still.
- **She is here.** She is hers.
- **The first day is almost over.** She has unpacked, talked to her father, locked the door, texted the family, gone to the practice room, and come back. She has done all of it alone. She has done all of it without feeling lonely.
- **She picks up her phone.** She calls Jessica.
- **No answer.** She leaves a voicemail. She says: *I'm here. The room is good. I'll call again when you're in Halifax.*
- **She puts the phone down.** She lies on her bed. She looks at the ceiling.
- **She is seventeen.** She is in Charlottesville, Virginia. She is learning what it means to be needed in a new place, in a new way, by herself.
- **The ceiling is white.** The light is fading. The room is quiet.
- **She closes her eyes.**
- **She is not afraid.**

## Character Work

### Mike Key
- **The one who leaves:** He does not linger. He says what needs to be said. He holds her. He goes. He trusts her to lock the door and text him and manage the first day without him.
- **The one who watches her walk:** *"I didn't let you go. I watched you walk."* The distinction matters. He is not letting her go — he is witnessing her going.
- **The one who knows:** He knows she will be okay. He tells her anyway. The telling is part of the letting go.

### Rachel Darcy (17)
- **The one who stands alone:** She locks the door because he asked her to. She locks it because she wants to. She is alone and she is not afraid.
- **The one who begins:** She sits at her desk and opens her notebook to a fresh page. She begins. Not with words — with the act of starting.
- **The one who calls:** She calls Jessica. No answer. She leaves a voicemail. She is already learning to reach out across the distance.
- **The one who is not afraid:** She closes her eyes on her bed in her new room and she is not afraid. That is the thing that matters most.

---

## Thematic Motifs

- **Lock the door:** The first act of independence and self-protection. He asks her to do it. She does it because she wants to.
- **Yours:** The room is small. She does not need big. She needs hers. The smallest space can be the truest one.
- **Watching her walk:** The difference between letting go and witnessing. He does not let her go — he watches her go. That is what a father does.
- **The practice room:** She goes there first. Not to perform — to work. To find the ground. To hear the flute somewhere above her, someone else in a room, working.
- **The first day:** She does everything she needs to do alone. She does not feel lonely. She feels the space, and she knows what to do with it.
