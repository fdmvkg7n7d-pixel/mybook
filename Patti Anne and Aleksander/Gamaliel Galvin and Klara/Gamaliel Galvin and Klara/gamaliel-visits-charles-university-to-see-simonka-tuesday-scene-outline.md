# Gamaliel Galvin Visits Charles University to See Simonka Káta

## Tuesday Scene Outline

## Placement

**When:** Tuesday, after Klárka Pavlînka tells Gamaliel Galvin and Klára in Gamaliel's office what she and Raphael found in Simonka Káta's apartment.

**Where:** Begins in **Gamaliel Galvin's office** at the academy, then moves to **Charles University** in Prague.

**Continuity Lead-in:**

- Klárka and Raphael found Simonka's apartment empty on Monday night.
- They found Simonka's diary on the kitchen table with the note **Přečti mě** / **Read me**.
- Klárka read enough to learn that Simonka chose to become Holic Bartunek's pet.
- On Tuesday, Klárka told Gamaliel and Klára in Gamaliel's office.
- Klára cried softly while Klárka spoke.
- Gamaliel's first instinct was to act, but Klára stopped him from rushing toward Holic.
- Instead of going to Holic's mansion, Gamaliel decides to go to **Charles University** to see Simonka in her own world.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene gives Gamaliel a way to act without immediately violating Simonka's adult autonomy.

He does not go to Holic first. He does not storm the mansion. He does not call the police. He goes to Charles University because it is Simonka's public life, her chosen intellectual world, and a place where he might see his daughter without entering Holic's territory.

The purpose is not to rescue Simonka by force.

The purpose is to see her.

Gamaliel needs to know whether she is safe, whether she is still herself, and whether there is any opening through which her family can reach her.

---

## Core Emotional Conflict

Gamaliel wants to be a father.

But Simonka is not a child.

That is the conflict.

His body wants to move like a parent of a missing little girl: find her, take her hand, bring her home. But Simonka is a grown woman, a Charles University psychology student, and the diary says she chose Holic. Gamaliel has to walk into Charles University not as a rescuer, not as a commander, but as a father trying not to lose his daughter by loving her too forcefully.

---

## Characters in the Scene

### Gamaliel Galvin

- Father of Simonka and Klárka.
- Ballet academy founder, used to discipline, structure, and decisive action.
- Shaken by what Klárka revealed.
- Wants to go to Holic but chooses Charles University instead.
- Must control his fear so he does not frighten Simonka away.
- His goal is simple: see Simonka with his own eyes.
- While speaking to Simonka, his mind keeps returning to **Klára's biological father** — the man whose abuse shaped Klára's childhood and the family's entire rescue history. He also thinks about the time **he and Klára visited Klára's father at Kent Institution in British Columbia when they were eighteen and nineteen**. Gamaliel has to fight the instinct to see Holic immediately through that lens, while also refusing to ignore the warning bells that lens creates.

### Klára

- Simonka's mother.
- Still crying softly or recently cried in Gamaliel's office.
- Understands why Gamaliel needs to go.
- Gives him emotional conditions before he leaves: no accusations, no shame, no threats, no dragging Simonka home.
- Her restraint is painful but necessary.

### Klárka Pavlînka

- Older sister.
- May want to go with Gamaliel.
- Still raw from Monday night and the office conversation.
- May be too emotionally charged to approach Simonka calmly.
- Her desire to come is loving, but Gamaliel may decide he should go alone.

### Raphael

- Optional presence, likely outside or near the office.
- Does not participate in the family decision unless asked.
- May stay with Klárka when Gamaliel leaves.

### Simonka Káta

- At Charles University, or expected to be there.
- Appears in her public identity: student, researcher, young woman moving through campus.
- The contrast matters: the family has just heard about Holic's world, but at university Simonka may look ordinary, focused, tired, guarded, or strangely calm.
- Whether she speaks to Gamaliel openly is the central tension of the encounter.

---

# PART ONE — THE DECISION IN GAMALIEL'S OFFICE

## Beat 1 — The Office After Klárka's Report

The previous scene leaves the family in Gamaliel's office with Simonka's diary on the desk.

Klára has been crying softly.

Klárka has told them what she and Raphael found.

Gamaliel is quiet now, but not calm. His quiet is the kind that comes before action.

The academy continues outside the office door: footsteps, music, students, teachers, normal life.

Inside the office, everything has narrowed to Simonka.

---

## Beat 2 — Gamaliel Rejects Doing Nothing

Gamaliel says he cannot sit in the office and wait.

Possible line:

> I need to see her.

Klára may answer:

> I know.

This is different from approving a reckless confrontation. Klára understands the need beneath his words. She may not want him to go, but she knows he cannot remain behind the desk while his daughter is somewhere in Prague carrying a secret this large.

---

## Beat 3 — He Chooses Charles University, Not Holic

Gamaliel makes the key decision:

> I am going to Charles University.

This matters because it shows he has heard Klára's warning. He is not going to Holic first. He is not starting with the man. He is starting with Simonka.

Possible line:

> If she has class today, I can find her there. I can see her. I can ask her to talk.

Klára may breathe out, relieved and frightened at the same time.

Charles University is still an intervention, but it is not an invasion of Holic's private space.

---

## Beat 4 — Klárka Wants To Go Too

Klárka immediately wants to come.

Possible line:

> I'm going with you.

This is instinctive. She is the one who found the diary. She is Simonka's older sister. She does not want to be left behind again.

But Gamaliel may say no.

Reasons:

- Klárka is too angry.
- Simonka may feel ambushed if both father and sister appear.
- Klárka has already crossed one threshold by entering the apartment.
- The first contact after the diary should be as calm as possible.

Possible Gamaliel line:

> Not this time.

Or:

> If she sees all of us, she may run.

Klárka hates this, but understands enough not to fight hard.

---

## Beat 5 — Klára Sets Conditions

Before Gamaliel leaves, Klára gives him rules.

These rules are not controlling him; they are protecting Simonka's trust.

Possible lines:

> You do not shame her.

> You do not tell her she is stupid.

> You do not threaten Holic in front of her.

> You do not say we read everything if we did not.

> You ask if she is safe.

> You tell her she can come home.

> You tell her we love her before you ask anything else.

Gamaliel may bristle at being instructed, but he knows Klára is right.

---

## Beat 6 — The Diary Stays Behind

Gamaliel does not take the diary with him.

This is important.

He is not going to confront Simonka with her own words like evidence in a trial. The diary stays in the office with Klára and Klárka, or is moved to a safer private place.

Possible line from Klára:

> Do not bring the diary to her like a weapon.

Gamaliel agrees.

He may take only his phone, coat, keys, and the knowledge that the diary exists.

---

## Beat 7 — Departure

Gamaliel leaves the office.

The academy hallway feels wrong: children stretching, teachers speaking, music from studios, posters for performances, the normal life he built.

He moves through it as a father, not as a director.

Possible image:

Gamaliel passes the Mike Key Studio door and pauses for half a second. The name on the wall reminds him that protection in this family has never been simple. Mike protected by not panicking. Gamaliel has to learn the same discipline now.

---

# PART TWO — CHARLES UNIVERSITY

## Beat 8 — Arrival At Charles University

Gamaliel arrives at Charles University.

The university should feel like Simonka's world, not his:

- Lecture halls.
- Old stone buildings.
- Students with books, laptops, coats, coffee.
- Flyers on boards.
- Academic notices.
- Psychology department corridors.
- A library or courtyard.

This is the life Simonka chose outside ballet.

Gamaliel is comfortable in studios, rehearsal rooms, and offices. Here, he is a visitor in his daughter's separate life.

---

## Beat 9 — He Looks For Her Carefully

Gamaliel does not make a scene.

He may check her class schedule if the family knows it, wait outside a building, ask at a department office in a general way, or sit near a place where Simonka usually passes between classes.

He must be careful not to expose her private life.

He does not say:

> I am looking for my daughter because she became someone's pet.

He says only:

> I am Simonka Key-Dimitrov's father. I need to see if she is on campus today.

Or he does not ask staff at all; he simply waits where he knows she will be.

---

## Beat 10 — Gamaliel Sees Simonka

Gamaliel sees Simonka before she sees him.

This moment should be quiet and devastating.

She may be:

- Coming out of a lecture.
- Sitting alone with a notebook.
- Walking with classmates.
- Leaving the psychology building.
- Standing in a courtyard checking her phone.
- Looking ordinary enough that the diary feels impossible.

Gamaliel's first reaction is not to the scandal or the choice.

It is recognition:

> There is my daughter.

He sees the child she was and the adult she is at the same time.

---

## Beat 11 — What He Notices

Gamaliel studies her without wanting to stare.

Possible observations:

- She looks tired.
- She looks guarded.
- She is dressed carefully, perhaps more covered than usual, perhaps with a scarf or high collar.
- She moves like someone carrying private knowledge in public.
- She may look completely normal, which makes Gamaliel feel even more disoriented.
- She may smile at a classmate, and the smile may hurt him because it proves she can still perform ordinary life.

Avoid explicit physical inspection. The emotional point is that Gamaliel cannot tell from looking whether she is safe.

That frightens him.

---

## Beat 12 — The Moment Before Contact

Gamaliel has a choice:

- Call her name from across the courtyard.
- Approach slowly.
- Text her that he is nearby.
- Wait until she is alone.

The best option is the one that least ambushes her.

Recommended choice:

He sends a simple text:

> Simonko, jsem na univerzitě. Můžeš se mnou chvíli mluvit?
>
> Simonka, I am at the university. Can you talk to me for a moment?

This gives Simonka a sliver of control.

If she looks up and sees him, the scene holds on that eye contact.

---

## Beat 13 — Simonka Sees Him

Simonka sees Gamaliel.

Her reaction should be complex:

- Surprise.
- Fear.
- Embarrassment.
- Anger at the boundary crossing.
- Relief that someone came.
- The immediate calculation of how much he knows.

She may know instantly that the diary has been found.

Possible line:

> You found it.

Or:

> Klárka found it.

This shows that Simonka expected the diary to be read, but not necessarily that she was ready for the consequences.

---

## Beat 14 — Gamaliel Opens With Love, Not Accusation

Gamaliel remembers Klára's rules.

He does not begin with Holic.

He does not begin with *How could you?*

He begins with love.

Possible line:

> I love you.

Or:

> I needed to see you with my own eyes.

Or:

> Nejsi v problémech. Jsi moje dcera.
>
> You are not in trouble. You are my daughter.

This is the most important test of the scene. If he leads with accusation, Simonka closes. If he leads with love, the conversation can continue.

---

## Beat 15 — Simonka Asks How Much They Know

Simonka may ask:

> How much did she read?

Or:

> How much do you know?

Gamaliel must answer honestly.

Possible line:

> Enough to know you chose Holic. Not enough to understand.

This is direct without being cruel.

It also preserves the truth: the family knows the central fact, but not the full interior meaning.

---

## Beat 16 — Gamaliel Thinks Of Klára's Father

While Gamaliel is talking to Simonka, part of his mind is not at Charles University at all.

It goes back to **Klára's father**.

Not in a clean flashback, necessarily — more like a pressure under every word. Gamaliel remembers the story that shaped all of them: Klára as a little girl, the danger in her first home, the abuse she survived, the rescue that brought her into Patti Anne's household, and the way one adult man's power over a child changed the course of an entire family.

But the memory does not stop at childhood. It also goes to **Kent Institution in British Columbia**, when **Klára and Gamaliel were eighteen and nineteen**.

They visited Klára's father there.

This should be treated as a powerful earlier-life memory: after the rescue, after foster care, after years of growing inside Patti Anne's family, Klára made the choice — or agreed to the necessity — of seeing the man again inside a prison visit setting. Gamaliel went with her. He was not a child vomiting at Mike Key's dinner table anymore. He was older, nearly adult or newly adult, old enough to stand beside Klára, but still young enough that the sight of her father behind institutional boundaries burned itself into him.

**The Kent Institution memory may include:**

- The long trip to British Columbia.
- The controlled entry process.
- Doors, checks, waiting, institutional lighting.
- Klára's hand in Gamaliel's hand, or Klára refusing to let anyone see her hand shake.
- Klára's father no longer as the unseen monster from childhood, but as a contained man in a prison visiting room.
- Gamaliel realizing that a dangerous man can look ordinary once the system has put walls around him.
- The sick feeling that prison did not erase what he had done; it only put distance around it.

The memory matters because Gamaliel has already once sat across from a man who used power, secrecy, and a closed domestic world to harm someone Gamaliel loved.

Now Gamaliel is standing in front of his own daughter, hearing her say that another man has given her another name, another role, another set of rules.

His fear reaches for the old category first:

> Is Holic another version of him?

The Kent memory sharpens the question. Gamaliel remembers the man in prison, remembers Klára at eighteen/nineteen surviving the visit with her spine straight, remembers wanting to put himself between her and the past even though a visiting room made that impossible. Now he wants to put himself between Simonka and Holic, and again he may not be allowed to.

But the comparison is dangerous, and Gamaliel knows it. Klára's father abused a child and was in Kent Institution because the law had named what he did. Simonka is an adult. Klára had no meaningful choice. Simonka says she chose. If Gamaliel collapses those two situations into one, he may erase Simonka's agency and make her feel even less safe telling the truth.

At the same time, Gamaliel cannot simply dismiss the association. The family's history taught him that danger can hide inside homes, inside adult authority, inside silence, inside the word *nothing*. Kent Institution taught him something else: sometimes the world does eventually name a dangerous man, but only after harm has already happened.

This thought makes his restraint harder. It also makes it more necessary.

He must not speak to Simonka as if she is Klára as a child.

He must not speak to Holic, in his mind, as if Holic has already become Klára's father.

He must speak to Simonka as Simonka: grown, frightened or not frightened, choosing or confused, but still his daughter and still entitled to be heard.

---

## Beat 17 — Gamaliel Asks The Safety Question

Gamaliel asks the question he came to ask:

> Are you safe?

Simonka may answer too quickly:

> Yes.

Gamaliel may ask again, more carefully:

> Are you safe with him?

Or:

> Are you safe, and are you able to leave if you want to?

This question matters because it does not deny her agency. It asks whether her agency still exists inside Holic's world.

---

## Beat 18 — Simonka Defends Her Choice

Simonka may become defensive.

Possible lines:

> I chose this.

> He did not force me.

> You don't understand.

> It is not what you think.

> I knew you would look at me like this.

This is painful for Gamaliel because he is trying not to look at her like anything except his daughter.

He may answer:

> Then help me understand enough not to hurt you by accident.

This is a strong father line: he admits ignorance without surrendering care.

---

## Beat 19 — Gamaliel Does Not Demand She Come Home

This is the scene's central restraint.

Gamaliel wants to say:

> Come home now.

But he does not make it a command.

Instead:

> You can come home at any time.

Or:

> Your room is there. Your mother is there. Klárka is there. I am there. No explanation required first.

This keeps the door open without making home into a courtroom.

---

## Beat 20 — Simonka's Emotional Crack

Simonka may not cry fully, but something in her changes.

Possibilities:

- She looks away.
- Her mouth trembles once.
- She says, very quietly, that she knows.
- She admits she was afraid to tell them.
- She says she left the diary because speaking was impossible.

Possible line:

> I could write it. I couldn't say it.

This connects directly to the diary's role as her chosen voice.

---

## Beat 21 — The Conversation Ends Without Resolution

The encounter should not solve everything.

Gamaliel does not fully understand.

Simonka does not come home immediately unless a later plot decision requires it.

The purpose is contact, not resolution.

Possible ending options:

### Option A — Simonka Has To Return To Class

She says she has a seminar.

The ordinary university schedule interrupts a family crisis.

Gamaliel lets her go.

This is painful but respectful.

### Option B — Simonka Agrees To Call Klára

She does not come home, but she agrees to call her mother later.

This gives the family a next step.

### Option C — Simonka Agrees To Meet Them That Evening

She agrees to meet in a neutral place or at home, but only if they do not attack Holic.

This sets up a later family conversation.

### Option D — Simonka Walks Away

She is overwhelmed and leaves, but she does not disappear. She has heard that home remains open.

This keeps the emotional tension high.

---

## Beat 22 — Gamaliel Watches Her Go

The final image at Charles University:

Gamaliel watches Simonka walk back into her university life.

He has seen her.

She is alive. She is herself. She is changed. She is unreachable in some ways and still his daughter in others.

He cannot carry her home.

He can only make sure the way home remains lit.

---

# PART THREE — AFTERMATH

## Beat 23 — Gamaliel Reports Back To Klára

Later, Gamaliel calls or returns to the office/home.

Klára asks immediately:

> Did you see her?

He says:

> Yes.

This one word may make Klára cry again, or breathe for the first time all day.

Then he tells her the most important facts:

- Simonka is physically present at university.
- She knows they found the diary.
- She says she chose Holic.
- She says she is safe, or claims she is.
- She did not come home immediately.
- She knows home is open.

Klára absorbs this.

The family is not relieved exactly. But they are no longer speaking only to a diary.

---

> I am not going to him. I am going to her.

> If she is at Charles today, I can find her.

### Klára

> Do not make her defend him before you tell her you love her.

> Do not bring the diary like evidence.

> If you go with anger first, she will hide deeper.

> Tell her she can come home. Tell her that before anything else.

### Klárka

> I should go with you.

> She is my sister.

> If you see her, tell her I'm angry. No — don't tell her that. Tell her... tell her I love her.

> Tell her I read because she told me to.

### Simonka

> You found it.

> Klárka read it?

> How much do you know?

> I chose this.

> He did not force me.

> You don't understand.

> I could write it. I couldn't say it.

### Gamaliel to Simonka

> I love you.

> You are not in trouble.

> I needed to see you with my own eyes.

> Are you safe?

> Are you able to leave if you want to?

> Help me understand enough not to hurt you by accident.

> I am trying not to confuse my fear with your truth.

## What This Scene Establishes

1. **Gamaliel acts, but not recklessly.** He chooses to seek Simonka at Charles University rather than confront Holic first.

2. **Klára's restraint shapes the encounter.** Her conditions prevent Gamaliel from leading with anger or shame.

3. **Charles University represents Simonka's separate adult life.** Gamaliel must meet her in a world she chose outside ballet and outside family.

4. **The first contact is about safety, not control.** Gamaliel asks whether Simonka is safe and whether she can leave if she wants to.

5. **Simonka's agency remains central.** She says she chose Holic, which does not end the family's concern but changes how they can respond.

6. **Gamaliel's thoughts of Klára's father complicate his restraint.** The family history of abuse makes him fear Holic immediately, especially because Gamaliel remembers visiting Klára's father at **Kent Institution** with Klára when they were eighteen and nineteen. But Gamaliel has to recognize the difference between Klára's childhood abuse and Simonka's adult choice. The comparison is emotionally unavoidable but ethically dangerous.

7. **Home remains open.** Gamaliel's most important message is not *come home now* but *you can come home anytime*.

8. **The family moves from diary-knowledge to direct contact.** They are no longer only interpreting Simonka's written disclosure; Gamaliel has seen and spoken to her.

9. **Resolution is deferred.** The scene should end with contact, not with all problems solved.

## Continuity Notes

- This scene follows the Tuesday office scene where Klárka tells Gamaliel and Klára what she and Raphael found.
- It should remain emotionally intense but non-explicit.
- Gamaliel should not become controlling or violent; his struggle is precisely that he wants to act forcefully but chooses restraint.
- While talking to Simonka, Gamaliel should think about **Klára's biological father** and the abuse Klára survived. This includes the specific memory of visiting him with Klára at **Kent Institution in British Columbia** when Klára and Gamaliel were eighteen and nineteen. This memory should heighten his fear of Holic without allowing him to erase Simonka's adult agency.
- Simonka should not be treated as a child. She is an adult, and the conversation must reflect that even when Gamaliel is frightened.
- Klára's instructions before he leaves are important because they keep the family response from becoming shame-based.
- This scene can lead to a later Simonka/Klára phone call, a family meeting, or a direct confrontation with Holic Bartunek depending on the next arc decision.
