# SIMONKA MEETS HER ADVISOR — Monday, 3:00 PM

## Scene Outline — Beats, Dialogue Cues, and Thematic Notes

> **Placement:** Monday afternoon, 3:00 PM. Simonka has left the kennel, composed herself, dressed as a student, and walked from Holic's mansion to the Faculty of Arts at Charles University. This is the appointment she mentioned to Holic in the kitchen.
> 
> **Companion to** `other-owner-proposes-breeding-deal-scene-outline.md`, `simonka-kata-profile.md`, and the existing thesis-defense outlines. **Read those first.** This scene is the first time the academic world and the household world intersect in spoken dialogue.

> **Content & craft warning.** Simonka discloses to her academic advisor the full reality of her life as GG — the weekend's events, what Klárka Pavlínka did, and the breeding proposal. This is a personal disclosure in an academic setting. The advisor must process information that falls entirely outside the normal student-supervisor relationship. Render with the saga's established discipline: no sensationalism, no judgement, no glossing. The facts are the facts. The advisor hears them. The advisor responds.

---

**POV:** **Simonka Káta.** She has decided, during the walk from the kennel to the university, that she will tell the advisor everything. Not as confession. Not as therapy. As *data*. The thesis cannot be written without the advisor understanding where the material comes from.

**Tone:** Professional, restrained, direct. The register is Czech-academic — formal, precise, courteous. Simonka does not cry. She does not dramatize. She sits in her advisor's office and speaks the way she would speak about a research finding. The content is extraordinary; the delivery is not.

**Location:** **The advisor's office at the Faculty of Arts, Charles University, Prague.** A small room — bookshelves lined with psychology journals and monographs, a heavy wooden desk, two chairs, a window onto the faculty courtyard. The same room where they have met many times before about methodology, literature, and proposal structure. Today the room holds a different conversation.

**Time in saga:** Monday, 3:00 PM. Approximately twelve hours after Simonka crawled to the kennel. She has come out of GG-form, changed into her university clothes, and walked from Holic's mansion to the faculty. The brand is under her clothing. She carries it into the room.

**The advisor:** Male. Mid-fifties or early sixties. An established academic in psychology at Charles University. Has supervised Simonka through the withdrawal of the first proposal and the construction of the second. Respects Simonka's intellectual seriousness. Has already voted in favor of the thesis proposal at committee. **Does not know any of what Simonka is about to tell him.**

## THE BEATS

### Beat 1 — The Arrival

Simonka arrives at the office at 3:00 PM. She is on time — she is always on time. She is dressed as a student: long skirt, blouse, cardigan, the clothes she wears when she is Simonka Káta, master's candidate in psychology at the oldest university in Central Europe.

She sits in the chair opposite the advisor's desk. The conversation begins with the usual academic greetings. The advisor has her file open. He has notes. He is ready to discuss the thesis proposal.

Then Simonka shifts the register.

Simonka: *"Before we discuss the proposal — I need to tell you about my weekend. About what I did, starting Friday. It is relevant to the thesis. It is the reason for the thesis."*

The advisor sets down his pen. He closes the file. He recognizes the shift. This is not a student coming to discuss methodology. This is something else. He listens.

### Beat 2 — Friday

Simonka tells the advisor what she did on Friday.

She went to Holic's mansion. She entered GG-form. The twenty-four-hour period began. She describes what GG is — not in euphemisms, not in academic jargon, but in the plain language of the household: the collar, the blindfold, the commands, the kennel, the bowls, the posture. She explains that she is GG and that she is also Simonka and that the two are not separate people but different registers of the same person.

She tells him about the Collegium — the society, the rules, the motto *Per vincula, cura* (through the bond, care). She explains the silence rule: members do not disclose their membership to outsiders. She explains that she is disclosing to him now because the thesis depends on it, and because the Collegium permits disclosure for academic study provided the community's anonymity is protected.

The advisor listens. He does not interrupt. He takes notes — literally, with a pen, on paper. The notebook is the academic's tool; it is also a shield. He has been a supervisor for twenty years. He has heard students disclose personal material before. But he has never heard a disclosure like this.

### Beat 3 — Saturday and Sunday

Simonka continues. She tells the advisor about Saturday — the ongoing test, the household's routines, the ways the weekend deepened.

She tells him about Sunday — the other owner's visit, the encounter with Bruno, the demonstration. She tells him what the other owner said: that GG is a "professional." She tells him about the breeding proposal.

The breeding proposal, in Simonka's words:

- The other owner proposed to Holic: GG will get pregnant with Bruno.
- Holic can keep the baby — the puppy.
- Holic said he needed to think about it.
- Holic said it was ultimately GG's decision.
- Holic would discuss it with Simonka at his mansion.

She tells the advisor what happened Monday morning: crawling to the kitchen, eating from the bowls, the walk in the yard, the pill, the release from the blindfold. She tells him about thanking Holic for being happy with her natural skills with Bruno. She tells him she said Bruno made her job easy but was very emphatic in the kennel.

Then she tells him what Holic asked: *"What do you want, Simonka?"*

And what she did: she knelt beside Holic and crawled to the kennel. She returned to GG-form not because she was commanded to but because she chose to. The breeding proposal remains unresolved. She has not decided.

### Beat 4 — Klárka Pavlínka

Then Simonka tells the advisor what Klárka Pavlínka did.

Klárka is Simonka's older sister — fifteen months older. Klárka has her own life, her own relationships, her own struggles. Simonka tells the advisor what Klárka has been doing — the specifics are established in the saga's other files (the Felician audition, crying in Raphael's arms on Monday night, the apartment visits, the relationship with Simonka's parents).

**What Klárka did matters to the thesis** because Klárka is part of the family architecture that Simonka is trying to understand academically. The family is the context. The family is the reason the Collegium matters to Simonka in the first place. Simonka cannot explain the thesis without explaining the family, and she cannot explain the family without explaining Klárka.

The advisor listens. He writes more notes. The room is quiet except for Simonka's voice and the scratch of the pen.

### Beat 5 — The Advisor's Response

The advisor stops writing. He sets down the pen. He looks at Simonka — really looks at her, the way he has never looked at her before. For the first time, he sees the full person: the student, the GG, the sister, the woman who crawled to a kennel on Monday morning and then walked to this office at three o'clock and told him everything.

He speaks. His response is measured, careful, academically grounded.

**The advisor's position: he needs to check this out.**

Advisor: *"Simonka. What you are telling me is extraordinary. I believe you. I believe you are telling me the truth. But before I take your changed thesis proposal to the university's committee — before I put my name behind a study that is built on this — on your life, on your sister's life, on this breeding arrangement, on this society you are part of — I need to check it."*

He continues:

*"I need to understand the framework. I need to verify that the Collegium is what you say it is — that it is an organized, ethical community with real rules and real protections, not something dangerous or exploitative. I need to read the bylaws you have shown me. I need to understand the consent architecture you are describing. I need to evaluate whether your insider position is a strength for this research or a liability."*

*"I will not dismiss what you are telling me. I will not, either, take it to the committee without satisfying myself that the methodology is sound, that the ethics are protected, and that the research you are proposing is research you are in a position to do."*

*"Give me time. I will check this out. Then we will talk again."*

### Beat 6 — What "Checking This Out" Means

The advisor outlines — or implies — what he needs to do:

1. **Read the Collegium bylaws.** Simonka may give him a copy of the household's rules — the same document that governs Holic's mansion, the same document that carries the motto *Per vincula, cura*. He will read it. He will evaluate it as an academic evaluates an institutional framework.

2. **Verify the community's existence and ethics.** He may need to speak to someone who can confirm that the Collegium is real, organized, and ethical — not necessarily Holic, not necessarily anyone in the household, but someone who can provide independent verification.

3. **Evaluate the insider-research problem.** Simonka is proposing to study a community she is a member of. This is a recognized methodological challenge in ethnographic research. The advisor needs to determine whether Simonka's insider status compromises the research or strengthens it — and whether the proposal's methodology adequately addresses the problem.

4. **Consider the ethics of disclosure.** Simonka has told the advisor about other people — Holic, Klárka, the other owner, the prostitute — without their consent. The advisor needs to consider whether the thesis can proceed without violating these people's privacy, and whether Simonka's disclosure to him is itself an ethical breach of the Collegium's silence rule.

5. **Decide whether to support the proposal to the committee.** The advisor has already voted in favor of the thesis proposal at committee — but he voted in favor of a proposal that did not include this information. Now he has new information. He may maintain his vote. He may withdraw his support. He may add conditions.

### Beat 7 — Simonka's Response

Simonka accepts the advisor's position. She expected it. She knows that what she has told him is extraordinary and that his job is to protect both her and the university. She does not argue. She does not push. She does not try to convince him.

Simonka: *"I understand. I will wait."*

Or: *"Yes. Take the time you need. I will be here."*

Her response is calm. She has told the truth. The truth is on the table. The advisor will do what he must. She will wait.

### Beat 8 — The Closing

The meeting ends. The advisor does not touch her arm. The physical distance between them has changed — not because of disgust or fear, but because the relationship has shifted. Simonka is no longer just a student. She is a person who has disclosed her whole life, and the advisor is a person who has heard it and must now decide what to do with that knowledge.

Simonka leaves the office. She walks through the Faculty of Arts building, down the stairs, out into the courtyard. The afternoon light is different from the morning light — the kennel is behind her, the breeding proposal is behind her, the thesis is in front of her, on hold, pending verification.

She goes home. The brand is under her clothing. She is Simonka. She is GG. She is both. And the thesis — the thing she has been writing for a year, the thing she came to this meeting to discuss — is now waiting on an advisor's judgment of a world he has never entered.

## KEY DETAILS TO INCLUDE

- **Simonka's delivery is calm, structured, factual.** She does not cry. She does not dramatize. She speaks the way she would deliver research findings — because that is what this is, for her. This *is* research. This is the data behind the proposal.

- **The advisor's note-taking is visible and continuous.** He writes as she speaks. The notebook fills. The pen scratches. The physical act of writing is the academic's response to information that has no academic frame yet. He is building the frame in real time.

- **The office's physical details matter.** The bookshelves, the desk, the window, the courtyard. The room is the same room where they have met before, but it feels different today. The furniture has not changed; the conversation has.

- **The breeding proposal is disclosed but not decided.** Simonka tells the advisor about it, but she does not tell him what she will decide. She has not decided. The proposal remains open. The advisor may or may not comment on it personally. **Recommendation: he does not. The breeding proposal is Simonka's life decision; the thesis is Simonka's academic work. He addresses only the academic.**

- **The advisor's "checking" is not skepticism — it is responsibility.** He is not doubting Simonka. He is doing his job as an academic supervisor: verifying the framework, protecting the student, protecting the university, protecting the research integrity.

- **The silence rule.** Simonka must tell the advisor about the Collegium's silence rule — that members cannot disclose their own membership, that the bylaws forbid speaking about the community to outsiders. This creates an ethical tension: Simonka is disclosing to her advisor, which is itself a disclosure. The advisor should note this — *"You are telling me things your community forbids you to tell me."* Simonka's response: *"I am not breaking the rules. The Collegium permits disclosure for academic study, provided the community's anonymity is protected. The proposal protects anonymity. So does this conversation."*

## SAGA-LEVEL NOTES

- **This is the first time the academic world and the household world intersect in spoken dialogue.** Until now, the thesis has existed on paper and in committee rooms; the household has existed behind closed doors and iron fences. This scene brings them into the same room. The intersection is unavoidable: the thesis is about the household. The household is the thesis.

- **The advisor's response — "I need to check this out" — is the saga's first external validation request.** Until now, the Collegium has been self-validating: its rules are its rules, its ethics are its ethics, its members trust the framework. The advisor's request for verification introduces the outside world's demand for evidence. This is not a challenge to the Collegium's legitimacy; it is the normal academic demand for methodological transparency. But the demand itself is a stress test.

- **Simonka's disclosure to the advisor is the saga's first voluntary breach of the silence rule.** She tells the advisor about Holic, about GG, about the breeding proposal, about Klárka, about the Collegium. She does this with the Collegium's academic-study exception — but the exception has never been tested this far before. The advisor now knows. The advisor now holds Simonka's secret. This changes the relationship permanently.

- **The breeding proposal remains unresolved at the end of this scene.** Simonka has told the advisor about it, but she has not decided. The advisor has not advised her on it. The proposal hangs — between Simonka and Holic, between GG and the other owner, between the kennel and the university. It will be resolved in a future scene.
