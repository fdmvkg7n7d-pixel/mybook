# Klárka Pavlînka – Grade Twelve Paris Trip Scene Outline

## Scene Goal
Establish the template that Simonka later inherits — Gamaliel as the parent-chaperone who shares a hotel room — and show Klárka's different personality (dancer, confident, slightly embarrassed but accepting) during her own Grade 12 trip.

## Characters
- **Klárka Pavlînka Key** – 17, Grade 12, ballet student at Prague academy. Older sister, fifteen months older than Simonka. Natural performer, comfortable with attention.
- **Gamaliel Galvin Key** – 42, father, former dancer, co-director. Speaks fluent French from touring.
- **Klára Key** – mother (home in Prague, running spring intensive)
- **M. Dubois** – French literature teacher, trip leader
- **Simonka Káta** – 16, referenced (at home, jealous she doesn't get to go yet)

## Premise
Klárka's Grade 12 class takes a 4-day French literature/art trip to Paris (Louvre, Musée d'Orsay, Opéra Garnier, Montmartre).

**Gamaliel comes along with the class and shares a hotel room with Klárka because they are family and always see each other in the morning.**

In the Prague academy apartment, mornings mean: Gamaliel making coffee, Klára at the barre, Klárka and Simonka fighting over the bathroom, the Golden Retrievers underfoot. When Klárka's school announced Paris, Gamaliel volunteered as parent chaperone (fluent French, DBS-cleared), and he booked a twin room at Hôtel Malte Astotel (Rue de Richelieu) because Klárka — even at 17 — still likes waking up to her dad's voice saying "dobré ráno" before a big day.

## Narrative Beats

### 1. Departure – Prague Main Station (EuroNight train)
- Klárka with pointe shoes packed "just in case" and a stack of Balzac. Gamaliel with a small notebook of French ballet terms.
- Klára's goodbye: "Send me a photo from the Opéra steps. And morning coffee, both of you." Simonka (16) waves from doorway, already planning her own Rome trip next year.
- M. Dubois grateful: "Monsieur Key, your French will save us at customs."

### 2. Hotel Check-in – Twin Room
- Receptionist in Paris assumes "père et fille" immediately — less awkward than Rome. 
- Klárka claims bed by window, immediately does tendus to test floor. Gamaliel unpacks, puts family photo on nightstand.
- Their established morning ritual: Gamaliel wakes first, makes instant coffee with travel kettle, they review the day's itinerary together before she meets classmates.

### 3. Day 2 – Paris (Louvre & Opéra Garnier)
**Morning – Louvre**
- Klárka moves through galleries like a dancer — posture perfect. Gamaliel translates French placards, stays three paces back.
- In front of Winged Victory, Klárka whispers: "She looks like she's about to land from a grand jeté." Gamaliel: "That's my girl."

**Afternoon – Opéra Garnier**
- Special backstage tour (Gamaliel's old company contact). Klárka stands on the stage where her parents once dreamed of dancing. She is moved, not embarrassed.
- Gamaliel watches his daughter in the place he never got to perform professionally — full circle.

**Evening – Hotel**
- Klárka admits she's nervous about auditions next year. Gamaliel makes tea, they talk until lights out — same as home.

### 4. Day 3 – Montmartre Morning
- 7am: They walk to a boulangerie for croissants before class meets. This is their "morning together" — the reason for the shared room.
- Klárka takes selfie with Sacré-Cœur behind them, sends to Klára and Simonka: "morning coffee in Paris"
- Classmates tease lightly: "Your dad is cooler than our chaperone." Klárka: shrugs, proud.

### 5. The Conversation
- On last night, Klárka asks: "Will you do this for Simonka next year?" 
- Gamaliel: "If she wants. We do mornings as family. Doesn't matter if it's Prague, Paris, or Rome."
- Klárka: "She'll want it. She's more nervous than me." Establishes Simonka's later Rome pattern.

### 6. Return – Prague
- Train home. Klárka writes in journal: "Paris with Dad."
- Klára and Simonka meet them at station with Kleopatra. Simonka immediately asks: "Did you share a room? Was it weird?" Klárka: "No. It was just morning."

## Thematic Resonance
- **Precedent:** Klárka's Paris trip is the direct precedent for Simonka's Rome trip one year later — same father, same twin-room, same morning ritual.
- **Dancer vs. Non-Dancer:** Klárka loves the Opéra; Simonka will later love the Vatican Museums. Both get Gamaliel's translation and quiet support.
- **Family Continuity:** The Key-Galvin family doesn't do "send kids away alone." Mornings together travel.
