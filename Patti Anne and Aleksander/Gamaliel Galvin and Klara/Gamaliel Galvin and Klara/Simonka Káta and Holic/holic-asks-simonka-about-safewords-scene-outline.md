# SCENE OUTLINE: HOLIC ASKS GG WHY SHE DIDN'T USE HER SAFEWORDS

**Part of the Simonka/Holic/GG arc.**
**Placement:** Friday morning at seven o'clock. After her Wednesday meeting with her thesis advisor at Charles University, Simonka went straight to Holic's mansion and the twenty-four-hour period began. She is now in the outside doghouse, resting from the deep nap Holic told her to take after the twenty-four hours ended. She has slept. She is waking. He comes to her.

**Characters:** Holic Bartunek, Simonka Káta Key (GG)

**Setting:** The fenced yard behind Holic's mansion. The doghouse — warm, prepared, exactly as it was during the weekend trial. Simonka is inside, waking from exhaustion. The afternoon or evening light filters through the yard. The air is cool. The fence holds the world out. This is GG's space, and Holic is entering it.

**Tone:** Quiet, serious, intimate. Not prurient, not graphic. The focus is on the question, the answer, and the dynamic between a Dominant who cares about his pet's limits and a submissive who pushed past them because she wanted to please him. This is about trust, boundary, and the gap between what GG says she can do and what her body actually needed.

---

## BEFORE THE SCENE

- The twenty-four hours are over — they ran Thursday. On Wednesday, Simonka met with her thesis advisor at Charles University. She asked him to let her change her thesis. He told her to bring a proposal. She left with her notebook heavier than it was when she walked in. Then came Thursday — the twenty-four hours with Holic. Then the nap. Now Friday morning at seven o'clock. She is waking. He comes to her.
- Simonka has been with women and men, with Holic and others from the Collegium, in the fenced yard and the rooms beyond it.
- She did not use her safewords. Not once. Not yellow. Not red. Even when her body wanted to. Even when her mind wandered to places she didn't want it to. Even when the cages in the box vans were a thing she had to step past. She held the frame. She was GG. She was Holic's.
- When it was over, Holic led her to the doghouse. He told her to nap. She slept — deep, exhausted, earned rest.
- Now she is waking. Her body is sore. Her mind is quiet in the way that only comes after something intense has passed through it. She is still in the doghouse. Still GG. Still his.
- He comes to her.

---

## THE SCENE

### He Arrives

- Simonka hears him before she sees him. His footsteps on the grass. The gate latch. The way he moves — not loudly, not with announcement, but with the certainty of a man who owns the space he is walking through.
- He kneels at the entrance of the doghouse. He does not reach in. He does not pull her out. He waits for her to come to him. That is how he has always done it: he offers the threshold; she chooses to cross it.
- She crawls out slowly. Her body is tired. Her hair is loose. Her clothes are folded neatly on top of her backpack nearby — she has not put them back on. She is still in her underwear, the way the Collegium requires. She does not cover herself. She is GG. This is her space.
- She looks at him. He looks at her.

### The Question

- He does not start with praise. He does not start with correction. He starts with the thing that matters.
- *You didn't use your safewords.*
- It is not an accusation. It is not a compliment. It is a statement. A question wrapped in a fact.
- *Not yellow. Not red. Not once. In twenty-four hours.*
- She says nothing. She waits. She has learned to wait.
- *Tell me why.*

### Her Answer

- She does not lie. She has learned, in this world, that lying is the one thing he will not tolerate. Not because he is cruel — because the frame only works if the words are true.
- *Because I wanted to please you.*
- He does not react. He is a man who has heard this before — from other pets, from other subs, from people who confused endurance with devotion.
- *I know you wanted to please me. That is not the question. The question is: why didn't you use your words when your body needed them?*
- She looks away. Not out of shame. Out of the difficulty of naming something she herself doesn't fully understand.
- *Because I thought if I used them, you would think I couldn't do it. That I wasn't strong enough. That I wasn't —* She stops. She doesn't say the word she means: *good enough.*
- He hears it anyway.
- *GG. Listen to me.*
- She looks at him.
- *The safewords are not a test. They are not a failure. They are the thing that makes the rest of it possible. You say yellow, and I slow down. You say red, and everything stops. That is the agreement. That is the frame. If you don't use them, the frame breaks. And if the frame breaks, this isn't care anymore. It's just endurance.*
- She nods. She understands the words. But her body — the body that just spent twenty-four hours holding itself together for him — hasn't caught up yet.
- *I didn't need them.*
- He says it quietly. *You're lying.*
- She flinches. Not from anger. From the accuracy of it.
- *You didn't need them in the moment. I know that. That's not the point. Your body needed them. Your mind needed them. And you chose to push past both because you thought pleasing me was more important than your own safety.*
- She is quiet. The air is cool. The fence holds the world out.
- *And then there was that man.*
- She flinches. He sees it. He knows she remembers.
- *He was on something. I saw the way he moved. You felt it too. His eyes were glassy. He wasn't present. He wasn't in control of his own body.*
- She closes her eyes. She remembers: the weight of him, the way his body moved without his mind, the way he didn't respond when she tried to check in. They didn't know *what* it was — didn't know the name of the pill, didn't know the rules that had been broken in secret. But they knew it wasn't right. And she didn't use her words.
- *You didn't say red.*
- *I didn't know what it was. I just thought...*
- *What did you think?*
- *I thought if I stopped it, you would think I couldn't handle it. That I was too weak. That I wasn't —* She stops again. She doesn't say the word: *your pet.*
- *GG. Listen to me. If a man is on something, he is not safe. For him. For you. For anyone. You don't know the name of the pill, you don't know what his owner gave him, but you know the body isn't right. You should have said red the moment you felt that. Not because you're weak. Because you're smart. Because you know the difference between endurance and danger. And what happened with that man was danger.*
- She nods. Her eyes are wet now. Not from pain. From the relief of being told — finally, clearly, by the one person whose opinion matters — that she doesn't have to survive everything to prove she belongs.
- *I won't let you do that again.*
- She looks at him. *What do you mean?*
- *If you don't use your safewords when you need them, I will stop the scene myself. I will call it. I will pull you out. I will not wait for you to break before I decide you've had enough. That is not how this works. You are not here to prove you can survive me. You are here to trust me enough to tell me when you can't.*

### The Aftercare

- He doesn't leave it at the correction. He never does. Holic is a man who understands that after the hard words come the soft hands.
- He reaches into the doghouse and pulls out a blanket — the one she used during the nap. He wraps it around her shoulders. Not because she is cold. Because she is shaking, and she doesn't even know she is shaking.
- *You did well.*
- Two words. But they are the ones she needed. Not because she needs praise — though she does, the way every person who has chosen surrender needs to know it was worth it — but because she needs to hear that her endurance mattered, even as he corrects the part of it that put her at risk.
- *You held the frame. You stayed. You were GG. I am proud of you.*
- She closes her eyes. The words land. They always do.
- *But next time, you use your words. When you need them. Not when I decide you do. When you decide you do.*
- She nods. *Yes, Holic.*
- *Good girl.*
- Not GG this time. Good girl. The long form. The one he uses only when it matters.
- She leans into the blanket. Into him. Into the space between correction and care that is exactly where this relationship lives.
- He doesn't let go of her immediately. He stays kneeling beside her in the grass, the fence holding the world out, the late afternoon light turning the yard gold.
- *I don't want you to suffer any pain out of this.*
- She looks up at him. The words are not a softening. They are a boundary, the same kind he just demanded from her: clear, direct, non-negotiable.
- *I don't want this to be pain. I don't want you to carry it. I don't want your body to remember it as suffering. What you did was hard. It was real. It was yours. But it is not supposed to hurt you. If it hurts you, I have failed. And I do not fail my pet.*
- She closes her eyes. The words land. Not because they erase the last twenty-four hours, but because they frame them. The pain she felt was real — the exhaustion, the soreness, the weight of the man who wasn't present. But Holic is telling her: the pain was not the point. The point was the surrender, the trust, the choice. And he will not let her confuse the two.
- *Okay,* she whispers.
- *Okay,* he says back.
- And then — because the correction is over, because the care has landed, because they are two people who know each other in a way that does not require words — she tells him about the thesis.
- *I went to Charles University on Wednesday. I met with my advisor. I asked him to let me change my thesis.*
- Holic waits. He knows enough about her life outside the fence to understand what a thesis means — the notebook, the tote bag, the hours of research, the academic rigor that has always been her chosen discipline.
- *I want to study this. The psychology of consensual power exchange. The architecture of trust. The way the body and the mind negotiate surrender and safety at the same time. Not from the outside — from the inside. From the perspective of someone who has chosen to be inside it.*
- He is quiet. Not surprised. Holic is a man who knows that the people who enter his world carry entire other worlds with them — careers, families, studies, the kind of intellectual life that has nothing to do with a fenced yard and a doghouse. But this is the first time she has told him about it. The first time she has named the thing that connects the GG she is here to the Simonka she is out there.
- *You want to write about us?*
- *I want to write about what I chose. About what it means to give someone power and still keep your voice. About the safewords. About the difference between surrender and loss. About the way you told me today that I didn't have to suffer to prove I belonged.*
- He nods. He doesn't smile — Holic doesn't smile much — but something in his posture shifts. Softens. Approves.
- *Then you should.*
- She blinks. She expected questions. Pushback. Maybe even a boundary — *some things stay in the yard.* But he doesn't give her any of that.
- *You chose this. You chose the collar. You chose the yard. You chose the frame. And now you are choosing to study it, to name it, to understand it with the mind your mother gave you. That is not a betrayal. That is the most honest thing you could do.*
- She doesn't cry. Not here. Not in the yard. But her breath catches, the way it does when someone says the thing she didn't know she needed to hear.
- *My advisor didn't say yes. He said sit with it. Bring me a proposal — not a feeling, a proposal. So I will. I will write it. And when I do, I will bring it to him. And he will say yes.*
- Holic reaches out. Not to touch her face this time. To touch the blanket, to pull it tighter around her shoulders, to make sure she is warm.
- *Write it, then.*
- And then, because he is Holic, and because he knows the difference between GG and Simonka and he honors both:
- *But write it as Simonka. Not as GG. GG belongs in the yard. Simonka belongs in the world. And the world needs to hear what she has to say.

## WHY THIS SCENE MATTERS

- **Holic's character.** He is not a man who demands obedience at the cost of safety. He demands honesty — the kind of honesty that includes saying *I can't* when the body and mind need it. The safewords are not a formality. They are the foundation. If GG won't use them, the frame collapses.
- **GG's growth.** This is the first time she is confronted not with the physical reality of what she endured, but with the emotional reason she endured it: the belief that pleasing Holic was more important than her own limits. He names it. He corrects it. He doesn't let her hide behind endurance.
- **The dynamic.** He is firm, but he is not cruel. He praises her, but he doesn't let the praise erase the correction. He wraps her in a blanket and calls her *good girl* — and in the same breath tells her that next time, he will stop the scene himself if she won't use her words. This is not punishment. This is the architecture of care.
- **The parallel to Klára.** Klára is across the city, teaching a ten-year-old girl that a broken floorboard is a reason to be angry — because safety matters, because the people who build the space must protect the people who use it. Holic is saying the same thing to GG: *I built this frame. I will not let you break it by pretending you're fine when you're not.*
- **The parallel to Simonka's thesis.** On Wednesday, she told her advisor she wants to study the psychology of consensual power exchange — the architecture of trust, the way the body and mind negotiate surrender and safety at the same time. This scene is the lived version of that thesis. She is inside it. She is the subject. And Holic is the one teaching her what safety actually looks like.
