# HOLIC INVITES A WOMAN OVER — GG WATCHES

## Scene Outline — Beats, Dialogue Cues, and Thematic Notes

> **Companion to** `simonka-kata-profile.md`, `holic-club-rules-and-bylaws.md`, and the existing Holic/Simonka outlines. Read the profile first for relationship and pet-training context.

> **Content & craft warning.** This scene depicts a private adult test inside a consensual human-pet relationship. **Simonka, as GG, is asked to be passively present** — collared, in pet-position, in the same room — while Holic and a paid woman are sexually together. She does not participate. She is not touched. **The test is whether GG can hold the role without breaking.** Render with the saga's established discipline: no glamour, no titillation, no eroticisation of the watching. The scene's content is the **interior cost** of being asked to absorb what one's owner is doing with someone else, while one's body is supposed to register nothing. This is not sex-scene material. It is interiority material, with sex in the room.

> **Saga placement.** This scene happens **before the thesis-defense scene** (`simonka-second-masters-thesis-proposal-defense-scene-outline.md`). Simonka has not yet begun to write the second thesis proposal. She does not yet have the academic vocabulary that, later, will let her name what is being done to her in formal Czech prose. **The contrast is structural and the saga should let it land in retrospect:** what she answers Marešová with, a year or two from now, she pays for tonight.

**POV:** **Simonka Káta.** Continuity from the thesis-defense outline, set *earlier* in her GG period — when interiority is what she has *instead of* academic distance. The reader sees the room from the only consciousness in it that is not being permitted to act on what it is registering.
**Tone:** Held. Slow. Geological inside a body required to be still. ## CANON ESTABLISHED BY THIS SCENE

*(So later writing can rely on it.)*

The prostitute is a professional Holic has used before [TBD how often — suggest two or three previous visits, none with GG present]. The test, in his mind, is whether GG can hold passive presence while Holic is sexually with someone else in the same room. *He believes — the saga should let the reader feel this without entering his head — that GG passing this test is part of her formation; that a pet who can hold this is a pet who has been correctly trained.* Whether his frame is correct is the saga's running question about him.

(CANON.)** Holic discloses each of these only when she has already crossed each threshold. - **Safewords are available but not refreshed. (CANON.)** GG has yellow/red, established in the profile and used once before in aftercare. **Holic does not, tonight, remind her of them before the encounter begins.** The omission is its own thing. Whether it is deliberate (the test includes whether GG knows she can stop), or a lapse Holic himself would not want to inspect, the prose refuses to decide.

- **GG is not touched.** Not by Holic during the encounter. Not by the prostitute. Not in passing. The not-touching is part of the test's frame and is, to Holic's mind, the line that distinguishes this test from the male-pet weekend. *The reader is to feel that the line matters to Holic and is, by some measures, real, and is, by others, beside the point.*
- **Holic is fully sexually with the prostitute in the same room with GG present and watching. (CANON.)** The prose does not render the encounter erotically. **The encounter is rendered through GG's interiority** — what she hears, what she does not let herself hear, the count she begins involuntarily, the count she stops, the involuntary movement of her own breath, the deliberate stillness of her hands. The bed is in the room. What happens on the bed happens. The prose does not look away and does not lean in.

(CANON.)** Holic, after the prostitute has gone, performs aftercare with GG — established household practice; warm bath, as in the male-pet weekend. **GG does not, in aftercare, tell him what tonight cost.** She also does not lie. ### The pill regime (CANON — established here, retroactive to the start of the GG arrangement)

## BEFORE THE SCENE — WHAT SIMONKA CARRIES INTO IT

*Establish — briefly — the GG-period Simonka, pre-thesis, who does not yet have the language she will later have.*

- **She has been GG for some months.** Long enough that the doghouse weekends have a rhythm. Long enough that the brand is fully healed and she has stopped noticing it under clothing. Long enough that the household — Holic's mansion — is a place she has a designated set of behaviors inside.
- **She is, in her city life, still a master's student.** Her first thesis (the safer one, attachment in Czech residential foster care) is underway. She has not yet had the encounter with the international human-pet literature that will eventually become the second proposal. **Tonight she does not have, anywhere in her mind, the sentence *I could write about this.*** That sentence belongs to the Simonka of a later night. **Tonight she has only the thing itself, with no door out into language.**
- **She has been told only that there will be a guest tonight.** Holic mentioned it in the morning, in his usual register — the small calm assertion that a thing will happen and GG will be present for it. She has not asked who. She did not ask because *in this household, GG does not ask.* That is a piece of the household's grammar she has, without entirely thinking about it, internalised.
- **She has prepared herself in the ways the household has trained her to prepare:** she has bathed; she has come to the mansion in the agreed clothing (which she removes inside the threshold, per the household's standing practice); she has accepted the collar from Holic's hand in the foyer. **The collaring happened forty minutes ago.** Since then she has been on the cushioned platform in the bedroom. There is a low light by the bed and a slightly brighter light by the sitting area and very little light where she is. **She is, she will later realise, in the room's darkest place — and the choice was Holic's.** Tonight she has not yet noticed.

## BEAT 1 — THE WAITING

*Short. Establish the platform, the dark place, the room she has been put in to wait.*

- The platform is at the foot of the bed, set diagonally so that GG, in correct pet-position (sitting on her heels, hands folded in her lap, eyes lowered, the position Holic has trained for months), is angled toward the bed but not facing it square. *The angle is the household's small precision. Facing the bed square would be cruel in a different way than facing it at this angle, and Holic has chosen the smaller cruelty.*

- She does not move on the platform. The position is the position. The training is the training. *(The prose can let the reader feel that, for a pet correctly trained, the position is not particularly difficult to hold for long stretches — and that the body's training is precisely the thing that will, tonight, make the rest of the evening possible. **The body knows what to do. The mind is the part that does not yet know.**)*

## BEAT 2 — THE ARRIVAL

*The first disclosure. Holic enters with the woman. GG, in correct position, does not raise her eyes — but she sees the lower half of the room.*

*The second disclosure. The conversation in the sitting area, which has been pleasantries, becomes something else.*

- The conversation softens. The woman's voice drops half a register. Holic's voice does the same. *The prose does not render the small intimate dialogue.* GG hears it; the reader does not need it; what is being established is the shift in atmosphere from social to intimate.

## BEAT 4 — WHAT HAPPENS ON THE BED

*The scene's centre. Render with the discipline named in the warning. No erotic register. The encounter is rendered through GG's interiority only — what she hears, what she does not let herself hear, what her body wants to do, what her body does instead.*

### 4b — The middle

- The bed receives their weight further. - **The emptiness, once achieved, is its own kind of unbearable.** Inside the count, she had a small private activity. Inside the emptiness, she has only the room and what is in it. *Both options are forms of what the evening is doing to her.* She does not yet have the language for this observation. She will, eventually, have the language. Tonight she has only the experience.

## BEAT 5 — THE WOMAN LEAVES

*Short. The most important beat of the entire visit. The thing the prostitute does here is what GG will remember longest.*

- Holic walks the woman to the door of the bedroom. The footsteps go to the doorway.
- **At the doorway, the woman pauses.** GG, in position, registers the pause.
- The woman turns — and the prose must render this without making it large; it is a small movement that is too large for the scene — and **she looks at GG.** Briefly. One beat. She does not break the rules in any way the rules describe; she does not address GG, she does not approach GG, she does not raise her hand or speak. **She simply looks at GG once, as one person looks at another person.**

It is not curiosity. *It is the recognition that GG is in the room and is a person.* It is the smallest possible refusal of the entire evening's frame, and it is performed by the woman in the room who, in the household's official accounting, has the least standing to perform it.

## TONE GUARDRAILS

- **No erotic register.** This is the load-bearing tone discipline of the scene. The prose does not render the sex. It renders GG, in position, in the dark, hearing what she does not let herself hear. The bed is furniture being used. The prose holds that frame absolutely.
- **No glamour around the discipline.** GG holding position for the duration of the encounter is not *impressive*. It is the result of months of training that the saga has rendered with seriousness. The prose must not let the reader feel that GG's stillness is admirable. *Stillness under these conditions is what the training was for, and the training is what the saga has been interrogating from the beginning.*
- **No titillation.** Not in the lower-vision details. Not in the small sounds. Not in the woman's professional competence. The reader is not invited to enjoy the room. The reader is asked to sit on the platform with GG and hold what GG is holding.
- **No villainising of the prostitute.** She is a competent professional doing a job. She is, in the saga's accounting tonight, the kindest person in the room — and her kindness is small and earned and is rendered as such, not heroised. She does not save GG. **She will, however, reappear — multiple times across the saga, in subsequent visits to the mansion (canon, established at saga level — see the "Saga-level notes").** This first visit must therefore not over-narrate her interiority or fix her permanently as *the woman of the doorway-look*; her relationship to GG and to Holic is to be allowed to evolve across the later visits. Tonight she is the kindest person in the room because tonight she is new to GG; whether she remains so is, from tomorrow, an open question.
- **No interiority for Holic.** The reader sees him only as GG sees him. *Especially during the encounter, when entering his head would be the prose's failure.*
- **No interiority for the prostitute.** The reader sees her only as GG sees her. The single beat in the closing image where the prose briefly indicates that she remembers the look she gave — and files it — is the maximum interiority the prose permits her, and it is permitted only because it is sealed in the closing-image register that has, by saga custom, more latitude.
- **No psychologising of Simonka's not-safewording.** The prose does not, in the moment or in the closing image, tell the reader what the not-safewording *meant*. The prose names that she did not say yellow, names that she had the word, names that she thought of it once, and leaves the meaning unresolved. **The unresolved is the moral content.** Anyone — including Simonka — who decides too quickly what tonight meant will be lying.
- **The look at the doorway is the scene's hinge.** Everything in the prose should orient toward that beat without underlining it. The look is *small*. The prose holds it as small. The smallness is what makes it the line GG will, in some sense, never recover from.
