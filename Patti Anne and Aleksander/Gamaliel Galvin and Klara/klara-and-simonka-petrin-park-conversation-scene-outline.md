# SCENE OUTLINE: KLÁRA AND SIMONKA KÁTA’S CONVERSATION IN PETŘÍN PARK (ELECTRIC DOG COLLAR)

*This scene is the long-awaited face-to-face meeting between Klára and her daughter Simonka. It takes place in Petřín Park — the location chosen by Holic. Simonka attends wearing the electric dog collar.*

---

## CANON BACKDROP & HEART HISTORY

* **The Meeting Request:** Klára asked for a neutral, public place to speak with Simonka. Holic granted permission but chose the location himself.
* **Simonka’s State:** She is fully in GG mode. The electric dog collar is a visible and active symbol of her current life with Holic. It is a sleek, black leather collar with a small rectangular receiver box on the front. It can deliver a range of corrections from a mild tingle to a sharp, painful shock, controlled remotely by Holic via a small device he keeps with him. The collar is locked and cannot be removed by Simonka herself. It is worn openly at her throat during this meeting — a deliberate choice on Holic’s part.
* **Klára’s Approach:** Klára is not trying to force Simonka to come home. She wants to see her daughter, understand what is happening, and keep the door open.
* **Klára’s Emotional State:** Beneath her calm exterior, Klára is devastated. Seeing the electric collar on her daughter triggers a wave of grief, horror, and helpless rage that she must keep tightly controlled. She feels as though she has failed as a mother. At the same time, she is terrified of pushing too hard and losing Simonka completely. Every word she speaks is measured because she knows one wrong sentence could end the meeting. Underneath it all is a deep, aching love mixed with the sickening realization that her child is wearing something designed to deliver pain at another person’s command.
* **Petřín Park Context:** While the park has open, public areas, it also contains more secluded paths. Holic’s comment about members walking their pets there adds an unsettling layer to the location.
* **Emotional Stakes:** This is the first direct conversation between mother and daughter since Simonka left. Both are navigating grief, love, and the limits of influence.

---

## BEAT 1 — ARRIVAL AND FIRST SIGHT

* **The Setting:** A relatively open but quiet area of Petřín Park — benches with a view, but not completely exposed. The time of day allows for some privacy without total isolation.
* **Simonka’s Appearance:** She arrives dressed in a way that balances the GG aesthetic with public acceptability. The electric dog collar is visible at her throat (or the outline of it is clear beneath her clothing).
* **Klára’s Reaction:** Klára sees the collar immediately. Her face registers shock, pain, and careful control. She does not comment on it right away. She simply opens her arms for a hug if Simonka allows it.
* **Simonka’s Posture:** She maintains GG body language — respectful, slightly lowered gaze, careful movements — even in this neutral space.

---

## BEAT 2 — OPENING THE CONVERSATION

* **Klára’s First Words:** She keeps her tone gentle and non-confrontational.
  * “Thank you for coming. I know you didn’t have to.”
* **Simonka’s Response:** She answers in the measured, deferential tone she uses with Holic.
  * “Holic allowed it.”
* **The Collar as Presence:** The electric dog collar sits between them like a third participant. Neither woman names it yet, but its presence shapes every word.

---

## BEAT 3 — THE HEART OF THE CONVERSATION

* **Klára’s Questions:** She asks about Simonka’s well-being, her studies at Charles University, and whether she is safe. She does not demand answers about Holic or the collar.
* **Simonka’s Defenses:** Simonka repeats variations of what she told Gamaliel:
  * “I chose this.”
  * “This is what I want.”
  * “Home is still open if I want it.”
* **Klára’s Deeper Wound:** Klára gently pushes back on the language Simonka is using — particularly the way she has adopted the same phrases of autonomy that Klára once used to protect herself as a child.
* **The Collar Moment:** At some point, Klára’s eyes return to the collar. She may ask, very quietly:
  * “Does it hurt?”
  * Or simply: “Can you take it off when you want to?”

---

## BEAT 4 — SIMONKA’S RESPONSE TO THE COLLAR QUESTION

* **Simonka’s Answer:** She does not give a simple yes or no. She may say something like:
  * “It’s part of the rules.”
  * “I’m a good girl.”
* **Emotional Crack:** For the first time, a small crack appears in Simonka’s composure. She does not cry, but her voice wavers slightly when she says she chose this.
* **Klára’s Reaction:** Klára does not argue or beg. She simply says:
  * “I love you. That has never changed. And it never will.”

---

## BEAT 5 — THE CLOSING OF THE MEETING

* **Time Limit:** Holic’s conditions are respected. The meeting has a set duration.
* **Simonka’s Departure:** She stands to leave. Before she goes, Klára may ask one final question:
  * “If you ever want to come home — even just for a night — will you tell me?”
* **Simonka’s Final Line:** She answers carefully:
  * “I’ll think about it.”
* **The Goodbye:** There is no dramatic embrace unless Simonka initiates it. Klára lets her daughter walk away, watching until she is out of sight.

---

## BEAT 6 — AFTER THE MEETING (OPTIONAL EXTENSION)

* **Klára’s Solitude:** Once Simonka leaves, Klára remains on the bench for a long time, processing what she saw — especially the electric collar.
* **Simonka’s Return:** Simonka returns to Holic and reports the conversation as required. The scene may end with her removing or adjusting the collar under Holic’s supervision, depending on protocol.

---

## WHAT THIS OUTLINE ADDS TO CANON

1. **The Collar as Symbol:** Establishes the electric dog collar as a visible, physical representation of Simonka’s current state and Holic’s control.
2. **Klára’s Strength:** Shows Klára choosing love and openness over confrontation, even in the face of something deeply painful.
3. **Simonka’s Internal Conflict:** Highlights the tension between her claimed choice and the visible signs of control.
4. **Petřín Park as Setting:** The park becomes a charged location — public on the surface, but carrying the weight of Holic’s world through the secluded pet-walking areas.
5. **Future Potential:** This meeting can lead to further scenes (Simonka’s report to Holic, Klára’s emotional aftermath, Klárka’s reaction, etc.).

---

*This scene is one of the emotional centerpieces of the Simonka arc. It can be written with heavy interiority for both women.*
