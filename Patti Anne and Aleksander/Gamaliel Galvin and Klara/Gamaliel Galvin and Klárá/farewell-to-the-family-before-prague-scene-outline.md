# SCENE OUTLINE: FAREWELL TO THE FAMILY — THE LAST DAYS BEFORE PRAGUE

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Gamaliel Galvin Key (19), Klára (18), Patti Anne, Aleksander, Palmer Peter (6), Salena Sarai (6), Dr. Naomi Hartwell, Rabbi Avram Loewy; Willow and Kleopatra (the two Golden Retrievers, both making the move to Prague)
- **Location:** Vaughan — the family house; Dr. Hartwell's office; the temple
- **When:** 2062, after the Kent Institution visit and the three Vancouver days. The last week in Canada.
- **Status:** Outline
- **Sequence (CANON):** Kent Institution → Vancouver (three days) → **back to Vaughan for the farewells** → **Orlando, to Terri Anne at Cypress Cove** → **Prague, permanently.** The farewell week is the hinge: the last days under Patti Anne's roof — ending with **the Friday: temple for the last time, then the barbecue as the farewell Shabbat dinner (Se'udat Shabbat).**

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

The leaving, done the Key way: nothing slipped out of, everything said. Three farewells in one week — the clinical room, the temple, the family table — and **two letters placed in Klára's hands** that will rebuild the first two rooms in Prague.

---

## BEAT 1 — THE LAST SESSION: DR. HARTWELL

- Klára's final session. Ten years of this room — the hypervigilance work, the disclosure at fifteen, the Kent visit debrief.
- Hartwell does not pretend this is a session like any other. Some of it is review; some of it is simply two people who did long work together, acknowledging it.
- **The letter:** Hartwell gives Klára an envelope — a letter to **Dr. Tereza Dvořáková of Prague**, the colleague she met at a conference and has trusted since. *"You don't have to use it the first week. You don't have to use it the first year. But when you're ready, she will already know who you are — as much as you want her to."* Records to follow only with Klára's own consent — she is eighteen; the signature is hers now, and Hartwell says so out loud, because the change matters.

## BEAT 2 — FRIDAY EVENING: THE TEMPLE, FOR THE LAST TIME (CANON)

- **Erev Shabbat.** Before sundown, the food goes on the grill and comes off again — everything cooked and ready, resting under foil, because tonight is done in the right order. Then **the whole household goes to the temple in Vaughan — Gamaliel and Klára's last time.** Kabbalat Shabbat: the service that welcomes the Sabbath, the candles, the singing — the same building Patti Anne or Aleksander has driven them to since they were eight.
- Who attends [proposal]: all of them — Patti Anne, Aleksander, the twins too. The family that delivered Klára to this building for ten years sits with her in it once more, the way they sat at the funeral Mass with Loewy in the back row: love crossing the aisle in both directions, one last time.
- Eleven years of pews, ending where they began: Klára and Gamaliel side by side — except everything is different now. She is grown. He is almost a convert. The building did its work.
- **The letter:** after the service, in his study, **Rabbi Loewy gives Klára the letter** — addressed to **Rabbi Eliáš Löwy, Prague**, his distant cousin. One name, two spellings; the branch that left and the branch that stayed. He tells them the family history plainly [how much detail — TBD]. *"I am not handing you to a stranger. I am sending you down my own line."* He gives it **to Klára** — not to Gamaliel. The student notices. The rabbi intends it: the letters of this leaving travel in the keeping of the girl who decides when doors open.
- To Gamaliel, last [candidate]: *"You asked me at thirteen if you could become this. The answer was always going to be Prague. I just didn't know I knew it."*

## BEAT 3 — FRIDAY NIGHT: THE BARBECUE — THE FAREWELL SE'UDAT SHABBAT (CANON)

- Home from temple, into the backyard: **the barbecue is the family's farewell Shabbat dinner — Se'udat Shabbat, Key-style.** Grilled food prepared before sundown and eaten after services (a warm-climate Shabbat custom the family arrives at honestly), at the picnic table where they have eaten a hundred summer dinners. The Vaughan backyard in the last of the light: Aleksander uncovering platters, the twins (6, loud, half-understanding) underfoot with the dogs.
- **The table is set for both inheritances:** Patti Anne has bought candles specially — and **Klára lights them** [she has done this at temple; whether she has ever done it at *this* house — and what it costs and gives her to do it at the family table at last — is the beat's quiet center]. **Gamaliel says Kiddush** [almost-convert, six years of Thursdays in his pronunciation; Loewy taught him this]. Two challot under a cloth beside the grilled corn — the braided bread and the barbecue sharing one table without apology. A Catholic family hosting Shabbat dinner for their daughter, one last time — eleven years of driving her to her faith, completed by serving it at their own table.
- The rest is the most ordinary possible goodbye — smoke, corn, burned fingers, the twins negotiating second desserts. No banquet, no speeches. The Key farewell is logistical love folded into ordinary life: Aleksander triple-checking the dogs' travel crates and Czech paperwork; Patti Anne packing food nobody needs for a flight that has meals.
- Patti Anne's goodbye to Klára happens here, in the kitchen, carrying things [TBD — but the shape: the woman who signed every form, who built both rooms and opened the door between them, releasing the permissions to the girl who is now grown. Something like: *"It's all yours to sign now. You'll sign it better than I did."* — she won't believe that; she'll say it anyway].
- **The echo (candidate):** after the twins are in bed, the four adults in the kitchen — and someone opens the freezer. **Mike's banana split, the farewell edition.** Melted, poured into glasses, drunk. *Zikhrono livrakha*, said now by all four [does Patti Anne say it in Hebrew? — a small, enormous beat, TBD].
- **What is NOT in this scene:** the airport. The saga can skip it. The farewell is the temple and the table, not the gate.

## SAGA CONNECTIONS

- The three rooms of Klára's childhood — clinical, temple, kitchen — each get their proper goodbye: Hartwell early in the week; then the Friday that holds the other two in the right order — **the last Kabbalat Shabbat at temple, then the barbecue as the farewell Se'udat Shabbat at home** — candles lit by Klára, Kiddush said by Gamaliel, challah beside the grilled corn: the household's two faiths at one table, eleven years of driving her to her faith completed by serving it at their own.
- The two letters enter Klára's keeping here (see the key profile, "Healing — The Two Rooms": she will contact no one until Prague is settled).
- The twins' last memory of the household intact: ages 6. The next time the whole unit assembles, it is Christmas across hemispheres.
