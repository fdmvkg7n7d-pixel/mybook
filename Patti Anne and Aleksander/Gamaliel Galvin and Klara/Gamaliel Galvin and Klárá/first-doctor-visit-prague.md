# SCENE OUTLINE: THE FIRST APPOINTMENT – THE FAMILY DOCTOR

## SCENE INFORMATION
- **Characters:** Gamaliel Galvin Key, Simonka Káta (9), Dr. Jelena (Family Pediatrician)
- **Location:** A familiar, warm pediatric office in Prague.
- **Date:** The first Monday after the Crete vacation (Summer, circa 2079).
- **Status:** Outline

---

## THE SETTING
- A familiar environment for Simonka. The walls have the same murals she’s seen since she was a toddler.
- The scent of the office—mild antiseptic and wooden toys—is grounding.
- Gamaliel is calm but focused, carrying the "medical folder" that all Key parents seem to inherit.

---

## SCENE ARC

### 1. The Waiting Room
- Simonka sits with Gamaliel. She isn't wearing a shirt under her light summer jacket, keeping with her comfort during the medical process. 
- They play a quiet game of "I Spy" to pass the time. Gamaliel’s hands are steady, though he is mentally rehearsing how to describe the lump.

### 2. Entering the Exam Room
- Dr. Jelena, who has known the girls since they were babies, greets them warmly. She notices the shift in the usual "back-from-vacation" energy.
- Gamaliel explains the discovery in Crete. "Simonka found a stone, Jelena. We checked it together in Vamos."

### 3. The Ask and the Examination
- Dr. Jelena follows the family's established protocol for bodily autonomy.
- **The Ask:** "Simonka, I’ve heard you found something new on your body. Would you be comfortable showing it to me and letting me feel it?"
- **The Response:** Simonka unzips her jacket. She is matter-of-fact. "It's right here. It feels like a pebble."
- **The Touch:** Dr. Jelena examines the mass. Her expression remains professional, but her movements become more precise, more focused. She spends a long time feeling the edges of the lump and checking the lymph nodes.

### 4. The Professional Pivot
- Dr. Jelena finishes the exam and gives Simonka a sticker or a small toy, a familiar ritual.
- She speaks to Gamaliel and Simonka together. "Simonka was very right to find this. It’s a very distinct little stone."
- She explains that because it's so distinct, she wants "the experts who specialize in stones" to take a look right away.

### 5. The Urgent Referral
- While Simonka looks at a book, Dr. Jelena speaks quietly with Gamaliel. 
- She makes a phone call immediately—not a standard referral, but an urgent one to the pediatric oncology department at the University Hospital.
- She gives Gamaliel the name: **Dr. Marek**. 

### 6. Leaving the Office
- As they walk out, Simonka feels a sense of accomplishment. She did the "right thing" by telling her parents and the doctor.
- Gamaliel feels the weight of the "urgent" label. He calls Klára in Cape Town as soon as they reach the car.

---

## CHARACTER NOTES
- **Dr. Jelena:** Compassionate and experienced. She knows the family well and respects their values regarding Simonka's agency.
- **Simonka (9):** Trusts Dr. Jelena. The familiarity of the office makes the scary reality feel like a manageable "check-up."
- **Gamaliel:** Appreciates the familiar setting but is acutely aware of the change in Dr. Jelena’s demeanor once she feels the lump.

---

## SAGA CONNECTIONS
- **Primary Care:** Highlights the importance of a trusted family doctor in the Key family’s medical network.
- **Trust:** Shows how the "Consent to Touch" started by the parents is mirrored by their chosen medical professionals.
