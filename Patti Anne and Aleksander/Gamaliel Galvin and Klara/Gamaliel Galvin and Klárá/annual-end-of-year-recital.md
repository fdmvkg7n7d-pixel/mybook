# Annual End-of-Year Recital – Prague Ballet Academy

## Canon Establishment
**Every year, the school puts on an end of year recital for the parents.**

This is a core tradition of the Prague ballet academy founded by Gamaliel Galvin Key and Klára Key.

## Details

### Timing
- Held every June, the week after the international school year ends
- Runs for three evenings (Junior, Intermediate, Senior divisions)
- Final night is the Senior Showcase — where Klárka and later Simonka's peers perform

### Purpose
- Not a competition — a family celebration
- Parents, grandparents (including Patti Anne and Aleksander when they visit from Canada), and academy donors attend
- Gamaliel and Klára personally greet every family at the door — continuing their "morning ritual" ethos into the evening

### Family Participation
- **Gamaliel** – backstage, stage manager, French/Italian/Czech translations for visiting families
- **Klára** – artistic director, final bow with students
- **Klárka Pavlînka** – performs every year from age 6 through 18 (her Grade 12 year, the Paris trip happens right before recital week)
- **Simonka Káta** – does NOT dance, but every year she works front-of-house: programs, ushering, psychology-observing the audience. This is where her "watching bodies" interest begins. She attends the recital with Gamaliel and Klára, sitting in the front row — maintaining the "see each other" family pattern even during performance season.
- **Morning After Tradition:** The day after the final recital, the whole family (including dogs Willow/Kleopatra) has a late breakfast together in the academy kitchen — the "recital morning" — reinforcing the "always see each other in the morning" rule that later justifies the Paris and Rome hotel room shares.

### Connection to Canon
- Explains why Klára couldn't go to Rome with Simonka (spring recital week conflicts)
- Explains why Gamaliel is comfortable sharing hotel rooms on school trips — the academy already normalizes families being together backstage, in dressing rooms, and at early-morning rehearsals
- Provides annual touchpoint for Simonka to observe performance psychology before Charles University
- Sets up future scene: Simonka's first year at Charles University, she skips the recital for exams — first time she breaks the "every year" pattern, causing quiet family tension
