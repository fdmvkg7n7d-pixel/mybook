# SCENE OUTLINE: THE NINETEENTH BIRTHDAY PARTY WITH GAMALIEL AND KLÁRA

## SCENE INFORMATION
- **Characters:** Simonka Káta (19), Gamaliel Galvin (father), Klára (mother), Klárka Pavlínka (older sister), Raphael (Klárka's partner), Ambroz (boyfriend), Patti Anne (grandmother), Aleksander (grandfather)
- **Location:** Gamaliel and Klára's Home, Prague.
- **Date:** The weekend of Simonka’s nineteenth birthday (Saturday afternoon/evening, after her return from the farm).
- **Status:** Outline

---

## THE SETTING
- The warm, bustling family home of Gamaliel Galvin and Klára in Prague. 
- The contrast is stark: Simonka has just returned from the sensory extremes of Alois’s farm (the barn, the 5,000-gallon water tank) and the suffocating "noise" of Ambroz’s jealous reclamation at her apartment. Now, she is in the bright, traditional space of her parents' home.
- Decorations, a cake, and a gathering of family to celebrate Simonka turning nineteen.

---

## SCENE ARC

### 1. The Family Gathering
- Gamaliel Galvin and Klára have thrown a lovely, traditional birthday party for their youngest daughter. They are proud of her independence and her studies in psychology.
- **Patti Anne and Aleksander** are in attendance. The grandparents bring a warm, multigenerational anchor to the room, happy to celebrate Simonka as she bridges into fuller adulthood.
- Simonka arrives, wearing long sleeves or clothes that carefully conceal any lingering physical evidence of her time at the farm or her rough morning with Ambroz. She relies heavily on her "dancer's guard" and her practiced ability to compartmentalize.

### 2. Ambroz’s Performance and The Sibling Promise
- Ambroz is present. Having "reclaimed" her that morning on her apartment floor, he is now playing the role of the devoted, perfect boyfriend in front of Gamaliel, Klára, Patti Anne, and Aleksander.
- He is overly attentive, standing too close, putting his arm around her. To the parents and grandparents, it might look like young love. To Simonka, it feels heavy, performative, and exhausting.
- **Klárka Pavlínka and Raphael** are watching him like hawks. They know that Ambroz has been hurting Simonka—they see the tension, the exhaustion, and the emotional toll his immature jealousy takes on her.
- Klárka and Raphael badly want to intervene. They want to yell at Ambroz, to drag him away from her, to expose him right there in the living room.
- However, they don't yell. They are bound by a promise: they promised Simonka Káta not to say anything. So they stand by, tight-lipped and furious, offering silent support to Simonka while forcing themselves to endure Ambroz's charade.

### 3. Gamaliel and Klára's Speeches
- The family gathers around, and Gamaliel Galvin and Klára both make heartfelt speeches.
- **Gamaliel's Speech:** He speaks to his daughter's intellect and quiet strength, expressing immense pride in her path in psychology. He talks about how she has grown and how deeply she is loved.
- **Klára's Speech:** Klára's words are perhaps softer but equally observant. With her dancer's eye, she might sense that Simonka carries an unseen weight, but she frames her toast around Simonka's resilience and grace.
- Gamaliel and Klára present her with gifts, completely unaware of the extreme weekend their daughter just experienced or the reality of the boyfriend standing beside her.

### 4. The Internal Contrast and the Rejected Apology
- While the party goes on around her, Simonka feels the profound chasm between her two worlds.
- She looks at her loving parents, her sister Klárka, and the "acceptable" boyfriend by her side. This is the life they expect her to lead.
- In her mind, she is still floating in the 5,000-gallon tank, experiencing the weightless sanctuary and intense stillness that only her hidden life provides. She realizes how successfully she is hiding her true self from her family.
- **The Apology:** During a brief moment when the family is distracted, Ambroz corners her near the kitchen or out on the balcony. His performative charm drops, and he tries to apologize for his aggressive, jealous behavior that morning at her apartment door. 
- "Simi, I'm sorry about earlier. I just missed you," he whispers, trying to grab her hand.
- **The Rejection:** Simonka doesn't want to listen to him. The apology feels just as "noisy" and manipulative as the jealousy. She pulls her hand away, keeping her dancer's guard up. "Not here, Ambroz. Do not do this here," she tells him coldly, refusing to grant him the absolution he is desperately seeking while in her parents' home.

### 5. The Birthday Cake
- Klára brings out the birthday cake. They sing to her.
- As Simonka blows out the candles, she feels the physical toll of her Osteogenesis Imperfecta and the events of the last 24 hours settling into her bones. 
- Her wish is silent and complex—a desire for a life where she doesn't have to constantly manage the "noise" and where her fragile body can feel safe without hiding.

## CHARACTER NOTES
- **Simonka Káta (19):** Deeply compartmentalized. She loves her parents and grandparents but feels alienated by the secret she carries and the burden of Ambroz's presence.
- **Gamaliel Galvin and Klára:** Loving, supportive parents throwing a typical family celebration, unaware of the extreme weekend their daughter just experienced. They deliver touching speeches to honor her.
- **Klárka Pavlínka and Raphael:** Furious at Ambroz for hurting Simonka but forced to remain silent to honor their promise to her. They act as her silent protectors.
- **Ambroz (19):** Playing the good boyfriend, masking the intense jealousy and conflict from earlier that morning.
- **Patti Anne and Aleksander:** Present as the proud, loving grandparents, adding to the layers of family presence that Simonka must perform in front of.

---

## SAGA CONNECTIONS
- **The Dual Life:** Beautifully illustrates the gap between Simonka's public, family-approved life and her private, hidden desires.
- **The Exhaustion Arc:** Further establishes how draining it is for Simonka to maintain the "Pact of Secrecy" while dealing with Ambroz's immaturity.
- **Family Dynamics:** Shows Gamaliel and Klára's enduring love for their daughter, setting up the emotional stakes for when her hidden life is eventually exposed to them.
