# SCENE OUTLINE: THE LAST DAY OF SCHOOL – JUNE 2079

## SCENE INFORMATION
- **Characters:** Simonka Káta (8), Klárka Pavlínka (10), Gamaliel Galvin Key, Klára
- **Location:** The Circular Staircase, Key-Dimitrov Apartment, Prague.
- **Date:** June, 2079 (The end of Grade 3 and Grade 4).
- **Status:** Outline

---

## THE SETTING
- A warm, golden morning in Prague. The school year is officially over.
- The apartment is filled with the energy of upcoming travel; bags are partially packed for the trip to Crete.
- This is the "Last Day" photo—the bookend to the one taken the previous September.

---

## SCENE ARC

### 1. The Morning Energy
- The girls are in their **regular clothes** today. The school has a tradition where students can wear their own clothes on the final day.
- They are dressed for the summer—light dresses or casual tops and shorts. There is a sense of looseness and freedom, and the girls are vibrating with the excitement of the "big summer" ahead.
- **The Context:** This is the last photo before the vacation in Crete. They are healthy, happy, and have no idea that the "stone" is coming.

### 2. The Staircase Tradition
- Gamaliel and Klára wait at the base of the circular staircase with the camera.
- **The Gallery Check:** Before they pose, the girls look at the photo from the start of the year (September 2078). They marvel at how much shorter they were and how their hair has grown.
- **The Pose:** Klárka (finishing Grade 4) and Simonka (finishing Grade 3) stand together. Simonka is 8; Klárka is 10. 
- They aren't trying to be "poised" today. They are giggling, leaning into each other. Gamaliel has to tell them to stay still for just one second.

### 3. The Click and the Secret
- The camera flashes. The photograph captures the sisters together.
- **The Hidden Discovery:** While she is posing, Simonka’s hand brush against her chest. She feels it—the "stone." It is hard, small, and unfamiliar.
- **The Internal Shift:** A wave of cold nervousness washes over her. She looks at her parents and her sister, but she doesn't say anything. She is too afraid of what it means, too afraid to break the "perfect" energy of the morning.
- The photograph captures her smile, but it is a "mask." This is the exact moment her intense secretiveness begins—the very first secret she keeps from her family.

### 4. Hanging the Record
- Gamaliel promises to have it printed and hung by the time they get back from vacation.
- He gestures to the wall. "Look at the line of you. You're getting higher and higher up the stairs."
- The staircase wall is becoming a map of their lives—from toddlers in Canada to students in Prague.

### 5. Into the Summer
- With the photo taken, the girls run to the door. They don't need to be walked to the gate with the same formality today.
- They head to their final half-day of school to say goodbye to teachers and friends.
- As they leave, Klára watches them from the doorway. "Next time we take a photo, they'll be in Grade 4 and 5," she muses to Gamaliel.

---

## CHARACTER NOTES
- **Simonka (8):** Pure, uninhibited joy. This is the "before" version of her character—the one who doesn't yet feel the need for intense secrecy.
- **Klárka (10):** Excited for the summer. She is still just a big sister, not yet a protector.
- **Gamaliel & Klára:** Proud of the girls' academic and dance progress. They are looking forward to the break before the "big push" of the Academy opening in September.

---

## SAGA CONNECTIONS
- **The "Before" Moment:** This scene serves as a poignant contrast to the medical journey that begins just weeks later in Crete.
- **The Staircase Tradition:** Establishes the visual record of their growth.
- **Timeline:** Pins the girls as Grade 3 and Grade 4 students during the discovery of the carcinoma.
