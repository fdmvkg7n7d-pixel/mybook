# SCENE OUTLINE: BACK TO THE BARRE – THE FIRST CLASS

## SCENE INFORMATION
- **Characters:** Simonka Káta (9), Klárka Pavlínka (10), Gamaliel Galvin Key, Klára
- **Location:** Mike Key Studio, Ballet Academy, Prague.
- **Date:** Six weeks post-surgery.
- **Status:** Outline

---

## THE SETTING
- The Mike Key Studio is the heart of the Academy. High ceilings, large mirrors, and the original portrait of Mike Key near the door.
- It is a "warm-up" hour before the official afternoon classes begin. The sun streams in, highlighting the dust motes in the air.

---

## SCENE ARC

### 1. The Preparation
- In the dressing room, Simonka is getting into her leotard. She moves slowly, still cautious of the right side of her chest.
- The scar is a thin, silver-pink line. It is her "badge." 
- Klára helps her with her hair, her touch exceptionally light. They don't talk about the scar, but they both see it in the mirror. It is a part of the "text" of Simonka's body now.

### 2. Entering the Studio
- Simonka walks into the Mike Key Studio. The smell of rosin and floor wax is like coming home.
- Gamaliel is at the piano, playing a soft, melodic scale. He looks up and smiles, his eyes tracking his daughter’s movement with a mix of professional scrutiny and fatherly pride.
- Klárka is already at the barre, stretching. She stops when Simonka enters and just watches her.

### 3. Finding the Barre
- Simonka takes her usual spot next to Klárka. 
- She places her hand on the wood. It feels solid. For weeks, her body has been "delicate." Here, at the barre, it is supposed to be strong.
- **The First Movement:** Gamaliel begins a simple *plié* exercise. Simonka moves. She feels the slight pull of the skin over her chest. It isn't pain, but it is a *reminder*. 

### 4. The Shift in Perspective
- As she watches her own reflection and Klárka’s beside her, Simonka finds herself distracted.
- Usually, she is focused on the turnout, the line of the leg, the height of the arch.
- Today, she is watching how the muscles in Klárka’s back react to the movement. She is thinking about how her own body "repaired" itself. 
- She realizes that the studio is a place of *mechanics* as much as *art*. She is more interested in the "why" of the movement than the "how."

### 5. The "Tighten" Hand
- Between exercises, Klárka reaches out and briefly squeezes Simonka’s hand on the barre. 
- No words are needed. It’s a check-in: *Are you okay? Is the stone-spot okay?*
- Simonka nods. She is okay. But she is also different.

### 6. Finishing the Set
- They finish the warm-up. Simonka is tired—more tired than she used to be—but she is upright.
- Gamaliel comes over and pulls both girls into a hug. "Good work, Simi. Welcome back."
- Klára watches from the doorway, her hand on the frame. The "restoration" is complete. The dancer has returned to her stage, even if she is already beginning to look toward a different one.

---

## CHARACTER NOTES
- **Simonka (9):** Re-learning her body. The studio feels the same, but her eyes are starting to see it analytically.
- **Klárka (10):** Hyper-aware of Simonka’s every breath. She is the physical shield for her sister’s recovery.
- **Gamaliel:** Using music to guide his daughter back to her strength.
- **Klára:** The silent witness to the resilience of the body.

---

## SAGA CONNECTIONS
- **Resilience:** Shows the physical side of recovery.
- **The "Text" of the Body:** Subtle foreshadowing of her interest in psychology without breaking the user's request to "save the third" for later.
- **Mike Key Studio:** Reinforces the studio as a sanctuary of family history.
