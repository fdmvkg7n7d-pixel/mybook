# Holic Tells GG About the Matthias Weekend Deal

## Scene Outline

## Placement

**When:** Immediately after the mansion-yard escalation in which Klárka Pavlînka appears on Holic's property, Matthias becomes angry at her intrusion, and Holic tries to please Matthias by offering **GG for the weekend**.

**Where:** Outside Holic's mansion — most likely on a private path around the grounds, away from the yard and away from Matthias's pets. The walk should feel like a controlled removal from the chaos: the shouting is behind them, the yard is behind them, and GG is being brought back into Holic's direct orbit.

**Who:** Holic Bartunek and Simonka Káta as GG.

**Witnesses:** None. Klárka and Raphael do **not** witness this walk. Matthias does not hear the whole conversation. This is a private Holic/GG scene, which means the family may later know about the weekend offer but not the fact that Holic gave GG a safeword opening before carrying it out.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene clarifies the ethical and emotional hinge of the Matthias-weekend offer.

Holic has tried to repair Matthias's anger by offering GG for the weekend. That makes GG transferable inside the owner/pet logic of the mansion. But Holic also takes GG aside and gives her a live chance to use her safewords before the deal becomes real.

The scene should hold both truths at once:

1. **Holic is treating GG as something he can offer to another owner.**
2. **Holic asks whether she wants to use her safewords now, and says he will not give her to Matthias if she does.**

Simonka's answer is the important canon beat:

> She looks up and tells him it is her job to be his pet.
>
> She tells him she does not need to say her safewords right now.

This does not make the moment simple. It makes it more complicated.

---

## Tone Guardrail

Keep the scene **non-explicit**. The focus is not the weekend's physical possibilities. The focus is the contract of power: offer, safeword, duty, obedience, and Simonka's belief that being Holic's pet includes being available for the consequences of his decisions.

---

# Scene Structure

## Beat 1 — Holic Removes GG From the Yard

- After Klárka's screaming and Raphael pulling her away, the yard does not settle immediately. The air still feels disturbed.
- Matthias is angry. His pets are present. Holic's authority has been publicly challenged by an outsider who entered as Simonka's sister.
- Holic does not discuss the matter with GG in front of everyone.
- He calls her to him and takes her on a walk around the grounds.
- The walk is calm on the surface. That calm is part of Holic's control.

**Key image:** GG walking beside or slightly behind Holic, the mansion behind them, the yard noise fading, the collar still at her throat.

---

## Beat 2 — Holic Names the Disruption

- Holic tells GG that Klárka's appearance has created a problem.
- He does not frame it as family trauma. He frames it as a breach of the property's order and a disruption of Matthias's visit.
- He may say that Matthias was insulted, angered, or inconvenienced.
- He makes clear that he has offered Matthias something to restore order.

Possible line:

> Matthias is angry. Your sister made herself part of a scene she had no right to enter.

Or:

> His evening was disrupted. I have made him an offer.

---

## Beat 3 — Holic Tells Her the Deal

- Holic tells GG directly: he has offered her to Matthias for the weekend.
- He does not hide it from her or let her discover it when Matthias claims her.
- The line should be plain and controlled.

Possible line:

> I offered him your weekend.

Or:

> I told Matthias he may have you for the weekend, if the terms are accepted.

Or, more bluntly:

> I offered you to him for the weekend.

- The wording matters. Holic's language should make the transferability clear without making the prose sensational.
- GG hears the deal as GG: not as a daughter, not as a sister, not as a Charles University student, but as Holic's pet receiving information about where she may be placed.

---

## Beat 4 — The Safeword Opening

- Holic stops walking or slows enough that GG knows this is the actual question.
- He reminds her that her safewords still exist here.
- He makes the consequence explicit: if she says them now, he will not give her to Matthias for the weekend.

Possible line:

> If you want to say your safewords right now, say them.

Then:

> If you say them, I will not give you to him for the weekend.

Or:

> If either word is in your mouth, the offer ends here.

- This should not be framed as a trick. Holic means it. If GG says yellow or red, the weekend handoff stops or is at least withdrawn until renegotiated.
- But the power imbalance remains: Holic has already offered her, and now she is being asked to refuse her owner after he has made a social promise to another owner.

**Important craft note:** The scene should not pretend this is clean consent. It is a real opening inside a highly pressurized structure.

---

## Beat 5 — Simonka Looks Up

- GG looks up at Holic.
- This is a small but important physical act. Looking up breaks the pure lowered-gaze obedience for one second and lets Simonka's adult self appear through GG.
- She is not confused about what he is asking.
- She understands that she can stop this, or at least that Holic is telling her she can.

**Key image:** Simonka's face lifting toward Holic, the collar visible, the path quiet around them.

---

## Beat 6 — Her Answer: “It Is My Job To Be Your Pet”

Simonka/GG answers with the line that defines the scene.

Possible wording:

> It is my job to be your pet.

Or:

> My job is to be your pet.

Then she clarifies:

> If this is what my owner needs from me, then I do it.

This sentence should land as devotion and danger at the same time.

She is not saying she wants Matthias in a simple personal way. She is saying that being Holic's pet means accepting what Holic asks of his pet, including being used to repair his social/owner obligation.

---

## Beat 7 — “I Don't Need My Safewords Right Now”

- Holic gives her room to say yellow or red.
- GG does not.
- She tells him she does not need to say her safewords right now.

Possible line:

> I don't need to say them right now.

Or:

> I don't need my safewords right now.

Or, with repetition for emphasis:

> Not right now. I don't need them right now.

- The phrase **right now** matters. It leaves open that she may need them later.
- Holic should hear that distinction.
- The scene should not erase the future possibility of yellow/red during the Matthias weekend.

---

## Beat 8 — Holic Confirms the Boundary

- Holic does not simply accept and move on.
- He confirms the boundary once more, because the point of the scene is that safewords remain active even when GG chooses obedience.

Possible line:

> If that changes, you use them.

Or:

> Your job is not to be silent when you need your words.

Or:

> Being my pet does not cancel yellow or red.

This can echo earlier safeword scenes where Holic insists that safewords are not a failure.

---

## Beat 9 — The Decision Settles

- GG accepts the weekend as part of her role.
- Holic accepts her answer.
- He may call her **Good Girl**, but the praise should feel weighted, not easy.
- The walk continues or turns back toward the mansion.
- The grounds feel different now: the offer is no longer only something Holic said to Matthias. It is something GG has heard, understood, and chosen not to stop.

**Possible closing image:** They turn back toward the house, and GG walks with him. The weekend is not yet shown. It has become real because she did not say the words that would have stopped it.

---

### Holic

> If you want to say your safewords right now, say them.

> If you say them, I will not give you to him.

> Being my pet does not cancel yellow or red.

> If that changes, you use your words.

### Simonka / GG

> It is my job to be your pet.

> If this is what you need from me, I do it.

> I don't need to say them right now.

> Not right now.

> I know my words. I don't need them now.

---

# What This Scene Establishes

1. **The weekend offer becomes explicit to GG.** Holic does not make the deal entirely over her head; he tells her directly.

2. **Holic offers a safeword exit before the handoff.** He tells GG that if she says her safewords now, he will not give her to Matthias for the weekend.

3. **Simonka does not use her safewords.** She says it is her job to be Holic's pet and that she does not need her safewords right now.

4. **The word “job” becomes a major Simonka/GG marker.** Simonka understands pethood not only as desire or surrender, but as duty. This is emotionally powerful and ethically dangerous.

5. **The scene preserves complexity.** GG's choice not to safeword is real, but it happens under pressure: Holic has already made the offer, Matthias is angry, and GG wants to be a good pet.

6. **The future remains open.** “Right now” means she may still use yellow or red later, during the weekend itself.
