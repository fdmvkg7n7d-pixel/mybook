# Patti Anne and Aleksander Depart Saudi Arabia for Prague — Scene Outline

## Placement

**When:** The end of Patti Anne and Aleksander's visit to Saudi Arabia. This marks the transition from the first, most culturally complex leg of their trip to their second stop in Europe.
**Where:** The gates of Palmer Peter's compound in Saudi Arabia, then the transition to the airport.
**Context:** Patti Anne and Aleksander have survived the overwhelming reality of Palmer Peter's plural household. They have managed their internal judgment, embraced Addyson and the seven children, and witnessed the supportive dynamic of the five women. Now, they must say goodbye to Palmer Peter's family and mentally prepare for Gamaliel and Klára's ballet academy in Prague.

---

## Characters

- **Patti Anne Key:** The family anchor. She is leaving Saudi Arabia exhausted but relieved that her strategy—focusing entirely on the grandchildren—worked. Now, she is pivoting her mental focus toward her eldest son, Gamaliel, in Europe.
- **Aleksander:** Steady, quiet, protective. He has kept the peace in the compound, mostly through watchful silence. 
- **Palmer Peter:** The patriarch. He is deeply grateful that his parents visited and managed not to tear his household apart with judgment. 
- **Addyson:** The veteran mother. She has established a quiet, mutual respect with Patti Anne. 
- **The Other Four Women & Seven Children:** Gathering to say a formal, compound-wide goodbye.

---

## Scene Outline

### 1. The Gathering at the Gates
- The departure is treated as a major household event. The sheer logistics of saying goodbye to five women and seven children take time.
- The family gathers near the front gates of the compound. The intense Saudi heat is a reminder of the physical and cultural environment Patti Anne is leaving behind.
- Aleksander loads their bags into the waiting car. He nods to Palmer Peter, a quiet acknowledgment between men that the visit was handled with respect. 

### 2. The Goodbye to the Women
- Patti Anne turns to the women. The formality that marked their arrival has been replaced by a functional, if somewhat cautious, domestic familiarity. 
- She hugs Dakota and Madison, thanking them for their help during the stay (referencing the dinner spill). 
- She places a hand on the pregnant bellies of Aurora and Theadora, wishing them safe deliveries. It still fundamentally breaks her Catholic worldview, but she does it because these are her future grandchildren. 
- Finally, she turns to Addyson. The two matriarchs look at each other. Patti Anne hugs her tightly. "Take care of those babies, Addyson. And take care of yourself."
- Addyson nods. "We will. Have a safe flight, Patti Anne."

### 3. Palmer Peter's Relief
- Palmer Peter walks his parents to the car door. The tension he carried on the day they arrived—the fear of rejection—is completely gone.
- He hugs his mother. He holds on for a long time. 
- "Thank you for coming," he whispers into her shoulder. "Thank you for being kind to them."
- Patti Anne pulls back and pats his cheek. "You're my son. I love you. Now, make sure you call your sister in Perth. She worries about you."
- Palmer Peter smiles, the mention of Salena Sarai grounding him. "I'll call her."

### 4. The Drive to the Airport
- Patti Anne and Aleksander sit in the back of the car as it pulls away from the compound. 
- For the first few minutes, neither speaks. The transition out of the plural household requires a mental decompression. 
- Finally, Aleksander exhales. "We did it."
- Patti Anne leans her head back against the seat, closing her eyes. "We did. I love those children, Aleksander. But Lord, am I glad to be leaving that house."

### 5. Bracing for Prague
- The silence stretches as the desert rolls by outside the window. 
- Aleksander reaches over and takes Patti Anne's hand. "Next stop, Prague."
- Patti Anne opens her eyes. The mental pivot begins. She is leaving Palmer Peter's Middle Eastern compound and preparing for Gamaliel's European ballet academy. 
- "Prague," she echoes. "Let's hope Gamaliel's house is a little quieter."
- (This provides a touch of dramatic irony, as the reader knows the Prague visit will involve Holic's mansion and intense emotional breakdowns).

---

## Tone & Style Notes

- **The Exhale:** The overriding emotion of the scene for the grandparents is relief. They played the game successfully, secured their relationship with the grandchildren, and are now escaping the theological pressure cooker.
- **Palmer Peter's Gratitude:** His thank-you to his mother is genuine and vulnerable. He knows what she had to suppress to be kind to his wives, and he loves her for it.
- **The Pivot:** The scene clearly demarcates the end of the Saudi Arabia leg and builds anticipation for the emotional complexity waiting in Prague.

---

## What This Scene Establishes

1. **The Success of the Strategy:** Patti Anne and Aleksander successfully navigated the plural household without causing a rift, prioritizing the family bond over their personal judgment.
2. **Mutual Respect with Addyson:** The goodbye cements Addyson as the primary female figure in the compound in Patti Anne's eyes.
3. **The Timeline Transition:** It explicitly moves the narrative from Saudi Arabia (Leg 1) to Prague (Leg 2).
