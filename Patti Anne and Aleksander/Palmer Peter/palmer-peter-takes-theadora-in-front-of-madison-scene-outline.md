# PALMER PETER TAKES THEADORA IN FRONT OF MADISON, CATRIN, AND RHIAN

## Scene Outline — Beats, Dialogue Cues, and Thematic Notes

> **Placement:** After the kitchen scene, on a later evening. Palmer Peter comes for Theadora in front of the twins and Madison. This is not the first time the twins have seen the rotation in action, but it is a different configuration — Madison is present, watching, while Theadora is taken.

> **Content & craft warning.** This scene depicts the same household dynamic as the kitchen scene — Palmer Peter taking one of his wives in front of witnesses — but with a different configuration of people in the room. The prose must hold the same discipline: no glamour, no sensationalism, no interiority for Palmer Peter. Render the moment through Catrin's eyes, as the saga's established POV for the twins' experience.

**POV:** **Catrin.** *(Same POV continuity as the kitchen scene. The reader has now spent the entire arc behind Catrin's eyes. **Tone:** Held. Quiet. The familiarity of the rotation makes it somehow worse, not better. The twins have now seen this before — they know what it means when Palmer Peter enters and names one of the women — and the knowledge is its own burden. **The prose must let the reader feel that the twins are no longer seeing this for the first time. They have been here. They know the shape. And knowing the shape does not make it easier.**

**Location:** The compound — somewhere in the shared spaces. The central living room, or the kitchen, or the corridor between them. The exact location is TBD; what matters is that the scene is in the household's communal space, where the twins have access and where the rotation plays out visibly.

**Time in saga:** Some weeks after the kitchen scene. The twins are no longer new to the compound. They have settled into their residence. They know the women's names. They know the rotation. But they have not yet — until this evening — seen Palmer Peter take Theadora in front of Madison.

**The configuration:** Palmer Peter enters. He is here for Theadora. Madison is present. Catrin is present. Rhian is present. Three witnesses: two who were taken, one who was not. The room holds them all.

## THE BEATS

### Beat 1 — The Entry

Palmer Peter enters the space. He does not greet anyone. He does not look surprised to see them. He walks to Theadora.

He addresses her by name. Not a question. A statement.

> *"Theadora."*

Or:

> *"Theadora. Come."*

The same four words, or close to them. The summons that is not a request.

**The room registers it.** The prose must hold the reactions of the three witnesses:

- **Madison.** She is present. She watches. She has watched this before — not Theadora, perhaps, but others. She does not look away. She does not intervene. She is the partner who is neither wife nor stranger, the one who knows the cost of going and the cost of staying. **Her watching is its own register.**

- **Catrin.** She has seen this before. The kitchen scene — Aurora. This is not the first time. But it is Theadora, one of the wives, and the wives are different from the partners in the household's geometry. The prose must let Catrin feel the difference without spelling it out.

- **Rhian.** Beside Catrin. Silent. She has learned not to look at the floor. She has learned to watch. The watching is its own discipline.

### Beat 2 — Theadora's Response

Theadora does not refuse. She has done this before — many times, enough that her body knows the shape of it. The rising is practised.

But there is a half-second of stillness before she rises. Not defiance. Not hesitation. The half-second a person takes when they have been spoken to in a register they did not expect. Theadora chooses, in the half-second, that she will not — not here, not in front of the twins, not in front of Madison, not over two words.

She rises.

She does not take Palmer Peter's hand. He does not extend it. He inclines his head — a single small acknowledgement, which after the bareness of the summons reads almost worse than no acknowledgement would have — and steps aside to let her pass.

### Beat 3 — The Going

Their footsteps cross the space together, and turn down his corridor. The corridor to his residence.

**Madison watches them go.** She does not look away. She does not look at the twins. She looks at Theadora going, at Palmer Peter taking, at the machinery of the household turning in front of witnesses who now include her.

**Catrin watches too.** She has learned not to look at the floor. She looks at Madison looking at Theadora going, and the doubling of the gaze is its own thing. *Two women watching one woman go.* The prose must hold this without interpreting it.

**Rhian watches.** She does not speak. She has learned that silence is the only honest response to what she cannot change.

The door closes. It is not loud. It is exactly the sound of a door closing.

### Beat 4 — The Three Left

The room contains, now: Catrin, Rhian, and Madison. Three women who have not been taken tonight.

**Madison is the senior presence.** She has been in the household longer than the twins. She knows the rotation. She knows Theadora. She knows Palmer Peter. She has watched this happen before — to Aurora, to Theadora, to herself, to the others. **Her presence in the room, after Theadora's departure, is its own statement.**

**Catrin and Rhian wait.** They have learned to wait. Waiting is the household's primary discipline.

**Madison speaks, or does not speak.** The outline leaves this open. If she speaks, it is brief. If she does not speak, the silence is its own communication. The prose must hold both possibilities.

## WHAT THE WITNESSES REGISTER

**Madison sees:**
- Theadora being taken without the asking-shape.
- Two new women watching her household's machinery.
- The difference between what she has accepted and what the twins have not yet accepted.

**Catrin sees:**
- Madison watching Theadora go.
- The doubling of the gaze — two women watching one woman go.
- The fact that this is not the first time, and will not be the last.
- The difference between a wife being taken and a partner being taken. The wives have a different register in the household, and Theadora's going carries a weight that Aurora's going did not.

**Rhian sees:**
- The same things Catrin sees, but processed differently. Rhian is the one who asks the questions, the one who pushes against the architecture. This time, she does not ask. She watches. The watching is its own kind of questioning.

## KEY DETAILS TO INCLUDE

- **The summons is the same.** *"Theadora. Come. My room."* or *"Theadora."* The plainness is the cruelty. The prose must not vary the summons from the kitchen scene's pattern. The sameness is the point.

- **Madison's watching is central.** She is the one who is present but not being taken. The partner who watches the wife go. The prose must let her watching be its own beat — not commentary, not intervention, just watching.

- **The twins' familiarity.** They have seen this before. The kitchen scene was the first time; this is not. The prose must let the reader feel that the twins know the shape of the rotation now, and that knowing the shape does not make it easier. If anything, it makes it harder.

- **No one intervenes.** Not Madison. Not Catrin. Not Rhian. The intervention not made is the room's hardest restraint.

- **Theadora does not refuse.** She has done this before. Her body knows the shape of it. The rising is practised. The prose must not dramatize her going. It is what it is.

## TONE GUARDRAILS

- **No exoticism.** Same as the kitchen scene. The compound is a compound. The rotation is the rotation. The prose does not dress this in Orientalist clothing.

- **No glamour.** Palmer Peter taking Theadora in front of witnesses is not a scene about power as aesthetic. It is a scene about power as fact. Render it as fact.

- **No interiority for Palmer Peter.** Same as the kitchen scene. He is the agent of the rotation; the prose stays with the witnesses.

- **No interiority for Theadora during the going.** Same as the kitchen scene. Whatever Theadora thinks as she rises and goes is not narrated. Catrin can see what she sees — and that is all the reader gets.

- **First impressions are no longer first.** The twins are no longer new. They know the women. They know the rotation. They know the door that closes. The prose must let this familiarity register without losing the shock of the thing. *The shock is not that it happens. The shock is that it keeps happening.*
