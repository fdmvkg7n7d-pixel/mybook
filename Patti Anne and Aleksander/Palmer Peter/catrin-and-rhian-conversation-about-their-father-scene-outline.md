# CATRIN AND RHIAN — CONVERSATION ABOUT THEIR STUPID FATHER

## Scene Outline

**POV:** Catrin (with Rhian driving most of the dialogue)

**Tone:** Raw, quiet, exhausted. No performance. The first real conversation between the twins since their father sold them. The register is flat, honest, and occasionally brutal. This is not catharsis. It is two sisters naming what happened to them.

**Location:** The small neutral room where they made (or did not make) the first call to their mother. Or their shared bedroom. Either works. The important thing is that they are alone — no staff, no Palmer Peter, no one else.

**Time:** A few days after the morning-after scene. Not the same day. They have had time to absorb the terms Palmer Peter laid out. The first call to their mother has already happened (or is about to happen). The weight of what their father did has settled.

---

## CANON ESTABLISHED BY THIS SCENE

- This is the first time Catrin and Rhian speak to each other at length, alone, about what their father did.
- Rhian is the one who names it first and most directly.
- Catrin is still processing. She is not yet ready to say the words Rhian says.
- The conversation does not resolve anything. It simply establishes that the two of them now live in the knowledge of what their father did, and that this knowledge belongs to them together.
- Neither twin defends Huw. Neither twin suggests he was desperate or didn’t mean it. The conversation treats what he did as what it was.

---

## BEAT 1 — THE SETUP

*They have not spoken like this since before the *majlis*.*

- They are in a room with the door closed. For the first time since they arrived, no one is waiting outside. The woman in the corridor has withdrawn. Palmer Peter is elsewhere in the compound.
- Rhian is sitting on the floor again, back against the wall, the way she has started to sit when she needs to think. Catrin is on the edge of the low bed.
- Neither has spoken for several minutes. The silence is not uncomfortable. It is the silence of two people who have been waiting for the other to begin.

---

## BEAT 2 — RHIAN NAMES IT

*Rhian is the one who says it first.*

- She does not raise her voice. She does not cry. She simply says, in the same flat register she used when she asked Palmer Peter about their mother:

> “He was always going to do it.”

- Catrin does not answer right away. She has known this was true since the night it happened, but hearing her sister say it out loud makes it real in a different way.
- Rhian continues, still without looking at Catrin:

> “He was always going to sell us. The only question was who he was going to sell us to.”

---

## BEAT 3 — CATRIN’S RESPONSE

*Catrin is not ready to be as direct.*

- She says, quietly:

> “He didn’t even look at us.”

- This is the sentence she has been carrying since the *majlis*. It is the one that hurts the most. Not the selling itself — the fact that he could not meet their eyes while he did it.
- Rhian nods once. She does not disagree. She says:

> “He couldn’t. If he looked at us he would have had to stop.”

---

## BEAT 4 — THE STUPID FATHER

*This is the heart of the scene.*

- Rhian says the thing neither of them has said yet:

> “Our father is stupid.”

- She says it without heat. It is not an insult. It is a diagnosis. She has done the numbers on their father the same way she did the numbers on his firm, and the conclusion is the same: he is not clever enough to be anything other than what he is.
- Catrin flinches at the word, but only slightly. She has been protecting the idea of her father in a corner of her mind. Rhian has just closed that corner.
- Rhian keeps going, still calm:

> “He thought he could borrow more to cover what he already owed. He thought the second investment would save him. He thought Palmer Peter would give him time. He thought he could offer us and Palmer Peter would say no. He thought a lot of things that were not true.”

- She stops. She does not need to say the last part out loud. They both know it.

> “He thought he could sell his daughters and still be our father.”

---

## BEAT 5 — CATRIN’S QUESTION

*Catrin asks the question she has been afraid to ask.*

- She says, after a long silence:

> “Do you think he’s sorry?”

- Rhian does not answer immediately. When she does, her voice is even flatter than before:

> “I don’t care if he is.”

- Catrin looks at her sister. Rhian meets her eyes for the first time in the conversation.

> “I don’t care if he cries every night for the rest of his life. I don’t care if he kills himself. I don’t care if he tries to find us. I don’t care what he feels. He sold us. That is all he is now.”

---

## BEAT 6 — THE SILENCE AFTER

*They sit with it.*

- Neither speaks for a long time after that.
- Catrin realizes, sitting there, that her sister has already made a decision she herself has not yet made: Rhian has stopped being their father’s daughter. Catrin is still in the process of stopping.
- The realization does not make her feel closer to Rhian or further from her. It simply is.
- Eventually Rhian says, very quietly:

> “We are not going home.”

- Catrin does not argue. She has known this since the morning Palmer Peter told them their passports were in the house and would not be returned.
- Rhian adds:

> “We are not going to be his daughters anymore.”

- This time Catrin answers.

> “No. We’re not.”

---

## BEAT 7 — CLOSING IMAGE

*They do not hug. They do not cry together. They simply sit in the same room with the same knowledge.*

- Rhian stays on the floor. Catrin stays on the edge of the bed.
- The light moves across the floor the way it did on the first morning.
- Somewhere in the compound, the *adhān* sounds again. Neither of them reacts to it.
- They are two sisters who have just finished naming the man who sold them. The naming does not change what happened. It only makes it theirs.

---

## WHAT THIS SCENE ADDS TO CANON

1. **The twins’ private processing** — This is the first time they speak to each other, alone, about what their father did.
2. **Rhian’s clarity** — She is further along in cutting emotional ties with Huw than Catrin is.
3. **Catrin’s slower grief** — She is still carrying some version of “our father” that Rhian has already discarded.
4. **The shared knowledge** — From this scene forward, the fact that their father sold them is something they carry together, not separately.
5. **No reconciliation with Huw** — This scene makes it clear that neither twin is interested in forgiving or understanding their father. That door is closed.

---

*This scene is quiet, necessary, and brutal. It belongs early in the Catrin/Rhian arc.*
