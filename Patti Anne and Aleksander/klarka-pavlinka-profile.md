## NAME MEANING

- **Klárka** — A Czech/Slovak diminutive of *Klára*, meaning "clear" or "bright." A direct tribute to her mother. In a family where Klára was given a new last name (Key) and a new life, her daughter carries her first name forward — not as a replacement, but as an echo. Klárka is not Klára. She is the next verse of the same song.
- **Pavlînka** — A Czech/Slovak feminine diminutive of *Pavel* (Paul), meaning "small" or "humble." The combination — clear and small — suggests someone who sees things plainly without needing to be loud about it.

## FAMILY CONTEXT

### Parents' Story
Klárka's parents met at Victoria Ballet Academy when they were five years old. They were classmates and training partners before they were foster siblings. When her father (Gamaliel) was eight years old, he saved her mother (Klára) from a dangerous situation. Klára became Gamaliel's foster sister in Patti Anne's household. They began a secret romantic relationship at fifteen, were caught kissing by her great-grandfather Mike Key — who protected them — and planned their future ballet academy in Prague together. After Mike's death, Terri Anne gave them twenty-five million dollars to start the school.

Klárka was born into this legacy: a family built on rescue, secrecy, ballet, and a grandmother's twenty-five-million-dollar act of faith.

### The Prague Academy
Klárka grows up in Prague, where her parents run their own ballet academy. The first studio in the school is named **Mike Key Studio**, after her great-grandfather. She is likely a student there from a young age — not because she is forced, but because she has grown up in the studios. The barre is as familiar to her as a kitchen table.

### The Dogs
Klárka grows up with Kleopatra (Willow's daughter) and whatever dogs follow. The household in Prague has at least one Golden Retriever at all times — it is her mother's one non-negotiable.
