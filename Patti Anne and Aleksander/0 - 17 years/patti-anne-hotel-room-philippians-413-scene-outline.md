# SCENE OUTLINE: PHILIPPIANS 4:13 — PATTI ANNE'S HOTEL ROOM MORNING

**Characters:**
- **Patti Anne Key** (~9½ years old)
- **Mike Key** — her father

**Setting:** A hotel room in Vancouver. Early morning, the day of Patti Anne's rhythmic gymnastics competition. The bathroom is warm and steamy — a deep hotel bathtub filled halfway, the water still and waiting. Her competition leotard hangs on the back of the bathroom door. Her apparatus bag — with her ribbon and hoop — sits by the door in the main room.

**Timeframe:** The morning of the competition, before warm-ups. This fits within the existing Vancouver competition scene outline, between the arrival in Vancouver and the warm-up.

---

## THE SCENE

### Beat 1 — The hotel room at dawn

- Patti Anne wakes before her alarm. She has been sleeping in one of the two beds; Mike has the other. The room is quiet except for the faint hum of the hotel heater.
- She lies still for a moment, looking at the ceiling. She can feel the competition waiting for her — a pressure in her chest, a lightness in her hands.
- She does not wake Mike. She slides out of bed quietly, padding across the carpet in her bare feet.

### Beat 2 — The Bible on the nightstand

- On the nightstand beside her bed is the small Bible she brought from home. It is not the large family Bible from the kitchen table — this one is hers, a gift from Terri Anne, small enough to fit in her competition bag.
- She reaches for it. The leather cover is soft, broken in from use.
- She holds it in both hands as she slips into the bathroom and closes the door without clicking the latch — leaving it open just a crack, so the light from the room spills in.

### Beat 3 — The bathtub

- She turns on the tap. The water runs hot, filling the deep hotel tub. Steam rises, fogging the mirror, softening the edges of the room.
- She sets her Bible on the closed toilet lid, within reach. She climbs into the tub carefully, the hot water receiving her, covering her knees, her stomach, her shoulders.
- She sits there for a moment, letting the heat settle her nerves. The water is still. The room is quiet. The competition is somewhere outside this door, but in here there is only warmth and waiting.

### Beat 4 — Mike wakes

- In the bedroom, Mike stirs. The bed beside him is empty. He sits up, looks around — then notices the bathroom door cracked open, the light on, the sound of water still settling.
- He swings his legs out of bed. He does not call out. He walks to the bathroom door and stops. He can see her through the crack — her head above the water, her small Bible open in her wet hands.
- He does not push the door open further. He leans against the doorframe and waits.

### Beat 5 — The verse in the steam

- Patti Anne does not know he is there. She is focused on the page, her thumb holding the ribbon marker — Philippians 4. She has turned to this page every night since she chose the verse. She has been practicing it the way she practices her ribbon routine: over and over, until it lives in her muscles.
- Her voice is soft, a whisper in the steam:
  > *"I can do all things through Christ which strengtheneth me."*
- She says it again. Slower. Letting the words settle into her chest the way the hot water settles into her muscles.
- Then she hears Mike's voice, low and gentle from the doorway: *"Say it again."*

### Beat 6 — Father and daughter

- Patti Anne looks up. Mike is standing in the doorway, still in his sleep clothes, hair rumpled, leaning against the frame. He is not looking at her like she is in a bathtub. He is looking at her like she is something precious.
- She blushes — just a little — but she does not hide. She holds his gaze and reads it a third time, louder this time, her voice carrying through the steam:
  > *"I can do all things through Christ which strengtheneth me."*
- Mike nods. He says, softly: *"I believe that. And I believe you."*

### Beat 7 — The moment between them

- Patti Anne closes the Bible but keeps her hands on it, resting on the surface of the water.
- She says, almost to herself: *"I chose it because it doesn't say I have to win. It just says I can do it. With Him."*
- Mike hears the distinction she is making — at nine years old, she already understands that the verse is not about trophies. It is about presence. It is about who is with her.
- He says: *"That's the right verse, Patti Anne. That's the one."*
- She smiles — small, relieved. She sets the Bible on the edge of the tub, safe from the water.

### Beat 8 — The quiet before the day

- They do not rush. Mike stays in the doorway. Patti Anne stays in the water. The steam curls between them.
- After a long, peaceful silence, Mike says: *"Breakfast in forty-five minutes. Take your time."*
- He steps away from the doorframe. Before he disappears, he adds, quieter: *"I'm proud of you. Not because of the competition. Because you know where your strength comes from."*
- The door clicks softly as he pulls it mostly closed, giving her privacy.

### Beat 9 — Getting ready

- Patti Anne stays in the tub for another minute, alone. She looks at her Bible on the edge, at the steam on the mirror, at her own reflection blurred and soft.
- She whispers one more time, just to herself: *"I can do all things."*
- Then she pulls the plug, steps out, and reaches for her leotard.

---

## THEMATIC RESONANCE

- **The seed planted and blooming:** In the homeschooling Bible scene, Patti Anne asked to learn a Psalm. Here, she has grown into a girl who carries her own Bible, chooses her own verse, and draws strength from it before a competition.
- **The verse chosen with understanding:** She did not pick Philippians 4:13 because it sounds powerful. She picked it because it separates *doing* from *winning* — a remarkably mature distinction for a nine-year-old.
- **The bathtub as sanctuary:** Warm water, steam, privacy. She is not performing faith for anyone. She is speaking scripture into her own chest, preparing her own heart.
- **Mike's restraint:** He does not lecture, does not interrupt, does not make the moment about him. He stands in the doorway and listens. He lets her have her own encounter with God, and simply witnesses it.
- **Patti Anne being the child:** For once, she is not the oldest sister managing things. She is the daughter being seen, being held, being believed in — even in a bathtub with steam in her hair.
