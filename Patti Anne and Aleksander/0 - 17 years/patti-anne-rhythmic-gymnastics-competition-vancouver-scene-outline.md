# Patti Anne’s Rhythmic Gymnastics Competition in Vancouver — Scene Outline

## Purpose of This File
This is a planning / scene-outline file for **Patti Anne Key’s rhythmic gymnastics competition in Vancouver**.

The competition takes place on the **next Saturday after Rachel Darcy’s first ballet recital at Mohawk College**. **Mike Key** takes Patti Anne to Vancouver for the competition.

## Core Premise
After taking Jessica Isabella to her first gymnastics competition in Ottawa, and then taking Rachel Darcy to her first ballet recital at Mohawk College the following Saturday, Mike gives Patti Anne her own performance-and-travel milestone.

The next Saturday, Mike takes Patti Anne to **Vancouver** for her **rhythmic gymnastics competition**. While they are in Vancouver, Mike also takes Patti Anne to **Science World**, giving her a child-friendly museum outing connected to motion, balance, gravity, and the way bodies and objects move through space.

This creates a three-week father-daughter sequence:
- Jessica Isabella — first gymnastics competition in Ottawa.
- Rachel Darcy — first ballet recital at Mohawk College.
- Patti Anne — rhythmic gymnastics competition in Vancouver.

Mike is making sure each daughter has her own moment, her own trip, and her own memory with him. **Terri Anne stays home at the Carlisle mansion with the rest of their children, including Kiley Rose, who is two years old and too young to go to these competitions.**

## Suggested Timeline Placement
- Patti Anne is approximately **nine and a half years old**, because she is thirty months older than Jessica Isabella and Rachel Darcy, who are seven during their first-performance milestones.
- This occurs the **next Saturday after** Rachel Darcy’s first ballet recital at Mohawk College.
- This occurs before Jessica Isabella and Emma meet at Burlington Gymnastics Club when Jessica Isabella is ten.
- This occurs before the kitchen accident involving Kiley Rose.

## Main Characters
- **Patti Anne Key** — eldest Key sister; approximately nine and a half; practicing rhythmic gymnastics; already carries some oldest-child seriousness, but this trip gives her space to be the child being taken care of.
- **Mike Key** — Patti Anne’s social/legal father; steady, proud, attentive, determined that the oldest daughter is not only the helper or witness but also the child who gets her own turn.
- **Terri Anne Williams-Key** — stays home at the Carlisle mansion with the rest of the children, including two-year-old Kiley Rose, because Kiley is too young to go to the competitions.

## Location
- **Vancouver, British Columbia** — rhythmic gymnastics competition.
- **Science World** — Vancouver museum outing after the competition, connecting Patti Anne’s rhythmic gymnastics to motion, balance, gravity, and hands-on discovery.
- **Carlisle mansion** — home base before and after the trip.

## Core Emotional Question
Can Patti Anne, the oldest sister, let herself be the daughter who is supported, watched, and celebrated?

- Patti Anne notices that Mike came all this way for her.

- The music begins.

- The routine requires her to control both her body and the apparatus.
- A small mistake is possible — a ribbon tangle, a slightly late catch, a step that lands too hard — but she keeps going.

### 8. Mike Watching Patti Anne
- Mike watches carefully.
- He understands that Patti Anne’s performance is not the same as Jessica Isabella’s gymnastics or Rachel Darcy’s ballet.
- Patti Anne is doing something that blends sport and art.
- He is proud of her effort, not only the result.
- He is also aware that his oldest daughter deserves to be watched with the same full attention as the younger girls.

### 11. Science World
- After the competition, Mike takes Patti Anne to **Science World**.

### 12. Vancouver Father-Daughter Moment

### 13. Returning to Carlisle
- Mike takes Patti Anne back to the Carlisle mansion after the Vancouver trip.
