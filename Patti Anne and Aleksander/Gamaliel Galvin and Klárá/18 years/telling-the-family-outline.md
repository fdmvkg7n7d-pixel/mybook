# Outline: The First Circle

**Setting:** A FaceTime call between the Prague apartment and the household in Canada. It is afternoon in Prague and morning in Canada. The screen is a patchwork of faces: Patti Anne, Aleksander, and the young twins, Palmer Peter and Salena Sarai.

---

## I. The Setup (The Nervous Energy)
*   **The Atmosphere in Prague:** Gamaliel and Klára are sitting close together on their sofa, their faces illuminated by the glow of the laptop. There is a shared, humming electricity between them—a secret they have carried for three months, which has become the center of their world.
*   **The Chaos in Canada:** The call opens to the typical Key family morning. Patti Anne is likely coordinating breakfast; Aleksander is steady in the background; and Palmer Peter and Salena Sarai (around 12 years old) are buzzing with energy, crowding into the frame to see their "big" siblings.

## II. The Reveal (The Concrete Proof)
*   **The Lead-in:** The conversation starts with the usual updates—the Academy’s progress, the storefront, the weather in Prague. But there is a lingering quality to their smiles, a sense that they are holding something back.
*   **The Moment:** Gamaliel and Klára don't just say the words; they share the proof. They hold up the first ultrasound image to the camera—the small, grainy, unmistakable shape of a life.
*   **The Sentence:** *"We're having a baby."*

## III. The Reactions (The Three Tiers of Joy)

### A. Patti Anne: The Matriarch's Heart
*   **The Initial Shock:** A momentary freeze, followed by an audible gasp.
*   **The Emotional Wave:** Tears of absolute, radiating joy. For Patti Anne, this is more than just a grandchild; it is the ultimate confirmation that the stability she helped build for Klára and Gamaliel has held. 
*   **The Blessing:** Her reaction is a mix of motherly pride and spiritual gratitude. She sees this child as the first official "fruit" of the life they've built in Prague.

### B. Aleksander: The Steady Anchor
*   **The Response:** A deep, booming laugh and a wide smile. 
*   **The Support:** His reaction is one of immense pride in Gamaliel. He recognizes the man Gamaliel has become—a founder, a partner, and now a father. His words are simple but grounding: *"You've got this. We're so happy for you."*

### C. The Twins: Pre-Teen Excitement
*   **The Realization:** Palmer Peter and Salena Sarai recognize the ultrasound image immediately. The energy shifts to a more mature but intense excitement.
*   **The "Promotion":** The sudden, proud realization that they are now an **Uncle** and an **Auntie**. 
*   **The Dialogue:** Their questions are focused on the future and the role they'll play—asking about the due date, whether they can help name the baby, and how soon they can fly to Prague to meet their new niece or nephew. Their excitement is a mixture of "big kid" pride and genuine affection for their siblings.

## IV. The Meaning (The Expansion of the Circle)
*   **The Connection:** Despite the thousands of miles between them, the call creates a feeling of absolute proximity. The "circle" of the family has just expanded.
*   **Klára’s Peace:** Seeing Patti Anne’s reaction brings Klára a profound sense of closure. The approval and joy of her foster mother seal the reality: she is not just a daughter in this family; she is the matriarch of her own new branch.
*   **Gamaliel’s Pride:** Gamaliel looks at Klára and then at the screen, feeling the weight of his own history shift. He is no longer just the "son" or the "brother"; he is the patriarch of a new generation.

## V. The Closing (The Promise of the Future)
*   **The Logistics of Love:** The call descends into a flurry of planning. Patti Anne already begins talking about what they'll need, what she can send, and the inevitable trip to Prague.
*   **The Final Image:** The call ends with a chaotic, loving goodbye, with the twins waving frantically at the screen. 
*   **The Silence After:** Once the laptop closes, Gamaliel and Klára sit in the sudden silence of their apartment, holding each other. The secret is no longer theirs alone; it now belongs to the family.
