# Outline: The Season of Permission

**Timeline Context:** The months following their return from Japan. They are in the midst of the final transition from the storefront to the full Academy. The air in their life is charged with the excitement of growth and the certainty of their shared future.

---

## I. The Shift: From Decision to Desire
*   **The Psychological Key:** In Japan, they agreed that they *wanted* a child and that their world was *stable enough* to hold one. This removes the subconscious "brake" that has existed in their relationship for years.
*   **The Atmosphere:** A sudden, electric shift in their chemistry. The tenderness they've always had remains, but it is now overlaid with a focused, urgent hunger.
*   **The "Permission" Aspect:** They are no longer just partners in art or business; they are two people who have explicitly decided to create life. This transforms their intimacy from a private comfort into a shared mission.

## II. The Physicality of the Dancers
*   **Synchronicity:** Their intimacy mirrors their ballet. It is an extension of their partnership—the same trust, the same awareness of the other's center of gravity, and the same physical fluency.
*   **The Release of Discipline:** For a lifetime, they have been the "perfect" students—disciplined, precise, and controlled. Now, in the privacy of their home, they embrace the opposite. They find a primal joy in the *lack* of control, in the messiness and the urgency.
*   **The "Rabbit" Energy:** It becomes a recurring theme in their days. A glance in the studio, a touch in the kitchen, a shared moment in the hallway of the new Academy—everything leads back to each other. The passion is an obsession, a way of celebrating the fact that they are finally, irrevocably, *home*.

## III. The Settings of Passion
*   **The Home Sanctuary:** Their apartment in Prague becomes the center of this intensity. The space where they once "kept things light" is now filled with the weight of their desire.
*   **The Storefront Shadows:** The quiet corners of the storefront they are preparing to leave. There is a poignant irony in finding this level of passion in the place where they first proved their life worked.
*   **The New Academy:** The echoey, unfinished halls of the full Academy. The excitement of the building’s birth fuels the desire to start their own family.

## IV. The Emotional Undercurrent
*   **The Joy of "Yes":** Every encounter is a reinforcement of their agreement. It is a physical "yes" to the future they envisioned under the cherry blossoms.
*   **The Mutual Devotion:** This isn't just lust; it is a form of worship. Gamaliel continues to be Klára's shield, but now he is also her catalyst, encouraging her to take and want and feel everything without apology.
*   **The Absence of Fear:** For the first time in Klára's life, the physical act is completely devoid of the "foster reflex." She isn't wondering if this is temporary; she knows it is the foundation of everything.

## V. The Transition to Discovery
*   **The Natural Progression:** The intensity continues until it becomes the new normal—a high-voltage baseline for their relationship.
*   **The Subtle Change:** The shift from the "hunt" to the "wait." The moment where the passion settles into a quiet, humming anticipation.
*   **The Lead-in:** The outline ends just as the physical signs begin to emerge—the first missed cycle, the sudden fatigue, the intuitive glance between them that says: *It happened.*
