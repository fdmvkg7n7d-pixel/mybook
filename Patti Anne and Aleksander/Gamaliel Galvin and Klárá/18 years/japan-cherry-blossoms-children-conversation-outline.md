# Scene Outline: The Sakura Covenant

**Setting:** Kyoto, Japan. A path lined with ancient cherry blossoms in full bloom. The petals are falling in a slow, constant drift, creating a "snow" of pale pink on the pavement. The atmosphere is romantic, ethereal, and quiet.

**Timeline Context:** The final year of the storefront's official status as their primary operation. They are transitioning to the full Academy, but have decided to keep the storefront open for the neighborhood students.

---

## I. The Atmosphere of Transition
*   **The Walk:** Gamaliel and Klára are walking close, their shoulders brushing. There is a comfortable, ingrained synchronicity to their movement—the result of a lifetime of dancing together.
*   **The Storefront Reflection:** The conversation begins with a reflection on their life in Prague. They talk about the official "end" of the storefront era. 
*   **The Heart of the Matter:** Gamaliel mentions how he’s finalizing the transition to the full Academy, but Klára reminds him of their pact: *the neighborhood students must keep their space.* The decision to keep the storefront open for the locals is a moment of shared values—they are not just building a prestige school; they are preserving a sanctuary.

## II. The Ephemeral and the Eternal
*   **The Visual Trigger:** A sudden breeze sends a cascade of petals around them. Klára stops to watch them fall. 
*   **The Metaphor:** Klára remarks on how beautiful the blossoms are *because* they don't last. For a moment, this touches on her past—the childhood where everything (houses, people, safety) was a "season" that eventually ended.
*   **The Shift:** This observation leads naturally into the question of the future. If the blossoms are the "fleeting," what is the "permanent"?

## III. The Conversation: Building the Foundation

### A. The Question of "Enough"
*   **The Opening:** Klára asks, almost in a whisper, if they have finally built enough. Not enough money or enough fame, but enough *stability*. 
*   **The Core Question:** *"Do you think we’ve made a world where a child would never have to wonder if they were staying?"*

### B. The Shared Fears (The Weight)
*   **Klára’s Vulnerability:** She speaks of the "Foster Reflex"—the ghost of the social worker, the fear that motherhood is a door that could be closed by someone else's pen. She worries that she might accidentally teach a child how to be "ready to leave."
*   **Gamaliel’s Shadow:** Gamaliel admits to the silence of his own father. He speaks of the fear of the biological mirror—that the "absence" in his own history is a seed that might grow in his children. He admits he has spent his life trying to be the "perfect" shield for Klára, and he fears the chaos a child might bring to that carefully curated peace.

## IV. The Synthesis: The Ultimate Lift
*   **The Hope:** They realize that their fears are actually the reason they are the right people for this. Because they know the void, they are the only ones who can truly fill it.
*   **The Ballet Connection:** They discuss the concept of *partnering*. They've spent years lifting each other; now they talk about a new kind of lift—one where they hold a third person between them, sharing the center of gravity.
*   **The Vision:** The hope isn't for a perfect life, but for a *steady* one. A child who grows up in the "room" they built together—a room where the door is always cracked open and the porch light is always on.

## V. The Resolution: A Promise in the Petals
*   **The Decision:** A quiet, mutual agreement. It isn't a loud celebration, but a deep, grounding certainty.
*   **The Romantic Closing:** Gamaliel pulls her close, the falling petals landing in Klára's hair and on their coats. 
*   **The Final Beat:** They acknowledge that while the cherry blossoms will be gone by next week, the decision they just made is the one thing that will never fade. They leave the path not just as partners in art and business, but as architects of a new generation.
