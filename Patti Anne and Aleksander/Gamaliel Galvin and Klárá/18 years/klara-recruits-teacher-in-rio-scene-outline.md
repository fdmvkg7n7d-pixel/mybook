# Klára Recruits a Teacher in Rio

## Scene Outline

## Placement

**When:** Immediately after the family's Australia Christmas/New Year trip. Gamaliel takes the girls (Klárka 6, Simonka 5) home to Prague; **Klára flies on alone to Rio de Janeiro to recruit teachers for the academy.** (See `../first-scattered-christmas-scene-outline.md` → "Coda — The Scatter.")

**Ages:** Klára is in her early thirties (Gamaliel is 31; same age band). The Prague academy has grown from a storefront into a full graded school and now needs faculty (`gamaliel-galvin-and-klara-key-profile.md` → SHARED DREAM).

**Where:** Rio de Janeiro — studios, a company class, the beach edges of the city. **Rio is an established Key-family location:** the family lived here for a time; it is where Fallon Glenda was shielded on the beach, and where Kiley Rose met Lazaro da Silva. Klára has heard these family stories; she arrives in a city that already means something to the Keys, a generation later, on entirely different business. *(See `../../Fallon Glenda and Charlie/fallon-glenda-rio-beach-shield-scene-outline.md`; `../../Kiley Rose and Julia/Kiley Rose.md`.)*

---

## Scene Purpose

1. **Show the academy maturing** — it has outgrown its two founders; hiring outside faculty is the proof it's a real institution, not a passion project.
2. **Show Klára working solo, as the artist-judge of teaching talent** — the inversion of the couple's usual split (she travels for talent; Gamaliel covers home).
3. **Introduce a recurring academy figure** — the Rio teacher Klára recruits, a new character who can carry forward into the Prague academy world (and intersect later with Klárka teaching there, or with the Simonka generation).
4. **Let Rio resonate quietly** — Klára, the foster child who never belonged anywhere as a girl, walks the city her adoptive family once lived in, doing the most rooted thing imaginable: building a school by choosing the people who will teach in it.

---

## Core Emotional Throughline

Klára is not just filling a job. She is deciding **who gets to shape children the way she and Gamaliel were shaped** — at the barre, where discipline and tenderness are the same thing. She knows, better than most, that a teacher can be the one stable thing in an unstable childhood (the Academy was that for her). So she is not hiring a résumé. She is looking for the thing she once needed: someone who makes the studio a place a kid can be safe inside. That is what she watches for in Rio.

*(New character. A Brazilian ballet teacher Klára recruits in Rio.)*

- **Who he is:** A Carioca dancer in the back half of a real performing career — a soloist/character artist with a company in Rio, now teaching more than performing, the body still eloquent but the knees telling the truth. Mid-to-late forties. [Details adjustable.]
- **What Klára sees in him:** Not his dancing — his **teaching.** She watches him take a class and sees the thing she can't fake-detect any other way: he holds the room. He corrects a frightened student without making her smaller. He gives weight to the weak ones and demands more from the gifted ones. He runs a hard class that kids *want* to come back to. That is the whole job, and most people who can dance cannot do it.
- **Why he'd leave Rio for Prague:** [TBD — options:] a career honestly winding down at home; the pull of building something new rather than maintaining something old; the specific draw of an *academics-integrated* school (the model Klára pitches — never make a child choose between school and ballet); money/stability the $25M-backed academy can offer without the strings of a struggling company; or a personal reason to want a fresh continent.

Klára, who knows exactly what it is to be the outsider arriving somewhere cold, can speak to that honestly rather than selling past it.

## Scene Structure

### Beat 1 — Arrival, alone
- Klára lands in Rio by herself — the first time in this trip (in a long time) she is fully alone, no Gamaliel, no girls. The quiet after the noise of the Sydney harbour and two small children. She is good at alone in a way that started in childhood; here it reads as competence, not loneliness.
- The city greets her with everything the Keys remember — heat, beach, language she half-follows. She carries the family's Rio stories with her (the beach shield; Kiley Rose's first love) without being part of them. She is here for her own reason.

### Beat 2 — Watching class (not auditioning dancers — auditing teachers)
- Klára's method: she doesn't hold auditions for *dancers,* she sits in the back of studios and **watches people teach.** She visits a company school / a respected studio and observes.
- Establish what she's looking for through what she rejects: a brilliant ex-principal who humiliates students; a sweet teacher with no rigor; a famous name coasting. None of them are it. Klára is patient and unimpressed — the artist's eye is also a *no* most of the time.

### Beat 3 — She sees Inácio teach
- The class that stops her. Inácio runs a hard barre; the room is alive; a nervous kid gets a correction that lands as *care,* not shame. Klára watches him do the exact thing the Academy once did for her — make the studio a safe place to be bad at something on the way to being good.
- **Key image:** Klára, in the back, recognizing her own childhood in a stranger's classroom on another continent. She knows within ten minutes. The decision is the easy part; the convincing is the work.

### Beat 4 — The approach
- Klára introduces herself — not as a Key heiress, not leading with Terri Anne's millions, but as a dancer and a co-founder of a school. She pitches the *school,* not the salary: academics integrated with ballet, a place built so no child has to choose, the Mike Key Studio at its heart.
- Inácio is wary — people don't usually fly from Prague to poach a teacher; what's the catch? Klára's credibility is that she's clearly *one of them* (a real dancer, a real teacher's eye), not an administrator with a checkbook.

### Beat 5 — The honest conversation (the friction)
- The real talk: leaving Rio. His home, his warmth, his language, his life. Klára doesn't oversell. **She tells the truth about the cold** — literally (Prague winters) and figuratively (being the foreigner). And she can say, with authority he'll feel: *I know what it is to arrive somewhere that isn't yours and have to make it yours. I did it. The school is how you do it — you build the place that holds you.*
- This is the heart of the scene: the recruiter's pitch is also Klára's autobiography. She's not selling a job; she's offering a person the thing she was once given — a studio to belong to.

### Beat 6 — Rio resonance (the husband's blood)
- The deeper resonance, now that it's canon: **Gamaliel is half-Brazilian.** His unknown father was a Brazilian man; Gamaliel grew up wholly Canadian, never speaking Portuguese, never knowing that side. So **Klára is recruiting in the country of her own husband's roots** — walking the city of the heritage Gamaliel never got to hold. She is closer, in this moment, to his paternal homeland than he has ever been. There's an ache in it: the half-Brazilian man is in Prague doing the dishes, and it's his foster-raised wife who is in Brazil.
- What she does with it [TBD, keep restrained]: maybe nothing said aloud — just Klára noticing a face in a crowd, a turn of a phrase, and thinking *Gamaliel could have been from here.* Maybe she brings something home for the girls (who are therefore one-quarter Brazilian) — a small acknowledgment that this country is, distantly, *theirs.* Maybe she and Gamaliel have a quiet phone moment about it: him, who never had the thread, hearing his wife describe the place it came from.
- The family-history layer underneath (lighter touch): the Keys also *lived* in Rio once — the beach where Fallon Glenda was shielded, where Kiley Rose met Lazaro. Klára, the foster child who married into all of it, is now *adding* to the family's Rio story rather than surviving it. A generation later, Rio is where the Keys *hire,* and where a Key wife touches her husband's lost heritage — not where the family was hurt.
- (Keep light — a few lines, not a detour. The husband's-blood angle is the stronger one; lead with it.)

### Beat 7 — The yes (or the not-yet)
- Resolution options:
  - **A — He says yes** (with conditions: a visit to Prague first, a trial term, his terms on contract). The clean win; the academy gains its faculty anchor.
  - **B — He says not yet** but the door is open — Klára plants it and flies home with a maybe, establishing recruiting as a *patient, recurring* effort, not a one-shot.
  - **C — He says yes for a reason that costs him** — leaving someone or something in Rio behind, so the gain has a shadow (richer, more saga-like).
  - *Recommended: A or C.*
- Klára calls Gamaliel (and the girls) from Rio — the hinge back to the family: *I found one.* The business trip's payoff reported home to the man doing the dishes in Prague.

### Beat 8 — Closing image
- Klára leaving Rio with what she came for (a teacher, or the promise of one) — the foster girl who once owned nothing she couldn't carry now flies home having acquired the one thing a school is actually made of: a person who can teach. The academy is realer than it was a week ago.
- Final beat back to the theme: she went for *business,* but what she actually did was recognize, in a stranger, the kind of adult who saves a kid the way she was saved. That's not business. That's the whole point of the school.

## Key Dialogue Possibilities

### Klára
> I'm not here for a dancer. I have dancers. I'm here for a *teacher.* Those are different people.

> *(watching Inácio)* There. That's it. That's the whole job, and almost no one can do it.

> I won't lie to you about the cold. The winters are real and the city won't be yours for a long time. *(beat)* I came to a place that wasn't mine once. The studio is how you make it yours.

> *(to Gamaliel, on the phone)* I found one. *(beat)* He's the real thing. How are the girls?

### Inácio
> People don't fly from Prague to watch me teach twelve-year-olds. What is this, really?

> You're asking me to leave the sun.

> *(the turn)* Tell me about the school. Not the money. The school.

## What This Scene Establishes

1. **The academy is a real institution** — it hires outside faculty; it has outgrown its founders.
2. **Klára as talent-judge** — the artist's eye for *teaching,* working solo and decisive; the couple's flexible role-split confirmed.
3. **A new recurring figure** — the Rio teacher (Inácio Moraes), a faculty anchor who can carry into the Prague academy world and the next generation.
4. **Rio re-enters the saga in a new key** — a generation on, the city is where the Keys *build,* not where they were hurt; Klára adds to the family's Rio story instead of surviving it.
4a. **Gamaliel's heritage surfaces** — because Gamaliel is half-Brazilian (unknown Brazilian father), Klára is unknowingly/knowingly recruiting in the land of her husband's roots, and their daughters are one-quarter Brazilian. The blood thread Gamaliel never got to hold is the soil Klára now stands on. (Available to develop later: does Gamaliel ever go to Brazil himself? does he ever look for his father?)
5. **The thesis of the school, restated** — Klára hires for the thing that once saved her: a teacher who makes the studio a safe place to become good at something.
