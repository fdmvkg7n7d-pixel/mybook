# Simonka Káta Tells Holic About Confronting Patti Anne and Aleksander — Thursday Scene Outline

## Placement

**When:** Later Thursday night, after Simonka Káta confronts Patti Anne and Aleksander.

**Where:** Holic Bartunek's mansion, Prague. The scene should begin in a non-pet space — preferably the kitchen, study, or Wolfdog room — before any full GG-form. The dog collar is already on and stays on permanently; Simonka needs to speak as **Simonka Káta** while collared about what she did as Simonka Káta.

**Continuity Lead-in:**

- Tuesday: Patti Anne goes to Holic's mansion and sees Simonka in the run. Patti Anne tells Simonka through the fence that she still loves her no matter what.
- Wednesday morning: Aleksander goes to Holic's mansion and sees Simonka / GG eating from a bowl on the kitchen floor. He confronts Holic and tells Simonka she is loved and welcome home.
- Thursday evening: Simonka goes to Patti Anne and Aleksander and confronts them directly. She tells Patti Anne that being watched before she knew Patti Anne was watching hurt her. She tells Aleksander that he spoke about her while she was on the floor instead of speaking to her first. She sets a boundary: no more unannounced visits to Holic's mansion unless she asks or they believe she is in immediate danger.
- Later Thursday night: Simonka returns to Holic and tells him what she did.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene lets Simonka carry the family confrontation back into Holic's world.

The key point: she does not report to Holic as if asking permission. She tells him because what she did changes the terms around him. Patti Anne and Aleksander are no longer only witnesses in Holic's mansion; they are now people Simonka has confronted, answered, and given boundaries to.

By the end of the scene:

1. Holic knows Simonka confronted Patti Anne and Aleksander directly.
2. Holic knows Simonka set a boundary against unannounced family visits to his mansion.
3. Simonka tells Holic that she defended her adult agency without rejecting her family.
4. Holic must decide whether he respects that boundary as Simonka's or treats it as a convenience for himself.
5. Simonka clarifies that the family door remains open — and that she is not asking Holic to close it.

---

## Emotional Core

Simonka has done something difficult: she faced the people who love her and told them where their love hurt.

Now she has to tell Holic without letting him turn that confrontation into proof that her family is the enemy. She needs him to understand the distinction:

- Patti Anne and Aleksander crossed lines.
- Patti Anne and Aleksander also love her.
- Simonka corrected them.
- She did **not** cut them off.

The emotional danger is that Holic may hear only the useful part: *they agreed not to come unannounced.* Simonka needs him to hear the harder part: *they are still my family, and I still have a door there.*

---

## Tone

Quiet, controlled, tense. No explicit sexual content. This is a scene about disclosure, power, family boundaries, and whether Holic can respect Simonka's ties outside his mansion.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — Simonka Arrives With Something Already Decided

Simonka arrives at Holic's mansion on Thursday.

She is not frantic. She is not ashamed in the same way she was after Patti Anne saw her or Aleksander saw her. She is tired, but there is a new steadiness in her. She has spoken. She has done the thing she came to do.

Holic notices.

He does not use the permanent collar to move her immediately into GG-form.

Possible line:

> You went to them.

Simonka answers:

> Yes.

Or:

> I confronted them.

The word **confronted** matters. She did not visit. She did not apologize. She confronted.

---

### Beat 2 — Simonka Tells Him the Facts

Simonka tells Holic what happened, in order.

- She went to Patti Anne and Aleksander.
- She asked to speak to them without Gamaliel and Klára in the room.
- She spoke first to Patti Anne.
- She told Patti Anne that being watched before she knew Patti Anne was watching felt like a violation.
- She spoke to Aleksander.
- She told him that he talked about her while she was on the floor, and that he should have spoken to her first.
- She told them she is not Klára as a child.
- She told them not to turn Holic into Klára's father or Simonka into an eight-year-old who needs rescuing.
- She set the boundary: no more unannounced visits to the mansion unless there is immediate danger or she asks them to come.

Holic listens without interrupting.

This is important: if he interrupts too early, he repeats the very problem she just confronted — men and elders speaking before she is finished.

---

### Beat 3 — Holic's First Reaction

Holic's first reaction should reveal him.

Possible reactions:

- Pride that she defended the boundary of his house.
- Satisfaction that Patti Anne and Aleksander will not appear unannounced again.
- Concern that she had to do that alone.
- Curiosity about what she said about him.
- Irritation that the family remains involved at all.

The strongest version: he begins with the wrong emphasis.

Possible Holic line:

> So they agreed not to come here again.

Simonka hears what he chose to notice.

She says:

> That is not the first thing.

This is the pivot.

---

### Beat 4 — Simonka Corrects Holic Too

Simonka tells Holic he is making the same mistake in a different direction.

Patti Anne and Aleksander looked at her and saw a child or a victim. Holic risks looking at her and seeing only GG, only his pet, only someone who defended the perimeter of his mansion.

Possible line:

> I did not go there to protect your house. I went because they hurt me, and because I love them, and because I needed them to hear me.

Holic is quiet.

Simonka continues:

> Do not make my boundary with them into your victory.

This is the core line of the scene.

---

### Beat 5 — The Family Door Remains Open

Simonka tells Holic exactly what Patti Anne and Aleksander said back.

They apologized for the right things. Not perfectly. Not enough to make everything clean. But enough that she believed them.

They promised not to come unannounced unless they believed she was in immediate danger.

They also told her the door remains open. Any hour. Any place. No explanation first.

Holic hears this.

Simonka says:

> I did not ask them to close that door.

A beat.

> And I am not asking you to close it either.

This line matters because it prevents Holic from treating the family boundary as isolation.

---

### Beat 6 — Holic Tests the Meaning of the Door

Holic may ask, carefully:

> Do you want to go through it?

Simonka answers honestly.

Possible answer:

> Not today.

Or:

> I do not know.

Or:

> I wanted it open. That is not the same as wanting to leave.

The last version best preserves the arc: Simonka wants an exit to exist without choosing it yet.

Holic must sit with the fact that an open door is part of her consent structure. If there is no door back to family, then her staying means something darker.

---

### Beat 7 — Holic's Boundary / Promise

Holic now has to respond.

The healthiest possible response from within his character is controlled and conditional, not sentimental.

Possible line:

> I will not close a door you want open.

But Holic may add:

> I will not let them use it to drag you out.

Simonka answers:

> They know that. I told them.

Holic:

> And if they come anyway?

Simonka:

> Then I will speak first.

This is the new condition: not Holic first, not Patti Anne first, not Aleksander first. Simonka first.

---

### Beat 8 — The Question of Thursday

Holic asks what changed in her after the confrontation.

Simonka may struggle to answer.

Possible line:

> I thought if I went to them, I would feel less like GG here. I don't.

A beat.

> I feel more like Simonka everywhere.

This is a major character beat. The goal is not to split Simonka and GG into separate selves, but to make Simonka more present inside every role she chooses.

She may add:

> That is harder.

Holic understands that it is harder for him too. A pet who is more fully a person is not less his — but she is less simple to own.

---

### Beat 9 — Before Full GG-Form

If the scene moves toward pet space, it must do so only after the conversation.

The collar is already on and will stay on permanently. Holic may ask if she wants to enter full GG-form, but he cannot use the collar as the switch that erases what she just said.

Possible Holic line:

> If we go there now, you do not leave that conversation outside the room.

Simonka:

> I know.

Holic:

> You bring it in with you.

Simonka:

> Yes.

This reframes GG: not as escape from family, but as a chosen state entered by a woman whose family still exists and whose collar is already permanent.

---

### Beat 10 — Closing Image

The collar does not go on or off. It stays on.

Either ending can work:

#### Option A — Simonka Enters Full GG-Form

Simonka chooses full GG-form after telling Holic everything. The closing image is not Holic fastening the collar, but Holic shifting her by name, posture, and command while both of them know the family door remains open. The collar is not a wall. It is the permanent threshold she carries with her.

#### Option B — Simonka Does Not Enter Full GG-Form Yet

Simonka asks to sit as Simonka a while longer. Holic permits it. The closing image is Simonka collared, upright, and still speaking as Simonka — a rare pause in the machinery of the mansion.

**Recommended:** Option B. It gives Thursday its own weight. She has just confronted her grandparents. She does not need to disappear into full GG-form immediately to prove she still chooses Holic.

Final possible image:

The collar is on her neck. Her family door is still open somewhere outside the mansion. Simonka sits between those two truths, and Holic lets the silence remain.

## What This Scene Establishes

1. **Simonka reports the confrontation to Holic without asking permission.** She tells him what she did because it affects him, not because he owns the story.
2. **Holic learns the family boundary.** Patti Anne and Aleksander agreed not to come unannounced except in immediate danger or if Simonka asks.
3. **Simonka prevents Holic from turning the boundary into isolation.** The family door remains open; Holic is not allowed to treat the confrontation as a victory over them.
4. **Simonka's adult voice strengthens.** She is not only defending Holic to her family; she is also correcting Holic when he hears the wrong thing.
5. **The permanent collar becomes more complicated.** If she enters full GG-form after this, she does so while carrying Simonka's family, not by erasing it; the collar stays on either way.
6. **The Thursday beat preserves tension without forcing resolution.** Simonka still chooses Holic, but she also keeps her family door open.
