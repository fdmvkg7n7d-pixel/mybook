# Fallout Outlines: The Shattering and Its Aftermath

This file contains the outlined sequences following Klárka Pavlînka witnessing the events at Holic's mansion.

---

## Sequence 1: The Extraction and the Return

**Location:** Holic Mansion $\rightarrow$ Raphael's car $\rightarrow$ Pražská Baletní Akademie.
**Characters:** Klárka Pavlînka, Raphael, (Holic and Simonka in the background), Gamaliel Galvin, and Klárá.

**The Beats:**
1. **Raphael's Arrival:** Raphael arrives in a state of urgency, his arrival abrupt and focused.
2. **The Dual Shock:** Raphael witnesses the horrific juxtaposition: Simonka bound and exposed on the pole, and Klárka collapsed and retching on the grass.
3. **The Support:** Raphael ignores Holic's indifference and moves directly to Klárka. He doesn't just pick her up; he helps her to her feet, supporting her trembling weight and guiding her carefully, step-by-step, toward the car.
4. **The Departure:** As they leave, the visual of Simonka—the "Good Girl" persona—lingers in Klárka's mind, a trauma that doesn't vanish just because they are moving away from the site.
5. **The Transit of Shock:** The drive to the academy. Raphael remains a silent, steady presence, attempting to offer comfort through proximity while Klárka remains in a state of hollow, exhausted shock.
6. **Arrival at the Academy:** They pull up to the Pražská Baletní Akademie. The contrast is stark: the serene, disciplined beauty of the academy versus Klárka's current state (clothes stained with vomit, face swollen from crying).
7. **The Reunion:** Raphael leads Klárka into the building to find Gamaliel and Klárá. The scene ends with the jarring moment the parents see their daughter, the immediate shift from the academy's normalcy to a family crisis.

## Sequence 2: The Retreat (Simonka/Good Girl and Holic)

**Location:** Holic's Property.
**Characters:** Simonka Káta / Good Girl, Holic, Bruno.

**The Beats:**
1. **The Switch:** As Raphael and Klárka disappear from sight, the "Simonka" who felt agony for her cousin recedes. The persona of **Good Girl (GG)** settles back over her completely—a psychological shield against the guilt and pain.
2. **The Withdrawal:** Once released from the pole, GG does not stand. She remains low, crawling across the property to one of her favorite secluded spots (a place of perceived safety or ritual).
3. **The Quiet Grief:** In this sanctuary, the facade softens slightly. GG begins to weep—not the panicked sobbing of Klárka, but a slow, rhythmic, and exhausted weeping.
4. **Holic’s Presence:** Holic follows her. He doesn't shout or punish; instead, he maintains his role as the "owner/caretaker." He kneels down to her eye level, bringing himself into her space.
5. **The Comfort/Control:** He gently pats her back—a gesture that is simultaneously comforting and a reminder of his absolute possession of her.
6. **The Boundary:** Holic looks at **Bruno** (the dog), giving a sharp, silent command for the animal to stay away from GG, ensuring that in this moment of vulnerability, the only source of comfort or authority is himself.

## Sequence 3: The Aftermath in the Office

**Location:** Klárá’s Private Office, Pražská Baletní Akademie.
**Characters:** Klárka Pavlînka, Raphael, Gamaliel Galvin, and Klárá.

**The Beats:**
1. **The Jarring Entry:** Raphael leads Klárka into the office. The visual contrast is stark: the professional serenity of the academy versus Klárka’s devastated state (shaking, face swollen, clothes stained with vomit).
2. **Immediate Stabilization:** Gamaliel and Klárá react with instinctive parental panic. Klárá provides water and a towel; Gamaliel guides her into a chair, focusing entirely on her physical distress before asking any questions.
3. **The Trauma Reveal:** In halting, broken sentences, Klárka describes the scene at the mansion.
    *   The image of Simonka bound to the pole.
    *   The public nature of the act.
    *   The devastating words of Holic: *"Good Girl likes sleeping with different people."*
4. **The "Good Girl" Realization:** The horror settles in for the parents. They aren't just processing a secret relationship, but the existence of a dehumanized persona ("Good Girl") that has seemingly replaced the daughter/cousin they knew.
5. **The Shift in Tone:** As Klárka sinks into a state of exhausted, hollow silence, Raphael looks at her with a profound, protective worry. He realizes that the stress of this trauma is now a risk to more than just Klárka's mental health.
6. **The Disclosure:** Raphael leans in and tells Gamaliel and Klárá the secret: **Klárka is pregnant, approximately ten weeks.**
7. **The Justification:** Raphael acknowledges the breach of protocol. He admits he knows the rule about waiting until the second trimester to announce a pregnancy, but he explains that given Klárka's current emotional and fragile state, the parents *must* know so they can provide the necessary care and protection.
8. **The Parental Reaction:** A moment of stunned silence. The shock of the Simonka revelation is now compounded by the shock of the pregnancy. The priority immediately shifts from "processing the trauma" to "protecting the pregnancy."
9. **The Hard Decision:** The conversation turns to the **new building opening** at the academy, scheduled for three weeks from now—a massive milestone for the family and the school.
10. **The Resolution:** Without hesitation, Gamaliel and Klárá decide that the building is secondary. They prioritize Klárka’s well-being and the health of the baby. They insist that Klárka go home immediately to rest and recover in a safe environment.
11. **The Closing Image:** The scene ends with the parents cocooning Klárka in their support, guiding her out of the office and toward home, while the heavy, unresolved tragedy of Simonka's "Good Girl" identity remains a dark cloud over the family.
