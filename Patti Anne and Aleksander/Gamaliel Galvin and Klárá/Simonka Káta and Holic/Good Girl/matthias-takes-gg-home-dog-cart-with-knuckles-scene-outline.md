# SCENE OUTLINE: MATTHIAS TAKES GG HOME — THE DOG CART WITH KNUCKLES

*This scene takes place after Holic offers GG to Matthias for the weekend (`holic-tells-gg-about-matthias-weekend-deal-scene-outline.md`) and the offer is accepted. Matthias loads his own pets to bring them home, and GG is sent with them. During the drive to Matthias's home she is not permitted to ride in the cab — she must stay in the back with Knuckles, in his dog cart.*

---

## CANON BACKDROP & HEART HISTORY

* **Timing:** End of the weekend-offer day at Holic's mansion. After Klárka's screaming, Raphael pulling her away, and Holic's private walk with GG. The handoff to Matthias now actually happens — resolving the open question "Does GG actually go?"
* **The Other Owner — Matthias:** Established in `klarka-follows-holic-and-gg-to-mansion-scene-outline.md`. He owns five pets — **Knuckles**, **Pretzel**, **Falafel**, **Wunjo** (males) and **Juno** (female) — who arrived in a **box van**. He was angry at Klárka's intrusion; Holic placated him by offering GG for the weekend.
* **Knuckles:** One of Matthias's energetic male pets. GG and Knuckles already had a public encounter in Holic's front yard (the yard scene). They are not strangers in the dynamic, which makes the cart pairing deliberate, not random — Matthias places GG with a pet she has already "met."
* **Simonka's State:** Still in GG mode. The electric collar remains locked at her throat. She has just chosen, on Holic's walk, not to use her safewords "right now." This scene is the first moment the weekend stops being words and becomes a body in a vehicle leaving Holic's grounds.
* **The Transfer Within the Rules:** Per the Collegium bylaws (`holic-club-rules-and-bylaws.md`), a shared/transferred arrangement requires negotiation and consent of every owner and pet (§5.3), safewords bind **every** owner and handler, not only her own (§4.3), and an owner who hands a pet to another owner does not transfer the duty of care — he **extends** it (§7.4). The drive must be read against these rules: Holic's duty does not end at his gate, and Matthias is now bound by GG's safewords too.

---

## TONE GUARDRAIL

Keep the scene **non-explicit**. The point is not what happens in the cart. The point is the **logistics of transfer** — the moment a person becomes cargo within the system's own logic, and what that does to Simonka internally. The dog cart, the box van, the locked collar, and the simple fact that she rides in the back and not the cab carry the entire weight. Stay centered on autonomy, dread, duty, consent-under-pressure, and the live presence of the safeword she has chosen not to use.

---

## WHO IS PRESENT

* **Matthias** — the receiving owner; drives, or rides in the cab while a handler drives.
* **Simonka Káta as GG** — handed over for the weekend; rides in the back, in the cart, with Knuckles.
* **Knuckles** — Matthias's male pet; shares the dog cart with GG.
* **Matthias's other pets** — Pretzel, Falafel, Wunjo, Juno — loaded into the box van for the journey home.
* **Holic** — present at the loading/departure only. He does not travel. His duty of care follows GG anyway (§7.4).
* **Klárka and Raphael** — **not present.** They have already gone. The family does not witness this and may not learn of it unless Simonka tells them later.

---

# SCENE STRUCTURE

## Beat 1 — The Handoff Is Real Now

* After the private walk, Holic brings GG back toward the vehicles. The grounds are quieter. Matthias's box van waits, doors open.
* Holic and Matthias speak as owners — calm, administrative. The terms of the weekend are confirmed in front of GG, about GG, without GG being addressed as a person.
* **The duty-of-care beat (canon-critical):** Holic does not simply give her away. Before she is loaded, he states the conditions Matthias must honor — chiefly that GG's **yellow and red bind Matthias exactly as they bind Holic** (§4.3), and that aftercare is owed (§8). This is the club's "control is care" self-justification doing its work: the handoff is wrapped in procedure, which is precisely what makes it feel orderly rather than alarming.
* **Possible Holic lines:**
  > Her words are her words. Yellow and red. You honor them the way I do.
  > She comes back to me Sunday. In good condition.
* **Possible Matthias line:**
  > Understood. She rides in the back.

**Key image:** GG standing between two men who are arranging her weekend, the collar at her throat catching the light, the open van behind her.

---

## Beat 2 — Loading the Pets

* Matthias's pets are loaded into the box van: **Pretzel** and **Falafel** energetic, **Wunjo** calm, tiny **Juno** moving with her usual dignity. Bells, name tags, harness clothing — the same kit established in the yard scene.
* **Knuckles** is loaded last, or settled into his **dog cart** in the back — a small cart/crate-on-wheels fixture clearly built for a human pet, not a comfort seat. Establish it plainly: low, enclosed enough to confine, fitted for transport.
* The ordinariness matters. The pets board like animals who have done this many times. The routine is what makes GG's place in it land.

---

## Beat 3 — GG Is Put in the Back, Not the Cab

* The decision is stated, not debated: GG does **not** ride up front. She rides in the back of the van.
* **And not loose in the back** — she must stay **with Knuckles, in his dog cart.** She is placed into the cart beside him, or the cart is sized to hold the two of them together. Either way the instruction is unambiguous: for the duration of the drive, GG belongs in the cart with Knuckles.
* **Possible Matthias line:**
  > Front is for me. You're in the cart. With him.
* GG complies. The compliance is the scene — there is no dramatic resistance, only the quiet, total following of the instruction, which is more disturbing than a struggle would be.

**Key image:** The van doors closing on GG folded into a dog cart beside Knuckles, the last of the daylight narrowing to a line and then gone.

---

## Beat 4 — The Drive

* The van pulls away from Holic's mansion. The grounds — the pole, the yard, the place all of this happened — fall behind.
* Inside the dark of the box van: the other pets settled in their spots, the engine hum, the soft jingle of bells over road noise, the cart's small movements with every turn.
* **Knuckles** — already familiar to GG from the yard — settles in beside her. Keep this **non-explicit.** His presence is close, animal, wordless. He may offer the same simple, body-warmth comfort the friendly pet offered her in the earlier alone-time scene (echo `holic-and-gg-meet-another-member-and-two-male-pets-scene-outline.md`, Beat 4.6) — or he may simply be a confined body next to hers. Decide on the day; either reading works.
* The cart confines her. The collar is locked. She is in a vehicle she does not control, going to a house she has never seen, with an owner who is not hers.

---

## Beat 5 — Simonka's Internal Experience

* This is the scene's center. Maintain GG composure on the surface; let Simonka's adult self move underneath.
* **The cargo realization:** On Holic's walk she said it was her *job* to be his pet. In the cart, "job" stops being a word and becomes a body riding in the back with the other animals. She feels the difference between choosing the sentence and living its consequence.
* **The safeword, present and unused:** She knows the rules — she can say *yellow* or *red* to Matthias, and he is bound to honor it (§4.3); she could even have said it to a handler. The word is in her mouth the entire drive. She does not say it. The tension is not "can she escape" but "why doesn't she," and the honest answer is layered: duty, the wish to be a good pet, fear of failing Holic, and a self she is only beginning to interrogate.
* **Knuckles as mirror:** Beside her is a pet fully inside the life — no human dignity expected, no human speech. She is closer to his condition than she wants to admit, and the cart makes the comparison literal: same vehicle, same cart, same back of the van. (Echo the male-pets scene's "wonders if they see her as closer to them than she wants to admit.")
* **Optional image:** A single thing she can see — a sliver of passing streetlight through a vent, the name tag on Knuckles's collar, her own breath — that she fixes on to stay GG and not Simonka for the length of the drive. (Echo the "single tear" image from the yard scene without repeating it.)

---

## Beat 6 — Arrival

* The van stops. Matthias's home. The doors open; the daylight or yard light returns.
* The pets are let out and led in. GG is brought out of the cart **last** or with Knuckles — re-establishing her place in the order: not first, not chosen, transferred.
* End before the weekend itself begins. The scene's job is the **journey**, not the stay. Close on the threshold of Matthias's house, the cart behind her, the collar still locked, the safeword still unused, the weekend not yet shown.

**Possible closing image:** GG on her hands and knees being led across an unfamiliar threshold among pets who are not hers, into a household that is not Holic's — the system having proved it can move her, intact and obedient, from one owner to another.
