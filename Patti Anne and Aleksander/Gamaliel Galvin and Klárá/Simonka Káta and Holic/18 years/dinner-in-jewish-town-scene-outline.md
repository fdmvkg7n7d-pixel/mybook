# SCENE OUTLINE: DINNER IN JEWISH TOWN

## SCENE INFORMATION
- **Characters:** Gamaliel Galvin Key, Klára, Klárka Pavlínka (10), Simonka Káta (9)
- **Location:** Simonka’s favorite restaurant in the Jewish Town (Josefov), Prague.
- **Date:** Wednesday evening, following Klára’s return and the studio dance.
- **Status:** Outline

---

## THE SETTING
- **The Jewish Town (Josefov):** A historic quarter of Prague with narrow streets, old synagogues, and a deep sense of mystery and history.
- **The Restaurant:** A cozy, wood-paneled establishment tucked away in a quiet alley near the Spanish Synagogue. It is warm, lit by candles, and smells of roasted meats and traditional spices.
- **Atmosphere:** A mix of immense relief (the family is reunited) and the quiet, heavy knowledge of the surgery ahead.

---

## SCENE ARC

### 1. The Walk through Josefov
- The family walks from the Academy to the Jewish Town. The cool evening air of Prague is a sharp contrast to the Greek heat they left behind.
- Simonka leads the way, her step lighter now that Klára is back. She knows every turn of these old streets.

### 2. Settling In
- They are greeted warmly at the restaurant; they are regular guests here. 
- Simonka is allowed to choose the table—a corner booth with a view of the street.
- **The Menu:** Simonka’s favorites:
    - Traditional Jewish-Prague dishes (perhaps a rich brisket or roasted duck).
    - A specific dessert she loves (like *jablečný závin* or a chocolate torte).

### 3. The Conversation
- For much of the meal, they deliberately avoid talking about "the stone." They talk about the villa in Crete, the rock jumping, and the funny things Koula said during the cooking class.
- Klára tells them about Cape Town—the mountains and the sea—and how much she missed them.
- Simonka is the center of attention, the "birthday girl" of the evening even though it isn't her birthday. She feels seen and protected.

### 4. The Shared Strength
- At one point, Klárka Pavlínka takes Simonka's hand under the table. It is a quiet, sisterly "tighten" moment.
- Gamaliel and Klára share a look over the candlelight. The crisis hasn't gone away, but the "circle" is closed again. They are a unit.

### 5. Walking Home
- They walk back through the quiet streets of the Jewish Town. 
- Simonka points out her favorite "secret" spots—a carved stone, a hidden courtyard.
- As they reach their front door, the feeling of the "tighten" hug from earlier in the week is replaced by a sense of quiet, unified resolve. They are home.

---

## CHARACTER NOTES
- **Simonka Káta (9):** Reclaiming her place as the "little one" who is cared for. The restaurant is her "kingdom."
- **Klárka Pavlínka (10):** Relaxing her guard slightly now that Klára is back to share the load.
- **Klára:** Savoring every second of being back. The "vertical" world of Cape Town is gone, replaced by the solid, historic stones of Prague.
- **Gamaliel:** The quiet observer. He watches his family in the candlelight, feeling the weight of the "Watcher" role transition back into a shared partnership with Klára.

---

## SAGA CONNECTIONS
- **Sense of Place:** Establishes the family’s deep connection to the specific history and geography of Prague.
- **Domestic Rituals:** Highlights how the family uses favorite places and shared meals to maintain normalcy during a crisis.
