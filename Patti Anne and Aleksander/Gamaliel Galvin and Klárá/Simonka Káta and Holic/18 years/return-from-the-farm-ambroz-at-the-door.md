# SCENE OUTLINE: THE RETURN FROM THE FARM – AMBROZ AT THE DOOR

## SCENE INFORMATION
- **Characters:** Simonka Káta (19), Ambroz (19)
- **Location:** Hallway outside Simonka’s Apartment, Prague.
- **Date:** Saturday morning after her nineteenth birthday (6:00 AM).
- **Status:** Outline

---

## THE SETTING
- Six o'clock in the morning. The hallway is cold and dimly lit, the gray light of dawn filtering through a dusty window at the end of the corridor.
- Simonka is arriving home, carrying her overnight bag. She is still in the immediate "afterglow" of the farm—her body vibrating from the intensity of the night in the barn and the 5,000-gallon tank.
- She has only been nineteen for six hours. The "intentional pressure" of her friends is still a physical memory on her skin.
- Her body feels heavy, the **Osteogenesis Imperfecta** making the climb up the stairs a conscious effort as the morning gravity hits her bones.

---

## SCENE ARC

### 1. The Discovery
- As Simonka reaches her floor, she sees a figure slumped against her apartment door.
- **The Sight:** It’s Ambroz. He looks like he hasn't slept well. His hair is messy, and his clothes are rumpled. He’s been waiting for her, potentially for hours.
- **The Reaction:** Simonka feels an immediate tightening in her chest—not from her operation, but from the "noise" he represents. She wanted to bring the "stillness" of the farm into her sanctuary; instead, her problem is waiting for her at the threshold.

### 2. The Inquisitor
- Ambroz jumps up the moment he sees her. His energy is frantic and sharp.
- "You're finally back," he says, his voice a mix of relief and accusation. "I’ve been calling. Why didn't you answer?"
- Simonka remains calm. The "dancer's guard" is up. "I told you I’d be away for the weekend, Ambroz. I was celebrating."

### 3. The Prying Eye
- Ambroz doesn't move out of the way. He stands close, trying to read her face, looking for "evidence" of what happened at the farm.
- **The Jealousy:** "Did you have a good time? Was Alois there the whole time? And Braxton? I saw his car in your street before you left."
- He is looking for bruises, for marks, for any sign that she is "different" now. He doesn't know about the **crop on the left hand** or the **bare floorboards**, but he senses the shift in her.

### 4. Maintaining the Pact
- Simonka feels the weight of the "Pact of Secrecy."
- She thinks about the 5,000-gallon tank and the way she felt weightless with Drahoslava. She could never tell Ambroz about that—he would turn the "sanctuary" into a source of suspicion.
- "It was a quiet weekend, Ambroz. We just talked and rested." The lie feels heavy, but necessary. It’s her only way to protect the "private face" she found at the farm.

### 5. The Suffocation
- Ambroz starts to plead. "I missed you so much. I hated every second of you being there without me. Let me come in. Let me stay tonight."
- His neediness is a "sharp impact" on her emotions. He wants to take up all her space, to fill the quiet she just worked so hard to find.
- Simonka looks at her door. Her apartment is her "den" (a concept she will later explore with Holic), and she doesn't want his chaotic jealousy inside it.

### 6. The Living Room Floor: Angry Reclamation
- **The Threshold:** Simonka unlocks the door, and Ambroz practically pushes his way inside. He doesn't wait for her to set down her bag.
- **The Action:** In the center of the living room, driven by a "noisy" and "chaotic" jealousy, Ambroz takes her to the floor. There is no negotiation, no slow induction—just the immediate, desperate need to "reclaim" what he feels he lost to the farm.
- **The Floor:** Like the barn, it is a hard surface. But here, there is no "intentionality." The impact of the floor against her back is a "sharp" sensation that triggers her medical anxiety.
- **The Physical Conflict:** Simonka is forced into a state of "hyper-vigilance." As Ambroz moves with angry, uncoordinated intensity, she has to use her dancer's strength to brace herself, protecting her ribs and her spine from his weight. He is the "wrong" kind of heavy—unpredictable and reckless.
- **The Contrast:** Her mind flashes to the "intentional pressure" of the previous night. There, the impact was a gift; here, it is a threat she has to manage in silence.
- **The Silence:** She doesn't tell him to stop, and she doesn't tell him about the OI. She maintains the "Pact of Secrecy," enduring the "noise" of his reclamation because she doesn't trust him with the truth of her fragility.

### 7. The Aftermath
- When it is over, they are still on the hard floor. Ambroz seems settled, his jealousy temporarily satiated by the physical act.
- Simonka feels a profound, bone-deep exhaustion. The encounter has confirmed the "chasm" between them. He has "taken" her, but he hasn't seen her. He has interacted with her body without understanding its "map."
- As the Saturday morning light fills the room, Simonka looks at the ceiling. She is nineteen years and six hours old, and she already knows that this relationship is a cage made of "noise." The stillness of the water tank feels like a lifetime ago.

## CHARACTER NOTES
- **Ambroz (19):** At his most insecure. He is using his physical presence as a way to "reclaim" Simonka after her weekend away.
- **Simonka (19):** Feeling the "exhaustion" more than ever. The contrast between the "controlled care" of the farm and the "chaotic need" of Ambroz is becoming unbearable.

- **OI and Gravity:** The physical metaphor of her bones feeling heavy when she’s around Ambroz.
