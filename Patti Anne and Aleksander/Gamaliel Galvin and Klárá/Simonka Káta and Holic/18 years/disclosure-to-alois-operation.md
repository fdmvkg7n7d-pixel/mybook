# SCENE OUTLINE: THE DISCLOSURE TO ALOIS

## SCENE INFORMATION
- **Characters:** Simonka Káta (18), Alois
- **Location:** Simonka’s Apartment, Prague.
- **Date:** One week before her nineteenth birthday.
- **Status:** Outline

---

## THE SETTING
- A quiet evening in Simonka’s apartment. The city lights of Prague are visible through the window.
- They are planning the logistics for her birthday trip to his farm—what to bring, who is coming, and the "private" plans for the barn.
- The atmosphere is intimate, but Simonka has been unusually quiet for the last hour.

---

## SCENE ARC

### 1. The Threshold of the Secret
- Simonka is sitting on the sofa next to Alois. For nearly a decade, she has kept the "Pact of Secrecy." Not even her closest university friends know about the "stone" or the hospital in Prague.
- She looks at Alois. She is about to enter a new level of physical and psychological intimacy with him at the farm. She realizes she cannot go to the barn or the water tank while holding onto this secret.

### 2. Breaking the Pact
- **The Ask:** Simonka takes his hand. "Alois, before we go to the farm... there is something I’ve never told anyone outside my family. Not ever."
- She explains the summer when she was nine. She uses the family’s old language: "The stone."
- She tells him about the discovery in June, the secret she carried through Crete, and the operation in August.

### 3. The Visual Truth
- **The Vulnerability:** Simonka is wearing a soft, loose sweater. She hesitates, then slowly pulls it up just enough to reveal the right side of her chest.
- **The Scar:** She shows him the thin, silver-pink line near her nipple. To her, it has always felt like a mark of being "broken" or "different."
- "I was embarrassed," she admits. "I didn't want anyone to see me as a patient. I wanted to be just Simonka."

### 4. The Second Disclosure: Fragility
- **The Connection:** "There's something else," she adds, her voice even quieter. "The operation... it's only part of it. My bones... they aren't like other people's."
- **The Explanation:** She explains the **Osteogenesis Imperfecta (OI) Type 1**. She tells him about the collagen deficiency, the **Fosamax** and **Actonel** she takes every morning, and the "Rules of Engagement" her family has regarding physical impact.
- **The "Mark of the Tribe":** She looks him in the eye, and for the first time, she points out the blue tint in the whites of her eyes. "It’s how we recognize each other," she says. "Me and my sister."

### 5. Alois’s Reaction
- Alois doesn't look away, and he doesn't look with pity. He looks at the scar and her eyes with the same analytical, appreciative gaze he uses for everything about her.
- **The Touch:** He follows the "Rules of Engagement" he has learned from her. "May I touch it, Simonka?"
- When she nods, he traces the silver line with one finger, then rests his palm very gently, very flat, against her shoulder—applying the "intentional, careful pressure" of a Key-Dimitrov hug.
- "It isn't a mark of being broken," he says quietly. "It’s a mark of how your body took care of you. And the fragility... it just means we have to be more intentional. More precise. It makes everything we do more deliberate."

### 6. The Connection to the Farm
- They talk about how this affects their plans. Simonka admits she was worried the BDSM equipment or the water tank might cause a fracture or make her feel "clinical" again.
- Alois reassures her. He explains that at the farm, *she* is the boss of what happens to her body—just like her parents taught her. The equipment is for her pleasure, and he will adapt everything to ensure her skeletal safety. The "intentional pressure" will be the rule of the barn.
- This conversation transforms the scar and the OI from "medical secrets" into a part of their shared erotic trust.

### 7. The Relief
- Simonka pulls her sweater back down. She feels lighter than she has in years.
- She has broken the "Pact," but she hasn't lost her power. If anything, sharing the full scope of her history with Alois has given her more control over her upcoming birthday.
- They return to the packing list, but the energy in the room has shifted from "planning" to "deep connection."
