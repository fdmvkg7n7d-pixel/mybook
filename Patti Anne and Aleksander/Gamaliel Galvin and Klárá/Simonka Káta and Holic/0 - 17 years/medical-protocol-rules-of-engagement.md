# MEDICAL PROTOCOL: THE KEY-DIMITROV "RULES OF ENGAGEMENT"

## OVERVIEW
This document outlines the specific medical and psychological protocols established by Gamaliel Galvin Key and Klára for their daughters, Simonka Káta and Klárka Pavlínka, during the Juvenile Breast Carcinoma (Secretory Breast Carcinoma) crisis (circa 2079).

---

## 1. THE MANDATORY ASK (BODILY AUTONOMY)
*   **The Rule:** No medical professional, parent, or family member may touch the girls' bodies without a verbal request and a verbal/physical "Yes" from the child.
*   **Implementation:**
    *   "Simi, I need to check your vitals. Is it okay if I put this cuff on your arm?"
    *   If the child says "No" or "Not yet," the procedure is paused for a minimum of two minutes to allow for emotional regulation.
*   **Saga Context:** This is a direct response to Klára's history of childhood abuse and her determination that her daughters will always be "the boss of their own bodies."

## 2. THE CIRCLE OF INCLUSION (SISTERLY BOND)
*   **The Rule:** Klárka Pavlínka is never excluded from the medical narrative unless it is physically impossible or Simonka explicitly asks for privacy.
*   **Implementation:**
    *   Klárka is present for the "Consent to Touch" exams.
    *   Medical explanations given to Simonka are repeated to Klárka in age-appropriate language.
    *   The "Tighten" hug is recognized by parents as a valid medical-emotional stabilizer.

## 3. NATURALISM AND PRIVACY
*   **The Rule:** The girls' comfort with shirtless naturalism (from their ballet training) is protected but never forced.
*   **Implementation:**
    *   During exams of the torso, the girls may remain shirtless if they feel more comfortable, rather than being forced into clinical gowns that feel "alien."
    *   In hospital settings, Simonka is allowed to wear her own dance shorts under gowns to maintain a link to her "normal" identity as a dancer.

## 4. THE "MIKE KEY" LEGACY OF PROTECTION
*   **The Rule:** Doctors are vetted not just for skill, but for their willingness to respect the family's autonomy-first approach.
*   **Implementation:**
    *   Dr. Jelena (Pediatrician) and Dr. Marek (Surgeon) are chosen because they adapt to the "Ask" protocol immediately.
    *   Any professional who treats the child as an "object" or speaks over them is replaced.

## 8. MANAGING FRAGILITY (OI TYPE 1 PROTOCOL)
*   **The Condition:** Both girls have **Osteogenesis Imperfecta Type 1**. Their bones are more susceptible to fractures from impact or pressure.
*   **The "Tighten" Hug (Emotional vs. Physical):**
    *   The "Tighten" hug is the family's signature emotional anchor.
    *   **The Physical Rule:** Despite the name, the hug is a *psychological* tightening rather than a physical squeeze. It is a slow, steady embrace where the participants lean into each other without applying sudden or sharp pressure to the ribs or spine.
    *   It is an act of **intentional, careful pressure**—a physical confirmation of "I have you" that respects the fragility of the bone.
*   **Ballet Adaptation:**
    *   At **Pražská Baletní Akademie**, the girls' training (and Klárka’s teaching) avoids high-impact jumps (*grand allegro*) that risk stress fractures.
    *   Focus is placed on *adagio* (slow, controlled movements), *barre* work for stability, and core strength to provide a muscular "shield" for the skeleton.
*   **The Blue Sclera:** The girls' eyes have the characteristic blue tint in the whites (sclera) common in Type 1 OI. This is viewed by the family as a "mark of the tribe," a visual connection between the sisters.
*   **Pharmacological Regimen:**
    *   To manage bone density and reduce fracture risk, the girls follow a strict medication protocol: **Fosamax Plus D** and **Actonel**.
    *   This is supplemented with **Calcium** to support bone mineralization.
    *   Administration follows strict protocols for oral bisphosphonates (e.g., remaining upright after taking Fosamax) which has become a routine part of their morning alongside Aleksander's pancakes.
