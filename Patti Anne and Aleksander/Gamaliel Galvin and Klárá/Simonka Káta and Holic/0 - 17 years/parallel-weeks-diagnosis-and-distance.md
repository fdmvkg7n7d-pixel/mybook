# SCENE OUTLINE: THE PARALLEL WEEKS (DIAGNOSIS AND DISTANCE)

## SCENE INFORMATION
- **Characters:** Gamaliel Galvin Key, Klára, Klárka Pavlínka (10), Simonka Káta (9), Dr. Marek (Pediatric Oncologist)
- **Locations:** Prague (Hospital and Ballet Academy), Cape Town (South Africa).
- **Date:** The week following the Crete vacation (Summer, circa 2079).
- **Status:** Outline

---

## STORYLINE 1: SIMONKA’S APPOINTMENT (PRAGUE)

### 1. The Hospital Environment
- Gamaliel takes Simonka to a specialized pediatric clinic in Prague. The atmosphere is professional but child-friendly.
- **Bodily Autonomy:** True to her upbringing, Simonka enters the exam room with a sense of agency. She is comfortable with her body and expects to be consulted.

### 2. The Examination with Dr. Marek
- **The Ask:** Following Gamaliel’s lead, Dr. Marek is careful to ask Simonka’s permission before any physical contact. "Simonka, may I examine the area where you found the stone?"
- **The Discovery:** The doctor confirms the firm, mobile mass. He uses terms like "Secretory Breast Carcinoma" but explains it in a way that is honest but not terrifying. He emphasizes that because she found it so early (thanks to her comfort being shirtless), the prognosis is excellent.
- **The Tests:** Simonka undergoes an ultrasound and a fine-needle aspiration. She remains calm, holding Gamaliel’s hand, treating it like a "science mission" for her own body.

### 3. Gamaliel’s Strength
- Gamaliel remains the "calm anchor." He records the doctor’s notes for Klára and handles the logistics of the upcoming biopsy with a steady hand, only allowing his worry to show when Simonka is in another room.

---

## STORYLINE 2: KLÁRKA AT SUMMER CAMP (PRAGUE ACADEMY)

### 1. Focused Energy
- At the Ballet Academy, Klárka (10) throws herself into the intensive summer program.
- Her usual grace is sharpened by a fierce, restless energy. She is the first in the studio and the last to leave.
- She uses the discipline of dance to manage her anxiety about Simonka. Every pirouette is a way to stop her mind from spinning.

### 2. The Weight of the Secret
- Klárka doesn't tell the other students about Simonka. She carries the knowledge like a shield.
- During breaks, she is seen sitting by the window, looking toward the part of the city where the hospital is, her face a mask of concentration.
- When she finally gets a video call from Simonka, the "protective leader" mask slips, and she just becomes a worried sister, asking a thousand questions about the "stone."

---

## STORYLINE 3: KLÁRA’S STRUGGLE (CAPE TOWN)

### 1. The Agony of Distance
- In Cape Town, Klára is physically present for her commitment (teaching/consulting), but her heart is in Prague.
- She is constantly checking her phone for updates from Gamaliel. The time difference (though small) feels like an ocean.

### 2. Facing the Vertical World
- Cape Town’s geography—the looming presence of Table Mountain and the sheer coastal cliffs—is a constant trigger for her acrophobia.
- She feels "exposed" and "unsteady" in more ways than one. The height of the mountain mirrors the "height" of the crisis her family is facing.
- She finds a quiet spot near the harbor—where the land is flat and the water is horizontal—to have her emotional breakdowns and her long calls with Gamaliel.

### 3. The Resolution to Return
- Receiving the news from the first appointment—the confirmation of the carcinoma—Klára feels a surge of clarity.
- She completes her duties with a professional steeliness, but her every thought is focused on the flight home. She is no longer just a "frightened woman"; she is a mother returning to her child's battle.

---

## FAMILY CONNECTION
- Despite the three different locations, the family is tethered by technology and a shared language of trust.
- The week ends with a four-way video call where they "hug" through the screen, reinforcing the bond that was solidified by the tight embrace at the front door.

---

## SAGA CONNECTIONS
- **Bodily Autonomy:** Reinforces the "Simonka's Body, Simonka's Choice" theme.
- **Legacy of Resilience:** Shows the second generation (Gamaliel and Klára) using the tools they learned from their own parents (Patti Anne, Mike, Terri Anne) to handle a crisis.
- **Medical History:** Adds the "Secretory Breast Carcinoma" arc to the Key family’s ongoing medical narrative.
