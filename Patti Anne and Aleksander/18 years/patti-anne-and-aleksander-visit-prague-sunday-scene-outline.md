# Patti Anne and Aleksander Visit Prague — Sunday Scene Outline

## Scene Outline

### Arrival at the Airport (Afternoon / Early Evening)
- Patti Anne and Aleksander land in Prague after a long flight from Canada.
- They are tired but excited. Patti Anne is particularly emotional about seeing her son (Gamaliel) after the distance.
- Gamaliel and Klára are waiting at arrivals (or have arranged a car/driver). Big hugs, tears from Patti Anne, warm but more reserved greeting from Aleksander.
- First impressions of Prague for Patti Anne and Aleksander (the architecture, the language, the feel of the city). Klára acts as informal translator/guide where needed.
- They drive or take transport to Gamaliel and Klára's home or the academy.

### Settling In / First Hours at the Family Home or Academy (Around 6 PM Parallel)
- While elsewhere in the city Holic is arriving to collect Good Girl and the other owner begins discussing the breeding deal with Bruno in the background, Patti Anne and Aleksander are being shown around Gamaliel and Klára's Prague life.
- Tour of the apartment/home, or a quick stop at the ballet academy (Mike Key Studio or the full academy).
- **Klárá starts crying in Patti Anne's arms**: During the initial tour of the home or right after arriving and showing them around, Klárá suddenly breaks down. She clings to Patti Anne, sobbing — the weight of the distance, the new life they've built, old foster memories, or the simple relief of having her foster mother there in their space hits her hard. Patti Anne holds her tightly, stroking her hair, murmuring the same kind of reassurances she used when Klárá was a child. Gamaliel watches quietly, moved. Aleksander stands nearby supportively but gives the women space.
- **Klárá tells Patti Anne about the memories and the call from Kent**: While still in Patti Anne's arms and crying, Klárá begins to remember what her father did to her. She tells Patti Anne about the memories in a raw, broken way. She also reveals that Kent Institution called them about her father dying in the prison hospital. This is heavy news — her abuser is gone. Patti Anne continues to hold her, absorbing the information and offering comfort. The moment deepens the emotional reunion.
- Klára shows them the studios; Gamaliel talks business side (students, growth, finances).
- Patti Anne asks questions about daily life, safety, how they are managing as a couple living together openly now.
- Small gifts from Canada are exchanged (perhaps something meaningful from the Key family, or practical items for their new life).

### Family Conversation / Dinner
- Evening meal (home-cooked or at a local spot).
- Conversation topics:
  - Updates from Canada (other family members, Mike's legacy, the twins if timeline fits).
  - How Gamaliel and Klára are doing in Prague — the academy, their relationship, integration into Czech life.
  - Patti Anne and Aleksander's impressions of the city and the life the couple has built.
  - Subtle references to the past (the disclosure of their relationship, Mike's blessing and gift, the move abroad).
- Klára may share a bit about her own adjustment (from foster child in Canada to co-director in Prague), still emotional from earlier and the news she just shared with Patti Anne.
- Gentle humor or warmth about the distance: "You came all the way from Canada just to see our little studio..."

### Private Conversation: Aleksander and Gamaliel (Very Deep)
- At some point during the evening (perhaps after dinner, while Patti Anne and Klárá are talking or resting, or during a walk), Aleksander pulls Gamaliel aside for a private, serious talk.
- **Topic**: Simonka Káta's decision to become Holic's pet.
  - Gamaliel shares what he knows or has observed about Simonka's choice (her involvement in the club, the pet dynamic, the relationship with Holic).
  - The conversation is very deep and emotional. Gamaliel expresses concern, confusion, or protective instincts — perhaps drawing parallels (subtly or directly) to how the family once protected Klárá, or his own past experiences with secrets and unconventional paths.
  - Aleksander listens intently, offers grounded wisdom, asks probing but caring questions. He may reference his role in the family, the importance of consent and choice (echoing earlier family dynamics with Klárá), or the challenges of loving someone who chooses a difficult or controversial path.
  - They discuss the implications for Simonka, for the people around her (including any connections to the academy or Prague life), and how Gamaliel is processing it as someone who has already walked unconventional roads (his conversion to Judaism, building the academy with Klára).
  - Tone: Intimate, father-son level of honesty. No judgment from Aleksander, but deep concern and guidance. Gamaliel opens up in ways he might not with Patti Anne or Klárá.
- **During the conversation, Gamaliel calls Klárka Pavlînka and Raphael**: While Aleksander and Gamaliel are deep in this talk, Gamaliel takes a moment to call Klárka Pavlînka and Raphael. He tells them that Patti Anne and Aleksander are visiting from Canada and that they should come over if they can. The call is quick but warm — Gamaliel wants the Prague circle to meet the Canadian family while they're here.
- This happens roughly in parallel with the dog events (the breeding proposal discussion happening in another part of the city the same evening).

### Klárka Pavlînka and Raphael Arrive
- Shortly after Gamaliel's call (still the same evening, same location), Klárka Pavlînka and Raphael arrive quickly at Gamaliel and Klára's home.
- They are excited and warm to see Patti Anne and Aleksander. Quick introductions and hugs. They bring a small gift or something thoughtful (e.g., local Prague items or something tied to the academy).
- **Klárka Pavlînka cries in Patti Anne's arms**: Soon after arriving and the initial greetings, Klárka Pavlînka breaks down emotionally and cries in Patti Anne's arms. The moment mirrors Klárá's earlier breakdown — perhaps triggered by the warmth of the Canadian visitors, the sense of extended family, or her own feelings about the Prague circle and the people they care about. Patti Anne holds her with the same steady comfort she gave Klárá. This creates a powerful parallel of vulnerability and maternal comfort on the same evening.
- Conversation flows naturally: questions about the flight, first impressions of Prague, how long they're staying, and updates on life in Canada vs. Prague.
- Klárka Pavlînka and Raphael help make the visit feel more connected to the local community. They may reference knowing Gamaliel and Klára's life here, or share light stories.
- The arrival adds energy and extends the family-style gathering without making it feel crowded. They stay for a while, overlapping with the ongoing visit.
- This keeps everything happening in the same place and on the same day as the rest of the events (including the parallel dog activities and the deep Aleksander-Gamaliel talk).

### Barbecue Outside: Raphael, Gamaliel, and Aleksander
- Later the same evening (still Sunday, same home in Prague), the group moves into a more relaxed gathering. While some stay inside (Patti Anne, Klára, and Klárka Pavlînka), Raphael, Gamaliel Galvin, and Aleksander go outside.
- **They make a barbecue for the family**: The three men prepare and cook a barbecue together — grilling, setting up, and handling the food for everyone. It's a casual, hands-on activity that creates space for conversation.
- **Raphael talks with Gamaliel Galvin and Aleksander**: While barbecuing, Raphael joins in the ongoing conversation. Building on the earlier private talk between Gamaliel and Aleksander, the discussion continues or expands with Raphael present. They talk about life in Prague, the visit from Canada, family connections, and deeper topics.
- **Wishing that Simonka Káta could join them**: During the talk and while cooking, the men express that they wish Simonka Káta could be there with them for the barbecue and the visit. It comes up naturally — perhaps in the context of the earlier conversation about her decision to become Holic's pet, or simply as a desire for the full circle of people they care about to be present. The wish is wistful, affectionate, and carries the weight of the day's events and the parallel dog-world activities happening elsewhere in the city. Gamaliel and Aleksander's prior deep discussion informs how they speak about it; Raphael adds his perspective.
- This outdoor scene provides contrast to the emotional indoor moments (the crying in arms) and keeps the men engaged in a practical, bonding activity while the family visit continues. Everything remains on the same Sunday evening in the same location.
