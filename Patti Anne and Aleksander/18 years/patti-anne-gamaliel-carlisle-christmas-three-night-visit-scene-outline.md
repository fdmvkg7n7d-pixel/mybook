# Patti Anne and Gamaliel Galvin Visit the Carlisle Mansion for Christmas — Three-Night Stay — Scene Outline

> **Scope:** Patti Anne Key and her young son **Gamaliel Galvin** visit **Mike and Terri Anne's Carlisle mansion** on Christmas Day and stay over for three nights. This is a separate Patti Anne branch scene, distinct from Jessica Isabella and Emma's Vancouver / Whistler Christmas arc and distinct from Kiley Rose's Natal Christmas.
>
> **Time:** Christmas Day through the morning after the third night. Exact year TBD, aligned with the wider scattered-family Christmas arc.
> **Place:** Mike and Terri Anne's **Carlisle mansion**, Ontario.
> **Characters present:** Patti Anne Key, Gamaliel Galvin, Mike Key, Terri Anne Williams-Key. Other Carlisle household members TBD depending on the exact year.
> **Mood:** Homecoming, eldest-daughter steadiness, Christmas warmth, the comfort of the original Key family home.

---

## 1. Christmas Day Arrival at Carlisle

- **The arrival:** Patti Anne arrives at the Carlisle mansion with Gamaliel Galvin on Christmas Day. The long driveway, the gates, the familiar stone of the house, and the winter Ontario air make this feel like a return to the family center.
- **The mansion:** Carlisle is fully decorated for Christmas — garland, lights, the kitchen warm, the big tree, presents, the kind of abundance Mike and Terri Anne create when even part of the family is coming home.
- **Patti Anne's role:** Patti Anne enters the house as the eldest daughter, not as a guest. She reads the room immediately, sets down bags, checks on Terri Anne, and begins helping before anyone asks.
- **Gamaliel's role:** Gamaliel brings immediate child energy into Carlisle. He runs toward the tree, asks about presents, and makes the enormous mansion feel alive in the particular way only a small child can.

---

## 2. The Three-Night Stay

- **The structure:** Patti Anne and Gamaliel stay for **three nights**. This is not a brief Christmas drop-in. They unpack, settle, and become part of the house rhythm.
- **The room:** Terri Anne has prepared a room for them — warm bedding, space for Gamaliel's things, small comforts placed exactly where Patti Anne will need them.
- **Patti Anne and Terri Anne:** Patti Anne helps in the kitchen, with gifts, with meals, with whatever needs doing. Terri Anne notices and gently reminds her she is allowed to be daughter, not only helper.
- **Patti Anne and Mike:** Mike is quietly glad to have his eldest daughter home. Their conversations may be practical on the surface — travel, schedules, meals, family logistics — but underneath is the old father-daughter anchor of the Key family.
- **Gamaliel at Carlisle:** Gamaliel explores the mansion under loving supervision, treating Carlisle like a Christmas kingdom. His presence gives Mike and Terri Anne a grandparent rhythm for the holiday.

---

## 3. Christmas Day at the Kitchen Table

- **The kitchen table:** The Carlisle kitchen table remains the emotional center of the house. Patti Anne, Gamaliel, Mike, and Terri Anne gather there across the day.
- **Patti Anne's feeling:** Patti Anne is aware that the family is scattered this Christmas — Jessica and Emma's branch is in Vancouver/Whistler, Kiley Rose's branch is in Natal, others may be elsewhere. Being at Carlisle carries both comfort and ache.
- **Gamaliel's innocence:** Gamaliel does not experience the holiday as scattered. He experiences it as Christmas at his grandparents' house. That innocence is a gift to the adults.

---

## 4. Connection to the Scattered Family

- **The calls/messages:** Over the three nights, Patti Anne and Gamaliel connect with other branches through phone calls, video calls, photos, or messages.
- **Jessica and Emma's branch:** Patti Anne knows Jessica Isabella, Emma, Samira Chaka, and Sahara Hannan are in Vancouver for Catherine's grave and then Whistler for presents. She understands the emotional weight of that trip.
- **Kiley Rose's branch:** Patti Anne also knows Kiley Rose and her girls are staying in Natal. Their absence is not treated as distance; it is another branch of the family doing Christmas where they need to be.
- **The scattered-family theme:** Carlisle, Whistler, Vancouver, and Natal all hold different pieces of the Key family Christmas.

---

## 5. Closing Image

- **The third night:** On the final night of their stay, Patti Anne looks in on Gamaliel sleeping in the room Terri Anne prepared. The house is quiet, the Christmas lights still glowing downstairs.
- **Patti Anne's realization:** Carlisle is no longer the place where everyone can always gather at once, but it is still the family root.
- **Final beat:** Patti Anne stands in the hallway of the Carlisle mansion — eldest daughter, mother, guest and not-guest — and understands that coming home for three nights still matters, even when the family is spread across the world.
