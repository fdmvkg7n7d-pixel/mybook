# Scene Outline: Mike and Terri Anne Take Gamaliel Galvin and Klárá to the Hospital to Meet Palmer Peter and Salena Sarai

## Placement

**When:** Shortly after **Palmer Peter** and **Salena Sarai** are born. Gamaliel Galvin is thirteen. Klárá is in the same age band and is still a child in Patti Anne and Aleksander's household, already loved as their oldest daughter but still carrying old foster-child reflexes.

**Where:** Hospital maternity / postpartum room; the drive there with Mike and Terri Anne; hallway / elevator outside the room.

**Characters:** Mike Key, Terri Anne Williams-Key, Gamaliel Galvin, Klárá, Patti Anne, Aleksander, newborn Palmer Peter, newborn Salena Sarai, nurses / hospital staff lightly referenced.

---

## Scene Purpose

1. Show **Mike and Terri Anne taking Gamaliel Galvin and Klárá to the hospital** so they can see **Palmer Peter and Salena Sarai for the first time**.
2. Establish Klárá's first meeting with the newborn twins as a family-rights moment, not a visitor moment.
3. Show **Patti Anne and Aleksander letting Klárá hold Palmer Peter**.
4. Let Klárá be genuinely surprised that she is allowed to hold Palmer Peter, because part of her still expects newborn-baby privileges to belong only to blood family.
5. Have Klárá become very emotional.
6. Have Patti Anne immediately hug Klárá and tell her that she is part of the family and has every right to hold **Palmer Peter** or **Salena Sarai**.

---

## Core Emotional Throughline

A newborn hospital room is one of the most intimate family spaces there is. Klárá understands that instinctively. Because of her history, she assumes there must be an invisible line: Patti Anne's biological baby, Aleksander's biological baby, Gamaliel's half-siblings, Mike and Terri Anne's grandchildren — and then Klárá, standing carefully at the edge, grateful to be allowed to look.

Patti Anne and Aleksander refuse that line.

When they let Klárá hold Palmer Peter, the gesture tells her something the family has already been saying in other ways: she is not a guest, not a tolerated foster child, not an extra child standing near the real children. She is part of the family. She is the oldest daughter in the household. She has every right to hold her newborn brother or sister.

---

## Scene Structure

### Beat 1: Mike and Terri Anne Come to Take Them

- Mike and Terri Anne arrive to take **Gamaliel Galvin and Klárá** to the hospital.
- They are careful and cheerful, treating both children as equally expected members of the hospital visit.
- Gamaliel is excited and nervous in a thirteen-year-old way: trying to be mature, trying not to show too much, already carrying the responsibility of being the older brother.
- Klárá is quieter. She wants to go, but she is careful with her wanting.
- Mike and Terri Anne do not frame the trip as "Gamaliel seeing his siblings and Klárá coming along." They frame it as both of them going to meet the babies.

### Beat 2: The Drive to the Hospital

- In the car, Mike and Terri Anne talk in practical, calming ways:
  - hospital rules;
  - quiet voices;
  - clean hands;
  - Patti Anne may be tired;
  - the babies are very small;
  - nobody has to hold them unless they want to.
- Klárá listens closely to the rules because rules make uncertain rooms survivable.
- Gamaliel may ask a direct question about whether he is allowed to hold one of the babies.
- Klárá does not ask the same question, even if she wants to know. Asking feels too risky.

### Beat 3: Outside the Hospital Room

- In the hallway or just before entering, Terri Anne notices Klárá becoming very still.
- Mike may quietly remind both children that Patti Anne wants to see them.
- The threshold matters: Klárá is about to enter a room where the family has just grown by two.
- She is afraid, without naming it, that the family growing could somehow prove her place is smaller.

### Beat 4: First Sight of Palmer Peter and Salena Sarai

- Patti Anne is in the hospital bed / chair, tired and radiant in the exhausted way of a mother who has just given birth.
- Aleksander is near her, holding one baby or standing close to both bassinets.
- **Palmer Peter** and **Salena Sarai** are tiny, bundled, and real.
- Gamaliel's excitement becomes awe.
- Klárá looks at the babies from a careful distance, overwhelmed by how intimate the room feels.

### Beat 5: The Family Greets Them

- Patti Anne greets Gamaliel and Klárá together.
- Aleksander does the same.
- There is no hesitation around Klárá's place in the room.
- Mike and Terri Anne stay present, protective, and proud, watching the household meet its newest members.
- The scene should show that Mike and Terri Anne taking both children to the hospital was an intentional act of family inclusion.

### Beat 6: Patti Anne and Aleksander Let Klárá Hold Palmer Peter

- Patti Anne and Aleksander decide that Klárá can hold **Palmer Peter**.
- They do not treat it like a special exception or a favour.
- The practical setup is careful:
  - Klárá sits down first;
  - a pillow / blanket supports her arms if needed;
  - clean hands;
  - someone helps place Palmer Peter safely in her arms.
- Klárá is visibly surprised.
- Her surprise is not because she does not want to hold him. It is because she did not think she had that right.

### Beat 7: Klárá Holds Palmer Peter

- Palmer Peter is placed in Klárá's arms.
- Klárá becomes very still, then very emotional.
- She looks down at him and understands that the family is trusting her with one of its smallest, newest people.
- The weight of the baby is small, but the meaning is enormous.
- Gamaliel watches closely, moved by the sight of Klárá holding his newborn brother.
- Mike and Terri Anne see the emotional truth of it immediately.

### Beat 8: Patti Anne Immediately Hugs Klárá

- Klárá becomes very emotional — tears, overwhelmed silence, or a whispered disbelief that she is allowed.
- Patti Anne immediately responds.
- Because Klárá is holding Palmer Peter, the hug is careful and protective: Patti Anne may lean in beside her, wrap an arm around her shoulders, hold her face, or hug around the baby without crowding him.
- Patti Anne tells Klárá clearly and directly:
  - she is part of the family;
  - she has every right to hold Palmer Peter;
  - she has every right to hold Salena Sarai too;
  - she is not a visitor in this room.

### Beat 9: Patti Anne's Words

Possible core line:

> You are part of this family. You have every right to hold Palmer Peter or Salena Sarai.

Possible fuller version:

> Klárá, sweetheart, listen to me. You are part of this family. You have every right to hold Palmer Peter. You have every right to hold Salena Sarai. You are their sister.

- The wording should be plain, not poetic.
- Patti Anne should not make Klárá explain why she is crying.
- Patti Anne names the belonging before Klárá has to ask for it.

### Beat 10: Aleksander's Support

- Aleksander supports Patti Anne's words without making the moment about himself.
- He may say something simple and paternal:

> Of course you can hold him.

> You are his sister too.

> When you are ready, you can hold Salena Sarai as well.

- His inclusion matters because Palmer Peter and Salena Sarai are also **his** children. He is confirming Klárá's place from the father's side of the newborn twins' family.

### Beat 11: Gamaliel's Reaction

- Gamaliel sees Klárá holding Palmer Peter and crying.
- He may not have the words yet for why the moment matters, but he understands that something important has happened.
- He stays close without taking over.
- If he is later handed Salena Sarai or Palmer Peter, the scene can briefly mirror the sibling line: both older children are trusted with the newborns.

### Beat 12: Closing Image

- Closing image options:
  - Klárá seated with Palmer Peter in her arms, Patti Anne's arm around her shoulders, Aleksander nearby, Gamaliel watching, Mike and Terri Anne at the edge of the room.
  - Salena Sarai sleeping nearby while Patti Anne tells Klárá she has every right to hold either twin.
  - Mike and Terri Anne looking at each other, quietly understanding that this is exactly why they brought both children to the hospital.
- The final feeling should be: a hospital room where the family has grown by two babies and, for Klárá, has grown deeper around her too.

## Key Dialogue Possibilities

### Patti Anne

> Klárá, sweetheart, you can hold him.

> You are part of this family.

> You have every right to hold Palmer Peter or Salena Sarai.

> You are their sister.

### Aleksander

> Of course you can hold him.

> Sit down first. We will put him right in your arms.

> When you are ready, Salena Sarai is yours to hold too.

### Klárá

> I can?

> I thought — I thought only family could.

> He is so small.

### Gamaliel Galvin

> That's Palmer Peter?

> She's holding him really carefully.

### Mike

> We brought both of you because both of you belong here.

### Terri Anne

> New babies need older hands around them. Yours count, Klárá.
