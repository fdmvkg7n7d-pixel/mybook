# Klára Asks Patti Anne for Her Mother’s Stuff — Scene Outline

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Klára Key (eight years old), Patti Anne Key, Gamaliel Galvin Key (eight years old)
- **Offstage / referenced characters:** Mike Key, Terri Anne Key, Andrea Jennifer, Ashley Erin, Klára's mother, Klára's father, the Police / Investigators, the Crown Prosecutor
- **Location:** Patti Anne's mansion (Vaughan, Ontario) and Mike and Terri Anne's cottage (Northern Ontario). Note: Both the mansion and the cottage are located in the province of Ontario.
- **When:** July, for the weekend (the month after Klára moves in in June, and before school starts in September)
- **Status:** Updated scene outline
- **Tone:** Tender, emotional, delicate, trauma-informed, hopeful, serene cottage setting, protective boundaries

---

## SCENE PURPOSE

This scene shows Klára taking a brave, critical step in her healing process. Having moved into Patti Anne's household in June, they are spending a quiet weekend in July visiting Mike and Terri Anne's cottage in Northern Ontario. 

Mike and Terri Anne are away in **Los Angeles with Andrea Jennifer and Ashley Erin for the weekend**, but because **they generously let their children use this cottage any time they want**, Patti Anne, Gamaliel, and Klára are using the peaceful place for some summer quiet over the weekend. 

The outline captures the emotional prelude to this trip: **Three days before they leave, Patti Anne tells Klára that they will pick Gamaliel Galvin up from Victoria Ballet Academy's summer camp and then all go to the cottage together.** Klára becomes intensely emotional, believing that as a foster child, she is not allowed to go or doesn't belong on a family getaway. Because Klára lives in Ontario, Patti Anne gently explains that **she is already living in Ontario and this cottage is also in Ontario**, making the trip a simple and safe car ride within their own province.

Once they arrive, surrounded by the peaceful nature of the north, Klára starts to realize she is indeed safe, welcome, and part of the family. She works up the courage to ask Patti Anne if she can have her mother's personal belongings ("her mother's stuff"). 

However, because her father abused her and was arrested, **the police have seized Klára’s childhood house as a crime scene/evidence.** Klára does not have her mother's items because they are locked inside the sealed house, and **she doesn't know where her father hid her mother's stuff.** 

Patti Anne must protect Klára from the trauma of dealing with the crime scene. She promises Klára that **she will contact both the police and the prosecutor herself to request, search for, and recover her mother's belongings from the seized house.** This scene establishes Patti Anne’s fierce protective advocacy and promises the future retrieval of the precious keepsake—the late mother's beautiful delicate necklace—before Klára wears it on her first day of Grade Three in September.

## SCENE ARC / BEATS

### Beat 0: Three Days Before — The Announcement and the Foster Child Fear
- Three days before the trip, inside Patti Anne's mansion in Vaughan, Ontario, Patti Anne gathers Klára to talk about their plans for the weekend. 
- Patti Anne's warm, affectionate nickname for Gamaliel Galvin is **"Mr. little man"**. 
- Before talking to Klára, Patti Anne has to call out to her son to get his things together because they are in a rush to get him to camp:

> “Mr. little man, you should get ready because we are running late to take you to the summer camp.”

- Once Gamaliel is dropped off at Victoria Ballet Academy's summer camp for his session, Patti Anne returns to sit down with Klára. She explains the upcoming weekend plan: **she and Klára will drive back over to the camp on Friday to pick Gamaliel up, and then all three of them will head straight up to her parents' cottage.**
- Klára, however, goes completely pale and silent. She slowly backs away toward the hallway.
- Patti Anne notices her reaction and follows her gently. When she asks what is wrong, Klára bursts into tears and becomes incredibly emotional.
- She cries and confesses her deep, painful assumption:

> “I can’t go. Foster kids aren't allowed to go to family cottages. What if they stop us at the border? My last house... they left me behind with a babysitter when they went to their cabin. I don't belong at your parents' cottage.”

- Patti Anne's heart breaks. She kneels down immediately, pulling Klára into a tight, reassuring hug. 
- She tells Klára with absolute, fierce certainty, pointing out the simple geography to comfort her:

> “Under my roof, we do everything together. You are not a visitor, and you are not left behind. This is your family now. And sweetheart, look at me: we are living in Ontario, and this cottage is in Ontario too. There are no borders to cross, just a beautiful, quiet drive. We are picking up Mr. little man from his camp on Friday, and then we are heading up. My parents' cottage is just as much yours as it is Gamaliel's. You are coming with us.”

- It takes Klára those three days to fully digest this, but she packs her bags, feeling a quiet bloom of anticipation about picking up Gamaliel and heading north.

### Beat 1: A Quiet July Afternoon and a Game of Uno
- It is a warm, peaceful July afternoon during their weekend stay at Mike and Terri Anne’s cottage in Northern Ontario.
- Mike and Terri Anne are in Los Angeles supporting Andrea Jennifer and Ashley Erin for the weekend. Because they allow their children to use the cottage whenever they wish, Patti Anne has brought Gamaliel and Klára here to enjoy the quiet solitude of the lake and pine woods over the weekend.
- On the living room floor, Patti Anne, Gamaliel Galvin, and Klára are sitting on a braided rug, **playing a gentle game of Uno** together.
- The colorful cards—reds, blues, greens, yellows—are scattered on the rug between them. Gamaliel plays enthusiastically, grinning as he calls out "Uno!" when he gets down to his last card and holds it up with a dramatic flourish. Patti Anne laughs and says:

> “Good job, Mr. little man!”

- Klára is playing too, but she is exceptionally quiet, holding her cards closely against her chest, watching Patti Anne's face and hands as cards are dealt and drawn. The initial fear of not belonging has softened into a quiet, tentative peace.

### Beat 2: Working Up the Courage
- Once the game of Uno winds down to an end, Gamaliel Galvin runs off to the screen porch with his toy, completely content to play in his own world.
- The cabin living room becomes completely still. Klára remains sitting on the braided rug, slowly sorting her left-over Uno cards into neat little color piles.
- Patti Anne notices how quiet she is, remains sitting on the floor with her, keeping herself small, open, and unthreatening.
- Klára approaches hesitantly, setting her neat piles of cards down on the rug, her fingers twisting the hem of her shirt, her voice barely a whisper.
- She asks the question she has been carrying:

> “Patti Anne? Do you... do you have my mother's stuff?”

- She explains that her father had locked her mother's things away inside their old house. She know her father was taken away by the police, and she is terrified that her mother's memory has been locked away forever, thrown out, or lost.

### Beat 3: The Hidden Items and the Seized House
- Klára whispers what she knows, revealing a heartbreaking roadblock:

> "The police put tape on the door. They locked the house. No one can go inside... and I don't know where my father hid her stuff. He locked it away, and he never let me see it."

- Patti Anne feels a profound ache of empathy and anger at what Klára's father did, but she keeps her voice entirely steady, warm, and comforting for Klára.
- She explains to Klára that even if her father hid the items, they are not gone, and they will be found.

### Beat 4: Klára Describes the Necklace
- With a small, soft voice, Klára tells Patti Anne about her mother's most precious piece:

> “She had a necklace. It was so beautiful and delicate... she wore it all the time. It had a little silver pendant. I just... I really want her necklace.”

- Hearing Klára describe the necklace with such quiet longing strengthens Patti Anne's resolve.

### Beat 5: Patti Anne's Promise to Contact the Police and Prosecutor
- Patti Anne kneels facing Klára on the braided rug, taking her small hands.
- She promises Klára that she will protect her from the house, the police, and the legal system, taking the burden of recovery entirely on herself:

> “Sweetheart, you are never going to have to go back to that house, and you don’t have to search for anything. I am going to call the police and the prosecutor myself. I will ask them to find and release your mother's stuff, and I will make sure they search every hiding spot until they find her necklace and bring it home to us.”

- Klára looks up, surprised and relieved. Hearing that Patti Anne will deal directly with both the police and the lawyers/prosecutors makes the terrifying legal wall feel small and manageable.

### Beat 6: Reassuring Her Place in the Family
- Patti Anne reasurres her that her mother’s things belong to her by law and by heart.

> “Those are your things, Klára. Your mother's memory belongs to you. I will fight the police, the prosecutor, and anyone else to make sure you get them.”

- For Klára, hearing an adult promise to use her power to retrieve her mother's memories is a massive emotional shift.
- She breaks down and cries, but it is a soft, healing release. Patti Anne holds her close on the cabin floor, letting her cry as the sound of the wind through the pines fills the silence.
- Klára touches her bare collarbone, imagining the weight of her mother's delicate necklace resting there, a physical promise of what is to come.

### Beat 7: Closing Image
- A little while later, Gamaliel Galvin comes back inside from the porch.
- He stops when he sees Klára sitting on the cabin floor next to their unfinished game of Uno, looking tearful but lighter than she has all summer.
- He doesn't make a big deal out of her tears; instead, he smiles warmly, sits back down on the rug near the cards, and holds up a smooth lake stone.
- Klára smiles back at him, her hand resting against her chest where her mother's necklace will soon be. For the first time since June, surrounded by the quiet woods of Northern Ontario, she knows she has an advocate who will bring her mother's memory home to her.
