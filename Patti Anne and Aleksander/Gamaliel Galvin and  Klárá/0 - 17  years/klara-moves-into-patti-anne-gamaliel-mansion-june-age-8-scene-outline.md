# Klárá Moves Into Patti Anne and Gamaliel Galvin’s Mansion  
**When:** June (Gamaliel Galvin and Klárá are eight years old)  
**Structure:** Two main sections – (1) Klárá & Patti Anne (Friday–Sunday), (2) Mike drops Gamaliel Galvin off Sunday evening

## Scene 1: Patti Anne Welcomes Klárá (Friday Afternoon)

- Klárá arrives at the mansion carrying a small suitcase and a backpack. She looks very nervous — shoulders hunched, eyes darting around the grand entrance hall.
- As soon as she steps inside, Klárá bends down and begins removing her running shoes and then her socks.
- Patti Anne watches for a moment, then gently asks, “What are you doing, sweetheart?”
- Klárá pauses and answers quietly, “My previous foster mother said all her foster daughters had to take their socks off at the front door.”
- Patti Anne kneels down facing Klárá and tells her gently, “Underneath my roof, no child is required to remove her socks to walk on these floors.”
- Klárá starts crying gently after hearing these kind words.
- After Klárá calms down, Patti Anne takes her hand and shows her the bedroom prepared for her, right next to Gamaliel Galvin’s. She also shows Klárá the private bathroom attached to her bedroom.
- Klárá starts crying again when she sees the room. She explains that she had to share a bedroom with three other girls before.
- While Patti Anne continues the tour of the mansion, Klárá asks about going to school for the rest of the year.
- Patti Anne gently explains that her social worker and she both want Klárá to wait until September before returning to Victoria Ballet Academy.
- After the tour, they go into the kitchen together to make dinner: homemade pizza.
- After a moment of silence, Klárá asks in a small voice, “Where’s Gamaliel Galvin?”
- Patti Anne kneels down to Klárá’s eye level and explains kindly that Gamaliel Galvin is visiting his grandfather Mike for the weekend and will be back Sunday evening.
- Klárá nods but still looks uncertain. Patti Anne reassures her that Gamaliel Galvin knows she’s moving in and is very happy about it.
- Patti Anne takes Klárá’s suitcase and leads her upstairs to the bedroom prepared for her right next to Gamaliel Galvin’s. She shows Klárá the new bedding, desk, and art supplies she bought for her.
- Klárá remains quiet and observant, asking only practical questions at first. Patti Anne gives her a gentle tour of the house, emphasizing that Klárá can make any part of it feel like home.

## Revised Timeline Note
Gamaliel Galvin has been staying with Mike for the weekend. Mike drops him off at the mansion on **Sunday evening**. Klárá moves in during Gamaliel Galvin’s absence (Friday–Sunday).

## Scene 2: Mike Drops Gamaliel Galvin Off – Sunday at 1:00 p.m.

- On Sunday at one o’clock, Mike drops Gamaliel Galvin off at the mansion because Kiley Rose and Julia need him on Monday. Gamaliel Galvin is excited to be home and runs inside ahead of Mike.
- Inside, Gamaliel Galvin immediately starts telling Patti Anne and Klárá how much fun he had with Mike. He describes swimming, eating barbecue food, and playing **Jenga** (the game where a tower is built from bricks and players take turns carefully pulling one brick from the middle and placing it on top without making the tower fall).
- Patti Anne meets them at the door. She is calm but clearly emotional.
- Gamaliel Galvin immediately senses something is different. He asks where Klárá is.
- Patti Anne gently tells him that Klárá has moved in and is already upstairs in her new room.
- Mike stays for a short conversation in the foyer. He asks Gamaliel Galvin how he feels about Klárá living with them permanently.
- Gamaliel Galvin (eight years old) is direct and protective: “She’s my best friend. She should live here.”
- Mike admits he feels guilty that he and Terri Anne couldn’t take Klárá themselves. Gamaliel Galvin reassures him in his own childlike but wise way.
- Mike promises to visit often and bring Klárá’s favorite pastries.
- Final beat: Gamaliel Galvin runs upstairs. He finds Klárá’s door slightly open. He knocks softly, then steps inside. Klárá is sitting on her new bed, looking small and uncertain. Gamaliel Galvin smiles and says something simple like “You’re here now.” He sits beside her. They don’t say much more — just sit together in comfortable silence as the sun sets.

## Tone & Key Emotional Beats
- Gentle, protective, slightly melancholic but ultimately hopeful.
- Focus on chosen family and quiet safety.
- Eight-year-olds’ dialogue should feel natural and age-appropriate while still carrying emotional weight.
