# Good Girl — Middle of the Three Hell Days — Scene Outline

## Placement

**When:** The middle of the second day (approximately 36 hours into the three-night arrangement).  
**Where:** The other owner’s mansion — specifically the large holding area and adjacent rooms where the twenty male pets are kept.  
**Continuity:** Takes place during the three nights Good Girl was given to the other owner.

---

## Characters

- **Good Girl (Simonka Káta / GG)**: Deeply traumatized, physically broken, and emotionally shattered.
- **The Other Owner**: Calm, manipulative, and deliberately cruel in his psychological tactics.
- **The Twenty Male Pets**: Present and active throughout the days and nights.
- **Handlers**: Several men overseeing the pets.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1: The Physical Toll

Good Girl lies on the rubber mat in the corner of the holding area. Her body is covered in bruises, bite marks, and handprints. One of her wrists is visibly swollen and likely fractured from being pinned too hard during the night. Her knees are raw and bleeding from hours on the floor. She trembles uncontrollably.

She has not eaten properly. Her stomach cramps from the absence of her medication. Without **Fosamax Plus D** and **Actonel with Calcium**, her bones feel fragile and achy. Without **the pill**, a low, constant nausea sits in her gut. Her head pounds. She feels feverish and weak.

Every movement sends sharp pain through her body. She has stopped trying to crawl. She can barely lift her head.

### Beat 2: The Emotional Breakdown

In the brief lull between groups of pets, something inside her finally snaps.

A raw, broken sob escapes her throat — the first real sound she has made in hours. Her shoulders shake violently as she curls into herself as much as her injuries allow. Tears stream down her face and drip onto the mat.

She is beyond exhaustion. Beyond pain. She is in full despair.

There is no fight left in her. Only hopelessness.

She whispers the same broken phrase over and over, so quietly only the mat hears it:

> “I can’t… I can’t anymore…”

### Beat 3: Numb Dissociation

After the breakdown, something shifts.

Her eyes go glassy. Her breathing slows. The trembling stops. She stares at a fixed point on the floor, completely still. The world around her becomes distant and muffled. The sounds of the other pets, the handlers’ voices, even the pain in her body all feel far away.

She has dissociated.

In this numb state, she is no longer fully present. She is somewhere else — somewhere quiet where none of this is happening to her. Her body continues to be used, but she is no longer inside it.

### Beat 4: The Other Owner’s Manipulation

At one point during the second day, the other owner approaches her while she is lying in her corner between sessions.

He crouches down so he is at her level and speaks in a calm, almost conversational tone:

> “You should know… Holic didn’t want to work with me. That’s why he agreed so quickly to send you here. He didn’t want any part of what I offered him. He didn’t abandon you. He simply refused me.”

He says this several times over the course of the days — always in the same measured voice.

Good Girl, even in her broken state, clings to these words like a lifeline.

She knows the other owner is trying to hurt her. But the words give her something terrible and precious at the same time:

**Holic didn’t choose this man.**  
**Holic didn’t want to collaborate with him.**  
**Holic didn’t willingly hand her over to someone he respected.**

In the middle of her hell, this small, twisted comfort is the only thing keeping the last pieces of her from completely shattering.

She repeats it silently in her mind like a mantra:

> *Holic didn’t want to work with him… Holic didn’t want to work with him…*

Even as another pet mounts her, even as her body screams in pain, even as she floats in numb dissociation — she holds onto that one sentence like it is the only truth left in the world.

---

## Emotional Core

- **Despair & Hopelessness**: She has reached the absolute bottom. There is no light, no end in sight, and no way to stop it.
- **Physical Destruction**: Broken bones, exhaustion, medication withdrawal, and constant pain.
- **Psychological Manipulation**: The other owner weaponizes the truth about Holic’s refusal to work with him, turning it into both a wound and a twisted comfort.
- **Dissociation**: Her mind has begun to detach as a survival mechanism.

### Beat 5: 10:00 PM — Holic Storms the Mansion

At exactly ten o’clock on the second night, the front doors of the mansion are thrown open with violent force.

Holic Bartunek storms inside.

He is not calm. He is not composed. His usual courtly demeanor is gone. His face is dark with fury, his coat still open from the drive, and his voice cuts through the entire mansion like a blade:

> “Where is she?”

The handlers freeze. The other owner steps out of the main room, surprised for the first time.

Holic does not wait for an answer. He moves through the mansion with terrifying purpose, following the sound of movement until he reaches the large holding area.

What he finds stops him for half a second.

Good Girl is on the floor in the corner — naked, bruised, shaking, and barely conscious. Her body is covered in marks. One wrist is clearly swollen. Her eyes are glassy and unfocused. She looks nothing like the composed, obedient pet he left in the parking lot.

For the first time in a very long time, something cracks in Holic’s expression.

He crosses the room in three strides, drops to one knee beside her, and carefully — almost gently — lifts her chin so he can see her face.

Good Girl’s eyes slowly focus on him.

In that moment, the dissociation shatters.

A broken, wet sound escapes her — half sob, half relief. Tears spill down her face as she tries to reach for him with her uninjured hand.

Holic’s gaze drops to her mouth.

Without a word, he reaches behind her head and unbuckles the gag that is still strapped in place. He removes it slowly, carefully. Good Girl’s jaw aches as it is finally freed. A thin string of saliva clings to the bit before it falls away.

She gasps for air, her lips trembling.

Holic looks at her — really looks at her — and his voice is low, rough, and filled with something no one has ever heard from him before:

> “Simonka Káta…”

He says her real name like it hurts him.

> “I’m sorry. I’m so sorry for this.”

His hand cradles the back of her head with surprising gentleness as he continues, voice tight:

> “I should never have let you go. This is my fault.”

Good Girl’s eyes fill with fresh tears. Her voice is hoarse, broken, and shaking with emotion as she forces the words out:

> “Holic… I already forgave you.”

She reaches up with her uninjured hand and weakly clutches the front of his shirt, her fingers trembling.

> “I forgave you the moment you walked through the door. I knew you didn’t abandon me. I knew you would come.”

Her voice cracks completely on the last word. She begins to cry harder, overwhelmed, her body shaking in his arms.

Holic freezes.

For several seconds, he simply stares at her — visibly stunned. He had expected anger, fear, or even hatred. He had braced himself for her to reject him. Instead, she is offering him forgiveness before he has even asked for it. Something shifts in his expression — a rare, deep surprise mixed with something almost like pain.

Before he can respond, a furious voice cuts through the room.

The other owner steps forward, his face twisted with rage.

> “You ungrateful little bitch,” he snarls. “After everything we gave you, you still crawl back to him like a dog?”

His voice rises, shaking with fury.

> “She was mine for three nights! You had no right to take her back!”

Holic slowly lifts his head and looks at the other owner. His voice is quiet, but lethal:

> “She was never yours.”

> “We are done. You will never touch her again.”

The other owner opens his mouth, but Holic cuts him off with a single, lethal glance.

> “If you or any of your pets come near her again, I will destroy you.”

Without another word, Holic carefully gathers Good Girl into his arms — treating her broken body with a tenderness no one in the room has ever seen from him — and carries her out of the mansion.

She clings to him weakly, her face buried against his chest, trembling so hard her teeth chatter.

Holic does not take her back to his mansion.

Instead, he drives straight to the **Collegium Club’s private medical facility**. He carries her inside personally and demands to see the club’s doctor immediately.

The doctor, an older man who has treated many human pets, prepares the examination table and asks Holic to place her on it.

Holic gently sets her down.

But before either man can say anything, **Simonka Káta moves on her own**.

Despite her injuries, despite the pain, despite everything she has just endured — she slowly pushes herself up and climbs onto the examination table **on all fours**.

She lowers her chest slightly, presenting herself in perfect pet posture. Her head is lowered. Her breathing is shallow. She has returned to **Good Girl** mode completely on her own.

Both Holic and the doctor freeze.

The doctor’s eyes widen in genuine shock. He has seen many conditioned pets, but never one who, after such extreme trauma, instinctively reverts to full pet presentation without any command.

Holic stares at her, visibly stunned. His hand, which had been hovering near her, slowly lowers. For several long seconds, he cannot speak. The sight of her — broken, exhausted, and still offering herself in the only way she knows how — hits him harder than anything else tonight.

The doctor finally finds his voice, speaking quietly:

> “She… didn’t need to be told.”

Holic’s jaw tightens. His voice is low and rough when he finally answers:

> “I know.”

Holic gently places a hand on her back for a moment, then steps back.

> “I’ll wait outside,” he says quietly to the doctor. “So you can examine her properly.”

He turns to leave the room.

The moment Holic moves toward the door, **Good Girl’s composure breaks**.

A sharp, panicked whimper escapes her. Then she starts crying — not the quiet, broken tears from before, but loud, desperate sobs. Her whole body shakes on the table as she reaches one trembling hand toward him.

> “Holic… please… don’t go…”

Her voice is raw and terrified. She sounds like a lost child.

Holic stops immediately.

He turns back, visibly conflicted. For a second, he looks at the doctor, then back at Good Girl, who is now crying harder, her shoulders heaving.

Without another word, Holic walks back to her side. He places his hand gently on the back of her neck — the same grounding touch he has used so many times before.

> “I’m not going anywhere,” he says softly. “I’m staying right here.”

Good Girl’s sobs quiet slightly, though tears continue to fall. She presses her forehead against the table, visibly relieved.

The doctor watches them for a moment, then nods once and steps forward to begin the examination.

After carefully checking her injuries, taking her vitals, and noting the signs of severe dehydration and medication withdrawal, the doctor steps back and speaks quietly but firmly:

> “She’s in rough shape. She needs fluids immediately. I’m going to start an IV.”

He prepares the IV line and inserts the needle with practiced care. Good Girl barely reacts, too exhausted to flinch. Once the IV is running, the doctor looks at Holic.

> “She’s severely dehydrated and her body is in withdrawal. I strongly recommend she stays here for at least a couple of days under observation. We can monitor her wrist, manage the pain, and slowly reintroduce her medications.”

Holic doesn’t hesitate.

> “She stays,” he says. “As long as she needs.”

He looks down at Good Girl, who is still on all fours on the table, and gently strokes her hair.

> “You’re safe now,” he murmurs. “I’m not leaving you.”

After a moment, he adds quietly:

> “I’ll come in the morning to check on you. I want to see how you’re doing.”

The doctor nods and steps out to prepare a room. Holic stays beside her for a few more seconds, then gently removes his hand from her back.

Good Girl slowly pushes herself up. Despite her exhaustion and the IV line still attached to her arm, she carefully climbs down from the examination table and drops to all fours.

Without looking at Holic or the doctor, she begins crawling out of the examination room on her own.

Holic watches her in silence, visibly surprised once again.

Good Girl crawls down the corridor until she reaches the club’s medical holding area — a long room lined with **twenty individual cages**. Currently, **seven female pets** and **eight male pets** occupy the cages. Four of the female pets are visibly pregnant, resting quietly in their enclosures.

Good Girl moves slowly past several cages until she finds an empty one near the end of the row. She crawls inside, turns around once, and curls up on the padded floor, her body still trembling slightly. She makes no sound. She simply lies there, waiting.

As Holic walks through the front doors with her in his arms earlier, the other owner’s final lie is exposed in the most brutal way possible:

**Holic came for her.**

He did not abandon her.

And now everyone — especially Good Girl — knows the truth.
