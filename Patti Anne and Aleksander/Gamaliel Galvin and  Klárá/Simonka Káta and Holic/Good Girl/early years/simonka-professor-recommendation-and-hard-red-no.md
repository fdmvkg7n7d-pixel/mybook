# SIMONKA’S PROFESSOR RECOMMENDATION AND THE HARD RED NO

## Placement

**When:** Friday evening.

**Where:** Holic Bartunek's mansion, Prague. The study, then the foyer.

**Continuity Lead-in:**
- Wednesday: The Squeeze Chute session (GG + 6 pets) and the two-hour encounter with Bruno in the run, witnessed by Simonka's professor.
- Simonka has received word from her advisor about the second master's proposal.

---

## Scene Purpose

This combined scene documents Simonka informing Holic of her academic success and her definitive "hard red" refusal of the breeding proposal, immediately followed by an encounter with the path she rejected.

---

## Emotional Core

The scene is about **Alignment and Perimeters**. Simonka secures her academic path while drawing a firm biological boundary: pregnancy is her **hard red no**. Holic’s uncertainty is resolved by her clarity. The arrival of the pregnant pet serves as a visceral confirmation of the "red" line, while Simonka explains that if she ever has a family, she wants that family to come out of love — not pet-space, not club politics, not money, not an owner’s breeding arrangement.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1 — The Notification
Simonka meets Holic in the study on Friday evening. She delivers the news: her professor is willing to recommend that the committee accept her second proposal. She shares that he was "very impressed" with the field site and the quality of the stillness he witnessed on Wednesday.

### Beat 2 — The Baby Proposal
Holic shifts the conversation to the breeding request from Bruno's owner and the $50,000 offer from the third owner for a puppy. He admits to Simonka that he doesn't know what to do, caught between the commercial valuation and the ethical weight.

### Beat 3 — The Hard Red No
Simonka tells Holic: **"It is my hard red no."** She defines the creation of a child as the absolute limit of her participation in the human-pet dynamic. She frames it as the point where the roleplay ends and reality begins. She explains that she does want the possibility of family in her life someday, but only **out of love** — from a relationship, a choice, a future she enters as Simonka, not as a pet being discussed by owners. A child cannot be a club outcome, a transaction, a prize, or a "puppy" owed to anyone.

### Beat 4 — Holic’s Acceptance
Holic immediately accepts her "red." The uncertainty is gone; he rejects the money and the other owners' requests, affirming that her boundary is the final word.

### Beat 5 — The Knock at the Door
The conversation is interrupted by a heavy knock. Holic and Simonka move to the foyer.

### Beat 6 — The Pregnant Pet
Another owner has arrived with two human pets—one of whom is visibly and heavily pregnant, and another male pet in role. As the humans discuss club business, Simonka stands in her university clothes, looking at the pregnant pet kneeling on the floor. It is the immediate, physical manifestation of the choice she just made.

### Beat 7 — GG Takes the Pregnant Pet
Without any command from Holic, Simonka shifts into GG-form.
- **The Action:** GG takes the pregnant pet immediately in front of the owners and the male pet.
- **The Dynamics:** This is a spontaneous, assertive, role-driven act. It demonstrates GG's "professional" status and her total internalisation of her role. By moving without a command, she proves that she knows exactly what is expected of her in this hierarchy, even as she has just refused pregnancy for herself.
- **The Witness:** The male pet and the visiting owner watch, as does a silent, observant Holic.

### Beat 8 — Leading to the Kennel and Run
Holic gives a short, sharp gesture or command, then turns back to the visiting owner.
- **The Action:** GG leads the other pets (the pregnant pet and the male pet) through the mansion and out to the kennel and the run.
- **The Humans:** Holic and the visiting owner move to the living room to continue their discussion, leaving the pets to settle into the enclosure. They do not follow the pets outside.

### Beat 9 — The Brutal Encounter
Once in the run, away from the owners' sight, the dynamic shifts.
- **The Action:** The male pet takes GG very brutally.
- **The Dynamics:** This is a harsh, unobserved physical display of power within the pet hierarchy. Without the immediate presence of the owners to moderate the behavior, the encounter reaches a level of brutality that tests GG's "professional" composure to its limit.
- **The Contrast:** GG endures the brutality in the silence of the run, while in the living room, the humans speak of club politics and "care-architecture." 
- **The Witness:** Only the pregnant pet watches from the corner of the run.

## Prose Expansion

The study was cool, the air filtered through the heavy velvet curtains that blocked out the late afternoon Prague sun. It was Friday, and the mansion felt charged with the residual energy of a week that had seen the academic world collide with the household’s private rituals.

Simonka stood by the window. She was dressed in her university clothes—a modest skirt and a navy cardigan—but the thin leather collar was snug against her neck, a reminder of the grueling session she had held in the squeeze chute earlier that week on Wednesday and the two hours she had spent with Bruno, all under her professor’s watchful gaze.

"The professor sent his decision," Simonka said. Her voice was steady, the academic register she had spent years perfecting. "He is willing to recommend that the committee accept the second proposal."

Holic looked up from his files. His expression didn't change, but his eyes tracked the movement of her hands. "Then the hours in the thing on Wednesday were worth the time. He saw the quality of the stillness. He had no choice but to recommend it."

"He said it was academically significant," Simonka added. "He thinks it will be an excellent thesis."

"It will be," Holic said simply. He leaned back in his chair. "But there is something else, Simonka. Something that occurred while your professor was touring the grounds, and which the other owner brought to me during the pickup on Sunday."

Simonka felt a prickle of intuition. "The breeding proposal?"

"Yes," Holic said. "Bruno’s caretaker wants the mating to become a permanent arrangement. He wants you pregnant with Bruno. But there is more. A third owner—the one who brought the six pets for the squeeze chute—has made a concrete offer. He views you as a premier professional. He offered fifty thousand dollars for the right to secure the resulting puppy."

The silence that followed was heavy. Simonka looked at Holic, her analytical mind already calculating the variables. 

"Fifty thousand dollars," she whispered. "For a puppy."

"I told them it was your decision," Holic said. He stood up slowly and walked toward her. For a brief moment, the owner’s mask slipped, and Simonka saw a flicker of genuine uncertainty in his face. "But I don't know what to do, Simonka. I don't know if this is the next step for us, or if it is the line we don't cross."

Simonka met his gaze. She thought of her family, of the "liquid gold" bond her aunt Fallon had with Lÿsa Mandy, and of the thesis she was trying to write.

"It is my hard red no, Holic," Simonka said.

Holic stopped. The use of the safeword—not as a cry of distress, but as a statement of philosophical and biological finality—stilled the room. 

"Red?" he asked, his voice low.

"The hardest red," Simonka replied. "I will be your pet. I will hold the stillness for four hours. I will be busy with Bruno in the run while the academic world watches. I will even wear your initials on my skin. But my body's future... the life of a child... that is the stop. That is where the roleplay ends and the reality begins."

She swallowed, then forced herself to say the fuller truth, because Holic had taught her that a boundary without the truth beneath it could be mistaken for fear.

"I am not saying I never want a family," she said. "I might. Someday. I want that possibility to belong to me. If I have a child, if I build a family, I want it to come out of love. Not because an owner offered money. Not because the club thinks GG is valuable. Not because someone wants a puppy from me. A family has to come from Simonka. From love. Not from this."

Holic watched her for a long time. He reached out and touched the leather of her collar, his thumb tracing the curve of her throat. 

"Then the decision is made," he said. The uncertainty was gone, replaced by the quiet authority of a man who had received the only data point that mattered. "The fifty thousand dollars is irrelevant. The third owner will be told no. Bruno's caretaker will be told no. Your red is the only rule in this house tonight."

Simonka allowed herself to breathe, her shoulders lowering a fraction of an inch. She was still collared, still his pet, and her thesis was still moving forward.

The moment of quiet finality was broken by a heavy, resonant knock at the front door.

Holic’s expression sharpened, returning to the impenetrable mask of the owner. "Stay in this register for a moment longer," he directed. He walked toward the foyer, with Simonka following a few paces behind, still standing upright in her student clothes.

He opened the door to reveal another owner. The man was accompanied by two figures who clearly belonged to pet-space. One was a standard human pet, but the other caught Simonka’s breath. She was a pregnant pet, her advanced term impossible to miss even beneath her simple shift. She moved with a slow, heavy deliberation, her body entirely occupied by the physical reality of the child she was carrying.

The visiting owner greeted Holic with a formal nod. "Holic. I have a message from the Circle regarding the Spark incident."

Holic stepped aside to let them in. "Come into the foyer."

As the humans spoke, Simonka stood perfectly still. She looked at the pregnant pet, who was now kneeling on the stone floor. This was the "puppy" the third owner had offered fifty thousand dollars for. 

Without any command from Holic, not even a tilt of his head, Simonka moved. She dropped her university bag and her cardigan on the floor, the "Simonka" part of her vanishing by her own choice.

In a sharp, spontaneous display of her professional role, GG took the pregnant pet immediately, right there in the foyer, in front of Holic, the visiting owner, and the other male pet. 

The scene was silent except for the sounds of the encounter and the low, business-like murmurs of the two men as they continued to discuss the Spark incident. Holic watched with a quiet, impenetrable intensity, noting his pet's unbidden initiative. The visiting owner paused for a fraction of a second, his eyebrows lifting in recognition of GG's caliber, before continuing his report. The male pet watched with a fixed, empty gaze. 

GG's movements were precise and commanding, her "natural skills" on full display. She did not wait for permission because she no longer needed it; she was the master of her own submission. Even as she handled the physical reality of the other pet's advanced term, she remained centered in her own "hard red no" boundary. She was proving, to Holic and to the visiting owner, that a "professional" knows her place without being told.

A short, sharp gesture from Holic signaled the next phase. GG rose and, with a silent authority, led the visiting pets through the mansion and out to the kennel and the run. Behind them, Holic and the visiting owner turned away, walking toward the living room to continue their discussion in a more comfortable setting, the heavy oak doors clicking shut behind them.

Once inside the iron-fenced enclosure, away from the presence and the potential moderation of the humans, the hierarchy shifted with a jarring speed. Without preamble, the male pet took GG, his movements very brutal—a harsh, unadorned display of physical dominance that pushed GG to the very edge of her endurance.

GG held the role. She did not cry out, did not attempt to shift away, and did not use her safewords. She absorbed the brutality with the same terrifying composure she had shown in the squeeze chute, her eyes fixed on the gravel of the run. In the silence of the night, the only witness was the pregnant pet, who stood in the corner of the run, a heavy, quiet observer of the cost of the floor.

Sealed beneath the role, Simonka could feel the bite of the gravel against her knees and the staggering weight of the male pet, but her mind remained anchored in the decision she had made in the study. In the living room, the men were likely discussing the "academically significant" nature of her life, while out here, she was simply proving she could survive it. She was a professional. She was a pet. And she would hold the line in the dark, even if no one was watching to see her do it.

Seeing it—the literal weight of it—sent a cold shiver down Simonka’s spine. She had just drawn her line in the dirt, and the world had immediately sent a witness to show her exactly how much it cost to cross it.
