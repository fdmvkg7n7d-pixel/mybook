# Holic Gives Good Girl to the Other Owner for Three Nights — Scene Outline

## Placement

**When:** Immediately following the arena psychological test and invitation question.  
**Where:** The private parking lot outside the Collegium’s pet park and arena complex, then the other owner’s mansion.  
**Continuity:** Direct continuation of the events in `holic-administers-psychological-test-to-gg-scene-outline.md`. Picks up the moment Holic and Good Girl exit the arena.

---

## Characters

- **Holic Bartunek**: Good Girl’s primary owner and steward. Calmly agrees to the request.
- **Good Girl (Simonka Káta / GG)**: Still in deep pet space after the arena experience. Her agency is overridden.
- **Bruno’s Owner**: The man who makes the initial request in the parking lot.
- **The Other Owner**: The man who wants his own human pet to breed with Good Girl. Receives her for the three nights.
- **The Other Owner’s Pet**: The male human pet chosen to breed with Good Girl during the three-night stay.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1: The Parking Lot Request

- Holic leads Good Girl out of the arena through a side exit into the private members’ parking area.
- Bruno’s owner is waiting beside a dark blue box truck with Bruno on a lead.
- After brief pleasantries, Bruno’s owner makes his request:

  > “Would you consider allowing her to come home with us for three nights? Bruno would have her exclusively.”

- Holic is silent for a moment, then answers without hesitation:

  > “Yes. You may take her for three nights.”

- Good Girl trembles at the immediate agreement. Holic hands her show lead to Bruno’s owner and gives her a final instruction:

  > “Be good for him, Good Girl.”

- Holic gets into his limousine alone and drives away, leaving Good Girl behind.

### Beat 2: Into the Box Truck — The Gag (The Dark Moment)

- Bruno’s owner does not take Good Girl to his own vehicle.
- He leads her to the rear of the box truck.
- Inside the padded compartment, he fits a thick black leather gag with a large silicone bit into her mouth and fastens the straps.
- The gag is tight and absolute — Good Girl cannot form any intelligible words. Any attempt at speech is reduced to muffled, wet, incoherent sounds.
- He chains her collar to a metal ring on the wall.
- The roll-up door is pulled down, plunging the interior into near darkness.
- The truck engine starts. Good Girl is transported gagged and restrained.

**The Dark Moment** occurs the instant the final buckle of the gag snaps shut. In that second, her ability to speak her safewords is not merely difficult — it is physically impossible. The one power she has always retained, even in the deepest pet space, has been taken from her. She is being delivered, silent and without recourse, into a three-night breeding arrangement she cannot refuse or stop.

### Beat 3: Arrival at the Other Owner’s Mansion

- After nearly an hour, the truck arrives at a large, elegant private mansion.
- The roll-up door opens. Bruno’s owner removes the gag and leads Good Girl out.
- Waiting at the top of the steps is **the other owner** — the man who previously proposed a breeding arrangement.
- Beside him, on all fours and collared, stands **his own human pet** — the male pet he intends to breed with Good Girl.
- Bruno’s owner hands over Good Girl’s lead:

  > “She’s yours for the three nights. Holic has agreed.”

- The other owner accepts the lead with satisfaction:

  > “My pet will have her. That is the arrangement.”

- He leads Good Girl up the steps and into the mansion. His own pet follows closely beside her.
- The heavy doors close behind them.

### Beat 4: The Twenty Pets (The True Scale)

- Once inside the mansion, Good Girl is led through the main hall and down a wide corridor.
- At the end of the corridor are two large double doors. The other owner opens them.
- Inside is a spacious, dimly lit room that has been converted into a large holding area.
- Twenty collared male human pets are present — all of them waiting.
- They are not chaotic. They are organized, leashed in pairs or small groups, under the watch of several handlers. Some are already aroused at the sight of her.
- Good Girl stops crawling.

- The moment she sees them, something inside her fractures.

- Her breath catches sharply behind the lingering ache of the gag. Her ears ring. For several long seconds she simply stares, frozen on her hands and knees, as the full, horrifying reality settles over her.

- She was not sent here for one pet.

- She was sent here for **twenty**.

- A deep, visceral wave of fear and grief crashes through her. Her shoulders begin to shake. Tears spill down her cheeks without sound. The pure, trusting part of her that had tried to remain in pet space shatters completely. In its place is a raw, aching understanding:

  > *Holic gave me away. He left me here. And I have no way to tell anyone that I’m afraid.*

- She lowers her head, her body trembling uncontrollably. A small, broken whimper escapes her throat — the only sound the gag allows her to make.

- The other owner speaks calmly behind her:

  > “They have been waiting for you.”

- Good Girl does not move. She cannot. The weight of twenty pairs of eyes, twenty bodies, and three nights of silence presses down on her until she feels like she might disappear beneath it.

- She has no idea this is a breeding program. She only knows that she has been delivered, voiceless and unprotected, into the hands of twenty pets — and that no one is coming to stop it.

### Beat 5: Withheld Medication

- During the three nights at the other owner’s mansion, Good Girl is **not** given her regular medications.
- The medicines withheld are:
  - **Fosamax Plus D**
  - **Actonel with Calcium**
  - **The pill** (oral contraceptive)
- No doses are administered, and no explanation is offered.
- This deliberate omission increases both the physical and psychological risk of the breeding arrangement, leaving Good Girl without the medical protections she normally receives under Holic’s care.

## Emotional & Psychological Notes

- Good Girl experiences a sharp shift from Holic’s structured stewardship to being handed over to a different owner and a different pet.
- The gag and box truck transport emphasize her complete loss of control and the transactional nature of the arrangement.
- The revelation that she is being sent specifically to breed with the other owner’s pet (not Bruno) creates a new layer of psychological weight.
- **Critical element**: Good Girl cannot say her safewords for the entire three-night period. The physical gag prevents speech during transport, and the terms of the arrangement with the other owner remove her ability to safeword once she arrives. This creates significant emotional and psychological tension.
- **Additional risk**: The deliberate withholding of her medications (Fosamax Plus D, Actonel with Calcium, and the pill) adds a layer of medical vulnerability during the breeding-focused stay.
- The scene plants direct tension for future developments around the breeding proposal, consent, and Simonka Káta’s eventual discussion with Holic.
