# Holic Meets the Club President — Scene Outline

## Placement

**When:** Late at night, shortly after leaving Good Girl at the club’s medical facility (around 11:30 PM on the second night of her ordeal).  
**Where:** The private office of the Club President at the Collegium.  
**Continuity:** Takes place immediately after Holic storms the other owner’s mansion and takes Good Girl to the doctor.

---

## Characters

- **Holic Bartunek**: Furious, protective, and demanding justice.
- **The Club President**: A powerful, older man who oversees the Collegium’s rules and discipline.
- **Good Girl (Simonka Káta / GG)**: Central to the conflict, though not physically present.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1: Holic’s Arrival

Holic does not wait to be announced. He storms past the secretary and pushes open the heavy double doors to the Club President’s office without knocking.

The President, an elegant man in his late sixties, looks up from his desk, surprised by the intrusion.

Holic’s coat is still open, his expression dark with barely contained rage. He does not sit.

### Beat 2: Holic Tells the President What Happened

The President studies him for a moment before speaking calmly.

> “Holic. I assume this is about the situation with your pet.”

Holic’s voice is low, cold, and shaking with fury as he begins to speak.

> “They took her from the parking lot. Bruno’s owner put a gag in her mouth so she couldn’t speak. So she couldn’t use her safewords. Then he chained her in the back of a box truck and drove her to the other owner’s mansion.”

He takes a step closer to the desk, his hands clenched.

> “They kept her there for two days. They had **twenty** male pets use her. They withheld her medication — Fosamax Plus D, Actonel with Calcium, and the pill. They broke her wrist. They broke her feet. They covered her body in bruises. They exhausted her until she could barely move.”

Holic’s voice grows rougher.

> “She was gagged for hours. She couldn’t say no. She couldn’t safeword. She was in pain, sick from the withdrawal, and they kept using her anyway.”

He leans forward, eyes burning.

> “My very gorgeous pet. My Simonka Káta. They treated her like disposable meat.”

He pauses, then continues with a different kind of intensity in his voice.

> “When I took her to the club’s doctor, she was barely conscious. The doctor told her to stay on the examination table so he could examine her. Instead, she got off the table on her own. She crawled out of the room. She crawled all the way to the medical holding area — the one with the twenty cages. There were already seven female pets and eight male pets inside. Four of the female pets are pregnant.”

Holic’s voice grows quieter, almost shaken.

> “She crawled into one of the empty cages and curled up on the floor like she belonged there. She didn’t wait for permission. She just… went.”

The President is silent for a long moment, clearly affected by the description.

Holic’s final words are heavy:

> “Even after everything they did to her, she still went back into a cage on her own. That’s what they reduced her to.”

The Club President is quiet for a long moment. Then he speaks with measured gravity:

> “This matter will be addressed at the next club meeting. Both owners will be summoned. The disciplinary committee will hear everything.”

Holic stares at him, still furious, but gives a single, sharp nod.

> “Make sure they do.”

### Beat 3: Holic’s Ultimatum

Holic’s next words are direct and merciless:

> “I want both their heads.”

The room falls silent.

Holic continues, voice like steel:

> “I want the other owner expelled. I want Bruno’s owner expelled. I want both of their memberships revoked. I want their names struck from every record. And if the club will not do it… I will handle it myself.”

He leans forward, both hands on the President’s desk.

> “They endangered something that belongs to me. They put my pet’s life and health at risk. That is not something I will forgive.”

### Beat 4: The President’s Reaction

The Club President leans back in his chair, studying Holic carefully. He has known Holic for years and has never seen him this personally invested in a pet.

After a long moment, he speaks:

> “You have never made such a demand before. Not even when other pets were mistreated.”

Holic’s reply is immediate and raw:

> “None of them were mine.”

The President nods slowly, understanding the weight of those words.

> “I will convene the disciplinary committee first thing in the morning. Until then, the other owner is banned from all club facilities.”

Holic straightens, his fury still simmering just beneath the surface.

> “That’s not enough. But it’s a start.”

He turns to leave, then pauses at the door.

> “If anything happens to her while she’s recovering… I will hold this club responsible.”

Without waiting for a reply, Holic walks out, slamming the door behind him.

## Emotional Core

- **Holic’s Protectiveness**: His fury is not just about rules being broken — it is deeply personal. Good Girl is precious to him.
- **Escalation**: This scene marks a major turning point in how seriously the club will treat the incident.
- **Holic’s Vulnerability**: His willingness to threaten the club itself shows how much Good Girl means to him.
