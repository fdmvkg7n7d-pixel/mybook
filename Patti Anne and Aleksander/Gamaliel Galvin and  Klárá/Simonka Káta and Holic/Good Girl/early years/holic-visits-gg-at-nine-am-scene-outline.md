# Holic Visits Good Girl at Nine AM — Scene Outline

## Placement

**When:** The next morning at exactly 9:00 AM.  
**Where:** The Collegium Club’s private medical facility — specifically the medical holding area with the cages.  
**Continuity:** Takes place the morning after Holic stormed the other owner’s mansion and took Good Girl to the doctor.

---

## Characters

- **Holic Bartunek**: Protective, concerned, and still simmering with anger.
- **Good Girl (Simonka Káta / GG)**: Recovering in one of the cages, physically broken and emotionally fragile.
- **The Club Doctor**: Present during the visit.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1: Holic’s Arrival

At precisely 9:00 AM, Holic walks into the medical facility. He is dressed more casually than usual — no jacket, sleeves rolled up — but his expression is serious. He moves with purpose, clearly having not slept much.

He goes straight to the medical holding area without asking for directions.

### Beat 2: Finding Good Girl

When Holic enters the medical holding area, he does not find her in any of the cages.

Instead, he is directed toward the recovery run — a large, enclosed space attached to the medical facility. 

Inside the run, Good Girl is on all fours with **another male pet**. The male pet is being gentle with her, staying close but not mounting her. Good Girl’s body is still covered in bruises. Her wrist and both feet are wrapped in bandages. An IV line is attached to her arm, the bag hanging from a portable stand that moves with her. She looks exhausted, but she is moving.

She lifts her head the moment she senses Holic.

Her eyes meet his.

### Beat 3: Good Girl’s Reaction

Good Girl does not make a sound at first. She simply stares at him, wide-eyed. Then, very slowly, her expression softens with visible relief. Her shoulders relax slightly.

She remains in the run with the other pet for a moment, then slowly begins crawling toward Holic.

Good Girl crawls over to the edge of the run for some **praise**.

Holic stops at the fence. He looks down at her, then gently reaches through the bars and pats her chest with slow, steady strokes — the same grounding gesture he has used with her many times before.

> “You are a very magnificent female pet,” he says quietly.

Good Girl closes her eyes and leans into the touch, visibly comforted.

### Beat 4: Simonka Káta Speaks

After a moment, Good Girl lifts her head. Her expression shifts slightly. When she speaks, it is **Simonka Káta’s** voice — hoarse, tired, but clear.

> “Holic… can you take me home this morning? As Good Girl?”

She looks up at him with quiet hope.

Before Holic can answer, the **assistant doctor** steps forward and speaks gently but firmly:

> “I’m sorry. The doctor would like her to stay for another night. Just one more night for observation.”

Simonka Káta’s shoulders slump slightly, but she nods in acceptance. She lowers her head again, returning to Good Girl mode as she presses her cheek against Holic’s hand through the bars.

### Beat 4: Holic Speaks

Finally, Holic speaks in a low, rough voice.

> “Good morning, Simonka Káta.”

He does not enter the run. He simply stands at the fence, watching her.

> “I told you I would come.”

Good Girl leans her head slightly toward him, acknowledging his presence even while staying with the other pet. She still doesn’t speak. She simply closes her eyes for a moment, drawing comfort from his voice.

### Beat 5: The Assistant Doctor Explains What Happened

The club doctor approaches Holic, but it is the **assistant doctor** who speaks.

> “The nurse took her out for a walk in the run earlier this morning. There was already another male pet inside. The moment Good Girl entered, he took her immediately. They’ve been together for ninety minutes now.”

A moment later, a nurse brings another pet into the run — a **pregnant female pet** who has **no lower legs or arms**. The nurse carefully places her inside the enclosure. The pregnant pet moves slowly on her stumps, staying close to the edge.

Simonka Káta watches the pregnant pet with visible confusion and concern. She has many questions. The **assistant doctor** notices and steps closer to the fence, speaking to her quietly so only she (and Holic) can hear:

> “Her owner did that to her. He had her lower legs and arms removed. He wanted her to be completely dependent on him. She’s pregnant with his child.”

Simonka Káta’s eyes widen. She looks at the assistant doctor, then back at the pregnant pet, clearly disturbed and full of questions.

Holic remains silent for a moment, watching her reaction. Then he speaks quietly, his voice low and steady:

> “I will never do that to you.”

Simonka Káta turns her head to look at him.

Holic continues, his tone firm:

> “I refuse to do this to you. I prefer my pet to be whole.”

He pauses, then adds something softer — something more personal:

> “I feel something deeper for Simonka Káta than just a pet.”

Simonka Káta stares at him, visibly moved by his words. She doesn’t respond verbally, but her eyes soften with emotion.

Holic remains silent after that, but his jaw tightens. He already knows this story. He has always known. And every time he is reminded of it, the same thought crosses his mind:

**The owner is sick.**

He finds what was done to this woman deeply disturbing — even within the world they live in.

The main doctor adds with genuine surprise in his voice:

> “Most pets in her condition would still be in a cage or sedated. She’s much stronger than she looks.”

Holic does not respond immediately. He remains standing at the edge of the run, one hand resting on the fence, watching Good Girl.

### Beat 6: Holic’s Realization

Holic is visibly surprised by what he is seeing.

After everything Good Girl had endured — the gag, the twenty pets, the broken bones, the medication withdrawal, the trauma — she is still choosing to stay in the run with another male pet. She did not retreat to a cage. She did not shut down. She went back into the pet space on her own.

As he watches her, a heavy realization settles over him.

**Good Girl needs to be in the pet persona for most of her life.**

He thinks about how deeply she responds to the squeeze chute — how calm and present she becomes when she is locked inside it. He thinks about what she did last night: after everything that happened to her, she still got on the examination table as a pet, on all fours, without being told. Then she crawled out of the examination room and into one of the cages on her own.

This is not just a role she plays. It is not something she can easily step out of. After the horror of the past two days, the only way she seems able to function — the only way she can still move and exist — is by remaining deep in pet space.

Holic’s expression grows serious. He understands now, more clearly than ever, that Simonka Káta’s survival may depend on how much time she is allowed to spend as Good Girl.

He stays at the fence a little longer, quietly watching her, the weight of this realization settling in his chest.

## Emotional Core

- **Holic’s Care**: He is visibly affected by her condition and is showing a rare tenderness.
- **Good Girl’s Conditioning**: Even in recovery, she remains in pet space and stays in the cage until told otherwise.
- **Quiet Intimacy**: The morning visit feels like a moment of safety and reconnection after the horror of the previous days.
