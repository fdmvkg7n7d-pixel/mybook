# Holic Administers a Psychological Test to GG at the Pet Park — Scene Outline

## Placement

**When:** [TBD]

**Where:** A **private, members-only pet park** — an outdoor, enclosed facility attached to the Collegium club's grounds. Fenced grass, trees, benches, open sky, and the ambient presence of other club members with their human pets. The setting is not clinical, but it is **not public either**. Every person and pet within the fence belongs to the club.

**Continuity:** Follows the establishment of GG's basic training and precedes or runs parallel to the Elizabethan cone, breeding stand, and show preparation.

## Characters

- **Holic Bartunek:** Owner, administrator, observer.
- **Simonka Káta / Good Girl (GG):** The subject. She arrives at the park in pet space — collar on, on all fours, possibly in minimal clothing or nude depending on the training arc's progression and the park's privacy rules. She is told this is a test. She is not told what kind. The ambiguity is intentional. She must navigate it as GG — or as Simonka, if the test cracks her open. The outdoor setting adds a new layer: she is visible to Holic, to other pets, to other owners — all of them club members who understand exactly what they are seeing. Her exposure is part of the test, but it is exposure to peers, not to strangers.
- **Other Club Members and Their Human Pets:** The park is active with fellow Collegium members — owners sitting on benches, chatting, training, or quietly observing. Their human pets are present on leashes, in bowls, in runs, at water stations, or kneeling beside benches. These are not random observers. They are peers who understand the culture, the rules, the safewords, and the significance of what Holic is doing. Their presence adds **community pressure** rather than public scandal. The judgment here is not "what is that?" but "how well does she do it?"
