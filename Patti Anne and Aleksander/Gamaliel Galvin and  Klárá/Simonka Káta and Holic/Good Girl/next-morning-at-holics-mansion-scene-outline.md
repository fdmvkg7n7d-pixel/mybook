# Next Morning at Holic’s Mansion — Scene Outline

## Placement
- **When:** The morning after Holic brings Good Girl home from the clinic.
- **Where:** Holic Bartunek’s bedroom, then the outdoor area and kennel attached to the mansion where staff can see the morning care.
- **Focus:** Good Girl’s first morning back under Holic’s direct care, waking on the floor beside his bed with a wet diaper, placing her head beside his head, being cleaned outside with cold water where staff can see, being fed outside from her bowls, being placed into the kennel without a diaper, and asking Holic for a man pet for company.

---

## Characters
- **Holic Bartunek** — Good Girl’s owner and steward; controlled, watchful, and quietly protective.
- **Good Girl / Simonka Káta** — Recovering after the trauma; physically sore, emotionally fragile, and still moving in and out of pet space.
- **Mansion Staff** — Present in the background and able to see the outside care routine, but not central to the scene.
- **Possible Man Pet** — An adult male pet who may later be brought in as supervised company for Good Girl.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1: Good Girl Wakes on the Floor
- Good Girl wakes slowly on the prepared bedding on the floor beside Holic’s bed.
- Clean blankets are tucked around her.
- The diaper Holic put on her the night before is wet.
- The wet diaper is treated as a practical recovery issue, not as a spectacle or a humiliation beat.
- Her water bowl and food bowl remain beside the bedding.
- The bedroom door is still partly open.
- The hallway light is still on.
- For several minutes, she remains still and listens to the room.
- Her body aches, especially her wrist and feet.
- She does not try to stand right away.
- She keeps her breathing quiet because she is afraid of disturbing Holic.

### Beat 2: Her Head Beside His Head
- Good Girl slowly lifts her head from the blankets.
- She looks up at Holic’s bed.
- Holic is sleeping near the edge of the mattress, turned slightly toward her side of the bed.
- She crawls only a few inches closer because moving too much hurts.
- She carefully places her head on the edge of Holic’s bed near his pillow.
- Her head ends up beside Holic’s head while the rest of her body remains on the floor.
- The gesture is small, cautious, and deeply vulnerable.
- She is not trying to climb into the bed.
- She is only trying to be close enough to know he is still there.
- One hand remains on the bedding beside her bowls.
- Her head stays resting near his head while she waits.

### Beat 3: Holic Wakes and Pats Her Head
- Holic wakes and notices her head beside his.
- He looks at her without startling her.
- He does not move too quickly.
- He lets the moment stay quiet.
- Good Girl watches him with wide, uncertain eyes.
- She is afraid she has done something wrong by touching the bed.
- Holic understands that she is seeking reassurance, not permission to break a rule.
- He gently pats her head.
- The pat is calm, steady, and reassuring.
- Good Girl stays still under his hand.
- The contact helps her settle before the morning care begins.

### Beat 4: The First Check
- Holic sits up slowly and checks her condition from where he is.
- He looks at her bandages.
- He checks whether she is shaking.
- He notices the bowls are still in place beside her bedding.
- He notices that she has not tried to leave the room.
- He notices that her diaper is wet and needs to be removed.
- He keeps his reaction practical and controlled.
- He does not make the wet diaper into a punishment or shame moment.
- He gives her time to understand that he is going to take her outside and clean her up.

### Beat 5: Taking Her Outside to Clean Her Up
- Holic takes Good Girl outside to the outdoor area attached to the mansion.
- He supports her carefully so she does not have to crawl farther than her injuries allow.
- The morning air is cool and quiet.
- The outdoor space is visible from parts of the house and from the staff paths.
- Mansion staff can see the morning care if they are passing or working nearby.
- The staff do not interrupt.
- The staff do not comment.
- Their visibility makes the routine part of the household order rather than a hidden crisis.
- Holic removes the wet diaper as part of ordinary morning care.
- He cleans her up with cold water, wipes, and a fresh towel.
- The cold water makes the cleanup brisk and uncomfortable.
- The cleanup is handled plainly and non-graphically.
- Good Girl stays compliant but tense.
- Holic keeps the process controlled so she can follow what is happening.
- He checks whether the bandages stayed dry and secure.
- He does not put a clean diaper on her outside.
- She remains without a diaper for the outdoor routine.

### Beat 6: Feeding Her Outside
- Holic keeps Good Girl outside after the cleanup.
- Her food bowl and water bowl are brought outside for the morning feeding.
- The bowls are placed on the ground within easy reach of her.
- The food is soft, plain, and easy for her to manage.
- Water is kept in the second bowl.
- Good Girl eats outside from the bowl after she has been cleaned.
- Holic stays nearby while she eats.
- The staff can see the feeding routine from the house or staff paths.
- The staff remain background witnesses and do not intrude.
- Feeding outside makes the cleanup and feeding part of one continuous morning routine.
- The familiar bowls help Good Girl stay oriented even though she is outside.
- She remains without a diaper while she eats outside.

### Beat 7: Putting Her Into the Kennel
- Holic does not return Good Girl inside after the feeding.
- He takes her to the kennel in the outdoor area.
- The kennel is visible enough that staff can see the routine from a distance.
- He puts Good Girl into the kennel after she has been cleaned and fed.
- He keeps her food bowl and water bowl with her or places them just inside the kennel within reach.
- The kennel becomes her outdoor resting place for the next part of the morning.
- Good Girl watches Holic closely to make sure he has not abandoned her.
- Holic remains controlled and nearby long enough for her to understand the new arrangement.
- The bedroom-floor arrangement from the night before gives way to the outdoor kennel arrangement.
- Good Girl remains without a diaper in the kennel.

### Beat 8: Medication and Settling in the Kennel
- Holic prepares her morning medication according to the clinic instructions.
- Water is available in the bowl inside or beside the kennel.
- He keeps the process clean, quiet, and practical.
- Medication becomes part of the same outdoor recovery routine as cleaning her, feeding her, and placing her into the kennel.
- Good Girl settles in the kennel after the routine is complete.
- She remains tired but less disoriented.
- The staff presence in the background reinforces that the kennel routine is visible and accepted within the mansion household.

### Beat 9: Asking for a Man Pet for Company
- After Good Girl settles in the kennel, she watches Holic as if trying to decide whether she is allowed to ask for anything.
- The kennel is safe, but it is lonely.
- The outdoor routine gives her structure, but it does not give her company.
- Good Girl asks Holic for a man pet for some company.
- The request is tentative and vulnerable.
- She is not asking to leave the kennel.
- She is asking not to be alone inside it.
- The request should be framed as a need for companionship and reassurance.
- The request should not be framed as a sexual demand.
- Holic studies her carefully before reacting.
- He considers whether another adult pet would calm her or overwhelm her.
- He understands that a companion could help her remain settled outside.
- He also understands that the wrong man pet could frighten her or disrupt the recovery routine.
- Holic does not answer impulsively.
- He treats the request as something that requires selection, supervision, and rules.
- If he agrees, the man pet must be calm, adult, obedient, and safe around an injured Good Girl.
- The possible man pet should be introduced later under Holic’s direct control.
- This beat sets up a follow-up scene where Holic chooses or brings a man pet to keep her company in the kennel.

### Beat 10: Simonka Káta Beneath Good Girl
- As the morning continues, Simonka Káta surfaces briefly beneath the Good Girl conditioning.
- The edge of the bed had been a boundary she could understand at the start of the morning.
- The kennel becomes the new boundary for the rest of the morning.
- Asking for a man pet shows that she can still identify a need and communicate it.
- She is outside, but she is not hidden away.
- She is low to the ground, but she is within Holic’s household order.
- The bowls, cleaned body, kennel, and possible promise of company give her enough structure to remain oriented.
- Holic sees that this outdoor kennel arrangement may be necessary for the next stage of her recovery.

### Beat 11: Final Image
- The morning settles into an outdoor kennel routine.
- Holic remains nearby or within sight from the mansion.
- Good Girl stays in the kennel with her bowls beside her.
- She remains without a diaper outside.
- The staff continue their work in the background, able to see her without intruding.
- Good Girl keeps watching Holic after asking for a man pet for company.
- The final image is Good Girl in the kennel after being cleaned with cold water and fed from her bowls, visible to the household, still watching for Holic, and waiting to learn whether he will give her company.
