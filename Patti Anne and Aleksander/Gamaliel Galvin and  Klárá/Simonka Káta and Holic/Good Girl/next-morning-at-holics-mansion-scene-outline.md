# Next Morning at Holic’s Mansion — Scene Outline

## Placement

**When:** The morning after Holic brings Good Girl home from the clinic.  
**Where:** Holic Bartunek’s mansion.  
**Focus:** Good Girl’s first morning back under Holic’s direct care.

---

## Characters

- **Holic Bartunek** — Good Girl’s owner and steward.
- **Good Girl (Simonka Káta / GG)** — Recovering after the trauma.

---

## Scene Outline

### Beat 1: Good Girl Wakes Up

Good Girl wakes up slowly in one of the guest rooms at Holic’s mansion. She is lying on a soft bed with clean sheets. Her bandages have been changed, and she is wearing a light robe.

For several minutes, she remains still, staring at the ceiling. Her body still aches, especially her wrist and feet. She is in deep pet space, but there is a quiet awareness behind her eyes.

### Beat 2: Holic Checks on Her

There is a soft knock on the door. Holic enters without waiting for an answer. He is dressed casually — shirt sleeves rolled up, no jacket.

He walks over to the bed and sits on the edge. Good Girl immediately shifts closer to him on all fours, pressing her forehead against his leg in greeting.

Holic gently places his hand on the back of her neck.

> “Good morning, Simonka Káta.”

She doesn’t speak. She simply stays pressed against him, breathing slowly.

Holic studies her for a moment, then speaks quietly:

> “You’re safe here. No one will touch you without my permission.”

### Beat 3: Her Behavior

Good Girl remains very quiet and obedient. She follows Holic with her eyes but doesn’t initiate movement unless he tells her to. When he stands up, she immediately crawls off the bed and follows him on all fours without being asked.

Holic notices this. He stops in the doorway and looks down at her.

> “You don’t have to crawl if it hurts.”

Good Girl pauses, then slowly stands up. She is unsteady on her bandaged feet, but she obeys. She walks behind him instead.

Holic watches her carefully. He says nothing more, but his expression softens slightly.

### Beat 4: Breakfast

Holic takes her to a small private dining area. He has prepared a simple breakfast. He sits at the table and gestures for her to sit on the chair beside him rather than on the floor.

Good Girl hesitates for a moment, then slowly sits down. She eats quietly, keeping her head lowered.

After a while, she speaks in a very small voice:

> “Thank you… for letting me come home.”

Holic looks at her for a long moment.

> “You were never not coming home.”

---

## Emotional Notes

- This scene shows the beginning of Good Girl’s recovery under Holic’s care.
- Holic is more gentle and attentive than usual.
- Good Girl is still deeply in pet space but shows small moments of vulnerability as Simonka Káta.
- The contrast between her instinctive submission and Holic’s quiet protectiveness is central.
