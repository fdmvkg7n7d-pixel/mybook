# SCENE OUTLINE: THE CALL FOR HELP – LOVE AND FRAGILITY

## SCENE INFORMATION
- **Characters:** Simonka Káta (19), Klárka Pavlínka (20), Raphael
- **Location:** Simonka’s Apartment, Prague.
- **Date:** Saturday morning, shortly after Ambroz leaves (approx. 8:00 AM).
- **Status:** Outline

---

## THE SETTING
- The living room is bright with the morning sun, but the atmosphere is heavy.
- Simonka is on the sofa, curled into a tight ball. Her breathing is shallow, and her face is pale.
- The "noise" of Ambroz’s departure still hangs in the air.

---

## SCENE ARC

### 1. The Decision to Call
- Simonka is in physical and emotional pain. The "angry reclamation" on the hard floor has left her back and ribs throbbing—a dangerous sensation for someone with **Osteogenesis Imperfecta**.
- She is conflicted. She is furious and hurt, but the "four years of love" (and the history they share) still has a hold on her.
- She realizes she cannot manage this "impact" alone. She reaches for her phone and calls the only person she trusts: **Klárka**.

### 2. The Arrival
- Within twenty minutes, there is a frantic knock at the door. **Klárka Pavlínka** and **Raphael** burst in.
- Klárka takes one look at her sister’s face and the state of the room and knows exactly what happened. Raphael remains by the door—his presence is a silent, protective weight, mirroring the way Gamaliel used to stand watch.

### 3. The Physical Check (The Rules)
- Klárka moves to the sofa. She doesn't rush to touch. She follows the family's "Rules of Engagement" even in a crisis.
- **The Ask:** "Simi, it's me. Can I feel your ribs? I need to know if the stone-spot is okay."
- Simonka nods, tears finally breaking through her "mask."
- **The Touch:** Klárka’s hands are expert—trained by years of ballet and the "intentional pressure" protocol. She gently palpates Simonka’s torso, checking for the tell-tale signs of a fracture.
- **The Relief:** There are no breaks, but the bruising will be deep. Simonka’s "dancer’s shield" held, but only just.

### 4. The Healing Bath
- **The Transition:** Klárka realizes that Simonka needs more than just a huddle. She needs to "wash off" the morning.
- **The Ritual:** Klárka leads her to the bathroom. With the same expertise she used for the physical check, she helps Simonka undress, mindful of the soreness in her ribs.
- **The Safety:** Klárka handles the bath with extreme care—the "Rules" in action. She steadies Simonka as she lowers herself into the warm water, ensuring there are no slips that could threaten her fragile bones.
- **The Observation:** In the water, the extent of the bruising from the hard floor is visible. Klárka says nothing, but her jaw tightens. She uses a soft cloth to wash Simonka’s back, her touch as light as a feather, providing a "liquid" version of the family's intentional pressure.

### 5. The Mask of Make-up
- **The Recovery:** After the bath, Klárka helps Simonka into fresh, soft clothes. Simonka sits at her vanity, looking at her reflection. She looks "broken," her eyes red and puffy from crying.
- **The Armor:** Simonka asks for help with her make-up. She isn't ready to let the world see her vulnerability yet.
- **The Task:** Klárka stands behind her, using her own brushes and palettes. She applies concealer to the shadows under Simonka’s eyes and a touch of color to her cheeks.
- **The Transformation:** As the make-up goes on, Simonka’s "public face" returns. It’s a mask of poise and "advanced academic" strength.
- **The Conversation:** "You don't have to be perfect for me," Klárka whispers as she applies a final touch of lip gloss. "But if this makes you feel like Simonka again, we'll do it."
- Simonka looks at herself. She still loves Ambroz, and she still hurts, but under the make-up and the afterglow of the bath, she feels like she has a "shield" again.

### 6. The Confession: "I Still Love Him"
- As they finish, Simonka’s composure slips for a moment.
- "He hurt me, Klárka. He was so angry. He didn't listen."
- Klárka’s face is a mask of "fierce protectiveness." She looks toward the door, where Raphael is listening. "He shouldn't have been here, Simi. He doesn't deserve the secret."
- Simonka shakes her head. "But I still love him. Why do I still love him?"
- This is the core of her nineteen-year-old heart: the agonizing gap between her need for "safety" and her attachment to the "noise" of her first major relationship.

### 5. Raphael’s Role
- Raphael approaches quietly. He brings a glass of water and Simonka’s **Calcium** and **Fosamax** from the kitchen.
- He doesn't offer advice; he offers "service." He helps her take her medication, his steady hands providing a "masculine stillness" that is the direct opposite of Ambroz’s chaos.
- He looks at Klárka. They share a silent agreement: Ambroz is now a "threat" to the family circle.

### 6. The "Tighten" Guard
- Klárka settles onto the sofa behind Simonka, pulling her into a careful, "intentional" huddle.
- "You’re staying with us for a few days," Klárka says. It isn't a suggestion; it’s a directive from the "medical sentinel."
- Simonka doesn't fight it. She is too exhausted to keep the "Pact" alone. As she leans into her sister, the "stillness" of the family begins to drown out the "noise" of Ambroz.

---

## CHARACTER NOTES
- **Simonka (19):** At her most vulnerable. Her love for Ambroz is a "phantom limb"—it hurts, but she isn't ready to let it go.
- **Klárka (20):** Transitioning from "sister" to "protector." She sees the danger of Ambroz’s immaturity more clearly than Simonka can.
- **Raphael:** Proving his worth as a partner to Klárka by supporting her sister without overstepping.

---

## SAGA CONNECTIONS
- **The Circle of Inclusion:** The secret expands to include Raphael, because "safety" now outweighs the "Pact."
- **OI Management:** A practical demonstration of how the family handles a "near-miss" fracture.
- **The Long Breakup:** This scene sets the stage for the next four years of Simonka trying to reconcile her love for Ambroz with her need for a different kind of life.
