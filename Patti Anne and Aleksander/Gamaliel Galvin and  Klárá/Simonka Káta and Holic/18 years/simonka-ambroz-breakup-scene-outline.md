# Simonka Káta Breaks Up with Ambroz — Scene Outline

## Placement

**When:** When Simonka Káta is twenty-three years old. This is **after** her chance meeting with Holic in Old Town Square and **after** her first date with him at Marie B, but two years after the farm birthday incident with Alois and Braxton.
**Where:** Simonka's apartment in Prague, or a quiet cafe nearby.
**Context:** Simonka has been dating Ambroz since she was at least nineteen. While she has explored complex, adult power dynamics (like the BDSM barn at Alois's farm), Ambroz has remained stubbornly immature. His jealousy over her other dynamics (especially being excluded from the farm birthday) has festered. At twenty-three, Simonka—who values control, psychology, and emotional intelligence—finally hits her limit with him.

---

## Characters

- **Simonka Káta:** Twenty-three years old. A psychology student who reads people effortlessly. She is calm, decisive, and increasingly aware of her own desires for structure and discipline—things Ambroz completely lacks.
- **Ambroz:** Twenty-three years old (he was 19 when they started). Immature, emotionally reactive, and deeply insecure about Simonka's other relationships. He uses jealousy as a weapon but lacks the maturity to actually hold her attention.

---

## Scene Outline

### 1. The Trigger
- The scene opens with Ambroz complaining—again. It is a minor, petty complaint about Simonka's schedule, her friends, or a perceived slight.
- Simonka is sitting across from him, perhaps drinking coffee. She is watching him not with anger, but with clinical detachment. As a psychology student, she sees the exact mechanics of his insecurity.

### 2. The Assessment
- Simonka realizes, in this specific moment, that she is entirely done. The contrast is too stark. She thinks briefly of her recent date with Holic at Marie B—the quiet authority he held, the way he confidently ordered her food without asking. She likes adults. She likes people who know what they want. She likes adults. She likes people who know what they want.
- Ambroz is whining like a teenager. The emotional labor required to constantly manage his jealousy is no longer worth it.

### 3. The Breakup
- Simonka sets her cup down. She doesn't yell. She doesn't cry.
- "We're done, Ambroz," she says evenly.
- Ambroz immediately pivots from complaining to panic. He tries to argue, bringing up old grievances (maybe even the farm). He tries to make her feel guilty.
- Simonka shuts it down with surgical precision. "You are immature. You use jealousy because you don't know how to ask for what you want. I cannot take it anymore. It is over."

### 4. The Aftermath
- Ambroz leaves, angry and pleading.
- Simonka is left alone in her apartment. Instead of sadness, she feels a profound sense of relief.
- She looks around her quiet space. She is twenty-three. She is free of the emotional dead weight. She is ready for someone who can match her intellect and her desire for discipline.
- This clears the slate completely, allowing her to focus entirely on her upcoming second date at Holic's mansion.

---

## What This Scene Establishes

1. **Simonka's Ruthless Clarity:** She doesn't drag out a dying relationship. When she sees it isn't serving her, she ends it.
2. **The Contrast for Holic:** By showing exactly what Simonka hates (whining, immaturity, unchecked jealousy), it perfectly explains why she will later be so drawn to Holic's absolute, quiet authority.
3. **The Timeline Check:** It establishes that Holic was the catalyst. Once she experienced his authority at Marie B, she could no longer tolerate Ambroz for another day. (He will return to her apartment shortly after this to beg for her back).
