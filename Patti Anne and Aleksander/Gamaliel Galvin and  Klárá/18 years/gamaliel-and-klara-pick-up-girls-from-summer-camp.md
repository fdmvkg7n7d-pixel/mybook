# SCENE OUTLINE: GAMALIEL AND KLÁRA PICK UP THE GIRLS FROM SUMMER CAMP

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Gamaliel Galvin Key, Klára Key, Klárka Pavlînka (6), Simonka Káta (5), Summer camp staff / teachers
- **Location:** Gamaliel Galvin and Klára’s Ballet Academy, Prague (pickup area outside the studios)
- **Date:** During the summer camp period (shortly after the OI diagnosis), circa 2079
- **Status:** New Scene Outline

---

## THE SETTING

- Late afternoon at the ballet academy.
- Parents and caregivers are arriving to pick up children from the day’s summer camp sessions.
- Gamaliel and Klára arrive together to collect Klárka and Simonka.
- The academy has a warm, familiar atmosphere — music drifting from studios, children in dancewear chatting as they leave.

---

## STORY ARC

### 1. Arrival at the Academy

- Gamaliel and Klára walk toward the pickup area hand-in-hand or close together.
- This is one of the first times they are picking the girls up since the diagnosis and the decision to let them attend camp.
- Both parents feel a mixture of nervousness and hope.

### 2. Watching the Girls Emerge

- Klárka and Simonka come out together with the other campers.
- Klárka is moving carefully, still mindful of her recently healed/buddy-taped foot, but clearly happy and engaged.
- Simonka stays close to her sister, as usual.
- Both girls light up when they see their parents.

### 3. The Pickup Conversation

- Gamaliel and Klára greet the girls warmly.
- They ask gentle questions about the day:
  - How the modified activities went
  - Whether the teachers were careful with Klárka
  - How the girls felt being back in the studio environment
- The girls are chatty and positive, reinforcing their stubborn insistence that they belong here.

### 4. Observing the Adaptations in Action

- A camp teacher approaches briefly to give a quick update to Gamaliel and Klára.
- She mentions that the instructors have been following the new guidelines (no high-impact jumps for Klárka, focus on strength and control).
- Klárka proudly shows that she participated in barre work and some gentle center work without issues.
- Simonka adds that she helped “watch out” for her sister (in her own five-year-old way).

### 5. Klára’s Quiet Emotions

- Klára watches the girls with a complex mix of pride, relief, and lingering worry.
- She is glad they are happy and included, but the reality of their condition is still fresh.
- Gamaliel notices and gently squeezes her hand — a quiet moment of partnership.

### 6. Heading Home

- The family walks to the car together.
- Klárka walks a little slower than usual but refuses any help beyond what’s necessary.
- Simonka holds Klárka’s hand on one side and Klára’s on the other.
- Light conversation continues about what they did that day and what’s for dinner.

### 7. Closing Moment

- As they drive home, Gamaliel and Klára exchange a look in the rearview mirror.
- The girls are tired but content in the back seat.
- This pickup reinforces that, despite the new challenges, the decision to keep the girls in their familiar ballet world was the right one for their emotional well-being.

---

## CHARACTER NOTES

- **Gamaliel:** Calm and observant. He pays close attention to how the academy is handling the OI accommodations and supports Klára emotionally.
- **Klára:** Still emotionally raw from the recent diagnosis. She feels relief seeing the girls happy but remains vigilant.
- **Klárka Pavlînka (6):** Stubborn and resilient. She is determined to participate fully and shows pride in managing her limitations.
- **Simonka Káta (5):** Loyal and protective. She stays close to her sister and takes the new rules in stride.
