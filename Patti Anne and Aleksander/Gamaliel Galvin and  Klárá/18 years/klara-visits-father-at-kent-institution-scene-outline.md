# Klára Visits Her Father at Kent Institution

## Scene Outline

## Placement

**When:** When Klára is eighteen and Gamaliel Galvin is nineteen, after Klára has aged out of childhood and after years of living inside Patti Anne's family.

**Where:** **Kent Institution**, British Columbia — a federal maximum-security institution. The scene takes place primarily in an **interview room** inside the prison.

**Continuity Function:** This scene becomes one of the memories Gamaliel later thinks about when he visits Simonka Káta at Charles University and fears Holic Bartunek through the lens of Klára's father.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene gives Klára a direct adult confrontation with her biological father.

As a child, she was rescued from him. As a teenager, she survived the aftermath of what he did. At eighteen, she chooses — or agrees — to see him again, not because she owes him anything, but because part of her needs to look at the man behind the fear and know he is contained.

The emotional reversal is that her father does **not recognize her at first**.

He thinks she is his new lawyer.

That mistake cuts Klára deeply. It means he has not held her in his mind as his daughter. Or he has chosen not to. Or prison, time, denial, and self-preservation have altered the frame through which he sees everyone who enters the room.

Klára cries softly while speaking to him because the scene is overwhelmingly emotional, but she does not collapse. Her tears are quiet. She keeps asking questions. She keeps sitting across from him.

---

## Core Emotional Conflict

Klára wants to be seen.

But being seen by him is also terrifying.

Her father first sees her as a function — lawyer, advocate, useful stranger — not as the daughter he harmed. Klára must decide whether to correct him immediately, let him continue in the mistake, or force him to recognize her.

The central wound:

> He does not know his own daughter.

Or worse:

> He knows, and pretending not to know is easier.

---

## Characters in the Scene

### Klára

- Eighteen years old.
- Legally an adult, but still young enough that the child she was remains close to the surface.
- Has survived years away from her father and has been loved by Patti Anne's family.
- Arrives with Gamaliel Galvin but asks him to wait outside the interview room.
- Cries softly while talking to her father.
- Does not want her father to see her as broken, but the tears come anyway.
- Needs to know whether he remembers, whether he regrets, and whether he can say her name.

### Gamaliel Galvin

- Nineteen years old.
- Comes with Klára because he has been beside her since childhood.
- Does not want her to go into the interview room alone.
- Respects her request and waits outside.
- His waiting is active, not passive: he is alert, tense, ready to enter if she needs him.
- This experience marks him permanently; years later, he remembers it while talking to Simonka about Holic.

### Klára's Father

- Incarcerated at Kent Institution.
- Klára's biological father.
- At first, he thinks Klára is his new lawyer.
- His misrecognition may be genuine, strategic, or a form of denial.
- He may be ordinary-looking in a way that disturbs Klára: not a monster in appearance, but a contained man in an institutional room.
- His reaction after learning who she is determines the emotional shape of the scene.

---

## Setting Details

The prison should feel controlled, procedural, and emotionally cold.

Possible details:

- Long drive or flight to British Columbia.
- Security checks.
- Identification.
- Institutional corridors.
- Doors that lock behind them.
- Waiting areas with plastic chairs or bolted-down furniture.
- Fluorescent lighting.
- A guard's neutral voice.
- The interview room: small table, two chairs, maybe a fixed window or camera, maybe a door with a narrow observation panel.

The prison does not heal anything. It only contains.

That distinction matters.

# Scene Structure

## Beat 1 — Arrival at Kent Institution

Klára and Gamaliel arrive at Kent Institution.

The exterior should feel both ordinary and impossible: a building, a gate, a road, a sign — and inside it, the man who shaped Klára's childhood terror.

Klára is quiet.

Gamaliel watches her without crowding her. He knows better than to ask too many questions now.

Possible line from Gamaliel:

> We can still leave.

Klára may answer:

> I know.

But she does not leave.

---

## Beat 2 — Processing Through Security

They go through the entry process.

This beat should emphasize how different this is from childhood danger.

When Klára was little, her father's power was private. It lived behind domestic doors. Here, every door is controlled by someone else. Every movement is supervised. Every sound has rules.

Klára may notice the irony:

- As a child, she was trapped with him.
- Now he is the one contained.

But knowing that does not make her body feel safe automatically.

---

## Beat 3 — Klára Asks Gamaliel to Wait Outside

Before they enter the interview room, Klára stops.

She tells Gamaliel she wants him to wait outside.

Possible line:

> I need to do this alone.

Or:

> Please wait outside the room.

Gamaliel reacts immediately. He does not like it.

Possible line:

> Klára, no.

Not controlling — afraid.

He knows what this man did. He knows what Klára survived. Every instinct in him wants to stand beside her inside the room.

Klára insists.

> If you come in, I will look at you instead of him.

Or:

> If you are beside me, I will let you be brave for me. I need to know if I can be brave for myself.

This is the emotional reason Gamaliel waits outside.

He does not abandon her. He obeys her.

---

## Beat 4 — Gamaliel's Waiting Position

Gamaliel waits outside the interview room.

He may sit in a chair, stand near the wall, or remain close enough that Klára knows he is nearby.

He cannot hear every word, or maybe he hears muffled voices but not meaning.

His waiting is torture.

Possible details:

- His hands clasped too tightly.
- His eyes on the door.
- His dancer's posture held too still.
- A guard nearby, uninterested in the emotional magnitude of the moment.
- The knowledge that the safest place for Klára may be exactly where Gamaliel is not allowed to stand.

This waiting becomes part of his later memory: wanting to protect someone he loves, but being asked to respect her boundary instead.

---

## Beat 5 — Klára Enters the Interview Room Alone

Klára enters.

Her father is already there, or he is brought in after her.

The room is smaller than she expected. Or colder. Or more ordinary.

She sits across from him.

This is the first time she has faced him as an adult.

She may have prepared words, but they disappear when she sees him.

---

## Beat 6 — Her Father Thinks She Is His New Lawyer

Her father looks at her and misreads the scene.

Possible lines:

> Are you the new lawyer?

> They said someone was coming about my case.

> You're young for counsel.

> Did legal aid send you?

The line should land hard.

Klára expected many possible reactions:

- Anger.
- Denial.
- Manipulation.
- Recognition.
- Mockery.
- Silence.

She did not expect him not to know her.

Or she did not expect how much it would hurt.

---

## Beat 7 — Klára's First Soft Tears

Klára begins to cry softly.

Not sobbing. Not dramatically. Her eyes fill, her voice changes, and the tears move quietly down her face while she remains seated.

The crying is involuntary. She may hate that he can see it.

But the tears do not mean she is weak. They mean the body has recognized a danger the adult mind is trying to manage.

She may wipe them away once, then stop trying.

---

## Beat 8 — Klára Corrects Him

Klára tells him who she is.

Possible line:

> I am not your lawyer.

A pause.

> I am Klára.

Or:

> I am your daughter.

The exact wording matters.

**Option A — Name first:**

> I am Klára.

This forces him to recognize her as a person before the family relation is stated.

**Option B — Relationship first:**

> I am your daughter.

This forces the fatherhood wound into the room immediately.

**Option C — Both:**

> I am Klára. Your daughter.

This is likely the strongest version.

---

## Beat 9 — Father's Reaction

His reaction can go several ways depending on the desired tone.

### Option A — Genuine Shock

He truly did not recognize her.

He stares, recalculates her age, her face, her voice.

Possible line:

> Klára?

This hurts because her name in his mouth still has power.

### Option B — Denial

He refuses the recognition.

> No. No, you are not.

This is defensive. He would rather she remain a lawyer than become the evidence of what he did.

### Option C — Manipulative Warmth

He shifts too quickly into fatherly language.

> My little girl.

This should feel wrong. Klára may recoil internally because he has no right to that tenderness.

### Option D — Legal Paranoia

He thinks she is part of some strategy.

> Who sent you?

This preserves his self-focus and shows he cannot meet her emotionally.

Recommended approach: **A mixed reaction** — he recognizes her after she says her name, but immediately tries to control the emotional frame.

---

## Beat 10 — Klára Stays Seated

Klára's body wants to leave.

She does not.

She stays seated because leaving immediately would let his first mistake become the whole visit.

Her tears continue softly.

She may say:

> I wanted to see if you would know me.

Or:

> I wanted to know what happened when I looked at you and you looked back.

This is not a trial. It is not forgiveness. It is a reckoning.

---

## Beat 11 — The Father Tries to Reframe the Past

He may try to minimize, deny, rationalize, or turn the conversation toward his own suffering.

Possible lines:

> You were too young to understand.

> Things were complicated.

> I have suffered too.

> They turned you against me.

> Patti Anne took you.

> That boy told lies.

The mention of Gamaliel as "that boy" may be especially charged.

Klára may stiffen at it.

She may say:

> Do not talk about him.

Or:

> He told the truth.

This protects Gamaliel even while Gamaliel waits outside, unable to hear.

---

## Beat 12 — Klára Names What She Needs

Klára does not need a full confession from him, though part of her may want one.

She needs to know whether he can recognize harm.

Possible questions:

> Do you remember what you did?

> Do you know why I was taken away?

> Do you know why I am here?

> Did you ever think about me as a person after I left?

> When you looked at me just now, why did you not know me?

These questions are emotionally dangerous because his answers may not satisfy her.

The outline should allow that he fails her again.

---

## Beat 13 — Klára Cries Softly Through the Conversation

The crying should be continuous but restrained.

It may appear in small physical details:

- She wipes one cheek with the heel of her hand.
- Her breath catches, but she forces the sentence out.
- Tears fall onto her hands on the table.
- She keeps her voice low so it does not shake too much.
- She refuses to let the tears end the conversation.

The emotional point:

Klára can cry and still be in control of her choice to stay.

---

## Beat 14 — Gamaliel Outside Feels The Time Stretch

Cut briefly to Gamaliel outside.

He is waiting.

He does not know she is crying, or he suspects it because he knows her.

He may hear a muffled change in her voice. He may stand up. A guard may look at him.

He almost knocks.

He does not.

Because she asked him to wait.

This restraint is crucial to Gamaliel's character. Love, for him, becomes obedience to Klára's boundary.

---

## Beat 15 — The Father Notices The Tears

Klára's father notices she is crying.

His response reveals who he is.

Possible responses:

### Cruel / Dismissive

> Still crying?

### Self-Pitying

> You think this is easy for me?

### False Tenderness

> Don't cry, sweetheart.

### Avoidant

He looks away because her tears accuse him more directly than her words.

Recommended approach: **false tenderness or avoidance**, because both are emotionally complicated. If he says something gentle, Klára has to reject the comfort because it is coming from the wrong person.

Possible Klára response:

> You do not get to comfort me.

---

## Beat 16 — Klára Takes Back Her Name

At some point, Klára should make a statement of identity.

She is not only his victim. She is not only his daughter. She is not the little girl in his house anymore.

Possible lines:

> My name is Klára Key now.

> Patti Anne is my mother.

> Mike believed Gamaliel. Mike believed me even before I could say it properly.

> You are not my home.

This is not about legal surname only. It is about chosen family, rescue, and survival.

---

## Beat 17 — The Father Reacts To The Key Name

The Key name may provoke him.

Possible responses:

- He mocks the Keys.
- He claims they stole her.
- He says she has been brainwashed.
- He refuses to use her chosen name.
- He calls her by her birth surname.

Klára does not need to win the argument.

She only needs to hear him reveal himself clearly enough that she can stop hoping for something he cannot give.

---

## Beat 18 — Klára Realizes Why She Came

The scene's emotional turn:

Klára realizes she did not come for an apology.

She came to learn whether his voice still controlled her body.

It does affect her. She is crying. She is shaken.

But she is still sitting there. She is not a child in his house. There are guards, doors, rules, and Gamaliel outside. There is a life waiting for her beyond the room.

She has survived him.

That is the answer the visit gives her, even if he gives her nothing.

---

## Beat 19 — Klára Ends The Visit

Klára chooses when the visit ends.

This is important.

She may stand up before he is finished speaking.

Possible line:

> I am done.

Or:

> I needed to see you. Now I have.

Or:

> You did not know me. That tells me enough.

She may still be crying when she says it.

The tears do not undermine the finality.

---

## Beat 20 — Leaving The Interview Room

Klára leaves the room.

The door opens.

Gamaliel is there.

He sees immediately that she has been crying.

He may step toward her, but not touch until she lets him.

This mirrors his respect before the visit.

Possible line:

> Klára?

She may say:

> Please take me out of here.

Or:

> I did it.

Or nothing at all.

---

## Beat 21 — Gamaliel Holds The Boundary Until She Reaches For Him

Gamaliel does not burst into the room. He does not confront the father. He does not turn Klára's pain into his anger.

He waits for Klára's cue.

If she reaches for his hand, he takes it.

If she leans into him, he holds her.

If she wants to walk, he walks.

His restraint is love.

---

## Beat 22 — Leaving Kent Institution

They leave the prison.

The exit process may feel surreal:

- More doors.
- More checks.
- Outside air.
- The sky.
- The parking lot.
- The sound of ordinary traffic.

Klára may cry harder only after they are outside, or she may become very quiet.

Gamaliel may finally ask:

> What did he say?

Klára may answer only the most painful fact:

> He thought I was his lawyer.

That line lands on Gamaliel and stays with him for years.

---

## Beat 23 — Aftermath Between Klára and Gamaliel

In the car, hotel room, airport, or somewhere private afterward, Klára and Gamaliel process the visit.

Klára may say:

> I wanted him to know me.

Or:

> I hated that I wanted him to know me.

Or:

> He looked at me and saw someone useful, not me.

Gamaliel may say:

> I knew you.

This is not a replacement for what her father failed to give, but it matters.

Possible Gamaliel line:

> When you came out, I knew you before you said anything.

That line contrasts directly with the father's failure to recognize her.

---

## Final Image

Klára and Gamaliel outside Kent Institution.

The prison is behind them.

Klára's face is wet from crying, but she is standing. Gamaliel is beside her, not in front of her. He did not rescue her from the interview room because she asked him not to. He waited, and she came out.

The man inside did not know her.

Gamaliel did.

# Emotional Notes

1. **Klára cries softly throughout the conversation.** The crying should be quiet, intimate, and continuous. It should not stop the scene; it should live underneath it.

2. **The father's mistake is the emotional knife.** Thinking Klára is his lawyer shows how much he has erased, denied, or failed to carry his daughter as a real person.

3. **Gamaliel waiting outside is essential.** He wants to protect her by entering. He actually protects her by respecting her request.

4. **This is not forgiveness.** Klára does not visit to absolve him. She visits to face him and learn what remains of his power over her.

5. **The prison contains him but does not repair the harm.** Kent Institution gives physical safety, not emotional closure.

6. **The scene explains a later Gamaliel memory.** Years later, when he talks to Simonka about Holic, he remembers this visit: the interview room, Klára's tears, and the helplessness of waiting outside while someone he loved faced a dangerous man.
