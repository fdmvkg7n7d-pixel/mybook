# Scene Outline: Felician’s First Day at Ballet Academy

## Placement

- **When:** **Monday morning**, the first real academy day after Felician has been received into Pražská Baletní Akademie for the new schoolyear.
- **Where:** Pražská Baletní Akademie, Prague — shared bedroom, showers, cafeteria, Grade One classroom, recess area, ballet studio, dormitory area, and a studio where older students practice.
- **Continuity Fit:** Follows `academy-receives-students-new-schoolyear-felician-scene-outline.md`. The receiving / move-in scene comes first; Felician’s first actual school day begins on **Monday morning**.
- **Focus:** Felician’s first real day as a Grade One academy student after being accepted on a full scholarship.

---

## Characters

- **Felician** — Newly accepted Grade One ballet academy student, full-scholarship student through grade twelve, serious and anxious about doing everything correctly.
- **Svjetlana** — Grade One student who shares the same bedroom with Felician and Agrippine.
- **Agrippine** — Grade One student who shares the same bedroom with Felician and Svjetlana.
- **Klára Key** — Academy co-director; emotionally receives the younger students and watches Felician carefully because she remembers the audition.
- **Gamaliel Galvin Key** — Academy co-director; manages systems, rosters, security wristbands, logistics, and teaches a small group of Grade Seven and Grade Eight students after Felician’s Ballet class.
- **Grade One Classroom Teacher** — Leads the Monday Grade One academic day; the students’ books, pencils, and crayons are already placed on their desks in the classroom.
- **Ballet Teacher** — Leads Ballet class from **2:15 p.m. to 4:00 p.m.**
- **Grade Seven and Grade Eight Students** — A few older students taught by Gamaliel Galvin after the Grade One Ballet class; they are learning lifts and jumps.
- **Dorm Supervisor** — Helps the Grade One girls follow the morning dorm routine.

---

## Scene Purpose

1. Show Felician’s first full day as an official Grade One ballet academy student.
2. Establish that Felician, Svjetlana, and Agrippine are in the **same bedroom**.
3. Establish the morning dorm routine: shower, uniform, cafeteria breakfast.
4. Establish that the **Grade One schedule is set** and given by routine / teacher direction, not by anything Felician carries.
5. Establish that Felician does **not** need to bring anything to the classroom because the classroom materials are provided there.
6. Establish that their **books, pencils, and crayons** are placed on their desks in the classroom.
7. Establish the Grade One Monday academic order: **reading, math, social studies, art, and English**.
8. Establish the day’s fixed times: classroom at **8:30 a.m.**, first recess **10:15–10:30 a.m.**, lunch **11:50 a.m.–12:45 p.m.**, afternoon recess **2:00–2:15 p.m.**, Ballet **2:15–4:00 p.m.**, and dinner at **5:30 p.m.**
9. Establish that before Ballet, they get dressed into their **leotards and Ballet flats**.
10. Establish that Felician, Svjetlana, and Agrippine can decide by themselves whether they need a shower after Ballet.
11. After Ballet class, have Felician, Svjetlana, and Agrippine wander around the dormitory / studio area and see **Gamaliel Galvin teaching a few Grade Seven and Grade Eight students**.
12. Establish that those older students are learning **lifts and jumps**.
13. Have **Klára** come into the studio to get Gamaliel Galvin for a ride home.
14. Show Felician beginning to bond with Svjetlana and Agrippine through the shared Grade One routine.
12. Keep the focus on ballet academy life, first-day nerves, structure, and belonging.

---

## Core Emotional Throughline

Felician has been accepted. She has the scholarship. Her name is in the system. Her security wristband works.

She does not need to carry anything to the Grade One classroom. The day is already structured for her by routine, teachers, and the classroom clock.

The first real school day begins in the shared bedroom with Svjetlana and Agrippine. The routine is simple and firm: shower, uniform, breakfast, classroom by **8:30 a.m.** Their materials will already be placed on their desks in the classroom.

The set schedule helps Felician. Reading, math, social studies, art, English, first recess, lunch, afternoon recess, Ballet, seeing Gamaliel Galvin teach older students lifts and jumps, a self-decided shower if needed, and dinner at **5:30 p.m.**: the order of the day gives her something to hold onto.

---

## Scene Structure

### Beat 1: Monday Morning in the Shared Bedroom

- It is **Monday morning**, Felician’s first real school day at the ballet academy.
- Felician wakes in the **same bedroom as Svjetlana and Agrippine**.
- The three girls are new enough to be quiet at first.
- Felician checks her security wristband on her wrist.
- The wristband confirms she belongs in the girl dormitory and her Grade One security group.
- A dorm supervisor reminds the girls that Grade One follows a set schedule.
- Felician does not need to bring classroom supplies from the bedroom.
- The Grade One day is directed by the dorm supervisor, classroom teacher, and set school routine.

### Beat 2: Shower and Uniform

- The morning routine begins with the girls getting a shower.
- After the shower, they get dressed in their **academy uniform**.
- Felician is careful because she wants to look correct.
- Svjetlana and Agrippine are getting ready in the same rhythm.
- The dorm supervisor keeps the routine moving without making the girls feel rushed.
- The uniform helps Felician feel like she belongs to the academy now.

### Beat 3: Cafeteria Breakfast

- Felician, Svjetlana, and Agrippine go to the cafeteria for breakfast.
- The cafeteria is busy but organized.
- Older students know exactly where to go.
- The Grade One girls stay together.
- Felician is nervous, but breakfast gives her something ordinary to do.
- The girls do not need to discuss what to bring to class because their materials will already be placed on their desks.

### Beat 4: Going to the Classroom for 8:30

- The Grade One students go to their classroom for **8:30 a.m.**
- Felician, Svjetlana, and Agrippine walk together.
- The classroom teacher greets them and shows them where to sit.
- The girls do not bring anything with them from the dorm.
- Their **books, pencils, and crayons** are already placed on their desks.
- This reassures Felician because she was worried about missing something.
- The classroom feels less frightening because the materials are already waiting for her.

### Beat 5: Reading

- The first morning class is **Reading**.
- Felician sits carefully and tries not to fidget.
- She listens to the teacher’s instructions.
- She is anxious about whether academy reading class will be different from what she knows.
- Svjetlana and Agrippine nearby help her feel less alone.

### Beat 6: Math

- The next class is **Math**.
- The books, pencils, and crayons are already on the students’ desks.
- Felician works carefully because she wants to be right.
- If she makes a mistake, she worries for a moment that it means she is already failing.
- The teacher corrects it normally and moves on.
- Felician begins learning that academic corrections are not punishments.

### Beat 7: First Recess — 10:15 to 10:30

- First recess is from **10:15 a.m. to 10:30 a.m.**
- Felician is relieved to stand up and move after the first two classes.
- She stays near Svjetlana and Agrippine.
- The short recess helps the girls reset before the next academic classes.
- Felician watches how the other Grade One students line up when recess ends.

### Beat 8: Social Studies

- After first recess, the class continues with **Social Studies**.
- The lesson may introduce the academy community, Prague, maps, classroom expectations, or the students’ place in the school.
- Felician is interested because she likes understanding where she is.
- The teacher connects the school to the city and to the students’ lives.
- Felician begins to understand that the academy trains dancers, but it is still a full school.

### Beat 9: Art

- **Art** gives Felician a different kind of quiet.
- The teacher uses the crayons already placed on the students’ desks.
- Felician is careful and serious.
- The Grade One students draw **stick people** with the crayons already placed on their desks.
- Felician draws stick people too, trying to make the bodies look like dancers even though they are simple.
- Art lets her breathe after the academic concentration of the morning.

### Beat 10: English

- **English** is the final morning class.
- Felician tries hard to pronounce, read, or write correctly depending on the lesson.
- She is tired by now, but she keeps paying attention.
- The teacher notices that Felician listens closely even when she is nervous.
- Completing English means Felician has made it through the full academic morning.

### Beat 11: Lunch — 11:50 to 12:45

- Lunch is from **11:50 a.m. to 12:45 p.m.**
- Felician, Svjetlana, and Agrippine sit together because they are in the same bedroom and the same Grade One routine.
- The dining room is loud, but the set lunch time makes it manageable.
- The girls talk quietly about reading, math, social studies, art, and English.
- Felician knows Ballet comes later, and the thought makes her nervous and excited.

### Beat 12: Early Afternoon Classroom Routine

- After lunch, the Grade One students return to the classroom routine.
- No new named subject needs to be added here; the important structure is that the class moves toward afternoon recess and Ballet.
- The teacher keeps the students organized.
- Felician is tired but still alert.
- Svjetlana and Agrippine remain close enough that Felician does not feel alone.

### Beat 13: Afternoon Recess — 2:00 to 2:15

- Afternoon recess is from **2:00 p.m. to 2:15 p.m.**
- Felician is relieved to move again.
- She stays near Svjetlana and Agrippine.
- She knows Ballet class starts immediately after recess.
- Recess becomes the bridge between the classroom day and the dance day.

### Beat 14: Getting Dressed for Ballet

- At the end of afternoon recess / during the supervised transition, the Grade One students get dressed into their **leotards and Ballet flats**.
- Felician becomes more nervous because Ballet is why she came to the academy.
- She remembers the audition and the full scholarship.
- She wants to prove Klára was right to accept her.
- The teacher / supervisor reminds the students that first Ballet class is for learning the routine, not proving everything at once.
- The Ballet teacher tells the students that they do **not** need to be perfect.

### Beat 15: Ballet Class — 2:15 to 4:00

- Ballet class is from **2:15 p.m. to 4:00 p.m.**
- The Ballet teacher tells the students that they do **not** need to be perfect.
- The class focuses on basic barre, posture, turnout, musicality, listening, and how to receive corrections.
- Felician still tries very hard to be perfect because she wants to do well.
- She watches the teacher’s face after every correction.
- She corrects herself quickly, the same trait Klára saw during the audition.
- The teacher notices that Felician listens with her whole body.

### Beat 16: The First Ballet Correction

- Felician receives her first Ballet correction as an official academy student.
- It is not harsh, but it lands heavily because she is so anxious.
- For a second, she thinks she has failed.
- The teacher repeats the correction calmly and shows her what to adjust.
- Felician makes the adjustment.
- The teacher moves on without drama.
- Felician realizes that corrections are not punishments here.
- They are the language of the school.

### Beat 17: Wandering After Ballet

- After Ballet class, Felician, Svjetlana, and Agrippine wander around the dormitory / studio area together.
- They are tired from Ballet, but they are curious about the academy now that they have survived their first class.
- They pass a studio where **Gamaliel Galvin** is teaching a few older students.
- The students are in **Grade Seven and Grade Eight**.
- The older students are learning **lifts and jumps**.
- Felician stops because the work looks impossible and beautiful to her.
- Svjetlana and Agrippine stop with her.
- The three Grade One girls watch quietly from where they are allowed to stand.
- Seeing the Grade Seven and Grade Eight students learning lifts and jumps shows Felician what the academy can grow into.
- Gamaliel Galvin is focused, precise, and calm while he teaches.

### Beat 18: Klára Comes to Get Gamaliel Galvin

- Klára comes into the studio while Gamaliel Galvin is teaching the Grade Seven and Grade Eight students.
- She has come to get Gamaliel Galvin for a ride home.
- She does not interrupt the students rudely.
- She waits for the right moment or speaks quietly from the studio doorway.
- Gamaliel Galvin finishes the correction or instruction he is giving.
- The Grade Seven and Grade Eight students remain focused on the lifts and jumps.
- Felician notices how Klára and Gamaliel Galvin move around each other like people who have run this school together for years.
- The moment is small, but it makes the academy feel like a real family-run institution.

### Beat 19: Felician, Svjetlana, and Agrippine Decide Whether to Shower

- After watching Gamaliel Galvin teach the older students, Felician, Svjetlana, and Agrippine can decide by themselves whether they need a shower.
- They are trusted to notice if Ballet made them sweaty, tired, or uncomfortable enough to shower before dinner.
- Showering after Ballet is presented as ordinary academy care, not a punishment or special treatment.
- Felician may decide to shower because Ballet was long and serious.
- Svjetlana and Agrippine can make their own decision too.
- Felician is tired, but the routine stays clear.

### Beat 20: Dinner — 5:30

- Dinner is at **5:30 p.m.**
- Felician, Svjetlana, and Agrippine sit together again if seating allows.
- The day feels enormous now that Ballet is over.
- Felician is tired in a way that feels different from fear.
- She has survived the first Monday.

### Beat 21: First Night After the First Day

- Felician prepares for bed in the shared bedroom with Svjetlana and Agrippine.
- The building sounds different at night.
- She misses home, but she does not want to leave.
- Her security wristband is still on her wrist.
- Svjetlana and Agrippine are nearby.
- The day has been enormous, but it did not break her.

### Beat 22: Closing Image

- Possible closing image: Felician lying in her dorm bed, looking at the security wristband on her wrist in the dark.
- The wristband is no longer only a device.
- It is proof that the academy doors opened and stayed open.
- Felician closes her eyes knowing that she survived the first Monday: shower, uniform, breakfast, classroom at 8:30, reading, math, first recess, social studies, drawing stick people in art, English, lunch, afternoon recess, Ballet, seeing Gamaliel Galvin teach Grade Seven and Grade Eight students lifts and jumps, seeing Klára come to get him for a ride home, deciding for herself whether to shower, and dinner.
