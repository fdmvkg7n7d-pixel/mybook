# Scene Outline: Monday After Klárá's Birthday Weekend — Work at Victoria Ballet Academy

## Placement

**When:** Monday morning after Klárá's November eighteenth-birthday weekend at Hilton Niagara Falls/Fallsview Hotel & Suites.

**Where:** Patti Anne and Aleksander's Vaughan home; Aleksander's vehicle; **Victoria Ballet Academy**.

**Characters:** Klárá, Gamaliel Galvin, Aleksander, Palmer Peter, Salena Sarai, Klárá's teacher supervisor, Grade Four students, Patti Anne referenced / possibly present during the morning routine, Victoria Ballet Academy staff / Senior Kindergarten teacher referenced.

---

## Scene Purpose

1. Show the Monday return to ordinary life after Klárá's very full eighteenth-birthday weekend.
2. Establish that **Gamaliel Galvin and Klárá go to work at Victoria Ballet Academy** on Monday.
3. Establish that **Klárá is teaching Grade Four**.
4. Establish that **Gamaliel Galvin is teaching Grade Eight**.
5. Establish that **Aleksander takes the children to Victoria Ballet Academy**.
6. Establish that **Palmer Peter and Salena Sarai are in Senior Kindergarten** at Victoria Ballet Academy.
7. Show Klárá wearing a **brown crop top** in a practical, non-sexualized way because her tattoo is on the left side of her ribcage and needs clothing that does not rub.
8. Show that **Klárá's Grade Four students are surprised about her tattoo**.
9. Show Klárá sitting in the middle of the studio, welcoming the Grade Four students into a half circle, and explaining the tattoo to them.
10. Establish that Klárá's teacher supervisor knows the whole story because Klárá was one of her past students.
11. Show the supervisor being very impressed by how Klárá explains the tattoo to the students.
12. Continue the tattoo-care thread: Gamaliel Galvin takes care of Klárá's tattoo because she trusts him and brings the cleaning supplies out for him.

---

## Core Emotional Throughline

The birthday weekend was extraordinary: tattoo, bandages, The Spaghetti Factory, the two vehicles, Hilton Niagara Falls/Fallsview Hotel & Suites, Steel Peak Climbing, swimming, and the Sunday-evening homecoming. Monday is the proof that the family can absorb all of that and still return to rhythm.

Klárá is eighteen now. She has a memorial tattoo on the left side of her ribcage. She is dating Gamaliel Galvin. She is still Patti Anne and Aleksander's oldest daughter. And on Monday morning, she still has to get dressed, protect the tattoo, go to work, and ride in the same family vehicle as the Senior Kindergarten twins.

The emotional center is ordinary care: Aleksander driving the children to Victoria Ballet Academy; Palmer Peter and Salena Sarai going to Senior Kindergarten; **Klárá teaching Grade Four**; **Gamaliel Galvin teaching Grade Eight**; Klárá's brown crop top making space for the tattoo; her Grade Four students being surprised by the tattoo; Klárá explaining her mother to them without bringing in her father; her teacher supervisor recognizing the maturity of that explanation because Klárá was once one of her students; and Gamaliel quietly continuing to care for the tattoo without making it a performance.

## Scene Structure

### Beat 1: Monday Morning After the Weekend

- The house is back in weekday motion after the Sunday-evening homecoming.
- The weekend is still present in small traces:
  - Klárá's tattoo-care supplies;
  - the memory of the twins hugging everyone at the door;
  - Palmer Peter and Salena Sarai still wanting to talk about Steel Peak Climbing and swimming;
  - Aleksander's iPad / book from the weekend;
  - laundry / overnight bags / small travel debris.
- Patti Anne may be helping keep the morning moving, but **Aleksander is the one taking the children to Victoria Ballet Academy**.

### Beat 2: Klárá Chooses the Brown Crop Top

- Klárá chooses a **brown crop top** for Monday.
- The choice is practical and specific:
  - the tattoo is on the **left side of her ribcage**;
  - tight fabric would rub the bandage / healing skin;
  - the crop top gives the tattoo space;
  - brown feels grounded, quiet, and not attention-seeking.
- The outfit should be written as work/dance-appropriate:
  - brown crop top;
  - high-waisted dance pants / leggings / warm-up pants;
  - optional cardigan, wrap, hoodie, or loose layer for the ride and hallways;
  - hair prepared for work at the academy.
- This is not framed as a provocative outfit. It is a practical choice for tattoo care and academy work.

### Beat 3: Gamaliel Takes Care of the Tattoo Before Work

- Klárá brings out the cleaning supplies for Gamaliel Galvin.
- She trusts him; that is why he does not need to ask for permission every time he helps.
- Gamaliel washes his hands, organizes the supplies, and helps take care of the tattoo before they leave.
- He checks:
  - whether the bandage / covering is sitting correctly;
  - whether the brown crop top rubs the left ribcage area;
  - whether she needs a looser layer;
  - whether she has supplies in her bag for the workday.
- The care is intimate, but calm. He is her boyfriend, but he is also steady and practical.
- Klárá lets him do it because the trust is already established.

### Beat 4: Palmer Peter and Salena Sarai Are in Senior Kindergarten

- Palmer Peter and Salena Sarai are getting ready for **Senior Kindergarten** at Victoria Ballet Academy.
- Their morning has a different rhythm from the older two:
  - small backpacks / school bags;
  - lunch / snack containers;
  - ballet or movement items as required by the academy;
  - Senior Kindergarten energy: questions, observations, interruptions.
- They are still processing the weekend:
  - they may ask if Klárá's tattoo still has a bandage;
  - they may ask whether Steel Peak Climbing was scary;
  - they may ask whether swimming hurt her tattoo;
  - they may repeat that they screamed because the tattoo was unexpected.
- No one shames them for the screaming. It has become part of the family story already.

### Beat 5: Aleksander Takes the Children to Victoria Ballet Academy

- Aleksander drives them to Victoria Ballet Academy.
- In this context, "the children" includes the whole household cluster in his vehicle:
  - Gamaliel Galvin and Klárá, eighteen and going to work;
  - Palmer Peter and Salena Sarai, in Senior Kindergarten.
- This should feel like a family Monday, not a chauffeur scene.
- Aleksander's role is steady and paternal:
  - he knows who needs to be dropped where;
  - he keeps the younger twins moving;
  - he respects that Gamaliel and Klárá are adults now but still his children in the household rhythm;
  - he does not make a speech about the weekend.

### Beat 6: The Car Ride

- Palmer Peter and Salena Sarai talk from the back / their seats.
- Possible child dialogue topics:
  - "Did Steel Peak Climbing have big walls?"
  - "Did you swim fast?"
  - "Can Senior Kindergarten go to the falls?"
  - "Does your tattoo still have a bandage?"
- Gamaliel and Klárá answer gently, without giving the twins too many private details.
- Klárá can say she is okay and that the tattoo is still healing.
- Gamaliel can mention Steel Peak Climbing and swimming in simple, child-friendly terms.
- Aleksander keeps the car calm and on schedule.

### Beat 7: Arrival at Victoria Ballet Academy

- Victoria Ballet Academy is in full Monday motion:
  - children arriving;
  - parents at drop-off;
  - academy staff moving through hallways;
  - studio bags and small voices;
  - the smell / feel of a school that is also a ballet academy.
- Aleksander takes Palmer Peter and Salena Sarai to the **Senior Kindergarten** area.
- The twins are not just tagging along; they belong there as students.
- Their Senior Kindergarten placement is clear and canon.

### Beat 8: Gamaliel and Klárá Go to Work

- Gamaliel Galvin and Klárá go to work at Victoria Ballet Academy.
- They are not written as Senior Kindergarten students or regular students here. They are young adults working at the academy after graduation / before the next life phase.
- Their teaching assignments are now canon:
  - **Klárá teaches Grade Four.**
  - **Gamaliel Galvin teaches Grade Eight.**
- The assignments show different kinds of responsibility:
  - Grade Four students are old enough to notice everything but still young enough to ask blunt questions.
  - Grade Eight students are older, more disciplined, and closer to the adolescent threshold Gamaliel and Klárá remember intensely.
- The academy is a bridge between their childhood and their future Prague academy.
- Working here matters because they are learning, in miniature, how a ballet school runs from inside the classroom.

### Beat 9: Klárá Teaches Grade Four in the Brown Crop Top

- Klárá's brown crop top remains practical at work.
- It allows her to move without fabric scraping the left side of her ribcage.
- In the Grade Four studio, the crop top / bandage / edge of the tattoo is noticeable enough that some of Klárá's students are surprised.
- **Klárá's Grade Four students are surprised about her tattoo.**
- Their surprise is childlike and immediate, not cruel:
  - they ask if it hurt;
  - they ask if it is real;
  - they ask why she has a bandage;
  - one student may ask whether teachers are allowed to have tattoos.
- Klárá does not answer while standing over them like a lecturer.
- **She sits down in the middle of the studio** and welcomes the Grade Four students to sit with her in a **half circle**.
- The half circle lowers the emotional temperature: she is still the teacher, but she is physically making the conversation safe, contained, and brief.
- Klárá keeps the answer age-appropriate and calm:
  - it is a new tattoo;
  - it is still healing;
  - nobody touches it;
  - it is for her mother;
  - class still starts on time.
- **Klárá tells her students about her mother without her father.** She can say the tattoo is for her mother, that her mother died, and that the tattoo helps her keep her mother with her.
- She does **not** bring her father into the explanation. No mention of him, no abuse context, no crime-scene context, and no family-history exposition belongs in the Grade Four classroom.
- She does **not** have to tell the Grade Four students every memorial detail: her mother's full name, birthday, and death date remain private unless she chooses to say them in a simple, controlled way.
- If a student asks about her father or why only her mother is on the tattoo, Klárá redirects gently: this tattoo is about her mother, and that is all the class needs to know.
- Klárá's teacher supervisor is present / observing the Grade Four class.
- The supervisor knows the whole story because **Klárá was one of her past students**. She knows the tattoo is not a fashion anecdote; she knows what Klárá is choosing to protect by explaining only the mother part.
- The supervisor is very impressed by how Klárá explains the tattoo to the students: truthful, calm, bounded, age-appropriate, and without exposing private trauma.
- The scene should not turn the tattoo into spectacle. It is a brief wave of student surprise, a small truthful sentence about her mother, and then Klárá calmly leads them back into class.
- She may wear a loose wrap / cardigan when not teaching or when moving through public hallways.
- Gamaliel tracks whether she is comfortable from his own Grade Eight workday / between classes, but he does not hover.
- The tattoo is private; the workday belongs to the academy.

### Beat 10: After Grade Four Class — The Supervisor Is Proud

- After the Grade Four class ends and the students leave, Klárá's teacher supervisor speaks to her privately.
- The supervisor knows the whole story because Klárá was one of her past students.
- She does not repeat the story back to Klárá or make Klárá stand inside it again.
- Instead, she focuses on what she just witnessed: Klárá teaching with maturity, boundaries, and gentleness.
- The supervisor tells Klárá that she is very impressed by how Klárá explained the tattoo to the Grade Four students.
- She also tells Klárá that she is **so proud of what Gamaliel Galvin and Klárá have achieved**.
- This lands hard because the supervisor knew Klárá as a student and now sees her as a teacher.
- Klárá becomes very emotional.
- The supervisor does not assume physical comfort is welcome. She asks first: **"Do you mind a hug?"**
- Klárá answers, **"Go for it."**
- The supervisor hugs her.
- The hug should be written as professional, maternal / teacherly, and consent-based — a past teacher honoring the grown student without taking over the moment.

### Beat 11: Gamaliel Teaches Grade Eight

- Gamaliel Galvin's Monday teaching assignment is **Grade Eight**.
- Grade Eight has a different energy from Klárá's Grade Four class:
  - older students;
  - more disciplined bodies;
  - more awareness of adolescent pride and embarrassment;
  - a stronger connection to the age when students start imagining who they might become.
- Gamaliel teaches with precision and calm.
- He does not let his worry about Klárá's tattoo distract him from his own students.
- If his Grade Eight students notice that he is watchful between classes, he keeps it professional.
- His Grade Eight teaching quietly foreshadows the school he and Klárá will later build in Prague: discipline, structure, safety, and respect in the same room.

### Beat 12: Aleksander Leaves Them There

- After dropping off the twins and making sure the older two are set for work, Aleksander leaves.
- He may remind Gamaliel and Klárá of practical things:
  - check in after work;
  - do not overdo the ribcage after the tattoo;
  - help the twins if needed;
  - be ready for pickup / transit later.
- He does not linger in a way that embarrasses them.
- The exit confirms the Monday structure: Aleksander has delivered all four children to the same academy, where two are in Senior Kindergarten, Klárá is teaching Grade Four, and Gamaliel Galvin is teaching Grade Eight.

### Beat 13: Closing Image

- Possible closing image:
  - Palmer Peter and Salena Sarai disappear into Senior Kindergarten with small backpacks.
  - Klárá steps toward her **Grade Four** students.
  - Gamaliel Galvin steps toward **Grade Eight**.
  - Klárá's brown crop top sits clear of the left ribcage tattoo.
  - Her Grade Four students' surprised questions are handled calmly and then folded back into class.
  - Her teacher supervisor tells her she is proud and asks before hugging her.
  - Gamaliel walks beside her when he can, close enough to help, not close enough to take over.
- The image connects their whole arc: they were five-year-old classmates at Victoria Ballet Academy; now, after Klárá's eighteenth birthday, she teaches Grade Four, Gamaliel Galvin teaches Grade Eight, and the younger twins begin their own school rhythm in Senior Kindergarten.

## Key Dialogue Possibilities

### Aleksander

> Senior Kindergarten first. Then you two go to work.

> Phones on. Do not overdo the ribcage today.

> I am taking all my children to the academy. Some of you are just taller now.

### Palmer Peter / Salena Sarai

> Did Steel Peak Climbing have big walls?

> Did swimming hurt your tattoo?

> Are you going to work or school?

> We are Senior Kindergarten. That is not babies.

### Grade Four Students

> You have a tattoo?

> Did it hurt?

> Are teachers allowed to have tattoos?

> Why did you get it?

### Gamaliel Galvin

> She brought the cleaning supplies out. I have it.

> The crop top is better. Nothing is rubbing the bandage.

> We are working today. Klárá has Grade Four. I have Grade Eight. You two are Senior Kindergarten today.

### Klárá

> Brown doesn't show everything. And it doesn't touch the bandage.

> It is still healing. I am okay.

> I can work. I just have to be careful with my side.

> Grade Four, hands off the bandage. Yes, it is new. No, we are not spending the whole class on it.

> Teachers can have tattoos. Teachers also still have to start class on time.

> It is for my mother. She died when I was little, and I wanted to keep her name with me. We are not talking about my father today.

> Come sit in a half circle. We can talk about it for a minute, and then we are starting class.

### Teacher Supervisor

> That was beautifully handled.

> I know what you chose not to say. I am very proud of how you protected yourself and still answered them honestly.

> I am so proud of what you and Gamaliel Galvin have achieved.

> Do you mind a hug?

### Klárá to the Supervisor

> Go for it.
