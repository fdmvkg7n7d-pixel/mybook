# SCENE OUTLINE: KLÁRKA’S BROKEN FOOT – EMERGENCY ROOM DIAGNOSIS

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Klára Key, Klárka Pavlînka (6), Simonka Káta (5), Emergency Room Doctor
- **Location:** Emergency Room, Prague
- **Date:** Sunday morning, circa 2079 (when Klárka is 6 and Simonka is 5)
- **Status:** New Scene Outline

---

## THE SETTING

- A typical busy Sunday morning in a Prague hospital emergency department.
- Klára arrives with both daughters. Klárka is in visible pain but trying to be brave; Simonka is quiet and worried, holding her sister’s hand.
- Gamaliel is away in Paris for ballet academy auditions, so Klára is managing this alone.

---

## STORY ARC

### 1. The Incident at Home (Sunday Morning)

- Klárka and Simonka are playing in the apartment.
- They decide to run to their parents’ bedroom (perhaps to show Klára something or to surprise her).
- While running down the hallway, Klárka trips or lands awkwardly.
- She immediately cries out in pain. Her right little toe/foot is clearly injured.
- Klára, hearing the cry, comes quickly and sees that Klárka cannot put weight on her right foot.

### 2. Decision to Go to the ER

- Klára quickly assesses the situation. The foot is already swelling and Klárka is in significant pain.
- She decides to take both girls to the emergency room immediately.
- Simonka insists on coming. Klára agrees.

### 3. Arrival at the Emergency Room

- Klára carries Klárka into the ER while Simonka walks beside them.
- They are seen relatively quickly on a Sunday morning.
- An X-ray is ordered for Klárka’s right foot.

### 4. The Examination and Doctor’s Questions

- The ER doctor examines Klárka’s foot and reviews the X-ray.
- The doctor notices the fracture is a clean break in the little toe/metatarsal area with minimal trauma.
- The doctor begins asking detailed questions about the girls’ daily life, activity levels, and home environment:
  - “Has Klárka had any previous fractures or injuries?”
  - “Do the girls play roughly at home?”
  - “Have you noticed any other bruises or injuries that seemed unusual?”
  - Questions about their physical activity, ballet training, and general health.

### 5. Klára’s Emotional Disclosure

- Klára senses the doctor is probing for signs of neglect or abuse.
- She becomes visibly emotional and decides to be completely transparent.
- Klára tells the doctor about her own childhood trauma — specifically what happened to her when she was seven years old (the abuse by her biological father that led to her removal and eventual fostering by Patti Anne).
- She explains her deep commitment to protecting her daughters and creating a safe home.
- The doctor is visibly moved by the raw honesty and emotion in Klára’s story and manner.

### 6. The Diagnosis of Osteogenesis Imperfecta Type 1

- The doctor explains that while the mechanism of injury seems minor for a fracture, combined with Klára’s openness and the family history, this raises clinical suspicion for a connective tissue/bone fragility disorder.
- The doctor orders further tests (possibly bloodwork or genetic screening referral) but states that the presentation is consistent with **Osteogenesis Imperfecta Type 1** (mild form).
- Key indicators mentioned:
  - Blue sclera (the doctor notes this during examination)
  - History of possible previous minor injuries
  - The low-impact nature of the current fracture
- The doctor reassures Klára that this is a manageable condition and praises her for being honest and bringing Klárka in promptly.

### 7. Immediate Aftermath

- Klárka’s foot is immobilized (buddy taping or small splint).
- Pain management is provided.
- Klára is given information about OI Type 1, precautions, and follow-up with a specialist.
- Both girls are quiet on the way home, processing what they’ve heard.
- Klára calls Gamaliel in Paris as soon as they return home to update him.

## CHARACTER NOTES

- **Klára:** Protective, emotional, and transparent. Her willingness to share her trauma history is what convinces the doctor of her credibility and shifts the conversation from suspicion to medical diagnosis.
- **Klárka Pavlînka (6):** Brave but in pain. She tries to stay strong for her little sister.
- **Simonka Káta (5):** Worried and quiet. She stays close to her sister throughout.
- **ER Doctor:** Initially cautious and investigative (standard protocol for pediatric fractures), but becomes supportive and compassionate after Klára’s disclosure.
