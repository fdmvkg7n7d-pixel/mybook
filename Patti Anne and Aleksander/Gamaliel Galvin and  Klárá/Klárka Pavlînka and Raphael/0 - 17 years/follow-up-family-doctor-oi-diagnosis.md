# SCENE OUTLINE: FOLLOW-UP WITH FAMILY DOCTOR – OI DIAGNOSIS CONFIRMATION

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Klára Key, Klárka Pavlînka (6), Simonka Káta (5), Dr. Jelena (Family Pediatrician)
- **Location:** Dr. Jelena’s pediatric office, Prague
- **Date:** Monday morning (the day after the Emergency Room visit), circa 2079
- **Status:** New Scene Outline

---

## THE SETTING

- Dr. Jelena’s familiar pediatric office — the same warm, child-friendly space the girls have known since they were babies.
- Klára has called first thing Monday morning and been given an urgent same-day appointment.
- Both girls are present. Klárka’s right foot is buddy-taped and she is using a small pair of crutches or being carried by Klára.
- The atmosphere is serious but caring.

---

## STORY ARC

### 1. Arrival at the Office

- Klára arrives with both daughters.
- Dr. Jelena has already received a report from the ER and is expecting them.
- The usual warm greeting is present, but everyone senses the weight of the previous day’s events.

### 2. Examination and Review of ER Findings

- Dr. Jelena gently examines Klárka’s foot and reviews the X-rays sent from the ER.
- She confirms the fracture is consistent with what was seen the day before.
- She notes Klárka’s blue sclera during the exam (a classic sign of OI Type 1).

### 3. Formal Discussion of Osteogenesis Imperfecta Type 1

- Dr. Jelena explains the diagnosis clearly and compassionately to Klára, with age-appropriate language for the girls.
- She confirms that both Klárka and Simonka are likely affected (mild Type 1 OI), given the genetic nature of the condition.
- Key points covered:
  - Bones are more fragile than average but not as severe as other types of OI.
  - Blue sclera is a visible marker.
  - Low-impact activities can still cause fractures.
  - The condition is lifelong but manageable.

### 4. Medical Management Plan

- Dr. Jelena outlines the initial protocol:
  - Referral to a pediatric endocrinologist or bone specialist for confirmation and long-term care.
  - Calcium and Vitamin D supplementation.
  - Consideration of bisphosphonate therapy (Fosamax / Actonel) once confirmed by specialist.
  - Physical therapy focused on muscle strengthening to protect bones.
- She emphasizes the importance of the family’s existing “Consent to Touch” and bodily autonomy protocols, especially now that physical handling must be even more careful.

### 5. Adaptations for Daily Life and Ballet

- Discussion of how to modify the girls’ ballet training at the family academy:
  - Avoidance of high-impact jumps.
  - Focus on core strength, barre work, and controlled movements.
  - Klárka’s teaching role (later in life) will also need to account for this.
- Klára asks practical questions about school, play, and safety at home.

### 6. Emotional Processing

- Klára becomes emotional again as the long-term implications sink in.
- She expresses guilt and worry, but Dr. Jelena reassures her that early diagnosis is a gift and that the girls are in excellent hands.
- Klárka listens quietly, processing that her bones are “different.”
- Simonka stays close to her sister, holding her hand.

### 7. The “Tighten” Hug Adaptation

- Dr. Jelena is introduced to (or reminded of) the family’s signature “Tighten” hug.
- She helps the family adapt the physical aspect so it remains emotionally supportive without risking bone stress — slow, careful leaning rather than squeezing.
- This becomes an official part of their medical-emotional protocol going forward.

### 8. Closing the Appointment

- Dr. Jelena books the specialist referral and gives Klára written information and precautions.
- She praises Klára again for her honesty at the ER and for bringing the girls in promptly.
- The family leaves with a clear next-steps plan and a sense of support rather than fear.

## CHARACTER NOTES

- **Klárka Pavlînka (6):** Quiet and observant. She is beginning to understand that her body requires special care.

- **Dr. Jelena:** Compassionate, knowledgeable, and respectful of the family’s values. She integrates the new OI diagnosis into the existing medical autonomy framework.
