# SCENE OUTLINE: FIRST DAY AT THE BALLET ACADEMY – KLÁRKA AND SIMONKA

## SCENE INFORMATION

- **Characters:** Gamaliel Galvin Key, Klára Key, Klárka Pavlînka (6), Simonka Káta (5), Academy teachers and staff
- **Location:** Gamaliel Galvin and Klára’s Ballet Academy, Prague
- **Date:** Last week of August
- **Status:** New Scene Outline

---

## THE SETTING

- The first day of the new school year at Gamaliel Galvin and Klára’s ballet academy.
- The academy combines academic classes with professional-level ballet training.
- Both Klárka Pavlînka (6) and Simonka Káta (5) are starting their first official day.
- All teachers and relevant staff have already been informed about the girls’ Osteogenesis Imperfecta Type 1 diagnosis and the necessary accommodations.
- The girls are familiar with the building from growing up there, but this is their first day as official students.
- Because of their age difference, they are placed in different grades/groups.

---

## STORY ARC

### 1. Morning at Home – Getting Ready

- The morning begins with the usual careful routine of the girls taking their **Fosamax Plus D**, **Actonel**, and **Calcium**.
- There is a quiet, focused energy as the family prepares.
- Klárka is visibly eager and tries to act capable, while Simonka stays close to her sister.
- Gamaliel and Klára help the girls get dressed in their academy uniforms, being mindful of Klárka’s recently healed right foot.

### 2. Arrival at the Academy

- The family arrives together.
- Several teachers greet them calmly at the entrance, already aware of the girls’ condition.
- Klárka and Simonka are assigned to their respective age-appropriate groups (different grades due to their fifteen-month age gap).
- The teachers make a point of quietly acknowledging the OI accommodations with the parents without drawing attention to the girls.

### 3. Drop-Off

- Klára gives both girls a careful, adapted “Tighten” hug.
- Gamaliel crouches down to eye level and reminds them that their teachers know how to keep them safe and that they can always come to the office if they need anything.
- Klárka holds Simonka’s hand for a moment before they are led in different directions to their respective classes.
- Simonka looks back once at her parents before following her teacher.

### 4. During the Day – Academic and Ballet Classes

- The teachers are attentive and follow the established accommodations:
  - In academic classes, seating is arranged to minimize any risk of bumps or falls.
  - During ballet and movement classes, Klárka is given modified exercises (no high-impact jumps). She participates in barre work, stretching, and controlled center movements.
  - Simonka is in a separate group and follows the standard curriculum for her age.
- The girls do not see each other much during the day due to being in different grades.
- Both handle the day independently but with the quiet confidence they have developed from their family environment.

### 5. Lunch and Social Moments

- Lunch is eaten in supervised areas with their respective grade groups.
- A few children ask innocent questions about why Klárka moves differently or skips certain activities.
- The teachers gently handle the questions while Klárka responds with the calm directness she has learned at home.

### 6. End of the Day – Pickup from the Parents’ Office

- At the end of the day, both girls walk separately to **Gamaliel and Klára’s office** inside the academy.
- They knock on the door and enter to find both parents waiting.
- Klárka and Simonka go to their parents for their usual careful hugs.
- They share brief highlights from their first day in a mix of tiredness and quiet satisfaction.
- Gamaliel and Klára listen attentively, asking practical questions and checking in about any discomfort or fatigue.

### 7. The Drive Home

- The family drives home together.
- Klárka sits in the back seat with Simonka beside her.
- On the drive, the girls talk quietly about their favorite parts of the day (especially the ballet portions) and what they are looking forward to tomorrow.
- Gamaliel and Klára keep the conversation calm and steady, focusing on practical details rather than emotional reactions.

## CHARACTER NOTES

- **Gamaliel Galvin Key:** Calm and practical. He focuses on clear instructions and reassurance without emotional display.
- **Klára Key:** Steady and observant. She handles the day with quiet efficiency and attentiveness to the girls’ physical needs.
- **Klárka Pavlînka (6):** Determined and independent. She wants to manage the day on her own terms.
- **Simonka Káta (5):** Quiet and observant. She handles being in a different group from her sister with quiet resilience.
