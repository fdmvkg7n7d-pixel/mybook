## THE SETTING

- A specialized pediatric bone and endocrinology clinic in Prague.
- The atmosphere is clinical but child-friendly.
- Both parents — **Gamaliel and Klára** — attend the appointment together with Klárka and Simonka.
- This is the first major medical appointment where Gamaliel is present since returning from Paris.
- The referral was fast-tracked because the Emergency Room doctor noticed blue-tinted sclera in Klárka’s eyes.

---

## STORY ARC

### 1. The Family Arrives Together

- Gamaliel and Klára bring both girls to the specialist’s office.
- This feels like a significant step — both parents are present and united.
- Klárka’s foot has mostly healed from the previous week’s fracture, though she is still moving carefully.
- The family is called in together.

### 2. Examination of Both Girls

- The specialist examines Klárka first, reviewing her recent fracture and X-rays.
- He then examines Simonka, even though she has no current injury.
- During the eye examination, the specialist confirms that **both girls** have the characteristic **blue-tinted sclera** associated with Osteogenesis Imperfecta.
- He explains this gently to the children: the blue shine in the whites of their eyes is a visible sign of how their bodies are built.

### 3. Formal Diagnosis of Osteogenesis Imperfecta Type 1

- The specialist confirms the diagnosis of **Osteogenesis Imperfecta Type 1** (mild form) for **both Klárka and Simonka**.
- He directly links the diagnosis to the ER doctor’s observation of the blue sclera the previous week.
- Key explanations:
  - The condition is genetic, which is why both sisters are affected.
  - Type 1 is the mildest form.
  - Klárka’s low-impact fracture is consistent with the condition.
  - Simonka is also affected even without a current fracture.

### 4. Medical Management Discussion

- The specialist outlines the recommended plan:
  - Daily **Fosamax Plus D** and **Actonel** (bisphosphonates) combined with **Calcium** supplementation to improve bone density and reduce fracture risk.
  - Physical therapy focused on muscle strengthening to protect the bones.
  - The specialist notes that the ballet academy has on-site physical therapists who can work with the girls.
  - Regular monitoring and follow-up appointments.
  - Activity modifications to reduce fracture risk while still allowing an active childhood.
- The girls can continue most of their normal activities with appropriate care and supervision.
- Regarding summer camp: Klárka will not participate in the dance portions but can still join the other activities such as painting, coloring, and non-physical creative sessions.

### 5. Both Parents Engage

- Gamaliel asks practical questions about long-term outlook and daily precautions.
- Klára shares details about the family’s existing “Consent to Touch” protocol and the adapted “Tighten” hug.
- The specialist is impressed by how proactive and emotionally attuned the parents are.

### 6. Emotional Processing

- Having both parents present makes the confirmation feel more real and weighty.
- Klára becomes emotional as the specialist confirms both daughters have OI.
- Gamaliel remains steady but clearly affected; he reaches for Klára’s hand during the discussion.
- The girls are quiet but attentive. Klárka seems to understand more of what is being said.

### 7. Closing the Appointment

- The specialist provides prescriptions, written information, and schedules the next follow-up.
- He reassures the family that with good management, the girls can live full and active lives.
- The family leaves together, processing the confirmed diagnosis as a unit.
