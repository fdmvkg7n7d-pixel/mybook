## CORE IDEA

Amazonat Jungle Lodge becomes a **recurring family destination** rather than a one-time childhood trip. After going there as a girl with Gamaliel and Klára, **Klárka returns as an adult with Raphael and her own family**, bringing the next generation into a place already layered with Key-Dimitrov memory.

---

## WHY THIS SCENE EXISTS

1. To show **Klárka as a mother** in a place she first knew as a daughter.
2. To make Amazonat part of the family's **intergenerational geography**.
3. To let Klárka carry forward the warmer, more openly joyful side of the inheritance.
4. To create a contrast with any later Simonka/Holic-family Brazil material.

## POSSIBLE BEATS

### 1. Return as an adult
- Klárka arrives at Amazonat no longer as a six-year-old child under Gamaliel and Klára's care, but as a parent herself.
- The return has emotional weight because the place is familiar and changed at once.

### 2. Raphael seeing the place through Klárka's memory
- Raphael experiences the lodge partly as a present-day place and partly as a location already storied in Klárka's childhood.
- This can deepen their partnership.

### 3. The children in water / jungle space
- Water, river, and heat become natural family material again.
- Strong chance to continue the branch's water motif.

### 4. Heritage becoming ordinary
- What was once a heritage trip for Gamaliel's paternal roots becomes, for Klárka's children, simply part of family life.

### 5. Echoes of Gamaliel and Klára
- A remembered story, place, or ritual from Klárka's childhood returns in altered form.
