# St Hilda's School Meeting — Scene Outline

## Placement

**When:** The Wednesday immediately following the weekend at Cottesloe Beach and the bath time revelation. The twins are preparing to start Junior Kindergarten (or early years).
**Where:** St Hilda's Anglican School for Girls in Mosman Park, Perth. Specifically, the office of the Head of Junior School or the Principal.
**Context:** The family has reached the "St Hilda's decision." St Hilda's is an independent, prestigious Anglican all-girls school. The parents need to finalize enrollment for both twins. Crucially, they need to formally introduce the school to Aleksandra Elya as she is now. 
**Special Circumstance:** Salena Sarai is about to leave Perth for Sydney temporarily for her ballet/dance job (a tour, a residency, or a choreography gig). Because she is leaving soon, the girls have come along to the meeting so they can all be together, and so the school can meet the twins in person before Salena Sarai has to fly out.

---

## Characters

- **Salena Sarai:** The perceptive one. She is usually the one reading rooms, understanding people's underlying motives. Today, she is fiercely protective. She is also balancing the emotional weight of her impending work trip to Sydney. She wants this settled before she gets on a plane.
- **Eli:** Steady, grounded, Australian. He doesn't read rooms the way his wife does; he just stands in them like a load-bearing wall. 
- **Aleksandra Elya & Xana Dylan:** The twins. They are present in the room—young, largely oblivious to the administrative tension, just two girls sitting in big chairs or playing quietly while the adults talk. Aleksandra Elya is dressed as herself.
- **The Head of Junior School (Placeholder name: Mrs. Aris / Fiona):** Professional, traditional but pastoral. Represents the institution—an Anglican girls' school with over 130 years of history. 

---

## Scene Outline

### 1. The Arrival and the Luggage
- Salena Sarai, Eli, and the twins arrive at the Mosman Park campus. It is beautiful, historic, and imposing. 
- There is a ticking clock: Salena Sarai is leaving for Sydney later that day (or the very next morning) for work. The meeting had to happen now, with all of them together. 
- Salena Sarai is vibrating with a quiet, fierce tension. She grew up Catholic; she knows what traditional religious institutions can be like. She is walking into an Anglican school about to tell them her young child is transgender. She is terrified but resolute.
- Eli manages the girls, holding hands as they walk up the limestone path. 

### 2. The Girls in the Office
- They are welcomed into the Head of Junior School’s office. 
- The twins are directed to a small play area or asked to sit in the oversized leather chairs. They are a visual focal point: two young children, separate but tethered by twinhood. Aleksandra Elya is wearing a dress or outfit that makes her presentation undeniable. 
- The meeting starts normally. The school is thrilled to have the Key twins joining them. The Head of School interacts warmly with the girls, asking them simple questions. 
- Then the Head of School opens the file. "Now, looking at the paperwork..." The original application might still say 'Junior' or the old male name. 

### 3. The Pivot and The Truth
- Salena Sarai takes a breath. She glances at her daughters playing quietly, then looks back at the Head of School. She doesn't apologize, she doesn't hedge. 
- She states it plainly. "We need to update that application. We are enrolling two daughters."
- She explains who Aleksandra Elya is. She explains that the child sitting right there in the office is a girl. 
- Salena Sarai watches the Head of School's face intently. Her "reader of people" instinct is dialed to the maximum, looking for a flinch, a judgment, a hesitation. She needs to know her daughter is safe here before she boards her flight to Sydney. 

### 4. Eli's Anchor
- There is a brief pause as the Head of School processes the information. 
- Before any institutional hedging can happen, Eli leans forward. He doesn't do politics, he doesn't do defensive posturing. He just does plain, Australian common sense.
- "She's a girl," Eli says, his voice completely level and calm. "This is a girls' school. Seems like the right place for her to be. We just need to know if you agree."
- It is a brilliant, understated challenge. He is boiling it down to the simplest possible equation.

### 5. The School's Response
- The Head of School looks at Eli, then at Salena Sarai, and finally at Aleksandra Elya, who might be busy looking at a picture book. 
- The Head of School closes the folder, folds her hands, and smiles—a genuine, pastoral smile. 
- "St Hilda's is a school for girls," she says softly. "If she is a girl, she belongs here. We will update the name on the roster to Aleksandra Elya. What pronouns is she using at home?"
- Salena Sarai exhales. The fight she had been bracing for, the defense she had built up in her chest all morning, suddenly dissolves. 
- They discuss practicalities: uniforms. The school confirms Aleksandra Elya will wear the same summer dress uniform as Xana Dylan. 

### 6. The Walk Out and The Departure
- The meeting concludes with handshakes and warmth. 
- Salena Sarai, Eli, and the twins walk out of the office, back into the bright Perth sunshine. 
- Salena Sarai stops on the path. She looks at the campus—the limestone, the trees, the older girls laughing on the lawns. This is where her daughters will grow up. This place is safe.
- But now the ticking clock returns. It is time for her to go to the airport for her Sydney flight. 
- She kneels down on the path to hug the girls. It is harder to leave now that the adrenaline of the meeting has faded, but it is also easier because she knows they are secure. 
- She hugs Xana Dylan. She hugs Aleksandra Elya, smoothing a hand over her hair. 
- She stands up and leans into Eli. She might shed a single, relieved tear. 
- Eli puts an arm around her. "Told you," he says gently. "Good school. We'll be fine here while you're gone."
- "Yeah," Salena Sarai says, picking up her bag or checking her phone for her flight. "Good school."

## Tone & Style Notes

- **The contrast of expectation vs. reality:** Salena Sarai is braced for a battle because of her own history with religious institutions and the general protectiveness of a mother. The profound relief comes from the school's grace and simple acceptance. 
- **Eli's plainspoken power:** Eli shouldn't give a grand speech. His strength is in his simplicity. "She's a girl, this is a girls' school." He neutralizes the "issue" by refusing to treat it as an issue.
- **Salena Sarai's perception:** Describe the office and the Head of School through Salena Sarai's highly attuned senses. She notices the way the woman blinks, the tone of her voice, the way she holds the pen—she is ready to protect her child from even the slightest hint of institutional bigotry.
- **The Uniform:** Mentioning the school uniform (the St Hilda's summer dress) grounds the victory in a physical object. Aleksandra Elya gets the dress, just like her sister. 

---

## What This Scene Establishes

1. **The World Outside the House:** Until now, the acceptance has been domestic (the beach, the bath, the bedroom). This scene proves that Salena Sarai and Eli can, and will, force the world to make space for their daughter. 
2. **St Hilda's is Locked In:** The girls are officially enrolled as sisters, setting the stage for their childhood education. 
3. **Eli's Role as Protector & Anchor:** While Salena Sarai is the emotional barometer and is preparing to leave for Sydney, Eli proves his steadiness. He will be the parent holding down the fort in Perth, and he proves he can handle it.
4. **Healing from Institutional Fear:** Salena Sarai's relief at the Anglican school's acceptance is a subtle healing moment regarding the family's complex relationship with faith and institutions.
5. **The Departure:** The scene serves as the emotional send-off for Salena Sarai's temporary move to Sydney. She leaves knowing her daughter is safe, protected by both her father and her new school.
