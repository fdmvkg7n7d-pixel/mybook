# The First Scattered Christmas — and New Year on Sydney Harbour

## Scene Outline (Intercut — Two Hemispheres)

> *Spans Christmas → New Year's Eve. Strand A: Palmer Peter home in snowy Canada. Strand B: the Prague family joins Salena mid-tour in the Australian outback for Christmas, then travels to **Sydney** for the **New Year's Eve harbour fireworks** before flying home. The New Year's Eve fireworks are the deliberate generational inversion of the Key family's snow-and-stars cottage New Year (where Klára named Willow).*

## Placement

**When:** The first Christmas after the twins move out. **Ages (locked):** Palmer Peter & Salena Sarai **18**; Gamaliel **31**, Klára (same age band); Klárka Pavlínka **6**; Simonka Káta **5**. (Anchor: twins 18 → Gamaliel 31 → Klárka 6 / Simonka 5; Gamaliel was ~25 at Klárka's birth.)

**Where (two places at once):**
- **Strand A — Canada.** Patti Anne and Aleksander's house (Vaughan). Winter, snow, the Catholic family Christmas. **Palmer Peter is home** — eighteen, **still Catholic (pre-conversion; that comes at 20).**
- **Strand B — Australia.** The middle of Salena Sarai's two-month Sydney→Perth tour (≈the red-centre / south-coast leg). High summer, heat, no snow. **Gamaliel, Klára, and the two little girls (Klárka 6, Simonka 5) fly out and join Salena mid-route** so the farthest-flung Key isn't alone.

**Continuity:** See `Patti Anne and Aleksander` → "The Empty Nest — and Christmas"; `Salena Sarai/salena-sarai-key-profile.md` (the tour + Christmas); `Palmer Peter/palmer-peter-key-profile.md` (pre-conversion at 18).

---

## Scene Purpose

The first Christmas the family is *scattered* — and the first proof that the Keys' core doctrine (*door cracked, porch light on, nobody alone at Christmas*) still holds when the family is spread across three continents. It establishes:

1. **Patti Anne's peace with the empty nest** — she *wants* her quiet, just not at Christmas — and her engineering of a Christmas where no child is alone.
2. **The balanced map:** Palmer Peter comes *home* (to Mom); the Prague family goes *out* (to Salena, mid-tour). The anchor holds both ends.
3. **The two little girls (6 and 5)** experiencing a hot, snowless Christmas — the opposite of every Key Christmas — as a first marker of the next generation being Prague-raised, not Canada-raised.
4. **A quiet seed:** Palmer Peter is still Catholic here. Whatever later takes him to Islam has not happened yet. This Christmas is the "before."

---

## Core Emotional Throughline

**Distance does not equal absence.** The family has been pulled apart by exactly the thing it most prizes — children brave enough to go far — and Christmas is where it proves the love travels. Patti Anne at her happiest is not a mother with all her chicks under one roof; she is a mother who has made sure each of them is *held,* then enjoys her quiet. The intercut structure is the argument: two Christmases, ten thousand kilometres apart, are still **one family's Christmas.**

## Characters

- **Patti Anne** — eldest Key sister, anchor; content in the quiet house all year, needs her people at Christmas. Bipolar disorder, devout Catholic, the mother whose love is *faster than annoyance.*
- **Aleksander** — her partner; the twins' father; steady, present.
- **Palmer Peter (18)** — home for Christmas; the still, watchful twin; **Catholic, pre-conversion.** First Christmas back after moving to Saudi Arabia for ballet.
- **Salena Sarai (18)** — mid-tour in Australia; the warm, restless twin; the one who reads people.
- **Gamaliel (31)** — Salena's half-brother (13 years up); business-minded academy director; flies the Prague household out.
- **Klára** — his wife; foster-raised, the one who knows what it is to be the kid who might be left.
- **Klárka Pavlínka (6)** and **Simonka Káta (5)** — their daughters; Prague-raised; a hot Christmas is novel and thrilling.
- **Absent presences:** each strand misses the other; the phone/video call is the hinge that joins them.

## STRAND A — CANADA: PALMER PETER COMES HOME

### Beat A1 — The quiet house, and the exception
- Establish Patti Anne's peace: the empty nest is a *rest.* She and Aleksander have settled into quiet — and she likes it. The house is calm in a way it never was with four kids and a dog.
- The exception lands the moment the calendar turns toward Advent: *not at Christmas.* Patti Anne, happy alone all year, starts getting the house ready for a child to come home.

### Beat A2 — Arrival
- Palmer Peter flies in from Saudi Arabia — eighteen, a few months into living on the other side of the world, **still Catholic.** The contrast he carries: a teenager who has been somewhere genuinely far, walking back into his childhood kitchen.
- Patti Anne's welcome — the mother's reflex, the *love faster than annoyance.* Whatever she feared about him being so far is answered by him simply being in the doorway. Snow on his shoulders.

### Beat A3 — The still one at the Catholic Christmas
- Palmer Peter at Mass with Patti Anne and Aleksander — **still her Catholic son,** no faith line yet crossed. (Quiet dramatic irony for the reader who knows what's coming at 20: this is the last Christmas of the "before.")
- The watchful twin observes home differently now — he has lived inside a society organized around another faith's daily rhythm, and he notices his own family's rituals with new eyes. Nothing announced; just a boy who has been away seeing home freshly. *(Light-touch seed, not foreshadowing-by-billboard.)*

### Beat A4 — Missing his twin
- The ache under the homecoming: **Salena Sarai is not here.** For eighteen years the twins have never had a Christmas apart. Palmer Peter is home; his other half is on the far side of the planet. The two who *belong by blood and never doubted it* are, for the first time, split.
- Patti Anne feels it too — one twin in her kitchen, one twin in a desert summer she can't picture.

## STRAND B — AUSTRALIA: THE PRAGUE FAMILY MEETS SALENA MID-TOUR

### Beat B1 — On the road
- Salena Sarai, eighteen, mid-tour — somewhere in the middle of the Sydney→Perth route (red centre / south coast). The summer heat; the vastness; a teenager who chose *far* and is in the thick of it.
- The thing she's bracing for: her first Christmas away from home, away from her twin, alone on the road at the bottom of the world.

### Beat B2 — They came
- **Gamaliel (31), Klára, and the girls (6 and 5) arrive** — flown out to join her *mid-route,* not waiting for Perth. The surprise/relief of it: the half-generation-up brother and the foster-sister she once blessed as a toddler crossed the world so she wouldn't be alone.
- **Key image:** Salena, braced for solitude, turning to find her two tiny nieces barrelling toward her in the heat — Prague-raised girls who have never seen a Christmas like this either.

### Beat B3 — A hot, snowless Christmas
- The season clash made concrete: no snow, no fireplace, no Canadian-winter anything. Sun, dust or surf, a Christmas in shorts. For two girls (6 and 5) raised on European/Canadian Christmas imagery, this is delightfully *wrong* — and they love it.
- Small textures: an improvised Christmas on the road (a motel/holiday-park/rental, not a mansion; a far cry from the Key cottage and the Carlisle mansion). The contrast underlines that this generation's Christmases will not look like the ones that built the family.
- Klára's quiet resonance: she, of all people, understands the assignment — *make sure the one who might be left is not left.* She was that kid once.

### Beat B4 — The girls and their aunt
- Klárka (6) and Simonka (5) and Salena (18): the next generation meeting the family's youngest adult on the far side of the world. Seeds available — Salena the natural reader of people; Simonka the watcher who'll become the psychologist; whether the aunt clocks something in the quiet five-year-old. (Keep light; no plot freight.)

## PART — NEW YEAR'S EVE: SYDNEY HARBOUR FIREWORKS

*(After Christmas mid-tour, the whole group travels to **Sydney** for New Year's Eve — then the Prague family flies home and Salena resumes her tour west toward Perth. The visit spans Christmas → New Year.)*

### Beat NY1 — Back to where the tour began
- The group goes to **Sydney** for New Year's Eve. Neat geography: Sydney is where Salena's tour *started* (Beat B1 of her route) — so the family converges on the harbour city for the year's turning before she heads west again. The tour's beginning becomes the family's gathering point.

### Beat NY2 — The harbour, the heat, the crowd
- Establish the spectacle: **Sydney Harbour on New Year's Eve** — the Opera House and the Harbour Bridge, the water packed with boats, hundreds of thousands of people, the warm summer night. One of the most famous fireworks displays on Earth, and among the first major cities to ring in the new year.
- The Prague family — Gamaliel (31), Klára, Klárka (6), Simonka (5) — and Salena (18) find a vantage. The girls, small in a vast crowd, hoisted onto shoulders.

### Beat NY3 — The fireworks over the water (the generational echo)
- **The deliberate callback:** the Key family's New Year's Eve has always been **the cottage in northern Ontario** — the night Klára, at thirteen/fourteen, ran outside and saw a willow shape in a sky *saturated* with stars and named her puppy *Willow*. That New Year's was **silence, snow, and stars over black water.** *(See `Gamaliel Galvin and Klara/gamaliel-galvin-and-klara-key-profile.md` → "The Name — New Year's Eve, Northern Ontario.")*
- **This New Year's is the inversion:** noise, heat, a packed harbour, and **fireworks** over the water instead of stars. Same family ritual (the year turning, all of them together, looking *up*), opposite sky — manufactured light bursting over Sydney Harbour vs. the natural light of the Shield. The next generation's New Year's Eve is as loud and warm and crowded as Klára's was quiet and cold and empty.
- **Klára feels both at once.** She is the bridge: the foster girl who found a name in a silent northern sky is now a mother watching her own daughters watch fireworks over a foreign harbour. What she once found in stillness, her girls find in spectacle — and both are *her family at New Year's, looking up.*

### Beat NY4 — The girls and the sky
- Klárka (6) and Simonka (5) seeing the harbour fireworks — awe, the reflections doubling on the water. A line available for Simonka-the-watcher (counting, noticing, explaining) and Klárka-the-elder (pure delight).
- Optional tender beat: Klára or Gamaliel tells the girls — lightly, not as a lecture — about *their* New Year's at the cottage, the stars, how Willow got her name. The girls, who only know fireworks, half-listen. The story passes down anyway. The motif travels generations and hemispheres.

### Beat NY5 — Midnight, two hemispheres again
- The hinge returns at the turn of the year: a call (or the knowledge) that **back in Canada it is not midnight yet** — Sydney rings in the new year ~16 hours *ahead* of Toronto. The family in Sydney crosses into the new year first; Patti Anne, Aleksander, and Palmer Peter (18, home, pre-conversion) are still in the old one.
- **Patti Anne gets a call from the future:** her Australia family wishing her a happy new year *hours before her own midnight.* The anchor, holding both ends of the earth across both the date line and the year line.
- Salena and Palmer Peter — the twins — turning the year on opposite sides of the planet, hours apart, but talking across it. First New Year apart, like the first Christmas apart; survived the same way.

### Beat NY6 — Closing image
- The fireworks end; the harbour smoke drifts; the girls are asleep on shoulders. Salena, who chose *far* and is about to continue even farther west, has had her whole family at the turning of the year — Christmas in the outback, New Year on the harbour — before she goes on alone to Perth.
- **Final echo:** two New Year's skies held in one family's memory now — *stars over northern snow* and *fireworks over a summer harbour.* Same ritual, same looking-up, same people who refuse to let each other turn the year alone.

## CODA — THE SCATTER (THE AIRPORT SPLIT)

*(The morning after New Year's. The gathering that pulled the family together now fans back out — in four directions at once. This is the theme made literal: converge, then scatter.)*

### Beat S1 — Klára flies to Rio de Janeiro — to find teachers
- The academy business that ends the trip: **Klára does not fly home with Gamaliel and the girls. She goes on to Rio de Janeiro to recruit teachers for the Prague academy.** The school is growing past what the two of them can staff alone — the storefront has become a graded academy (`Gamaliel Galvin and Klara/gamaliel-galvin-and-klara-key-profile.md` → SHARED DREAM), and a graded school needs more faculty. Klára goes to **Rio's ballet world to find them** — to watch class, meet dancers and pedagogues, and bring teachers (or the promise of them) back to Prague.
- **Why Klára, not Gamaliel, on this one:** it is a *teaching/talent* errand, not an administrative one — judging how someone teaches, how they hold a room, whether they have the thing you can't fake. That is the **artist's** eye, and Klára is the artist. A nice inversion of the couple's usual split: normally Gamaliel handles business/logistics — but recruiting faculty is *her* expertise, so **she takes the business trip while he takes the diaper-bag duty.** The partnership is flexible, not rigid.
- *(Rio is an established saga location — see the Fallon Glenda / Charlie thread. Whether Klára's Rio trip ever brushes that thread is open; for now it is purely academy business. In Rio she recruits **Inácio Moraes** — see `Gamaliel Galvin and Klara/klara-recruits-teacher-in-rio-scene-outline.md`.)*

### Beat S2 — Gamaliel takes the girls home
- **Gamaliel takes Klárka (6) and Simonka (5) home to Prague** — the long-haul, multi-leg flight from Australia, solo, with two small children. The founder-director doing the most unglamorous job in the world: getting two tired little girls across the planet by himself.
- Character payoff: the man whose gift is *carrying the institutional weight so Klára never feels it* here literally carries the parenting weight so she can do the work only she can do. The boy who once learned at the barre to *hold a partner without making her feel heavy* is now the father holding the home steady while his wife travels. Tender, ordinary, exactly him.
- Small image: Gamaliel on the plane between two sleeping girls, one on each side, somewhere over the Indian Ocean — the academy's whole future (the daughters, the school Klára is staffing in Rio) suspended in transit.

### Beat S3 — Four directions (closing the whole piece)
- The full scatter, stated: from one gathered family at New Year, everyone goes their own way —
  - **Klára → Rio** (find teachers).
  - **Gamaliel + the girls → Prague** (home).
  - **Salena → onward west to Perth** (resume the tour, then WAAPA).
  - **Palmer Peter → back to Saudi Arabia** (Strand A); **Patti Anne & Aleksander → their quiet house** (the empty nest resumes, content until next Christmas).
- **Final thematic image:** the family is a thing that *converges and scatters* — pulled together for the turning of the year, then flung back to four continents to do the work and live the lives that being this brave, far-flung family requires. The porch light goes back on in the quiet Canadian house. Everyone is exactly where they chose to be — and not one of them was alone when the year turned.

## THE HINGE — THE CALL THAT JOINS THE TWO CHRISTMASES

### Beat C1 — Two ends of the earth, one call
- The strands connect: a video call between the Canada house and the Australia road. **Both twins on screen at once** — Palmer Peter in snow, Salena in summer — the single image that says *one family, two hemispheres.*
- Time-zone comedy/poignancy: it is a different day in each place; Christmas morning for one is Christmas night (or the day after) for the other. The family literally spans the date line of their own holiday.

### Beat C2 — Patti Anne sees both her far children
- Patti Anne gets what she actually wanted: not everyone in one room, but **every child held and not alone,** and her own eyes on all of them. The anchor's version of a complete Christmas.
- The twins to each other across the screen — the first Christmas apart, made survivable by being able to see each other. *The twins who never doubted they belonged, learning to belong across distance.*
- Gamaliel and Patti Anne — mother and eldest son — a beat between them: he took her instruction (don't let Salena be alone) and executed it. The anchor's work, delegated and done.

### Beat C3 — Closing image
- Mirror the family doctrine across the hemispheres: **the porch light on in the snow** (Canada) / **a light on the road in the summer dark** (Australia). Same family, same promise — *nobody alone at Christmas* — kept twice, ten thousand kilometres apart.
- Final grace note (the "before"): Palmer Peter, still Catholic, still eighteen, home — the reader holding the quiet knowledge that the next chapters take him somewhere none of them yet imagine. Tonight, none of that. Tonight he's just home for Christmas, and his twin is safe and laughing on a screen in the summer.

## What This Scene Establishes

1. **The scattered family still does Christmas** — the doctrine survives three continents.
2. **Patti Anne's mature motherhood** — engineer everyone's belonging, then enjoy the quiet; needs her people only at Christmas.
3. **The balanced map** — Palmer Peter home to Mom; Prague family out to Salena mid-tour.
4. **Palmer Peter is still Catholic at 18** — this is the "before" Christmas; the conversion (20) has not happened.
5. **The next generation (6 and 5) is Prague-raised** — a hot, snowless Christmas marks how different their childhood is from the Canadian-winter Keys.
6. **The twin bond across distance** — first Christmas *and* first New Year apart, survived by the call; "belonging across distance" introduced as their adult theme.
7. **The generational New Year's echo** — Sydney Harbour fireworks as the deliberate inversion of the Keys' snow-and-stars cottage New Year (where Klára named Willow). Same ritual (the year turning, the family looking up), opposite sky. The motif passes down a generation and across hemispheres; Klára is the bridge between the two skies.
8. **The academy keeps growing** — Klára going on to Rio to *find teachers* shows the school has outgrown its founders' own teaching (storefront → graded academy → needs faculty). Establishes recruiting as an ongoing academy activity and Klára as the one whose artist's eye judges teaching talent.
9. **The flexible partnership** — the couple's usual split (Gamaliel business / Klára art) inverts cleanly: Klára takes the talent-recruiting trip; Gamaliel takes the solo-parent long-haul home. The division of labor follows expertise, not gender or habit.
10. **Converge, then scatter** — the structural payoff: a family pulled together at New Year fans back out to four continents the next morning, each to the life that being this far-flung family requires.
