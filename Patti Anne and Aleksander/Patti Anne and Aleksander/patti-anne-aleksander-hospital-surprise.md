# SCENE OUTLINE: THE GRANDPARENT SURPRISE

## SCENE INFORMATION
- **Characters:** Simonka Káta (9), Klárka Pavlínka (10), Gamaliel Galvin Key, Klára, Patti Anne Key, Aleksander
- **Location:** University Hospital, Prague (Pediatric Recovery Room).
- **Date:** Two days post-surgery.
- **Status:** Outline

---

## THE SETTING
- A bright, sunny morning in the hospital room. 
- Simonka is sitting up in bed, propped by pillows, eating a light breakfast or drawing. Klárka is sitting on the edge of the bed, showing her something on a tablet.
- The atmosphere is one of "the morning after the storm"—relief has settled in, but the room still feels small and clinical.

---

## SCENE ARC

### 1. The Quiet Morning
- Gamaliel is checking his emails by the window; Klára is brushing Simonka's hair. 
- They are talking about what Simonka wants to do when she gets home. The "stone" is gone, and the focus is shifting back to normal life.

### 2. The Unannounced Guests
- There is a soft, rhythmic knock on the heavy hospital door.
- Before anyone can say "Enter," the door pushes open slowly.
- **The Reveal:** Patti Anne Key and Aleksander are standing in the doorway. They are still wearing their travel jackets, looking slightly tired from the transatlantic flight, but their faces are radiant.

### 3. The Girls' Reaction
- Simonka’s eyes go wide. "Babi! Děda!" (Grandmother! Grandfather!)
- Klárka nearly knocks over the breakfast tray as she jumps up.
- The surprise is total. The girls knew their grandparents were worried in Canada, but they didn't know they were on a plane.

### 4. The Grandparent Embrace
- Patti Anne moves straight to the bed. She doesn't hesitate. She ignores the IV pole and the monitors, leaning down to pull both girls into a soft, fragrant hug. She smells like home—lavender and Toronto air.
- **The Check-in:** Patti Anne pulls back just enough to look at Simonka. She does the "Grandmother scan," checking the color of her cheeks and the light in her eyes. "Oh, my brave girl," she whispers.
- Aleksander stands behind her, his large hand resting on Gamaliel’s shoulder. It’s a silent, masculine transfer of support—from father to son.

### 5. The Expansion of the Circle
- For the first time since the discovery in Crete, the "Circle of Inclusion" feels complete. It isn't just the four of them anymore; the elders have arrived to hold the perimeter.
- Patti Anne produces a small gift from her bag—perhaps something from Canada that the girls miss, like a specific maple candy or a small stuffed animal from a Toronto shop.
- Aleksander pulls up a chair, sitting close to the bed, and starts asking Klárka about her dance classes, immediately grounding the conversation in "normalcy."

### 6. The Permission to Let Go
- Seeing Patti Anne and Aleksander in the room, Gamaliel and Klára finally look like they can breathe. 
- Patti Anne looks at them and says, "Go. Go get a real coffee. Go walk for twenty minutes. We’ve got them."
- The shift in responsibility is immediate. The grandparents have taken the watch.

---

## CHARACTER NOTES
- **Patti Anne:** The emotional anchor. Her presence turns the hospital room into a family living room.
- **Aleksander:** The steady, quiet support. He doesn't need many words to make his presence felt.
- **Simonka (9):** Feels "doubly safe." If Babi and Děda came all this way, then everything must really be okay.
- **Klárka (10):** Relieved to share the protective burden. She can just be a granddaughter for a while.

---

## SAGA CONNECTIONS
- **Key Family Loyalty:** Demonstrates that when a Key is in trouble, the family crosses oceans to be there.
- **The "Babi" Role:** Reinforces Patti Anne’s central role as the matriarch of the younger generations.
